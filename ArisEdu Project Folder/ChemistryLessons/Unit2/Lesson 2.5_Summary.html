Lesson 2.5: Unit Conversions Summary

Key Concepts: Unit Conversions

Core Definitions

  • Dimensional Analysis: A method of converting units using conversion factors to cancel unwanted units.
  • Conversion Factor: A ratio equal to 1 that relates two different units (e.g., 1 km / 1000 m).
  • Setting Up Conversions: Place the given unit in a position to cancel, and the desired unit in the answer position.
  • Multi-step Conversion: Some conversions require more than one conversion factor chained together.
  • Unit Cancellation: When a unit appears in both numerator and denominator, it cancels out.
  • Always Check Units: The final answer must have the correct desired unit to verify the conversion.
