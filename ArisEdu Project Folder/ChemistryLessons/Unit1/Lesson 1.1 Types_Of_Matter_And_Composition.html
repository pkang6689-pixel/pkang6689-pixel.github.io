Lesson 1.1: Types of Matter & Composition Summary

Key Concepts: Types of Matter & Composition

Core Definitions

  • matter: Anything that has mass and takes up space
  • a pure substance: Matter with a fixed composition
  • an element: A substance that cannot be broken down chemically
  • a compound: Two or more elements chemically combined
  • a mixture: Two or more substances physically combined
  • an atom: The smallest unit of an element
  • Is air a pure substance or a mixture: Mixture
  • Is Au (Gold) an element or compound: Element
  • Is water (H2O) an element or compound: Compound
  • What are the two types of mixtures: Homogeneous and Heterogeneous

Practice Review

  • Q: Which of the following best describes a solid?
    A: Definite shape, definite volume
  • Q: What is an example of an extensive property?
    A: Mass
  • Q: True or False: A homogeneous mixture has a uniform composition.
    A: True
  • Q: Which state of matter has an indefinite shape and indefinite volume?
    A: Gas
  • Q: Which of the following is a PHYSICAL change?
    A: Melting ice
  • Q: A compound is defined as...
    A: Two or more elements chemically combined.
  • Q: Which method is best for separating a solid from a liquid in a heterogeneous mixture?
    A: Filtration
  • Q: The Law of Conservation of Mass states that:
    A: Mass is neither created nor destroyed.
  • Q: What is the smallest unit of an element that retains its properties?
    A: Atom
  • Q: Which is a Chemical Property?
    A: Flammability
