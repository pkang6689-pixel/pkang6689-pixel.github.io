Lesson 8.9: Real Gases & Deviations Summary

Key Concepts: Real Gases & Deviations

Core Definitions

  • Real Gases: Actual gases that deviate from ideal behavior, especially at high pressure and low temperature.
  • Intermolecular Forces: Real gas particles DO attract each other, unlike ideal gas assumptions.
  • Particle Volume: Real gas particles DO occupy space, unlike the ideal assumption of negligible volume.
  • High Pressure: Particles are forced close together; volume and intermolecular forces become significant.
  • Low Temperature: Particles move slowly; attractive forces have greater effect, causing deviation.
  • Most Ideal Behavior: Gases behave most ideally at HIGH temperature and LOW pressure.
