Lesson 7.8: Percent Yield Summary

Key Concepts: Percent Yield

Core Definitions

  • Actual Yield: The amount of product actually obtained from an experiment.
  • Theoretical Yield: The maximum amount of product calculated from stoichiometry (from limiting reagent).
  • Percent Yield: (Actual yield / Theoretical yield) Ã— 100%.
  • Why Less Than 100%: Side reactions, incomplete reactions, loss during transfer, or impurities.
  • 100% Yield: Means the actual yield equals the theoretical yield (rare in practice).
  • Greater Than 100%: Usually indicates experimental error (e.g., impurities adding mass).
