Lesson 12.1: Nuclear Fusion and Fission Summary

Key Concepts: Nuclear Fusion and Fission

Core Definitions

  • Nuclear Fission: The splitting of a heavy nucleus into two lighter nuclei, releasing large amounts of energy.
  • Nuclear Fusion: The combining of two light nuclei into one heavier nucleus, releasing even more energy.
  • Chain Reaction: In fission, released neutrons trigger further fission events, sustaining the reaction.
  • Mass Defect: The difference between the mass of the nucleus and the sum of its individual protons and neutrons — converted to energy via E = mc².
