AP Physics 2 - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Energy and Mass Equivalence is summarized by Einstein's famous equation E = mc². Mass and energy are interconvertible: the mass defect in nuclear reactions is converted to kinetic energy of products and radiation. One atomic mass unit (u) = 931.5 MeV/c². The Q-value of a reaction: Q = (Δm)c² = (mass of reactants - mass of products)c²; Q > 0 for exothermic (energy-releasing) reactions. This equivalence explains why the Sun loses 4 million tons of mass per second as it fuses hydrogen into helium, converting mass to radiant energy.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Mass-energy equivalence: E = mc²; mass is a form of energy; even tiny mass changes correspond to enormous energy due to c² ≈ 9 × 10¹⁶ m²/s²
  • Atomic mass unit: 1 u = 1.661 × 10⁻²⁷ kg = 931.5 MeV/c²; convenient unit for nuclear mass comparisons
  • Q-value: Q = (Σm_reactants - Σm_products)c²; Q > 0: exothermic (releases energy); Q < 0: endothermic (requires energy input)
  • Pair production: A gamma ray (E ≥ 1.022 MeV) creates an electron-positron pair near a nucleus: energy → mass; the reverse of annihilation
  • Annihilation: Particle + antiparticle → 2 gamma rays; mass → energy; e⁻ + e⁺ → 2γ (each 0.511 MeV); complete mass-to-energy conversion

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • The Sun converts 600 million tons of hydrogen into 596 million tons of helium each second; the 4 million ton mass deficit becomes 3.8 × 10²⁶ W of radiant power via E = mc²
  • PET scans detect positron annihilation: the 1.022 MeV of mass-energy from an electron-positron pair converts to two 0.511 MeV gamma rays traveling in opposite directions
  • Nuclear weapons: a 20-kiloton bomb converts only about 1 gram of matter to energy (E = mc² = 0.001 kg × 9 × 10¹⁶ = 9 × 10¹³ J ≈ 20 kilotons of TNT)
