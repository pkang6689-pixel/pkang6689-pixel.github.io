AP Physics 2 - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Electromagnetic Force unifies electric and magnetic forces acting on charged particles. The full Lorentz force is F = qE + qv × B, combining electric and magnetic effects. When E and B are perpendicular, a velocity selector filters particles: only those with v = E/B pass through undeflected (electric and magnetic forces balance). The electromagnetic force governs the behavior of all charged matter, from electrons in atoms to current in wires. Electromagnetic interactions are one of the four fundamental forces of nature.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Lorentz force: F = qE + qv × B; the total electromagnetic force on a charge; electric part depends on charge, magnetic part depends on charge and velocity
  • Velocity selector: Crossed E and B fields: only particles with v = E/B pass straight through; magnetic and electric forces cancel exactly
  • Work by electromagnetic forces: Electric force does work (changes KE); magnetic force does no work (only changes direction); F_mag ⊥ v always
  • Thomson's experiment: J.J. Thomson used crossed E and B fields to measure the electron's charge-to-mass ratio e/m, discovering the electron
  • Electromagnetic force as fundamental: One of four fundamental forces; governs chemistry, materials science, and all electrical/magnetic phenomena

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • CRT televisions and oscilloscopes: electron beams are steered by electric and magnetic fields to paint images on a phosphor screen
  • Velocity selectors in mass spectrometers: only ions with the correct speed enter the separation chamber, improving measurement accuracy
  • Charged particle therapy: proton beams for cancer treatment are steered using electromagnetic forces to precisely target tumors deep in the body
