AP Biology - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Cellular Respiration (overview) is the complete oxidation of glucose through glycolysis, pyruvate oxidation, the Krebs cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation, yielding approximately 30-32 ATP per glucose molecule. The overall equation is C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ~30-32 ATP. Cellular respiration is regulated at multiple control points to match ATP production to cellular demand, with glucose, fatty acids, and amino acids all serving as fuel sources.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Cellular respiration (overall): C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ~30-32 ATP; the complete aerobic oxidation of glucose
  • ATP yield per glucose: ~30-32 ATP total: 2 from glycolysis, 2 from Krebs cycle, ~26-28 from oxidative phosphorylation
  • Aerobic vs. anaerobic: Aerobic respiration requires O₂ and yields ~30-32 ATP; anaerobic fermentation produces only 2 ATP per glucose
  • Metabolic rate: The rate of energy expenditure by an organism; basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the minimum rate at rest
  • Redox reactions: Oxidation (loss of electrons/hydrogen) and reduction (gain of electrons/hydrogen); glucose oxidation transfers electrons to O₂ via NAD⁺ and FAD

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Exercise physiology: transitioning from aerobic to anaerobic respiration during intense activity when O₂ delivery cannot meet demand
  • Caloric content of foods measured by the energy released during complete oxidation: carbohydrates ~4 kcal/g, lipids ~9 kcal/g, proteins ~4 kcal/g
  • Metabolic syndrome and diabetes: dysregulation of glucose metabolism affecting cellular respiration, insulin signaling, and energy storage pathways
