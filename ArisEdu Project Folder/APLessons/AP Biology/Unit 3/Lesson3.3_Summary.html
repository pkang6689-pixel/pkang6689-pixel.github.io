AP Biology - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Regulation of Enzyme Activity controls metabolic pathways through allosteric regulation, competitive and noncompetitive inhibition, and feedback inhibition. Allosteric regulators bind to sites other than the active site, changing the enzyme's conformation to activate or inhibit it. Competitive inhibitors resemble the substrate and compete for the active site, while noncompetitive inhibitors bind elsewhere and reduce catalytic activity. Environmental factors (temperature, pH, salt concentration) also affect enzyme activity.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Allosteric regulation: Regulatory molecules bind to a site other than the active site, changing the enzyme's conformation and activity; can be activating or inhibiting
  • Competitive inhibitor: A molecule resembling the substrate that competes for the active site; increases apparent Km but does not affect Vmax (can be overcome by more substrate)
  • Noncompetitive inhibitor: A molecule that binds to a site other than the active site, reducing enzyme activity by changing the active site's shape; decreases Vmax but Km is unchanged
  • Feedback inhibition: The end product of a metabolic pathway inhibits an early enzyme in the pathway, preventing overproduction (e.g., isoleucine inhibiting threonine deaminase)
  • Cofactors and coenzymes: Non-protein helpers required for enzyme function; cofactors are inorganic ions (Zn²⁺, Fe²⁺); coenzymes are organic molecules (NAD⁺, vitamins)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Aspirin acting as an irreversible inhibitor of cyclooxygenase (COX), blocking prostaglandin synthesis and reducing pain, inflammation, and blood clotting
  • Lead poisoning: lead (Pb²⁺) replaces zinc in enzymes, acting as a noncompetitive inhibitor and disrupting nervous system, kidney, and blood cell function
  • Feedback inhibition in the cholesterol synthesis pathway: statins mimic the substrate of HMG-CoA reductase, acting as competitive inhibitors to lower cholesterol levels
