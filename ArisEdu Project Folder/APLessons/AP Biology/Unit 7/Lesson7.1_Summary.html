AP Biology - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Evolution and Evidence establishes that populations change over time through multiple converging lines of evidence. The fossil record shows transitional forms (Tiktaalik between fish and tetrapods), comparative anatomy reveals homologous structures (bat wing, whale flipper, human arm sharing the same bone pattern), molecular biology shows DNA/protein sequence similarities correlating with evolutionary relatedness, and biogeography explains species distribution patterns. Evolution is the unifying theory of biology.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Homologous structures: Anatomical features in different species that share a common evolutionary origin but may serve different functions (e.g., vertebrate forelimbs)
  • Analogous structures: Structures with similar function but different evolutionary origins, resulting from convergent evolution (e.g., bird wing vs. insect wing)
  • Vestigial structures: Reduced or functionless remnants of structures that were functional in ancestors (e.g., human appendix, whale pelvis bones)
  • Fossil record: The chronological collection of fossils in rock strata documenting evolutionary changes over millions of years
  • Molecular evidence: DNA and protein sequence comparisons that reveal evolutionary relationships; more similar sequences indicate more recent common ancestry

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Whale evolution documented through a remarkable fossil series: Pakicetus (land mammal) → Ambulocetus (walking whale) → Dorudon (aquatic) → modern whales over ~50 million years
  • Antibiotic resistance in bacteria as observable evolution: mutations conferring resistance are selected for in the presence of antibiotics, producing resistant populations within months
  • Darwin's finches on the Galápagos Islands illustrating adaptive radiation: 14 species with different beak shapes evolved from a single ancestor to exploit different food sources
