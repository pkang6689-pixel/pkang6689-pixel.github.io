AP Human Geography - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Agricultural Biodiversity and Land Use investigates the relationship between crop and livestock diversity and land management practices. This lesson examines how agricultural systems maintain or reduce biodiversity and how land use choices affect ecological sustainability. Understanding these connections is crucial for recognizing the environmental and cultural dimensions of agriculture.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Monoculture: Growing a single crop repeatedly, which increases yields but reduces biodiversity
  • Polyculture: Growing multiple crops together, which maintains diversity but may reduce yields
  • Indigenous Crop Varieties: Locally adapted plant species developed over generations by traditional farming communities
  • Soil Depletion: Loss of soil fertility from intensive cultivation without adequate restoration practices
  • Agroforestry: Integrating trees with crop or livestock production for ecological and economic benefits

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Critical to food security challenges and climate adaptation in developing regions
  • Applied in environmental policy, conservation efforts, and sustainable development planning
  • Essential for understanding indigenous knowledge systems and cultural preservation
