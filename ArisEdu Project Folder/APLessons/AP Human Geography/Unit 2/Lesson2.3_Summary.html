AP Human Geography - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Population Change examines the factors that influence population growth and decline. This lesson analyzes birth rates, death rates, and natural increase, plus how these vary by development level and region. Understanding population change dynamics is crucial for predicting future social and environmental pressures.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Birth Rate: The number of births per 1,000 people in a population per year
  • Death Rate: The number of deaths per 1,000 people in a population per year
  • Natural Increase Rate: The difference between birth rate and death rate
  • Total Fertility Rate: The average number of children a woman is expected to have in her lifetime
  • Population Momentum: The continued growth of a population even after fertility rates decline due to age structure

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains high population growth in sub-Saharan Africa and low growth in Europe and East Asia
  • Critical for understanding social services needs, education, and economic planning
  • Essential for analyzing sustainability, resource consumption, and environmental pressures
