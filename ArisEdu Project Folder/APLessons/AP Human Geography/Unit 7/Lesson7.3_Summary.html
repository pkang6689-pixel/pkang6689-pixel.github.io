AP Human Geography - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Service Industries and Economic Development examines the shift from manufacturing to service-based economies and its significance for development. This lesson analyzes financial services, tourism, retail, technology, and how service sectors shape contemporary economies. Understanding services geography reveals post-industrial economic transformations.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Tertiary Sector: Economic activities providing services including retail, healthcare, education, and entertainment
  • Quaternary Sector: High-level services like financial services, research, and administration
  • Quinary Sector: Top-level decision-making services including government and corporate headquarters
  • World Cities: Global centers for finance, culture, and power like New York, London, and Tokyo
  • Tourism: Economic activity based on visitors traveling for leisure, business, or other purposes

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains concentration of financial and high-level services in certain world cities
  • Critical for understanding tourism's economic importance and cultural costs for developing nations
  • Essential for analyzing post-industrial economic structures in developed nations
