AP Human Geography - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Economic Inequality and Development analyzes disparities in wealth and opportunity between and within nations, and strategies to address development gaps. This lesson examines core-periphery relationships, structural adjustment, and development aid. Understanding economic inequality reveals causes of poverty and contested solutions.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Gross Domestic Product (GDP): Total value of goods and services produced within a country's borders
  • Core-Periphery: Wallerstein's world-systems theory dividing countries into wealthy cores exploiting poor peripheries
  • Structural Adjustment Programs: IMF/World Bank conditions requiring developing nations to adopt market-oriented policies
  • Microfinance: Small loans and financial services for poor entrepreneurs unable to access traditional banking
  • Informal Economy: Unregulated economic activity including street vending, day labor, and underground markets

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains persistent poverty despite economic growth and global wealth accumulation
  • Critical for understanding international debt, exploitation, and power imbalances
  • Essential for analyzing poverty reduction strategies and development policy effectiveness
