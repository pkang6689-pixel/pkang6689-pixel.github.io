AP Human Geography - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Industrial Origins and Diffusion traces the emergence of manufacturing and its global spread from its origins in Europe. This lesson examines factors enabling industrialization, the diffusion of industry to other regions, and contemporary manufacturing patterns. Understanding industrial geography reveals how production systems shape economies and labor.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Industrial Revolution: The transformation from agricultural to manufacturing economies, beginning in 18th-century Britain
  • Cottage Industries: Small-scale household manufacturing before factory-based production
  • Factory System: Centralized manufacture of goods in large facilities using machinery and divided labor
  • Industrial Heartland: The primary concentration of industrial manufacturing in Europe, then North America
  • Industrial Diffusion: The spread of manufacturing from core industrial nations to other regions seeking to industrialize

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains unequal development and technological advantages of early industrializers
  • Critical for understanding why certain regions developed and others remained agricultural
  • Essential for analyzing historical paths to development and global economic inequality
