AP Human Geography - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.6 - Summary

Key Concepts

Globalization and Economic Interdependence examines the integration of world economies and societies through trade, investment, and technology. This lesson analyzes multinational corporations, free trade agreements, and cultural impacts of globalization. Understanding globalization reveals both opportunities and threats from increasing interconnectedness.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Globalization: The worldwide process of increasing economic, political, cultural, and social integration
  • Multinational Corporations (MNCs): Companies with operations, ownership, and control across multiple countries
  • Foreign Direct Investment: Investment by firms or individuals in another country's businesses, infrastructure, or real estate
  • Free Trade Agreements: Bilateral or multilateral agreements eliminating tariffs and barriers to trade between nations
  • Glocalization: The adaptation of global products and practices to local cultural contexts

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains rapid economic growth in some developing nations and challenges in others
  • Critical for understanding cultural homogenization versus local resistance and adaptation
  • Essential for analyzing environmental, labor, and social justice concerns raised by globalization
