AP Human Geography - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Urban Change and Revitalization examines transformations in cities through deindustrialization, urban renewal, and regeneration strategies. This lesson analyzes how cities adapt to economic change, address decline, and pursue revitalization. Understanding urban change reveals resilience, inequality, and contested visions of urban futures.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Deindustrialization: The decline of manufacturing and industrial production in previously industrial cities
  • Urban Renewal: Government-led programs to rebuild declining urban neighborhoods through demolition and reconstruction
  • Gentrification: The transformation of working-class areas through investment, in-migration of wealthier residents, and displacement
  • Post-Industrial City: A city that has shifted from manufacturing-based to service-based, information-based, or tourism-based economy
  • Adaptive Reuse: Converting old buildings like factories or warehouses to new purposes like lofts, galleries, or offices

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains urban decline in Rust Belt cities and their revitalization attempts
  • Critical for understanding displacement pressures on low-income residents from gentrification
  • Essential for analyzing urban economic transitions and quality-of-life changes
