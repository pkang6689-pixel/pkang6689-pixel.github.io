AP Human Geography - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Urban Structure and Land Use examines how cities are organized spatially and how land is allocated to different functions. This lesson investigates models of urban form, residential patterns, and economic zones within cities. Understanding urban structure reveals power, inequality, and environmental impacts of city organization.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Central Business District (CBD): The core of a city containing major commercial, financial, and administrative functions
  • Concentric Zone Model: Burgess's model of urban structure with rings of different land uses and residential classes radiating from the center
  • Sector Model: Hoyt's model showing urban development along transportation corridors and economic sectors
  • Zoning: Government regulations restricting land use in particular areas for residential, commercial, or industrial purposes
  • Sprawl: The uncontrolled expansion of urban development into surrounding rural areas

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains residential segregation by class and environmental hazard placement in lower-income neighborhoods
  • Critical for understanding traffic congestion, pollution, and quality of life variation within cities
  • Essential for analyzing urban planning and land-use conflicts
