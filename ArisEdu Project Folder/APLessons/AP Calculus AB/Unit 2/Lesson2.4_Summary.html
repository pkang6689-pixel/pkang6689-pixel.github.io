AP Calculus AB - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Product, Quotient, and Chain Rules are the essential rules for differentiating composite expressions. The product rule: d/dx[f·g] = f'g + fg'. The quotient rule: d/dx[f/g] = (f'g - fg')/g². The chain rule: d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x))·g'(x). These three rules, combined with basic derivatives, enable differentiation of virtually any function. The chain rule is the most frequently used and connects to implicit differentiation and related rates.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Product rule: d/dx[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x); 'first times derivative of second plus second times derivative of first'
  • Quotient rule: d/dx[f(x)/g(x)] = [f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)] / [g(x)]²; 'low d-high minus high d-low over low squared'
  • Chain rule: d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) · g'(x); differentiate the outer function evaluated at the inner, then multiply by the derivative of the inner
  • Nested chain rule: For triply composed functions: d/dx[f(g(h(x)))] = f'(g(h(x))) · g'(h(x)) · h'(x)
  • When to use which rule: Product rule: two functions multiplied; Quotient rule: division; Chain rule: composition (function inside another function)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Finding the rate of change of kinetic energy KE = ½mv²: d(KE)/dt = mv·(dv/dt) = mva, using the chain rule with velocity as an intermediate variable
  • Compound interest: if A = P·e^(rt), then dA/dt = rP·e^(rt) = rA, showing the growth rate is proportional to the current amount
  • Optics: the rate of change of image position with respect to object position in a lens system requires the chain rule applied to the thin lens equation
