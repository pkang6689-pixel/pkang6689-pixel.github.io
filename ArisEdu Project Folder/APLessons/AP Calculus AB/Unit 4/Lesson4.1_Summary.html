AP Calculus AB - Unit 4 - Lesson 4.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Related Rates (Applications) solves real-world problems where multiple quantities change simultaneously with respect to time. Key problem types include: expanding circles/spheres (area, volume rates), ladder problems (Pythagorean theorem), shadow problems (similar triangles), and angular rate problems (trigonometric relationships). Success requires carefully identifying what is given, what is unknown, drawing a diagram, and choosing the correct geometric or physical relationship between variables.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Expanding/contracting shapes: Circles: dA/dt = 2πr·dr/dt; Spheres: dV/dt = 4πr²·dr/dt; relates radius rate to area/volume rate
  • Shadow problems: Use similar triangles to relate the heights and distances of an object and its shadow; differentiate to find shadow length rate
  • Distance rate problems: If D² = x² + y², then D·dD/dt = x·dx/dt + y·dy/dt; used for approaching/receding objects
  • Angular rate problems: Use inverse trig relationships: if tan θ = y/x, then dθ/dt relates to dy/dt and dx/dt via sec²θ
  • Cone problems: A conical tank: V = (1/3)πr²h with similar triangles r = (R/H)h gives V in terms of h only; differentiate for dh/dt

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Weather balloon expansion: as a balloon rises, the pressure decreases and its radius increases; related rates determine how fast volume changes with altitude
  • Oil spill spreading: a circular oil slick expanding on water has dA/dt = 2πr·dr/dt, used to estimate cleanup time and containment needs
  • Surveillance camera tracking: as a car drives past a fixed camera, the rate of angle change dθ/dt determines how fast the camera must pan to keep the car in frame
