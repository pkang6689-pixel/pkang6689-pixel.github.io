AP Calculus AB - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Net Change and Accumulation applies the Fundamental Theorem in context: ∫ₐᵇ f'(x)dx = f(b) - f(a), so the integral of a rate of change over [a,b] equals the net change. If f'(t) is the rate of water flowing into a tank, then ∫₀ᵀ f'(t)dt is the net change in water volume. Net change can be positive or negative. To find the total accumulated quantity when rate can be negative, integrate the absolute value: ∫|f'(t)|dt gives total variation.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Net change theorem: ∫ₐᵇ f'(x)dx = f(b) - f(a); the integral of a rate gives the net change in the quantity
  • Accumulation interpretation: ∫ₐˣ f'(t)dt represents the accumulated change from time a to time x; add to initial value for total: f(x) = f(a) + ∫ₐˣ f'(t)dt
  • Total vs. net: Net change: ∫f'(t)dt (can cancel positive and negative); total change: ∫|f'(t)|dt (always adds up)
  • Rate in minus rate out: Net accumulation = ∫(rate in - rate out)dt; used for tank, population, and economic problems
  • Units of the integral: Units of ∫f'(t)dt = [units of f'(t)] × [units of t] = units of f(t)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Traffic flow: ∫₇ᵃᵐ⁹ᵃᵐ (cars entering - cars leaving)dt gives the net change in cars on a highway during morning rush hour
  • Economics: ∫₀ᵀ (revenue rate - cost rate)dt = total profit over the period [0,T]
  • Hydrology: ∫(inflow rate - outflow rate)dt gives the net change in reservoir water level over a drought period
