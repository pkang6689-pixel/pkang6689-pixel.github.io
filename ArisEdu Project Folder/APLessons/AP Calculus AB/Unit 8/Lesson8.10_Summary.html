AP Calculus AB - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.10 - Summary

Key Concepts

Net Change in Applied Contexts applies the net change theorem to specific AP-style problems involving rates. Given a rate function r(t) and initial condition f(0) = A, the value at time T is f(T) = A + ∫₀ᵀ r(t)dt. When the rate changes character (positive to negative), the function switches from increasing to decreasing. Maximum and minimum values of the accumulation function correspond to zeros and sign changes of the rate function.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Applied net change: f(T) = f(0) + ∫₀ᵀ f'(t)dt; the final value = initial value + accumulated change
  • Rate sign and accumulation: When rate is positive: quantity increasing; when rate is negative: quantity decreasing; when rate is zero: momentary extreme
  • Maximum accumulation: The accumulated quantity is maximized when the rate changes from positive to negative (f' changes sign from + to -)
  • Interpreting ∫ₐᵇ r(t)dt: The integral of a rate function gives the total net change in the quantity over [a,b]
  • Context-specific units: ∫(gallons/min)dt gives gallons; ∫(people/hour)dt gives people; ∫(dollars/year)dt gives dollars

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Water tank problems: given inflow rate and outflow rate functions, compute water level at any time t and find when the tank is fullest
  • Population dynamics: given a birth rate minus death rate function, calculate total population change over a decade
  • Budget analysis: integrating income rate minus spending rate over a fiscal year to determine net financial position at year-end
