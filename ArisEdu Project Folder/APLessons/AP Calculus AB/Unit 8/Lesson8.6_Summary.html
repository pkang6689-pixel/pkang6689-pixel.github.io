AP Calculus AB - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.6 - Summary

Key Concepts

Work and Energy Applications extends the work integral to more complex scenarios. Work against gravity: W = ∫mgy'(x)dx for lifting objects along curves. Chain problems: work to lift a hanging chain involves integrating the varying weight as each segment is raised. Rope/cable problems similarly involve variable force due to changing weight of the remaining hanging portion. Energy conservation connects work to kinetic and potential energy changes.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Work lifting a chain/rope: W = ∫₀ᴸ ρg·x dx = ½ρgL² for lifting an entire chain of length L; integrates the weight of each segment times its lift distance
  • Variable density work: If density varies: W = ∫₀ᴸ ρ(x)·g·x dx; each infinitesimal segment has different weight
  • Work-energy theorem: Net work = change in kinetic energy: W_net = ½mv² - ½mv₀²; connects integration to energy principles
  • Conservative forces: Forces where work depends only on endpoints, not path (gravity, springs); W = -ΔPE
  • Pump problems: Work = ∫(weight of slice) × (distance lifted) dy; slice the water horizontally, each slice has different lift distance

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Elevator cable problems: computing the work to raise an elevator considering the changing weight of the cable being wound up
  • Oil well pumping: calculating the work to pump oil from a deep well where the weight of the oil column varies with depth
  • Rocket propulsion: as fuel is burned, the rocket becomes lighter, changing the force equation—integration handles the continuously varying mass
