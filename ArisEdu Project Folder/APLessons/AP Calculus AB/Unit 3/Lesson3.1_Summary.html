AP Calculus AB - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Chain Rule and Implicit Differentiation Combined handles the most complex differentiation scenarios. When equations involve nested compositions of functions with implicit variables, both techniques are applied simultaneously. For example, differentiating sin(xy) = x requires the chain rule for sin(·), the product rule for xy, and implicit treatment of y: cos(xy)·(y + x·dy/dx) = 1. These combined techniques are essential for related rates problems and advanced curve analysis.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Combined technique: Apply chain rule to composed functions while treating y as a function of x and including dy/dx wherever y is differentiated
  • Differentiating products with y: d/dx[xy] = y + x·dy/dx; product rule where one factor is the implicit function y
  • Nested implicit derivatives: d/dx[sin(y²)] = cos(y²)·2y·dy/dx; chain rule through multiple layers with implicit y
  • Solving for dy/dx: After differentiating, collect all dy/dx terms on one side, factor out dy/dx, and divide
  • Tangent lines to implicit curves: Once dy/dx is found, use point-slope form: y - y₁ = (dy/dx)|_(x₁,y₁) · (x - x₁)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Modeling the trajectory of a projectile with air resistance: the implicit equation relating position components requires combined chain rule and implicit differentiation
  • Thermodynamics: the ideal gas law PV = nRT implicitly relates P, V, and T; implicit differentiation reveals how pressure changes with volume at constant temperature
  • Computer-aided design (CAD): finding normal vectors to complex curved surfaces defined implicitly requires combined differentiation techniques
