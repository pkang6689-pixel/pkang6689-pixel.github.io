AP Calculus AB - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.6 - Summary

Key Concepts

Integration by Parts is the integration counterpart of the product rule. The formula: ∫u dv = uv - ∫v du. Choose u and dv from the integrand using the LIATE priority: Logarithmic, Inverse trig, Algebraic, Trigonometric, Exponential (choose u from earlier in the list). Integration by parts may need to be applied more than once. For definite integrals: ∫ₐᵇ u dv = [uv]ₐᵇ - ∫ₐᵇ v du.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Integration by parts formula: ∫u dv = uv - ∫v du; derived from the product rule: d(uv) = u dv + v du
  • LIATE rule: Priority for choosing u: Logarithmic > Inverse trig > Algebraic > Trigonometric > Exponential; u is differentiated, dv is integrated
  • Tabular method: For repeated integration by parts (e.g., ∫x³eˣdx): organize u derivatives and dv antiderivatives in a table with alternating signs
  • Cyclic integration by parts: For ∫eˣ sin x dx: applying IBP twice returns to the original integral; solve algebraically for the integral
  • Definite integral version: ∫ₐᵇ u dv = [uv]ₐᵇ - ∫ₐᵇ v du; evaluate the boundary term at a and b

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Computing moments of inertia in physics: ∫x²·f(x)dx often requires integration by parts, essential for structural and mechanical engineering
  • Probability: finding expected values E[X] = ∫x·f(x)dx for continuous random variables regularly requires integration by parts
  • Signal analysis: computing Laplace transforms ∫₀^∞ f(t)·e^(-st) dt, a fundamental tool in control systems engineering, uses integration by parts
