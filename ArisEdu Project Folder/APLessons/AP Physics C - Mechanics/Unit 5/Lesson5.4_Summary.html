AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Center of Mass is the mass-weighted average position of a system: x_cm = Σm_i x_i / M_total (or r_cm = ∫r dm / M for continuous distributions). The center of mass moves as if all the system's mass were concentrated there with F_ext = Ma_cm. For an isolated system, the center of mass velocity is constant regardless of internal interactions, making it invaluable for analyzing explosions and multi-body systems.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Center of mass (discrete): x_cm = Σm_i x_i / M_total; the mass-weighted average position of a collection of particles
  • Center of mass (continuous): x_cm = (1/M)∫x dm; requires integration for objects with continuous mass distributions
  • Center of mass motion: F_ext,net = M_total × a_cm; the center of mass accelerates as if all mass and net external force were concentrated there
  • Velocity of center of mass: v_cm = Σm_i v_i / M_total = p_total / M_total; constant for an isolated system
  • Center of mass reference frame: A frame moving with v_cm; simplifies collision analysis because total momentum is zero in this frame

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Spacecraft docking maneuvers calculating the center of mass of the combined vehicle to plan thruster firings for orbit adjustments
  • High jump technique (Fosbury Flop) allows the athlete's center of mass to pass below the bar even as their body clears it
  • Automotive engineering placing a vehicle's center of mass low and centrally to optimize handling, stability, and rollover resistance
