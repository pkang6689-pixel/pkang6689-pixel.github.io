AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Rotational Motion describes objects spinning about an axis using angular analogs of linear quantities: angular position θ, angular velocity ω = dθ/dt, and angular acceleration α = dω/dt. The relationship between linear and angular quantities (v = rω, a_t = rα, a_c = rω²) connects translational and rotational motion. For constant angular acceleration, rotational kinematic equations mirror their linear counterparts with θ, ω, α replacing x, v, a.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Angular velocity (ω): ω = dθ/dt; the rate of change of angular position, measured in rad/s; positive for counterclockwise rotation
  • Angular acceleration (α): α = dω/dt = d²θ/dt²; the rate of change of angular velocity, measured in rad/s²
  • Tangential velocity: v = rω; the linear speed of a point at distance r from the rotation axis
  • Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v²/r = rω²; the inward acceleration required for circular motion, directed toward the center
  • Rotational kinematics: ω = ω₀ + αt, θ = θ₀ + ω₀t + ½αt², ω² = ω₀² + 2α(θ - θ₀) for constant angular acceleration

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Engineering centrifuge design calculating the g-forces (a_c = rω²) experienced by samples in medical and materials science applications
  • Hard drive engineering specifying angular velocity (7200 RPM) and computing read/write head linear speed at different radii
  • Wind turbine design optimizing blade angular velocity to maximize energy capture while maintaining safe centripetal acceleration at blade tips
