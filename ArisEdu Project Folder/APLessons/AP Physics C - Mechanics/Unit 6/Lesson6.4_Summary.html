AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Angular Momentum and Conservation define angular momentum as L = Iω (for rotation about a fixed axis) or L = r × p (for a particle). Newton's Second Law for rotation in momentum form gives τ_net = dL/dt. When net external torque is zero, angular momentum is conserved: L_i = L_f (I₁ω₁ = I₂ω₂). Conservation of angular momentum explains phenomena from spinning ice skaters to the formation of galaxies.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Angular momentum (rigid body): L = Iω; the rotational analog of linear momentum, measured in kg·m²/s
  • Angular momentum (particle): L = r × p = r × mv; magnitude L = mvr sinθ; perpendicular distance times linear momentum
  • Conservation of angular momentum: When τ_net,ext = 0: I₁ω₁ = I₂ω₂; if I decreases, ω increases proportionally
  • Torque-angular momentum relation: τ_net = dL/dt; the rotational analog of F = dp/dt
  • Angular impulse: ∫τ dt = ΔL; the angular analog of linear impulse equals the change in angular momentum

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Ice skater spins: decreasing I by pulling arms in causes ω to increase to conserve L; extending arms slows the spin
  • Stellar evolution: collapsing gas clouds spin faster as they shrink (I decreases → ω increases), explaining the rapid rotation of neutron stars
  • Helicopter tail rotor design: compensating for the angular momentum of the main rotor to prevent the helicopter body from spinning in the opposite direction
