AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Orbital Motion applies Newton's Second Law with gravitational force to derive orbital parameters. Setting gravitational force equal to centripetal force: GMm/r² = mv²/r gives orbital speed v = √(GM/r) and period T = 2π√(r³/GM), which is Kepler's Third Law. Geosynchronous orbits, escape velocity (v_esc = √(2GM/r)), and energy in orbits (E = -GMm/2r) are key applications of gravitational and energy concepts.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Orbital velocity: v = √(GM/r); the speed needed for a stable circular orbit at distance r from the center of mass M
  • Kepler's Third Law: T² = (4π²/GM)r³; orbital period squared is proportional to orbital radius cubed
  • Escape velocity: v_esc = √(2GM/r); the minimum speed needed to escape a gravitational field entirely (total energy = 0)
  • Geosynchronous orbit: An orbit with T = 24 hours at r ≈ 42,200 km from Earth's center; the satellite stays above one point on the equator
  • Total orbital energy: E = KE + PE = -GMm/(2r); negative for bound orbits; more negative means a lower, tighter orbit

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • SpaceX and NASA calculating orbital insertion burns using v = √(GM/r) to place satellites at specific altitudes and orbital velocities
  • Communication satellite placement in geosynchronous orbit at r ≈ 42,200 km ensuring 24-hour period for continuous coverage of a fixed ground region
  • Interplanetary mission design computing escape velocity from Earth and transfer orbits (Hohmann transfers) to reach Mars or other planets
