AP Statistics - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

t-Distribution and t-Procedures provide the inferential framework for means when the population standard deviation is unknown. The one-sample t-statistic t = (x̄ - μ₀)/(s/√n) follows a t-distribution with df = n-1 under H₀. t-procedures include one-sample t-tests (testing a claim about μ) and one-sample t-intervals (estimating μ), both requiring random sampling and approximate normality of the population (or large sample size via the CLT).

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • One-sample t-test: Tests H₀: μ = μ₀ using t = (x̄ - μ₀)/(s/√n) with df = n - 1; used when σ is unknown
  • One-sample t-interval: x̄ ± t*·(s/√n); a confidence interval for the population mean using the t-distribution critical value
  • Standard error of x̄: SE = s/√n; estimates the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of x̄ using the sample standard deviation s
  • Conditions for t-procedures: Random sample, 10% condition (n < 10% of population), and Normal/Large Sample (population approximately normal or n ≥ 30)
  • t* critical value: The t-value with area C in the middle of the t-distribution with df = n-1; found from t-table or technology for the desired confidence level

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • A nutritionist testing whether the mean sodium content of a product exceeds 500 mg using a sample of 15 packages and a one-sample t-test
  • Engineering firms constructing a 95% t-interval for the mean breaking strength of a new alloy based on testing 20 specimens
  • Educational researchers using a one-sample t-test to determine whether average ACT scores in a district differ from the national mean of 21
