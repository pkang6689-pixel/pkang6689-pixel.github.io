AP Statistics - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Probability quantifies uncertainty on a scale from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain), with the Law of Large Numbers stating that relative frequency approaches true probability as the number of trials increases. The complement rule (P(Aᶜ) = 1 - P(A)), addition rule for unions, and multiplication rule for intersections form the core probability toolkit. Understanding the difference between theoretical, empirical, and subjective probability is essential for statistical reasoning.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Probability: A number between 0 and 1 that represents the long-run relative frequency of an outcome occurring
  • Law of Large Numbers: As the number of trials increases, the sample proportion of successes converges toward the true probability
  • Sample space: The set of all possible outcomes of a random phenomenon, denoted S
  • Event: A subset of the sample space; a collection of outcomes of interest
  • Complement rule: P(not A) = 1 - P(A); the probability that an event does NOT occur equals one minus the probability it does occur

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Casino probability calculations showing that the house edge guarantees long-run profit despite individual player wins, illustrating the Law of Large Numbers
  • Weather forecasting models that assign probabilities to precipitation events based on historical data and atmospheric conditions
  • Insurance companies using probability to set premiums by estimating the likelihood of claims based on actuarial data
