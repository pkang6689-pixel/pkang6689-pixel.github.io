AP Statistics - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Descriptive Statistics provide numerical and graphical summaries that reveal the center, spread, and shape of data distributions. Measures of center include the mean (arithmetic average sensitive to outliers), the median (resistant middle value), and the mode. Choosing between mean and median depends on the distribution's shape and presence of outliers, making it critical to examine data graphically before computing summaries.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Mean (x̄): The arithmetic average of all data values, calculated by summing all values and dividing by n; sensitive to outliers and skewness
  • Median: The middle value when data are ordered from least to greatest; resistant to outliers and preferred for skewed distributions
  • Interquartile range (IQR): The difference between Q3 (75th percentile) and Q1 (25th percentile), measuring the spread of the middle 50% of data
  • Five-number summary: Minimum, Q1, median, Q3, and maximum—the basis for constructing boxplots and identifying outliers
  • Outlier (1.5×IQR rule): A data point falling more than 1.5 × IQR below Q1 or above Q3, potentially indicating unusual observations or data errors

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Comparing median household income (resistant to billionaire outliers) versus mean income when evaluating economic policy and wealth distribution
  • Using boxplots and five-number summaries to compare test score distributions across multiple schools or demographic groups
  • Analyzing customer wait times at a restaurant by examining shape, center, and spread to identify service improvement opportunities
