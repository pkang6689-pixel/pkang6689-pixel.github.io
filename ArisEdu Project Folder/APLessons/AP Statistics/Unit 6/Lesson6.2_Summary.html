AP Statistics - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Hypotheses and P-Values form the logical framework for statistical significance testing. The null hypothesis H₀ states the default assumption (typically no effect or no difference), while the alternative Hₐ states what we suspect is true. The p-value measures the probability of observing data as extreme as or more extreme than the sample result, assuming H₀ is true. Small p-values (typically < 0.05) provide evidence against H₀.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Null hypothesis (H₀): The claim of no effect, no difference, or no change; the default assumption we test against using sample data
  • Alternative hypothesis (Hₐ): The claim we suspect is true and are trying to find evidence for; can be one-sided (< or >) or two-sided (≠)
  • P-value: The probability of obtaining a test statistic as extreme as or more extreme than the observed value, assuming H₀ is true
  • Significance level (α): The threshold for rejecting H₀, typically 0.05; if p-value ≤ α, we reject H₀ in favor of Hₐ
  • Statistical significance: A result is statistically significant at level α if the p-value is less than or equal to α, providing evidence against H₀

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • FDA drug approval requiring p < 0.05 evidence that a new medication performs better than placebo before allowing it on the market
  • A/B testing at tech companies: testing whether a new feature increases click-through rate, with H₀: no difference and p-value determining rollout decisions
  • Educational research testing whether a new teaching method improves student test scores, using the p-value to determine if observed improvements are statistically significant or likely due to chance
