AP Chemistry - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Metallic Bonding and Structures describes how metal atoms share their valence electrons in a 'sea of electrons' that surrounds a lattice of positive metal cations. This delocalized electron model explains metallic properties: electrical conductivity (electrons flow freely), thermal conductivity (electrons transfer kinetic energy), malleability and ductility (layers of cations slide without breaking bonds), and metallic luster (electrons absorb and re-emit photons). Alloys (mixtures of metals) modify properties by introducing different-sized atoms.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Metallic bond: The electrostatic attraction between delocalized valence electrons and positively charged metal cations in a lattice structure
  • Electron sea model: A model of metallic bonding where valence electrons are shared among all atoms in the metal, moving freely throughout the structure
  • Alloy: A mixture of two or more metals (or a metal and nonmetal); substitutional alloys have similar-sized atoms, interstitial alloys have small atoms in gaps
  • Malleability: The ability of metals to be hammered into thin sheets without breaking, due to layers of cations sliding past each other in the electron sea
  • Band theory: An advanced model of metallic bonding: overlapping atomic orbitals form continuous energy bands; partial filling of bands allows electron mobility

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Copper wiring in electrical systems exploiting metallic bonding's delocalized electrons for excellent electrical conductivity (second only to silver)
  • Steel alloys: adding carbon (interstitial) to iron increases hardness; adding chromium and nickel (substitutional) creates stainless steel resistant to corrosion
  • Gold's malleability allowing it to be hammered into gold leaf just 0.1 μm thick, used in electronics connections, architectural decoration, and aerospace heat shields
