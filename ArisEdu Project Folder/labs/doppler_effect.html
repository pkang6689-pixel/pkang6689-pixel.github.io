Doppler Effect

When a wave source moves toward you, wavefronts are compressed → higher frequency (blue-shift). Moving away → stretched → lower frequency (red-shift). Formula: f = f₀ × v/(v ± vₛ). When vₛ = v (Mach 1), wavefronts pile up into a shock wave (sonic boom). This applies to sound, light (used to measure stellar velocities), and radar (speed guns).
