The Derivative

The derivative f'(x) gives the slope of the tangent line at any point. It measures how fast f changes with x. Where f'(x)=0, f has a horizontal tangent (possible max/min). The derivative curve shows slopes everywhere: positive slope → increasing, negative slope → decreasing. Key rules: d/dx[xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹, d/dx[sin x] = cos x, d/dx[eˣ] = eˣ.
