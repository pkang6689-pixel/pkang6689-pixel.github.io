How Photosynthesis Works

Light Reactions (thylakoid): Water is split using light energy, producing O₂, ATP, and NADPH.
Calvin Cycle (stroma): CO₂ is fixed into glucose using ATP and NADPH.
The rate depends on light intensity, CO₂ concentration, and temperature. Too much heat denatures enzymes.
