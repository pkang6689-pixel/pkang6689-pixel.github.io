Coastal Erosion Processes

Hydraulic action: waves compress air in rock cracks, breaking them apart. Abrasion: sediment carried by waves grinds the cliff face. Corrosion: salt water dissolves minerals. Softer rock erodes faster, creating bays. Harder rock remains as headlands. Sea level rise accelerates erosion by bringing waves to higher positions.
