The Carbon Cycle

Carbon moves between four reservoirs: Atmosphere (CO₂, ~420 ppm), Biosphere (plants fix CO₂ via photosynthesis), Oceans (dissolved CO₂, largest sink), and Lithosphere (fossil fuels, rocks). Burning fossil fuels releases stored carbon, increasing atmospheric CO₂. This enhances the greenhouse effect, trapping heat. Oceans absorb ~25% of emissions but become more acidic.
