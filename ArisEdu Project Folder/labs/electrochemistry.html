Galvanic Cell

A galvanic (voltaic) cell converts chemical energy to electrical energy. The more reactive metal (anode) loses electrons through oxidation, which flow through an external wire to the less reactive metal (cathode) where reduction occurs. A salt bridge completes the circuit by allowing ion flow.
