Polar Coordinate System

Instead of (x, y), polar coordinates use (r, θ) where r is the distance from the origin and θ is the angle from the positive x-axis. Classic polar curves include the cardioid r = a(1+cosθ), rose curves r = a·cos(nθ) with n petals, the Archimedean spiral r = aθ, and the lemniscate r² = a²·cos(2θ). Conversions: x = r·cosθ, y = r·sinθ.
