Riemann Sums & Integrals

The definite integral ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx gives the exact area under the curve from a to b. Riemann sums approximate this with rectangles. Left sums use the left endpoint of each sub-interval; right sums use the right; midpoint uses the center. As n → ∞ all three converge to the exact integral. This is the foundation of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
