Surface Currents

Wind-driven surface currents circulate in large loops called gyres. In the Northern Hemisphere they flow clockwise, in the Southern Hemisphere counter-clockwise (Coriolis effect). The Gulf Stream carries warm water from the tropics northward along the US East Coast.
