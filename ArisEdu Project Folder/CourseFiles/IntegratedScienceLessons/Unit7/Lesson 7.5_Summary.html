Key Concepts: Careers in Earth & Environmental Sciences

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the primary work of an environmental consultant?

Environmental consultants help organizations comply with environmental laws by conducting site assessments, environmental impact studies, and remediation plans. They work in the private sector advising businesses, developers, and government agencies on environmental regulations.

What is a growing area of employment for Earth scientists that combines technology with environmental monitoring?

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and remote sensing represent rapidly growing career paths that combine Earth science knowledge with technology skills. These professionals analyze satellite data, create spatial analyses, and support environmental monitoring and resource management.

What is an environmental scientist?

An environmental scientist uses knowledge of the natural sciences to study the environment and develop solutions to environmental challenges such as pollution, climate change, and resource depletion. Their work integrates biology, chemistry, physics, and Earth science.

What is a seismologist?

A seismologist is a scientist specializing in the study of earthquakes and seismic waves. They analyze how seismic energy travels through Earth's interior, locate earthquake epicenters, assess earthquake hazards, and contribute to understanding Earth's internal structure.

What is an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)?

An Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) is a formal process used to evaluate the likely environmental effects of a proposed project, including construction, mining, or land development. It identifies potential impacts and proposes mitigation measures before the project begins.

What is a climatologist?

A climatologist studies long-term weather patterns and climate trends over years, decades, and centuries. Unlike meteorologists who forecast short-term weather, climatologists analyze historical climate data, investigate climate change, and model future climate scenarios.

What is environmental remediation?

Environmental remediation involves removing, reducing, or neutralizing contaminants from polluted sites such as former industrial areas, abandoned mines, or chemical spill locations. Techniques include bioremediation, soil excavation, pump-and-treat systems, and chemical treatment.

What is an oceanographer?

An oceanographer is a scientist who studies the ocean, which can include ocean currents and circulation (physical), seawater chemistry (chemical), marine organisms and ecosystems (biological), or the ocean floor and its geology (geological).

What is a conservation biologist?

A conservation biologist applies principles of ecology and biology to protect and restore biodiversity. They study endangered species, design wildlife reserves, assess ecosystem health, and develop strategies to maintain genetic diversity and habitat connectivity.

What is a soil scientist (pedologist)?

A soil scientist (pedologist) studies how soils form, their chemical and physical properties, their classification, and their distribution across landscapes. They advise on land use, agriculture, construction suitability, and environmental contamination assessment.

What is an environmental engineer?

Environmental engineers use engineering, soil science, biology, and chemistry to design practical solutions to environmental problems. They design water treatment plants, waste disposal systems, air pollution control equipment, and site remediation strategies.

A recent college graduate with a degree in environmental science is offered two positions: one as a field technician collecting water samples at monitoring stations, and another as a data analyst processing satellite vegetation data at a desk. She enjoys both outdoor work and technology. Her mentor advises that both positions build valuable but different skills. What is the most accurate career advice?

Modern environmental science careers increasingly require professionals who understand both fieldwork and technology. Those who can collect quality field data AND analyze it using GIS, remote sensing, and statistical tools are especially valuable, as they can bridge the gap between data collection and interpretation.

Real-World Applications

Example: Which government agency in the United States is primarily responsible for protecting human health and the environment through regulation and enforcement?

The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) is the U.S. federal agency tasked with protecting human health and the environment by writing and enforcing regulations based on laws passed by Congress, including the Clean Air Act and Clean Water Act.

Example: Which career focuses specifically on the study and management of water resources, including groundwater, rivers, and the water cycle?

Hydrologists study the distribution, movement, and quality of water on Earth. They analyze water supply problems, predict flooding, manage water resources, and assess how land use changes affect water systems.

Example: A chemical manufacturing plant is closing, and the surrounding soil and groundwater have been contaminated with industrial solvents. The state environmental agency requires cleanup before the land can be sold. Which type of professional would lead this project?

Environmental engineers and remediation specialists design and implement cleanup strategies for contaminated sites. They assess the extent of contamination, select appropriate treatment technologies (such as pump-and-treat, bioremediation, or soil vapor extraction), and oversee the cleanup to meet regulatory standards.

Example: A city in a drought-prone region needs to develop a long-term water management plan. They want to understand how much groundwater is being recharged annually, where surface water can be stored, and how climate change might affect future water availability. Which professional would they hire to lead this analysis?

A hydrologist specializes in studying the water cycle, groundwater recharge rates, surface water availability, and the effects of climate change on water resources. They are the primary professionals who would develop a comprehensive water management plan for a drought-prone region.

Example: A developing country wants to map its natural resources including mineral deposits, water availability, and forest cover to guide sustainable economic development. They need professionals who can integrate satellite imagery with ground survey data. Which career specialty would be central to this project?

GIS and remote sensing specialists can process satellite imagery to map land cover, identify mineral signatures, assess water resources, and integrate all data layers into a comprehensive geographic database that decision-makers can use for sustainable resource planning.

Example: After a magnitude 7.0 earthquake damages a coastal city, officials want to understand the risk of future earthquakes and tsunamis to guide reconstruction efforts. They also need to identify which areas of the city sit on unstable ground that amplified shaking. Which professionals should lead this assessment?

Seismologists can assess fault activity, earthquake recurrence intervals, and tsunami risk, while geotechnical engineers evaluate how subsurface geology (such as soft sediments or fill) amplifies seismic shaking. Together, they provide the hazard assessment needed to guide safer reconstruction.
