AP Calculus AB - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Exponential Growth and Decay models situations where the rate of change is proportional to the current amount: dy/dt = ky. The solution is y = y₀eᵏᵗ, where y₀ is the initial value. When k > 0: exponential growth (population, investment); when k < 0: exponential decay (radioactive decay, drug clearance). The doubling time (growth) is t₂ = ln(2)/k and the half-life (decay) is t₁/₂ = ln(2)/|k|. Continuous compound interest follows A = Pe^(rt).

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Exponential growth/decay DE: dy/dt = ky has solution y(t) = y₀eᵏᵗ; k > 0 = growth, k < 0 = decay
  • Doubling time: t₂ = ln(2)/k; the time for the quantity to double (when k > 0)
  • Half-life: t₁/₂ = ln(2)/|k|; the time for the quantity to decrease to half its initial value (when k < 0)
  • Continuous compound interest: A = Pe^(rt); the limit of compound interest as compounding frequency → ∞
  • Relative growth rate: (1/y)(dy/dt) = k; the growth rate as a fraction of the current value; constant for exponential models

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Radiocarbon dating: ¹⁴C decays with t₁/₂ ≈ 5,730 years; measuring remaining ¹⁴C in organic material determines its age up to ~50,000 years
  • Investment growth: at 7% continuous compound interest, doubling time = ln(2)/0.07 ≈ 10 years (the 'Rule of 70')
  • Bacterial growth: E. coli doubles every 20 minutes in ideal conditions; 1 bacterium becomes ~70 billion in 12 hours (2³⁶ ≈ 6.9 × 10¹⁰)
