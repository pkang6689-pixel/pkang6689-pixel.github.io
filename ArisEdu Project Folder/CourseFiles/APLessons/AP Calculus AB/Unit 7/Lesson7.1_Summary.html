AP Calculus AB - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Differential Equation Basics introduces equations involving derivatives. A differential equation relates a function to its derivatives: dy/dx = f(x,y). Solutions are functions y(x) that satisfy the equation. A general solution contains an arbitrary constant C; a particular solution satisfies an initial condition y(x₀) = y₀ that determines C. Verifying a solution means substituting it into the DE and confirming equality. Order of a DE = highest derivative present.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Differential equation (DE): An equation involving an unknown function and one or more of its derivatives: dy/dx = f(x,y)
  • General solution: The family of all solutions, containing an arbitrary constant C: y = F(x) + C for first-order DEs
  • Particular solution: A specific solution obtained by applying an initial condition y(x₀) = y₀ to determine the constant C
  • Initial condition: A known value of the function: y(x₀) = y₀; used to find the specific C in the general solution
  • Verifying a solution: Substitute the proposed solution into the DE and check that both sides are equal for all x in the domain

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Newton's second law F = ma is a differential equation: m·d²x/dt² = F(x,v,t), the foundation of classical mechanics
  • Population models: dP/dt = rP is a DE describing exponential growth; dP/dt = rP(1-P/K) describes logistic growth toward carrying capacity
  • RC circuits: the voltage across a charging capacitor satisfies dV/dt = (V_s - V)/(RC), a first-order DE governing charging and discharging
