AP Calculus AB - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Basic Derivative Rules provide shortcuts for finding derivatives without using the limit definition every time. The power rule: d/dx[xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹ for any real n. The constant multiple rule: d/dx[cf(x)] = cf'(x). The sum/difference rule: d/dx[f(x) ± g(x)] = f'(x) ± g'(x). These rules, combined with knowing that d/dx[c] = 0 for constants, allow differentiation of all polynomial functions.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Power rule: d/dx[xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹; works for any real exponent n, including negative and fractional powers
  • Constant rule: d/dx[c] = 0; the derivative of any constant is zero (horizontal tangent line)
  • Constant multiple rule: d/dx[cf(x)] = c·f'(x); constants factor out of the derivative
  • Sum/difference rule: d/dx[f(x) ± g(x)] = f'(x) ± g'(x); derivative of a sum/difference is the sum/difference of derivatives
  • Polynomial differentiation: Applying the power rule term-by-term: d/dx[3x⁴ - 2x² + 5x - 1] = 12x³ - 4x + 5

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Physics: if position s(t) = 4.9t², then velocity v(t) = s'(t) = 9.8t, showing free-fall acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)
  • Engineering design: finding the slope of a bridge cable at any point using derivatives to ensure structural integrity under varying loads
  • Financial mathematics: differentiating revenue functions R(x) to find marginal revenue and determine the optimal quantity to maximize profit
