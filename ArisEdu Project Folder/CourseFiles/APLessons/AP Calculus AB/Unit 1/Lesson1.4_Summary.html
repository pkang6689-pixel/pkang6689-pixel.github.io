AP Calculus AB - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Limit Laws and Algebraic Techniques provide systematic methods for evaluating limits. The sum, difference, product, quotient, and power laws allow limits to be computed piece by piece. When direct substitution yields indeterminate forms (0/0), algebraic techniques resolve them: factoring and canceling, rationalizing (multiplying by the conjugate), or simplifying complex fractions. These techniques transform indeterminate expressions into evaluable limits.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Direct substitution: If f is continuous at c, then lim(x→c) f(x) = f(c); the simplest method when it yields a determinate value
  • Indeterminate form 0/0: When direct substitution gives 0/0, the limit may still exist; algebraic manipulation is needed to evaluate it
  • Factoring technique: Factor numerator and denominator, cancel the common factor causing 0/0, then substitute: lim(x→2) (x²-4)/(x-2) = lim(x→2) (x+2) = 4
  • Rationalizing: Multiply by the conjugate to eliminate radicals: multiply (√x - a) by (√x + a)/(√x + a) to create a difference of squares
  • Limit laws: lim[f ± g] = lim f ± lim g; lim[f·g] = lim f · lim g; lim[f/g] = lim f / lim g (if lim g ≠ 0); lim[fⁿ] = (lim f)ⁿ

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Error analysis in numerical methods: understanding limits helps predict how rounding errors accumulate as step sizes approach zero in computer calculations
  • Population models: evaluating limits of rational functions to determine long-term carrying capacity as time approaches infinity
  • Signal processing: limits of sampling functions determine the resolution boundary in digital audio and image processing
