AP Calculus AB - Unit 4 - Lesson 4.7 - Summary

Key Concepts

Rectilinear Motion analyzes objects moving along a straight line using calculus. Given a position function s(t), all motion characteristics are determined: velocity v(t) = s'(t), acceleration a(t) = s''(t), turning points (where v(t) = 0 and changes sign), and intervals of increasing/decreasing position. Particle motion problems require careful attention to signs: positive velocity means moving in the positive direction, and the total distance traveled requires integrating |v(t)|.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Rectilinear motion: Motion along a straight line (one dimension); described completely by position as a function of time s(t)
  • When is the particle at rest?: When v(t) = s'(t) = 0; the particle momentarily stops (may or may not change direction)
  • When does the particle change direction?: When v(t) = 0 AND v(t) changes sign; the particle reverses its direction of travel
  • Position vs. displacement vs. distance: Position: s(t); displacement: s(b)-s(a); distance: ∫|v(t)|dt; all three are different quantities
  • Average velocity vs. average speed: Average velocity = displacement/time = [s(b)-s(a)]/(b-a); average speed = distance/time = [∫|v(t)|dt]/(b-a)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Modeling an elevator: s(t) must be continuous and differentiable, with v(t) bounded for safety and a(t) limited for passenger comfort (smooth ride)
  • Drag racing analysis: measuring position at checkpoints, computing velocity and acceleration to optimize launch and shifting strategy
  • Robotics arm movement: planning smooth one-dimensional trajectories with constraints on velocity and acceleration to prevent mechanical stress
