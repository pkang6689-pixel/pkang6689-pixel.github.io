AP Calculus AB - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Related Rates problems find how the rates of change of two or more related quantities are connected. The method: (1) identify changing variables and given rates, (2) write an equation relating the variables, (3) differentiate implicitly with respect to time t, (4) substitute known values and solve for the unknown rate. Common scenarios include expanding/contracting shapes, Pythagorean relationships, trigonometric relationships, and similar triangles.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Related rates method: Find dy/dt given dx/dt (or vice versa) by differentiating an equation relating x and y with respect to time t
  • Implicit differentiation with time: All variables are functions of time, so d/dt[x²] = 2x·dx/dt, d/dt[xy] = x·dy/dt + y·dx/dt
  • Pythagorean related rates: For a right triangle with changing sides: d/dt[a² + b² = c²] gives 2a·da/dt + 2b·db/dt = 2c·dc/dt
  • Volume/area related rates: d/dt[V = (4/3)πr³] = 4πr²·dr/dt; d/dt[A = πr²] = 2πr·dr/dt
  • Trigonometric related rates: d/dt[tan θ = y/x] gives sec²θ · dθ/dt = (x·dy/dt - y·dx/dt)/x²

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Ladder sliding down a wall: if the base moves away at 2 ft/s, how fast does the top slide down? Classic Pythagorean related rates problem used in construction safety
  • Filling a conical tank with water: dr/dt and dh/dt are related through similar triangles and volume formula V = (1/3)πr²h
  • Air traffic control: two planes approaching an intersection at different speeds—related rates using the Pythagorean theorem to find the rate of distance change
