AP Calculus AB - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.10 - Summary

Key Concepts

Mean Value Theorem: Graphical Interpretation develops visual understanding of MVT. On the graph of f, the MVT guarantees a point where the tangent line is parallel to the secant line connecting (a,f(a)) and (b,f(b)). The slope of the secant line is the average rate of change [f(b)-f(a)]/(b-a). Multiple points on the curve may satisfy MVT (the theorem guarantees at least one). Visualizing MVT helps interpret derivative graphs and connect graphical and analytical representations.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Secant line slope: [f(b)-f(a)]/(b-a); the average rate of change over [a,b]; the slope of the line connecting endpoints
  • Parallel tangent line: MVT guarantees at least one c where the tangent slope f'(c) equals the secant slope [f(b)-f(a)]/(b-a)
  • Multiple MVT points: A function can have more than one point satisfying MVT; each is where the tangent is parallel to the secant
  • Graphical verification: On a graph, draw the secant and visually identify points where the tangent appears to have the same slope
  • Derivative graph connection: The MVT point c corresponds to where the derivative graph f'(x) crosses the horizontal line y = [f(b)-f(a)]/(b-a)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Speed cameras: two cameras measure a car's time between them; if the average speed exceeds the limit, MVT proves the car exceeded the limit at some instant
  • Flight planning: if a plane gains 30,000 feet over 20 minutes, MVT guarantees the climb rate was exactly 1,500 ft/min at some moment during ascent
  • Growing crops: if crop height increases by 12 cm over 6 days, MVT guarantees the growth rate was exactly 2 cm/day at some point
