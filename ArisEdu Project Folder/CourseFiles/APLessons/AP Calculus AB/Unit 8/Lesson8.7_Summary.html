AP Calculus AB - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.7 - Summary

Key Concepts

Fluid Pressure and Force computes the total force exerted by a fluid on a submerged surface. Hydrostatic pressure at depth h: P = ρgh (in Pa or lb/ft²). For a vertical plate, the force is F = ∫₀ᴴ ρg·w(h)·h dh, where w(h) is the width of the plate at depth h. The key insight is that pressure increases with depth, so different parts of the plate experience different pressures. This integration sums the varying force contributions across the entire surface.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Hydrostatic pressure: P = ρgh; pressure at depth h below the surface; ρ = fluid density, g = gravitational acceleration
  • Force on a submerged plate: F = ∫₀ᴴ ρg·w(h)·h dh; integrates pressure × width over the depth range of the plate
  • Horizontal strip method: Divide the plate into thin horizontal strips of width dh; each strip has approximately constant pressure ρgh and area w(h)·dh
  • Coordinate system setup: Choose h measuring depth down from the surface (h = 0 at surface); or set up y-axis and convert
  • Units: Pressure in pascals (Pa = N/m²) or lb/ft²; force in newtons (N) or pounds (lb)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Dam design: engineers compute total hydrostatic force on dam faces to determine required structural strength and materials
  • Submarine hull design: calculating pressure at operating depth determines hull thickness needed to prevent collapse
  • Aquarium design: the total force on viewing windows increases dramatically with depth, requiring thicker glass for deeper tanks
