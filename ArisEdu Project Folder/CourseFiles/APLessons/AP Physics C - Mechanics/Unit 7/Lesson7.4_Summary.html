AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Gravitational Potential Energy in the universal form is U = -GMm/r, where r is the distance between centers of mass. This formula (with U = 0 at r = ∞) replaces the approximate U = mgh valid only near Earth's surface. Energy conservation with U = -GMm/r is essential for orbit analysis: total mechanical energy E = ½mv² - GMm/r determines whether an orbit is bound (E < 0) or unbound (E ≥ 0), and escape velocity follows from setting E = 0.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Gravitational PE (universal): U = -GMm/r; defined as zero at infinite separation; always negative for bound systems
  • Total energy in orbit: E = KE + PE = ½mv² - GMm/r = -GMm/(2r) for circular orbits; negative means bound
  • Escape condition: E ≥ 0; when kinetic energy equals or exceeds |PE|, the object can escape to infinity
  • Binding energy: The energy required to completely separate a system; equals |E_total| for a gravitationally bound system
  • Energy conservation in orbits: ½mv₁² - GMm/r₁ = ½mv₂² - GMm/r₂; used to find speed at any orbital distance without knowing forces

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Calculating the energy required for rocket launches: the difference in gravitational PE between Earth's surface and the desired orbital altitude plus the kinetic energy needed
  • Analyzing asteroid capture: determining whether an approaching asteroid's total energy is negative enough to become bound to a planet's gravitational field
  • Space elevator feasibility studies computing the potential energy change of lifting payload from Earth's surface to geosynchronous altitude versus traditional rocket propulsion
