AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Springs and Wave Motion connect oscillating springs to the fundamental properties of waves. A mass on a spring executing SHM with ω = √(k/m) demonstrates how periodic oscillations at a source generate traveling waves when coupled to a medium. Wave properties—wavelength λ, frequency f, speed v = λf, and amplitude—are described mathematically by y(x,t) = A sin(kx - ωt), linking oscillation parameters to wave propagation.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Mass-spring system: A mass m on a spring with constant k oscillates with ω = √(k/m) and T = 2π√(m/k)
  • Wave equation: y(x,t) = A sin(kx - ωt); describes a sinusoidal traveling wave with wave number k = 2π/λ and angular frequency ω = 2πf
  • Wave speed: v = λf = ω/k; the speed at which the wave pattern propagates through the medium
  • Transverse vs. longitudinal: Transverse: displacement perpendicular to wave direction (e.g., string); Longitudinal: displacement parallel (e.g., sound)
  • Superposition: When two waves overlap, the resultant displacement is the sum of individual displacements; leads to interference and standing waves

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Musical instrument design: string tension and density determine wave speed and thus the fundamental frequency of vibrating strings
  • Ultrasound imaging using the wave equation v = λf to determine how ultrasound wave properties affect resolution and penetration depth in tissue
  • Earthquake engineering analyzing seismic wave propagation (P-waves longitudinal, S-waves transverse) through different geological layers
