AP Physics C - Mechanics - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Centripetal Force applications involve identifying which real force(s) provide the center-directed force for circular motion. In vertical circles, the net centripetal force changes with position because gravity's radial component varies. The critical condition at the top of a vertical loop (minimum speed for maintaining contact) occurs when the normal force equals zero: mg = mv²/r, giving v_min = √(gr). Multiple-force problems require careful free-body analysis at specific positions.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Centripetal force equation: F_c = mv²/r = mrω²; the net radial force required to maintain circular motion at a given radius and speed
  • Vertical circle dynamics: At the top: F_c = mg + N (both inward); at the bottom: F_c = N - mg (normal force must exceed weight)
  • Critical speed: v_min = √(gr); the minimum speed at the top of a vertical loop to maintain contact (when N = 0)
  • Conical pendulum: A mass on a string tracing a horizontal circle; T sinθ provides centripetal force while T cosθ balances gravity
  • Apparent weight: The normal force experienced by an object; differs from true weight during circular motion (heavier at bottom, lighter at top of loop)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Roller coaster engineering ensuring the car speed at the top of a loop exceeds √(gr) so riders maintain contact with their seats
  • Spin cycle in washing machines using centripetal force to push water outward through drum holes for efficient drying
  • Orbital mechanics where gravitational force provides the centripetal force: mg = mv²/r leads to orbital velocity v = √(gr) for low orbits
