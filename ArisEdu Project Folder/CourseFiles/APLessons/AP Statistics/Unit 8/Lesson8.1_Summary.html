AP Statistics - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Chi-Square Test for Independence tests whether two categorical variables are associated in a single population. Expected counts are calculated as E = (row total × column total)/grand total under the assumption of independence. The test statistic χ² = Σ(O-E)²/E with df = (r-1)(c-1) measures overall departure from independence, with large values suggesting the variables are associated.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Chi-square test for independence: Tests H₀: two categorical variables are independent (not associated) in a single population
  • Expected count formula: E = (row total × column total) / grand total; the count expected in each cell if the variables were independent
  • Degrees of freedom: df = (r - 1)(c - 1), where r = number of rows and c = number of columns in the two-way table
  • Conditions: Random sample, all expected counts ≥ 5 (or no more than 20% below 5), and independent observations
  • Interpreting results: A significant result means the variables are associated; examine which cells contribute most to χ² to understand the nature of the association

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Public health research testing whether smoking status (smoker/non-smoker) is independent of lung disease status (yes/no) in a random sample of adults
  • Market research testing whether product preference is associated with age group to determine whether different demographics need different marketing strategies
  • Sociological research examining whether political party affiliation is independent of geographic region using survey data from a national random sample
