AP Statistics - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Chi-Square Test for Homogeneity compares the distribution of a categorical variable across two or more independent populations or groups. The calculation is identical to the independence test: χ² = Σ(O-E)²/E with df = (r-1)(c-1) and E = (row total × column total)/grand total. The distinction is in the study design: independence tests sample from one population and classify by two variables, while homogeneity tests sample separately from multiple populations.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Chi-square test for homogeneity: Tests H₀: the distribution of a categorical variable is the same across two or more populations
  • Homogeneity vs. independence: Same χ² calculation, but homogeneity compares distributions across known groups; independence tests association within one population
  • Study design difference: Independence: one random sample classified by two variables; Homogeneity: separate random samples from each population
  • Expected counts: Calculated the same way as the independence test: E = (row total × column total) / grand total
  • Follow-up analysis: After rejecting H₀, compare individual cell contributions to χ² or construct pairwise comparisons to identify which groups differ

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Comparing the distribution of car color preferences across three countries to determine whether consumer preferences differ internationally
  • Clinical trials comparing the distribution of side effects (none, mild, moderate, severe) across three different drug dosages
  • Education policy comparing the distribution of reading proficiency levels (below, at, above grade level) across different school districts
