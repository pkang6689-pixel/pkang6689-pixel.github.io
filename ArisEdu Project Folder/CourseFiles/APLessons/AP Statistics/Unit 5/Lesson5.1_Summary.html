AP Statistics - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Sampling Distribution of Sample Means describes the probability distribution of the sample mean x̄ across all possible samples of size n from a population. If the population has mean μ and standard deviation σ, then the sampling distribution of x̄ has mean μx̄ = μ and standard deviation σx̄ = σ/√n (the standard error). This predictable behavior of x̄ forms the foundation for confidence intervals and hypothesis tests about population means.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Sampling distribution: The distribution of all possible values of a statistic computed from every possible sample of the same size from a population
  • Standard error of x̄: σ/√n; the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample mean; decreases as sample size increases
  • Unbiased estimator: A statistic whose sampling distribution is centered on the true parameter value; x̄ is an unbiased estimator of μ
  • Variability of a statistic: The spread of the sampling distribution; determined by sample size n (larger n = less variability)
  • Sampling variability: The natural variation of a statistic from sample to sample; different random samples yield different values of x̄

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Airport security using sampling distributions to determine how many bags to inspect (sample size) to achieve a desired precision in estimating the rate of prohibited items
  • Polling organizations reporting margin of error, which depends on the standard error σ/√n, explaining why larger polls are more precise
  • Manufacturing process monitoring using the sampling distribution of x̄ to set control chart limits for detecting when a process mean shifts
