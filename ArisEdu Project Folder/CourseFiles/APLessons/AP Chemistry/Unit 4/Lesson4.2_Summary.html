AP Chemistry - Unit 4 - Lesson 4.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

Balancing Chemical Equations ensures the law of conservation of mass is satisfied: the same number of each type of atom appears on both sides. Equations are balanced by adjusting stoichiometric coefficients (never subscripts). For redox reactions, the half-reaction method balances electron transfer in acidic or basic solutions. Net ionic equations show only the species that participate in the reaction, removing spectator ions.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Balanced equation: A chemical equation with equal numbers of each atom on both sides, satisfying conservation of mass
  • Stoichiometric coefficient: The numbers placed before formulas in a balanced equation indicating the mole ratio of reactants and products
  • Half-reaction method: Balancing redox equations by separating into oxidation and reduction half-reactions and balancing electrons transferred
  • Net ionic equation: An equation showing only the species that change during a reaction; spectator ions are omitted
  • Spectator ion: An ion present in solution that does not participate in the reaction; appears unchanged on both sides of the complete ionic equation

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Airbag deployment: 2NaN₃ → 2Na + 3N₂; precisely balanced stoichiometry ensures the correct amount of N₂ gas inflates the airbag in milliseconds
  • Balancing combustion equations for emissions testing: ensuring complete combustion (sufficient O₂) minimizes toxic CO and particulate emissions from vehicles
  • Stoichiometric calculations in pharmaceutical manufacturing ensuring precise drug composition, maintaining consistent dosage and efficacy
