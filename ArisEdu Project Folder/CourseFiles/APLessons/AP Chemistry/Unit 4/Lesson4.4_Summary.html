AP Chemistry - Unit 4 - Lesson 4.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Percent Yield and Reaction Efficiency quantify how productive a real reaction is compared to the theoretical maximum. Percent yield is always ≤ 100% due to side reactions, incomplete reactions, loss during transfer, or impure reactants. Atom economy measures the fraction of reactant atoms incorporated into the desired product. Green chemistry aims to maximize both percent yield and atom economy while minimizing waste and hazardous byproducts.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Percent yield: (Actual yield / Theoretical yield) × 100%; values < 100% result from side reactions, loss, or incomplete reaction
  • Actual yield: The amount of product actually obtained from a reaction; measured experimentally
  • Atom economy: (Mass of desired product / Total mass of all products) × 100%; measures efficiency of atom utilization in a reaction
  • Green chemistry: The design of chemical products and processes that minimize the use and generation of hazardous substances
  • Side reaction: An unwanted reaction that competes with the desired reaction, consuming reactants and reducing the yield of the target product

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Pharmaceutical synthesis often involving multi-step reactions where overall yield is the product of individual step yields: 5 steps at 80% each = 32.8% overall
  • Green chemistry principles in industry: catalytic hydrogenation of alkenes (100% atom economy) preferred over reactions producing significant waste byproducts
  • Quality control in manufacturing: monitoring percent yield to detect equipment problems, contaminated reagents, or process deviations
