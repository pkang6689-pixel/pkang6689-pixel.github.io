AP Chemistry - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.2 - Summary

Key Concepts

pH and pOH provide logarithmic scales for expressing H⁺ and OH⁻ concentrations. pH = -log[H⁺], pOH = -log[OH⁻], and pH + pOH = 14.00 at 25°C. The autoionization of water (Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴ at 25°C) connects [H⁺] and [OH⁻]. Acidic solutions have pH < 7, neutral = 7, basic > 7. For weak acids/bases, Ka or Kb calculations with ICE tables determine equilibrium pH. The 5% approximation simplifies calculations when [HA]₀/Ka > 400.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • pH: -log[H⁺]; a logarithmic scale where each unit represents a 10-fold change in [H⁺]; pH 3 is 1000× more acidic than pH 6
  • pOH: -log[OH⁻]; related to pH by pH + pOH = 14.00 at 25°C
  • Kw (autoionization constant): Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴ at 25°C; derived from the autoionization of water: 2H₂O ⇌ H₃O⁺ + OH⁻
  • pKa: -log(Ka); lower pKa = stronger acid; provides a convenient logarithmic scale for comparing acid strengths
  • 5% approximation: If x/[initial] < 5%, the approximation [HA] ≈ [HA]₀ is valid; avoids solving the quadratic equation for weak acid/base equilibria

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Blood pH maintenance at 7.35-7.45: even small deviations cause acidosis (<7.35) or alkalosis (>7.45), both life-threatening conditions
  • Soil pH affecting nutrient availability for plants: most crops grow best at pH 6.0-7.0; acid rain lowering soil pH reduces crop productivity
  • Swimming pool chemistry: maintaining pH 7.2-7.8 using acid (HCl) or base (NaHCO₃) additions to optimize chlorine disinfection effectiveness
