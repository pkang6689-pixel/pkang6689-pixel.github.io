AP Chemistry - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Atomic Structure and Subatomic Particles forms the foundation of chemistry. Atoms consist of protons (p⁺, +1 charge, ~1 amu) and neutrons (n⁰, neutral, ~1 amu) in the nucleus, surrounded by electrons (e⁻, -1 charge, ~1/1836 amu) in orbitals. The atomic number (Z) equals the number of protons and defines the element. Mass number (A) = protons + neutrons. Isotopes are atoms of the same element with different neutron counts, affecting atomic mass but not chemical properties.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Atomic number (Z): The number of protons in an atom's nucleus; uniquely identifies the element and determines its chemical properties
  • Isotope: Atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons; same Z but different A (e.g., ¹²C, ¹³C, ¹⁴C)
  • Mass number (A): The total number of protons plus neutrons in the nucleus; A = Z + N
  • Atomic mass unit (amu): Defined as 1/12 the mass of a ¹²C atom; 1 amu = 1.661 × 10⁻²⁴ g
  • Ion: An atom that has gained or lost electrons; cations are positive (lost e⁻), anions are negative (gained e⁻)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Radiocarbon dating using the ¹⁴C isotope to determine the age of organic materials up to ~50,000 years old
  • Medical PET scans using positron-emitting isotopes (¹⁸F-FDG) to image metabolic activity and detect cancers
  • Mass spectrometry separating isotopes by mass-to-charge ratio to determine elemental composition in forensics and pharmaceutical analysis
