AP Chemistry - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Energy and Heat introduces thermodynamic concepts. Energy exists as kinetic energy (motion) and potential energy (position/composition). Heat (q) flows from hot to cold objects until thermal equilibrium. The first law of thermodynamics (ΔE = q + w) states energy is conserved. Exothermic reactions release heat (q < 0, temperature increases), while endothermic reactions absorb heat (q > 0, temperature decreases). Specific heat capacity (c) relates heat transfer to temperature change: q = mcΔT.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Exothermic reaction: A reaction that releases heat to the surroundings; ΔH < 0; products are lower in energy than reactants
  • Endothermic reaction: A reaction that absorbs heat from the surroundings; ΔH > 0; products are higher in energy than reactants
  • Specific heat capacity: The amount of heat needed to raise 1 g of a substance by 1°C; water = 4.18 J/(g·°C)
  • First law of thermodynamics: Energy is conserved: ΔE = q + w (q = heat, w = work); energy cannot be created or destroyed
  • System vs. surroundings: System = the reaction or process being studied; surroundings = everything else; q_system = -q_surroundings

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Hand warmers using exothermic iron oxidation (4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃) to release heat on demand for hours
  • Cold packs using endothermic dissolution of ammonium nitrate (NH₄NO₃) in water to absorb heat and cool injuries
  • Solar water heating systems using water's high specific heat capacity to absorb and store thermal energy efficiently
