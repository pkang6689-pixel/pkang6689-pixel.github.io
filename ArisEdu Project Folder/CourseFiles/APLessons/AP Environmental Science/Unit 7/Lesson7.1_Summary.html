AP Environmental Science - Unit 7 - Lesson 7.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Air Pollution Sources and Types comprise particulate matter, gaseous pollutants (nitrogen oxides, sulfur dioxide, ozone, carbon monoxide), and heavy metals causing respiratory disease, cardiovascular impacts, and premature mortality affecting over 7 million people annually. Major sources include combustion (vehicles, power plants, heating), industrial processes, agriculture, and natural sources (dust, volcanic emissions), with concentrations varying by season, weather, and proximity to sources. Air quality varies dramatically globally with south Asian cities experiencing hazardous pollution during winter months.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Particulate matter: Solid particles and liquid droplets suspended in air, classified by size (PM10, PM2.5) with smaller particles penetrating deeper in lungs
  • Air quality index (AQI): Numerical scale converting pollutant concentrations to public health categories (good, moderate, unhealthy, hazardous)
  • NOx (nitrogen oxides): Gases (NO, NO₂) produced from combustion at high temperatures, contributing to ozone and acid formation
  • Primary pollutant: Pollutant directly emitted to atmosphere (vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions)
  • Secondary pollutant: Pollutant formed in atmosphere through chemical reactions (ozone formed from NOx and volatile organic compounds)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Monitoring air quality using satellite and ground-based measurement networks to issue health warnings and trigger emission reduction policies
  • Implementing vehicle emission standards and catalytic converters to reduce nitrogen oxides and particulate matter from transportation
  • Establishing industrial emission trading systems (cap-and-trade) to reduce NOx and SO₂ from power plants through economic incentives
