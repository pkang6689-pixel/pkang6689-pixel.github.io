AP Environmental Science - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Ecosystem Services and Biomes provide tangible and intangible benefits to humans, including provisioning services (food, water, materials), regulating services (climate regulation, disease control), supporting services (nutrient cycling, photosynthesis), and cultural services (recreation, spiritual value). Biomes are large-scale ecosystems classified by climate and dominant vegetation, each providing distinct services shaped by their physical and biological characteristics. Quantifying and protecting ecosystem services is increasingly central to conservation policy and sustainable development planning.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Ecosystem services: The benefits and goods humans obtain from ecosystems, supporting survival, economic development, and quality of life
  • Biome: A large geographic area with characteristic climate, vegetation, and animal communities adapted to specific environmental conditions
  • Provisioning services: Ecosystem benefits that directly provide resources for human use (food, freshwater, timber, genetic resources)
  • Regulating services: Ecosystem processes that maintain environmental conditions (carbon sequestration, pollination, water purification)
  • Biodiversity: The variety of genes, species, and ecosystems within a region or the entire biosphere

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Valuing wetlands by calculating their ecosystem services (water filtration, flood control, carbon storage) to justify protection over development
  • Implementing payment for ecosystem services (PES) programs where farmers maintain forest cover on agricultural land to preserve carbon sequestration
  • Designing urban green spaces to provide multiple ecosystem services including stormwater management, air quality improvement, and human recreation
