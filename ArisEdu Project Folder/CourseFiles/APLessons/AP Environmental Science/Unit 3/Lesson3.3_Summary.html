AP Environmental Science - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Population Age Structure describes the distribution of individuals across age classes, and fundamentally determines population growth rates, migration potential, and demographic projections. Populations with pyramid-shaped age structures (young-heavy) have high growth potential, while column-shaped structures indicate stable populations and inverted pyramids indicate declining populations. Age structure shapes future population changes independent of current birth and death rates, making it crucial for long-term planning.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Age structure: The distribution of a population among different age classes, often displayed as a population pyramid
  • Population pyramid: A bar graph showing the distribution of males and females across age classes, revealing reproductive potential
  • Reproductive value: The average number of offspring a female is expected to produce for the rest of her life at a given age
  • Survivorship curve: A graph plotting the proportion of individuals surviving to each age class, revealing species-specific mortality patterns
  • Demographic transition: The change from high birth and death rates to low rates accompanying economic development

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Using Kenya's youthful population pyramid to project rapid future population growth and plan educational infrastructure expansion
  • Projecting Japan's shrinking population using an inverted age pyramid to anticipate workforce shortages and retirement system challenges
  • Analyzing tick population age structure to predict disease transmission intensity in coming years and guide Lyme disease control strategies
