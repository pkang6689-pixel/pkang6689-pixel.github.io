AP Environmental Science - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Population Growth Models mathematically describe how populations change over time, ranging from exponential growth under unlimited resources to logistic growth limited by carrying capacity. The exponential model (Nt = N₀e^rt) represents unrealistic unlimited growth, while the logistic model (dN/dt = rN(K-N)/K) incorporates resource limitation and stabilization. Model selection and parameterization directly influence management decisions for harvested species, invasive species control, and endangered species recovery.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Exponential growth: Population increase at a constant rate per capita, producing J-shaped growth curve when resources are unlimited
  • Logistic growth: Population growth limited by carrying capacity, producing S-shaped (sigmoid) growth curve with slow, fast, and plateau phases
  • Intrinsic rate of increase (r): The per capita population growth rate under ideal conditions with unlimited resources
  • Lambda (λ): The finite rate of increase; λ = 1 equals stable population, λ > 1 equals growth, λ < 1 equals decline
  • Doubling time: The period required for a population to double in size under exponential growth, calculated as ln(2)/r

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Predicting invasive zebra mussel population spread in freshwater lakes using exponential models and planning control strategies
  • Managing fish populations using the logistic model to calculate maximum sustainable yield harvest levels from fisheries
  • Projecting human population growth using demographic transition models to plan infrastructure, food production, and resource allocation
