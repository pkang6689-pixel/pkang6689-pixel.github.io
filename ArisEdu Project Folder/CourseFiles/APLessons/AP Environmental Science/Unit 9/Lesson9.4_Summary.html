AP Environmental Science - Unit 9 - Lesson 9.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Resource Depletion describes how humanity is depleting renewable and non-renewable resources faster than they are replenished or regenerated, including fossil fuels, topsoil, freshwater aquifers, fisheries, and forests, creating scarcity and economic vulnerability. Resource depletion reflects overconsumption in wealthy nations (approximately 2.7 global hectares per person on average, but ~8 gha in the United States and other high-consumption nations) and accelerating extraction in developing countries. Meeting demand within planetary boundaries requires reducing consumption, improving efficiency, and transitioning to regenerative resource use.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Ecological footprint: Land area required to support human activity sustainably, including production of resources and absorption of waste
  • Planetary boundaries: Nine sustainability thresholds for earth system processes (climate, biodiversity, nutrient cycles, freshwater, land use, etc.) beyond which risk increases substantially
  • Peak resource: Point of maximum extraction rate followed by decline, already occurring for conventional oil and some mineral resources
  • Material footprint: Mass of material extracted to meet human consumption, greater than direct material input due to supply chain waste
  • Linear economy: Take-make-waste economy extracting resources, producing goods, and disposing waste, unsustainable with current consumption levels

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Accounting for material footprint of goods (mining waste, energy), prompting consumption reduction and circular economy transitions
  • Monitoring freshwater aquifer depletion rates (Ogallala, Arabian, North China Plain) to prevent permanent loss of irrigation capacity
  • Assessing seafood supply depletion through ecosystem models, restricting catch to sustainable levels and allowing stock recovery
