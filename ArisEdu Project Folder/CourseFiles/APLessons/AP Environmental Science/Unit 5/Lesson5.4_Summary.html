AP Environmental Science - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Water Usage and Conservation address freshwater's uneven global distribution with 70% of human withdrawals supporting agriculture, yet 2 billion people face high water stress, creating conflicts between agricultural, municipal, industrial, and environmental water needs. Water scarcity is intensifying due to groundwater depletion, drought, pollution, and population growth, requiring conservation strategies and sustainable management of shared water resources. Climate change is altering precipitation patterns and increasing water stress in many regions.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Water stress: Scarcity of available water relative to demand, measured as annual freshwater withdrawal relative to renewable freshwater supply
  • Irrigation: Artificial application of water to crops, accounting for 70% of freshwater withdrawals and often unsustainable in arid regions
  • Drought: Extended period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to water shortage and environmental degradation
  • Desalination: Process of removing salt from seawater to produce freshwater, energy-intensive and increasingly used in arid regions
  • Water-use efficiency: Ratio of water used productively to total water supplied, improved through drip irrigation and deficit irrigation techniques

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Implementing drip irrigation and soil moisture monitoring in arid regions to reduce agricultural water use by 30–50% while maintaining yields
  • Protecting aquifer recharge zones and restricting groundwater extraction rates to prevent aquifer depletion in regions like the Ogallala Aquifer
  • Developing water-efficient crops and improving distribution systems to reduce agricultural water demand in irrigation-dependent regions
