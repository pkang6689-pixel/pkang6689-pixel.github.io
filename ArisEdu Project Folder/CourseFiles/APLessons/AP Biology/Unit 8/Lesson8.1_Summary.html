AP Biology - Unit 8 - Lesson 8.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Population Ecology studies how populations grow, interact with their environment, and are regulated. Exponential growth (dN/dt = rN) occurs when resources are unlimited, producing J-shaped curves. Logistic growth (dN/dt = rN(K-N)/K) incorporates carrying capacity K and produces S-shaped curves. Populations are characterized by density, age structure, survivorship curves (Type I, II, III), and life history strategies (r-selected vs. K-selected).

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Exponential growth: dN/dt = rN; unlimited population growth producing J-shaped curves; occurs when resources are abundant and competition is minimal
  • Logistic growth: dN/dt = rN(K-N)/K; growth that slows as population approaches carrying capacity K, producing S-shaped (sigmoid) curves
  • Carrying capacity (K): The maximum population size an environment can sustain indefinitely given available resources
  • r-selected species: Species with high reproductive rates, short lifespans, and little parental care (e.g., bacteria, insects); adapted to unstable environments
  • K-selected species: Species with low reproductive rates, long lifespans, and extensive parental care (e.g., elephants, whales); adapted to stable environments

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Human population growth following a roughly exponential pattern from 1 billion (1800) to 8 billion (2022), with demographic transition slowing growth in developed nations
  • Invasive species (zebra mussels, kudzu) exhibiting exponential growth upon introduction to new environments lacking natural predators or competitors
  • Wildlife management using logistic growth models to calculate maximum sustainable yield for harvested populations (fisheries, deer management)
