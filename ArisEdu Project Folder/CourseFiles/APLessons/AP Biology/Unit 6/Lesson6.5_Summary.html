AP Biology - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Biotechnology Applications encompasses the tools and techniques used to manipulate DNA. Restriction enzymes cut DNA at specific sequences, gel electrophoresis separates fragments by size, PCR amplifies specific DNA regions, and recombinant DNA technology combines DNA from different sources. Genomic technologies (DNA sequencing, gene editing with CRISPR-Cas9, and bioinformatics) have revolutionized biology, medicine, agriculture, and forensics.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Restriction enzyme: An endonuclease that cuts DNA at specific recognition sequences (4-8 bp palindromes), producing fragments with blunt or sticky ends
  • Gel electrophoresis: A technique separating DNA, RNA, or protein fragments by size using an electric field; smaller fragments migrate faster through the agarose or polyacrylamide gel
  • PCR (polymerase chain reaction): A technique that amplifies a specific DNA sequence exponentially using thermal cycling, primers, and Taq DNA polymerase; produces millions of copies in hours
  • CRISPR-Cas9: A gene-editing system using guide RNA to direct the Cas9 nuclease to cut DNA at a precise location, enabling precise gene knockout, correction, or insertion
  • Recombinant DNA: DNA molecules formed by combining fragments from different sources, typically inserted into plasmid vectors and expressed in host organisms (bacteria, yeast)

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • CRISPR-Cas9 clinical trials treating sickle cell disease by editing the BCL11A gene in patient stem cells to reactivate fetal hemoglobin production
  • DNA fingerprinting using STR analysis for forensic identification, with Combined DNA Index System (CODIS) databases matching crime scene samples to individuals
  • Golden Rice engineered with β-carotene biosynthesis genes to combat vitamin A deficiency in developing countries, preventing blindness in millions of children
