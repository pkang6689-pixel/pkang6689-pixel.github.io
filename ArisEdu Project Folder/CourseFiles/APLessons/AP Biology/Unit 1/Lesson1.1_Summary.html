AP Biology - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Properties of Water make it essential for life due to its polar covalent bonds and hydrogen bonding. Water's high specific heat stabilizes temperatures, its high heat of vaporization enables evaporative cooling, its cohesion and adhesion enable capillary action and surface tension, and its density anomaly (ice floats) insulates aquatic ecosystems. These properties arise from water's bent molecular geometry and electronegativity difference between O and H.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Hydrogen bond: A weak attraction between a hydrogen atom covalently bonded to an electronegative atom (O, N, F) and another electronegative atom; responsible for water's unique properties
  • Cohesion: Attraction between water molecules due to hydrogen bonding; creates surface tension and enables water transport in plants
  • Adhesion: Attraction between water molecules and other polar surfaces; contributes to capillary action in plant xylem
  • Specific heat: The amount of energy needed to raise 1 g of a substance by 1°C; water's high specific heat (4.18 J/g·°C) moderates temperature changes
  • Hydrophobic interaction: The tendency of nonpolar molecules to aggregate in aqueous solutions to minimize disruption of hydrogen bonding networks

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Evaporative cooling in humans through sweating, utilizing water's high heat of vaporization (2,260 J/g) to dissipate excess body heat
  • Capillary action in plant xylem vessels transporting water against gravity from roots to leaves through cohesion-adhesion-tension
  • Ice insulation of lakes in winter: because ice (density 0.917 g/cm³) floats, aquatic organisms survive below the frozen surface
