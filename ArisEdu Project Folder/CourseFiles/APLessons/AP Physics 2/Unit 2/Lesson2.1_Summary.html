AP Physics 2 - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Electricity studies electric charges, forces, and fields. Coulomb's law gives the electrostatic force between point charges: F = kq₁q₂/r² (attractive for opposite charges, repulsive for like charges). The electric field E = F/q is the force per unit positive test charge. Field lines point away from positive charges and toward negative charges. Superposition: the net electric field from multiple charges is the vector sum of individual fields. Charge is conserved and quantized (integer multiples of e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C).

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Coulomb's law: F = kq₁q₂/r²; k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²; inversely proportional to r²; analogous to gravitational force
  • Electric field: E = F/q = kQ/r² for a point charge; a vector field showing the force a positive test charge would experience at each point
  • Field lines: Visual representation of E: start on + charges, end on - charges; density indicates field strength; never cross
  • Superposition of fields: Net E at a point = vector sum of fields from all individual charges; requires component-wise addition
  • Charge conservation: Total charge in an isolated system remains constant; charges can transfer between objects but cannot be created or destroyed

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Lightning: charge separation in thunderclouds creates enormous electric fields (≈3 × 10⁶ V/m) that ionize air and create conductive channels, equalizing charge via massive current discharge
  • Xerox copiers use electrostatic attraction: a charged drum attracts toner particles to form an image, then heat fuses the toner to paper
  • Electric field therapy (tumor treating fields): alternating electric fields disrupt cancer cell division—a novel application of electrostatics in medicine
