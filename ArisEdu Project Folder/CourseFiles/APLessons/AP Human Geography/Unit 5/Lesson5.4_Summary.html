AP Human Geography - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Intensification and Extensification of Agriculture explores the different strategies farmers use to increase production. This lesson contrasts strategies that increase output per unit of land (intensification) with those that expand the cultivated area (extensification). Understanding these approaches reveals the trade-offs between productivity, sustainability, and environmental impact.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Intensification: Increasing agricultural output per unit of land through inputs like fertilizers, pesticides, irrigation, and technology
  • Extensification: Increasing agricultural output by expanding into new land, often at the expense of forests or grasslands
  • Green Revolution: The global shift to high-yield crop varieties and chemical inputs that dramatically increased production
  • Frontier Agricultural Expansion: Moving agriculture into previously uncultivated lands, particularly in developing regions
  • Agricultural Productivity: The output of crops or livestock per hectare or per worker

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains debates over deforestation, particularly in tropical regions like the Amazon and Southeast Asia
  • Central to understanding food security strategies and their environmental consequences
  • Essential for analyzing agricultural policy in developing vs. developed nations
