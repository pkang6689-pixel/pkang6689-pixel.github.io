AP Human Geography - Unit 5 - Lesson 5.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Agricultural Diffusion and Innovation examines how new crops, technologies, and farming practices spread from one region to another. This lesson traces the historical and contemporary patterns of agricultural innovation, including the global spread of staple crops and modern farming techniques. Understanding diffusion patterns helps explain agricultural change and development disparities worldwide.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Crop Diffusion: The global spread of crop species from their origin centers to other regions
  • Columbian Exchange: The transfer of crops, animals, and ideas between the Americas and Afro-Eurasia after 1492
  • Innovation Adoption: The process by which farmers adopt new crops, varieties, or technologies
  • Agricultural Technology Transfer: The movement of modern farming techniques, equipment, and knowledge to developing regions
  • Hearth and Hierarchical Diffusion: How innovations spread from core regions to peripheral areas through trade networks and influence

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains global food systems and agricultural interdependencies among nations
  • Critical to understanding agricultural development disparities and technology gaps
  • Essential for comprehending food security solutions and agricultural adaptation strategies
