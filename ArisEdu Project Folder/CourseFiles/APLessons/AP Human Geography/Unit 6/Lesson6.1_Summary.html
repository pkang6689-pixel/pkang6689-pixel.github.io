AP Human Geography - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.1 - Summary

Key Concepts

Origin and Development of Cities traces the historical emergence of urbanization and the functions of early cities. This lesson examines why cities developed, the roles they played in early civilizations, and how urbanization patterns have evolved. Understanding city origins reveals how urban centers became centers of power, trade, and culture.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Urbanization: The process by which cities grow and rural areas become more densely settled
  • Site: The physical location and characteristics of a city, including topography and resources
  • Situation: The relative location of a city in relation to other places, trade routes, and geographic features
  • Primary City: The dominant city in a country that is disproportionately large compared to other cities
  • Primate City: A city that is significantly larger than other cities in a country and has undue political and economic influence

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains why certain cities emerged as regional or national centers
  • Critical for understanding historical development patterns and path dependence in city formation
  • Essential for analyzing contemporary megacities and urban competition
