AP Human Geography - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.6 - Summary

Key Concepts

Urban Sustainability and Planning addresses how cities can develop in environmentally sustainable and socially just ways. This lesson investigates green cities, transportation planning, and balancing urban growth with environmental protection. Understanding sustainable urbanism reveals tensions between development, equity, and ecological limits.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Sustainable Cities: Urban areas designed to minimize environmental impact while meeting residents' needs
  • Green Infrastructure: Environmental infrastructure like green spaces, wetlands, and permeable surfaces that manage water and reduce pollution
  • Public Transit: Mass transportation systems designed to efficiently move large numbers of people
  • Urban Agriculture: Food production within cities including community gardens, rooftop farms, and small-scale farming
  • Smart Cities: Cities using technology and data to optimize services, reduce energy, and improve quality of life

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Addresses climate change mitigation strategies and urban resilience to environmental hazards
  • Critical for meeting Sustainable Development Goals and creating equitable, livable cities
  • Essential for analyzing urban innovations and green city initiatives globally
