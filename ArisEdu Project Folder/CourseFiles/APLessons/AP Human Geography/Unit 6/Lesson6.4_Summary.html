AP Human Geography - Unit 6 - Lesson 6.4 - Summary

Key Concepts

Urban Socioeconomic Segregation investigates how cities are stratified socially and economically, creating unequal neighborhoods and communities. This lesson analyzes segregation by class, race, ethnicity, and religion, and its consequences for opportunity and well-being. Understanding segregation patterns reveals systemic inequality and its geographic manifestations.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Residential Segregation: The separation of different racial, ethnic, or socioeconomic groups into distinct neighborhoods
  • Redlining: The discriminatory practice of denying services and investment to particular neighborhoods based on racial or ethnic composition
  • Ghettoization: The concentration of a marginalized group in a particular area due to discrimination or necessity
  • Gentrification: The transformation of working-class neighborhoods through investment, increasing property values and displacement of original residents
  • Environmental Justice: The principle that all people deserve equal protection from environmental hazards regardless of race, class, or geographic location

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains persistent racial and ethnic segregation in American and global cities
  • Critical for understanding injustices in hazard exposure, school quality, and health outcomes
  • Essential for analyzing urban equity, civil rights, and social justice movements
