AP Human Geography - Unit 1 - Lesson 1.3 - Summary

Key Concepts

Spatial Interaction investigates how people, goods, and ideas move across space and the factors that facilitate or hinder this interaction. This lesson examines patterns of movement and communication between places, including trade networks, migration patterns, and cultural diffusion. Understanding spatial interaction explains interconnectedness and inequality in our globalized world.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Distance Decay: The principle that interaction decreases as distance increases between places
  • Accessibility: The ease of reaching one place from another based on transportation networks and distance
  • Connectivity: The degree to which places are linked through networks of trade, communication, and migration
  • Diffusion: The spread of ideas, innovations, or cultural practices across space
  • Supply Chains: Networks of producers, distributors, and consumers connected through global economic systems

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains global economic inequality and development patterns
  • Critical to understanding cultural globalization and local resistance movements
  • Essential for analyzing pandemic spread, trade networks, and digital connectivity
