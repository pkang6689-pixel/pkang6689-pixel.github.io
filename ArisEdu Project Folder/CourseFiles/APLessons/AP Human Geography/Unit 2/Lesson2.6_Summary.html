AP Human Geography - Unit 2 - Lesson 2.6 - Summary

Key Concepts

Environmental Migration examines population movements triggered by environmental degradation, resource scarcity, or natural disasters. This lesson analyzes how climate change, desertification, and sea-level rise create climate migrants and environmental refugees. Understanding environmental migration is increasingly critical as climate change intensifies.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Environmental Refugee: A person displaced by environmental degradation or climate change who cannot or will not return
  • Climate Migrant: A person who relocates due to changes in climate patterns and environmental conditions
  • Sea-Level Rise: The increase in ocean levels due to thermal expansion and melting ice sheets, threatening coastal populations
  • Desertification: The degradation of dryland ecosystems into desert, rendering land unsuitable for agriculture
  • Carrying Capacity: The maximum population size an environment can sustainably support

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains migration crises in the Sahel, Pacific island nations, and drought-stricken regions
  • Critical for understanding climate justice and responsibility for climate-induced displacement
  • Essential for analyzing future migration pressures from climate change
