AP Human Geography - Unit 3 - Lesson 3.5 - Summary

Key Concepts

Ethnic and National Identity examines how ethnic and national identities are constructed geographically and how they motivate political action. This lesson investigates ethnicity as a social construct, nationalist movements, and conflicts arising from competing identities. Understanding identity geography is essential for analyzing nationalism and ethnic conflicts worldwide.

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Essential concepts in this lesson include:

  • Ethnicity: A group's identity based on shared nationality, language, religion, culture, or ancestry
  • Nationalism: The belief that a nation (ethnic or territorial group) should form its own independent state
  • Nation-State: A political unit where the government represents a nation and controls a territory
  • Separatism: The desire or movement for a group to separate from an existing political unit to form their own state
  • Ethnic Cleansing: Forced removal or genocide of a particular ethnic group from a territory

Real-World Applications and Examples

This topic has significant real-world importance:

  • Explains nationalist conflicts in Afghanistan, Sri Lanka, Rwanda, and the Balkans
  • Critical for understanding indigenous peoples' rights and land claims
  • Essential for analyzing political identity movements and state formation
