Lesson 4.4: Fossils & the Fossil Record - Summary

Key Concepts: Fossils & the Fossil Record

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the most common type of fossilization?

Permineralization is the most common fossilization process, occurring when mineral-rich groundwater seeps into pore spaces of bone, shell, or wood, depositing minerals that harden and preserve the original structure.

What is a fossil?

A fossil is any preserved evidence of past life, including body remains (bones, shells, teeth), impressions (molds, casts), and traces of activity (footprints, burrows, coprolites), from any type of organism from bacteria to dinosaurs.

What is a trace fossil?

Trace fossils are indirect evidence of ancient life that record the behavior and activities of organisms rather than preserving their physical remains, including footprints, trackways, burrows, feeding traces, and coprolites (fossilized dung).

What is amber preservation?

Amber preservation occurs when small organisms, typically insects, are trapped in sticky tree resin that hardens over millions of years into amber, preserving the organism in extraordinary three-dimensional detail, sometimes including soft tissues.

What is permineralization?

Permineralization is the most common fossilization process, where groundwater carrying dissolved minerals such as silica, calcite, or pyrite fills the microscopic pore spaces in bones, shells, or wood, hardening and preserving the original structure.

What is a mold fossil?

A mold fossil forms when an organism is buried in sediment that hardens into rock, and then the organism's remains dissolve away, leaving a negative impression (hollow space) in the rock that preserves the shape and surface details of the original organism.

What is a transitional fossil?

Transitional fossils display a mixture of features from both an ancestral group and a derived group, providing physical evidence of evolutionary transitions, such as Tiktaalik, which has both fish-like and amphibian-like features.

What is a coprolite?

Coprolites are fossilized feces (dung) that are classified as trace fossils, and they provide valuable information about an ancient organism's diet, digestive system, and the ecosystem it lived in.

Real-World Applications

Example: A team of geologists in Africa discovers a set of footprints preserved in volcanic ash. The tracks show two individuals walking upright on two legs, and radiometric dating of the ash gives an age of 3.6 million years. What type of fossil is this, and why is it significant?

These are the famous Laetoli footprints, which are trace fossils that provide direct evidence of upright bipedal walking in early hominins (likely Australopithecus afarensis) 3.6 million years ago, demonstrating behavior that no skeleton alone could prove.
