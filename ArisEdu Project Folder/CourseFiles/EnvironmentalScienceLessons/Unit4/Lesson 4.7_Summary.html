Key Concepts: AP Prep: Resource Management Models

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is carrying capacity (K) in population ecology?

Carrying capacity is determined by the availability of limiting resources like food, water, space, and shelter, and it can change over time due to environmental conditions, resource depletion, or ecological disturbances.

What is the logistic growth model?

The logistic growth model modifies exponential growth by incorporating a carrying capacity term, resulting in growth that slows as population density increases and stabilizes near K, reflecting real-world resource limitations.

What is the IPAT equation?

The IPAT equation (I = P x A x T) provides a framework for understanding how population size, per-capita consumption, and the environmental intensity of technology together determine total human environmental impact.

What is a limiting factor in ecology?

Limiting factors such as water, nutrients, space, or predation constrain population growth; the most scarce factor determines the carrying capacity, following Liebig's Law of the Minimum.

What is sustainable yield?

Sustainable yield represents the harvest level that can be maintained indefinitely because it equals the natural regeneration rate, analogous to living off the interest of a bank account without spending the principal.

What is environmental resistance?

Environmental resistance encompasses all biotic and abiotic factors that prevent a population from growing exponentially, including predation, parasitism, disease, competition, and limited food, water, and space.

What is the concept of overshoot in population ecology?

Overshoot occurs when population growth momentum causes numbers to exceed carrying capacity, depleting resources and potentially degrading the environment so severely that the new carrying capacity is lower than the original.

What is the doubling time of a population?

Doubling time is estimated using the Rule of 70: dividing 70 by the annual growth rate percentage gives the approximate years to double; for example, a 2% growth rate means doubling in about 35 years.

What is density-dependent population regulation?

Density-dependent factors like food competition, disease transmission, and predation become stronger as population density rises, providing the negative feedback that produces logistic growth and stabilizes populations near K.

What is the Rule of 70?

The Rule of 70 is a quick estimation tool: 70 divided by the annual percentage growth rate gives the approximate number of years for the quantity to double, useful for populations, investments, or resource consumption.
