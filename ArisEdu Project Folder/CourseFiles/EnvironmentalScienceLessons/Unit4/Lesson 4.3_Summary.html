Lesson 4.3: Soil & Agriculture - Summary

Key Concepts: Soil & Agriculture

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is soil erosion?

Soil erosion occurs when the thin layer of fertile topsoil is carried away by wind or water faster than natural processes can rebuild it, representing a critical threat to agricultural productivity worldwide.

What is desertification?

Desertification transforms once-productive land into arid, desert-like conditions through a combination of human overuse (overgrazing, overcultivation, deforestation) and climate factors, affecting about 25% of Earth's land surface.

What is humus?

Humus is the stable, dark organic component of soil produced by microbial decomposition of dead plant and animal material, and it improves soil structure, water retention, and nutrient availability for plants.

What is soil compaction?

Soil compaction occurs when heavy machinery, livestock, or foot traffic compress soil particles together, destroying the pore spaces needed for water infiltration, root penetration, and gas exchange with the atmosphere.

What is integrated pest management (IPM)?

IPM minimizes pesticide use by combining multiple strategies including biological controls (predator insects), crop rotation, resistant varieties, and monitoring, resorting to targeted chemicals only when pest populations exceed economic thresholds.

What is a soil horizon?

Soil profiles consist of distinct horizontal layers called horizons (O, A, B, C, R from top to bottom), each with unique color, texture, and composition reflecting different stages of weathering and biological activity.

What is contour plowing?

Contour plowing follows the natural curves of the terrain, creating small ridges perpendicular to the slope that act as barriers to slow water flow, reducing erosion by up to 50% compared to plowing downhill.

What is agroforestry?

Agroforestry combines agriculture with tree cultivation, where trees provide shade, wind protection, nitrogen fixation, and habitat for beneficial organisms while farmers grow crops or raise animals on the same land.

What is bioaccumulation of pesticides?

Bioaccumulation occurs when organisms absorb pesticides from food, water, or soil faster than they can metabolize or excrete them, causing concentrations to increase in body tissues over the organism's lifetime.

What is organic farming?

Organic farming prohibits synthetic pesticides and fertilizers, instead using compost, cover crops, crop rotation, and biological pest control to maintain soil health and reduce environmental contamination.

A large-scale farm in Australia experiences a severe dust storm that strips 3 inches of topsoil from its fields. Given that it takes about 500 years to form 1 inch of topsoil, what is the significance of this event?

Losing 3 inches of topsoil that took approximately 1,500 years to form demonstrates why soil erosion is such a critical environmental issue: it destroys in hours what nature takes centuries to build.

A school garden project in an urban neighborhood discovers that the soil on their lot is contaminated with lead from old paint and gasoline. What is the safest approach to still grow food at this site?

Raised beds with imported clean soil and a barrier layer prevent plant roots from reaching lead-contaminated ground, allowing safe food production while the underlying contamination is managed through proper remediation.

Outliers and Resistant Measures

GM crop debates highlight real trade-offs: pest-resistant varieties can reduce chemical pesticide use, but concerns exist about effects on beneficial insects, gene transfer to wild relatives, and long-term ecological consequences.

IPM addresses pesticide resistance by diversifying pest control methods including biological controls, habitat management, and crop rotation, reducing reliance on any single chemical that pests can evolve resistance to.

Real-World Applications

Example: Healthy soil is composed of approximately what percentage of mineral particles, organic matter, water, and air?

Ideal soil contains roughly 45% mineral particles, 5% organic matter (humus), 25% water, and 25% air in its pore spaces, providing the right balance for plant growth and microbial activity.
