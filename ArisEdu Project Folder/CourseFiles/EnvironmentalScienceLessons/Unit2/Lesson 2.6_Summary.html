Key Definitions

What is the primary cause of biodiversity loss worldwide?

Habitat destruction and fragmentation is the leading driver of biodiversity loss globally. When natural habitats are cleared for agriculture, development, or resource extraction, species lose the space and resources they need to survive and reproduce.

What is species richness?

Species richness is simply the count of different species present in a defined area, without considering how many individuals of each species exist. It is one of the simplest measures of biodiversity and is often used alongside species evenness to characterize community diversity.

What is an endemic species?

An endemic species is one that is native to and found only in a particular geographic region. Endemic species are especially vulnerable to extinction because their entire population is confined to a limited area, making them susceptible to localized threats.

What is a keystone species?

A keystone species has an outsized impact on community structure and function relative to its population size. Removal of a keystone species, such as sea otters in kelp forest ecosystems, can trigger cascading changes that fundamentally alter the entire community.

What is habitat fragmentation?

Habitat fragmentation occurs when large, continuous habitats are divided into smaller, disconnected patches by roads, agriculture, or development. Fragmentation reduces habitat area, increases edge effects, isolates populations, and limits gene flow between them.

What is ex situ conservation?

Ex situ conservation involves maintaining and breeding species outside their natural habitat in facilities like zoos, aquariums, botanical gardens, and seed banks. This approach serves as an insurance policy against extinction and can support reintroduction programs when wild habitats are restored.

What is genetic diversity?

Genetic diversity refers to the total variety of genes, alleles, and genotypes within a species or population. High genetic diversity enhances a population's ability to adapt to changing environmental conditions, resist diseases, and maintain long-term reproductive fitness.

What is an indicator species?

An indicator species is an organism whose presence, absence, or condition reflects the overall health of an ecosystem. For example, amphibians are often used as indicator species because their permeable skin makes them highly sensitive to water and air quality changes.

What is the edge effect in ecology?

The edge effect describes the altered environmental conditions and species composition found at the transition zone between two different habitats. Habitat edges often experience increased light, wind, temperature fluctuations, and invasion by generalist or invasive species.

What is a wildlife corridor?

A wildlife corridor is a strip of natural habitat that connects two or more larger habitat patches, allowing animals to move safely between them. Corridors help maintain genetic diversity, support migration patterns, and enable species to access resources across fragmented landscapes.

Real-World Applications

Example: What percentage of Earth's species are scientists estimated to have formally identified and described?

Scientists estimate that only about 10-20% of all species on Earth have been formally described. Millions of species, particularly insects, fungi, and deep-sea organisms, remain undiscovered, making conservation prioritization challenging.

Example: A marine biologist discovers that removing sea otters from a coastal kelp forest leads to a population explosion of sea urchins, which then overgraze and destroy the kelp beds. What does this example best illustrate about biodiversity conservation?

This is a classic example of a trophic cascade triggered by removing a keystone species. Sea otters control sea urchin populations, and without this predation, urchins overgraze kelp, transforming diverse kelp forests into barren urchin barrens.

Example: An island nation reports that an introduced rat species has driven three endemic bird species to extinction. Wildlife managers are now considering eradicating the rats. Which conservation challenge does this scenario primarily address?

Invasive species are one of the leading causes of extinction, particularly on islands where endemic species evolved without exposure to mainland predators. Invasive rat eradication programs on islands have successfully allowed native species to recover in many documented cases.
