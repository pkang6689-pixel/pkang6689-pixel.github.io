Key Concepts: Community Interactions (Competition, Symbiosis)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is symbiosis?

Symbiosis literally means 'living together' and describes any close, prolonged interaction between two different species. The three main types are mutualism, commensalism, and parasitism.

What is an ecological niche?

An ecological niche includes everything about how a species lives: what it eats, when it is active, where it lives, how it reproduces, and all its interactions with other species and the environment.

What is the competitive exclusion principle?

The competitive exclusion principle states that complete competitors cannot coexist. If two species occupy exactly the same niche, one will eventually exclude the other through superior resource use.

What is resource partitioning?

Resource partitioning is the evolutionary process by which competing species evolve to use different portions of a resource to reduce competition. It allows multiple similar species to coexist.

What is mutualism?

Mutualism is a +/+ symbiotic relationship where both partners benefit. Classic examples include mycorrhizal fungi helping plant roots absorb nutrients in exchange for sugars from the plant.

What is parasitism?

Parasitism is a +/- relationship where the parasite gains nutrition or shelter from the host while causing harm. Unlike predators, parasites typically do not kill their hosts immediately.

What is commensalism?

Commensalism is a +/0 relationship where one species benefits while the other is unaffected. Examples include birds nesting in trees or remora fish attaching to sharks for transportation.

What is coevolution?

Coevolution is reciprocal evolutionary change where interacting species drive each other's adaptation. Predator-prey arms races and pollinator-plant specialization are classic examples.

What is character displacement?

Character displacement occurs when similar species in the same area evolve differences in morphology or behavior (such as beak size) to reduce competition for the same resources.

What is amensalism?

Amensalism is a -/0 interaction where one species is negatively affected while the other is not. For example, a large tree shading out small plants beneath it without being affected by them.

Outliers and Resistant Measures

The oxpecker-rhino relationship is mutualism (both benefit), while the egret-herbivore relationship is commensalism (egrets benefit from insects stirred up, but herbivores are unaffected).
