Key Concepts: Fossil Fuels

Overview

Key Definitions

What is a fossil fuel?

Fossil fuels are energy-rich hydrocarbons originating from buried remains of prehistoric plants and marine organisms, transformed by geological heat and pressure over tens to hundreds of millions of years.

What is acid mine drainage?

Acid mine drainage occurs when sulfur-bearing minerals exposed by mining oxidize to form sulfuric acid, which leaches toxic heavy metals from rock and contaminates surrounding waterways for decades.

What is methane and why is it significant as a greenhouse gas?

Methane is a potent greenhouse gas with approximately 80 times the warming potential of CO2 over 20 years, and significant quantities leak from natural gas wells, pipelines, and coal mines.

What is carbon capture and storage (CCS)?

CCS captures CO2 at the point of emission and injects it into deep geological formations for permanent storage, though it remains expensive and not yet deployed at the scale needed for significant emission reduction.

What is EROI (Energy Return on Investment)?

EROI measures net energy: conventional oil has an EROI of about 15:1, while tar sands may be as low as 3:1, meaning increasingly difficult-to-access fossil fuels yield less net energy per unit invested.

What is petroleum?

Petroleum, or crude oil, is a complex mixture of hundreds of different hydrocarbons found in sedimentary rock formations, processed in refineries into transportation fuels, heating oil, and thousands of petrochemical products.

What is the greenhouse effect?

The greenhouse effect is a natural process where gases like CO2, methane, and water vapor absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, but human emissions of these gases are intensifying the effect and causing global warming.

What is a carbon tax?

A carbon tax charges fossil fuel producers or users based on the CO2 their fuel generates, internalizing the external cost of climate change and creating economic incentives for energy efficiency and renewable energy.

What is strip mining?

Strip mining removes vegetation, soil, and rock in strips to access shallow coal or mineral seams, causing severe land disturbance, habitat destruction, and water contamination if not properly reclaimed.

What is cogeneration (combined heat and power)?

Cogeneration captures waste heat from power generation for building heating or industrial processes, raising overall energy efficiency from roughly 33% to 60-80% and reducing total fuel consumption.

Real-World Applications

Example: Fossil fuels currently provide approximately what percentage of global energy?

Despite renewable energy growth, fossil fuels still supply approximately 80% of global primary energy, highlighting the enormous scale of the transition needed to decarbonize the world energy system.
