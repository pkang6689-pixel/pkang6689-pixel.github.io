Key Definitions

What is the ecological footprint?

The ecological footprint measures how much biologically productive land and water is needed to produce the resources a person or population consumes and to absorb the waste they generate.

What is the tragedy of the commons?

The tragedy of the commons is an economic concept describing how shared resources are overexploited when each user acts in self-interest, ultimately degrading the resource for everyone.

What is an ecosystem service?

Ecosystem services are the benefits humans derive from natural ecosystems, including provisioning (food, water), regulating (climate, flood control), supporting (nutrient cycling), and cultural (recreation) services.

What is sustainable development?

Sustainable development integrates economic growth, environmental protection, and social equity so that present needs are met while preserving resources and opportunities for future generations.

What is a carbon footprint?

A carbon footprint quantifies the total greenhouse gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by an individual, organization, event, or product, usually expressed as tonnes of CO2 equivalent.

What is a circular economy?

A circular economy is an alternative to the linear take-make-dispose model, designed so that products, materials, and resources are maintained at their highest value for as long as possible.

What is greenwashing?

Greenwashing is a deceptive marketing practice where companies make unsubstantiated or misleading claims about their environmental practices to appeal to eco-conscious consumers without making real changes.

What is the precautionary principle?

The precautionary principle states that when an activity raises threats to the environment or human health, preventive action should be taken even if some cause-and-effect relationships are not fully established.

What is biocapacity?

Biocapacity measures the ability of ecosystems to produce useful biological materials and absorb waste, expressed in global hectares. When ecological footprint exceeds biocapacity, resources are being depleted.
