Lesson 2.7: Applications of Linear & Quadratic Models - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications of Linear & Quadratic Models

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A rental company: profit P(x) = −2x² + 80x − 600 per unit rented. What is the break-even number of units?

−2x²+80x−600=0, x²−40x+300=0. x=(40±√(1600−1200))/2=(40±20)/2. x=10 or x=30.

Real-World Applications

Example: Average rate of change of f(x) = x² from x = 1 to x = 3:

Average rate of change = [f(3)−f(1)]/(3−1) = (9−1)/2 = 4.

Example: A phone battery drains linearly from 100% to 0% in 10 hours. What percentage remains after 3.5 hours?

B(t) = 100 − 10t. B(3.5) = 100 − 35 = 65%.
