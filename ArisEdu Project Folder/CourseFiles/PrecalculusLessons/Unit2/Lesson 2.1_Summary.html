Lesson 2.1: Linear Equations & Graphs - Summary

Key Concepts: Linear Equations & Graphs

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A phone plan costs $25/month plus $0.10 per text. If a student sends 300 texts, what is the monthly bill?

C = 25 + 0.10(300) = 25 + 30 = $55.

A car's value depreciates linearly from $20,000 to $8,000 over 6 years. What is the annual depreciation rate?

Depreciation = (20000 - 8000)/6 = 12000/6 = $2,000 per year.

A pool is being filled at a constant rate. It has 50 gallons at t = 0 and 200 gallons at t = 5 hours. What is the linear equation?

Rate = (200-50)/5 = 30 gal/hr. Starting at 50: V = 30t + 50.

Real-World Applications

Example: A bakery sells cupcakes for $3 each. Fixed daily costs are $60. How many cupcakes must be sold to earn $150 in revenue?

Revenue = 3x = 150, so x = 50 cupcakes.

Example: A salesperson earns $500/week plus $20 per sale. She needs $900 this week. How many sales are required?

500 + 20s = 900, so 20s = 400, s = 20 sales.
