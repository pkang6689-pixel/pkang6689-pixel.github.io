Lesson 2.2: Slope, Intercepts, and Parallel/Perpendicular Lines - Summary

Key Concepts: Slope, Intercepts, and Parallel/Perpendicular Lines

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A road rises 150 feet over a horizontal distance of 3,000 feet. What is the grade (slope) of the road?

Slope = rise/run = 150/3000 = 0.05 = 5%.

A wheelchair ramp must have a slope no steeper than 1/12. If the ramp rises 2 feet, what is the minimum horizontal length?

Slope = 2/x = 1/12, so x = 24 feet minimum.

Two streets are parallel with slopes of 3/4. A new street must be perpendicular to them. What is its slope?

Perpendicular slope is the negative reciprocal: −4/3.

A cable car rises from elevation 2,000 ft to 3,500 ft over a horizontal distance of 6,000 ft. What is the slope?

Slope = (3500 − 2000)/6000 = 1500/6000 = 1/4.

An architect designs a roof with slope 5/12. A drain pipe must run perpendicular to the roof line. What is the pipe's slope?

Perpendicular slope = negative reciprocal of 5/12 = −12/5.

A stock price was $40 on day 1 and $55 on day 31. What is the average rate of change per day?

Rate = (55 − 40)/(31 − 1) = 15/30 = $0.50 per day.

Train track A has equation y = 2x + 5. Track B runs parallel through point (3, 1). What is Track B's equation?

Parallel means same slope m = 2. Using point (3,1): 1 = 2(3) + b, b = −5. So y = 2x − 5.

A hiking trail drops 400 feet in elevation over 2 miles (10,560 ft). What is the trail's slope?

Slope = −400/10560 = −1/26.4 (negative because it drops).

A water pipe runs along y = −3x + 10. A new pipe must cross it at a right angle at x = 2. What is the new pipe's equation?

At x=2: y = −6+10 = 4. Perpendicular slope = 1/3. y − 4 = (1/3)(x − 2), so y = (1/3)x + 10/3.

Real-World Applications

Example: A stock price was $40 on day 1 and $55 on day 31. What is the average rate of change per day?

Rate = (55 − 40)/(31 − 1) = 15/30 = $0.50 per day.
