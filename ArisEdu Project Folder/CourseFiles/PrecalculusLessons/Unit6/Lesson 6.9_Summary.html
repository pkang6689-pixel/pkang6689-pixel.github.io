Lesson 6.9: Case Studies in Navigation - Summary

Key Concepts: Case Studies in Navigation

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

Using the Law of Sines, if a/sin A = 10, and sin B = 0.5, what is b?

b/sin B = 10, so b = 10 × sin B = 10 × 0.5 = 5.

By the Law of Cosines, c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos C. If a = 3, b = 4, C = 90°, what is c?

c² = 9 + 16 − 2(3)(4)cos 90° = 25 − 0 = 25, so c = 5 (Pythagorean triple).

A ship sails 10 km due north, then 10 km due east. What is the straight-line distance from the starting point?

d = √(10² + 10²) = √200 = 10√2 km.

What is the bearing S 25° W as a standard compass bearing (clockwise from north)?

S 25° W = 180° + 25° = 205° measured clockwise from north.

What is a 'bearing' in navigation?

A bearing is a direction expressed as an angle measured clockwise from north (000° to 360°).

What is the 'Law of Sines'?

The Law of Sines states that in any triangle, the ratio of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is constant.

What is the 'Law of Cosines'?

The Law of Cosines relates the sides and an angle of any triangle; it reduces to the Pythagorean theorem when C = 90°.

What is the 'angle of elevation'?

The angle of elevation is measured from horizontal upward to the line of sight to a higher object.

What is the 'angle of depression'?

The angle of depression is measured from horizontal downward to the line of sight to a lower object.

What is a 'heading' in aviation?

A heading is the compass direction (0°-360°) that the aircraft's nose points.

A surveyor stands at point C and measures angles to points A and B. Angle ACB = 40°, CA = 100 m, CB = 80 m. By the Law of Cosines, what is AB?

AB² = 100² + 80² − 2(100)(80)cos 40° = 10000 + 6400 − 16000(0.766) ≈ 16400 − 12256 = 4144. AB ≈ 64.3 m.

A plane flies 250 km on bearing 150° then turns and flies 300 km on bearing 060°. What is the included angle between the two flight legs?

At the turn point, the angle between continuing on 150° and turning to 060° = 180° − 150° + 060° = 90° (the external and internal bearing angles give 90°).
