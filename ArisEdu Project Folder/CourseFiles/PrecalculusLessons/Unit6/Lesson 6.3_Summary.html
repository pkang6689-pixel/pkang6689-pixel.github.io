Lesson 6.3: Unit Circle & Special Angles - Summary

Key Concepts: Unit Circle & Special Angles

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is sin(π/6)?

On the unit circle, sin(30°) = sin(π/6) = 1/2.

What is cos(π/4)?

cos(45°) = cos(π/4) = √2/2.

What is tan(π/3)?

tan(60°) = sin(60°)/cos(60°) = (√3/2)/(1/2) = √3.

What is cos(π)?

The point on the unit circle at π (180°) is (−1, 0), so cos(π) = −1.

What is sin(3π/2)?

At 3π/2 (270°), the point is (0, −1), so sin(3π/2) = −1.

What is cos(2π/3)?

2π/3 is in Q II with reference angle π/3. cos is negative in Q II, so cos(2π/3) = −1/2.

What is sin(5π/4)?

5π/4 is in Q III with reference angle π/4. sin is negative in Q III, so sin(5π/4) = −√2/2.

What is tan(π/4)?

tan(π/4) = sin(π/4)/cos(π/4) = (√2/2)/(√2/2) = 1.

What is cos(7π/6)?

7π/6 is in Q III with reference angle π/6. cos is negative in Q III, so cos(7π/6) = −√3/2.

What is the 'unit circle'?

The unit circle has radius 1 and is centered at the origin; a point at angle θ has coordinates (cos θ, sin θ).

What is a 'reference angle'?

A reference angle is the acute angle formed between the terminal side of the angle and the nearest part of the x-axis.

What is the 'ASTC' sign pattern for cosine across the four quadrants?

Cosine (the x-coordinate) is positive in Q I and Q IV and negative in Q II and Q III.

What is the meaning of 'ordered pair' on the unit circle at angle θ?

On the unit circle, the x-coordinate is cos θ and the y-coordinate is sin θ.

A clock shows 2:00. If the hour hand's position corresponds to an angle on the unit circle measured from 12 o'clock (the positive y-axis), what is the equivalent standard-position angle and the cosine of that angle?

At 2:00 the hour hand is at 60° clockwise from 12, which is 90° − 60° = 30° from the positive x-axis, so cos 30° = √3/2.

An architect designs a ramp that rises at an angle whose sine equals 1/2. Using the unit circle, what is this angle?

sin θ = 1/2 at θ = 30° (π/6) from the unit circle special values.

A boat sails on a heading that makes an angle of 5π/3 with the positive x-axis. Using the unit circle, what is sin(5π/3)?

5π/3 is in Q IV with reference angle π/3. sin is negative in Q IV, so sin(5π/3) = −√3/2.

A physics student needs the exact value of cos(3π/4) for a force-component calculation. What is it?

3π/4 is in Q II with reference angle π/4. cos is negative in Q II, so cos(3π/4) = −√2/2.

A Ferris wheel seat starts at the 3 o'clock position (0 radians) and rotates to 2π/3. What is the y-coordinate (height component relative to center) at that point?

The y-coordinate is sin(2π/3). Reference angle is π/3, and sin is positive in Q II, so sin(2π/3) = √3/2.

A drone flies in a circular pattern. At an angle of 11π/6 from the starting direction, what is the cosine of that angle, representing the east-west component of its position?

11π/6 is in Q IV with reference angle π/6. cos is positive in Q IV, so cos(11π/6) = √3/2.

An engineer calculates the horizontal displacement using cos(4π/3). What is the exact value?

4π/3 is in Q III with reference angle π/3. cos is negative in Q III, so cos(4π/3) = −1/2.

A light beam hits a surface at an angle of 7π/4 from the horizontal. What is tan(7π/4)?

7π/4 is in Q IV with reference angle π/4. tan is negative in Q IV, so tan(7π/4) = −1.

A carnival game wheel is divided into equal sections. A pointer stops at 240° from the starting mark. Using the unit circle, what is sin(240°)?

240° is in Q III with reference angle 60°. sin is negative in Q III, so sin(240°) = −√3/2.
