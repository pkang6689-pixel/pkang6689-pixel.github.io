Lesson 6.1: Angles in Degrees & Radians - Summary

Key Concepts: Angles in Degrees & Radians

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a radian?

A radian is defined as the central angle for which the intercepted arc length equals the radius.

What is the 'terminal side' of an angle?

The terminal side is the ray that has been rotated from the initial side; its position determines the measure of the angle.

What is the conversion factor from degrees to radians?

To convert degrees to radians, multiply the degree measure by π/180.

What is a 'quadrantal angle'?

A quadrantal angle has its terminal side on the x-axis or y-axis (e.g., 0°, 90°, 180°, 270°).

What is meant by the 'degree' as a unit of angle measure?

One degree is defined as 1/360 of a full revolution.

A skateboard ramp rises at an angle of π/6 radians above the horizontal. What is this angle in degrees?

(π/6)(180/π) = 30°.

A surveyor measures a turn of −150° while mapping a trail. What is this angle in radians?

−150 × π/180 = −5π/6 radians. The negative sign indicates a clockwise rotation.

A compass app shows a hiker has turned 7π/4 radians clockwise from north. What is the equivalent positive angle in degrees, measured counter-clockwise from north?

7π/4 radians clockwise = 315° clockwise. The equivalent counter-clockwise angle is 360° − 315° = 45°.
