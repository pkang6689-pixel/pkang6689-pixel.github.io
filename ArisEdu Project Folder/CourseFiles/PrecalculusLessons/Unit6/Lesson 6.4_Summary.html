Lesson 6.4: Sine, Cosine, Tangent Definitions - Summary

Key Concepts: Sine, Cosine, Tangent Definitions

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

In a right triangle with opposite = 3, hypotenuse = 5, what is cos θ?

Adjacent = √(25 − 9) = 4. cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse = 4/5.

What is csc θ if sin θ = 3/5?

csc θ = 1/sin θ = 1/(3/5) = 5/3.

If tan θ = 7/24, and the triangle is a right triangle, what is sin θ?

Hypotenuse = √(7² + 24²) = √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25. sin θ = 7/25.

What is sec θ if cos θ = 5/13?

sec θ = 1/cos θ = 1/(5/13) = 13/5.

If sin θ = 0.6 and cos θ = 0.8, what is tan θ?

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = 0.6/0.8 = 0.75.

What is cot θ if tan θ = 4/3?

cot θ = 1/tan θ = 3/4.

In a right triangle, if the adjacent side is 8 and the hypotenuse is 10, what is tan θ?

Opposite = √(100 − 64) = 6. tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 6/8 = 3/4.

What is sin(90°)?

sin(90°) = 1, as the point on the unit circle at 90° is (0, 1).

What is the 'Pythagorean identity' in trigonometry?

The fundamental Pythagorean identity states that sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 for all angles θ.

What is the 'cosecant' function?

Cosecant is the reciprocal of sine: csc θ = 1/sin θ = hypotenuse/opposite.

What is the 'opposite side' in a right triangle relative to an angle θ?

The opposite side is the side directly across from angle θ and does not form either ray of that angle.

What is the 'hypotenuse' of a right triangle?

The hypotenuse is the longest side, always opposite the 90° angle.

A 13-ft ladder leans against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 5 ft from the wall. What is sin θ, where θ is the angle between the ladder and the ground?

Height = √(13² − 5²) = 12. sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 12/13.

A ramp rises 6 ft over a horizontal distance of 8 ft. What is the tangent of the ramp's angle of incline?

tan θ = opposite/adjacent = rise/run = 6/8 = 3/4.

A security camera is mounted 12 ft high on a wall. The camera tilts down at angle θ to view a point on the floor 9 ft from the wall. What is sec θ?

Hypotenuse = √(12² + 9²) = √(225) = 15. cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse = 12/15 = 4/5. sec θ = 5/4.

An electrician needs to run cable from the top of a 15-ft pole to a junction box on the ground 20 ft from the base. What is csc θ, where θ is the angle of elevation from the junction box?

Hypotenuse = √(15² + 20²) = 25. sin θ = 15/25 = 3/5. csc θ = 5/3.

A ship sails 10 km due east, then turns and sails 10 km due north. What is the sine of the angle between the ship's final position (from the starting point) and the eastward direction?

The displacement makes a 45° angle with the east direction (since both legs are 10 km). sin 45° = √2/2.
