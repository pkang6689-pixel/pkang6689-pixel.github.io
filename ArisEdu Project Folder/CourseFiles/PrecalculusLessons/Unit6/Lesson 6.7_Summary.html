Lesson 6.7: Applications in Physics & Engineering - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Physics & Engineering

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

In projectile motion, the horizontal displacement is x = v₀ cos θ · t. If v₀ = 50 m/s, θ = 60°, and t = 2 s, what is x?

x = 50 × cos 60° × 2 = 50 × 0.5 × 2 = 50 m.

An AC voltage is V(t) = 120 sin(120πt). What is the peak voltage?

The peak voltage is the amplitude V₀ = 120 V.

A wave is described by y = 3 sin(2x − 4t). What is the amplitude?

The amplitude is the coefficient in front of sine, which is 3.

Resolve a 100 N force at 30° from the horizontal. What is the horizontal component?

F_x = F cos θ = 100 cos 30° = 100(√3/2) = 50√3 N.

For x(t) = 5 cos(3t), what is the angular frequency ω?

In x(t) = A cos(ωt), the angular frequency ω = 3 rad/s.

A projectile is launched at v₀ = 40 m/s at angle 45°. What is the vertical component of velocity?

v_y = v₀ sin 45° = 40(√2/2) = 20√2 m/s.

What is the wave number k if the wavelength λ = π m?

k = 2π/λ = 2π/π = 2.

An AC circuit has V(t) = 170 sin(2π · 60 · t). What is the frequency?

V(t) = V₀ sin(2πft), so f = 60 Hz.

What is 'simple harmonic motion'?

Simple harmonic motion (SHM) is oscillation where the restoring force is proportional to displacement, resulting in sinusoidal motion.

What is the 'wavelength' of a wave?

Wavelength λ is the spatial period -- the distance for one complete wave cycle.

What is the 'wave number' k?

The wave number k = 2π/λ counts how many radians of phase fit in one unit of distance.

What is the 'angle of elevation' in projectile motion?

The angle of elevation (launch angle) θ is measured from the horizontal to the initial velocity vector.

What is the 'frequency' (f) of an AC voltage signal?

Frequency f (in Hz) is the number of complete oscillation cycles per second; f = 1/T = ω/(2π).

A mass on a spring oscillates with x(t) = 0.1 cos(10t). What is the period of oscillation?

T = 2π/ω = 2π/10 = π/5 seconds.

A projectile is launched at 30° with v₀ = 80 m/s. What is the horizontal range if the ground is level? (Use g = 10 m/s²)

Range = v₀² sin(2θ)/g = 6400 sin(60°)/10 = 640(√3/2) = 320√3 m.

An engineer resolves a 200 N force at 45° to find the vertical component. What is it?

F_y = F sin 45° = 200(√2/2) = 100√2 N.

A sonar wave is described by y = 5 sin(πx/4 − 6πt). What is the wavelength?

k = π/4, so λ = 2π/k = 2π/(π/4) = 8 m.

A pendulum bob completes 5 full swings in 10 seconds. What is the angular frequency ω?

T = 10/5 = 2 s. ω = 2π/T = 2π/2 = π rad/s.

A crane cable applies a force of 500 N at 60° to the horizontal. What is the horizontal component used to drag a load?

F_x = 500 cos 60° = 500 × 0.5 = 250 N.

US household AC voltage is V(t) = 170 sin(120πt). What is the RMS voltage? (V_rms = V₀/√2)

V_rms = 170/√2 ≈ 120.2 V, which is the standard US household voltage.

A ball is thrown at 20 m/s at an angle of 30° above the horizontal. What is the maximum height reached? (g = 10 m/s²)

v_y = 20 sin 30° = 10 m/s. Max height = v_y²/(2g) = 100/20 = 5 m.

A spring-mass system has x(t) = 4 cos(6t + π/4). What is the displacement at t = 0?

x(0) = 4 cos(π/4) = 4(√2/2) = 2√2.
