Lesson 6.5: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (amplitude, period, phase shift) - Summary

Key Concepts: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (amplitude, period, phase shift)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the amplitude of y = 3 sin x?

Amplitude = |A| = |3| = 3.

What is the period of y = sin(2x)?

Period = 2π/|B| = 2π/2 = π.

What is the phase shift of y = sin(x − π/4)?

Phase shift = C/B = (π/4)/1 = π/4 to the right.

What is the vertical shift (midline) of y = cos x + 5?

The vertical shift D = 5, so the midline is y = 5.

What is the period of y = tan x?

The standard tangent function has period π.

What is the amplitude of y = −2 cos(3x)?

Amplitude = |A| = |−2| = 2. The negative sign reflects the graph but does not change amplitude.

What is the period of y = cos(x/2)?

Period = 2π/|B| = 2π/(1/2) = 4π.

What is the maximum value of y = 4 sin x − 1?

Max = A + D = 4 + (−1) = 3.

What is 'amplitude' of a sinusoidal function?

Amplitude is |A|, the vertical distance from the midline to a peak or trough.

What is 'phase shift'?

Phase shift = C/B, the horizontal displacement of the standard sine or cosine graph.

What is a 'sinusoidal function'?

Sinusoidal functions have the shape of a sine or cosine wave, described by y = A sin(Bx − C) + D.

A sound wave is modeled by y = 5 sin(440 · 2πt). What is the amplitude and frequency of this sound?

Amplitude = 5. B = 880π, period = 2π/(880π) = 1/440, so frequency = 440 Hz.

Ocean tides at a harbor are modeled by h(t) = 3 cos(πt/6) + 5, where h is in feet and t is in hours. What is the period of the tides?

Period = 2π/|B| = 2π/(π/6) = 12 hours.

The temperature in a city is modeled by T(t) = 15 sin(2πt/365 − π/2) + 55, where t is the day of the year. What is the average (midline) temperature?

The midline (average temperature) is D = 55°.

A pendulum's displacement is given by d(t) = 8 cos(πt). What is the maximum displacement and the period?

Amplitude = 8 (max displacement). Period = 2π/π = 2 seconds.

An AC voltage is given by V(t) = 170 sin(120πt). What is the period and frequency of the voltage?

Period = 2π/(120π) = 1/60 s. Frequency = 1/period = 60 Hz.

A spring oscillation is modeled by y = 6 sin(4t − π). What is the phase shift?

Phase shift = C/B = π/4 to the right.

A buoy bobs up and down with h(t) = 2 sin(πt/3) + 4. What is the minimum height of the buoy?

Minimum = D − |A| = 4 − 2 = 2 ft.

What does 'frequency' mean for a trigonometric function

Frequency = 1/period = |B|/(2π), the number of cycles completed per unit.
