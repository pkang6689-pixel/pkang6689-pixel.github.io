Lesson 3.3: Factoring Techniques (synthetic division, long division) - Summary

Key Concepts: Factoring Techniques (synthetic division, long division)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

Divide x³ − 1 by x − 1. What is the quotient?

x³ − 1 = (x − 1)(x² + x + 1), a difference of cubes factorization.

Divide 2x² + 5x + 3 by x + 1. What is the quotient?

Long division or synthetic division gives quotient 2x + 3 with remainder 0.

f(x) = x³ − 6x² + 11x − 6. What is f(1)?

1 − 6 + 11 − 6 = 0, so x = 1 is a zero and (x − 1) is a factor.

Divide x⁴ − 16 by x − 2. What is the remainder?

f(2) = 16 − 16 = 0, so the remainder is 0.

What is the degree of the quotient when dividing a degree-5 polynomial by a degree-2 polynomial?

The degree of the quotient is deg(dividend) − deg(divisor) = 5 − 2 = 3.

Divide x³ + 8 by x + 2. What is the quotient?

x³ + 8 = (x + 2)(x² − 2x + 4) by the sum of cubes formula.

What is the Remainder Theorem?

The Remainder Theorem states that when a polynomial f(x) is divided by (x − c), the remainder is the value f(c).

What is the Division Algorithm for polynomials?

The Division Algorithm states that for polynomials f(x) and d(x), there exist unique polynomials q(x) (quotient) and r(x) (remainder) such that f(x) = d(x) · q(x) + r(x), with deg(r) < deg(d).

What is meant by a 'placeholder zero' in synthetic division?

When a polynomial is missing terms (like x³ + 1 which is missing x² and x terms), you must insert 0 as a coefficient placeholder for synthetic division to work correctly.

What is the 'difference of cubes' factorization formula?

The difference of cubes formula is a³ − b³ = (a − b)(a² + ab + b²). Note the signs follow the pattern: same, opposite, always positive.

A chemist models the rate of a reaction as R(t) = t⁴ − 5t³ + 5t² + 5t − 6. She suspects that t = 1 is a time when the rate is zero. After confirming R(1) = 0 and dividing, what is the quotient polynomial?

Synthetic division with c = 1: coefficients 1, −5, 5, 5, −6 yield quotient t³ − 4t² + t + 6 with remainder 0.

A logistics company models shipping costs as C(x) = 6x³ − 7x² − 16x + 12. They need to determine if x = 2 (representing 2000 packages) gives zero cost. What does evaluation reveal, and what is the resulting quotient?

C(2) = 48 − 28 − 32 + 12 = 0. Synthetic division gives quotient 6x² + 5x − 6.

Real-World Applications

Example: An electronics company models its profit as P(x) = x³ − 2x² − 5x + 6, where x is in thousands of units. Management wants to know at which production levels profit is zero. After finding P(1) = 0 and dividing, the remaining quadratic factors as:

Dividing by (x − 1) gives x² − x − 6 = (x − 3)(x + 2). The zeros are x = 1, 3, −2.
