Lesson 7.7: Case Studies in Engineering - Summary

Key Concepts: Case Studies in Engineering

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

Two forces of 100 N and 150 N act at 60° to each other. What is the resultant magnitude?

R² = 100² + 150² + 2(100)(150)cos60° = 10000+22500+15000 = 47500. R ≈ 218 N.

What is 'Fourier analysis'?

Fourier analysis represents any periodic function as a sum (series) of sines and cosines.

What is 'signal processing'?

Signal processing uses mathematical tools (including trig identities and Fourier methods) to analyze and manipulate signals.

What is 'phase margin' in control systems?

Phase margin indicates how close a system is to instability; larger phase margin means more stability.

What is 'stress analysis' in structural engineering?

Stress analysis computes forces within structural members, using trig when loads are applied at angles.

What is 'aliasing' in signal processing?

Aliasing occurs when sampling is too slow; high-frequency components fold into lower frequencies, distorting the signal.

What is 'resonance' in engineering?

Resonance occurs when the driving frequency matches the system's natural frequency, causing large oscillations.

An engineer must find the resultant of two forces: 200 N at 0° and 300 N at 90°. What is the resultant force magnitude?

R = √(200² + 300²) = √(40000 + 90000) = √130000 ≈ 361 N.

A truss member carries 1000 N at 37° from the horizontal. What is the horizontal component? (cos 37° ≈ 0.8)

F_x = 1000 × cos37° ≈ 1000 × 0.8 = 800 N.

A robot arm applies a torque at angle θ = 45° to a wrench of length 0.5 m with 100 N force. What is the torque?

Torque = rFsinθ = 0.5 × 100 × sin45° = 50 × √2/2 = 25√2 N·m.

In an RLC circuit, Z = 50 Ω at a phase angle of 60°. What is the resistance (real component)?

R = |Z|cosφ = 50cos60° = 50 × 0.5 = 25 Ω.

A suspension bridge cable hangs at angle θ from horizontal. The vertical load supported is W = T sinθ, where T is cable tension. If T = 5000 N and θ = 30°, what is the vertical load?

W = 5000 sin30° = 5000 × 0.5 = 2500 N.

A vibration sensor detects a machine oscillating at 3sin(100πt) + 4cos(100πt). What is the amplitude of the combined vibration?

Amplitude = √(3² + 4²) = √(9+16) = 5.

Important Concepts

A digital signal is sampled at twice its highest frequency component. This minimum rate is called the:

The Nyquist rate = 2 × maximum frequency, the minimum sampling rate to avoid aliasing.

An audio signal sin(2π·1000t) is sampled at 1500 Hz, below the Nyquist rate of 2000 Hz. What frequency will appear in the sampled signal

The aliased frequency = |sample rate − signal frequency| = |1500 − 1000| = 500 Hz.

A control system has gain = 2 and phase = −135° at a certain frequency. The system is:

Phase margin = 180° − |−135°| = 45°. While the system is stable, 45° is considered a marginal phase margin.
