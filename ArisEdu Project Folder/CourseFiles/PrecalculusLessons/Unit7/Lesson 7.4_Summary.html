Lesson 7.4: Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product Identities - Summary

Key Concepts: Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product Identities

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'product-to-sum' identity?

Product-to-sum identities rewrite products like sinAcosB as sums involving sin(A±B).

What is a 'sum-to-product' identity?

Sum-to-product identities rewrite expressions like sinC + sinD as products such as 2sin((C+D)/2)cos((C−D)/2).

What is the sum-to-product formula for sin C + sin D?

sinC + sinD = 2sin((C+D)/2)cos((C−D)/2).

What is the sum-to-product formula for cos C − cos D?

cosC − cosD = −2sin((C+D)/2)sin((C−D)/2).

What is the product-to-sum formula for sin A cos B?

sinAcosB = (1/2)[sin(A+B) + sin(A−B)].

What is the sum-to-product formula for sin C − sin D?

sinC − sinD = 2cos((C+D)/2)sin((C−D)/2).

What is the sum-to-product formula for cos C + cos D?

cosC + cosD = 2cos((C+D)/2)cos((C−D)/2).

An audio engineer needs to convert cos(500t)cos(10t) into a sum for spectral analysis. What is the result?

cosAcosB = (1/2)[cos(A−B)+cos(A+B)] = (1/2)[cos(490t)+cos(510t)].

An engineer evaluates cos(75°)cos(15°) for a bridge design. What is the exact value?

cosAcosB = (1/2)[cos(60°)+cos(90°)] = (1/2)(1/2+0) = 1/4.

A tuning fork produces sin(2π·256·t) + sin(2π·258·t). What is the beat frequency perceived by the listener?

Using sum-to-product: 2sin(2π·257·t)cos(2π·1·t). The cosine modulation has frequency 1 Hz, but beats are heard at |f₁−f₂| = 2 Hz (because the amplitude envelope cycles twice per second).

Important Concepts

Two musical notes produce frequencies that create the sum sin(440t) + sin(442t). Using a sum-to-product identity, express this as a product to reveal the 'beat frequency'.

sin(440t)+sin(442t) = 2sin((440t+442t)/2)cos((440t−442t)/2) = 2sin(441t)cos(t). The beat frequency is 1 Hz.
