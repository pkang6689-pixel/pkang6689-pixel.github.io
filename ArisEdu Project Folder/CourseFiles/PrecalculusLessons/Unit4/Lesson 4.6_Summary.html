Lesson 4.6: Applications in Real-World Ratios (rates, proportions) - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Real-World Ratios (rates, proportions)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

Drive 60 miles at 30 mph, then 60 miles at 60 mph. What is the average speed for the whole trip?

Total distance = 120 mi. Time = 60/30 + 60/60 = 2 + 1 = 3 hrs. Average speed = 120/3 = 40 mph.

What is 'average speed'?

Average speed is total distance traveled divided by total time elapsed, not the arithmetic mean of individual speeds.

What is a 'combined work rate' in a work problem?

When working together, rates add: if A completes 1 job in a hours and B in b hours, combined rate = 1/a + 1/b.

What is a 'proportion'?

A proportion is an equation like a/b = c/d that states two ratios are equal.

What is 'inverse variation'?

Inverse variation means y = k/x for some constant k; as one quantity increases, the other decreases proportionally.

What is 'direct variation'?

Direct variation means y = kx; doubling x doubles y, and the ratio y/x = k is constant.

What is an 'extraneous solution' in a rational equation?

Extraneous solutions arise when multiplying by the LCD; they satisfy the cleared equation but cause division by zero in the original.

What is a 'mixture problem' in the context of rational equations?

Mixture problems use rational equations to model combining solutions of different concentrations to determine the resulting mixture.

What is 'joint variation'?

Joint variation involves multiple variables simultaneously, such as y = kxz (direct with both) or y = kx/z (direct with x, inverse with z).

A boat travels 24 km upstream in the same time it takes to travel 36 km downstream. The current is 3 km/h. What is the boat's speed in still water?

24/(r − 3) = 36/(r + 3). Cross multiply: 24(r + 3) = 36(r − 3) → 24r + 72 = 36r − 108 → 180 = 12r → r = 15 km/h.

A round trip covers 100 km each way. Going at 50 km/h and returning at 100 km/h, what is the average speed for the entire trip?

Total distance = 200 km. Total time = 100/50 + 100/100 = 2 + 1 = 3 hrs. Average = 200/3 ≈ 66.7 km/h.

Real-World Applications

Example: Drive 60 miles at 30 mph, then 60 miles at 60 mph. What is the average speed for the whole trip?

Total distance = 120 mi. Time = 60/30 + 60/60 = 2 + 1 = 3 hrs. Average speed = 120/3 = 40 mph.

Example: Average cost function: C̄(x) = (500 + 3x)/x. Find C̄(100).

C̄(100) = (500 + 300)/100 = 800/100 = 8.

Example: What does the horizontal asymptote of an average cost function C̄(x) = (fixed + variable·x)/x represent?

As production increases, the fixed cost is spread over more units. The HA equals the variable cost per unit, the lowest the average cost can approach.

Example: A company produces widgets with cost C(x) = 1500 + 4x dollars. How many widgets must be produced so the average cost is $10 per widget?

(1500 + 4x)/x = 10 → 1500 + 4x = 10x → 1500 = 6x → x = 250 widgets.
