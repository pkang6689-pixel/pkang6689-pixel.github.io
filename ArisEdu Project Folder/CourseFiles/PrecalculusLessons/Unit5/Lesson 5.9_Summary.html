Lesson 5.9: Case Studies in Biology & Finance - Summary

Key Concepts: Case Studies in Biology & Finance

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A fossil has 12.5% of its original C-14. What is the approximate age? (Half-life ≈ 5730 years.)

12.5% = 1/8 = (1/2)³, so 3 half-lives have passed. Age = 3 × 5730 = 17,190 years.

$10,000 invested at 5% continuously for 20 years. What is the value?

A = 10000e^(0.05 · 20) = 10000e¹ ≈ 10000(2.7183) ≈ $27,183.

A car worth $30,000 depreciates at 15% per year. What is its value after 3 years?

V = 30000(1 − 0.15)³ = 30000(0.85)³ = 30000(0.6141) ≈ $18,402.

C₀ = 200 mg, half-life 4 hours. What is the decay constant k in C(t) = 200e^(−kt)?

k = ln(2)/half-life = ln(2)/4 ≈ 0.693/4 ≈ 0.1733.

An investment doubles in 12 years with continuous compounding. What is the rate?

r = ln(2)/12 ≈ 0.693/12 ≈ 0.0578, or about 5.78%.

A culture starts at 1000 bacteria and reaches 8000 in 6 hours. What is the doubling time?

8000/1000 = 8 = 2³, so 3 doublings occurred in 6 hours. Doubling time = 6/3 = 2 hours.

What is the biological significance of half-life in pharmacology?

In pharmacology, half-life is the time required for the concentration of a drug in the bloodstream to decrease to half its current level through metabolism and excretion.

What is the difference between discrete and continuous exponential models?

Discrete models apply change at fixed intervals (annually, monthly, etc.), while continuous models apply change instantaneously at every moment, using the base e.

What is the relationship between doubling time and growth rate in continuous growth?

Doubling time T = ln(2)/k. A larger growth rate k produces a shorter doubling time; they are inversely proportional.

What is the key advantage of logarithmic models in science?

Logarithmic models compress enormous ranges of data into manageable numbers, which is why they are used in pH, decibels, the Richter scale, and other scientific measurements.

$5000 grows to $8000 in 5 years with annual compounding. What is the approximate annual rate?

(1 + r)⁵ = 8000/5000 = 1.6, so r = 1.6^(1/5) − 1 ≈ 1.0986 − 1 = 0.0986, or about 9.86%.

A $500 savings bond matures to $1000 in 20 years with continuous compounding. What is the rate?

1000 = 500e^(20r), so e^(20r) = 2, giving r = ln(2)/20 ≈ 0.693/20 ≈ 0.0347, or about 3.47%.

Real-World Applications

Example: What advantage does the exponential model have for biological growth compared to linear models?

In biology, more organisms produce more offspring, so growth is proportional to the current population. Exponential models (dP/dt = kP) capture this proportional growth.
