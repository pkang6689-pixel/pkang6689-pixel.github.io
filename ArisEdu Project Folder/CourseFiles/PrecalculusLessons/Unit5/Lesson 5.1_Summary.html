Lesson 5.1: Exponential Growth & Decay Models - Summary

Key Concepts: Exponential Growth & Decay Models

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

If k = 0.05, what is the approximate doubling time?

T = ln 2 / 0.05 ≈ 0.693 / 0.05 ≈ 13.86 years.

What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = 5 · 2ˣ?

As x approaches negative infinity, 2ˣ approaches 0, so f(x) approaches 0. The horizontal asymptote is y = 0.

What is the domain of an exponential function f(x) = bˣ where b > 0 and b ≠ 1?

An exponential function is defined for every real number x, so its domain is all real numbers (−∞, ∞).

What is the range of f(x) = 3ˣ?

Since 3ˣ is always positive and never equals zero, the range is y > 0, or (0, ∞).

What is the y-intercept of every function of the form f(x) = a · bˣ?

Setting x = 0 gives f(0) = a · b⁰ = a · 1 = a. The y-intercept is always equal to the coefficient a.

What is the 'growth factor' in the discrete model P(t) = P₀ · rᵗ?

The growth factor r is the constant multiplier applied each period. If r > 1 the quantity grows; if 0 < r < 1 it decays.

What is the approximate value of the mathematical constant e?

Euler's number e ≈ 2.71828. It is the base of the natural exponential function and arises naturally as the limit of (1 + 1/n)ⁿ as n approaches infinity.

A pharmacist notes that a drug decays to 25% of its original potency in 8 hours. What is the half-life of this drug?

25% = (1/2)², so 8 hours represents 2 half-lives. Each half-life is 8/2 = 4 hours.

A town's population grows continuously at 2% per year from 10,000 residents. A city planner wants to know the population after 5 years. What is the best estimate?

P(5) = 10,000 · e^(0.02 · 5) = 10,000 · e^0.1 ≈ 10,000 · 1.10517 ≈ 11,052.

A substance decays from 80 g to 10 g. A chemistry student needs to determine how many half-lives have elapsed. What is the answer?

Tracking the decay: 80 → 40 → 20 → 10. That is three successive halvings, so 3 half-lives have passed.

An investor puts $5000 into an account that grows continuously at 4% per year. She checks the balance after 10 years. What is the approximate value?

A = 5000e^(0.04 · 10) = 5000e^0.4 ≈ 5000(1.4918) ≈ $7,459.

A sample of a chemical compound decays continuously. If 60% of the sample remains after 5 years, what is the approximate decay constant k?

0.60 = e^(5k) gives 5k = ln(0.60) ≈ −0.5108, so k ≈ −0.5108/5 ≈ −0.102.
