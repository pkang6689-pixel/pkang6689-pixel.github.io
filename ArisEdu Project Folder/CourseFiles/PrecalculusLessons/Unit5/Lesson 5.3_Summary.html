Lesson 5.3: Properties of Logarithms - Summary

Key Concepts: Properties of Logarithms

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the product rule for logarithms?

The product rule states that the logarithm of a product equals the sum of the logarithms: log_b(xy) = log_b(x) + log_b(y).

What is the power rule for logarithms?

The power rule brings the exponent in front as a coefficient: log_b(xⁿ) = n · log_b(x).

What is the domain of f(x) = ln(x)?

The natural logarithm is only defined for positive inputs. The domain is x > 0, or (0, ∞).

What is the value of log_b(1) for any valid base b?

Since b⁰ = 1 for any valid base, log_b(1) = 0. This is a fundamental property of all logarithms.

What is the quotient rule for logarithms?

The quotient rule states that the logarithm of a quotient equals the difference of the logarithms: log_b(x/y) = log_b(x) − log_b(y).

A chemist measures the hydrogen ion concentration of a solution as [H⁺] = 10⁻⁴·⁵. She uses the formula pH = −log[H⁺]. What is the pH?

pH = −log(10⁻⁴·⁵) = −(−4.5) = 4.5. The power rule for logarithms gives log(10⁻⁴·⁵) = −4.5.

An acoustics engineer needs to expand log₃(9x²/y) for a signal processing formula. What is the expanded form?

log₃(9x²/y) = log₃(9) + log₃(x²) − log₃(y) = 2 + 2 log₃(x) − log₃(y), using the product, quotient, and power rules.

A data scientist condenses the expression (1/2) ln(x) − ln(3) into a single logarithm. What is the result?

By the power rule, (1/2)ln(x) = ln(x^(1/2)) = ln(√x). Then by the quotient rule, ln(√x) − ln(3) = ln(√x / 3).

A physicist simplifies ln(e⁷) while working on a thermodynamics problem. What is the simplified value?

By the inverse property, log_b(bˣ) = x. Therefore ln(e⁷) = 7.

A math tutor asks a student to evaluate log₅(1/25). What is the correct answer?

Since 1/25 = 5⁻², we have log₅(5⁻²) = −2 by the inverse property.

An environmental researcher uses the expression 3 ln(t) + ln(k) − 2 ln(r) in a pollution model. What is this expression condensed into a single logarithm?

Apply the power rule: 3 ln(t) = ln(t³) and 2 ln(r) = ln(r²). Then product and quotient rules give ln(t³) + ln(k) − ln(r²) = ln(kt³/r²).

A student solving an equation arrives at log(x) = 2. The teacher asks: what is x?

log₁₀(x) = 2 means x = 10² = 100. The common logarithm uses base 10.

An engineer working with decibel formulas needs to evaluate the expression log(10⁴ · 10⁶) without a calculator. What is the result?

Using the product rule: log(10⁴ · 10⁶) = log(10⁴) + log(10⁶) = 4 + 6 = 10. Alternatively, 10⁴ · 10⁶ = 10¹⁰.

A financial analyst needs to condense the expression log(P) + log(1 + r) · t into a single log for a report. What is the condensed form?

By the power rule, t · log(1 + r) = log((1 + r)ᵗ). Then by the product rule, log(P) + log((1 + r)ᵗ) = log(P(1 + r)ᵗ), which is the compound interest formula inside a logarithm.
