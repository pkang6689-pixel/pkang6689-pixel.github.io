Lesson 5.2: Compound Interest & Continuous Growth - Summary

Key Concepts: Compound Interest & Continuous Growth

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

$1000 is invested at 5% compounded annually for 2 years. What is the final amount?

A = 1000(1.05)² = 1000(1.1025) = $1102.50.

$1000 is invested at 6% compounded monthly for 1 year. What is the final amount?

A = 1000(1 + 0.06/12)^12 = 1000(1.005)^12 ≈ $1061.68.

$500 is invested at 4% compounded continuously for 5 years. What is the final amount?

A = 500e^(0.04 · 5) = 500e^0.2 ≈ 500(1.2214) ≈ $610.70.

What is the APY (annual percentage yield) when the nominal rate is 10% compounded semi-annually?

APY = (1 + 0.10/2)² − 1 = (1.05)² − 1 = 1.1025 − 1 = 0.1025 = 10.25%.

$10,000 is invested at 6% compounded monthly. What is the monthly interest rate used in each period?

The monthly rate is the annual rate divided by 12: 6%/12 = 0.5% (or 0.005 as a decimal).

What is the difference between APR and APY?

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the nominal rate, while APY (Annual Percentage Yield) is the effective rate that includes the effect of compounding within the year.

What is the Rule of 72?

The Rule of 72 is a shortcut: divide 72 by the annual interest rate percentage to estimate the number of years needed for an investment to double.

What is the effective annual rate always compared to the nominal rate when interest is compounded more than once per year?

When compounding occurs more than once per year, interest earns interest within the year, making the effective rate strictly greater than the nominal rate.

An entrepreneur invests $5000 in a business venture. After 10 years the investment is worth $8000. Assuming annual compounding, what is the approximate annual rate?

8000 = 5000(1 + r)^10, so (1 + r)^10 = 1.6, giving r = 1.6^(1/10) − 1 ≈ 0.048 or about 4.8%.

A parent sets aside $1000 at 5% compounded continuously for their newborn. What is the APY for this account?

APY = e^r − 1 = e^0.05 − 1 ≈ 1.05127 − 1 = 0.05127, or 5.127%.

A small business borrows $10,000 at 6% compounded monthly for a 5-year term. What is the total amount owed at maturity?

A = 10000(1 + 0.06/12)^(12·5) = 10000(1.005)^60 ≈ 10000(1.34885) ≈ $13,488.50.

A grandparent deposits $10,000 at 4% compounded monthly for a grandchild. After 20 years, the account is withdrawn. What is the approximate final value?

A = 10000(1 + 0.04/12)^(12·20) = 10000(1.00333)^240 ≈ 10000(2.2167) ≈ $22,167.

Real-World Applications

Example: What is the APY (annual percentage yield) when the nominal rate is 10% compounded semi-annually?

APY = (1 + 0.10/2)² − 1 = (1.05)² − 1 = 1.1025 − 1 = 0.1025 = 10.25%.

Important Concepts

What does 'compounding frequency' refer to

Compounding frequency is how often interest is computed and added to the balance (e.g., annually = 1, quarterly = 4, monthly = 12, daily = 365).
