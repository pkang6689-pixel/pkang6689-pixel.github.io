Lesson 5.6: Applications: pH, Richter Scale, Population Growth - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications: pH, Richter Scale, Population Growth

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

If [H⁺] = 10⁻⁵ M, what is the pH?

pH = −log(10⁻⁵) = −(−5) = 5.

A solution has pH = 3. What is [H⁺]?

[H⁺] = 10^(−pH) = 10⁻³.

What is the pH of pure water?

Pure water has [H⁺] = 10⁻⁷ M, so pH = 7 (neutral).

Sound at 80 dB versus 60 dB: what is the intensity ratio?

A 20 dB difference means the intensity ratio is 10^(20/10) = 10² = 100.

Carbon-14 has a half-life of approximately 5730 years. What is the decay constant k?

k = −ln(2)/half-life = −ln(2)/5730 ≈ −0.000121 per year.

A whisper is about 20 dB. Normal conversation is 60 dB. What is the intensity ratio?

40 dB difference: intensity ratio = 10^(40/10) = 10⁴ = 10,000.

What is the normal pH range of human blood?

Human blood is slightly basic with a normal pH range of about 7.35 to 7.45. Deviations outside this narrow range can be life-threatening.

A chemist measures [H⁺] = 3.16 × 10⁻⁸ M in a sample of tap water. What is the pH?

pH = −log(3.16 × 10⁻⁸) = −(log 3.16 + log 10⁻⁸) = −(0.5 − 8) = 7.5.

A population doubles every 15 years. An ecologist needs the continuous growth rate k. What is the approximate value?

k = ln(2)/doubling time = ln(2)/15 ≈ 0.693/15 ≈ 0.0462 per year.

A city's population is modeled by P(t) = 50,000e^(0.03t). A planner wants to know the population after 10 years. What is the approximate answer?

P(10) = 50,000e^(0.3) ≈ 50,000(1.3499) ≈ 67,493.

A coffee shop serves coffee at 200°F in a room at 70°F. Using Newton's law of cooling T(t) = 70 + 130e^(−0.05t), what is the coffee temperature after 20 minutes?

T(20) = 70 + 130e^(−0.05·20) = 70 + 130e^(−1) ≈ 70 + 130(0.368) ≈ 70 + 47.8 ≈ 118°F.
