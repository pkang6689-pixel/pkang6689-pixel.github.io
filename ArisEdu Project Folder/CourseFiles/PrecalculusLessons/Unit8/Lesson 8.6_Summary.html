Lesson 8.6: Applications in Finance & Growth Patterns - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Finance & Growth Patterns

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the compound interest formula for annual compounding?

For annual compounding: A = P(1 + r)ⁿ, where P is principal, r is annual rate, and n is the number of years.

If $1,000 is invested at 5% annual interest compounded annually, what is it worth after 3 years?

A = 1000(1.05)³ = 1000(1.157625) = $1,157.63.

What is the simple interest formula?

Simple interest is I = Prt, where P is principal, r is annual rate, and t is time in years.

A population of 2,000 grows at 3% per year. What is the population after 5 years?

P = 2000(1.03)⁵ = 2000(1.15927) ≈ 2,319.

What is the formula for compound interest with n compounding periods per year?

With n compoundings per year: A = P(1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ, dividing the rate and multiplying the exponent by the compounding frequency.

What is the continuously compounded interest formula?

Continuous compounding uses A = Peʳᵗ, the limit of A = P(1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ as n → ∞.

A car depreciates from $25,000 at 12% per year. What is its value after 4 years?

V = 25000(0.88)⁴ = 25000(0.59969) ≈ $14,984.

What is the present value of $10,000 to be received in 5 years at 8% annual interest?

PV = 10000/(1.08)⁵ = 10000/1.46933 ≈ $6,806.

What is 'compound interest'?

Compound interest is earned on both the original principal and the previously accumulated interest, leading to exponential growth.

What is 'present value'?

Present value (PV) is the current equivalent of a future amount, computed as PV = FV/(1+r)ⁿ.

What is an 'annuity'?

An annuity is a financial product that provides a series of equal payments at regular intervals, such as monthly retirement payments.

What is the 'Rule of 72'?

The Rule of 72 estimates doubling time by dividing 72 by the annual interest rate percentage. At 8%, money doubles in about 9 years.

A retiree deposits $200 per month into an account earning 6% annual interest compounded monthly. What is the value after 20 years? Use FV = PMT · [(1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ − 1]/(r/n).

FV = 200 · [(1.005)²⁴⁰ − 1]/0.005 = 200 · [3.3102 − 1]/0.005 = 200 · 462.04 ≈ $92,408.

A student takes out a $20,000 loan at 4.5% annual interest compounded monthly. If the loan term is 10 years, what is the monthly payment? Use PMT = P · (r/n)/[1 − (1 + r/n)^(−nt)].

PMT = 20000 · (0.00375)/[1 − (1.00375)⁻¹²⁰] = 75/[1 − 0.6387] = 75/0.3613 ≈ $207.

A homeowner's property is worth $300,000 and appreciates at 4% annually. What is the property worth after 10 years?

FV = 300000(1.04)¹⁰ = 300000(1.48024) ≈ $444,073.

A company invests $50,000 at 7% annual interest compounded continuously. What is the investment worth after 8 years?

A = 50000 · e^(0.07·8) = 50000 · e^0.56 = 50000 · 1.7507 ≈ $87,379.

A college fund starts with $10,000 and earns 5% annually. Simultaneously, $500 is withdrawn each year. Using the modified growth formula, what is the balance after 5 years?

Each year: balance = previous · 1.05 − 500. After 5 years: computing iteratively gives approximately $10,026.

Real-World Applications

Example: What does 'annual percentage rate (APR)' mean?

The APR is the nominal annual interest rate. It does not reflect the effect of intra-year compounding.
