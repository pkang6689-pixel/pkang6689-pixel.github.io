Lesson 4.7: Applications in Games of Chance - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Games of Chance

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is 'expected value' in probability?

EV = Σ(xᵢ × P(xᵢ)). It represents the theoretical average if the experiment were repeated infinitely.

What is the Gambler's Fallacy?

Each spin, flip, or roll is independent. A roulette wheel doesn't remember previous results; thinking it does is the Gambler's Fallacy.

What is a 'fair game' in probability terms?

In a mathematically fair game, EV = 0. Most casino games are NOT fair because the house edge makes EV negative for the player.

What is 'independent trials' in the context of gambling?

In games like roulette and slots, each trial is independent. The wheel has no memory of previous spins.

What is a 'random number generator' (RNG) in gaming?

RNGs are used in online casinos, video slots, and digital card games to simulate randomness and ensure each outcome is independent.

In American roulette, a player bets $10 on a single number. The payout is 35:1. With 38 slots, what is the expected value of this bet?

EV = (1/38)(35×10) + (37/38)(−10) = 350/38 − 370/38 = −20/38 ≈ −$0.53.

You play a game where you flip a fair coin. Heads: you win $3. Tails: you lose $2. What is the expected value per flip?

EV = 0.5(3) + 0.5(−2) = 1.50 − 1.00 = +$0.50. This game favors the player.

A scratch-off lottery ticket costs $5. Prize structure: 1 in 4 tickets wins $5, 1 in 100 wins $50, 1 in 10,000 wins $10,000. What is the approximate expected value?

EV = (0.25)(5) + (0.01)(50) + (0.0001)(10,000) + (remaining prob)(0) − 5 = 1.25 + 0.50 + 1.00 − 5 = −$2.25.

Real-World Applications

Example: A slot machine has a 95% payback rate. A player inserts $100. On average, how much will the player have after one complete cycle of play?

A 95% payback rate means the machine returns $0.95 per $1 wagered on average. $100 × 0.95 = $95. House edge = 5%.
