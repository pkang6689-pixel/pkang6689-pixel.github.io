Lesson 4.6: Venn Diagrams - Summary

Key Concepts: Venn Diagrams

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a Venn diagram?

Venn diagrams use circles (usually within a rectangle representing the universal set) to show logical relationships between sets.

What is the 'universal set' in a Venn diagram?

The universal set (U) contains every element relevant to the problem and is depicted as the enclosing rectangle.

What is De Morgan's Law?

De Morgan's Laws allow us to transform complements of compound sets: the complement of a union becomes an intersection of complements, and vice versa.

What is the 'empty set'?

The empty set has no members. It is a subset of every set and represents impossible events in probability.

A tech company surveyed 300 employees. 200 use Slack, 150 use Teams, and 100 use both. What is P(Slack or Teams) for a randomly selected employee?

P(S ∪ T) = P(S) + P(T) − P(S ∩ T) = 200/300 + 150/300 − 100/300 = 250/300 = 5/6.

Using a Venn diagram, if P(A) = 0.7, P(B) = 0.5, and P(A ∩ B) = 0.3, what is P(A only), i.e., P(A but not B)?

P(A only) = P(A) − P(A ∩ B) = 0.7 − 0.3 = 0.4.

A marketing team surveys 500 customers. 300 received email ads, 250 received social media ads, and 150 received both. If a customer from the survey is selected at random, what is the probability they received neither type of ad?

Union = 300 + 250 − 150 = 400. Neither = 500 − 400 = 100. P(neither) = 100/500 = 0.20.

A student club has members who play soccer (S), basketball (B), or both. |S| = 45, |B| = 30, |S ∩ B| = 12. A rival club claims the total membership is 45 + 30 = 75. Why is this wrong, and what is the correct total of members who play at least one sport?

Adding set sizes directly double-counts members in both sets. The inclusion-exclusion principle: |S ∪ B| = 45 + 30 − 12 = 63.
