Lesson 1.7: Case Studies in Statistical Misuse - Summary

Key Concepts: Case Studies in Statistical Misuse

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the best defense against statistical misuse?

Statistical literacy allows individuals to critically evaluate claims and detect misuse of data.

What is Simpson's Paradox?

Simpson's Paradox occurs when aggregated data show a different or opposite trend compared to what the data show within individual subgroups.

What is a 'leading question' in survey design?

A leading question is phrased to push respondents toward a specific answer, introducing response bias.

Real-World Applications

Example: A company claims '90% of dentists recommend our toothpaste.' Upon investigation, they surveyed only 10 dentists, and the question was 'Which toothpastes do you recommend?' allowing multiple answers. How is this misleading?

With only 10 dentists and a question allowing multiple recommendations, '90% recommend' is misleading because those dentists likely recommended many brands.
