Lesson 1.2: Populations vs. Samples - Summary

Key Concepts: Populations vs. Samples

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a census?

A census is a complete count or study that gathers information from every individual in the population of interest.

What is a point estimate?

A point estimate is a single value from a sample, such as the sample mean, used as the best guess for the corresponding population parameter.

What is the difference between a parameter and a statistic?

Parameters are numerical summaries of populations, while statistics are numerical summaries of samples drawn from those populations.

What is the meaning of 'sample size' (denoted n)?

Sample size (n) is the count of individual observations or data points included in a particular sample.
