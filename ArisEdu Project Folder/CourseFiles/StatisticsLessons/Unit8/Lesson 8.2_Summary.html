Lesson 8.2: Least Squares Regression Line - Summary

Key Concepts: Least Squares Regression Line

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the least squares regression line?

The LSRL is the line that makes the total squared prediction error as small as possible.

What is extrapolation in regression?

Extrapolation extends the model beyond the data range, where the linear pattern may no longer hold.

What is the formula for the slope of the LSRL?

The slope equals the correlation times the ratio of the standard deviation of y to the standard deviation of x.

What is meant by 'predicted value' (ŷ)?

ŷ is the output of the regression equation for a specific x value, representing the expected average y.

A real estate agent fits a regression: ŷ = 50,000 + 120x, where x is square footage and ŷ is predicted sale price in dollars. A 2,000 sq ft house recently sold for $310,000. What is the residual?

ŷ = 50,000 + 120(2,000) = $290,000. Residual = 310,000 − 290,000 = $20,000, meaning the house sold for $20,000 more than predicted.

A car manufacturer models fuel efficiency: ŷ = 45 − 0.005x, where x = vehicle weight (lbs) and ŷ = miles per gallon. For a 3,000-lb car, ŷ = 45 − 0.005(3000) = 30 mpg. The actual fuel efficiency is 28 mpg. What is the residual and what does it mean?

Residual = y − ŷ = 28 − 30 = −2. The negative residual means the actual mpg was 2 less than the model predicted.

A school district collects data on class size (x, number of students) and average standardized test score (y). The LSRL is ŷ = 95 − 0.4x with r² = 0.36. An administrator considers reducing class size from 30 to 20 students. According to the model, what is the predicted change in average test score?

The slope is −0.4, so each additional student is associated with a 0.4-point decrease. Reducing by 10 students predicts a 0.4 × 10 = 4-point increase.

Real-World Applications

Example: A real estate agent fits a regression: ŷ = 50,000 + 120x, where x is square footage and ŷ is predicted sale price in dollars. A 2,000 sq ft house recently sold for $310,000. What is the residual?

ŷ = 50,000 + 120(2,000) = $290,000. Residual = 310,000 − 290,000 = $20,000, meaning the house sold for $20,000 more than predicted.

Example: A teacher has the regression ŷ = 40 + 6x, where x = hours studied and ŷ = predicted test score. She wants to predict the score for a student who studied 12 hours, but students in the dataset studied between 1 and 8 hours. What concern applies?

Predicting at x = 12 when data only goes up to x = 8 is extrapolation; the relationship may level off or change beyond 8 hours.
