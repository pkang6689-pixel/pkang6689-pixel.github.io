Lesson 10.2: AP-Style Practice Problems - Summary

Key Concepts: AP-Style Practice Problems

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the purpose of the investigative task (Question 6) on the AP exam?

The investigative task tests your ability to combine multiple concepts and think critically about a novel statistical problem.

What is the best time management strategy for the AP Statistics exam?

Balanced time management across both sections maximizes your total score.

The AP rubric requires contextual interpretation that names both variables and uses language like 'predicted' or 'on average.'

Example: A researcher claims the mean commute time in a city is less than 30 minutes. She surveys 50 workers and finds x-bar = 27.4 with s = 8.2. On the AP exam, which is the correct set of hypotheses?

Hypotheses are about the population parameter μ, not the sample statistic. H₀ states the null value and Hₐ reflects the research claim (less than 30).

Example: On an AP free-response question, a student finds a regression equation ŷ = 22.5 + 3.8x for predicting test score (y) from hours of tutoring (x), with r² = 0.64. The student writes: 'The slope is 3.8.' What is missing from the interpretation?

The AP rubric requires contextual interpretation that names both variables and uses language like 'predicted' or 'on average.'

Example: A school nurse surveys 150 students and finds 45 reported having allergies. She calculates a 90% CI for the true proportion. The AP exam expects her to check which conditions before computing the interval?

For a one-proportion z-interval, the AP rubric requires checking random sampling, the 10% condition for independence, and the Large Counts condition (np-hat and n(1−p-hat) both ≥ 10).

Example: A student performs a matched pairs t-test comparing students' scores before and after a review session (n = 20 pairs). She finds t = 2.89 with df = 19 and a p-value of 0.009. On the AP exam, which conclusion in context is best?

The conclusion cites the p-value, makes the correct decision, and states the finding in context. Note that 'convincing evidence' avoids the word 'prove.'

Example: An AP exam question provides a residual plot from a linear regression of height vs. shoe size. The residuals show a clear curved (U-shaped) pattern. A student is asked what this means. Which response would earn full credit?

A pattern in the residual plot indicates the linear model misses systematic structure in the data. A full-credit response identifies the pattern, states the model is inappropriate, and suggests a nonlinear alternative.

Important Concepts

An AP free-response question presents data on exercise frequency (none, moderate, vigorous) and cholesterol level (normal, high) in a two-way table. Which procedure should the student use to test whether exercise frequency and cholesterol level are associated

Two categorical variables in a two-way table call for a chi-square test of independence to determine if they are associated.
