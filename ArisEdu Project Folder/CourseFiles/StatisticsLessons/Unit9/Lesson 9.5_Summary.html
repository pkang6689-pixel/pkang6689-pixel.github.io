Lesson 9.5: ANOVA (Analysis of Variance) - Summary

Key Concepts: ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A farmer tests four fertilizers on crop yield. The ANOVA table shows SSB = 450, SSW = 900, with k = 4 groups and n = 40 total plants. What is the F-statistic?

MSB = SSB/(k-1) = 450/3 = 150. MSW = SSW/(n-k) = 900/36 = 25. F = MSB/MSW = 150/25 = 6.00.

A nutritionist compares calorie intake across three age groups. She has 15, 18, and 12 participants in the groups respectively (n = 45 total). If SSB = 12,000 and SSW = 36,000, what is the F-statistic?

MSB = 12,000/(3-1) = 6,000. MSW = 36,000/(45-3) = 36,000/42 = 857.14. F = 6,000/857.14 = 7.00.

An ANOVA with k = 4 groups and n = 60 yields SSB = 300 and SST = 1500. What is eta-squared, and how would you interpret it?

Eta-squared = SSB/SST = 300/1500 = 0.20. This means 20% of the total variance in the outcome is explained by group membership, which is a medium-to-large effect in many fields.

Real-World Applications

Example: Why is running multiple t-tests instead of ANOVA problematic?

With k groups, there are k(k-1)/2 possible pairwise comparisons. Each t-test has its own chance of a Type I error, and these compound, greatly inflating the overall false positive rate.

Example: A psychologist tests three therapy methods (CBT, medication, combined) on depression scores. With 20 patients per group (60 total) and k = 3 groups, how many degrees of freedom does MSB have?

df(between) = k - 1 = 3 - 1 = 2. The within-group df would be n - k = 60 - 3 = 57.

Example: A teacher compares test scores across four sections of the same course. ANOVA gives F = 1.12 with p = 0.35. At alpha = 0.05, what should the teacher conclude?

Since p = 0.35 > alpha = 0.05, we fail to reject H-null. F = 1.12 is close to 1, suggesting little difference between sections. Post-hoc tests are not warranted when ANOVA fails to reject.

Example: A nutritionist compares calorie intake across three age groups. She has 15, 18, and 12 participants in the groups respectively (n = 45 total). If SSB = 12,000 and SSW = 36,000, what is the F-statistic?

MSB = 12,000/(3-1) = 6,000. MSW = 36,000/(45-3) = 36,000/42 = 857.14. F = 6,000/857.14 = 7.00.

Example: A pharmaceutical company tests three drug dosages (low, medium, high) on blood pressure reduction. ANOVA yields p = 0.008. Tukey's HSD shows: Low vs. Medium (p = 0.42), Low vs. High (p = 0.005), Medium vs. High (p = 0.03). Which pairs differ significantly at alpha = 0.05?

Low vs. High (p = 0.005 < 0.05) and Medium vs. High (p = 0.03 < 0.05) are both significant. Low vs. Medium (p = 0.42 > 0.05) is not. The high dose produces significantly different results from both other doses.
