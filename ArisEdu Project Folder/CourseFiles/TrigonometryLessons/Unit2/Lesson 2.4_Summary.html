Lesson 2.4: Graphs of Tangent, Cotangent, Secant, Cosecant - Summary

Key Concepts: Graphs of Tangent, Cotangent, Secant, Cosecant

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

An engineer models vibration data with y = 3 tan(2t). What is the period of this tangent function?

Period of tan(Bt) = π/B = π/2.

A signal processing model uses y = sec(πx/4). What is the period of this secant function?

Period of sec(Bx) = 2π/B = 2π/(π/4) = 8.

An oscilloscope displays a waveform modeled by y = 4 csc(2x). What is the period?

Period of csc(Bx) = 2π/B = 2π/2 = π.

A radar screen shows a pattern following y = tan(πx/3). What is the distance between consecutive asymptotes?

Period of tan(Bx) = π/B = π/(π/3) = 3. The distance between consecutive asymptotes equals the period.

A bridge cable's shape is modeled by y = sec(x) − 1. What is the minimum value of y?

The minimum of sec(x) is 1 (at x = 0). So minimum of sec(x) − 1 = 1 − 1 = 0.
