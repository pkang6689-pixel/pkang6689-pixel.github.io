Lesson 2.5: Transformations of Trig Graphs (amplitude, period, phase shift) - Summary

Key Concepts: Transformations of Trig Graphs (amplitude, period, phase shift)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A Ferris wheel's height is modeled by h(t) = 25 sin(π/20 · t − π/2) + 30. What is the amplitude of the ride?

The amplitude is the coefficient of the sine function: 25.

A sound wave is modeled by y = 0.5 sin(440πt). What is the period of the wave?

Period = 2π/(440π) = 2/440 = 1/220 s.

A tide is modeled by y = 3 cos(π/6 · (t − 2)) + 5. What is the phase shift?

The (t − 2) term shifts the graph 2 units to the right.

A heartbeat monitor shows y = −4 cos(2πt/0.8). What is the period of the heartbeat?

Period = 2π/(2π/0.8) = 0.8 s.

An amusement ride's height is h(t) = −30 cos(πt/10) + 35. What is the minimum height?

Minimum = midline − amplitude = 35 − 30 = 5 m.
