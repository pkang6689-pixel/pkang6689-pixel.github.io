Lesson 2.1: Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent - Summary

Key Concepts: Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A Ferris wheel has radius 20 m. A rider is at an angle of 30° from the horizontal center. What is the rider's height above the center using sine?

Height above center = r sin θ = 20 sin 30° = 20(0.5) = 10 m.

A child on a swing reaches a maximum angle of 45° from vertical. If the swing chain is 3 m long, what is the horizontal displacement at the peak? (Use sin 45° = √2/2)

Horizontal displacement = chain × sin 45° = 3(√2/2) = 3√2/2 m.

A surveyor stands on flat ground and needs to find the height of a tower. She measures the angle of elevation as θ where sin θ = 3/5 and stands 40 m from the base. What is tan θ?

sin θ = 3/5 → cos θ = 4/5 (Pythagorean triple). tan θ = sin θ/cos θ = (3/5)/(4/5) = 3/4.

A drone flies at a constant altitude. Its camera is tilted at angle θ from vertical, where cos θ = 5/13. What is sin θ for this camera angle?

cos θ = 5/13 → sin θ = √(1 − 25/169) = √(144/169) = 12/13.

A cable car travels along a wire that makes angle θ with the horizontal. If sin θ = 0.6 and cos θ = 0.8, what is tan θ?

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = 0.6/0.8 = 0.75.

A roof has a pitch angle θ with sin θ = 5/13. The roof spans 24 m horizontally. What is the length of the roof surface (the hypotenuse)?

sin θ = 5/13 → cos θ = 12/13. Horizontal = hyp × cos θ → 24 = hyp × 12/13 → hyp = 26 m.
