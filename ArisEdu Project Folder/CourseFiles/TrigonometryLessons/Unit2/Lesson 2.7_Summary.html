Key Concepts: Applications in Sound Waves

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A4 = 440 Hz. What is A5?

One octave up doubles the frequency: 880 Hz.

A4 = 440 Hz. What is A3?

One octave down halves the frequency: 220 Hz.

A concert speaker emits a pure tone modeled by y = 0.02 sin(880πt). What is the frequency of this sound in Hz?

Frequency = ω/(2π) = 880π/(2π) = 440 Hz.

A microphone picks up a sound with amplitude 0.05 Pa and frequency 1000 Hz. What is the angular frequency ω?

ω = 2πf = 2π(1000) = 2000π rad/s.

A noise-canceling headphone generates an anti-sound wave y₂ = −0.01 sin(600πt) to cancel y₁ = 0.01 sin(600πt). What is y₁ + y₂?

y₁ + y₂ = 0.01 sin(600πt) − 0.01 sin(600πt) = 0 (destructive interference).

A sound wave y = A sin(2πft) has period 0.005 s. What is the frequency of the sound?

f = 1/T = 1/0.005 = 200 Hz.

A dog whistle emits y = 0.001 sin(50000πt). What is the frequency in Hz, and can humans hear it?

f = 50000π/(2π) = 25,000 Hz. Human hearing tops out around 20,000 Hz, so this is ultrasonic.

Two identical speakers play y = sin(1000πt), but one is phase-shifted by π. The combined wave is y₁ + y₂ = sin(1000πt) + sin(1000πt + π). What is the result?

sin(x + π) = −sin(x), so the sum is sin(x) + (−sin(x)) = 0 (complete destructive interference).

Important Concepts

Frequency is measured in:

Hz = cycles per second.

Higher frequency means:

Frequency determines pitch: higher frequency → higher pitch.

Period and frequency are related by:

Period is the reciprocal of frequency.

Beat frequency =

Beat frequency equals the absolute difference of the two frequencies.

Two tuning forks vibrate at 440 Hz and 442 Hz. The beat frequency is:

|442 − 440| = 2 Hz.

If a sound has period 0.005 s, its frequency is:

f = 1/T = 1/0.005 = 200 Hz.
