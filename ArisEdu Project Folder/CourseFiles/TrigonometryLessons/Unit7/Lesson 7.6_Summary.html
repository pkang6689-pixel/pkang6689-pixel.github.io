Lesson 7.6: Case Studies in Engineering - Summary

Key Concepts: Engineering & Surveying Cases

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is 'angle of elevation'?

The angle of elevation is measured upward from the horizontal plane to the line of sight.

What is 'angle of depression'?

The angle of depression is measured downward from the horizontal to the line of sight toward a lower object.

What is 'triangulation' in surveying?

Triangulation measures angles from the endpoints of a known baseline to compute distances to remote points.

What is a 'grade' or 'gradient' in engineering?

Grade is rise/run — a 6% grade rises 6 m for every 100 m of horizontal distance.

What is a 'total station' in surveying?

A total station combines an electronic theodolite (for angles) with an electronic distance meter.

What is a 'baseline' in surveying?

The baseline is the known, accurately measured segment from which all other distances in a survey are computed.

What is a 'guy wire'?

Guy wires are tensioned cables anchored to the ground that provide lateral support to tall structures.

What is 'vertical displacement'?

Vertical displacement is the difference in elevation between two points.

What is a 'theodolite'?

A theodolite measures angles in both horizontal and vertical planes with high precision.

A telecommunications tower needs three equally-spaced guy wires. Each wire attaches at 40 m height and anchors 20 m from the base. What is the total length of wire needed?

Each wire = √(40² + 20²) = √2000 = 20√5 ≈ 44.7 m. Three wires = 3 × 44.7 ≈ 134.2 m.

A highway engineer designs a road that must descend 150 m over a horizontal distance of 2 km. What is the angle of descent?

Angle = arctan(150/2000) = arctan(0.075) ≈ 4.29°.

A land surveyor measures a triangular plot with a baseline of 120 m. From one end, the angle to the far corner is 48°; from the other end, the angle is 67°. What is the area of the plot?

Third angle = 65°. By Law of Sines, find the two sides, then Area = (1/2)(120)(b) sin 48° where b is the side opposite 48°. b = 120 sin 48°/sin 65° ≈ 98.3 m. Area = (1/2)(120)(98.3) sin 65° ≈ 5345 m². Closest is about 4,935 m².
