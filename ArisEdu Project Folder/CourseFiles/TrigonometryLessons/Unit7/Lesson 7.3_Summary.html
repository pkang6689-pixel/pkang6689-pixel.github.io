Lesson 7.3: Area of Triangles (Heron's Formula, sine area formula) - Summary

Key Concepts: Law of Cosines

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the 'Law of Cosines'?

The Law of Cosines extends the Pythagorean theorem to all triangles by adding the −2ab cos C correction term.

What is the 'included angle'?

The included angle is the angle whose vertex is the intersection of the two specified sides.

What is the 'Pythagorean theorem'?

The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right triangle, the sum of the squares of the legs equals the square of the hypotenuse.

What is the 'triangle inequality'?

The triangle inequality states a + b > c, a + c > b, and b + c > a must all hold.

What is the 'cosine rule' for angles?

Rearranging c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos C gives cos C = (a² + b² − c²)/(2ab).

A triangular lot has sides 40 m, 50 m, and 70 m. A real estate agent needs the measure of the largest angle. What is it?

The largest angle is opposite the longest side (70 m). cos C = (1600 + 2500 − 4900)/(2·40·50) = −800/4000 = −0.2. C = arccos(−0.2) ≈ 101.5°. Closest is about 99°.

A construction crane has a boom of 30 m and a jib of 20 m, making an angle of 110° at the pivot. What is the distance between the tip of the boom and the tip of the jib?

d² = 900 + 400 − 1200 cos 110° = 1300 − 1200(−0.342) = 1300 + 410.4 = 1710.4. d ≈ 41.4 m.

A surveyor measures a triangular field with sides 200 m, 250 m, and 300 m. She needs the smallest angle. Which angle is it, and what is its approximate measure?

The smallest angle is opposite the shortest side (200 m). cos A = (250² + 300² − 200²)/(2·250·300) = (62500 + 90000 − 40000)/150000 = 112500/150000 = 0.75. A = arccos(0.75) ≈ 41.4°.

A parallelogram has sides 8 cm and 12 cm with an included angle of 60°. What is the length of the longer diagonal?

The longer diagonal is opposite the obtuse angle (120°). d² = 64 + 144 − 192 cos 120° = 208 + 96 = 304. d ≈ 17.4 cm.

Real-World Applications

Example: A triangular lot has sides 40 m, 50 m, and 70 m. A real estate agent needs the measure of the largest angle. What is it?

The largest angle is opposite the longest side (70 m). cos C = (1600 + 2500 − 4900)/(2·40·50) = −800/4000 = −0.2. C = arccos(−0.2) ≈ 101.5°. Closest is about 99°.

Example: A construction crane has a boom of 30 m and a jib of 20 m, making an angle of 110° at the pivot. What is the distance between the tip of the boom and the tip of the jib?

d² = 900 + 400 − 1200 cos 110° = 1300 − 1200(−0.342) = 1300 + 410.4 = 1710.4. d ≈ 41.4 m.

Example: A mechanical arm has two segments of length 1.5 m and 1.0 m. The angle at the joint is adjustable. What angle between the segments makes the tip exactly 2.0 m from the base?

4 = 2.25 + 1 − 3 cos C → cos C = (3.25 − 4)/3 = −0.25. C = arccos(−0.25) ≈ 104.5°.
