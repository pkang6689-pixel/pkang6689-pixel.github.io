Lesson 7.1: Law of Sines (ambiguous case) - Summary

Key Concepts: Law of Sines

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the 'Law of Sines'?

The Law of Sines establishes that a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C for any triangle.

What is the 'circumradius' of a triangle?

The circumradius R is the radius of the circle that passes through all three vertices of the triangle.

What is an 'included side'?

An included side is the side located between (shared by) two specified angles.

What is the 'opposite side' relative to an angle?

The opposite side to angle A is the side that does not have vertex A as an endpoint.

What is the 'triangle angle sum property'?

In any triangle, A + B + C = 180°.

A rescue helicopter spots two stranded hikers. The angle of depression to hiker A is 28° and the horizontal distance is 1.2 km. The angle between the directions to both hikers (from the helicopter) is 45°, and hiker B is at angle of depression 35° at 0.9 km horizontal distance. Using the Law of Sines in the ground triangle, what is the approximate distance between the hikers?

Using the Law of Cosines in the ground triangle with sides 1.2 and 0.9 km and included angle 45°: d² = 1.2² + 0.9² − 2(1.2)(0.9)cos 45° ≈ 1.44 + 0.81 − 1.527 ≈ 0.723. d ≈ 0.85 km.
