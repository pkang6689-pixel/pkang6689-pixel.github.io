Lesson 4.7: Case Studies in Physics - Summary

Key Concepts: Physics Cases – Identities in Action

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

An inclined plane problem uses the identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 to show that the sum of the squared force components equals W². If the parallel component is W sin θ = 30 N, what is the normal component (given W = 50 N)?

(W sin θ)² + (W cos θ)² = W². 30² + N² = 50². N² = 2500 − 900 = 1600. N = 40 N.

In optics, Snell's law gives sin θ₁ / sin θ₂ = n₂/n₁. If n₁ = 1, n₂ = √2, and θ₁ = 45°, what is sin θ₂?

sin 45°/sin θ₂ = √2/1 → sin θ₂ = sin 45°/√2 = (√2/2)/√2 = 1/2.
