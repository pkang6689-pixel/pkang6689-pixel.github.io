Lesson 4.5: Verifying Identities - Summary

Key Concepts: Verifying Identities

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A student tries to verify cos θ/(1 + sin θ) = (1 − sin θ)/cos θ by multiplying the left side by the conjugate. What is the conjugate used?

Multiply by (1 − sin θ)/(1 − sin θ): cos θ(1 − sin θ)/(1 − sin²θ) = cos θ(1 − sin θ)/cos²θ = (1 − sin θ)/cos θ.

Real-World Applications

Example: Why shouldn't you cross-multiply when verifying?

Cross-multiplying treats both sides as equal, which is what you're trying to show.

Example: Multiplying by a conjugate is useful when the expression contains:

Conjugates create difference-of-squares, which work with Pythagorean identities.

Example: An exam asks to verify: (sin θ + cos θ)² = 1 + 2 sin θ cos θ. Expanding the left side gives:

(sin θ + cos θ)² = sin²θ + 2 sin θ cos θ + cos²θ = 1 + 2 sin θ cos θ.
