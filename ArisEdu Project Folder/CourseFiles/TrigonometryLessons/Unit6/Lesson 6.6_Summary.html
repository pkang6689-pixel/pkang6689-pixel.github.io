Lesson 6.6: Case Studies in Physics - Summary

Key Concepts: Physics Cases – Oscillations & Waves

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A mass on a spring has x(t) = 0.1 cos(10t) m. What is the period?

Period T = 2π/ω = 2π/10 = π/5 s.

A wave is described by y = 3 sin(2πx/λ − 2πft). If f = 440 Hz, what is the angular frequency ω?

ω = 2πf = 2π(440) = 880π rad/s.

A damped oscillation is x(t) = 5e^(−0.1t) cos(4t). What is the initial amplitude?

At t = 0, the envelope is 5e^0 = 5, so the initial amplitude is 5.

What is 'simple harmonic motion' (SHM)?

SHM occurs when a restoring force proportional to displacement drives the object back and forth sinusoidally.

What is 'angular frequency' (ω)?

Angular frequency ω = 2πf measures how fast the phase angle changes, in rad/s.

What is a 'node' in a standing wave?

A node is a fixed point on a standing wave where destructive interference causes zero displacement permanently.

What is an 'antinode' in a standing wave?

An antinode is a point on a standing wave where constructive interference produces the largest oscillation amplitude.

What is 'wavelength' (λ)?

Wavelength λ is the distance between two consecutive points in phase (e.g., crest to crest).

What is 'superposition' of waves?

Superposition states that when waves overlap, the net displacement equals the algebraic sum of individual displacements.

What is the 'phase' of a wave?

The phase is the total angle argument (like ωt + φ) that determines where in its cycle the wave is at any time/position.

What is 'resonance' in an oscillating system?

Resonance occurs when an external force oscillates at the system's natural frequency, producing dramatically larger oscillations.

What is the 'wave number' (k)?

The wave number k = 2π/λ tells how many radians of phase change occur per unit distance.

A tuning fork produces pressure waves p(t) = 0.003 sin(2π · 256 · t) Pa. A lab report requires the time between consecutive zero crossings (half-period). What is this time?

Period T = 1/256 s. Half-period = 1/512 s ≈ 1.95 ms.

An ocean wave has the equation y(x,t) = 1.5 sin(0.5x − 2t) m. A surfer wants to know the wave speed. What is it?

Wave speed v = ω/k = 2/0.5 = 4 m/s.

When the driving frequency equals the natural frequency, resonance occurs and the amplitude grows dramatically.

A physics student combines two waves: y₁ = 3 sin(10t) and y₂ = 3 sin(10.5t). The resulting 'beat frequency' heard is:

Beat frequency = |f₁ − f₂| = |10/(2π) − 10.5/(2π)| = 0.5/(2π) ≈ 0.08 Hz.
