Lesson 6.4: General Solutions & Periodicity - Summary

Key Concepts: General Solutions & Periodicity

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'general solution' of a trigonometric equation?

The general solution captures every solution by expressing them with a parameter n, such as θ = π/6 + 2nπ.

What is the 'fundamental period' of a function?

The fundamental period is the smallest positive T for which the function values repeat exactly.

What is the period of sin θ?

The sine function repeats every 2π radians, so its period is 2π.

What is the period of cot θ?

Like tangent, cotangent has a period of π.

What is a 'particular solution' of a trig equation?

A particular solution is one specific angle, such as θ = π/6, obtained from the general solution by plugging in a value of n.

What is an 'interval of restriction' when finding solutions?

An interval of restriction (like [0, 2π) or [0°, 360°)) limits which solutions from the general solution you report.

What is a 'coterminal angle'?

Coterminal angles land on the same terminal side and differ by integer multiples of 2π (or 360°).

A pendulum swings with θ(t) = 0.3 cos(2πt). A physics student needs to know every time the pendulum passes through θ = 0 rad. What is the general solution?

cos(2πt) = 0 gives 2πt = π/2 + nπ, so t = 1/4 + n/2.

A satellite orbits Earth every 90 minutes. Its latitude is modeled by L(t) = 51.6 sin(2πt/90)°. An astronomer wants to know all times the satellite crosses the equator (L = 0). What is the general solution?

sin(2πt/90) = 0 gives 2πt/90 = nπ, so t = 45n minutes (every 45 min the satellite crosses the equator).

A sound wave is described by p(t) = sin(440πt). A recording engineer needs all times when p = −1 (maximum compression). What is the general solution?

sin(440πt) = −1 gives 440πt = 3π/2 + 2nπ, so t = 3/880 + 2n/440 = 3/880 + n/220.

An oceanographer models wave crests with y(x) = 2 sin(πx/5). She needs to know every x-position where y = 2 m (i.e., a crest). What is the general solution?

sin(πx/5) = 1 gives πx/5 = π/2 + 2nπ, so x = 5/2 + 10n.

A piston moves vertically according to y(t) = 4 cos(100πt) cm. A mechanic wants all times the piston is at y = −4 cm (bottom). What is the general solution?

cos(100πt) = −1 gives 100πt = π + 2nπ, so t = 1/100 + 2n/100 = 1/100 + n/50.

Real-World Applications

Example: An AC generator produces voltage V(t) = 170 sin(120πt). An electrician needs to know all times when V = 85 V. Express the general solution.

sin(120πt) = 1/2, so 120πt = π/6 + 2nπ or 5π/6 + 2nπ. Then t = 1/720 + n/60 or t = 5/720 + n/60.

Example: A clock's second hand angle is θ(t) = 2πt/60 radians. A student asks: at what general times does the tip pass the 3 o'clock position (θ = 0 mod 2π)?

θ = 2nπ gives 2πt/60 = 2nπ, so t = 60n seconds — every full minute.
