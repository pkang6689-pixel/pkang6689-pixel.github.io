Lesson 10.5: Comprehensive Review & AP Exam Practice - Summary

Key Concepts: AP Exam Review & Practice

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the exact value of sin(5pi/6)?

5pi/6 is in Quadrant II with reference angle pi/6. Since sine is positive in QII, sin(5pi/6) = sin(pi/6) = 1/2.

What is the period of y = 3 sin(2x - pi/4)?

For y = A sin(Bx + C), the period is 2pi/|B|. Here B = 2, so the period is 2pi/2 = pi.

If tan(theta) = -3/4 and theta is in Quadrant II, what is cos(theta)?

In QII, cosine is negative. From tan = -3/4, opposite = 3, adjacent = 4, so hypotenuse = 5. Therefore cos(theta) = -4/5.

What is the exact value of cos(pi/12)?

cos(pi/12) = cos(pi/3 - pi/4) = cos(pi/3)cos(pi/4) + sin(pi/3)sin(pi/4) = (1/2)(sqrt(2)/2) + (sqrt(3)/2)(sqrt(2)/2) = (sqrt(2) + sqrt(6))/4.

What is the magnitude of the vector v = <-5, 12>?

|v| = sqrt((-5)^2 + 12^2) = sqrt(25 + 144) = sqrt(169) = 13.

What is the derivative of f(x) = tan(3x)?

By the chain rule, d/dx[tan(3x)] = sec^2(3x) * 3 = 3 sec^2(3x).

What is the unit circle?

The unit circle is defined as a circle with radius 1 centered at the origin (0, 0). It is fundamental for defining trigonometric functions for all real angles.

What is a reference angle?

A reference angle is the positive acute angle formed between the terminal side of an angle in standard position and the nearest part of the x-axis. It is used to evaluate trig functions in any quadrant.

What is meant by the 'ambiguous case' in triangle solving?

The ambiguous case occurs with SSA (Side-Side-Angle) information. Because the given angle is not between the two known sides, the Law of Sines may produce zero, one, or two valid triangles.

What is a polar coordinate system?

A polar coordinate system locates each point by a radial distance r from the origin (pole) and an angle theta measured from the positive x-axis (polar axis). It is especially useful for curves with circular symmetry.

What is De Moivre's Theorem?

De Moivre's Theorem states that for a complex number in polar form, [r cis(theta)]^n = r^n cis(n*theta). It simplifies raising complex numbers to powers and finding nth roots.

What is a parametric equation?

Parametric equations express the coordinates x and y as separate functions of a third variable (parameter), usually t. This allows representation of curves that fail the vertical line test and adds the concept of direction and timing.

A surveyor stands 50 meters from the base of a building and measures the angle of elevation to the rooftop as 58 degrees. A flagpole on the roof adds an additional angle of 7 degrees above the roofline. What is the height of the flagpole alone? (Use tan(58) = 1.600 and tan(65) = 2.145.)

The height to the rooftop is 50 tan(58) = 80.00 m. The total height to the top of the flagpole is 50 tan(65) = 107.25 m. The flagpole height is 107.25 - 80.00 = 27.25 meters.

An AP student must find the area of a triangle with sides a = 8, b = 11, and included angle C = 30 degrees. Using the formula Area = (1/2)ab sin(C), what is the area?

Area = (1/2)(8)(11) sin(30) = (1/2)(88)(1/2) = 22 square units. The sine area formula is a standard AP topic for finding triangle areas without needing the height.

A ship sails 15 km on a bearing of N40E, then turns and sails 22 km on a bearing of S30E. What is the straight-line distance between the starting point and the ending point? (Use cos(110) = -0.342.)

The angle between the two paths is 180 - 40 - 30 = 110 degrees. By the Law of Cosines: d^2 = 15^2 + 22^2 - 2(15)(22)cos(110) = 225 + 484 - 660(-0.342) = 709 + 225.72 = 934.72. So d = sqrt(934.72) approximately equals 30.6 km.

An electrical engineer measures two AC voltages: V1 = 120 sin(60pi*t) and V2 = 120 sin(60pi*t + pi/3). Using the sum-to-product identity, what is the amplitude of V1 + V2?

When adding two sinusoids of equal amplitude A with phase difference phi, the resultant amplitude is 2A cos(phi/2). Here A = 120 and phi = pi/3, so amplitude = 2(120) cos(pi/6) = 240(sqrt(3)/2) = 120sqrt(3) approximately equals 207.8 V.

On the AP exam, a student is asked to evaluate the integral of sin^2(x) dx from 0 to pi. Using the power-reduction identity sin^2(x) = (1 - cos(2x))/2, what is the result?

Integral of sin^2(x) dx = integral of (1 - cos(2x))/2 dx = [x/2 - sin(2x)/4] from 0 to pi = (pi/2 - 0) - (0 - 0) = pi/2.
