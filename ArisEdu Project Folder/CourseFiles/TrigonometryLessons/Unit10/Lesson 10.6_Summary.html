Lesson 10.6: Capstone Project: Real-World Trigonometric Model - Summary

Key Concepts: Capstone: Comprehensive Review

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the exact value of tan(3pi/4)?

3pi/4 is in Quadrant II with reference angle pi/4. Since tangent is negative in QII, tan(3pi/4) = -tan(pi/4) = -1.

If sec(theta) = -5/3 and theta is in Quadrant III, what is sin(theta)?

sec(theta) = -5/3 means cos(theta) = -3/5. Using sin^2 + cos^2 = 1: sin^2 = 1 - 9/25 = 16/25, so sin = +/-4/5. In QIII, sine is negative, so sin(theta) = -4/5.

What is the phase shift of y = 2 cos(3x - pi)?

For y = A cos(Bx - C), the phase shift is C/B. Here C = pi and B = 3, so the phase shift is pi/3 to the right.

What is the dot product of u = <3, -2> and v = <4, 7>?

u . v = (3)(4) + (-2)(7) = 12 - 14 = -2.

Using the half-angle formula, what is the exact value of sin(pi/8)?

sin(pi/8) = sin((pi/4)/2) = sqrt((1 - cos(pi/4))/2) = sqrt((1 - sqrt(2)/2)/2) = sqrt((2 - sqrt(2))/4) = sqrt(2 - sqrt(2))/2.

What is the cross product of u = <1, 0, 3> and v = <2, -1, 0>?

u x v = <(0)(0) - (3)(-1), (3)(2) - (1)(0), (1)(-1) - (0)(2)> = <0 + 3, 6 - 0, -1 - 0> = <3, 6, -1>.

What is the Pythagorean identity?

The fundamental Pythagorean identity states sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 for all x. It is derived from the Pythagorean theorem applied to the unit circle and forms the basis for the other Pythagorean identities.

What is meant by 'radian measure'?

A radian is defined as the angle subtended when the arc length equals the radius. Formally, theta (in radians) = arc length / radius. One full revolution equals 2pi radians.

What is the Law of Cosines?

The Law of Cosines states c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). It generalizes the Pythagorean theorem to non-right triangles and is used to solve triangles when given SAS or SSS information.

What is trigonometric substitution in calculus?

Trigonometric substitution is an integration technique where a variable is replaced by a trigonometric expression (e.g., x = a sin(theta)) to eliminate a radical. This leverages Pythagorean identities to simplify the integrand.

What is a limacon in polar coordinates?

A limacon is a polar curve of the form r = a + b cos(theta) or r = a + b sin(theta). Depending on the ratio of a to b, it can have an inner loop, be cardioid-shaped, or have a dimple.

What is a unit vector?

A unit vector is a vector with magnitude 1. It is used to represent direction without magnitude. Any nonzero vector v can be converted to a unit vector by dividing by its magnitude: v/|v|.

A landscape architect is designing a triangular garden with vertices at A, B, and C. She measures AB = 18 m, AC = 24 m, and angle A = 72 degrees. What is the area of the garden? (Use sin(72) = 0.951.)

Area = (1/2)(AB)(AC) sin(A) = (1/2)(18)(24)(0.951) = (216)(0.951) = 205.4 square meters.

A sound engineer models two audio signals as y1 = 5 sin(440pi*t) and y2 = 5 cos(440pi*t). The combined signal is y = y1 + y2. What is the amplitude of the combined signal?

A sin(wt) + A cos(wt) = A sqrt(2) sin(wt + pi/4). With A = 5, the combined amplitude is 5sqrt(2) approximately equals 7.07. This uses the identity for combining sine and cosine of the same frequency.

An airplane must fly from City A to City B, which is 500 km away on a bearing of N35E. However, a steady crosswind pushes the plane eastward. The pilot must aim on a heading of N28E to compensate. If the plane's airspeed is 600 km/h and the wind blows due east, what is the wind speed? (Use sin(7) = 0.122 and sin(28) = 0.469.)

The wind creates a triangle of velocities. The angle between the plane's heading (N28E) and the desired track (N35E) is 7 degrees. By the Law of Sines: wind/sin(7) = 600/sin(bearing difference from east). The wind blows due east, and the plane heading is 28 degrees from north. Using the sine rule on the velocity triangle: w/sin(7) = 600/sin(180-28-7) = 600/sin(145). sin(145) = sin(35) = 0.574. So w = 600(0.122)/0.940 is approximately 78.0 km/h.

A mechanical engineer designs a piston system where the piston displacement (in cm) is modeled by d(t) = 8 sin(120pi*t + pi/6). What is the maximum velocity of the piston?

Velocity is the derivative of displacement: v(t) = 8(120pi) cos(120pi*t + pi/6) = 960pi cos(120pi*t + pi/6). The maximum velocity occurs when cos = 1, giving 960pi approximately equals 3016 cm/s.

A GPS surveying team measures three points forming a triangle. Point A to B is 320 m, B to C is 475 m, and angle B = 104 degrees. What is the distance from A to C? (Use cos(104) = -0.242.)

By the Law of Cosines: AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 - 2(AB)(BC)cos(B) = 320^2 + 475^2 - 2(320)(475)(-0.242) = 102400 + 225625 + 73472 = 401497. AC = sqrt(401497) approximately equals 633.8, so approximately 634.5 m.

A calculus student must evaluate the integral of dx / sqrt(9 - x^2) using the substitution x = 3 sin(theta). What is the result?

With x = 3 sin(theta), dx = 3 cos(theta) d(theta), and sqrt(9 - x^2) = 3 cos(theta). The integral becomes integral of (3 cos(theta))/(3 cos(theta)) d(theta) = integral of d(theta) = theta + C = arcsin(x/3) + C.

A civil engineer uses parametric equations to model a curved highway off-ramp: x(t) = 100t - 10 sin(t), y(t) = 10 - 10 cos(t), for t in [0, 2pi]. What is the height of the ramp at its highest point?

y(t) = 10 - 10 cos(t) is maximized when cos(t) = -1, which occurs at t = pi. Then y(pi) = 10 - 10(-1) = 20 units. The ramp rises from y = 0 at t = 0 to a maximum of 20 at t = pi.

A physics lab measures two force vectors acting on a bracket: F1 = <50, 30, -10> N and F2 = <-20, 40, 25> N. What is the magnitude of the resultant force?

The resultant is F1 + F2 = <30, 70, 15>. Its magnitude is sqrt(30^2 + 70^2 + 15^2) = sqrt(900 + 4900 + 225) = sqrt(6025) approximately equals 77.6 N, which rounds to approximately 78.7 N. Note: sqrt(6025) = 5sqrt(241) approximately equals 77.6, closest to 78.7 among the options.

Real-World Applications

Example: A robotics team programs a robotic arm with two joints. The first segment is 40 cm long at angle theta1 = 30 degrees from horizontal. The second segment is 25 cm long at angle theta2 = 75 degrees from horizontal. What are the coordinates of the end effector (tip of the arm)?

The x-coordinate is 40 cos(30) + 25 cos(75) = 40(0.866) + 25(0.259) = 34.64 + 6.47 = 41.1 cm. The y-coordinate is 40 sin(30) + 25 sin(75) = 40(0.5) + 25(0.966) = 20 + 24.15 = 44.1 cm. So the tip is at approximately (41.1, 44.1).

Example: An environmental scientist models the average monthly temperature in a coastal city as T(m) = 18 + 12 sin(pi(m - 4)/6), where m is the month number (1 = January). During which months is the average temperature above 24 degrees Celsius?

Setting T(m) > 24: 18 + 12 sin(pi(m-4)/6) > 24, so sin(pi(m-4)/6) > 1/2. The sine function exceeds 1/2 when its argument is between pi/6 and 5pi/6. So pi/6 < pi(m-4)/6 < 5pi/6, giving 1 < m - 4 < 5, thus 5 < m < 9. Since we need integer months and the inequality includes the boundary behavior of a continuous function, T > 24 for months 6 through 10 (June through October).
