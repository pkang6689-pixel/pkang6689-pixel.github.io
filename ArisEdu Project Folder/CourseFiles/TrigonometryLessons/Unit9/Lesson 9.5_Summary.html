Lesson 9.5: Applications in Physics & Engineering - Summary

Key Concepts: Physics & Engineering Applications of Vectors

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A 50 kg crate sits on a ramp inclined at 30° from horizontal. What is the component of the gravitational force parallel to the ramp? (Use g = 9.8 m/s^2)

The gravitational force is mg = 50(9.8) = 490 N downward. The component parallel to the ramp is mg sin(theta) = 490 sin(30°) = 490(0.5) = 245 N down the slope.

A projectile is launched at 50 m/s at an angle of 53° above horizontal. What is its initial horizontal velocity?

v_x = v cos(theta) = 50 cos(53°) ≈ 50(0.6018) ≈ 30.1 m/s. The horizontal component uses cosine of the launch angle.

An airplane flies at 300 km/h heading north. A wind blows from the west at 50 km/h. What is the plane's ground speed?

Plane velocity: <0, 300>. Wind from west means wind blows east: <50, 0>. Resultant: <50, 300>. Speed = sqrt(2500 + 90000) = sqrt(92500) ≈ 304.1 km/h.

A bridge cable supports a load at 40° below horizontal with a tension of 1000 N. What is the vertical component of the tension?

The vertical component is T_y = 1000 sin(40°) ≈ 643 N. Since the cable hangs below horizontal at 40°, the sine gives the vertical (downward) component.

Two forces act on a bolt: F1 = 100 N at 0° and F2 = 150 N at 90°. What is the magnitude of the resultant force?

F1 = <100, 0>, F2 = <0, 150>. Resultant = <100, 150>. |R| = sqrt(10000 + 22500) = sqrt(32500) ≈ 180.3 N.

A satellite orbits Earth and its velocity vector is <4000, 3000> m/s. What is the satellite's speed?

Speed = |v| = sqrt(4000^2 + 3000^2) = sqrt(16000000 + 9000000) = sqrt(25000000) = 5000 m/s.

A crane lifts a 2000 kg steel beam. If the crane cable makes a 15° angle with the vertical, what is the tension in the cable? (Assume equilibrium, g = 9.8 m/s^2)

Weight = mg = 2000(9.8) = 19600 N. The vertical component of tension must equal the weight: T cos(15°) = 19600, so T = 19600/cos(15°) ≈ 20291 N.

What is the normal force in physics?

The normal force is the perpendicular force exerted by a surface on an object in contact with it. On a flat surface, it opposes gravity; on an inclined plane, it is perpendicular to the surface.

What is an equilibrant force?

The equilibrant force is the negative of the resultant force. When added to the system, it makes the net force zero, putting the system in static equilibrium.

What is static equilibrium?

Static equilibrium occurs when an object is at rest with zero net force (sum of all forces = 0) and zero net torque. This requires careful vector analysis of all forces acting on the object.

What is a component of a force?

A component of a force is the effective value of that force along a particular direction. For a force F at angle theta: horizontal component = F cos(theta), vertical component = F sin(theta).

What is a bearing in navigation?

A bearing is a direction measured clockwise from true north, expressed as a three-digit angle (e.g., 045° for northeast, 180° for south, 270° for west). Converting to standard angle: standard = 90° - bearing.

What is the difference between velocity and speed?

Velocity is a vector quantity with both magnitude and direction (e.g., 30 m/s north). Speed is the scalar magnitude of velocity (e.g., 30 m/s). Two objects can have the same speed but different velocities.

What is a free body diagram?

A free body diagram isolates an object and shows all external forces acting on it as vectors originating from the object. It is an essential tool for analyzing force problems and applying Newton's laws.

What is the relationship between Newton's Second Law and vectors?

Newton's Second Law F_net = ma is a vector equation. The net force vector and the acceleration vector point in the same direction. Each component equation (F_x = ma_x, F_y = ma_y) can be solved independently.

What is relative velocity?

Relative velocity is the velocity of object A as measured by observer B: v_AB = v_A - v_B (vector subtraction). For example, a boat's velocity relative to the shore includes both its own velocity and the river current.

What is the inclined plane force decomposition?

On an inclined plane, gravity (mg) is decomposed into two components: mg sin(theta) parallel to the surface (causing sliding) and mg cos(theta) perpendicular to the surface (balanced by the normal force).

A ship sails at 20 knots on bearing 060°. A current flows at 5 knots on bearing 150°. What is the ship's resultant speed over the ground?

Convert bearings: 060° bearing → 30° standard, 150° bearing → -60° standard. Ship: 20 = <10sqrt(3), 10>. Current: 5 = <2.5, -2.5sqrt(3)>. Sum ≈ <10sqrt(3)+2.5, 10-2.5sqrt(3)> ≈ <19.82, 5.67>. |R| = sqrt(392.8 + 32.1) ≈ sqrt(425) ≈ 20.6 knots.

An engineer designs a zip line. A 70 kg person hangs from the middle, creating a 10° sag below horizontal on each side. What is the tension in the cable?

Weight = 70(9.8) = 686 N. At the midpoint, two cable segments pull upward. Vertical equilibrium: 2T sin(10°) = 686, so T = 686/(2 sin(10°)) = 686/0.3472 ≈ 1976 N. Small angles mean large tension.

A ball is thrown from a 20 m high cliff at 15 m/s horizontally. What is the velocity vector just before it hits the ground? (Use g = 10 m/s^2)

Horizontal velocity is constant: v_x = 15 m/s. Time to fall: 20 = (1/2)(10)t^2, t = 2 s. Vertical velocity at impact: v_y = -gt = -10(2) = -20 m/s. The velocity vector is <15, -20> m/s.

An escalator moves at 1.5 m/s at 30° above horizontal. A person walks up the escalator at 1 m/s relative to the escalator surface. What is the person's total vertical speed?

Both velocities are along the escalator direction at 30°. Total speed along escalator = 1.5 + 1 = 2.5 m/s. Vertical component = 2.5 sin(30°) = 2.5(0.5) = 1.25 m/s upward.

Two tugboats pull a barge. Tugboat A applies 8000 N at 25° from the forward direction and Tugboat B applies 6000 N at -35° from forward. What is the forward component of the total force?

Forward component = 8000cos(25°) + 6000cos(35°) ≈ 8000(0.9063) + 6000(0.8192) ≈ 7250 + 4915 ≈ 12163 N. Both tugboats contribute positively to the forward direction.
