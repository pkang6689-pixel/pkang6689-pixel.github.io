Lesson 9.3: Dot Product & Applications - Summary

Key Concepts: The Dot Product

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is <5, -1> . <5, -1>?

v . v = 5^2 + (-1)^2 = 25 + 1 = 26. The dot product of a vector with itself equals the square of its magnitude: v . v = |v|^2.

If u . v = 0 and neither u nor v is the zero vector, what is the angle between them?

cos(theta) = (u . v)/(|u||v|) = 0/(|u||v|) = 0. So theta = arccos(0) = 90 degrees. The vectors are perpendicular (orthogonal).

If |u| = 5, |v| = 8, and the angle between them is 60°, what is u . v?

u . v = |u||v|cos(theta) = 5 * 8 * cos(60°) = 40 * 1/2 = 20.

What is the dot product of two vectors?

The dot product (or scalar product) u . v = a1*a2 + b1*b2 for 2D vectors. It produces a scalar (number), not a vector. It can also be computed as |u||v|cos(theta).

What is the scalar projection of one vector onto another?

The scalar projection of v onto u is comp_u(v) = (u . v)/|u|. It gives the signed length of v's projection along u's direction. Positive means same general direction; negative means opposite.

What is the vector projection of v onto u?

The vector projection proj_u(v) = ((u . v)/|u|^2) u is the vector component of v parallel to u. It is the scalar projection times the unit vector in the direction of u.

What is the geometric meaning of the dot product u . v = |u||v|cos(theta)?

The dot product u . v = |u||v|cos(theta) measures the extent to which u and v align. When theta = 0 (same direction), it equals the product of magnitudes; when theta = 90° (perpendicular), it is 0; when theta = 180° (opposite), it is negative.

What is work in physics as it relates to the dot product?

Work is defined as W = F . d = |F||d|cos(theta), where theta is the angle between force and displacement. Only the component of force parallel to displacement does work.

What is the relationship between the dot product and the angle between two vectors?

The angle theta between vectors u and v satisfies cos(theta) = (u . v)/(|u| |v|). This formula, derived from the dot product definition, allows us to find the angle between any two nonzero vectors.

What is the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality for vectors?

The Cauchy-Schwarz inequality states |u . v| <= |u| |v|. Since cos(theta) ranges from -1 to 1, the absolute value of the dot product cannot exceed the product of magnitudes. Equality holds when vectors are parallel.

A physicist determines two velocity vectors: v1 = <5, 2> m/s and v2 = <-1, 3> m/s. What is the angle between them?

v1 . v2 = -5 + 6 = 1. |v1| = sqrt(29), |v2| = sqrt(10). cos(theta) = 1/(sqrt(29)*sqrt(10)) = 1/sqrt(290) ≈ 0.0587. theta = arccos(0.0587) ≈ 86.6°.

An inclined plane has a slope vector s = <4, 3> (horizontal 4, vertical 3). A gravity force on a block is F = <0, -10> N. What is the component of gravity along the slope?

Scalar projection of F onto s: comp_s(F) = (F . s)/|s| = (0*4 + (-10)*3)/sqrt(16+9) = -30/5 = -6 N. The negative sign indicates the component is in the direction opposite to s (down the slope).

A GPS system tracks an object at displacement d1 = <3, 4> km from a base station, then it moves to d2 = <7, 1> km. The angle between the two position vectors is needed. What is it?

d1 . d2 = 21 + 4 = 25. |d1| = 5, |d2| = sqrt(49+1) = sqrt(50) = 5sqrt(2). cos(theta) = 25/(5*5sqrt(2)) = 25/(25sqrt(2)) = 1/sqrt(2). theta = arccos(1/sqrt(2)) = 45°.
