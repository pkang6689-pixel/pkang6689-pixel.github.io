Lesson 9.1: Vector Basics (magnitude, direction) - Summary

Key Concepts: Introduction to Vectors

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A vector has magnitude 10 and direction angle 60 degrees from the positive x-axis. What is its horizontal component?

The horizontal component is v_x = |v| cos(theta) = 10 cos(60°) = 10(1/2) = 5. The cosine function gives the x-component of a vector from its magnitude and direction angle.

What is the magnitude of the vector <3, 4>?

The magnitude is |v| = sqrt(3^2 + 4^2) = sqrt(9 + 16) = sqrt(25) = 5. This follows from the Pythagorean theorem.

What is the direction angle of the vector <1, sqrt(3)>?

theta = arctan(sqrt(3)/1) = arctan(sqrt(3)) = 60 degrees. Since both components are positive, the vector is in Quadrant I and no adjustment is needed.

A vector goes from point A(1, 2) to point B(4, 6). What is the component form of vector AB?

The component form is found by subtracting the initial point from the terminal point: AB = <4-1, 6-2> = <3, 4>.

What is the unit vector in the direction of <6, 8>?

First find the magnitude: |v| = sqrt(36 + 64) = 10. The unit vector is v/|v| = <6/10, 8/10> = <3/5, 4/5>. Note that <0.6, 0.8> is the same as <3/5, 4/5>, but the exact fractional form is preferred.

A vector has magnitude 8 and direction angle 150 degrees. What is the component form?

v_x = 8 cos(150°) = 8(-sqrt(3)/2) = -4sqrt(3) and v_y = 8 sin(150°) = 8(1/2) = 4. At 150 degrees the vector points into Quadrant II (negative x, positive y).

If vector u = <2, -3> and vector v = <-1, 5>, what is u + v?

Vector addition is performed component-wise: u + v = <2+(-1), -3+5> = <1, 2>.

What is 3 times the vector <-2, 5>?

Scalar multiplication multiplies each component: 3<-2, 5> = <3(-2), 3(5)> = <-6, 15>.

What is a vector?

A vector is a mathematical quantity that has both magnitude (size/length) and direction. Examples include force, velocity, and displacement, which cannot be fully described by a single number.

What is a scalar quantity?

A scalar is a quantity described by a single real number representing magnitude only, with no direction. Examples include temperature, mass, speed, and distance.

What is a unit vector?

A unit vector is any vector whose magnitude is exactly 1. It is used to specify direction only. Any nonzero vector v can be converted to a unit vector by dividing by its magnitude: v-hat = v/|v|.

What is the zero vector?

The zero vector 0 = <0, 0> is the unique vector with magnitude 0. It has no defined direction and acts as the additive identity: v + 0 = v for any vector v.

What is the component form of a vector?

The component form of a 2D vector writes it as where a is the horizontal (x) component and b is the vertical (y) component. This fully specifies the vector's magnitude and direction.

What is the resultant of two vectors?

The resultant of two vectors is their vector sum, which is the single vector that produces the same effect as applying both vectors together. Graphically, it is found using the parallelogram law or tip-to-tail method.

What is the direction angle of a vector?

The direction angle theta of a vector is measured counterclockwise from the positive x-axis to the vector. For v = , theta = arctan(b/a), adjusted for the correct quadrant.

What is the parallelogram law for vector addition?

The parallelogram law states that if two vectors share the same initial point, their sum is the diagonal of the parallelogram formed by the two vectors as adjacent sides. This gives the same result as the tip-to-tail method.

A pilot flies a plane at 300 mph heading due north, but a crosswind blows at 40 mph due east. What is the magnitude of the plane's resultant velocity?

The plane's velocity is <0, 300> and the wind is <40, 0>. Resultant = <40, 300>. Magnitude = sqrt(40^2 + 300^2) = sqrt(1600 + 90000) = sqrt(91600) ≈ 302.7 mph. The wind slightly increases the speed while pushing the plane off course.

Two tugboats pull a barge. Tugboat A exerts a force of 5000 N at 30 degrees from the forward direction, and Tugboat B exerts 5000 N at -30 degrees. What is the resultant force?

Tugboat A: <5000cos(30°), 5000sin(30°)> = <2500sqrt(3), 2500>. Tugboat B: <5000cos(-30°), 5000sin(-30°)> = <2500sqrt(3), -2500>. Sum = <5000sqrt(3), 0>. The lateral forces cancel, giving 5000sqrt(3) ≈ 8660 N straight forward.

A hiker walks 4 km east, then 3 km at a bearing of N30°E (60° from east). What is the magnitude of her total displacement?

First leg: <4, 0>. Second leg: <3cos(60°), 3sin(60°)> = <1.5, 1.5sqrt(3)>. Total: <5.5, 1.5sqrt(3)>. Magnitude = sqrt(5.5^2 + (1.5sqrt(3))^2) = sqrt(30.25 + 6.75) = sqrt(37) ≈ 6.08 km. Actually more precisely: sqrt((4+1.5)^2 + (1.5sqrt(3))^2) = sqrt(30.25 + 6.75) = sqrt(37) ≈ 6.08 km.

An object is suspended by two cables making angles of 45° and 60° with the horizontal. If the object weighs 100 N, what is the tension T1 in the cable at 45°? (Use the equilibrium condition: T1 sin(45°) + T2 sin(60°) = 100 and T1 cos(45°) = T2 cos(60°))

From T1 cos(45°) = T2 cos(60°): T2 = T1 cos(45°)/cos(60°) = T1(sqrt(2)/2)/(1/2) = T1 sqrt(2). Substituting: T1(sqrt(2)/2) + T1 sqrt(2)(sqrt(3)/2) = 100, so T1 sqrt(2)(1 + sqrt(3))/2 = 100. T1 = 200/(sqrt(2)(1+sqrt(3))) ≈ 200/(2.732*1.414) ≈ 51.8 N.

A car accelerates at 5 m/s^2 at 20° above the horizontal up a ramp. What is the horizontal component of the acceleration?

The horizontal component of acceleration is a_x = 5 cos(20°) ≈ 5(0.9397) ≈ 4.70 m/s^2. The cosine gives the component along the horizontal direction.

A sailboat experiences a wind force of 200 N at 120° and a current force of 150 N at 210° (both measured from east). What is the x-component of the resultant force?

Wind x: 200 cos(120°) = 200(-1/2) = -100 N. Current x: 150 cos(210°) = 150(-sqrt(3)/2) = -75sqrt(3) ≈ -129.9 N. Total x-component = -100 + (-75sqrt(3)) ≈ -229.9 N.

A ball is kicked with an initial velocity of 20 m/s at 45° above horizontal. Ignoring air resistance, what is the initial vertical component of velocity?

v_y = 20 sin(45°) = 20(sqrt(2)/2) = 10sqrt(2) ≈ 14.14 m/s. At 45 degrees, both the horizontal and vertical components are equal.

A rescue helicopter lowers a stretcher at an angle. The cable tension is 800 N at 15° from vertical. What is the horizontal force component pulling the stretcher sideways?

Since the angle is measured from vertical, the horizontal component uses sine: F_h = 800 sin(15°) ≈ 800(0.2588) ≈ 207.1 N. The vertical component would be 800 cos(15°) ≈ 772.7 N.

A GPS system tracks a delivery drone that flies from a warehouse at the origin to point A at <3, 4> km, then from A to point B with displacement <-1, 2> km. What is the drone's total displacement from the warehouse and its distance?

Total displacement = <3, 4> + <-1, 2> = <2, 6>. Distance = |<2, 6>| = sqrt(4 + 36) = sqrt(40) = 2sqrt(10) ≈ 6.32 km. The total distance traveled along the path is longer, but displacement measures the straight-line distance.
