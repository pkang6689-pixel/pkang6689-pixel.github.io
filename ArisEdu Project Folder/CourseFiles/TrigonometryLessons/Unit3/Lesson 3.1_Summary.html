Lesson 3.1: Definition & Domains - Summary

Key Concepts: Definition & Domains of Inverse Trig Functions

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A surveyor measures that sin θ = √3/2 for an elevation angle. Using arcsin, what is θ (in radians, principal value)?

arcsin(√3/2) = π/3, since sin(π/3) = √3/2 and π/3 is in [−π/2, π/2].

A physics student needs arccos(0). This gives the angle where the cosine is zero. What is the principal value?

arccos(0) = π/2, since cos(π/2) = 0 and π/2 is in [0, π].

A GPS device computes arctan(−√3) to determine a bearing angle. What is the result?

arctan(−√3) = −π/3, since tan(−π/3) = −√3 and −π/3 is in (−π/2, π/2).

A game developer programs a character to face a target. The target is at relative coordinates (5, 5). What is arctan(5/5) in degrees?

arctan(5/5) = arctan(1) = π/4 = 45°.

A cable car rises 100 m vertically while traveling 100√3 m horizontally. What is the angle of ascent using arctan?

arctan(100/(100√3)) = arctan(1/√3) = π/6 = 30°.
