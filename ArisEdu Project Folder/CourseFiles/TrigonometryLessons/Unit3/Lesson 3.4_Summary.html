Lesson 3.4: Applications in Geometry & Physics - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Geometry & Physics

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A ramp must reach a loading dock 2 m high, and the ramp surface is 4 m long. What is the angle of inclination?

sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 2/4 = 1/2 → θ = arcsin(1/2) = 30°.

A ball is thrown with initial speed 20 m/s. For maximum range, the launch angle should be arctan(1). What is this angle?

arctan(1) = 45°. Maximum projectile range occurs at 45°.

A pilot descends from 10,000 ft to land, covering 30,000 ft of horizontal distance. What is the angle of descent?

tan θ = 10000/30000 = 1/3 → θ = arctan(1/3) ≈ 18.4°.

A car drives up a hill with a 12% grade (rise/run = 0.12). What is the incline angle?

Grade = rise/run = tan θ → θ = arctan(0.12) ≈ 6.8°.

A pendulum swings to a height where its string makes angle θ with the vertical. If the bob moves 0.5 m horizontally and the string is 1 m long, what is θ?

sin θ = horizontal/string = 0.5/1 = 0.5 → θ = arcsin(0.5) = 30°.

A security camera can see from the floor to a point 8 m up a wall, mounted 6 m away. What is the maximum angle of elevation the camera covers?

tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 8/6 = 4/3 → θ = arctan(4/3) ≈ 53.1°.

A satellite orbits at 400 km altitude. An observer on Earth (radius 6371 km) can see the satellite at angle θ above the horizon. If cos θ = 6371/6771, what is the viewing angle approximately?

θ = arccos(6371/6771) = arccos(0.9409) ≈ 19.8°.

A trebuchet launches a stone at 30 m/s. To hit a target at the same height 75 m away, the launch angle θ satisfies sin(2θ) = 75(9.8)/900. What is 2θ approximately, given sin(2θ) ≈ 0.817?

sin(2θ) = 0.817 → 2θ = arcsin(0.817) ≈ 54.8°.
