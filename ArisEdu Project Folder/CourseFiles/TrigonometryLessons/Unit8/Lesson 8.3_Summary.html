Lesson 8.3: Converting Between Polar & Rectangular Coordinates - Summary

Key Concepts: Converting Polar & Rectangular Coordinates

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is r^2 equal to in terms of rectangular coordinates?

By the Pythagorean theorem applied to the right triangle formed by the point (x, y), the origin, and the x-axis projection, r^2 = x^2 + y^2.

What is the rectangular coordinate system also commonly called?

The rectangular coordinate system is also known as the Cartesian coordinate system, named after Rene Descartes, who developed it using perpendicular x and y axes.

What is the pole in the polar coordinate system?

The pole is the fixed reference point in the polar coordinate system, equivalent to the origin in rectangular coordinates. It is the point where r = 0 regardless of theta.

What is the meaning of a negative r-value in polar coordinates?

When r is negative, the point is plotted at a distance |r| from the pole but in the direction opposite to that given by theta. Essentially, (-r, theta) is the same as (r, theta + pi).

What is meant by 'multiple representations' of a point in polar coordinates?

Unlike rectangular coordinates which are unique, every point in the polar plane can be represented by infinitely many polar pairs, since adding 2pi to theta or using a negative r with an adjusted angle gives the same point.

What is the reference angle in the context of rectangular-to-polar conversion?

The reference angle is the positive acute angle between the terminal side of the angle and the nearest portion of the x-axis. It is used along with quadrant information to determine the correct theta when converting from rectangular to polar.

What is the term for the process of rewriting a polar equation using x and y variables?

Converting to rectangular form means rewriting a polar equation (in terms of r and theta) using the substitutions x = r cos(theta), y = r sin(theta), and r^2 = x^2 + y^2 to produce an equivalent equation in x and y.

An engineer describes a machine part's cross-section with the polar equation r = 6 cos(theta). To manufacture it, the CNC machine requires rectangular coordinates. What is the rectangular equation?

Multiply both sides by r: r^2 = 6r cos(theta). Since r^2 = x^2 + y^2 and r cos(theta) = x, this gives x^2 + y^2 = 6x, which is a circle of radius 3 centered at (3, 0).

A ship at position (5, 5sqrt(3)) km relative to a lighthouse must radio its polar bearing. What is the ship's distance from the lighthouse and angle from east?

r = sqrt(25 + 75) = sqrt(100) = 10. theta = arctan(5sqrt(3)/5) = arctan(sqrt(3)) = pi/3. The ship is 10 km from the lighthouse at an angle of pi/3 from east.

A satellite's orbit around a planet is modeled by the polar equation r = 10/(2 + cos(theta)). A ground station converts this to rectangular form for display. What is the rectangular equation?

Starting with r(2 + cos(theta)) = 10: 2r + r cos(theta) = 10, so 2r + x = 10, giving 2r = 10 - x, so 4r^2 = (10 - x)^2. Since r^2 = x^2 + y^2: 4(x^2 + y^2) = 100 - 20x + x^2, which simplifies to 3x^2 + 4y^2 + 20x - 100 = 0.

A weather station tracks a storm moving along the line y = -x + 4. To input this into a polar-based tracking system, what is the equivalent polar equation?

Substituting x = r cos(theta) and y = r sin(theta) into y = -x + 4: r sin(theta) = -r cos(theta) + 4, so r sin(theta) + r cos(theta) = 4, giving r = 4/(sin(theta) + cos(theta)).

An architect uses the polar equation r = 4 sin(theta) to design a circular window. What is the center and radius of this circle in rectangular coordinates?

Multiply both sides by r: r^2 = 4r sin(theta), so x^2 + y^2 = 4y. Completing the square: x^2 + (y - 2)^2 = 4. This is a circle with center (0, 2) and radius 2.
