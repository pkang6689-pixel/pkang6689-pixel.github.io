Lesson 8.6: Applications in Electrical Engineering - Summary

Key Concepts: Electrical Engineering Applications

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

An AC circuit has a voltage phasor V = 120 cis(pi/3) volts. What is the peak voltage (modulus)?

The modulus of the phasor V = 120 cis(pi/3) is 120, which represents the peak (or amplitude) of the voltage waveform. The angle pi/3 represents the phase shift.

If the impedance of a resistor is Z_R = 50 ohms (purely real) and the impedance of an inductor is Z_L = 30i ohms, what is the total series impedance?

In a series circuit, impedances add: Z_total = Z_R + Z_L = 50 + 30i ohms. The real part (50) is the resistance and the imaginary part (30) is the inductive reactance.

Using Ohm's law for AC circuits (V = IZ), if I = 2 cis(0) amps and Z = 5 cis(pi/6) ohms, what is V?

V = IZ = 2 cis(0) * 5 cis(pi/6) = 10 cis(0 + pi/6) = 10 cis(pi/6) volts. The moduli multiply and the arguments add.

A capacitor has impedance Z_C = -20i ohms. What is the phase angle of this impedance?

Z_C = -20i = 20 cis(-pi/2). The phase angle is -pi/2 (or -90 degrees). Capacitive impedance always has a phase angle of -pi/2, meaning current leads voltage by 90 degrees.

The current in a circuit is I = 3 - 4i amps. What is the magnitude of the current?

|I| = sqrt(3^2 + (-4)^2) = sqrt(9 + 16) = sqrt(25) = 5 amps. The magnitude of the complex current gives the peak current value.

Two impedances Z1 = 6 + 8i and Z2 = 6 - 8i are connected in series. What is the total impedance?

Z_total = Z1 + Z2 = (6 + 8i) + (6 - 8i) = 12 + 0i = 12 ohms. The reactive parts cancel, leaving a purely resistive impedance. This occurs when inductive and capacitive reactances are equal.

What is the power factor of a circuit with impedance Z = 4 + 3i ohms?

The power factor is cos(phi) where phi is the phase angle. phi = arctan(3/4), and cos(arctan(3/4)) = 4/5 = 0.8. Alternatively, power factor = R/|Z| = 4/sqrt(16+9) = 4/5.

If V = 100 cis(pi/4) volts and Z = 25 cis(pi/12) ohms, what is the current I = V/Z?

I = V/Z = (100/25) cis(pi/4 - pi/12) = 4 cis(3pi/12 - pi/12) = 4 cis(2pi/12) = 4 cis(pi/6) amps.

What is impedance in an AC circuit?

Impedance (Z) is the total opposition to alternating current in a circuit. It is a complex quantity Z = R + Xi where R is resistance (real part) and X is reactance (imaginary part), measured in ohms.

What is reactance?

Reactance is the imaginary part of impedance, arising from capacitors (capacitive reactance, negative) and inductors (inductive reactance, positive). It represents energy storage rather than energy dissipation.

What is a phasor?

A phasor is a complex number (or rotating vector) that represents a sinusoidal signal's amplitude and phase. Using phasors converts differential equations of AC circuits into simpler algebraic equations involving complex numbers.

What is the power factor of an AC circuit?

The power factor is cos(phi), where phi is the phase angle between the voltage and current phasors. It equals the ratio of real (active) power to apparent power and ranges from 0 to 1.

What is Ohm's law in phasor form for AC circuits?

In phasor form, Ohm's law is V = IZ, where V is the voltage phasor, I is the current phasor, and Z is the complex impedance. This is analogous to V = IR in DC circuits but uses complex arithmetic.

What is apparent power in an AC circuit?

Apparent power is the magnitude of complex power S = V * I*, equal to |V| * |I|, measured in volt-amperes (VA). It combines both real power (watts) and reactive power (VAR) as |S| = sqrt(P^2 + Q^2).

What is the formula for capacitive reactance?

Capacitive reactance is X_C = 1/(omega * C), where omega is the angular frequency and C is the capacitance. The impedance of a capacitor is Z_C = -jX_C = 1/(j*omega*C) = -j/(omega*C).

What is complex power in circuit analysis?

Complex power is S = V * I* (voltage phasor times the conjugate of the current phasor). It equals P + jQ, where P is real power (watts) and Q is reactive power (VARs).

An electrical engineer designs a circuit with a 100-ohm resistor in series with a 50-ohm inductive reactance. If the source voltage is 250 cis(0) volts, what is the current?

Z = 100 + 50i. I = V/Z = 250/(100 + 50i). Multiplying by the conjugate: 250(100 - 50i)/(10000 + 2500) = 250(100 - 50i)/12500 = (100 - 50i)/50 = 2 - i amps.

A power plant delivers electricity at V = 10000 cis(0) volts through a transmission line with impedance Z_line = 5 + 2i ohms to a load Z_load = 95 + 48i ohms. What is the current flowing through the circuit?

Z_total = Z_line + Z_load = (5+2i) + (95+48i) = 100 + 50i. I = 10000/(100+50i) = 10000(100-50i)/12500 = 80 - 40i amps. |I| = sqrt(6400+1600) = sqrt(8000) = 40sqrt(5) ≈ 89.4 amps.

A factory has a power factor of 0.6 lagging. The plant manager installs capacitors to improve it to 0.9. If the original impedance angle was arccos(0.6), what is the new impedance angle?

The new impedance angle is arccos(0.9) ≈ 25.84 degrees. The original angle was arccos(0.6) ≈ 53.13 degrees. By adding capacitance, the phase angle was reduced from 53.13° to 25.84°, improving efficiency.

An electronics technician measures V = 12 + 5i volts across a component and current I = 3 + i amps through it. What is the impedance of the component?

Z = V/I = (12+5i)/(3+i). Multiplying by conjugate: (12+5i)(3-i)/((3+i)(3-i)) = (36-12i+15i-5i^2)/(9+1) = (36+3i+5)/10 = (41+3i)/10 = 4.1 + 0.3i ohms.

A three-phase power system has phase voltages V_A = 240 cis(0), V_B = 240 cis(2pi/3), and V_C = 240 cis(4pi/3). What is V_A + V_B + V_C?

In a balanced three-phase system, the three phasors are equally spaced by 120° (2pi/3). Their sum is always zero because they form a closed triangle (or equivalently, the sum of the three cube roots of unity times 240 is zero).

An audio amplifier circuit has two stages with transfer functions H1 = 5 cis(pi/6) and H2 = 3 cis(-pi/12). What is the overall transfer function H = H1 * H2?

H = H1 * H2: modulus = 5 * 3 = 15, argument = pi/6 + (-pi/12) = 2pi/12 - pi/12 = pi/12. The overall transfer function is 15 cis(pi/12), giving a gain of 15 and phase shift of pi/12.

A telecommunications engineer analyzes a signal with voltage V = 20 cis(pi/3) across an impedance Z = 4 + 3i. She needs the complex power S = V * I*. What is S?

S = V * I* = V * (V/Z)* = V * V*/(Z*) = |V|^2/Z*. |V|^2 = 400 and Z* = 4 - 3i. S = 400/(4-3i) = 400(4+3i)/25 = 16(4+3i) = 64 + 48i VA. So P = 64 W and Q = 48 VAR.

Important Concepts

An engineer is designing a bandpass filter centered at the resonant frequency. If L = 0.1 H and C = 10 microfarads, at what angular frequency does resonance occur (where X_L = X_C)

Resonance occurs when omega*L = 1/(omega*C), giving omega^2 = 1/(LC) = 1/(0.1 * 10 * 10^(-6)) = 1/(10^(-6)) = 10^6. So omega = 1000 rad/s.
