Lesson 8.4: Complex Numbers in Polar Form - Summary

Key Concepts: Complex Numbers in Polar Form

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the argument of the complex number -2i?

The complex number -2i has real part 0 and imaginary part -2, placing it on the negative imaginary axis. The argument is 3pi/2 (or equivalently -pi/2).

What is the product of moduli when multiplying 5 cis(30°) and 4 cis(60°)?

When multiplying complex numbers in polar form, the moduli are multiplied: 5 * 4 = 20. The resulting product has modulus 20 and argument 30° + 60° = 90°.

What is the polar form of the complex number 5 (a real number)?

The real number 5 lies on the positive real axis, so its modulus is 5 and its argument is 0. The polar form is 5(cos(0) + i sin(0)). Note that cos(2pi) + i sin(2pi) also equals 1, but the principal argument is 0.

If z = 6(cos(pi/4) + i sin(pi/4)), what is |z|^2?

The modulus is |z| = 6, so |z|^2 = 36. The modulus squared of a complex number z = a + bi is a^2 + b^2, which equals r^2 in polar form.

What is the modulus (or absolute value) of a complex number z = a + bi?

The modulus (or absolute value) of z = a + bi is |z| = sqrt(a^2 + b^2). It represents the distance from the origin to the point (a, b) when the complex number is plotted in the complex plane.

What is the argument of a complex number?

The argument (arg) of a complex number is the angle theta measured counterclockwise from the positive real axis to the line segment from the origin to the point representing the complex number in the complex plane.

What is the trigonometric form (polar form) of a complex number?

The trigonometric (polar) form of a complex number is z = r(cos(theta) + i sin(theta)), where r = |z| is the modulus and theta = arg(z) is the argument. This form is especially useful for multiplication, division, and powers.

What is the complex conjugate of z = a + bi?

The complex conjugate of z = a + bi is z-bar = a - bi. It is obtained by negating the imaginary part while keeping the real part unchanged. In polar form, conjugation negates the argument.

What is the complex plane?

The complex plane (also called the Argand plane) is a two-dimensional plane where the horizontal axis is the real axis and the vertical axis is the imaginary axis. Each complex number a + bi corresponds to the point (a, b).

What is the relationship between a complex number z and its conjugate z-bar in terms of modulus?

A complex number and its conjugate have the same modulus: |z-bar| = |z|. If z = r(cos(theta) + i sin(theta)), then z-bar = r(cos(-theta) + i sin(-theta)), and both have modulus r.

An electrical engineer represents an AC voltage as V = 120 + 120sqrt(3)i volts. To analyze the circuit using phasor methods, she needs the polar form. What is the voltage in polar form?

r = sqrt(120^2 + (120sqrt(3))^2) = sqrt(14400 + 43200) = sqrt(57600) = 240. theta = arctan(120sqrt(3)/120) = arctan(sqrt(3)) = pi/3. The polar form is 240(cos(pi/3) + i sin(pi/3)).

Two signal generators produce waves represented by z1 = 3 cis(pi/4) and z2 = 4 cis(pi/12). The combined signal is found by multiplying these complex amplitudes. What is the resulting signal?

Multiplying polar forms: r = 3 * 4 = 12 and theta = pi/4 + pi/12 = 3pi/12 + pi/12 = 4pi/12 = pi/3. The resulting signal is 12 cis(pi/3).

A physics student calculates the impedance of a circuit as Z_total = Z1/Z2, where Z1 = 20 cis(pi/2) ohms and Z2 = 5 cis(pi/6) ohms. What is Z_total?

Dividing polar forms: r = 20/5 = 4 and theta = pi/2 - pi/6 = 3pi/6 - pi/6 = 2pi/6 = pi/3. The total impedance is 4 cis(pi/3) ohms.

A quantum mechanics problem requires finding the square of the wave function amplitude z = -3 + 4i. What is |z|^2?

|z|^2 = (-3)^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25. In quantum mechanics, |z|^2 represents the probability density, which is always a real, non-negative number.

A navigation system combines two displacement vectors as complex numbers: z1 = 8(cos(pi/6) + i sin(pi/6)) and z2 = 6(cos(2pi/3) + i sin(2pi/3)). First, convert both to rectangular form and add. What is z1 + z2 in rectangular form?

z1 = 8(sqrt(3)/2 + i/2) = 4sqrt(3) + 4i. z2 = 6(-1/2 + i*sqrt(3)/2) = -3 + 3sqrt(3)i. Adding: z1 + z2 = (4sqrt(3) - 3) + (4 + 3sqrt(3))i.

A mechanical engineer models vibrations using the complex number z = 10 cis(5pi/4). The machine's display requires rectangular coordinates. What is the rectangular form?

z = 10(cos(5pi/4) + i sin(5pi/4)) = 10(-sqrt(2)/2 + i(-sqrt(2)/2)) = -5sqrt(2) - 5sqrt(2)i. The angle 5pi/4 is in Quadrant III, so both components are negative.

A control systems engineer needs to find the gain of a transfer function, which is the modulus of H = (6 cis(pi/3))/(2 cis(pi/6)). What is the gain?

The gain is the modulus of H, which equals |z1|/|z2| = 6/2 = 3. When dividing complex numbers in polar form, the modulus of the quotient is the ratio of the individual moduli.

A drone receives instructions to move to position z1 = 5 + 5i, then from there to an additional displacement of z2 = -3 + 4i. What is the drone's final position as a complex number, and what is its distance from the origin?

z1 + z2 = (5 + (-3)) + (5 + 4)i = 2 + 9i. The distance from the origin is |2 + 9i| = sqrt(4 + 81) = sqrt(85).

A radio tower broadcasts a signal whose voltage phasor is V = 50(cos(2pi/3) + i sin(2pi/3)) and the current phasor is I = 10(cos(pi/3) + i sin(pi/3)). The complex power is S = V * I-bar (conjugate of I). What is S?

I-bar = 10 cis(-pi/3). So S = V * I-bar = 50 * 10 cis(2pi/3 + (-pi/3)) = 500 cis(2pi/3 - pi/3) = 500 cis(pi/3). This represents the complex power with real power 250 W and reactive power 250sqrt(3) VAR.

Real-World Applications

Example: Multiply z1 = 2(cos(pi/3) + i sin(pi/3)) and z2 = 3(cos(pi/6) + i sin(pi/6)) using polar form.

When multiplying complex numbers in polar form, multiply the moduli and add the arguments: r = 2 * 3 = 6 and theta = pi/3 + pi/6 = pi/2. The product is 6(cos(pi/2) + i sin(pi/2)) = 6i.
