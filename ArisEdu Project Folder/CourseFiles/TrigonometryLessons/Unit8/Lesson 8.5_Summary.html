Lesson 8.5: De Moivre's Theorem - Summary

Key Concepts: De Moivre's Theorem

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is (1 + i)^4 using De Moivre's Theorem?

First convert: 1 + i = sqrt(2) cis(pi/4). Then (sqrt(2))^4 cis(4 * pi/4) = 4 cis(pi) = 4(cos(pi) + i sin(pi)) = 4(-1 + 0i) = -4.

What is the meaning of 'nth roots of unity'?

The nth roots of unity are the n complex numbers z satisfying z^n = 1. They are equally spaced around the unit circle and given by cis(2k*pi/n) for k = 0, 1, ..., n-1.

What is a primitive nth root of unity?

A primitive nth root of unity is a root omega such that omega, omega^2, ..., omega^n = 1 gives all n distinct nth roots of unity. The root cis(2pi/n) is always a primitive nth root of unity.

What is the geometric relationship among the nth roots of a complex number in the complex plane?

The nth roots of any complex number are equally spaced around a circle of radius |z|^(1/n) centered at the origin. Adjacent roots are separated by an angle of 2pi/n.

What is the relationship between De Moivre's Theorem and Euler's formula?

De Moivre's Theorem can be derived from Euler's formula: (e^(i*theta))^n = e^(i*n*theta), which gives (cos(theta) + i sin(theta))^n = cos(n*theta) + i sin(n*theta).

What is the term for the process of finding all z such that z^n = w for a given complex number w?

Finding all complex numbers z such that z^n = w is called finding the nth roots of w. De Moivre's Theorem provides a systematic way to compute all n distinct nth roots.

What is the sum of all nth roots of unity?

The sum of all nth roots of unity is always 0 (for n >= 2). This follows from the fact that they are equally spaced around the unit circle and their vector sum cancels out, or from the formula for the sum of a geometric series.

What is the product of all nth roots of unity?

The product of all nth roots of unity equals (-1)^(n+1). For odd n, the product is 1; for even n, the product is -1. This can be derived from the constant term of the polynomial z^n - 1.

An electrical engineer needs to compute the impedance Z^5 where Z = 2(cos(pi/10) + i sin(pi/10)) ohms. Using De Moivre's Theorem, what is Z^5?

By De Moivre's Theorem: Z^5 = 2^5 cis(5 * pi/10) = 32 cis(pi/2) = 32(0 + i) = 32i ohms. The modulus is raised to the 5th power and the argument is multiplied by 5.

A physicist studying wave interference needs to compute (1 + i)^8. What is the result?

1 + i = sqrt(2) cis(pi/4). By De Moivre's: (sqrt(2))^8 cis(8 * pi/4) = (sqrt(2))^8 cis(2pi) = 16 * 1 = 16. Note (sqrt(2))^8 = (2^(1/2))^8 = 2^4 = 16.

A cryptography algorithm requires computing z^12 where z = cos(pi/6) + i sin(pi/6). What is the result?

By De Moivre's Theorem with r = 1: cis(12 * pi/6) = cis(2pi) = cos(2pi) + i sin(2pi) = 1 + 0i = 1. The number z = cis(pi/6) is a 12th root of unity.

A structural engineer analyzes stress using the formula sigma = (1 - sqrt(3)i)^3. What is the value of sigma?

1 - sqrt(3)i has modulus sqrt(1 + 3) = 2 and argument -pi/3 (or 5pi/3). By De Moivre's: 2^3 cis(3 * (-pi/3)) = 8 cis(-pi) = 8(-1) = -8.

A quantum computing researcher needs the 8th roots of unity to define quantum gates. What is the primitive 8th root of unity with the smallest positive argument?

The primitive 8th root of unity with smallest positive argument is cis(2pi/8) = cis(pi/4) = cos(pi/4) + i sin(pi/4) = sqrt(2)/2 + (sqrt(2)/2)i. Its powers generate all eight 8th roots of unity.

A mathematician uses De Moivre's Theorem to derive a triple angle identity. Expanding (cos(theta) + i sin(theta))^3 and comparing real parts, what is cos(3*theta)?

Expanding (cos(theta) + i sin(theta))^3 using the binomial theorem gives cos^3(theta) + 3i cos^2(theta)sin(theta) - 3cos(theta)sin^2(theta) - i sin^3(theta). The real part is cos^3(theta) - 3cos(theta)sin^2(theta), which equals cos(3*theta) by De Moivre's Theorem. Note: this can be simplified to 4cos^3(theta) - 3cos(theta) using sin^2(theta) = 1 - cos^2(theta).
