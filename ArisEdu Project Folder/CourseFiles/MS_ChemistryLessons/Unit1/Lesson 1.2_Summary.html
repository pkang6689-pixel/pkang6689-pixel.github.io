Lesson 1.2: Phase Changes

Phase Changes

Phase changes occur when matter changes from one state (solid, liquid, gas) to another. This happens when energy (heat) is added or removed.

Heat Added (Endothermic)

  • Melting: Solid → Liquid. Particles gain energy and break out of fixed positions.
  • Vaporization: Liquid → Gas. Includes boiling and evaporation. Particles move fast enough to escape completely.
  • Sublimation: Solid → Gas. Skips the liquid phase (e.g., Dry Ice).

Heat Removed (Exothermic)

  • Freezing: Liquid → Solid. Particles lose energy and lock into place.
  • Condensation: Gas → Liquid. Particles slow down and clump together (e.g., dew on grass).
  • Deposition: Gas → Solid. Skips the liquid phase (e.g., Frost).

Key Concept

During a phase change, the temperature remains constant. The energy is used to change the arrangement of particles, not to increase their speed (temperature). Temperature only rises again after the phase change is complete.

Study Tip

Remember: Melting and Freezing happen at the same temperature (0°C for water). Boiling and Condensing also happen at the same temperature (100°C for water).
