Lesson 3.8: AP Prep: Kepler's Laws - Summary

Key Concepts: AP Prep: Kepler's Laws

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

For a planet with a semi-major axis of 9 AU, what is its orbital period (using T² = a³)?

T² = 9³ = 729, so T = √729 = 27 years.

What is an 'ellipse'?

An ellipse is the geometric shape of all planetary orbits — a stretched circle with two focal points.

What is a 'focus' of an ellipse?

The two foci define the ellipse's shape. In a planetary orbit, the star occupies one focus; the other is empty.

What is 'eccentricity'?

Eccentricity e = c/a, where c is the distance from center to focus and a is the semi-major axis.

What is 'aphelion'?

At aphelion, a planet is at its maximum distance from the Sun and moves at its minimum orbital speed.

What is the 'areal velocity'?

Kepler's Second Law states that areal velocity (dA/dt) is constant throughout the orbit.

What is the 'harmonic law'?

The harmonic law relates orbital period (T) to semi-major axis (a), providing a universal relationship.

What is 'angular momentum' in orbit?

Conservation of angular momentum in a central force field (gravity) directly produces Kepler's Second Law.

What is a 'conic section'?

Orbits under gravity are always conic sections. Bound orbits are ellipses (or circles); unbound paths are parabolas or hyperbolas.

What is 'orbital mechanics'?

Orbital mechanics (celestial mechanics) uses Kepler's and Newton's laws to predict and plan spacecraft trajectories.

What is a 'Hohmann transfer orbit'?

A Hohmann transfer uses two engine burns to move between orbits along an ellipse tangent to both — the most fuel-efficient two-impulse method.

A student measures that a planet's period is 8 years. Using Kepler's Third Law, what is its semi-major axis?

T² = a³ → 8² = 64 → a = ∛64 = 4 AU.

Two planets orbit the same star. Planet A is at 1 AU with period 1 year. Planet B is at 8 AU. What is Planet B's period?

T² = a³ = 8³ = 512, so T = √512 ≈ 22.6 years.
