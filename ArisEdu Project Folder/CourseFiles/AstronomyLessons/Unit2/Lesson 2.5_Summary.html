Lesson 2.5: Tides & Gravitational Effects - Summary

Key Concepts: Tides & Gravitational Effects

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'spring tide'?

Spring tides (from 'springen,' to leap) produce the greatest tidal range, occurring at new and full moons.

What is a 'neap tide'?

Neap tides have the smallest difference between high and low water, occurring when Sun and Moon are at right angles.

What is 'tidal range'?

Tidal range is the vertical difference between the high water mark and the low water mark.

What is a 'tidal bulge'?

Tidal bulges are the raised areas of ocean water on the near and far sides of Earth relative to the Moon.

What is 'tidal friction'?

Tidal friction converts Earth's rotational energy into heat, gradually slowing Earth's spin.

What is a 'tidal bore'?

A tidal bore is a wave front that rushes up certain rivers and estuaries with the incoming tide.

What is the 'Roche limit'?

The Roche limit is the distance within which a celestial body held together only by gravity would be pulled apart by tidal forces.

What is 'tidal locking' in this context?

Tidal locking occurs when tidal forces synchronize a body's rotation with its orbital period, as with the Moon always showing one face to Earth.

What is 'tidal resonance'?

Tidal resonance occurs in bays like the Bay of Fundy where the basin's natural oscillation period enhances tidal amplitude.

Outliers and Resistant Measures

Tidal force varies inversely with the cube of distance (1/r³), so doubling distance would reduce tidal force by a factor of 8.

Jupiter's massive gravity, combined with orbital resonances with other moons, flexes Io's interior, generating extreme heat and volcanism.

Real-World Applications

Example: Two fishing villages are on the same coastline but 200 km apart. One has a 10-meter tidal range while the other has only 2 meters. What explains this?

Coastal features like narrow bays, underwater topography, and resonance effects dramatically influence local tidal range.
