Lesson 2.2: Seasons & Tilt of Axis - Summary

Key Concepts: Seasons & Tilt of Axis

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the term for the longest day of the year in a given hemisphere?

The summer solstice marks the longest day and shortest night of the year for that hemisphere.

What is a 'solstice'?

A solstice occurs when Earth's axial tilt is most inclined toward or away from the Sun, producing the longest or shortest day.

What is an 'equinox'?

An equinox occurs when the Sun is directly above the equator, and day and night are roughly equal worldwide.

What is the 'Tropic of Cancer'?

The Tropic of Cancer at 23.5°N marks the northernmost latitude where the Sun can be directly overhead.

What is the 'Tropic of Capricorn'?

The Tropic of Capricorn at 23.5°S marks the southernmost latitude where the Sun can be directly overhead.

What is the 'Arctic Circle'?

The Arctic Circle (~66.5°N) marks the latitude above which 24-hour daylight (midnight sun) occurs at the June solstice.

What is 'obliquity'?

Obliquity is the technical term for the tilt of a planet's rotational axis relative to its orbital plane.

What is 'photoperiod'?

Photoperiod is the length of daylight during a day, which changes with seasons and affects plant and animal behavior.

What is the 'midnight sun'?

The midnight sun occurs at high latitudes when the Sun does not set for extended periods around the summer solstice.

A researcher at the North Pole experiences 24 hours of darkness on December 21. What is this phenomenon called?

Polar night occurs when the Sun stays below the horizon for 24 hours, happening at polar latitudes during their winter solstice.

A student argues that Earth is closer to the Sun in summer, causing warmer weather. What is wrong with this reasoning?

Earth's perihelion occurs in January. Seasons are caused by axial tilt, not distance from the Sun.
