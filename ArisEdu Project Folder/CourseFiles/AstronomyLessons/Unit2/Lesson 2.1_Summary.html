Lesson 2.1: Earth's Rotation & Revolution - Summary

Key Concepts: Earth's Rotation & Revolution

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the approximate tilt of Earth's axis?

Earth's axis is tilted about 23.5° relative to its orbital plane, which causes the seasons.

What is the shape of Earth's orbit around the Sun?

Earth's orbit is nearly circular with an eccentricity of about 0.017, making it only slightly elliptical.

What is 'rotation' in the context of Earth's motion?

Rotation refers to Earth spinning on its own axis, which takes about 24 hours and produces day and night.

What is 'revolution' in astronomy?

Revolution is the movement of one celestial body in an orbit around another, such as Earth orbiting the Sun.

What is a 'sidereal day'?

A sidereal day is measured by Earth's rotation relative to distant stars, about 23 hours 56 minutes.

What is the 'ecliptic'?

The ecliptic is the apparent path the Sun traces across the sky throughout the year, corresponding to Earth's orbital plane.

What is 'precession'?

Precession is the slow, conical wobble of Earth's axis over a ~26,000-year cycle, changing which star is the 'North Star.'

What is the 'Coriolis effect'?

The Coriolis effect is an apparent deflection of moving objects caused by Earth's rotation, influencing weather patterns and ocean currents.

What is the 'celestial equator'?

The celestial equator is the imaginary great circle on the celestial sphere directly above Earth's equator.

What is an 'analemma'?

An analemma is the figure-8 shape traced by the Sun's position in the sky when observed at the same time and place throughout the year.

A photographer takes a picture of the Sun at the same time every day for a year and notices a figure-8 pattern. What is this called?

The figure-8 pattern formed by the Sun's position at the same time each day over a year is called an analemma.
