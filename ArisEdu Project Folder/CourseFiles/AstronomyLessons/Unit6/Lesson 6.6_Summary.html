Lesson 6.6: Case Studies in Cosmology - Summary

Key Concepts: Case Studies in Cosmology

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the CMB power spectrum?

The CMB power spectrum reveals the contributions of baryonic matter, dark matter, and dark energy through acoustic peak patterns.

What is a standard candle?

Type Ia supernovae and Cepheid variables are standard candles — their known luminosities enable distance estimation.

What is the cosmic distance ladder?

Each rung of the distance ladder calibrates the next: parallax → Cepheids → Type Ia SNe → Hubble's Law.

What is Big Bang nucleosynthesis?

BBN correctly predicts the abundances of hydrogen (~75%), helium (~25%), and trace lithium in the universe.

What is the cosmological principle?

The cosmological principle is supported by the CMB's uniformity and the large-scale distribution of galaxies.

What is the ΛCDM model?

ΛCDM successfully explains the CMB, large-scale structure, expansion history, and element abundances.

What is look-back time?

A galaxy at a look-back time of 10 billion years is seen as it was 10 billion years ago.

What is recombination?

Despite the name, this was the first time atoms formed; the universe became transparent to light, releasing the CMB.

What is the dark ages?

The cosmic dark ages ended with the epoch of reionization, when the first stars and galaxies lit up.

Gravitational waves from a neutron star merger are detected simultaneously with electromagnetic signals. What is this called?

GW170817 inaugurated multi-messenger astronomy, using both gravitational waves and light to study the same event.
