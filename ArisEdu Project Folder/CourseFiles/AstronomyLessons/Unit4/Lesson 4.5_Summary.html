Lesson 4.5: Impact on Earth's Climate & Technology - Summary

Key Concepts: Impact on Earth's Climate & Technology

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the total solar irradiance (TSI)?

TSI measures the Sun's total power per square meter at the top of Earth's atmosphere, about 1,361 W/m².

What is total solar irradiance (TSI)?

TSI measures the Sun's total energy output per square meter striking the top of Earth's atmosphere.

What is a Carrington-class event?

A Carrington-class event would cause massive disruptions to modern technology, power grids, and communications.

What is the ionosphere?

The ionosphere (60-1,000 km altitude) contains ions created by solar UV and X-ray radiation, affecting radio propagation.

What is the solar constant?

Despite its name, the 'solar constant' actually varies slightly; modern usage prefers 'total solar irradiance.'

What is a solar proton event?

Solar proton events are dangerous for astronauts and can increase radiation levels at aircraft altitudes.

What is space weather forecasting?

Space weather forecasting helps prepare for solar events that could disrupt power, communications, and navigation systems.

What is the Forbush decrease?

The magnetic field of a CME can temporarily shield Earth from galactic cosmic rays, causing a Forbush decrease.

What is solar forcing?

Solar forcing refers to changes in the Sun's output that can affect Earth's energy balance and climate.

A Carrington-class event strikes Earth today. What is the most likely catastrophic consequence?

A modern Carrington event could knock out power grids worldwide, potentially taking months to fully repair.

Outliers and Resistant Measures

Ionospheric disturbances from solar activity cause delays in GPS signals, reducing positioning accuracy.

Solar flares enhance ionization in the ionosphere, causing absorption of HF radio waves (radio blackouts).
