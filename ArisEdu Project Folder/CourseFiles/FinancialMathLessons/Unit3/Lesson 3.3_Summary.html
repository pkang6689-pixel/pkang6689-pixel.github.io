Lesson 3.3: Mortgages (fixed vs. variable rates) - Summary

Key Concepts: Mortgage Calculations

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is an 'escrow account' in mortgage banking?

An escrow account ensures that property taxes and insurance are paid on time. The lender collects 1/12 of the annual tax and insurance amounts with each mortgage payment and makes those payments when they are due.

What is 'PMI' (Private Mortgage Insurance)?

PMI protects the lender if you default. It is an additional monthly cost, typically 0.5-1% of the loan amount annually. PMI can be removed once you reach 20% equity in the home.

What is 'title insurance'?

Title insurance protects against legal challenges to property ownership, such as undisclosed liens, forgery, or errors in public records. It is a one-time cost paid at closing and is typically required by lenders.

Refinancing a $280,000 mortgage from 6.5% to 5% (remaining 25 years) changes the monthly payment from approximately $1,770 to approximately $1,500. If closing costs are $5,000, what is the break-even point and total savings over the remaining term?

Monthly savings: $1,770 - $1,500 = $270. Break-even: $5,000 / $270 = 18.5 months. Total savings over 25 years: ($270 x 300) - $5,000 = $81,000 - $5,000 = $76,000.

A family's home is appraised at $450,000. They owe $290,000 on their mortgage. Their annual property tax is 1.5% of the assessed value, and homeowner's insurance is $2,400/year. What is their monthly escrow payment for taxes and insurance?

Annual property tax: $450,000 x 0.015 = $6,750. Monthly tax: $6,750 / 12 = $562.50. Monthly insurance: $2,400 / 12 = $200. Total monthly escrow: $562.50 + $200 = $762.50. This is added to the principal and interest payment.

A homeowner purchased their home for $350,000 with a $280,000 mortgage. After 10 years, the home is worth $420,000 and they owe $220,000. What is their current home equity and LTV ratio?

Equity = market value - mortgage balance = $420,000 - $220,000 = $200,000. LTV = mortgage balance / market value = $220,000 / $420,000 = 52.4%. The homeowner has built significant equity through both principal payments and home appreciation.

A couple is considering a 5/1 ARM at 4.5% initial rate versus a 30-year fixed at 6.0% on a $350,000 mortgage. They plan to stay 7 years. If the ARM adjusts to 7.5% in year 6, what is the approximate total cost difference over 7 years?

ARM: 5 years at ~$1,773/month + 2 years at ~$2,350/month = ($1,773 x 60) + ($2,350 x 24) = $106,380 + $56,400 = $162,780. Fixed: $2,098 x 84 = $176,232. ARM savings: approximately $13,452 plus additional savings from faster principal paydown in years 1-5. Even with the rate jump, the ARM is cheaper for a 7-year hold.

Real-World Applications

Example: One discount point on a $280,000 mortgage costs:

One point = 1% of the loan amount. $280,000 x 0.01 = $2,800. Paying points reduces the interest rate, typically by 0.25% per point.

Example: A 5/1 ARM (Adjustable Rate Mortgage) means:

In a 5/1 ARM, the rate is fixed for the first 5 years (the initial period) and then adjusts once per year (the adjustment period). ARMs typically have rate caps limiting how much the rate can increase.

Example: Total interest on a $280,000 mortgage at 6.5% for 30 years is approximately:

Monthly payment: approximately $1,770. Total payments: $1,770 x 360 = $637,200. Total interest: $637,200 - $280,000 = $357,200. The total interest exceeds the original loan amount.

Example: What is an 'escrow account' in mortgage banking?

An escrow account ensures that property taxes and insurance are paid on time. The lender collects 1/12 of the annual tax and insurance amounts with each mortgage payment and makes those payments when they are due.

Example: What are 'discount points' in a mortgage?

Discount points let you prepay interest at closing to get a lower rate. Each point costs 1% of the loan amount and typically reduces the rate by about 0.25%. Whether points are worthwhile depends on how long you keep the loan (break-even analysis).

Example: What does 'pre-approval' mean for a mortgage?

Pre-approval involves a thorough review of your financial profile (credit report, income verification, asset verification). It provides a more reliable estimate than pre-qualification and shows sellers you are a serious, qualified buyer.

Example: What is 'PMI' (Private Mortgage Insurance)?

PMI protects the lender if you default. It is an additional monthly cost, typically 0.5-1% of the loan amount annually. PMI can be removed once you reach 20% equity in the home.

Example: What does 'mortgage amortization' mean?

Mortgage amortization spreads the repayment across the loan term. Each payment covers interest on the remaining balance plus a portion of principal. The payment stays the same, but the split between interest and principal shifts over time.

Example: What does 'rate lock' mean when obtaining a mortgage?

A rate lock protects you from rate increases during the loan processing period. If rates rise after you lock, your rate stays the same. However, if rates drop, you typically cannot take advantage of the lower rate unless you have a float-down option.

Example: A homeowner is comparing a 15-year mortgage at 5.8% versus a 30-year mortgage at 6.5% on a $280,000 loan. The 15-year payment is $2,350 and the 30-year payment is $1,770. How much total interest is saved by choosing the 15-year option?

30-year total: $1,770 x 360 = $637,200; interest = $357,200. 15-year total: $2,350 x 180 = $423,000; interest = $143,000. Savings: $357,200 - $143,000 = $214,200. The 15-year option saves over $214,000 despite higher monthly payments.

Example: Refinancing a $280,000 mortgage from 6.5% to 5% (remaining 25 years) changes the monthly payment from approximately $1,770 to approximately $1,500. If closing costs are $5,000, what is the break-even point and total savings over the remaining term?

Example: A buyer is deciding whether to pay 2 discount points on a $300,000 mortgage. Each point reduces the rate by 0.25% (from 6.5% to 6.0%). The monthly savings is $100. How many months until the buyer breaks even on the points?

Cost of 2 points: $300,000 x 0.02 = $6,000. Monthly savings: $100. Break-even: $6,000 / $100 = 60 months (5 years). If the buyer stays longer than 5 years, paying points saves money.
