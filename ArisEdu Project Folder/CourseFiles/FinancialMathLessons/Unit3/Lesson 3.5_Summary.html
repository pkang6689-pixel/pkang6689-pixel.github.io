Lesson 3.5: Student Loans & Repayment Options - Summary

Key Concepts: Student Loan Analysis

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is interest capitalization in the context of student loans?

Interest capitalization occurs when accrued but unpaid interest is added to the loan's principal balance, meaning the borrower subsequently pays interest on a larger amount.

What is the difference between a subsidized and unsubsidized federal student loan?

The key distinction is that the government covers interest on subsidized loans during enrollment, grace period, and deferment, while unsubsidized loans accrue interest from the day funds are disbursed.

What is the meaning of 'discretionary income' as used in income-driven repayment plans?

For federal income-driven plans, discretionary income is specifically defined as the borrower's adjusted gross income minus 150% of the poverty guideline for their family size and state.

What is a 'Master Promissory Note' (MPN)?

The MPN is a binding legal document that outlines the borrower's obligation to repay the loan, the interest rate, repayment terms, and borrower rights. It can cover multiple loan disbursements over up to 10 years.
