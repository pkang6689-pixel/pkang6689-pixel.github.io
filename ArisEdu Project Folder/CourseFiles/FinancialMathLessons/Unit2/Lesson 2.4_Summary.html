Lesson 2.4: Growth vs. Decay Models - Summary

Key Concepts: Growth vs. Decay Models

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A new laptop costs $1,200 and loses 25% of its value each year. What is it worth after 4 years?

A = $1,200(1 - 0.25)^4 = $1,200(0.75)^4 ≈ $1,200 × 0.3164 ≈ $379.69. The laptop retains only about 32% of its original value after 4 years.

A house purchased for $250,000 appreciates at 4% per year. What is its approximate value after 10 years?

A = $250,000(1.04)^10 ≈ $250,000 × 1.4802 ≈ $370,061. Real estate appreciation is a prime example of exponential growth in personal finance.

A $35,000 car depreciates at 18% per year while a $35,000 investment grows at 8% per year. After 5 years, what is the approximate difference between the investment value and the car value?

Car: $35,000(0.82)^5 ≈ $13,044. Investment: $35,000(1.08)^5 ≈ $51,427. Difference ≈ $51,427 - $13,044 = $38,383. This powerfully illustrates why investing beats buying depreciating assets.

A collector buys art for $5,000 that appreciates at 7% per year, and also buys electronics for $5,000 that depreciate at 20% per year. After 10 years, what is the combined value of both purchases?

Art: $5,000(1.07)^10 ≈ $9,836. Electronics: $5,000(0.80)^10 ≈ $537. Combined: $9,836 + $537 = $10,373. The appreciating asset nearly doubles while the depreciating one loses 89% of its value.

A stock portfolio grows at 10% per year while inflation runs at 3%. What is the approximate real (inflation-adjusted) value of a $100,000 portfolio after 15 years?

Real growth rate ≈ 10% - 3% = 7%. Real value = $100,000(1.07)^15 ≈ $275,903. Alternatively: nominal value $100,000(1.10)^15 ≈ $417,725 divided by inflation factor (1.03)^15 ≈ 1.558 ≈ $268,000.

A small business owner buys a delivery van for $45,000 (depreciates at 15%/year) and also invests $45,000 in a business expansion (grows revenue at 12%/year). After 6 years, what is the approximate difference between the expansion value and the van value?

Van: $45,000(0.85)^6 ≈ $17,001. Expansion: $45,000(1.12)^6 ≈ $88,827. Difference ≈ $88,827 - $17,001 = $71,826. Investing in growth assets dramatically outperforms holding depreciating assets.

Real-World Applications

Example: Depreciation in financial math is an example of:

Depreciation reduces the value of an asset over time, typically at a percentage rate, which is modeled by exponential decay: A = P(1-r)^t.

Example: Inflation averages 2.5% per year. A retiree needs $50,000/year today. In 20 years, approximately how much will they need annually to maintain the same purchasing power?

Future need = $50,000(1.025)^20 ≈ $50,000 × 1.6386 ≈ $81,931. Inflation growth means the retiree needs 64% more income in 20 years to buy the same goods and services.
