Lesson 4.8: Technology in Investment Planning - Summary

Key Concepts: Technology in Investment Analysis

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'robo-advisor'?

Robo-advisors use algorithms to create and manage diversified portfolios of low-cost ETFs. They automate rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, and dividend reinvestment, making professional-grade portfolio management accessible to everyone.

What is a 'stock screener'?

Stock screeners let you search thousands of stocks using filters. For example, you could screen for large-cap stocks with P/E below 15, dividend yield above 3%, and positive earnings growth — narrowing thousands of options to a manageable list.

What is a 'Monte Carlo simulation'?

Monte Carlo simulations generate thousands of possible future scenarios using random variations in returns, inflation, and other variables. Instead of one projection, you get a probability distribution (e.g., 92% chance your money lasts 30 years).

What is the 'XIRR' function in Excel?

XIRR handles irregular cash flows with specific dates, making it ideal for calculating the true return on investments where you add money, receive dividends, and make withdrawals at different times throughout the year.

What is 'algorithmic trading'?

Algorithmic trading uses computer programs to execute trades based on predetermined criteria like price, volume, and timing. It accounts for over 60% of US equity trading and ranges from simple rebalancing algorithms to complex high-frequency trading.

What is a 'data table' (sensitivity analysis) in Excel?

Data tables let you see how changing one or two variables affects results. For example, showing how different return rates (5%-10%) and contribution amounts ($200-$1,000/month) affect retirement savings — creating a grid of outcomes at a glance.

What is the best technology tool for investing?

The best tool is one that helps you save and invest consistently. A simple auto-investment into a target-date fund through your employer's 401(k) outperforms a sophisticated trading platform that leads to overtrading and emotional decisions.
