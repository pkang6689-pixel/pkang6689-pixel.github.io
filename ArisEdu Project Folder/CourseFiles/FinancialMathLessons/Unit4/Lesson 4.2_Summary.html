Lesson 4.2: Risk vs. Return - Summary

Key Concepts: Risk vs. Return

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is 'unsystematic risk' (specific risk)?

Unsystematic risk includes events like a CEO scandal, product recall, or industry downturn. Owning 20-30 stocks across different sectors largely eliminates this risk, which is why diversification is so powerful.

What is 'risk tolerance'?

Risk tolerance is shaped by age, time horizon, financial goals, income stability, and personal temperament. It determines how aggressively or conservatively your portfolio should be allocated.

What is 'correlation' in portfolio management?

Correlation of +1 means perfect co-movement (no diversification benefit), -1 means perfect opposite movement (maximum diversification), and 0 means no relationship. Lower correlations between portfolio holdings reduce overall risk.

Real-World Applications

Example: What is 'correlation' in portfolio management?

Correlation of +1 means perfect co-movement (no diversification benefit), -1 means perfect opposite movement (maximum diversification), and 0 means no relationship. Lower correlations between portfolio holdings reduce overall risk.

Example: Investment A has returns of 8%, 12%, 6%, 14% over four years. Investment B has returns of 9%, 10%, 9.5%, 10.5%. Both average 10%, but which carries more risk and why?

Investment A's returns range from 6% to 14% (spread of 8 points) while B's range from 9% to 10.5% (spread of 1.5 points). A's higher standard deviation means greater uncertainty about future outcomes, even with the same average.

Example: A 30-year-old using the '110 minus age' rule would allocate their portfolio as:

110 - 30 = 80% stocks, with 20% in bonds. This aggressive allocation suits the 30-year-old's long time horizon. As they age, the formula automatically shifts toward more bonds (at 60: 110 - 60 = 50% stocks).
