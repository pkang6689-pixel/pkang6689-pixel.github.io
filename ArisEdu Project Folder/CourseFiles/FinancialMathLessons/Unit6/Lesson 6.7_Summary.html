Lesson 6.7: Insurance in Real Life - Summary

Key Concepts: Insurance in Real Life

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is an 'HDHP' (High-Deductible Health Plan)?

An HDHP has a higher deductible (at least $1,600 individual in 2024) and lower premiums. The key benefit is eligibility for HSA contributions, providing triple tax advantages.

What is 'long-term care insurance'?

Long-term care insurance covers custodial care that health insurance and Medicare generally do not, including nursing homes ($8,000+/month), assisted living, and in-home aide services.

What is an 'insurance rider' or 'endorsement'?

A rider (or endorsement) adds or modifies coverage beyond the standard policy. Common examples include jewelry riders, home office riders, or identity theft protection endorsements.

Maria has a $4,150 HSA contribution in the 24% bracket. She invests it and it grows to $9,000 over 15 years. She withdraws $9,000 for qualified medical expenses. What is her total tax savings compared to using a taxable brokerage account?

Contribution deduction: $4,150 x 0.24 = $996. Tax-free growth on $4,850 gain: $4,850 x 0.24 = $1,164. Total savings = $996 + $1,164 = $2,160. The triple tax advantage compounds over time.

Tom pays $4,800/year for auto insurance. He gets quotes and finds he can bundle auto + renters with a new company for $4,000/year total (including $300 renter's insurance). What is his net annual savings, considering he currently has no renter's insurance?

Old cost: $4,800 auto + $0 renters = $4,800. New cost: $4,000 bundled (auto + renters). Savings = $800/year, AND he now has renter's insurance protection that he lacked before.

Lisa, age 55, is estimating long-term care costs. Nursing home care averages $8,500/month in her area. If she needs 3 years of care, what is the total cost, and how does a long-term care policy paying $6,000/month compare?

Total cost = $8,500 x 36 months = $306,000. Policy covers $6,000 x 36 = $216,000. Gap = $306,000 - $216,000 = $90,000. Long-term care policies typically do not cover 100% of costs, so supplemental savings are still needed.

Real-World Applications

Example: A family earning $80,000 should have approximately _____ in term life insurance coverage.

The general guideline is 10-12 times annual income. $80,000 x 10 = $800,000. This amount would replace the earner's income for approximately 10 years.

Example: Auto insurance with 100/300/100 liability coverage means:

The three numbers represent: maximum per-person bodily injury ($100K), maximum per-accident total bodily injury ($300K), and maximum property damage ($100K).

Example: What does 'uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage' protect against?

Uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage pays for your injuries and damages when the at-fault driver has no insurance or not enough to cover your losses. About 13% of drivers are uninsured.

Example: A couple (both age 35) needs $1 million in life insurance. A 20-year term policy costs $45/month and a whole life policy costs $600/month. If they buy term and invest the $555/month difference at 7% annual return for 20 years, approximately how much will the investment grow to?

Using the future value of annuity formula: $555/month for 20 years at 7% annual return yields approximately $287,000. This illustrates why 'buy term and invest the difference' often outperforms whole life cash value.

Example: Maria has a $4,150 HSA contribution in the 24% bracket. She invests it and it grows to $9,000 over 15 years. She withdraws $9,000 for qualified medical expenses. What is her total tax savings compared to using a taxable brokerage account?

Example: A family is reviewing their insurance annually after having a baby. Which coverage changes should they prioritize?

A new baby triggers multiple insurance updates: adding the child to health coverage (within 30 days for a qualifying life event), increasing life insurance for the additional dependent, and ensuring disability coverage to protect the family's income.

Example: A couple owns a $500,000 home with $300,000 in personal assets. They have 100/300/100 auto liability coverage. A serious accident results in a $600,000 lawsuit. How much of their personal assets are at risk without an umbrella policy?

Auto liability covers $300,000 per accident. The $600,000 judgment leaves a $300,000 gap that the couple must pay from personal assets. A $1M umbrella policy (costing ~$300/year) would have covered this.
