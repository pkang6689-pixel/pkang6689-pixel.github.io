Lesson 1.2: Sources of Income (wages, salaries, investments) - Summary

Key Concepts: Sources of Income (wages, salaries, investments)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'salary'?

A salary is a fixed annual compensation amount divided into regular pay periods (weekly, biweekly, or monthly), paid regardless of the exact number of hours worked.

What is 'commission-based income'?

Commission-based income is compensation calculated as a percentage of the sales an individual generates, commonly found in real estate, car sales, and financial services.

What is a '1099 form' used for?

A 1099 form reports various types of non-employment income to the IRS, including independent contractor earnings, bank interest, investment dividends, and other miscellaneous income.

What is 'investment income'?

Investment income is money earned from assets you own, including stock dividends, bond interest, capital gains from selling investments, and net rental income from property.

Sarah works 40 hours per week at $18/hour. She also works 8 hours of overtime this week. What is her gross pay for the week?

Regular pay = 40 × $18 = $720. Overtime pay = 8 × ($18 × 1.5) = 8 × $27 = $216. Total gross pay = $720 + $216 = $936.

Marcus earns a $65,000 annual salary. His employer matches 100% of his 401(k) contributions up to 5% of salary. If Marcus contributes 5%, what is his total annual compensation including the match?

Marcus's 401(k) contribution = 5% × $65,000 = $3,250. Employer match = $3,250. Total compensation = $65,000 salary + $3,250 match = $68,250. Not taking the full match is leaving free money on the table.

A real estate agent earns a 3% commission on each home sale. She sells a house for $350,000. What is her commission?

Commission = 3% × $350,000 = 0.03 × $350,000 = $10,500. Commission-based income can be highly variable since it depends entirely on sales volume.

Tom earns $55,000/year. His FICA deduction (7.65%), federal tax (12%), and state tax (4%) are his only deductions. What is his approximate annual net income?

Total deduction rate = 7.65% + 12% + 4% = 23.65%. Deductions = 23.65% × $55,000 = $12,992.50. Net income = $55,000 - $12,993 ≈ $42,008. Over 23% of gross pay goes to taxes.

Lisa owns rental property that generates $1,800/month in rent. After $600/month in expenses (mortgage, maintenance, taxes), what is her annual net rental income?

Monthly net rental income = $1,800 - $600 = $1,200. Annual net rental income = $1,200 × 12 = $14,400. This passive income continues as long as she owns the property.

Alex has the following income sources: $45,000 salary, $2,000 in stock dividends, $500 in savings interest, and $3,600 from a side gig. What is his total gross income?

Total gross income = $45,000 + $2,000 + $500 + $3,600 = $51,100. All income sources, including investment returns and side gig earnings, must be reported and are generally taxable.

Jennifer works at a restaurant earning $2.13/hr base plus tips. In a 30-hour week, she earns $450 in tips. What is her total weekly income?

Base pay = $2.13 × 30 = $63.90. Tips = $450. Total = $63.90 + $450 = $513.90. Tipped employees often receive a lower base wage with the expectation that tips bring total compensation above minimum wage.

Mike bought 100 shares of stock at $25 per share and sold them a year later at $32 per share. What is his capital gain?

Purchase price = 100 × $25 = $2,500. Sale price = 100 × $32 = $3,200. Capital gain = $3,200 - $2,500 = $700. This gain is taxable as investment income.

A couple earns combined salaries of $95,000/year. They also receive $4,000 in dividends, $1,200 in savings interest, and $6,000 from a rental property. If their combined effective tax rate is 22%, what is their approximate annual net income?

Total gross income = $95,000 + $4,000 + $1,200 + $6,000 = $106,200. Taxes = 22% × $106,200 = $23,364. Net income = $106,200 - $23,364 = $82,836. Diversified income sources improve financial resilience.

Real-World Applications

Example: Wages are typically paid on a(n) _____ basis.

Wages are compensation calculated on a per-hour basis, meaning workers earn a set dollar amount for each hour worked.

Example: FICA taxes fund Social Security and Medicare, totaling what percentage of gross wages for an employee?

Employees pay 7.65% in FICA taxes: 6.2% for Social Security and 1.45% for Medicare. Employers match this amount, making the combined rate 15.3%.

Example: The federal minimum wage as of 2024 is:

The federal minimum wage is $7.25 per hour, though many states and cities have set higher minimum wages that supersede the federal floor.
