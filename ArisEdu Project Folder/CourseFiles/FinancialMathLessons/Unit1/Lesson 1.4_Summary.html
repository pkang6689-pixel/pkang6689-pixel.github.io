Lesson 1.4: Needs vs. Wants - Summary

Key Concepts: Needs vs. Wants

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'need' in personal finance?

A need is an expense essential for basic living and survival, including food, shelter, clothing, healthcare, basic transportation, and utilities without which you cannot safely function.

What is a 'want' in personal finance?

A want is a non-essential good or service that improves comfort or enjoyment but is not necessary for basic survival, such as entertainment, dining out, vacations, and luxury upgrades.

What is the 'needs vs. wants spectrum'?

Many expenses exist on a spectrum: for example, basic shelter is a need, but a luxury apartment in a premium location represents the want end of that spectrum.

What is the '24-hour rule' for purchases?

The 24-hour rule is a personal finance strategy where you wait at least 24 hours before buying a non-essential item, allowing emotions to subside and rational evaluation to determine if you truly want or need it.

The Rodriguez family is deciding between a basic internet plan ($50/month) and a premium plan ($120/month). They only need basic internet for work and school. What is the annual opportunity cost of choosing the premium plan?

Monthly difference = $120 - $50 = $70. Annual opportunity cost = $70 × 12 = $840. The basic plan meets their needs; the premium adds wants (faster streaming, more channels) at $840/year.

Real-World Applications

Example: Sarah's monthly income is $5,000. She categorizes her expenses: rent $1,400 (need), car payment $350 (need), groceries $500 (need), dining out $300 (want), phone $80 (need), gym $60 (want), streaming $45 (want), clothing $200 (want). What percentage of her income goes to wants?

Total wants = $300 + $60 + $45 + $200 = $605. Percentage = $605 / $5,000 = 12.1%. This is well below the 30% guideline, leaving room for savings and financial goals.
