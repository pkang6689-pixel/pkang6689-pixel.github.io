Lesson 8.5: Global Financial Systems - Summary

Key Concepts: Global Finance & Currency

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is an 'exchange rate'?

An exchange rate is the price of one currency in terms of another. For example, 1 USD = 0.92 EUR means one US dollar can be exchanged for 0.92 euros.

What is 'currency hedging'?

Currency hedging uses financial instruments (forwards, options, futures) to lock in exchange rates and protect against adverse currency movements that could reduce profits from international operations.

What is a 'forward contract' in currency trading?

A forward contract locks in an exchange rate for a future transaction. This allows businesses to know exactly how much they will receive or pay in their home currency, regardless of market fluctuations.

What is the 'Big Mac Index'?

The Big Mac Index, created by The Economist, uses the price of a Big Mac across countries as a lighthearted way to test purchasing power parity and estimate whether currencies are over- or undervalued.

What is 'international diversification' in investing?

International diversification means investing in markets across different countries. Since global markets don't move in perfect lockstep, losses in one country may be offset by gains in another.

What is a 'trade surplus'?

A trade surplus occurs when a country's exports exceed its imports. Countries like Germany and China have historically run large trade surpluses.

An investor holds $50,000 in a European stock fund. The stocks gain 10% in euro terms, but the euro depreciates 5% against the dollar during the same period. What is the approximate return in US dollar terms?

Approximate USD return = (1 + 10%) x (1 - 5%) - 1 = 1.10 x 0.95 - 1 = 1.045 - 1 = 4.5%. The euro's 5% depreciation reduces the investor's dollar-denominated return from 10% to about 4.5%.

An investor converts $100,000 to euros at 1 USD = 0.90 EUR, invests in European bonds earning 5% for one year, then converts back at 1 USD = 0.85 EUR. What is the total return in US dollars?

Convert to EUR: $100,000 x 0.90 = €90,000. After 5% return: €90,000 x 1.05 = €94,500. Convert back: €94,500 / 0.85 = $111,176. Total return = ($111,176 - $100,000) / $100,000 = 11.18%. The euro appreciated against the dollar, boosting returns.
