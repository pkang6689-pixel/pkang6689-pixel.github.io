Lesson 8.1: Stock Market Basics - Summary

Key Concepts: Stock Market Basics

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is 'market capitalization'?

Market capitalization (market cap) is the total dollar value of all outstanding shares, representing the market's assessment of a company's total equity value.

What is 'NYSE'?

The NYSE (New York Stock Exchange) is the world's largest stock exchange by total market capitalization of its listed companies, located on Wall Street in New York City.

Sarah buys 200 shares of a tech company at $45 per share. After one year, the stock is at $54 per share and she received $1.50 per share in dividends. What is her total return (dollar and percentage)?

Capital gain = ($54 - $45) x 200 = $1,800. Dividends = $1.50 x 200 = $300. Total return = $1,800 + $300 = $2,100. Percentage = $2,100 / ($45 x 200) = $2,100 / $9,000 = 23.3%.

An investor buys 50 shares at $80 and the stock drops to $64. What is the loss in dollars and percentage?

Loss per share = $80 - $64 = $16. Total loss = $16 x 50 = $800. Percentage loss = $16 / $80 = 20%.

A stock pays quarterly dividends of $0.85 per share. If you own 300 shares, what is your total annual dividend income?

Annual dividend per share = $0.85 x 4 quarters = $3.40. Total annual income = $3.40 x 300 shares = $1,020.

Company XYZ has a market cap of $50 billion and 500 million shares outstanding. An investor owns 1,000 shares. What percentage of the company does the investor own, and what is their investment worth?

Ownership = 1,000 / 500,000,000 = 0.000002 = 0.0002%. Price per share = $50B / 500M = $100. Investment value = 1,000 x $100 = $100,000.

An investor buys 150 shares at $30 each. The stock rises to $37.50 and pays $0.60 in total dividends per share over the holding period. What is the total return in dollars and as a percentage?

Capital gain = ($37.50 - $30) x 150 = $1,125. Dividends = $0.60 x 150 = $90. Total return = $1,125 + $90 = $1,215. Percentage = $1,215 / ($30 x 150) = $1,215 / $4,500 = 27%.

Real-World Applications

Example: The stock market has historically returned approximately _____ per year (long-term S&P 500 average).

The S&P 500 has historically returned about 10% per year on average (nominal), or approximately 7% after accounting for inflation.
