Lesson 8.2: Reading Stock Tables - Summary

Key Concepts: Reading Financial Tables

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a 'ticker symbol' in financial tables?

A ticker symbol is a unique short alphabetic code (such as TSLA for Tesla or AMZN for Amazon) used to identify a specific publicly traded security on an exchange.

What is the 'PEG ratio'?

The PEG ratio equals P/E divided by the earnings growth rate. A PEG below 1 may indicate the stock is undervalued relative to its growth, while above 1 may suggest it is overvalued.

What is an 'income statement' in financial reporting?

An income statement (also called a profit and loss statement) reports a company's revenue, expenses, and resulting net income over a specific period, showing profitability.

What is a 'balance sheet'?

A balance sheet provides a snapshot of a company's financial position at a specific date, listing what it owns (assets), what it owes (liabilities), and the residual value for shareholders (equity).

A stock table shows: ACME Corp (ACME) -- Price: $120, EPS: $6.00, Annual Dividend: $2.40, Shares Outstanding: 50 million. What is ACME's P/E ratio?

P/E = Price / EPS = $120 / $6.00 = 20. Investors are paying $20 for every $1 of ACME's earnings.

Using the same ACME data (Price: $120, Annual Dividend: $2.40), what is ACME's dividend yield?

Dividend yield = Annual Dividend / Price = $2.40 / $120 = 0.02 = 2.0%.

TechGrow Inc. has a P/E of 30 and an earnings growth rate of 25% per year. What is TechGrow's PEG ratio, and what does it suggest?

PEG = P/E / Growth Rate = 30 / 25 = 1.20. A PEG above 1 suggests the stock may be slightly overvalued relative to its earnings growth rate.

SafeBank Corp. has total assets of $800 million, total liabilities of $500 million, and 30 million shares outstanding. Its stock trades at $8. What is the P/B ratio?

Book value = Assets - Liabilities = $800M - $500M = $300M. Book value per share = $300M / 30M shares = $10. P/B = $8 / $10 = 0.80, meaning the stock trades below book value.

A company's income statement shows: Revenue $2 million, COGS $1.1 million, Operating Expenses $400,000, Interest $50,000, Taxes $90,000. What is the net income?

Net income = Revenue - COGS - Operating Expenses - Interest - Taxes = $2,000,000 - $1,100,000 - $400,000 - $50,000 - $90,000 = $360,000.

MegaCorp trades at $75, has 200 million shares outstanding, and pays a quarterly dividend of $0.50 per share. What is the annual dividend yield and market cap?

Annual dividend = $0.50 x 4 quarters = $2.00. Dividend yield = $2.00 / $75 = 0.0267 = 2.67%. Market cap = $75 x 200M = $15 billion.

A financial table shows Stock X opened at $92, hit a high of $97, a low of $90, and closed at $95 with volume of 8 million shares. The previous close was $93. What is the day's percentage change from the previous close?

Day's change is measured from previous close to today's close: ($95 - $93) / $93 x 100 = +2.15%.

Example: What does 'market capitalization' mean on a stock quote page?

Market capitalization (market cap) equals the current share price multiplied by total shares outstanding. It represents the market's total valuation of the company's equity.
