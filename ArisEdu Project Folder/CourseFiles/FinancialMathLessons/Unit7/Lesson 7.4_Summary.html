Lesson 7.4: Small Business Financing - Summary

Key Concepts: Small Business Financing

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is 'bootstrapping' in business finance?

Bootstrapping refers to building a business from the ground up with personal savings and operating revenue rather than relying on outside investors or borrowed money.

What is 'cost of capital'?

Cost of capital is the blended rate of return a business must earn on its investments to satisfy both its debt holders (interest) and equity investors (expected returns). It serves as a hurdle rate for evaluating new projects.

Maria invests $50,000 of personal savings to start a bakery. After one year, the bakery generates $18,000 in net profit. What is her ROI?

ROI = Net Profit / Investment = $18,000 / $50,000 = 0.36 = 36%.

A business takes a $150,000 bank loan at 8% annual interest for 5 years. The monthly payment is approximately $3,042. What is the total interest paid over the life of the loan?

Total payments = $3,042 x 60 months = $182,520. Total interest = $182,520 - $150,000 = $32,520.

An entrepreneur gives up 25% equity to an angel investor when the business is valued at $400,000. If the business later grows to $2,000,000, what is the value of the equity the investor now holds?

The investor holds 25% of the business. At $2,000,000 valuation, their stake = 25% x $2,000,000 = $500,000. The original equity cost was $100,000 (25% of $400K), so the true cost of that equity financing grew to $500,000.

A small business factors $80,000 in invoices at a 15% discount to receive immediate cash. How much cash does the business receive, and what is the cost of factoring?

Cash received = $80,000 x (1 - 0.15) = $80,000 x 0.85 = $68,000. The cost of factoring = $80,000 - $68,000 = $12,000. The business trades $12,000 for immediate cash flow.

A business chooses to lease equipment for $800/month for 5 years instead of buying it for $35,000. What is the total lease cost, and how does it compare to buying?

Total lease cost = $800 x 60 months = $48,000. Compared to buying at $35,000, leasing costs $13,000 more over the full term. However, leasing preserves cash upfront and may offer tax advantages.

A restaurant owner needs $200,000 to open. She uses $60,000 personal savings (bootstrapping), a $100,000 SBA loan at 6.5%, and a $40,000 angel investment for 10% equity. If the restaurant is worth $800,000 after 5 years, what is the total cost of the angel investment?

The angel investor holds 10% equity. At $800,000 valuation, that stake is worth 10% x $800,000 = $80,000. The cost of the equity financing is $80,000 in ownership value, which is double the original $40,000 investment.

Real-World Applications

Example: The main advantage of bootstrapping is:

Bootstrapping lets the owner retain full ownership and control of the business without owing debt payments or answering to outside investors.

Example: A business with $15,000 monthly revenue and a $100,000 loan at 8% (monthly payment ~$2,028) has what percentage of revenue going to debt service? Is this considered healthy?

Debt service ratio = $2,028 / $15,000 = 13.5%. Generally, keeping debt service below 25% of revenue is considered manageable. At 13.5%, the business has a comfortable margin for repayment.
