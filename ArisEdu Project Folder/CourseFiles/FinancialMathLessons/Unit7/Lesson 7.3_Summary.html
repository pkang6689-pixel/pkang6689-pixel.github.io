Lesson 7.3: Break-Even Analysis - Summary

Key Concepts: Break-Even Analysis

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is 'break-even point' in business finance?

The break-even point is the sales volume (in units or dollars) at which total revenue equals total costs (fixed plus variable), meaning the business earns zero profit and incurs zero loss.

What is a 'target profit analysis'?

Target profit analysis extends break-even analysis by adding the desired profit to fixed costs before dividing by contribution margin: Units = (Fixed Costs + Target Profit) / CM per unit.

A gym has $20,000 in monthly fixed costs. Each membership is $80/month with a variable cost of $20/member. The gym currently has 350 members. What is its monthly profit and margin of safety percentage?

CM per member = $80 - $20 = $60. Total CM = 350 x $60 = $21,000. Profit = $21,000 - $20,000 = $1,000. BEP = $20,000 / $60 = 333.33, rounded up to 334 members. Margin of safety = (350 - 334) / 350 = about 4.76%.

A toy store sells two products: Product A ($20 price, $8 variable cost, 60% of sales) and Product B ($35 price, $20 variable cost, 40% of sales). What is the weighted-average contribution margin?

CM for A = $20 - $8 = $12. CM for B = $35 - $20 = $15. Weighted-average CM = ($12 x 0.60) + ($15 x 0.40) = $7.20 + $6.00 = $13.20.

A software company sells licenses at $100 each with a variable cost of $10 (mostly hosting). Monthly fixed costs are $36,000. It currently sells 500 licenses/month. What is the DOL (Degree of Operating Leverage)?

CM per unit = $100 - $10 = $90. Total CM = 500 x $90 = $45,000. Net income = $45,000 - $36,000 = $9,000. DOL = Total CM / Net Income = $45,000 / $9,000 = 5.0. A high DOL means profits are very sensitive to revenue changes.

A food truck has $6,000 monthly fixed costs and sells meals for $15 each with a variable cost of $6. If the owner wants to earn $3,000 profit per month, what is the minimum number of meals needed?

CM per meal = $15 - $6 = $9. Units for target profit = (Fixed costs + Target profit) / CM = ($6,000 + $3,000) / $9 = $9,000 / $9 = 1,000 meals.

Real-World Applications

Example: Margin of safety percentage = 600 / 3,000 =

Margin of safety % = (Actual sales - BEP) / Actual sales = 600 / 3,000 = 20%.

Example: What does 'Degree of Operating Leverage' (DOL) refer to?

DOL = Contribution Margin / Net Income. A high DOL means that small changes in revenue lead to large percentage changes in operating income, representing both higher risk and higher reward potential.

Example: What does 'weighted-average contribution margin' mean?

For multi-product businesses, the weighted-average CM accounts for the sales mix. Each product's CM is multiplied by its percentage of total sales, and the results are summed to get the blended CM used in break-even calculations.

Additional fixed costs = $15,000 - $12,000 = $3,000. CM per meal = $22 - $10 = $12. Additional meals needed = $3,000 / $12 = 250 meals.
