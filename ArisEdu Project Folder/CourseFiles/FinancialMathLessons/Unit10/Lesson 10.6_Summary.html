Key Concepts: Comprehensive Review

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the primary benefit of global diversification?

International diversification works because global economies have different growth cycles. When the US market underperforms, other regions may outperform, smoothing overall portfolio returns and reducing volatility.

What is the ultimate goal of financial math education?

Financial math education empowers you to evaluate options, run calculations, and make informed choices about mortgages, investments, retirement, taxes, and more. These skills benefit everyone, regardless of career path.

A single filer earns $60,000 taxable income. Using 2024 brackets (10%: $0-$11,600; 12%: $11,601-$47,150; 22%: $47,151+), what is the approximate total federal tax?

10% on $11,600 = $1,160. 12% on $35,550 ($47,150 - $11,600) = $4,266. 22% on $12,850 ($60,000 - $47,150) = $2,827. Total = $1,160 + $4,266 + $2,827 = $8,253. Effective rate = $8,253/$60,000 = 13.8%.

Real-World Applications

Example: Your credit score affects:

Your credit score is a financial reputation number that impacts many areas of life. A higher score means lower interest rates (saving thousands on mortgages), better insurance rates, and easier rental approvals.

Example: A borrower with a 780 credit score qualifies for a 6.0% mortgage rate. A borrower with a 620 score gets 7.5% on the same $300,000, 30-year mortgage. The lower-score borrower pays approximately how much more in total interest?

At 6.0%: PMT ≈ $1,799, total = $647,500. At 7.5%: PMT ≈ $2,098, total = $755,200. Difference ≈ $107,700. A good credit score saves over $100,000 on a single mortgage — making credit building one of the highest-ROI financial activities.

Example: A 28-year-old earns $55,000, saves 15% ($8,250/year) in a 401(k) with a 4% employer match ($2,200/year). Total $10,450/year at 8% for 37 years to age 65 will grow to approximately:

FV = $10,450 × [(1.08^37 - 1) / 0.08] = $10,450 × 187.10 ≈ $1,955,000 ≈ $1,960,000. Total contributed: $386,650. Compound interest adds over $1.5 million. At the 4% rule, this supports $78,400/year in retirement income plus Social Security.
