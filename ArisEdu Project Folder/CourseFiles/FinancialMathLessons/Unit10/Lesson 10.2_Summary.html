Lesson 10.2: AP-Style Practice Problems - Summary

Key Concepts: AP Exam Practice

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is amortization in the context of a loan?

Amortization means each payment covers both interest and principal. Early payments are mostly interest; as the balance decreases, more of each payment goes to principal.

What is the difference between the marginal tax rate and the effective tax rate?

The marginal rate is the rate applied to the next dollar of income (your top bracket). The effective rate is total tax divided by total income — always lower than the marginal rate in a progressive system.

What is a 401(k) plan?

A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored defined-contribution retirement plan. Employees contribute a portion of their salary, often with employer matching, and investments grow tax-advantaged until withdrawal.

What is the key tax difference between Traditional and Roth retirement accounts?

Traditional: tax deduction now, taxed on withdrawal. Roth: no deduction now, but withdrawals are completely tax-free. The choice depends on whether you expect higher or lower tax rates in retirement.

What is the PMT (payment) formula used to calculate?

The PMT formula calculates the equal periodic payment required to pay off a present value (loan) or accumulate a future value (savings), given an interest rate and number of periods.

What is an annuity in financial math?

An annuity is a series of equal payments occurring at regular intervals. Examples include mortgage payments, retirement contributions, and pension payouts. The FV and PV of annuity formulas are key tools.

What is the weighted contribution margin used for?

The weighted CM averages each product's contribution margin by its share of total sales. It allows break-even calculation when products have different prices and variable costs.

A machine costs $200,000 and saves $55,000/year for 5 years at a 10% discount rate. It also has a $20,000 salvage value at year 5. The PV of the annuity is $208,493 and PV of salvage is $12,418. What is the NPV?

NPV = PV of annuity + PV of salvage - cost = $208,493 + $12,418 - $200,000 = $20,911. The salvage value significantly improves the project's NPV from $8,493 to $20,911.

A portfolio is 60% stocks (10% expected return, 16% standard deviation) and 40% bonds (5% expected return, 6% standard deviation). What is the portfolio's expected return?

Portfolio return = (0.60 × 10%) + (0.40 × 5%) = 6% + 2% = 8%. Expected return is the weighted average of each asset's return.

You invest $10,000 in a Japanese stock at an exchange rate of 150 yen per dollar (buying 1,500,000 yen). The stock gains 12% to 1,680,000 yen, but the yen weakens to 155 yen per dollar. What is your dollar return?

Convert back: 1,680,000 yen / 155 = $10,839. Despite a 12% gain in yen, the weakening yen reduces the dollar return to 8.4%. Currency movements can significantly affect international investment returns.

A company sells two products: A ($20 CM, 60% of sales) and B ($10 CM, 40% of sales). Fixed costs are $80,000. What is the break-even in total units?

Weighted CM = (0.60 × $20) + (0.40 × $10) = $12 + $4 = $16. BEP = $80,000 / $16 = 5,000 total units. At the 60/40 mix: 3,000 units of A and 2,000 units of B.

A single filer has $95,000 taxable income. Using 2024 brackets (10% up to $11,600; 12% to $47,150; 22% to $100,525), the total federal tax is approximately $15,953. What is the effective tax rate?

Effective rate = $15,953 / $95,000 = 16.8%. Tax: $1,160 (10%) + $4,266 (12%) + $10,527 (22% on $47,850) = $15,953. The effective rate is well below the 22% marginal rate.

AP multi-step problem: An employee earns $70,000, contributes 10% to a Traditional 401(k), and receives a 50% employer match up to 6%. What is the total annual amount going into their retirement account?

Employee contributes 10% × $70,000 = $7,000. Employer matches 50% up to 6% of salary: 50% × (6% × $70,000) = 50% × $4,200 = $2,100. Total = $7,000 + $2,100 = $9,100/year going into the account.

Example: What does 'salvage value' mean in capital budgeting?

Salvage value is what an asset is worth at the end of its useful life. In NPV calculations, the salvage value is discounted back to present value and added to the project's cash inflows.

Example: What does a free-response question on the AP exam require compared to multiple choice?

AP free-response scoring rewards multiple components: identifying the correct formula, showing calculation steps, arriving at the right answer, AND providing economic interpretation of the result.

Example: A homeowner has a $200,000 mortgage at 7% for 30 years (PMT ≈ $1,331). After 5 years, they refinance the remaining $190,000 at 5% for 25 years (new PMT ≈ $1,110). How much do they save per month?

Monthly savings = $1,331 - $1,110 = $221/month or $2,652/year. Refinancing to a lower rate can produce significant savings, but closing costs should be factored in to determine the break-even period.
