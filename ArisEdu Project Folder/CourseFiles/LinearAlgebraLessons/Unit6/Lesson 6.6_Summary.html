Lesson 6.6: Scaling & Shearing - Summary

Key Concepts: Scaling & Shearing

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the matrix for uniform scaling by factor k in R^2?

Uniform scaling multiplies every component by k, so the matrix is kI = [[k,0],[0,k]]. Both axes are scaled by the same factor, making the transformation a dilation (k>1) or contraction (0

What is the matrix for scaling x by 3 and y by 5 in R^2?

Non-uniform scaling uses a diagonal matrix with different scale factors: [[3,0],[0,5]] maps (x,y) to (3x, 5y). The x-axis is stretched by 3 and the y-axis by 5.

What is a horizontal shear matrix in R^2 with shear factor k?

A horizontal shear [[1,k],[0,1]] maps (x,y) to (x+ky, y). It shifts the x-coordinate by k times the y-coordinate while leaving y unchanged, tilting vertical lines.

If the scaling matrix is A = [[2,0],[0,4]] and v = (3, 1), what is Av?

Av = [[2,0],[0,4]] * (3,1) = (2*3 + 0*1, 0*3 + 4*1) = (6, 4). The x-component is doubled and the y-component is quadrupled.

If a horizontal shear with k=2 is applied to the vector (1, 3), what is the result?

[[1,2],[0,1]] * (1,3) = (1 + 2*3, 0 + 1*3) = (7, 3). The shear adds 2 times the y-component to x, while y remains unchanged.

What is the determinant of the scaling matrix [[a, 0],[0, b]]?

For a diagonal matrix [[a,0],[0,b]], the determinant is a*b - 0*0 = ab. This represents the area scaling factor: a unit square is stretched to a rectangle with area |ab|.

What is the determinant of the horizontal shear matrix [[1, k],[0, 1]]?

det([[1,k],[0,1]]) = 1*1 - k*0 = 1. Shear transformations always have determinant 1, meaning they preserve area even though they distort shapes.

What is the inverse of the scaling matrix [[3, 0],[0, 2]]?

The inverse of a diagonal matrix [[a,0],[0,b]] is [[1/a, 0],[0, 1/b]], provided a and b are nonzero. So the inverse is [[1/3, 0],[0, 1/2]], which undoes the scaling.

What is 'scaling' as a linear transformation?

Scaling is a linear transformation represented by a diagonal matrix that multiplies each coordinate by a constant factor. Uniform scaling uses the same factor for all axes; non-uniform scaling uses different factors per axis.

What is a 'shear' transformation?

A shear displaces each point in a fixed direction by an amount proportional to its signed distance from a line parallel to that direction. One axis stays fixed while the other tilts, like pushing the top of a deck of cards sideways.

What is a 'shear factor'?

The shear factor k is the proportionality constant: for each unit of y-coordinate, the x-coordinate shifts by k units (in a horizontal shear). Larger |k| means more distortion.

What is an 'affine transformation'?

An affine transformation is of the form T(x) = Ax + b, combining a linear transformation (matrix A) with a translation (vector b). Unlike pure linear transformations, affine transformations can shift the origin.

What is a 'stretch' in one direction?

A stretch in one direction scales one axis by factor k while leaving others unchanged. For example, [[k,0],[0,1]] stretches along x by k while y is unchanged. Circles become ellipses with this transformation.

A mechanical engineer models wind load on a building. Wind at 45 degrees applies a shear force. The deformation is modeled by composing a horizontal shear (k=0.5) with a vertical shear (k=0.5). What is the combined transformation matrix?

Horizontal shear: H = [[1, 0.5],[0, 1]]. Vertical shear: V = [[1, 0],[0.5, 1]]. Composing H after V: HV = [[1*1+0.5*0.5, 1*0+0.5*1],[0*1+1*0.5, 0*0+1*1]] = [[1.25, 0.5],[0.5, 1]].

Real-World Applications

Example: A printer needs to correct for paper stretching during printing. The paper stretches 5% in the horizontal direction and 2% in the vertical direction. What pre-compensation scaling matrix should be applied to the digital image so it prints correctly?

The paper stretch applies [[1.05, 0],[0, 1.02]] to the image. To compensate, pre-multiply by the inverse: [[1/1.05, 0],[0, 1/1.02]]. After stretching, the final result is [[1.05, 0],[0, 1.02]] * [[1/1.05, 0],[0, 1/1.02]] = I, restoring the correct proportions.

Example: A biologist magnifies a cell image by 400x horizontally and 400x vertically. The original cell measures 10 micrometers by 5 micrometers. What are the dimensions of the magnified image in micrometers?

The uniform scaling matrix [[400, 0],[0, 400]] maps (10, 5) to (400*10, 400*5) = (4000, 2000). Uniform scaling preserves the aspect ratio: the image is 400 times larger in both dimensions.

Example: A film studio needs to convert widescreen footage (16:9) to standard format (4:3). They scale the horizontal dimension by 3/4 and the vertical by 1 (no change). A character at position (160, 90) in the original: where are they in the converted frame?

[[3/4, 0],[0, 1]] * (160, 90) = (0.75*160, 1*90) = (120, 90). The horizontal dimension is compressed to 75% while vertical stays the same, which matches the width ratio change from 16:9 to 4:3.
