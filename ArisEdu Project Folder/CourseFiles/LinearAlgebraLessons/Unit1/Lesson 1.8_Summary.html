Lesson 1.8: Applications in Physics - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Physics

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A force F = (10, 0) N is applied to an object. What is the magnitude of this force?

The magnitude is sqrt(10^2 + 0^2) = sqrt(100) = 10 N.

Two forces F1 = (3, 4) N and F2 = (-3, -4) N act on an object. What is the net force?

The net force is F1 + F2 = (3+(-3), 4+(-4)) = (0, 0) N. The forces cancel out, resulting in equilibrium.

A velocity vector is v = (5, 12) m/s. What is the speed (magnitude of velocity)?

Speed = ||v|| = sqrt(5^2 + 12^2) = sqrt(25 + 144) = sqrt(169) = 13 m/s.

A projectile is launched with velocity v = (20, 30) m/s. After 2 seconds with gravity a = (0, -10) m/s^2, what is the velocity? (v_new = v + a*t)

v_new = v + a*t = (20, 30) + (0, -10)*2 = (20, 30) + (0, -20) = (20, 10) m/s.

Momentum is mass times velocity. If an object of mass 3 kg has velocity (4, -2) m/s, what is its momentum?

Momentum p = m*v = 3*(4, -2) = (12, -6) kg*m/s.

What is a 'force vector' in physics?

A force vector captures both how strong a force is (magnitude) and which way it acts (direction). Forces are added as vectors to find the net effect.

What is 'momentum' in vector terms?

Momentum is a vector quantity: p = m * v, where m is the scalar mass and v is the velocity vector. It has the same direction as velocity.

What is the 'resultant' of multiple vectors?

The resultant is the vector sum of all given vectors. It represents the single vector that has the same effect as all the individual vectors combined.

What is 'kinetic energy' and how does the velocity vector relate to it?

Kinetic energy KE = (1/2)m||v||^2 is a scalar that depends on the magnitude of the velocity vector (speed), not its direction.

A car of mass 1500 kg has velocity v = (20, 0) m/s (moving east). The brakes apply a force F = (-3000, 0) N. What is the acceleration vector?

By F = ma, a = F/m = (-3000, 0)/1500 = (-2, 0) m/s^2. The negative sign shows deceleration opposing the eastward motion.

Two soccer players kick a ball simultaneously. Player A applies F_A = (200, 100) N and Player B applies F_B = (100, -150) N. What is the resultant force on the ball?

Resultant force = F_A + F_B = (200+100, 100+(-150)) = (300, -50) N.

A swimmer moves with velocity v_s = (2, 0) m/s (east) in a river with current v_c = (0, 1) m/s (north). What is the swimmer's actual velocity relative to the ground?

The ground velocity is the sum of the swimmer's velocity and the current: v_s + v_c = (2+0, 0+1) = (2, 1) m/s.

In a collision, object A (mass 2 kg, velocity (3, 0) m/s) hits object B (mass 1 kg, at rest). If they stick together, conservation of momentum gives: total momentum before = total momentum after. What is the total momentum before collision?

Total momentum = m_A * v_A + m_B * v_B = 2*(3,0) + 1*(0,0) = (6,0) + (0,0) = (6, 0) kg*m/s.

A pendulum bob at the lowest point has velocity v = (4, 0) m/s and mass 0.5 kg. What is its kinetic energy?

KE = (1/2)*m*||v||^2 = (1/2)*0.5*(4^2+0^2) = (1/2)*0.5*16 = 4 J.

A spacecraft needs to change its velocity from v_i = (100, 200, 50) m/s to v_f = (120, 180, 60) m/s. What is the required change in velocity (delta-v)?

Delta-v = v_f - v_i = (120-100, 180-200, 60-50) = (20, -20, 10) m/s.
