Lesson 1.7: Applications in Geometry - Summary

Key Concepts: Applications in Geometry

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the magnitude (length) of the vector v = (3, 4)?

The magnitude is sqrt(3^2 + 4^2) = sqrt(9 + 16) = sqrt(25) = 5.

What is the midpoint of the line segment from point A = (2, 6) to point B = (8, 4)?

The midpoint is the average of coordinates: ((2+8)/2, (6+4)/2) = (5, 5).

If v = (1, 1), what is ||v|| (the magnitude)?

||v|| = sqrt(1^2 + 1^2) = sqrt(2), which is approximately 1.414.

What is the 'dot product' of two vectors?

The dot product u . v = u1*v1 + u2*v2 + ... + un*vn is a scalar value computed by summing the products of corresponding components.

What is a 'unit vector'?

A unit vector has magnitude (length) exactly 1. Any nonzero vector v can be normalized to a unit vector by dividing by its magnitude: v/||v||.

What is meant by 'collinear' points?

Collinear points lie on the same straight line. Three points A, B, C are collinear if the vector AB is a scalar multiple of AC.

What is the 'projection' of vector u onto vector v?

The projection of u onto v is the vector component of u in the direction of v, computed as ((u . v) / (v . v)) * v.

What is a 'displacement vector'?

A displacement vector represents the difference between two positions: if an object moves from point A to point B, the displacement vector is B - A.

An architect designs a triangular roof with vertices at A = (0, 0), B = (6, 0), and C = (3, 4). What is the length of side AB?

The length of AB is ||B - A|| = ||(6-0, 0-0)|| = ||(6, 0)|| = sqrt(36 + 0) = 6.

A pilot flies from city A at coordinates (100, 200) to city B at (400, 600). Air traffic control asks for the direction as a unit vector. What is it?

Displacement: (400-100, 600-200) = (300, 400). Magnitude: sqrt(90000+160000) = sqrt(250000) = 500. Unit vector: (300/500, 400/500) = (0.6, 0.8).

A farmer wants to find the area of a parallelogram-shaped field with sides defined by vectors (5, 0) and (2, 3). What is the area?

Area = |u1*v2 - u2*v1| = |5*3 - 0*2| = |15 - 0| = 15 square units.

A robot arm needs to project force vector F = (8, 6) onto the direction of motion d = (1, 0). What is the scalar projection (component of F along d)?

Scalar projection = (F . d) / ||d|| = (8*1 + 6*0) / 1 = 8. Only the horizontal component contributes along direction (1, 0).

A game designer places three towns at coordinates A = (0, 0), B = (4, 0), and C = (0, 3). What is the distance from B to C?

Distance from B to C: sqrt((0-4)^2 + (3-0)^2) = sqrt(16+9) = sqrt(25) = 5.

A construction worker needs to verify that two beams meet at exactly 90 degrees. Beam 1 goes in direction (2, 5) and Beam 2 in direction (5, -2). What is their dot product?

Dot product: 2*5 + 5*(-2) = 10 - 10 = 0. A dot product of zero confirms the beams meet at a right angle.

A ship at position (10, 20) needs to travel to a lighthouse at (16, 28). The captain wants the midpoint of the journey for a scheduled radio check-in. What is the midpoint?

Midpoint = ((10+16)/2, (20+28)/2) = (26/2, 48/2) = (13, 24).

A physics simulation needs to decompose velocity v = (10, 10) into a component along the x-axis and a component along the y-axis. What is the component of v along the unit vector (0, 1)?

Projecting v = (10,10) onto (0,1): scalar projection = v . (0,1) = 10. Vector projection = 10 * (0,1) = (0, 10).
