Key Concepts: Applications in Physics

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

A quantum system is in state |psi> = (3/5)|+> + (4/5)|->. If |+> and |-> are eigenstates of an operator with eigenvalues +1 and -1, what is the expectation value?

The expectation value is sum of (probability)(eigenvalue): (3/5)^2(+1) + (4/5)^2(-1) = 9/25 - 16/25 = -7/25.

What is a 'normal mode' in the physics of oscillating systems?

A normal mode is a pattern of oscillation in which all parts of the system move sinusoidally at the same frequency. Each normal mode corresponds to an eigenvector of the system matrix.

What is the 'spectral decomposition' of a Hermitian operator in quantum mechanics?

The spectral decomposition writes A = sum of lambda_i |v_i>

What is the physical meaning of a 'degenerate eigenvalue' in quantum mechanics?

A degenerate eigenvalue means multiple linearly independent eigenstates share the same energy. This degeneracy often arises from symmetry in the system.

What is a 'unitary matrix' and why is it important in quantum mechanics?

A unitary matrix satisfies U^dagger U = I. In quantum mechanics, time evolution is unitary, meaning it preserves inner products and hence probabilities.

What is the 'strain tensor' in continuum mechanics?

The strain tensor is a symmetric matrix describing deformation. Its eigenvalues are the principal strains (maximum and minimum stretching), and eigenvectors are the principal strain directions.

What is an 'Hermitian matrix' and why must physical observables be Hermitian?

An Hermitian matrix satisfies A = A^dagger. Physical observables must be Hermitian because measurement outcomes must be real numbers, and Hermitian matrices are guaranteed to have real eigenvalues.

A quantum computing researcher prepares a qubit in state |psi> = (1/sqrt(2))|0> + (1/sqrt(2))|1> and measures the Pauli-Z operator (eigenvalues +1, -1). What is the probability of getting +1?

The probability of measuring eigenvalue +1 is |<0|psi>|^2 = |1/sqrt(2)|^2 = 1/2. The state has equal amplitudes in both eigenstates, giving 50-50 measurement probabilities.

An astrophysicist models the moment of inertia tensor of an asteroid as I = [[4, 0, 0], [0, 6, 2], [0, 2, 3]] kg*m^2. What is the smallest principal moment of inertia?

One eigenvalue is 4 (from the 1,1 block). For the 2x2 block [[6,2],[2,3]]: trace=9, det=14, eigenvalues = (9 +/- sqrt(81-56))/2 = (9 +/- 5)/2 = 7 and 2. The smallest principal moment is 2 kg*m^2.

Important Concepts

A plasma physicist models charged particle motion in crossed E and B fields. The gyration is described by eigenvalues +/-i*omega_c where omega_c is the cyclotron frequency. What type of motion occurs

Purely imaginary eigenvalues +/-i*omega_c produce circular motion (center-type solution). The particle gyrates around the magnetic field line at the cyclotron frequency omega_c = qB/m.
