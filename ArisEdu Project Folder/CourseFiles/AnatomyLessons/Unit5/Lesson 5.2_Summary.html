Lesson 5.2: Major Endocrine Glands - Summary

Key Concepts: Major Endocrine Glands

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the anterior pituitary?

The anterior pituitary (adenohypophysis) is the glandular lobe of the pituitary gland that independently synthesizes and secretes multiple hormones including growth hormone, TSH, ACTH, FSH, LH, and prolactin in response to hypothalamic releasing hormones.

What is the posterior pituitary?

The posterior pituitary (neurohypophysis) is not a true gland but a neural extension of the hypothalamus that stores and releases ADH and oxytocin, which are manufactured in the hypothalamic nuclei and transported along axons to the posterior lobe.

What is TSH?

TSH (thyroid-stimulating hormone or thyrotropin) is a tropic hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary that acts on the thyroid gland to stimulate the production and release of T3 and T4 thyroid hormones.

What is ACTH?

ACTH (adrenocorticotropic hormone) is a tropic hormone from the anterior pituitary that stimulates the adrenal cortex, specifically the zona fasciculata, to synthesize and secrete cortisol as part of the HPA axis.

What is PTH (parathyroid hormone)?

Parathyroid hormone (PTH) is secreted by the four parathyroid glands in response to low blood calcium and works through three mechanisms: stimulating osteoclasts to release calcium from bone, increasing renal calcium reabsorption, and activating vitamin D to enhance intestinal calcium absorption.

What is the thymus gland?

The thymus is a bilobed organ behind the sternum that functions as both an endocrine gland (producing thymosin) and a lymphoid organ critical for the development and maturation of T lymphocytes, especially during childhood.

What is glucagon?

Glucagon is a peptide hormone secreted by pancreatic alpha cells in response to low blood glucose, acting primarily on the liver to stimulate glycogenolysis (glycogen breakdown) and gluconeogenesis (new glucose synthesis) to raise blood sugar levels.

A patient has a blood glucose level of 350 mg/dL after eating. A medical student asks the attending physician which cells in the pancreas should respond to bring glucose down. What is the correct answer?

Beta cells in the islets of Langerhans detect elevated blood glucose and respond by secreting insulin, which promotes glucose uptake into muscle and fat cells, stimulates glycogen synthesis in the liver, and thereby lowers blood sugar levels.

Real-World Applications

Example: A 10-year-old child is significantly shorter than peers of the same age. After a thorough evaluation, the pediatric endocrinologist finds that the child has very low levels of growth hormone. Which gland is most likely not functioning properly?

Growth hormone is produced by somatotroph cells of the anterior pituitary, so a deficiency in GH production indicates anterior pituitary dysfunction, which can lead to pituitary dwarfism if untreated during childhood.

Example: During a stressful exam, a student feels her heart racing, palms sweating, and breathing becoming rapid. Her professor explains that a specific gland released hormones causing this fight-or-flight response. Which gland is primarily responsible?

The adrenal medulla releases catecholamines (primarily epinephrine and norepinephrine) during sympathetic nervous system activation, producing the classic fight-or-flight responses including increased heart rate, sweating, and bronchodilation.

Example: A shift worker who rotates between day and night shifts reports chronic insomnia and difficulty maintaining a normal sleep schedule. An endocrinologist explains that a small brain gland is being disrupted by irregular light exposure. Which gland and hormone are involved?

The pineal gland secretes melatonin in response to darkness, and irregular light-dark cycles from shift work disrupt normal melatonin secretion patterns, leading to circadian rhythm disorders and insomnia.
