Key Definitions

What is hyperglycemia?

Hyperglycemia means abnormally high blood glucose levels (hyper- = above, glyc- = sugar, -emia = blood condition). It is the hallmark of diabetes mellitus and can lead to serious complications including diabetic ketoacidosis if untreated.

What is diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)?

DKA occurs when the body cannot use glucose for energy (due to lack of insulin) and instead breaks down fat, producing ketone bodies as a byproduct. These ketones are acidic and can lower blood pH to dangerous levels, making DKA a life-threatening emergency.

What is the role of ADH (antidiuretic hormone) in water balance?

ADH (also called vasopressin) acts on the kidney collecting ducts, making them more permeable to water so that more water is reabsorbed into the bloodstream rather than excreted in urine. This helps conserve water during dehydration.

What is thermoregulation?

Thermoregulation is the homeostatic process by which the body maintains its core temperature near 37 degrees C through mechanisms like sweating and vasodilation (to cool) or shivering and vasoconstriction (to warm).

What is vasoconstriction?

Vasoconstriction is the narrowing of blood vessel diameter caused by contraction of smooth muscle in the vessel walls. It serves to reduce blood flow to the skin (conserving heat in cold conditions) or maintain blood pressure during hemorrhage.
