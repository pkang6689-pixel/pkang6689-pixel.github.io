Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the chemical level of organization?

The chemical level is the most fundamental level of structural organization, encompassing atoms (carbon, hydrogen, oxygen) and molecules (water, glucose, DNA) that form the building blocks of all living structures.

What is a tissue in anatomical terms?

A tissue is defined as a group of structurally similar cells, along with their extracellular matrix, that work together to carry out a specific function such as protection, support, or contraction.

What is the organismal level of organization?

The organismal level is the highest level of structural organization, representing the complete living human in which all organ systems function interdependently to sustain life.

What is connective tissue?

Connective tissue is the most widespread and varied tissue type in the body, including bone, blood, cartilage, and adipose (fat), all characterized by cells scattered within an extracellular matrix.

What is epithelial tissue?

Epithelial tissue forms the coverings and linings of body surfaces and cavities (such as the skin and the lining of the digestive tract) and also forms glands that secrete substances like hormones and enzymes.

What is the organ level of organization?

The organ level is where two or more different tissue types unite to form a recognizable structure, such as the heart or liver, that performs one or more specific functions.

Outliers and Resistant Measures

The levels of organization are hierarchically interconnected, so damage at any level (chemical, cellular, tissue, organ, or system) can cascade upward and affect the entire organism, which is why trauma care addresses all levels simultaneously.

Real-World Applications

Example: The stomach is an example of which level of organization?

The stomach is an organ because it is composed of multiple tissue types (epithelial, muscle, connective, and nervous) that work together to perform the function of digestion.

Example: A medical student is examining a tissue sample under a microscope and sees flat cells lining a blood vessel. She notes that this represents which level of organization?

A group of similar cells (such as the flat endothelial cells lining a blood vessel) working together represents the tissue level of organization, which is one step above the cellular level.

Example: A pathologist examining a biopsy sample identifies a group of similar cells embedded in a hard, mineralized matrix. Based on the levels of organization, this sample most likely represents:

Cells embedded in a hard, mineralized extracellular matrix is characteristic of bone (osseous) tissue, which is classified as connective tissue at the tissue level of organization.

Example: A patient is diagnosed with liver cirrhosis, which damages all tissue types within the liver. A medical student realizes this demonstrates that the liver is classified as an organ because:

The liver qualifies as an organ because it contains epithelial tissue (hepatocytes and bile ducts), connective tissue (blood vessels and stroma), smooth muscle, and nerve fibers, all working together to carry out metabolic functions.
