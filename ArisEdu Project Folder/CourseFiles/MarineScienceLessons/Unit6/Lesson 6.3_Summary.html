Lesson 6.3: Crustaceans (Crabs, Lobsters, Shrimp) - Summary

Key Concepts: Crustaceans (Crabs, Lobsters, Shrimp)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the hard outer covering of a crustacean called?

Crustaceans have a hard outer covering called an exoskeleton, which is made primarily of chitin. This rigid structure provides protection and support but must be shed periodically as the animal grows.

What is the primary material that makes up a crustacean's exoskeleton?

The exoskeleton of crustaceans is primarily composed of chitin, a tough polysaccharide. In many crustaceans, the chitin is also reinforced with calcium carbonate, making the shell especially hard.

What is the cephalothorax?

The cephalothorax is the body region formed by the fusion of the head (cephalo-) and thorax. It houses the brain, eyes, mouthparts, and is covered by the protective carapace in many crustaceans.

What is a carapace?

The carapace is the hard, protective shield that covers the dorsal side of the cephalothorax in many crustaceans. It protects vital internal organs and often houses the gill chambers.

What is a zoea?

A zoea is an early larval stage of many crustaceans, particularly crabs and shrimp. Zoea larvae are tiny, planktonic organisms that look very different from their adult forms and undergo several molts before reaching the juvenile stage.

What is the telson?

The telson is the last segment of the crustacean abdomen, forming the central part of the tail fan. Together with the uropods (paired tail appendages), the telson helps lobsters and shrimp perform rapid backward escape movements.

What is an arthropod?

Arthropods are invertebrates characterized by jointed appendages, segmented bodies, and an exoskeleton. Crustaceans belong to the phylum Arthropoda, which is the largest animal phylum and also includes insects, spiders, and centipedes.

A marine biologist notices that blue crabs in a coastal bay are growing faster than usual during an unusually warm summer. What is the most likely explanation?

Warmer water temperatures increase the metabolic rate of ectothermic crustaceans, which can lead to more frequent molting and faster growth. However, this also means they need more food to sustain the higher metabolic demand.

After a major oil spill, researchers find that fiddler crab populations in contaminated marshes have declined significantly. What is the most likely reason for their vulnerability?

Fiddler crabs live in intertidal sediments and are directly exposed to oil that settles on mudflats. Oil can coat their gills, blocking gas exchange, and contaminate the sediment they burrow in and feed from, causing toxicity and death.

Real-World Applications

Example: A lobster fisherman finds a lobster that has just molted and its shell is very soft. Why is this lobster especially vulnerable to predators at this stage?

Immediately after molting, the new exoskeleton is soft and provides little protection. The lobster is also weakened from the energy-intensive molting process. It takes several days to weeks for the new shell to fully harden.

Example: An invasive species of shore crab has established itself in a new region, outcompeting native crabs for food and habitat. What management approach would most directly address this problem?

Active removal through trapping combined with habitat restoration is the most direct and commonly used approach for managing invasive crustacean species. Introducing additional non-native species could create further ecological problems.

Example: A commercial crab fishery notices declining catches over several years. Which management strategy would best help the crab population recover?

Setting minimum size limits ensures juveniles can grow to reproductive age, while seasonal closures protect crabs during breeding periods. Together these measures allow the population to replenish itself sustainably.
