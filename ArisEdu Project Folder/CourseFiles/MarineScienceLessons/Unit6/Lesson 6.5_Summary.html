Key Concepts: Case Studies in Invertebrate Biology

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is coral bleaching?

Coral bleaching occurs when stressed corals expel their symbiotic zooxanthellae algae, which give corals their color and provide up to 90% of their energy through photosynthesis. Without these algae, the coral turns white and may starve if the stress continues.

What is the primary benefit that coral reef ecosystems provide to coastal communities?

Coral reefs act as natural breakwaters, reducing wave energy by up to 97% and protecting coastlines from storm damage and erosion. They also support fisheries that provide food and income for hundreds of millions of people worldwide.

What is an invasive species?

An invasive species is a non-native organism that has been introduced (intentionally or accidentally) to an ecosystem outside its natural range and causes harm. Invasive species often lack natural predators, allowing them to outcompete native species and disrupt ecosystems.

What is meant by 'trophic cascade' in marine ecology?

A trophic cascade occurs when changes in the abundance of organisms at one trophic level ripple through the food web. For example, removing top predators can cause prey populations to explode, which then overgraze producers—a cascading effect across multiple trophic levels.

What is 'bycatch' in commercial fishing?

Bycatch refers to the unintentional capture of non-target species such as sea turtles, dolphins, sharks, and juvenile fish during commercial fishing operations. Bycatch is a major conservation concern and contributes to the decline of many marine species.

A coastal community that depends on horseshoe crab blood harvesting for LAL production notices declining horseshoe crab populations. What is the most sustainable approach going forward?

Recombinant Factor C (rFC) is a synthetic alternative to LAL that does not require horseshoe crab blood. Transitioning to rFC while reducing wild harvesting allows continued pharmaceutical safety testing while protecting declining horseshoe crab populations and the shorebirds that depend on their eggs.

Researchers found that when coral reefs bleach, the populations of reef fish that depend on coral for shelter also decline. A nearby seagrass bed, however, shows increased fish density. What is happening?

When coral habitat degrades, many species seek refuge in alternative habitats like seagrass beds. This demonstrates the functional connectivity between marine habitats and why protecting a network of different habitats, not just coral reefs, is important for marine conservation.

Real-World Applications

Example: Which invasive crustacean has caused significant ecological damage to coastal ecosystems in North America and Europe?

The European green crab (Carcinus maenas) is one of the world's most successful invasive species. It outcompetes native crabs, preys on shellfish, damages eelgrass beds, and undermines shoreline stability by burrowing into salt marsh banks.
