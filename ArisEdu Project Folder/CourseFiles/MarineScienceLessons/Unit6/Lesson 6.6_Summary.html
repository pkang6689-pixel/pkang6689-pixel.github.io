Lesson 6.6: AP Prep: Comparative Anatomy - Summary

Key Concepts: AP Prep: Comparative Anatomy

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the term for structures in different species that share a common evolutionary origin but may serve different functions?

Homologous structures share a common ancestral origin even though they may serve different functions in different species. For example, the flipper of a whale and the arm of a human are homologous because they evolved from the same limb structure in a common ancestor.

What is a 'vestigial structure'?

A vestigial structure is a body part that has lost most or all of its original function through evolution. These structures provide evidence of an organism's evolutionary history. Examples include the tiny hind limb bones in some whale and snake species.

What is a 'shared derived characteristic' (synapomorphy) in cladistics?

A synapomorphy is a derived (newly evolved) trait shared by two or more groups that inherited it from their most recent common ancestor. In cladistics, synapomorphies are used to determine which organisms belong to the same clade or evolutionary group.

What is meant by 'comparative anatomy'?

Comparative anatomy is the study of similarities and differences in the anatomical structures of different species. By comparing body structures, scientists can infer evolutionary relationships and understand how organisms have adapted to their environments over time.

While dissecting a dolphin, a veterinary student finds tiny bones embedded in the body wall near the pelvis that do not connect to any other bones. What is the most likely explanation?

These small, unattached bones are vestigial pelvic bones, remnants of the hind limb skeleton that dolphins' terrestrial ancestors used for walking. As dolphins evolved into fully aquatic animals, their hind limbs disappeared, but small traces of the pelvic structure remain embedded in muscle tissue.

Real-World Applications

Example: Sharks and dolphins both have streamlined body shapes and dorsal fins even though they are not closely related. This is an example of which evolutionary process?

Convergent evolution occurs when unrelated organisms independently evolve similar traits due to similar environmental pressures. Sharks (fish) and dolphins (mammals) evolved similar body plans because both are adapted to fast swimming in the ocean.
