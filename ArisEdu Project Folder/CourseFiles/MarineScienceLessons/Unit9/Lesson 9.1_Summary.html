Lesson 9.1: Submersibles & ROVs - Summary

Key Concepts: Submersibles & ROVs

Overview

Key Definitions

What is the primary difference between a manned submersible and an unmanned submersible?

The fundamental distinction is that manned (human-occupied) submersibles carry people aboard, while unmanned submersibles operate without human occupants. Unmanned vehicles are controlled remotely or autonomously.

What is a key advantage of AUVs over ROVs?

AUVs (Autonomous Underwater Vehicles) are untethered and pre-programmed to carry out missions independently. This gives them greater range and flexibility compared to ROVs, which are limited by their tether length.

What is the typical depth rating of the Alvin submersible after its most recent upgrades?

After upgrades completed in recent years, Alvin's depth rating was increased to 6,500 meters, allowing it to reach approximately 98% of the ocean floor. This is a significant increase from its previous 4,500-meter rating.

What is an ROV?

ROV stands for Remotely Operated Vehicle. It is an unmanned underwater robot connected to a ship by cables, allowing operators to control it in real time while receiving live video and sensor data from the deep sea.

What is a bathyscaphe?

A bathyscaphe is a type of deep-diving submersible that uses a large tank filled with a lighter-than-water liquid (like gasoline) for buoyancy and iron shot ballast for descent. The Trieste, which reached the Challenger Deep, was a bathyscaphe.

What is a depth rating?

A depth rating is the maximum depth to which a submersible or instrument is designed and certified to safely operate. Exceeding this depth could compromise the structural integrity of the vessel due to extreme pressure.

What is an umbilical cable in ROV operations?

An umbilical cable is the tether that physically connects an ROV to the support vessel on the surface. It transmits electrical power to the ROV and carries video, sensor data, and control commands between the operator and the vehicle.

What is meant by the term 'submersible' in ocean science?

A submersible is a small watercraft designed to operate below the surface of the water. Unlike submarines, submersibles typically have limited power reserves, require a support ship, and are used primarily for research and exploration.

What is syntactic foam?

Syntactic foam is an engineered composite material consisting of tiny hollow glass or ceramic spheres embedded in a polymer resin. It provides buoyancy while withstanding the extreme pressures of the deep ocean, making it essential for submersible construction.

What is a launch-and-recovery system (LARS)?

A LARS is specialized mechanical equipment installed on a support vessel used to safely deploy and recover submersibles, ROVs, and other heavy underwater equipment over the side or stern of the ship.

During an ROV dive, the umbilical cable becomes tangled on a rocky outcrop at 2,500 m depth. What is the most likely consequence?

A tangled umbilical is a serious operational risk for ROVs. It can restrict the vehicle's movement, damage the cable, or sever the connection entirely, leading to loss of power, video feed, and control. Recovery operations can be complex and costly.

A research institution has limited funding and needs to survey a deep-sea canyon for geological features. They cannot afford a dedicated support ship for an ROV. What is their best option?

AUVs can be deployed from smaller vessels because they do not require the heavy winches, large A-frames, and extensive deck space needed for ROV umbilical management. This makes them more cost-effective for institutions with limited budgets.

Real-World Applications

Example: What is a key advantage of AUVs over ROVs?

AUVs (Autonomous Underwater Vehicles) are untethered and pre-programmed to carry out missions independently. This gives them greater range and flexibility compared to ROVs, which are limited by their tether length.

Example: A documentary film crew wants to shoot footage of deep-sea life at 4,000 m while having a marine biologist provide live narration of what they see. Which approach is best?

An ROV provides real-time video transmission to the surface via its umbilical cable, enabling live commentary and interactive exploration. An AUV could record footage but wouldn't allow real-time narration or steering adjustments based on what is observed.
