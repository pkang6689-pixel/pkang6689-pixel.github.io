Lesson 1.6: Case Studies in Ocean Research - Summary

Key Concepts: Case Studies in Ocean Research

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the deepest known point in the ocean, explored by the bathyscaphe Trieste in 1960?

The Challenger Deep, located in the Mariana Trench, reaches a depth of approximately 10,935 meters (about 36,000 feet). In 1960, Jacques Piccard and Don Walsh descended to the bottom aboard the bathyscaphe Trieste, making it the first crewed dive to the deepest point on Earth.

What is a hydrothermal vent?

A hydrothermal vent is an opening on the ocean floor where geothermally heated water, rich in dissolved minerals, erupts into the cold surrounding seawater. These vents often support unique ecosystems of organisms that rely on chemosynthesis rather than photosynthesis.

What is a marine protected area (MPA)?

A marine protected area (MPA) is a defined region of ocean or coast where human activities such as fishing, mining, or development are limited or prohibited to conserve marine habitats and biodiversity. MPAs range from fully no-take reserves to areas with managed-use regulations.

What is chemosynthesis?

Chemosynthesis is a biological process in which organisms produce energy by oxidizing inorganic chemicals such as hydrogen sulfide or methane, rather than relying on sunlight. This process was first observed in ecosystems around hydrothermal vents, where it forms the base of the food chain in total darkness.

What is a bathyscaphe?

A bathyscaphe is a type of deep-diving submersible consisting of a crew cabin attached to a large float filled with gasoline for buoyancy. The Trieste, the most famous bathyscaphe, carried two explorers to the Challenger Deep in 1960, reaching the greatest ocean depth ever recorded at that time.

What is 'bioremediation' in the context of oil spill response?

Bioremediation involves using naturally occurring or introduced microorganisms to degrade petroleum compounds into less harmful substances. After the Deepwater Horizon spill, bioremediation played a significant role as oil-eating bacteria helped break down hydrocarbons in the Gulf of Mexico.

What is 'satellite oceanography'?

Satellite oceanography uses instruments aboard Earth-orbiting satellites to measure sea surface temperature, ocean color (chlorophyll), sea level, wave height, and other properties. This field has revolutionized our ability to monitor global ocean conditions continuously and in near real-time.

A team of marine biologists discovers a thriving community of large tube worms, clams, and bacteria near a volcanic ridge on the deep ocean floor, far from any sunlight. What is the most likely primary energy source for this ecosystem?

The presence of tube worms, clams, and bacterial mats near a volcanic ridge strongly indicates a hydrothermal vent community. In these ecosystems, chemosynthetic bacteria use hydrogen sulfide from vent fluids as an energy source, forming the base of the food web in total darkness.

Real-World Applications

Example: Which expedition is widely considered the first major oceanographic research voyage?

The HMS Challenger expedition (1872–1876) is considered the birth of modern oceanography. The ship traveled nearly 70,000 nautical miles and collected data on ocean temperatures, currents, chemistry, and thousands of new marine species.

Example: The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is primarily composed of what type of pollution?

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch is a massive accumulation of plastic debris and microplastics concentrated by ocean currents in the North Pacific Subtropical Gyre. Studies estimate it covers an area roughly twice the size of Texas and contains trillions of plastic fragments.

Example: Which U.S. federal agency is the primary organization responsible for ocean and atmospheric research in the United States?

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) is the leading U.S. agency for ocean and atmospheric science. NOAA conducts research, manages fisheries, monitors weather and climate, and maintains a fleet of research vessels and satellites.

Example: A satellite image shows a large area of ocean with unusually high sea surface temperatures persisting for several weeks near a coral reef. Based on ocean research case studies, what outcome would scientists most likely predict?

Prolonged elevated sea surface temperatures are the primary trigger for mass coral bleaching events. Research on the Great Barrier Reef and other reef systems has repeatedly shown that when water temperatures rise even 1–2°C above the seasonal maximum for several weeks, corals expel their zooxanthellae and bleach.

Example: A coral restoration team attaches small coral fragments to underwater frames in a damaged reef area and monitors their growth over two years. This approach is an example of which conservation strategy?

Coral gardening involves growing coral fragments in nurseries or on artificial structures and then transplanting them onto degraded reefs. This active restoration technique has been used successfully in projects around the world, including in the Caribbean and the Great Barrier Reef, to accelerate reef recovery.
