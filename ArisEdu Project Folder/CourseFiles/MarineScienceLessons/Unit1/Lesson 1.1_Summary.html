Key Concepts: What is Marine Science?

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is oceanography?

Oceanography is a major branch of marine science that examines seawater, currents, waves, seafloor features, and marine life.

What is a marine ecosystem?

An ecosystem includes living things and the nonliving environment around them. In marine ecosystems, that includes water, light, temperature, nutrients, and habitat.

What is an estuary?

Estuaries are transition zones between river systems and the ocean. Because nutrients are plentiful there, they are among the most productive habitats on Earth.

What is the intertidal zone?

The intertidal zone is covered with water at high tide and exposed at low tide. Organisms there must survive changing moisture, temperature, and wave conditions.

What is salinity?

Salinity measures how much dissolved salt is present in water. It affects water density, circulation, and which organisms can survive in a habitat.

What is marine conservation?

Marine conservation aims to protect habitats, preserve biodiversity, and manage fisheries and coastal resources so ecosystems remain healthy over time.

After several days of heavy rain, a scientist measures lower salinity in an estuary. What is the best explanation?

Salinity is the amount of dissolved salt in water. When more river water enters an estuary, it lowers salinity by diluting the seawater already there.
