Key Concepts: Oceanographic Disciplines (Biology, Chemistry, Geology, Physics)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is biological oceanography?

Biological oceanography is the branch that investigates marine organisms — from microscopic plankton to large marine mammals — and the ecological relationships that connect them.

What is geological oceanography?

Geological oceanography examines the geology of the ocean floor, including seafloor features, sediment types, and tectonic processes that shape ocean basins.

What is physical oceanography?

Physical oceanography investigates the physical characteristics and movements of ocean water, such as wave formation, tidal cycles, current systems, and temperature-density relationships.

What is ocean acidification?

Ocean acidification is a chemical process in which the ocean absorbs excess atmospheric CO2, forming carbonic acid, which lowers the water's pH. It is a key topic in chemical oceanography.

What is a mid-ocean ridge?

A mid-ocean ridge is a long underwater mountain chain where tectonic plates move apart and magma rises to form new oceanic crust. This is a central concept in geological oceanography.

What is a thermocline?

A thermocline is a distinct layer in the ocean where temperature drops sharply with depth. It is an important concept in physical oceanography because it affects water density and circulation.

What is seafloor sediment?

Seafloor sediment consists of particles that accumulate on the ocean bottom, originating from land erosion, volcanic activity, and biological remains. Studying these sediments is a key focus of geological oceanography.
