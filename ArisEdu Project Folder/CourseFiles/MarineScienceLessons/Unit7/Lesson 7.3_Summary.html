Lesson 7.3: Marine Reptiles (Sea Turtles, Sea Snakes) - Summary

Key Concepts: Marine Reptiles (Sea Turtles, Sea Snakes)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the primary diet of the green sea turtle as an adult?

Adult green sea turtles are primarily herbivorous, feeding on seagrasses and algae. They are the only sea turtle species that is largely herbivorous as adults. Their name comes from the green color of their body fat, which results from their plant-based diet.

What is the largest living reptile that inhabits marine and brackish water environments?

The saltwater crocodile is the largest living reptile, with males reaching up to 6-7 meters in length. It inhabits coastal and brackish waters across Southeast Asia and Australia and has salt-excreting glands on its tongue.

What is a major threat to sea turtles from commercial fishing operations?

Bycatch is one of the greatest threats to sea turtle populations. Turtles become accidentally entangled in trawl nets, gill nets, and longlines intended for fish and shrimp, often leading to drowning since they must surface to breathe.

What is temperature-dependent sex determination (TSD)?

Temperature-dependent sex determination is a form of environmental sex determination where the temperature during a critical period of egg incubation determines the sex of the offspring. In sea turtles, higher temperatures typically produce females and lower temperatures produce males.

What is a salt gland in marine reptiles?

Salt glands in marine reptiles are specialized organs that filter excess salt from the blood and excrete it as a highly concentrated solution. In sea turtles, these are located near the eyes; in marine iguanas, they are nasal glands.

What is a 'carapace'?

The carapace is the dorsal (upper) portion of a turtle's shell. In most sea turtles, it is made of bony plates covered with keratinous scutes. The leatherback is unique in having a flexible, leathery carapace rather than a rigid, bony one.

What is a 'plastron'?

The plastron is the flat, ventral (underside) portion of a turtle's shell. Together with the carapace (top), it forms the turtle's protective shell. In sea turtles, the plastron is relatively small compared to freshwater turtles.

What is a 'neurotoxin'?

A neurotoxin is a poisonous substance that acts on the nervous system, disrupting nerve signal transmission. Sea snake venom contains potent neurotoxins that can cause paralysis and respiratory failure in prey and potentially in humans.

Researchers monitoring a sea turtle nesting beach notice that over 90% of hatchlings are female in recent years. What is the most likely cause?

Due to temperature-dependent sex determination, warmer nest temperatures produce more females. As climate change raises global and sand temperatures, nesting beaches are producing heavily female-biased clutches, which could threaten population viability if males become too scarce.

A Galápagos marine iguana is observed sneezing a white crystalline substance from its nostrils after returning from feeding in the ocean. What is happening?

Marine iguanas have nasal salt glands that filter excess salt from their blood. After feeding on marine algae in saltwater, they 'sneeze' out concentrated salt crystals through their nostrils. This is a normal and essential osmoregulatory behavior.

A loggerhead sea turtle is tracked by satellite as it migrates 10,000 km across the Atlantic Ocean. What is the primary reason for this migration?

Sea turtles undertake long migrations between nutrient-rich feeding grounds and their natal nesting beaches. Loggerheads born on beaches in the southeastern US may feed across the Atlantic before returning to nest. Navigation is guided by Earth's magnetic field.

A marine biologist studying hawksbill turtles notices their population decline correlates with increased coral bleaching events. What is the most likely connection?

Hawksbill turtles rely on healthy coral reefs as habitat for their primary food source — sponges. When coral bleaching degrades reef ecosystems, sponge populations decline along with overall reef health, reducing food availability for hawksbill turtles.

A wildlife rescue center receives a cold-stunned sea turtle found in unusually cold northern waters. The turtle is lethargic and unresponsive. Knowing that sea turtles are ectotherms, what is the most appropriate treatment?

Cold-stunned sea turtles must be warmed gradually to avoid thermal shock, which can be fatal. As ectotherms, their body temperature matches their environment. Slow rewarming allows their metabolism to safely increase, and they can then be released in warmer waters.

Important Concepts

A conservation team wants to protect a sea turtle nesting beach from the effects of climate change. Which intervention would most directly address the risk of skewed sex ratios

Shading nests with vegetation or artificial covers, or relocating eggs to cooler nest sites, directly reduces sand temperature during incubation. This can help produce more male hatchlings and counteract the feminizing effect of rising temperatures.
