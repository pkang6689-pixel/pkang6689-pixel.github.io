Lesson 7.4: Marine Birds (Penguins, Albatross) - Summary

Key Concepts: Marine Birds (Penguins, Albatross)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is a major modern threat to seabirds worldwide?

Plastic pollution is a devastating threat to seabirds. Many species, especially albatrosses and petrels, mistake floating plastic for food and ingest it or feed it to their chicks. This causes starvation, internal injuries, and toxic chemical exposure.

What is 'dynamic soaring'?

Dynamic soaring is a flight technique used by albatrosses and other pelagic seabirds. By alternately climbing into stronger headwinds at higher altitudes and descending into calmer air near the surface, they extract energy from the wind gradient and can fly for hours without flapping.

What is the 'uropygial gland' (preen gland)?

The uropygial gland, located at the base of a bird's tail, secretes a waxy oil that birds spread over their feathers during preening. In seabirds, this waterproofing oil is critical for maintaining feather insulation and buoyancy in marine environments.

What is 'countercurrent heat exchange' in penguins?

In countercurrent heat exchange, arteries carrying warm blood from the body core pass close to veins returning cold blood from the flippers and feet. Heat transfers from the arteries to the veins, warming the returning blood and reducing heat loss through the extremities.

What is 'guano'?

Guano is the accumulated excrement of seabirds (or bats), composed primarily of uric acid, nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. It has historically been harvested as a valuable fertilizer and plays a natural role in nutrient cycling on coastal islands.

What is a 'breeding colony' in seabirds?

A breeding colony is a dense aggregation of seabirds nesting together in one location, often on remote islands. Colonial nesting provides safety in numbers against predators and helps birds find mates. Some colonies contain millions of individuals.

What is 'plunge-diving'?

Plunge-diving is a foraging strategy where seabirds like gannets, boobies, and pelicans spot fish from the air, then dive headfirst at speeds up to 100 km/h to catch prey underwater. Specialized anatomical features protect them from the impact.

What is a 'gular pouch'?

A gular pouch is a flexible, expandable throat sac found in birds like pelicans and frigatebirds. Pelicans use theirs as a scoop net for catching fish, while male frigatebirds inflate their bright red gular pouch as a courtship display.

A marine ecology class finds that islands with large seabird colonies have significantly more productive surrounding coral reefs than islands without seabirds. What is the most likely explanation?

Seabird guano is extremely rich in nitrogen and phosphorus. Rain washes these nutrients from island nesting sites into surrounding waters, fertilizing the marine ecosystem and boosting productivity of nearby coral reefs through nutrient cycling.

During a rescue operation, a wildlife team finds a seabird that cannot stay afloat and is rapidly losing body temperature. Upon examination, its feathers are matted with an oily substance. What is the connection between the matted feathers and hypothermia?

Seabird feathers work by trapping a layer of air close to the body that provides buoyancy and insulation. When feathers are matted by oil, this air layer is lost, cold water contacts the skin directly, and the bird rapidly loses body heat, leading to hypothermia.
