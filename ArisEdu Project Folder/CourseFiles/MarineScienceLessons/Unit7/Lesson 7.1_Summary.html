Key Concepts: Fish (Cartilaginous vs. Bony)

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

Key Definitions

What is the primary function of gills in fish?

Gills are the respiratory organs of fish, allowing them to extract dissolved oxygen from water and expel carbon dioxide. Water flows over the gill filaments where gas exchange occurs across thin membranes.

What is a common advantage of schooling behavior in fish?

Schooling behavior helps fish avoid predators through the confusion effect, making it harder for a predator to single out one individual. It also provides hydrodynamic advantages and increases foraging efficiency.

What is the lateral line system in fish?

The lateral line system is a series of sensory receptors running along the sides of fish that detect vibrations, water movement, and pressure changes. It helps fish navigate, detect predators, and locate prey even in dark or murky water.

What is a dermal denticle?

Dermal denticles are placoid scales found on sharks and rays that are structurally similar to teeth, with a dentine core and enamel-like coating. They reduce hydrodynamic drag and protect the animal's skin.

What is a swim bladder?

A swim bladder is an internal gas-filled organ found in most bony fish that allows them to adjust their buoyancy. By inflating or deflating the bladder, a fish can remain at a given depth without expending energy to swim.

What is countercurrent exchange?

Countercurrent exchange is a biological mechanism where two fluids flow in opposite directions across a membrane, maintaining a gradient that maximizes the transfer of substances. In fish gills, this allows highly efficient oxygen extraction from water.

Real-World Applications

Example: What is a common advantage of schooling behavior in fish?

Schooling behavior helps fish avoid predators through the confusion effect, making it harder for a predator to single out one individual. It also provides hydrodynamic advantages and increases foraging efficiency.

Example: A marine biologist observes a fish that has no swim bladder, a skeleton made entirely of cartilage, and rough skin. Using gill slits instead of an operculum, the fish must swim continuously. What type of fish is this most likely?

All the described features — cartilage skeleton, no swim bladder, rough placoid scales, exposed gill slits, and obligate swimming — are characteristic of cartilaginous fish (Chondrichthyes). Sharks and most rays must keep swimming to ventilate their gills.

Example: Scientists compare the oxygen extraction efficiency of fish gills to mammalian lungs and find fish gills extract a much higher percentage of available oxygen. What mechanism best explains this?

Countercurrent flow in fish gills ensures that blood always encounters water with a higher oxygen concentration, maintaining a diffusion gradient along the entire gill surface. This allows fish to extract up to 80-90% of dissolved oxygen from water.

Example: A fishery biologist examines a fish and finds it has smooth, round, overlapping scales without comb-like edges. What type of scales are these, and what group of fish likely has them?

Cycloid scales are smooth, circular, and overlap like shingles. They are found on many bony fish such as salmon and trout. Unlike ctenoid scales, they lack the tiny comb-like teeth on their edges.
