Lesson 6.4: Transformations of Quadratic Functions

Key Concepts: Transformations of Quadratic Functions

y = a(x − h)² + k: h shifts horizontally, k shifts vertically, a stretches/compresses and reflects.

|a| > 1: vertical stretch (narrower). 0 < |a| < 1: vertical compression (wider).

a < 0: reflection over the x-axis.

Combine transformations: e.g., y = −2(x − 3)² + 1 is a reflection, stretch by 2, right 3, up 1.
