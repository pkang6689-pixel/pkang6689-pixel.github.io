Lesson 3.2: Line of Best Fit

Key Concepts: Line of Best Fit

A line of best fit (trend line) is a straight line that best represents the data in a scatter plot.

The line minimizes the distances between the data points and the line. The most common method is least-squares regression.

The equation is written as Å· = mx + b, where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.

Use the line of best fit to predict values. Interpolation (within data range) is more reliable than extrapolation (beyond data range).
