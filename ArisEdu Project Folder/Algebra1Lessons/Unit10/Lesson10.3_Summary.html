Lesson 10.3: Rational Exponents

Key Concepts: Rational Exponents

A rational exponent a^(m/n) = ⁿ√(aᵐ) = (ⁿ√a)ᵐ.

a^(1/2) = √a, a^(1/3) = ∛a, a^(2/3) = (∛a)² = ∛(a²).

Laws of exponents apply to rational exponents: aᵐ · aⁿ = a^(m+n), (aᵐ)ⁿ = a^(mn), etc.

Rational exponents provide an alternative notation for radicals.
