Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations

Key Concepts: Solving Rational Equations

A rational equation has at least one rational expression. Solve by multiplying every term by the LCD to clear fractions.

After clearing, solve the resulting polynomial equation.

Always check for extraneous solutions — solutions that make a denominator zero are not valid.

Example: 1/x + 1/2 = 1 → LCD = 2x → 2 + x = 2x → x = 2.
