Lesson 5.5: Compound Interest

Key Concepts: Compound Interest

The compound interest formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compoundings per year, t = time in years.

Common compounding: annually (n=1), semi-annually (n=2), quarterly (n=4), monthly (n=12), daily (n=365).

Continuous compounding: A = Pe^(rt).

Compound interest grows faster than simple interest (I = Prt) because interest earns interest.
