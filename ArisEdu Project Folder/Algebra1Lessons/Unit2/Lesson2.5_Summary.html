Lesson 2.5: One-Step & Two-Step Inequalities

Key Concepts: One-Step & Two-Step Inequalities

Inequalities use the symbols: < (less than), > (greater than), ≤ (less than or equal), ≥ (greater than or equal).

Solve inequalities like equations, but flip the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.

Graph solutions on a number line: use an open circle for < or >, and a closed circle for ≤ or ≥.

Example: −2x > 6 → x < −3 (flip the sign because you divided by −2).
