Lesson 12.3: Conditional Probability

Key Concepts: Conditional Probability

Conditional probability: P(B|A) = P(A and B) / P(A).

P(B|A) is the probability of B occurring given that A has already occurred.

Use two-way tables to calculate conditional probabilities by restricting to a row or column.

If P(B|A) = P(B), then A and B are independent.
