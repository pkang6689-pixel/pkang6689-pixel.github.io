Lesson 4.5: Rate of Change & Slope

Key Concepts: Rate of Change & Slope

The rate of change describes how one quantity changes relative to another.

Slope = rise/run = (y₂ − y₁)/(x₂ − x₁). It measures the steepness and direction of a line.

Positive slope = line rises left to right. Negative slope = line falls. Zero slope = horizontal. Undefined slope = vertical.

In context, slope represents the rate at which the dependent variable changes per unit of the independent variable.
