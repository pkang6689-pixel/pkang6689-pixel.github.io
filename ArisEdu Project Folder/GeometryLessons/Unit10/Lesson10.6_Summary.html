Lesson 10.6: Secants, Tangents, and Angle Measures

Key Concepts: Secants, Tangents, and Angle Measures

Angles Formed by Secants and Tangents

  • Two secants from an interior point: angle = ½(sum of intercepted arcs).
  • Two secants from an exterior point: angle = ½(difference of intercepted arcs).
  • Secant and tangent from an exterior point: angle = ½(difference of intercepted arcs).
  • Two tangents from an exterior point: angle = ½(difference of intercepted arcs).
  • Tangent and chord: angle = ½(intercepted arc).

Key Insight

For interior angles: use the sum of arcs. For exterior angles: use the difference of arcs.
