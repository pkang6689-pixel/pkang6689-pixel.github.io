Lesson 11.5: Areas of Similar Figures - Quiz

1. Two similar figures have a scale factor of 3:5. What is the ratio of their areas?

2. Two similar triangles have areas of 16 cm² and 64 cm². What is the scale factor of their corresponding sides?

3. If the scale factor between two similar figures is 2:7, what is the ratio of their perimeters?

4. A figure has an area of 50 cm². A similar figure has a scale factor of 3 relative to the original. What is the area of the larger figure?

5. The ratio of the areas of two similar pentagons is 25:81. What is the ratio of their corresponding sides?

6. Does the area ratio rule for similar figures apply only to triangles?

7. Two similar rectangles have perimeters of 20 cm and 30 cm. If the smaller has an area of 24 cm², what is the area of the larger?
