Lesson 12.7: Spherical Geometry ⭐

Key Concepts: Spherical Geometry ⭐

Lines in Spherical Geometry

In spherical geometry, "lines" are great circles. There are no parallel lines — every pair of great circles intersects at two points.

Triangles on a Sphere

  • The sum of angles in a spherical triangle is greater than 180° and less than 540°.
  • The excess over 180° is related to the triangle's area.

Comparing Euclidean and Spherical Geometry

  • Euclidean: through a point not on a line, exactly one parallel exists (Parallel Postulate).
  • Spherical: no parallels exist — all great circles intersect.
  • Euclidean: triangle angle sum = 180°. Spherical: triangle angle sum > 180°.
