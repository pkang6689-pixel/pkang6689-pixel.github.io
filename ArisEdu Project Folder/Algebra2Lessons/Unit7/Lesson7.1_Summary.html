Lesson 7.1: Counting Principles

Key Concepts: Counting Principles

Fundamental Counting Principle: If first event has m outcomes and second event has n outcomes, total outcomes is m × n. Extends to multiple events: multiply all possibilities. Example: 3 appetizers, 4 mains, 2 desserts → 3 × 4 × 2 = 24 meals.

Permutations: Arrangement of objects where ORDER MATTERS. n! = n × (n-1) × ... × 1. Permutations of n objects taken r at a time: P(n,r) = n!/(n-r)!. Example: arranging 3 of 5 books: P(5,3) = 5!/2! = 60.

Factorial: 0! = 1 (definition), 1! = 1, 2! = 2, 3! = 6, 4! = 24, etc. Grows very fast. Used in permutation and combination calculations.

Applications: Passwords: each position could be different letter/digit. Seating arrangements. Top finishers in race. Anagrams of words (with and without repeated letters).
