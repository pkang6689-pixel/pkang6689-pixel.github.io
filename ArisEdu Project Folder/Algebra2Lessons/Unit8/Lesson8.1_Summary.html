Lesson 8.1: Angles & Angle Measures

Key Concepts: Angles & Angle Measures

Degree Measure: Full rotation = 360°. Right angle = 90°. Straight angle = 180°. Acute angle < 90°, obtuse angle 90° < angle < 180°. Angle measured counterclockwise from standard position (positive ray of x-axis).

Radian Measure: Based on arc length. 1 radian = arc length equals radius. Full rotation = 2π radians = 360°. Conversion: radians = degrees × π/180°, degrees = radians × 180°/π.

Coterminal Angles: Angles that differ by multiple of 360° (or 2π radians). Example: 30°, 390°, -330° are coterminal. Coterminal angle formula: θ + 360n° or θ + 2πn.

Reference Angles: Acute angle between terminal side and x-axis. Used to find trig values for any angle. Reference angle always between 0° and 90°.
