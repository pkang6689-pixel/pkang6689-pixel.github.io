--- Lesson 10.1: Derivative as Slope of Tangent Line (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Derivative as Slope of Tangent Line
Derivative as Slope of Tangent Line
The derivative of f at x = a is defined as the limit of the difference quotient:
  • f'(a) = lim_{h→0} [f(a+h) − f(a)] / hGeometric Interpretation  • f'(a) = slope of the tangent line to y = f(x) at x = a.  • The tangent line is the best linear approximation of f near x = a.  • Equation: y − f(a) = f'(a)(x − a).

    [ ] x
    [ ] x²
    [*] 2x
    [ ] 2
    Explanation: lim_{h→0} [(x+h)²−x²]/h = lim (2x+h) = 2x.

  Q2. f'(3) for f(x) = x²:
    [ ] 3
    [ ] 9
    [*] 6
    [ ] 12
    Explanation: f'(x) = 2x → f'(3) = 6.

    [ ] y = 3x

    [ ] y = 6x + 9
    [ ] y = 9x − 18

  Q4. f(x) = 5x + 2. f'(x) = ?
    [ ] 5x
    [ ] 2
    [*] 5
    [ ] 7
    Explanation: Linear function: derivative = slope = 5.

  Q5. f(x) = 7 (constant). f'(x) = ?
    [ ] 7
    [ ] 1

    [ ] Undefined
    Explanation: Derivative of a constant is 0.

  Q10. Tangent line gives the best ___ approximation near a:

    [ ] 2
    [ ] 1/2
    [*] 1/4
    [ ] 4

    [ ] 1/x²

  Q15. f'(2) for f(x) = 1/x:
    [ ] 1/2

  Q16. Which function is NOT differentiable at x = 0?

  Q17. Differentiability implies:

  Q18. Continuity implies differentiability:

--- Lesson 10.2: Derivative Rules (power rule basics) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Derivative Rules (power rule basics)
Derivative RulesBasic Rules  • Constant Rule: d/dx[c] = 0  • Power Rule: d/dx[xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹  • Constant Multiple: d/dx[cf(x)] = c · f'(x)  • Sum/Difference: d/dx[f ± g] = f' ± g'Common Derivatives  • d/dx[sin x] = cos x, d/dx[cos x] = −sin x  • d/dx[eˣ] = eˣ, d/dx[ln x] = 1/x

--- Lesson 10.3: Applications in Optimization (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Optimization
Optimization with Derivatives
Derivatives help find maximum and minimum values of functions.
Process  • 1. Find f'(x) and set f'(x) = 0 to find critical points.  • 2. Use the first derivative test (sign changes) or second derivative test (f''(x) > 0 for min,   • 3. On a closed interval, also check endpoints.Real-World Optimization  • Maximize profit/area/volume or minimize cost/distance.  • Write a function for the quantity to optimize, set its derivative to zero.

--- Lesson 10.4: Area Under Curves (intro to integration) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Area Under Curves (intro to integration)
Area Under Curves
The area under a curve y = f(x) from x = a to x = b is found using integration.
Riemann Sums  • Divide [a, b] into n subintervals of width Δx = (b−a)/n.  • Approximate area: Σ f(x_i) · Δx.  • As n → ∞, the Riemann sum → definite integral ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx.Key Idea  • Integration is the 'reverse' of differentiation.  • The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus connects derivatives and integrals.

--- Lesson 10.4: Area Under Curves (intro to integration) (Quiz) ---

  Q1. Area under f(x) = 2 from x=0 to x=5:
    [ ] 2
    [ ] 5
    [*] 10
    [ ] 7
    Explanation: Rectangle: 2 × 5 = 10. Or ∫₀⁵ 2 dx = 2(5) = 10.

  Q2. Δx for [0, 4] with n = 4 subintervals:
    [ ] 4
    [ ] 2
    [*] 1
    [ ] 0.5

    [ ] 10

    [ ] 8

  Q4. Right Riemann sum for the same:
    [ ] 6
    [*] 10
    [ ] 8
    [ ] 16
    Explanation: f(1)+f(2)+f(3)+f(4) = 1+2+3+4 = 10.

  Q5. Exact area under f(x) = x from 0 to 4:
    [ ] 6
    [ ] 10
    [*] 8
    [ ] 16
    Explanation: ∫₀⁴ x dx = x²/2 |₀⁴ = 8. (Triangle: (1/2)(4)(4) = 8.)

  Q6. Antiderivative of x²:

    [ ] x²/3
    [*] x³/3 + C

    [ ] 4

  Q10. As n increases, Riemann sum becomes:

  Q13. Antiderivative of 1/x:

--- Lesson 10.5: Connecting Precalculus to Calculus (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Connecting Precalculus to Calculus
Connecting Precalculus to Calculus
Every major precalculus topic directly supports calculus concepts.
Connections  • Functions & graphs: Needed for reading and interpreting derivative/integral graphs.  • Polynomial/rational functions: Easiest functions for differentiation and integration.  • Trigonometry: Trig functions and identities are used constantly in calculus.  • Exponentials & logs: Model growth/decay; have elegant derivatives.  • Limits: Define derivatives and integrals.  • Sequences & series: Lead to Taylor series, the backbone of approximation.

--- Lesson 10.7: Capstone Project: Modeling with Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Capstone Project: Modeling with Functions
Capstone Project: Modeling with Functions
This capstone integrates all precalculus skills: building a mathematical model from data, analyzing it, and making predictions.
Modeling Process  • 1. Collect/identify real-world data or a scenario.  • 2. Choose an appropriate function type (linear, quadratic, exponential, trig, logistic).  • 3. Fit the model to data (regression or algebraic methods).  • 4. Analyze: find key features (max, min, zeros, asymptotes, rates of change).  • 5. Predict and validate: use the model for forecasting, check reasonableness.
