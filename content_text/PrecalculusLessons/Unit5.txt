--- Lesson 5.1: Exponential Growth & Decay Models (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential Growth & Decay Models
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If k = 0.05, what is the approximate doubling time?
T = ln 2 / 0.05 ≈ 0.693 / 0.05 ≈ 13.86 years.
What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = 5 · 2ˣ?
As x approaches negative infinity, 2ˣ approaches 0, so f(x) approaches 0. The horizontal asymptote is y = 0.
What is the domain of an exponential function f(x) = bˣ where b > 0 and b ≠ 1?
An exponential function is defined for every real number x, so its domain is all real numbers (−∞, ∞).
What is the range of f(x) = 3ˣ?
Since 3ˣ is always positive and never equals zero, the range is y > 0, or (0, ∞).
What is the y-intercept of every function of the form f(x) = a · bˣ?
Setting x = 0 gives f(0) = a · b⁰ = a · 1 = a. The y-intercept is always equal to the coefficient a.
What is the 'growth factor' in the discrete model P(t) = P₀ · rᵗ?
The growth factor r is the constant multiplier applied each period. If r > 1 the quantity grows; if 0 < r < 1 it decays.
What is the approximate value of the mathematical constant e?
Euler's number e ≈ 2.71828. It is the base of the natural exponential function and arises naturally as the limit of (1 + 1/n)ⁿ as n approaches infinity.
A pharmacist notes that a drug decays to 25% of its original potency in 8 hours. What is the half-life of this drug?
25% = (1/2)², so 8 hours represents 2 half-lives. Each half-life is 8/2 = 4 hours.
A town's population grows continuously at 2% per year from 10,000 residents. A city planner wants to know the population after 5 years. What is the best estimate?
P(5) = 10,000 · e^(0.02 · 5) = 10,000 · e^0.1 ≈ 10,000 · 1.10517 ≈ 11,052.
A substance decays from 80 g to 10 g. A chemistry student needs to determine how many half-lives have elapsed. What is the answer?
Tracking the decay: 80 → 40 → 20 → 10. That is three successive halvings, so 3 half-lives have passed.
An investor puts $5000 into an account that grows continuously at 4% per year. She checks the balance after 10 years. What is the approximate value?
A = 5000e^(0.04 · 10) = 5000e^0.4 ≈ 5000(1.4918) ≈ $7,459.
A sample of a chemical compound decays continuously. If 60% of the sample remains after 5 years, what is the approximate decay constant k?
0.60 = e^(5k) gives 5k = ln(0.60) ≈ −0.5108, so k ≈ −0.5108/5 ≈ −0.102.

--- Lesson 5.2: Compound Interest & Continuous Growth (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Compound Interest & Continuous Growth
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
$1000 is invested at 5% compounded annually for 2 years. What is the final amount?
A = 1000(1.05)² = 1000(1.1025) = $1102.50.
$1000 is invested at 6% compounded monthly for 1 year. What is the final amount?
A = 1000(1 + 0.06/12)^12 = 1000(1.005)^12 ≈ $1061.68.
$500 is invested at 4% compounded continuously for 5 years. What is the final amount?
A = 500e^(0.04 · 5) = 500e^0.2 ≈ 500(1.2214) ≈ $610.70.
What is the APY (annual percentage yield) when the nominal rate is 10% compounded semi-annually?
APY = (1 + 0.10/2)² − 1 = (1.05)² − 1 = 1.1025 − 1 = 0.1025 = 10.25%.
$10,000 is invested at 6% compounded monthly. What is the monthly interest rate used in each period?
The monthly rate is the annual rate divided by 12: 6%/12 = 0.5% (or 0.005 as a decimal).
What is the difference between APR and APY?
APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the nominal rate, while APY (Annual Percentage Yield) is the effective rate that includes the effect of compounding within the year.
What is the Rule of 72?
The Rule of 72 is a shortcut: divide 72 by the annual interest rate percentage to estimate the number of years needed for an investment to double.
What is the effective annual rate always compared to the nominal rate when interest is compounded more than once per year?
When compounding occurs more than once per year, interest earns interest within the year, making the effective rate strictly greater than the nominal rate.
An entrepreneur invests $5000 in a business venture. After 10 years the investment is worth $8000. Assuming annual compounding, what is the approximate annual rate?
8000 = 5000(1 + r)^10, so (1 + r)^10 = 1.6, giving r = 1.6^(1/10) − 1 ≈ 0.048 or about 4.8%.
A parent sets aside $1000 at 5% compounded continuously for their newborn. What is the APY for this account?
APY = e^r − 1 = e^0.05 − 1 ≈ 1.05127 − 1 = 0.05127, or 5.127%.
A small business borrows $10,000 at 6% compounded monthly for a 5-year term. What is the total amount owed at maturity?
A = 10000(1 + 0.06/12)^(12·5) = 10000(1.005)^60 ≈ 10000(1.34885) ≈ $13,488.50.
A grandparent deposits $10,000 at 4% compounded monthly for a grandchild. After 20 years, the account is withdrawn. What is the approximate final value?
A = 10000(1 + 0.04/12)^(12·20) = 10000(1.00333)^240 ≈ 10000(2.2167) ≈ $22,167.
Real-World Applications
Example: What is the APY (annual percentage yield) when the nominal rate is 10% compounded semi-annually?
APY = (1 + 0.10/2)² − 1 = (1.05)² − 1 = 1.1025 − 1 = 0.1025 = 10.25%.
Important Concepts
What does 'compounding frequency' refer to
Compounding frequency is how often interest is computed and added to the balance (e.g., annually = 1, quarterly = 4, monthly = 12, daily = 365).

--- Lesson 5.3: Properties of Logarithms (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Properties of Logarithms
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the product rule for logarithms?
The product rule states that the logarithm of a product equals the sum of the logarithms: log_b(xy) = log_b(x) + log_b(y).
What is the power rule for logarithms?
The power rule brings the exponent in front as a coefficient: log_b(xⁿ) = n · log_b(x).
What is the domain of f(x) = ln(x)?
The natural logarithm is only defined for positive inputs. The domain is x > 0, or (0, ∞).
What is the value of log_b(1) for any valid base b?
Since b⁰ = 1 for any valid base, log_b(1) = 0. This is a fundamental property of all logarithms.
What is the quotient rule for logarithms?
The quotient rule states that the logarithm of a quotient equals the difference of the logarithms: log_b(x/y) = log_b(x) − log_b(y).
A chemist measures the hydrogen ion concentration of a solution as [H⁺] = 10⁻⁴·⁵. She uses the formula pH = −log[H⁺]. What is the pH?
pH = −log(10⁻⁴·⁵) = −(−4.5) = 4.5. The power rule for logarithms gives log(10⁻⁴·⁵) = −4.5.
An acoustics engineer needs to expand log₃(9x²/y) for a signal processing formula. What is the expanded form?
log₃(9x²/y) = log₃(9) + log₃(x²) − log₃(y) = 2 + 2 log₃(x) − log₃(y), using the product, quotient, and power rules.
A data scientist condenses the expression (1/2) ln(x) − ln(3) into a single logarithm. What is the result?
By the power rule, (1/2)ln(x) = ln(x^(1/2)) = ln(√x). Then by the quotient rule, ln(√x) − ln(3) = ln(√x / 3).
A physicist simplifies ln(e⁷) while working on a thermodynamics problem. What is the simplified value?
By the inverse property, log_b(bˣ) = x. Therefore ln(e⁷) = 7.
A math tutor asks a student to evaluate log₅(1/25). What is the correct answer?
Since 1/25 = 5⁻², we have log₅(5⁻²) = −2 by the inverse property.
An environmental researcher uses the expression 3 ln(t) + ln(k) − 2 ln(r) in a pollution model. What is this expression condensed into a single logarithm?
Apply the power rule: 3 ln(t) = ln(t³) and 2 ln(r) = ln(r²). Then product and quotient rules give ln(t³) + ln(k) − ln(r²) = ln(kt³/r²).
A student solving an equation arrives at log(x) = 2. The teacher asks: what is x?
log₁₀(x) = 2 means x = 10² = 100. The common logarithm uses base 10.
An engineer working with decibel formulas needs to evaluate the expression log(10⁴ · 10⁶) without a calculator. What is the result?
Using the product rule: log(10⁴ · 10⁶) = log(10⁴) + log(10⁶) = 4 + 6 = 10. Alternatively, 10⁴ · 10⁶ = 10¹⁰.
A financial analyst needs to condense the expression log(P) + log(1 + r) · t into a single log for a report. What is the condensed form?
By the power rule, t · log(1 + r) = log((1 + r)ᵗ). Then by the product rule, log(P) + log((1 + r)ᵗ) = log(P(1 + r)ᵗ), which is the compound interest formula inside a logarithm.

--- Lesson 5.5: Solving Exponential & Logarithmic Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Exponential & Logarithmic Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is an 'extraneous solution' in logarithmic equations?
An extraneous solution arises from algebraic manipulation but makes a logarithmic argument negative or zero, violating the domain. It must be rejected.
What is the one-to-one property of logarithms?
The one-to-one property states that if two logarithms with the same base are equal, their arguments must be equal: log_b(x) = log_b(y) implies x = y.
What is the standard first step when solving an exponential equation like 5ˣ = 200?
To isolate x in the exponent, take the logarithm (ln or log) of both sides: x ln(5) = ln(200), then solve for x.
What is the standard approach to solving log(x) + log(x − 3) = 1?
First combine: log(x(x − 3)) = 1. Then convert to exponential form: x(x − 3) = 10¹. Solve the quadratic and check for extraneous solutions.
A biologist models a cell culture with N(t) = 50 · 2^(t/3). She needs to find when the culture reaches 6400 cells. What is t?
6400 = 50 · 2^(t/3) gives 2^(t/3) = 128 = 2⁷, so t/3 = 7, thus t = 21.
A chemist dissolves a compound and measures the equation log(x) + log(x − 3) = 1 for the concentration x (in moles). What is x?
Combine: log(x(x − 3)) = 1, so x² − 3x = 10, giving x² − 3x − 10 = 0, which factors as (x − 5)(x + 2) = 0. Since x = −2 makes log(x) undefined, x = 5.
A physicist needs to solve 5ˣ⁺¹ = 5³ˣ⁻² for a symmetry analysis. What is x?
Same base: equate exponents. x + 1 = 3x − 2 gives 3 = 2x, so x = 3/2.
A financial planner needs to solve e^(0.06t) = 3 to find when an investment triples. What is the approximate value of t?
0.06t = ln(3) ≈ 1.0986, so t = 1.0986/0.06 ≈ 18.3 years.
A population ecologist encounters log₂(x) + log₂(x + 2) = 3 while modeling species interaction. What is x?
Combine: log₂(x(x + 2)) = 3, so x² + 2x = 8, giving x² + 2x − 8 = 0, factoring as (x + 4)(x − 2) = 0. Since x = −4 is invalid (log argument negative), x = 2.
A technician needs to solve 7^(2x+1) = 300 for an amplifier circuit calibration. What is the approximate value of x?
Take ln: (2x + 1) ln(7) = ln(300). So 2x + 1 = ln(300)/ln(7) ≈ 5.7038/1.9459 ≈ 2.932. Then 2x = 1.932, giving x ≈ 0.966, which rounds to about 0.97.
A quality control analyst solves ln(2x + 1) = 0 to determine the baseline measurement. What is x?
ln(2x + 1) = 0 means 2x + 1 = e⁰ = 1, so 2x = 0 and x = 0.
Real-World Applications
Example: A medical researcher solves ln(x²) = 4 for a dosage calculation. What are the solutions?
ln(x²) = 2 ln|x| = 4, so ln|x| = 2, giving |x| = e². Therefore x = e² or x = −e².

--- Lesson 5.6: Applications: pH, Richter Scale, Population Growth (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications: pH, Richter Scale, Population Growth
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If [H⁺] = 10⁻⁵ M, what is the pH?
pH = −log(10⁻⁵) = −(−5) = 5.
A solution has pH = 3. What is [H⁺]?
[H⁺] = 10^(−pH) = 10⁻³.
What is the pH of pure water?
Pure water has [H⁺] = 10⁻⁷ M, so pH = 7 (neutral).
Sound at 80 dB versus 60 dB: what is the intensity ratio?
A 20 dB difference means the intensity ratio is 10^(20/10) = 10² = 100.
Carbon-14 has a half-life of approximately 5730 years. What is the decay constant k?
k = −ln(2)/half-life = −ln(2)/5730 ≈ −0.000121 per year.
A whisper is about 20 dB. Normal conversation is 60 dB. What is the intensity ratio?
40 dB difference: intensity ratio = 10^(40/10) = 10⁴ = 10,000.
What is the normal pH range of human blood?

A chemist measures [H⁺] = 3.16 × 10⁻⁸ M in a sample of tap water. What is the pH?
pH = −log(3.16 × 10⁻⁸) = −(log 3.16 + log 10⁻⁸) = −(0.5 − 8) = 7.5.
A population doubles every 15 years. An ecologist needs the continuous growth rate k. What is the approximate value?
k = ln(2)/doubling time = ln(2)/15 ≈ 0.693/15 ≈ 0.0462 per year.

T(20) = 70 + 130e^(−0.05·20) = 70 + 130e^(−1) ≈ 70 + 130(0.368) ≈ 70 + 47.8 ≈ 118°F.

--- Lesson 5.8: Logistic Growth Models (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logistic Growth Models
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
For P(t) = 1000/(1 + 9e^(−0.5t)), what is the carrying capacity?
In the form P(t) = L/(1 + be^(−kt)), L is the numerator. Here L = 1000.
P(t) = 500/(1 + 24e^(−0.3t)). What is P(0)?
P(0) = 500/(1 + 24) = 500/25 = 20.
For P(t) = 500/(1 + 24e^(−0.3t)), what is the inflection point population?
The inflection point occurs at L/2 = 500/2 = 250.
If L = 5000 and P(0) = 500, what is the value of b in P(t) = L/(1 + be^(−kt))?
P(0) = L/(1 + b), so 500 = 5000/(1 + b), giving 1 + b = 10, thus b = 9.
What is the difference between logistic growth and exponential growth?
Exponential growth models unlimited increase (Peᵏᵗ), while logistic growth (L/(1 + be⁻ᵏᵗ)) includes a maximum (L) that constrains the population.
A fish hatchery stocks a lake with 200 fish. The lake can support 4000 fish. A biologist uses the logistic model. What is b?
P(0) = L/(1 + b), so 200 = 4000/(1 + b), giving 1 + b = 20, thus b = 19.
Real-World Applications
Example: A wildlife manager models a deer population with P(t) = 800/(1 + 3e^(−0.2t)). What population level triggers the fastest growth?
The fastest growth occurs at the inflection point: P = L/2 = 800/2 = 400 deer.
Example: A pharmaceutical company models drug adoption as P(t) = 100,000/(1 + 999e^(−0.05t)). What percentage of the carrying capacity is the initial adoption?
P(0) = 100,000/(1 + 999) = 100,000/1000 = 100. As a percentage of L: 100/100,000 = 0.1%.

    Explanation: Set 500 = 1000/(1 + 49e^(−0.1t)). Then 1 + 49e^(−0.1t) = 2, so 49e^(−0.1t) = 1, giving e^(−0.1t) = 1/49. Thus t = ln(49)/0.1 ≈ 38.9 months.

  Q30. A public health official models vaccine adoption in a city of 500,000 people. Initially 10,000 are vaccinated and the model is logistic with k = 0.08. After a very long time, what fraction of the population will be vaccinated?

--- Lesson 5.9: Case Studies in Biology & Finance (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Case Studies in Biology & Finance
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A fossil has 12.5% of its original C-14. What is the approximate age? (Half-life ≈ 5730 years.)
12.5% = 1/8 = (1/2)³, so 3 half-lives have passed. Age = 3 × 5730 = 17,190 years.
$10,000 invested at 5% continuously for 20 years. What is the value?
A = 10000e^(0.05 · 20) = 10000e¹ ≈ 10000(2.7183) ≈ $27,183.
A car worth $30,000 depreciates at 15% per year. What is its value after 3 years?
V = 30000(1 − 0.15)³ = 30000(0.85)³ = 30000(0.6141) ≈ $18,402.
C₀ = 200 mg, half-life 4 hours. What is the decay constant k in C(t) = 200e^(−kt)?
k = ln(2)/half-life = ln(2)/4 ≈ 0.693/4 ≈ 0.1733.
An investment doubles in 12 years with continuous compounding. What is the rate?
r = ln(2)/12 ≈ 0.693/12 ≈ 0.0578, or about 5.78%.
A culture starts at 1000 bacteria and reaches 8000 in 6 hours. What is the doubling time?
8000/1000 = 8 = 2³, so 3 doublings occurred in 6 hours. Doubling time = 6/3 = 2 hours.
What is the biological significance of half-life in pharmacology?
In pharmacology, half-life is the time required for the concentration of a drug in the bloodstream to decrease to half its current level through metabolism and excretion.
What is the difference between discrete and continuous exponential models?
Discrete models apply change at fixed intervals (annually, monthly, etc.), while continuous models apply change instantaneously at every moment, using the base e.
What is the relationship between doubling time and growth rate in continuous growth?
Doubling time T = ln(2)/k. A larger growth rate k produces a shorter doubling time; they are inversely proportional.
What is the key advantage of logarithmic models in science?
Logarithmic models compress enormous ranges of data into manageable numbers, which is why they are used in pH, decibels, the Richter scale, and other scientific measurements.
$5000 grows to $8000 in 5 years with annual compounding. What is the approximate annual rate?
(1 + r)⁵ = 8000/5000 = 1.6, so r = 1.6^(1/5) − 1 ≈ 1.0986 − 1 = 0.0986, or about 9.86%.
A $500 savings bond matures to $1000 in 20 years with continuous compounding. What is the rate?
1000 = 500e^(20r), so e^(20r) = 2, giving r = ln(2)/20 ≈ 0.693/20 ≈ 0.0347, or about 3.47%.
Real-World Applications
Example: A fossil has 12.5% of its original C-14. What is the approximate age? (Half-life ≈ 5730 years.)
12.5% = 1/8 = (1/2)³, so 3 half-lives have passed. Age = 3 × 5730 = 17,190 years.
Example: What advantage does the exponential model have for biological growth compared to linear models?
In biology, more organisms produce more offspring, so growth is proportional to the current population. Exponential models (dP/dt = kP) capture this proportional growth.
Example: What is the key advantage of logarithmic models in science?
Logarithmic models compress enormous ranges of data into manageable numbers, which is why they are used in pH, decibels, the Richter scale, and other scientific measurements.

    [ ] A linear decrease at a constant dollar amount per year
    [ ] An increase in value over time
    Explanation: Exponential depreciation models an asset losing a fixed percentage of its value each period, resulting in V = V₀(1 − r)ᵗ. This differs from linear depreciation which loses a fixed dollar amount.
