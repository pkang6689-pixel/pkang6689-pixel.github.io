--- Lesson 5.1: Exponential Growth & Decay Models (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential Growth & Decay Models
Exponential Growth & Decay Models
An exponential function has the form f(x) = a · bˣ, where a ≠ 0 and b > 0, b ≠ 1.
Growth vs. Decay  • Growth: b > 1. The function increases rapidly.  • Decay: 0 Natural Exponential  • f(x) = eˣ, where e ≈ 2.71828 (Euler's number).  • Continuous model: A(t) = A₀ · eᵏᵗ, k > 0 for growth, k Half-Life & Doubling Time  • Half-life: time for quantity to halve. A(t) = A₀ · (1/2)^(t/h).  • Doubling time: time for quantity to double. 2 = e^(k·T_d) → T_d = ln 2 / k.

--- Lesson 5.2: Compound Interest & Continuous Growth (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Compound Interest & Continuous Growth
Compound Interest & Continuous GrowthCompound Interest  • A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compoundings per year, t = years.  • More frequent compounding yields more interest.Continuous Compounding  • As n → ∞: A = Peʳᵗ.  • This is the maximum possible growth for a given rate r.APY (Annual Percentage Yield)  • Effective annual rate: APY = (1 + r/n)ⁿ − 1.  • For continuous compounding: APY = eʳ − 1.

--- Lesson 5.3: Properties of Logarithms (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Properties of Logarithms
Properties of Logarithms
A logarithm is the inverse of an exponential: if bˣ = y, then log_b(y) = x.
Key Properties  • Product Rule: log_b(MN) = log_b(M) + log_b(N).  • Quotient Rule: log_b(M/N) = log_b(M) − log_b(N).  • Power Rule: log_b(Mⁿ) = n · log_b(M).  • log_b(1) = 0 and log_b(b) = 1.  • b^(log_b(x)) = x and log_b(bˣ) = x (inverse properties).Common & Natural Logarithms  • Common: log(x) = log₁₀(x).  • Natural: ln(x) = logₑ(x).

--- Lesson 5.4: Change of Base Formula (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Change of Base Formula
Change of Base Formula
Calculators typically only have log (base 10) and ln (base e). To evaluate log_b(x) for other bases, use the change of base formula.
Formula  • log_b(x) = log(x) / log(b) = ln(x) / ln(b).  • More generally: log_b(x) = log_a(x) / log_a(b) for any valid base a.Applications  • Evaluate log₅(20) on a calculator: ln(20)/ln(5).  • Compare logarithms in different bases.  • Solve equations involving different bases.

--- Lesson 5.5: Solving Exponential & Logarithmic Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Exponential & Logarithmic Equations
Solving Exponential & Logarithmic EquationsExponential Equations  • If same base: bˣ = bʸ → x = y.  • Otherwise: take log or ln of both sides → x · ln(b) = ln(result) → solve for x.Logarithmic Equations  • Isolate the log expression, then convert to exponential form.  • Combine logs using properties first, if needed.Extraneous Solutions  • Always check solutions in the original equation.  • Reject any solution that makes a log argument ≤ 0.

--- Lesson 5.8: Logistic Growth Models (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logistic Growth Models
Logistic Growth Models
The logistic model accounts for a carrying capacity that limits exponential growth.
Formula  • P(t) = L / (1 + be⁻ᵏᵗ), where L = carrying capacity, b and k are positive constants.Characteristics  • S-shaped (sigmoid) curve.  • Initially grows roughly exponentially.  • Growth rate slows as P approaches L.  • Horizontal asymptotes: y = 0 (as t → −∞) and y = L (as t → ∞).  • Inflection point at P = L/2 (fastest growth occurs here).

--- Lesson 5.9: Case Studies in Biology & Finance (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Case Studies in Biology & Finance
Case Studies in Biology & Finance
Exponential and logarithmic functions are widely used in both biological and financial modeling.
Biology  • Bacterial growth: N(t) = N₀ · 2^(t/d) where d = doubling time.  • Drug elimination: C(t) = C₀ · e⁻ᵏᵗ (first-order kinetics).  • Carbon dating: A(t) = A₀ · e^(−0.000121t).Finance  • Compound interest: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt).  • Present value: P = A · e⁻ʳᵗ (continuous discounting).  • Depreciation: V(t) = V₀(1 − r)ᵗ.

    Explanation: Depreciation typically uses a discrete annual rate.

  Q17. A $500 bond matures to $1000 in 20 years. Continuous rate?
    [ ] 5%
    [ ] 2.5%
    [*] 3.47%
    [ ] 10%
    Explanation: r = ln(2)/20 ≈ 0.0347.

  Q18. Blood alcohol content decays with half-life ≈ 1.5 hrs. After 4.5 hrs of no drinking, BAC is __ of initial.

    [*] 1/8
    [ ] 1/16
    Explanation: 4.5/1.5 = 3 half-lives. (1/2)³ = 1/8.

  Q19. Which grows faster: $1000 at 5% compounded continuously, or $1000 at 5.1% compounded annually?

    [*] Annual 5.1%

    [ ] Depends on time
    Explanation: APY of 5% continuous = e^0.05 − 1 ≈ 5.127%. 5.1% annual = 5.1%. 5.127% > 5.1%, so continuous 5% is slightly better.
