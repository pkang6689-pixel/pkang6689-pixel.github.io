--- Lesson 2.1: Linear Equations & Graphs (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Linear Equations & Graphs
Linear Equations & Graphs
A linear equation in two variables has the form Ax + By = C (standard form) or y = mx + b (slope-intercept form).
  ‚ÄĘ Slope-intercept form: y = mx + b. m = slope, b = y-intercept.  ‚ÄĘ Point-slope form: y ‚ąí y‚āĀ = m(x ‚ąí x‚āĀ). Useful when you know a point and the slope.  ‚ÄĘ Standard form: Ax + By = C. A, B, C are integers, A ‚Č• 0.  ‚ÄĘ Slope: m = (y‚āā ‚ąí y‚āĀ)/(x‚āā ‚ąí x‚āĀ) ‚ÄĒ the rate of change.  ‚ÄĘ Horizontal line: y = c (slope = 0). Vertical line: x = c (slope undefined).
