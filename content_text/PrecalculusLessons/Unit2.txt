--- Lesson 2.1: Linear Equations & Graphs (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Linear Equations & Graphs
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A phone plan costs $25/month plus $0.10 per text. If a student sends 300 texts, what is the monthly bill?
C = 25 + 0.10(300) = 25 + 30 = $55.
A car's value depreciates linearly from $20,000 to $8,000 over 6 years. What is the annual depreciation rate?
Depreciation = (20000 - 8000)/6 = 12000/6 = $2,000 per year.
A pool is being filled at a constant rate. It has 50 gallons at t = 0 and 200 gallons at t = 5 hours. What is the linear equation?
Rate = (200-50)/5 = 30 gal/hr. Starting at 50: V = 30t + 50.
Real-World Applications
Example: A bakery sells cupcakes for $3 each. Fixed daily costs are $60. How many cupcakes must be sold to earn $150 in revenue?
Revenue = 3x = 150, so x = 50 cupcakes.
Example: A salesperson earns $500/week plus $20 per sale. She needs $900 this week. How many sales are required?
500 + 20s = 900, so 20s = 400, s = 20 sales.

--- Lesson 2.2: Slope, Intercepts, and Parallel/Perpendicular Lines (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Slope, Intercepts, and Parallel/Perpendicular Lines
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A road rises 150 feet over a horizontal distance of 3,000 feet. What is the grade (slope) of the road?
Slope = rise/run = 150/3000 = 0.05 = 5%.
A wheelchair ramp must have a slope no steeper than 1/12. If the ramp rises 2 feet, what is the minimum horizontal length?
Slope = 2/x = 1/12, so x = 24 feet minimum.
Two streets are parallel with slopes of 3/4. A new street must be perpendicular to them. What is its slope?
Perpendicular slope is the negative reciprocal: −4/3.
A cable car rises from elevation 2,000 ft to 3,500 ft over a horizontal distance of 6,000 ft. What is the slope?
Slope = (3500 − 2000)/6000 = 1500/6000 = 1/4.
An architect designs a roof with slope 5/12. A drain pipe must run perpendicular to the roof line. What is the pipe's slope?
Perpendicular slope = negative reciprocal of 5/12 = −12/5.
A stock price was $40 on day 1 and $55 on day 31. What is the average rate of change per day?
Rate = (55 − 40)/(31 − 1) = 15/30 = $0.50 per day.
Train track A has equation y = 2x + 5. Track B runs parallel through point (3, 1). What is Track B's equation?
Parallel means same slope m = 2. Using point (3,1): 1 = 2(3) + b, b = −5. So y = 2x − 5.
A hiking trail drops 400 feet in elevation over 2 miles (10,560 ft). What is the trail's slope?
Slope = −400/10560 = −1/26.4 (negative because it drops).
A water pipe runs along y = −3x + 10. A new pipe must cross it at a right angle at x = 2. What is the new pipe's equation?
At x=2: y = −6+10 = 4. Perpendicular slope = 1/3. y − 4 = (1/3)(x − 2), so y = (1/3)x + 10/3.
Real-World Applications
Example: A stock price was $40 on day 1 and $55 on day 31. What is the average rate of change per day?
Rate = (55 − 40)/(31 − 1) = 15/30 = $0.50 per day.

--- Lesson 2.3: Quadratic Functions (vertex form, standard form) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Quadratic Functions (vertex form, standard form)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A ball is thrown upward with h(t) = −16t² + 96t + 5. What is the maximum height?
Vertex at t = −96/(2(−16)) = 3. h(3) = −16(9) + 96(3) + 5 = −144 + 288 + 5 = 149 feet.
A company's profit is P(x) = −2(x − 50)² + 5000. What price x maximizes profit and what is the maximum?
In vertex form −2(x−50)²+5000, the vertex is (50, 5000). Max profit is $5,000 at x = 50.
A farmer wants to maximize a rectangular pen with 100 m of fencing. The area is A(w) = w(50 − w). What is the maximum area?
A = 50w − w² = −(w−25)² + 625. Maximum area is 625 m² at w = 25 m.
A bridge arch is modeled by y = −0.01(x − 100)² + 100. What is the maximum height of the arch?
Vertex form shows the vertex at (100, 100). Maximum height is 100 feet.
A diver's height above water: h(t) = −5t² + 10t + 3 meters. What is the diver's maximum height?
Vertex at t = −10/(2(−5)) = 1. h(1) = −5 + 10 + 3 = 8 meters.
A parabolic antenna is described by y = (1/8)x² − 2x + 10. What is the vertex (lowest point)?
x = −(−2)/(2(1/8)) = 2/(1/4) = 8. y = (1/8)(64) − 16 + 10 = 8 − 16 + 10 = 2. Vertex: (8, 2).
