--- Lesson 8.1: Arithmetic Sequences (nth term, partial sums) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arithmetic Sequences (nth term, partial sums)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common difference of the arithmetic sequence 3, 7, 11, 15, ...?
The common difference d = 7 − 3 = 4. Each term increases by 4.
What is the 10th term of the arithmetic sequence with a₁ = 5 and d = 3?
aₙ = a₁ + (n−1)d = 5 + 9(3) = 5 + 27 = 32.
What is the formula for the nth term of an arithmetic sequence?
The nth term formula for an arithmetic sequence is aₙ = a₁ + (n−1)d, where a₁ is the first term and d is the common difference.
If the 3rd term of an arithmetic sequence is 14 and the 7th term is 30, what is the common difference?
a₇ − a₃ = (7−3)d, so 30 − 14 = 4d, giving d = 16/4 = 4.
What is the sum formula Sₙ for an arithmetic series?
The sum of the first n terms of an arithmetic series is Sₙ = n/2 · (a₁ + aₙ), which is the average of the first and last terms multiplied by n.
What is a₁ if the arithmetic sequence has a₅ = 23 and d = 4?
a₅ = a₁ + 4d → 23 = a₁ + 16 → a₁ = 7.
What is the 50th term of the sequence 1, 4, 7, 10, ...?
a₅₀ = 1 + 49(3) = 1 + 147 = 148.
What is an 'arithmetic sequence'?
An arithmetic sequence is one in which consecutive terms differ by a constant amount called the common difference d.
What is an 'arithmetic series'?
An arithmetic series is the sum of the terms of an arithmetic sequence, often denoted Sₙ = a₁ + a₂ + ... + aₙ.
What is a 'linear function' model, and how does it relate to arithmetic sequences?
An arithmetic sequence aₙ = a₁ + (n−1)d is a discrete version of y = mx + b. Both have a constant rate of change (slope m or common difference d).
What is the 'arithmetic mean' of two numbers a and b?
The arithmetic mean of two numbers is (a + b)/2. In an arithmetic sequence, each term (except the first and last) is the arithmetic mean of its neighbors.
What is an 'explicit formula' for a sequence?
An explicit (or closed-form) formula expresses aₙ directly in terms of n. For arithmetic sequences, it is aₙ = a₁ + (n−1)d.
A company offers a starting salary of $35,000 with an annual raise of $2,500. What is the total salary earned over the first 10 years?
a₁ = 35000, d = 2500, a₁₀ = 35000 + 9(2500) = 57500. S₁₀ = 10/2 · (35000 + 57500) = 5 · 92500 = $462,500.
A brick wall pattern has 40 bricks in the bottom row and each row above has 2 fewer bricks. If the wall has 15 rows, what is the total number of bricks?
a₁ = 40, d = −2, a₁₅ = 40 + 14(−2) = 12. S₁₅ = 15/2 · (40 + 12) = 15/2 · 52 = 390.

--- Lesson 8.2: Geometric Sequences (nth term, infinite series) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Geometric Sequences (nth term, infinite series)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common ratio of the geometric sequence 3, 6, 12, 24, ...?
The common ratio r = 6/3 = 2. Each term is multiplied by 2.
What is the 8th term of the geometric sequence with a₁ = 5 and r = 2?
a₈ = 5 · 2⁷ = 5 · 128 = 640.
What is the formula for the nth term of a geometric sequence?
The nth term of a geometric sequence is aₙ = a₁ · rⁿ⁻¹, where a₁ is the first term and r is the common ratio.
What is the sum of the infinite geometric series 12 + 4 + 4/3 + 4/9 + ...?
a₁ = 12, r = 4/12 = 1/3. S = a₁/(1 − r) = 12/(1 − 1/3) = 12/(2/3) = 18.
What is the common ratio if a₃ = 36 and a₁ = 4?
a₃ = a₁ · r² → 36 = 4r² → r² = 9 → r = 3 (assuming positive ratio).
What is the 5th term of the geometric sequence 2, −6, 18, −54, ...?
r = −6/2 = −3. a₅ = 2(−3)⁴ = 2(81) = 162.
What is a 'geometric sequence'?
A geometric sequence has a constant ratio r between consecutive terms: aₙ₊₁ = aₙ · r.
What is a 'geometric series'?
A geometric series is the sum of the terms of a geometric sequence: S = a₁ + a₁r + a₁r² + ...
What is the 'geometric mean' of two positive numbers a and b?
The geometric mean of two positive numbers a and b is √(ab). In a geometric sequence, each interior term is the geometric mean of its neighbors.
What is 'exponential decay'?
Exponential decay occurs when a quantity is repeatedly multiplied by a factor r where 0 < r < 1, causing it to decrease toward zero.
What is an 'infinite geometric series'?
An infinite geometric series sums infinitely many terms: S = a₁ + a₁r + a₁r² + ... = a₁/(1−r) when |r| < 1.
A ball is dropped from a height of 10 meters and bounces back to 60% of its previous height each time. What is the total distance traveled before it comes to rest?
Total distance = 10 + 2(10)(0.6)/(1 − 0.6) = 10 + 2(10)(0.6)/0.4 = 10 + 30 = 40 meters (the initial drop plus the infinite series of bounces up and down).
A car depreciates at 15% per year. If the car is worth $30,000 new, what is it worth after 5 years?
Value = 30000(0.85)⁵ = 30000(0.4437) ≈ $13,311. Each year the car retains 85% of its value.
An investment of $5,000 earns 8% annual interest compounded annually. What is the value after 10 years?
A = 5000(1.08)¹⁰ = 5000(2.1589) ≈ $10,795.
A fractal pattern starts with a triangle of area 100 cm². At each step, the total new area added is 1/3 of the previous addition. What is the total area after infinitely many steps?
Total = 100 + 100(1/3) + 100(1/3)² + ... = 100/(1 − 1/3) = 100/(2/3) = 150 cm².
A company's revenue was $2 million in its first year and has grown by 20% each year. What is the total revenue over the first 5 years?
S₅ = 2(1.2⁵ − 1)/(1.2 − 1) = 2(2.48832 − 1)/0.2 = 2(1.48832)/0.2 = 2(7.4416) ≈ $14.88 million.
A pendulum swings through an arc of 80 cm on its first swing, with each subsequent swing being 90% as long. What is the total distance the pendulum travels before stopping?
S = 80/(1 − 0.9) = 80/0.1 = 800 cm.
A savings plan pays 5% interest compounded annually. If you deposit $1,000 at the start of each year for 20 years, what is the total value at the end of 20 years? (Use the annuity formula.)
Annuity due: FV = 1000 · [(1.05²⁰ − 1)/0.05] · 1.05 = 1000 · [33.066]/1 · 1.05 ≈ 1000 · 33.066 · 1.05 ≈ $34,719.

--- Lesson 8.4: Mathematical Induction (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Mathematical Induction
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the first step in a proof by mathematical induction?
The first step is the base case: verify the statement holds for n = 1 (or the smallest applicable value).
Prove by induction: 1 + 3 + 5 + ... + (2n−1) = n². What is the base case for n = 1?
For n = 1: LHS = 2(1) − 1 = 1, RHS = 1² = 1. The base case holds.
Prove: 3ⁿ − 1 is divisible by 2 for all n ≥ 1. What is the base case?
For n = 1: 3¹ − 1 = 2, and 2 is divisible by 2. The base case holds.
What is 'mathematical induction'?
Mathematical induction is a formal proof technique: prove P(1), then prove that P(k) implies P(k+1), thereby establishing P(n) for all positive integers.
What is the 'base case' in mathematical induction?
The base case is the initial verification — checking that the statement is true for the smallest relevant integer (usually n = 1).
What is the 'inductive hypothesis'?
The inductive hypothesis is the assumption that the statement P(k) holds for some arbitrary positive integer k, which is used to prove P(k+1).
What is the 'inductive step'?
The inductive step is the proof that P(k) → P(k+1). Combined with the base case, it establishes the statement for all positive integers.
What is a 'counterexample' and how does it relate to induction?
A counterexample is a single instance where a statement is false. A single counterexample disproves a universal statement, making induction unnecessary.
What is a 'recursive definition'?
A recursive definition gives a base case value and a rule expressing each subsequent term in terms of earlier ones (e.g., n! = n · (n−1)!).
A network engineer wants to prove that n routers can be connected in a line using exactly n − 1 cables. What is the inductive step?
Inductive step: if k routers need k−1 cables (hypothesis), adding 1 router requires 1 more cable, totaling k cables for k+1 routers.
A student uses induction to prove n! > 2ⁿ for all n ≥ 4. What is the appropriate base case?
The statement is claimed for n ≥ 4, so the base case is n = 4: 24 > 16 ✓. For n < 4, the inequality fails.
A biology student wants to prove that after n cell divisions, a single cell produces 2ⁿ cells. What is the inductive step?
Inductive step: if k divisions produce 2ᵏ cells, each cell divides once more, doubling the count to 2 · 2ᵏ = 2ᵏ⁺¹.
A teacher wants to prove that the sum of the interior angles of any convex polygon with n sides is (n − 2) · 180°. Using induction, what is the base case?
The base case is n = 3 (triangle): (3 − 2) · 180° = 180°, which is the well-known angle sum of a triangle.
Real-World Applications
Example: What is a 'counterexample' and how does it relate to induction?
A counterexample is a single instance where a statement is false. A single counterexample disproves a universal statement, making induction unnecessary.
Example: A finance manager wants to prove the compound interest formula A = P(1 + r)ⁿ by induction on n (the number of compounding periods). What does the inductive step show?
The inductive step multiplies both sides by (1+r) for one more compounding period: P(1+r)ᵏ · (1+r) = P(1+r)ᵏ⁺¹.

--- Lesson 8.5: Binomial Theorem (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Binomial Theorem
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the value of C(5,2)?
C(5,2) = 5!/(2!3!) = 120/(2·6) = 10.
What is the expansion of (a + b)²?
(a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b², with binomial coefficients 1, 2, 1.
What is the third term in the expansion of (x + y)⁵?
The kth term is C(5,k-1)x^(5-k+1)y^(k-1). For k=3: C(5,2)x³y² = 10x³y².
What is C(6,0)?
C(n,0) = 1 for any n, since there is exactly one way to choose 0 items.
What is the coefficient of x⁴ in the expansion of (x + 2)⁶?
The x⁴ term: C(6,2)x⁴ · 2² = 15 · 4 = 60.
What is (1 + 1)⁵ using the Binomial Theorem?
(1+1)⁵ = 2⁵ = 32. This also equals Σ C(5,k) = 1 + 5 + 10 + 10 + 5 + 1 = 32.
What is the coefficient of x²y³ in the expansion of (x + y)⁵?
The coefficient is C(5,3) = 10.
What is the 'Binomial Theorem'?
The Binomial Theorem states (a + b)ⁿ = Σ(k=0 to n) C(n,k)aⁿ⁻ᵏbᵏ for any non-negative integer n.
What is a 'binomial coefficient'?
A binomial coefficient C(n,k) = n!/(k!(n−k)!) counts the number of ways to choose k items from n, and appears as the coefficient in the binomial expansion.
What is 'Pascal's Triangle'?
Pascal's Triangle arranges binomial coefficients in rows, where each interior entry equals the sum of the two entries above it.
What is a 'binomial'?
A binomial is an algebraic expression consisting of exactly two terms, such as (a + b) or (x − 3).
What is 'Pascal's Identity'?
Pascal's Identity states C(n,k) = C(n−1,k−1) + C(n−1,k), which is the rule that builds Pascal's Triangle.
What is the 'general term' of a binomial expansion?
The (k+1)th term in the expansion of (a+b)ⁿ is T(k+1) = C(n,k)aⁿ⁻ᵏbᵏ for k = 0, 1, ..., n.
A genetics problem involves (p + q)⁴ where p = 0.6 and q = 0.4. What is the probability of exactly 3 dominant alleles (the term C(4,3)p³q¹)?
C(4,3)(0.6)³(0.4)¹ = 4 · 0.216 · 0.4 = 0.3456.
An architect uses the expansion of (1 + x)¹⁰ to approximate (1.02)¹⁰. Using the first three terms, what is the approximation?
(1+0.02)¹⁰ ≈ 1 + 10(0.02) + 45(0.02)² = 1 + 0.2 + 0.018 = 1.218.
A pharmaceutical company tests a drug with 70% efficacy. In a trial of 6 patients, what is the probability that exactly 4 are cured? Use P = C(6,4)(0.7)⁴(0.3)².
C(6,4)(0.7)⁴(0.3)² = 15 · 0.2401 · 0.09 = 15 · 0.021609 ≈ 0.3241.
In the expansion of (2x − 3)⁴, what is the coefficient of x²?
The x² term: C(4,2)(2x)²(−3)² = 6 · 4x² · 9 = 216x². The coefficient is 216.
A scientist uses the binomial expansion to find the constant term in (x + 1/x)⁶. What is it?
General term: C(6,k)x⁶⁻ᵏ(1/x)ᵏ = C(6,k)x⁶⁻²ᵏ. Constant when 6−2k=0, so k=3. C(6,3) = 20.
A data analyst needs to determine the number of unique 5-card poker hands from a standard 52-card deck. What is C(52,5)?
C(52,5) = 52!/(5!47!) = 2,598,960 unique 5-card hands.

--- Lesson 8.6: Applications in Finance & Growth Patterns (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Finance & Growth Patterns
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the compound interest formula for annual compounding?
For annual compounding: A = P(1 + r)ⁿ, where P is principal, r is annual rate, and n is the number of years.
If $1,000 is invested at 5% annual interest compounded annually, what is it worth after 3 years?
A = 1000(1.05)³ = 1000(1.157625) = $1,157.63.
What is the simple interest formula?
Simple interest is I = Prt, where P is principal, r is annual rate, and t is time in years.
A population of 2,000 grows at 3% per year. What is the population after 5 years?
P = 2000(1.03)⁵ = 2000(1.15927) ≈ 2,319.
What is the formula for compound interest with n compounding periods per year?
With n compoundings per year: A = P(1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ, dividing the rate and multiplying the exponent by the compounding frequency.
What is the continuously compounded interest formula?
Continuous compounding uses A = Peʳᵗ, the limit of A = P(1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ as n → ∞.
A car depreciates from $25,000 at 12% per year. What is its value after 4 years?
V = 25000(0.88)⁴ = 25000(0.59969) ≈ $14,984.
What is the present value of $10,000 to be received in 5 years at 8% annual interest?
PV = 10000/(1.08)⁵ = 10000/1.46933 ≈ $6,806.
What is 'compound interest'?
Compound interest is earned on both the original principal and the previously accumulated interest, leading to exponential growth.
What is 'present value'?
Present value (PV) is the current equivalent of a future amount, computed as PV = FV/(1+r)ⁿ.
What is an 'annuity'?
An annuity is a financial product that provides a series of equal payments at regular intervals, such as monthly retirement payments.
What is the 'Rule of 72'?
The Rule of 72 estimates doubling time by dividing 72 by the annual interest rate percentage. At 8%, money doubles in about 9 years.
A retiree deposits $200 per month into an account earning 6% annual interest compounded monthly. What is the value after 20 years? Use FV = PMT · [(1 + r/n)ⁿᵗ − 1]/(r/n).
FV = 200 · [(1.005)²⁴⁰ − 1]/0.005 = 200 · [3.3102 − 1]/0.005 = 200 · 462.04 ≈ $92,408.
A student takes out a $20,000 loan at 4.5% annual interest compounded monthly. If the loan term is 10 years, what is the monthly payment? Use PMT = P · (r/n)/[1 − (1 + r/n)^(−nt)].
PMT = 20000 · (0.00375)/[1 − (1.00375)⁻¹²⁰] = 75/[1 − 0.6387] = 75/0.3613 ≈ $207.
A homeowner's property is worth $300,000 and appreciates at 4% annually. What is the property worth after 10 years?
FV = 300000(1.04)¹⁰ = 300000(1.48024) ≈ $444,073.
A company invests $50,000 at 7% annual interest compounded continuously. What is the investment worth after 8 years?
A = 50000 · e^(0.07·8) = 50000 · e^0.56 = 50000 · 1.7507 ≈ $87,379.
A college fund starts with $10,000 and earns 5% annually. Simultaneously, $500 is withdrawn each year. Using the modified growth formula, what is the balance after 5 years?
Each year: balance = previous · 1.05 − 500. After 5 years: computing iteratively gives approximately $10,026.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'annual percentage rate (APR)' mean?
The APR is the nominal annual interest rate. It does not reflect the effect of intra-year compounding.

--- Lesson 8.7: Case Studies in Probability (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Case Studies in Probability
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the probability of rolling a 4 on a standard six-sided die?
A standard die has 6 equally likely outcomes, so P(4) = 1/6.
If two events A and B are independent, what is P(A and B)?
For independent events, P(A ∩ B) = P(A) · P(B).
A bag has 5 red and 3 blue marbles. What is the probability of drawing a red marble?
P(red) = 5/(5+3) = 5/8.
What is the probability of flipping two heads in a row with a fair coin?
P(HH) = (1/2)(1/2) = 1/4.
What is P(A or B) for mutually exclusive events?
For mutually exclusive events, P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) since P(A ∩ B) = 0.
What is the complement of an event A?
P(A') = 1 − P(A). The complement is the probability that event A does not occur.
A card is drawn from a standard 52-card deck. What is the probability it is a heart?
There are 13 hearts in a 52-card deck, so P(heart) = 13/52 = 1/4.
If P(A) = 0.3 and P(B) = 0.5 with A and B independent, what is P(A and B)?
P(A ∩ B) = 0.3 × 0.5 = 0.15.
Two dice are rolled. What is the probability the sum is 7?
There are 6 ways to get a sum of 7 out of 36 outcomes: 6/36 = 1/6.
What is 'probability'?
Probability is a number between 0 and 1 (inclusive) that represents the likelihood of an event. P = 0 means impossible, P = 1 means certain.
What is 'conditional probability'?
Conditional probability P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B)/P(B) gives the probability of A occurring, given that B has already occurred.
What is a 'permutation'?
A permutation is an ordered arrangement. P(n,r) = n!/(n−r)! counts the arrangements of r items from n.
What is a 'binomial probability distribution'?
The binomial distribution gives P(X = k) = C(n,k)pᵏ(1−p)ⁿ⁻ᵏ for k successes in n independent trials.
What is the 'addition rule' for probability?
The general addition rule is P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B), subtracting the overlap to avoid double counting.
A medical test has a 95% accuracy rate (true positive). If 2% of the population has a disease, and a random person tests positive, what is the probability they actually have the disease? Assume a 3% false positive rate.
By Bayes' Theorem: P(D|+) = P(+|D)P(D)/P(+) = (0.95)(0.02)/[(0.95)(0.02) + (0.03)(0.98)] = 0.019/(0.019 + 0.0294) = 0.019/0.0484 ≈ 39.2%.
A basketball player makes 80% of free throws. In a game, she shoots 10 free throws. What is the probability she makes exactly 8?
P(X=8) = C(10,8)(0.8)⁸(0.2)² = 45 · 0.16777 · 0.04 = 45 · 0.006711 ≈ 0.302.
An insurance company finds that 5% of drivers file a claim each year. Among 20 policyholders, what is the expected number of claims?
E(X) = np = 20(0.05) = 1 expected claim.
A quality control process finds that 2% of items are defective. A batch of 50 items is inspected. What is the probability that exactly 0 items are defective?
P(X=0) = C(50,0)(0.02)⁰(0.98)⁵⁰ = (0.98)⁵⁰ ≈ 0.364.
A genetics experiment crosses two heterozygous plants (Aa × Aa). What is the probability that exactly 3 out of 5 offspring show the dominant phenotype?
P(dominant) = 3/4. P(X=3) = C(5,3)(3/4)³(1/4)² = 10 · 27/64 · 1/16 = 270/1024 ≈ 0.264.
A company surveyed 200 employees: 120 like coffee, 80 like tea, and 50 like both. What is the probability a randomly selected employee likes coffee or tea?
P(C ∪ T) = P(C) + P(T) − P(C ∩ T) = 120/200 + 80/200 − 50/200 = 150/200 = 3/4.
A weighted die has P(6) = 1/3 and all other faces equally likely. What is P(1)?
Remaining probability: 1 − 1/3 = 2/3 shared equally among 5 faces: P(1) = (2/3)/5 = 2/15.
A delivery company finds that 90% of packages arrive on time. If you order 4 packages, what is the probability that all 4 arrive on time?
P(all 4 on time) = (0.9)⁴ = 0.6561.
