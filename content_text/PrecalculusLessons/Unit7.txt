--- Lesson 7.1: Pythagorean Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Pythagorean Identities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the fundamental Pythagorean identity?
The fundamental Pythagorean identity is sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.
If sin θ = 3/5 and θ is in Q I, what is cos θ?
cos²θ = 1 − sin²θ = 1 − 9/25 = 16/25. In Q I, cos θ = 4/5.
What is a 'trigonometric identity'?
A trig identity is universally true (wherever defined), unlike a conditional equation that holds only for specific values.
What is meant by 'rewriting in terms of sine and cosine'?
Converting all functions to sine and cosine (e.g., tan = sin/cos, sec = 1/cos) is a common strategy for simplification.
What is the relationship between sin²θ and the other Pythagorean identity forms?
Rearranging sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 gives sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ.
What is the strategy of 'factoring' when verifying trig identities?
Factoring techniques like a² − b² = (a+b)(a−b) work with trig expressions, e.g., sin²θ − cos²θ = (sinθ+cosθ)(sinθ−cosθ).
A physics student needs to simplify the expression sin²θ/(1 − cos θ) for a calculation. What is the simplified form?
sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ = (1 − cosθ)(1 + cosθ). Dividing by (1 − cosθ) gives 1 + cos θ.
A structural engineer needs to simplify (tan²θ + 1)/(cot²θ + 1) for a load calculation. What is the result?
(tan²θ+1)/(cot²θ+1) = sec²θ/csc²θ = (1/cos²θ)/(1/sin²θ) = sin²θ/cos²θ = tan²θ.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'conjugate multiplication' mean in trig simplification?
Multiplying by the conjugate (e.g., 1 − sinθ becomes 1 − sin²θ = cos²θ) is a powerful simplification technique.

--- Lesson 7.2: Sum & Difference Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Sum & Difference Formulas
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is cos(A + B) equal to?
cos(A+B) = cosAcosB − sinAsinB. Note the minus sign for cosine sum.
What is sin(A − B)?
sin(A−B) = sinAcosB − cosAsinB.
If sin A = 3/5 (Q I) and cos B = 5/13 (Q I), what is sin(A + B)?

What is tan(A − B)?
tan(A−B) = (tanA − tanB)/(1 + tanAtanB). Note the plus sign in the denominator for difference.
What is the 'sum formula for sine'?
The sine sum formula: sin(A+B) = sinAcosB + cosAsinB.
What is the 'difference formula for cosine'?
cos(A−B) = cosAcosB + sinAsinB. Note the plus sign for cosine difference.
What is the key sign difference between cos(A + B) and cos(A − B)?
cos(A+B) = cosAcosB − sinAsinB and cos(A−B) = cosAcosB + sinAsinB. The sign between terms is opposite to the operation.
What is the sign pattern in the sine sum vs. sine difference formulas?
sin(A+B) = sinAcosB + cosAsinB, sin(A−B) = sinAcosB − cosAsinB. The sign matches the operation.
What is the 'sum formula for tangent' used for?
The tangent sum formula allows computation of tan(A+B) from tanA and tanB.
Two sound waves with phases θ₁ and θ₂ are combined. The resultant involves sin(θ₁ + θ₂). If θ₁ = π/6 and θ₂ = π/4, what is the exact value of sin(θ₁ + θ₂)?
sin(5π/12) = sin(π/6+π/4) = sin(π/6)cos(π/4)+cos(π/6)sin(π/4) = (1/2)(√2/2)+(√3/2)(√2/2) = (√2+√6)/4.
An engineer needs cos(105°) for a structural calculation. Using 105° = 60° + 45°, what is the exact value?
cos(60°+45°) = cos60cos45 − sin60sin45 = (1/2)(√2/2) − (√3/2)(√2/2) = (√2−√6)/4.
A satellite dish is oriented at angle (α − β) where α and β are known. If tan α = 2 and tan β = 1/3, what is tan(α − β)?
tan(α−β) = (2−1/3)/(1+2·1/3) = (5/3)/(5/3) = 1.
A physics experiment requires sin(π/4 − π/6). What is the exact value?
sin(π/4−π/6) = sin(π/4)cos(π/6)−cos(π/4)sin(π/6) = (√2/2)(√3/2)−(√2/2)(1/2) = (√6−√2)/4.
A surveyor needs to compute sin(A + B) where sin A = 4/5, cos A = 3/5 (Q I) and sin B = 5/13, cos B = 12/13 (Q I). What is sin(A + B)?
sin(A+B) = sinAcosB + cosAsinB = (4/5)(12/13) + (3/5)(5/13) = 48/65 + 15/65 = 63/65.
A robotics rotation involves computing cos(30° − 45°). What is the exact value?
cos(−15°) = cos(15°) = cos(45°−30°) = cos45cos30+sin45sin30 = (√6+√2)/4.
A navigation system computes the angle between two headings using tan(α − β). If α = 135° (tan α = −1) and β = 120° (tan β = −√3), what is tan(α − β)?
tan(135°−120°) = tan15° = (−1−(−√3))/(1+(−1)(−√3)) = (√3−1)/(1+√3). Rationalizing: (√3−1)²/((1+√3)(√3−1)) = (4−2√3)/2 = 2−√3.
A physicist simplifies sin(x + π/2) in a wave equation. What is the simplified form?
sin(x+π/2) = sinxcos(π/2) + cosxsin(π/2) = sinx(0) + cosx(1) = cosx.

--- Lesson 7.6: Applications in Modeling Periodic Phenomena (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Modeling Periodic Phenomena
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A temperature varies from 40°F to 80°F over a year. What is the amplitude of a sinusoidal model?
Amplitude = (max−min)/2 = (80−40)/2 = 20°F.
A tide has a maximum height of 8 ft and minimum of 2 ft. What is the midline (vertical shift)?
Midline = (max+min)/2 = (8+2)/2 = 5 ft.
A Ferris wheel takes 60 seconds for one revolution. What is B in the model h(t) = A sin(Bt) + D?
Period = 60 s, so B = 2π/60 = π/30.
Daylight hours vary sinusoidally with period 365 days. What is B?
B = 2π/period = 2π/365.
A sound wave has frequency 440 Hz. What is the period?
Period = 1/frequency = 1/440 seconds.
What is a 'sinusoidal model'?
Sinusoidal models use y = Asin(Bx−C)+D or y = Acos(Bx−C)+D to fit periodic data.
What is meant by 'regression' when fitting sinusoidal models to data?
Sinusoidal regression uses computational methods to find A, B, C, D that minimize the sum of squared errors.
A harbor tide has max depth 12 ft at 2:00 AM and min depth 4 ft at 8:15 AM. What is the amplitude and period?
A=(12−4)/2=4 ft. Time from max to min is 6.25 hours = half period, so period ≈ 12.5 hours.
Blood pressure oscillates sinusoidally. Systolic = 120 mmHg, diastolic = 80 mmHg, heart rate = 72 bpm. What is B in the model?
72 bpm = 72/60 = 1.2 beats/sec. Period = 1/1.2 sec. B = 2π/(1/1.2) = 2.4π.
Electricity demand varies from 500 MW (4 AM) to 900 MW (4 PM) daily. What is the amplitude and midline?
A=(900−500)/2=200, D=(900+500)/2=700.
Real-World Applications
Example: The temperature model T(t) = 20sin(πt/6) + 60 has what average temperature?
The average (midline) is D = 60°.
Example: A seasonal sales model has minimum sales in January (month 1) and maximum in July (month 7). If using cosine, should A be positive or negative?
Negative cosine starts at its minimum, matching a January minimum.
Example: A city's average monthly temperature ranges from 30°F (January) to 90°F (July). Write a cosine model T(t) where t = 1 is January.
A=30, D=60, period=12→B=π/6. Min at t=1: −cos with shift gives T=−30cos(π(t−1)/6)+60.
Example: Monthly ice cream sales peak at $50,000 in July and bottom at $10,000 in January. Model the sales S(t) where t is months since January.
A=20000, D=30000, period=12→B=π/6. Min at t=0: −cos starts at min. S(t)=−20000cos(πt/6)+30000.
Important Concepts
A sound wave has frequency 440 Hz. What is the period
Period = 1/frequency = 1/440 seconds.
What does 'frequency' mean in a sound wave model
Frequency (Hz) = number of cycles per second. Higher frequency = higher pitch.

--- Lesson 7.8: AP Calculus Prep (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: AP Calculus Prep
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the exact value of sin(π/6)?
From the unit circle, sin(π/6) = sin 30° = 1/2.
What is the value of lim(x→0) [sin x / x]?
The special trigonometric limit lim(x→0) sin x / x = 1, which is fundamental in calculus.
What is cos(π/4)?
From the unit circle, cos(π/4) = cos 45° = √2/2.
What is the derivative of sin x?
The derivative of sin x is cos x, a key result derived using the limit definition and lim(h→0) sin h / h = 1.
What is tan(π/3)?
tan(π/3) = sin(π/3)/cos(π/3) = (√3/2)/(1/2) = √3.
What is the derivative of cos x?
The derivative of cos x is −sin x, which uses both lim(h→0) sin h / h = 1 and lim(h→0) (cos h − 1)/h = 0.
What is the exact value of cos(π/6)?
From the unit circle, cos(π/6) = cos 30° = √3/2.
What is the Squeeze Theorem?
The Squeeze Theorem (or Sandwich Theorem) states that if g(x) ≤ f(x) ≤ h(x) near x = a and both outer limits equal L, then lim f(x) = L as well.
What is 'continuity at a point' in calculus?
The three conditions for continuity at x = a are: f(a) is defined, lim(x→a) f(x) exists, and lim(x→a) f(x) = f(a).
What is the 'limit definition of the derivative'?
The derivative is defined as f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) − f(x)] / h, representing the instantaneous rate of change.
What is a 'removable discontinuity'?
A removable discontinuity (hole) occurs where the limit exists but either f(a) is undefined or f(a) does not equal the limit. It can be 'removed' by redefining f(a).
What is 'L'Hôpital's Rule' used for?
L'Hôpital's Rule states that if lim f(x)/g(x) gives 0/0 or ∞/∞, then it equals lim f'(x)/g'(x), provided this latter limit exists.
What is a 'secant line' in the context of calculus?
A secant line passes through two points on a curve. Its slope equals the average rate of change, and as the two points merge, it becomes the tangent line.
A ball is thrown upward with height h(t) = −16t² + 48t + 5 feet. Using the limit definition, what is the instantaneous velocity at t = 1 second?
h'(t) = −32t + 48. At t = 1, h'(1) = −32(1) + 48 = 16 ft/s.
An engineer models the displacement of a vibrating beam as s(t) = 3 sin(2t). What is s'(t), the velocity function?
Using the chain rule, s'(t) = 3 · cos(2t) · 2 = 6 cos(2t).
A population is modeled by P(t) = 500/(1 + 9e^(−0.2t)). What is the value of lim(t→∞) P(t), the carrying capacity?
As t → ∞, e^(−0.2t) → 0, so P(t) → 500/(1 + 0) = 500. The carrying capacity is 500.
A student needs to evaluate lim(x→0) [tan x / x]. Using the fact that tan x = sin x / cos x and the known limit lim(x→0) sin x / x = 1, what is the answer?
tan x / x = (sin x / x) · (1/cos x). As x → 0, sin x/x → 1 and 1/cos x → 1, so the limit is 1.
A rocket's altitude is given by h(t) = t³ − 6t² + 12t (in km). What is the average rate of change of altitude from t = 0 to t = 3 seconds?
h(3) = 27 − 54 + 36 = 9, h(0) = 0. Average rate = (9 − 0)/(3 − 0) = 3 km/s.
A drug concentration in the bloodstream is modeled by C(t) = 5t/(t² + 4) mg/L. What is lim(t→∞) C(t)?
The degree of the denominator (2) exceeds the degree of the numerator (1), so lim(t→∞) C(t) = 0. The drug eventually clears from the bloodstream.
On an AP exam, a student encounters f(x) = (x² − 9)/(x − 3). What is lim(x→3) f(x)?
Factor: (x² − 9)/(x − 3) = (x − 3)(x + 3)/(x − 3) = x + 3 for x ≠ 3. So lim(x→3) = 3 + 3 = 6.
A physics student models the angle of a pendulum as θ(t) = 0.5 cos(4πt). What is the angular velocity θ'(t)?
θ'(t) = 0.5 · (−sin(4πt)) · 4π = −2π sin(4πt).
A manufacturing company's cost function is C(x) = 1000 + 50x − 0.1x². Using the limit definition, what is the marginal cost C'(x) at x = 100 units?
C'(x) = 50 − 0.2x. At x = 100, C'(100) = 50 − 0.2(100) = 50 − 20 = $30.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'average rate of change' mean for a function?
The average rate of change over [a, b] is [f(b) − f(a)]/(b − a), which is the slope of the secant line connecting the two endpoints.
Example: A rocket's altitude is given by h(t) = t³ − 6t² + 12t (in km). What is the average rate of change of altitude from t = 0 to t = 3 seconds?
h(3) = 27 − 54 + 36 = 9, h(0) = 0. Average rate = (9 − 0)/(3 − 0) = 3 km/s.
Example: On an AP exam, a student encounters f(x) = (x² − 9)/(x − 3). What is lim(x→3) f(x)?
Factor: (x² − 9)/(x − 3) = (x − 3)(x + 3)/(x − 3) = x + 3 for x ≠ 3. So lim(x→3) = 3 + 3 = 6.

  Q11. What is the Squeeze Theorem?

    Explanation: The Squeeze Theorem (or Sandwich Theorem) states that if g(x) ≤ f(x) ≤ h(x) near x = a and both outer limits equal L, then lim f(x) = L as well.

  Q12. What does the term 'indeterminate form' mean in the context of limits?

  Q13. What is 'continuity at a point' in calculus?
    [ ] A function is continuous at x = a if it passes the vertical line test
    [*] A function is continuous at x = a if f(a) is defined, lim(x→a) f(x) exists, and lim(x→a) f(x) = f(a)
    [ ] A function is continuous at x = a if it has a derivative at x = a
    [ ] A function is continuous if it is increasing on an interval
    Explanation: The three conditions for continuity at x = a are: f(a) is defined, lim(x→a) f(x) exists, and lim(x→a) f(x) = f(a).

  Q14. What is the 'limit definition of the derivative'?
    [ ] f'(x) = lim(x→∞) f(x) / x

    [ ] f'(x) = Δy / Δx
    Explanation: The derivative is defined as f'(x) = lim(h→0) [f(x+h) − f(x)] / h, representing the instantaneous rate of change.

  Q15. What does 'average rate of change' mean for a function?

    Explanation: The average rate of change over [a, b] is [f(b) − f(a)]/(b − a), which is the slope of the secant line connecting the two endpoints.

  Q16. What is a 'removable discontinuity'?

    [ ] A point where the left and right limits are different

    [ ] A point where the derivative is zero
    Explanation: A removable discontinuity (hole) occurs where the limit exists but either f(a) is undefined or f(a) does not equal the limit. It can be 'removed' by redefining f(a).

    [ ] A line segment connecting two points on a curve
    Explanation: An asymptote is a line that a function's graph approaches arbitrarily closely as x or y approaches some value. Asymptotes can be vertical, horizontal, or oblique.

  Q18. What is 'L'Hôpital's Rule' used for?

    [ ] Computing the area under a curve
    Explanation: L'Hôpital's Rule states that if lim f(x)/g(x) gives 0/0 or ∞/∞, then it equals lim f'(x)/g'(x), provided this latter limit exists.

  Q19. What is a 'secant line' in the context of calculus?

    [ ] A line perpendicular to the curve at a point

    Explanation: A secant line passes through two points on a curve. Its slope equals the average rate of change, and as the two points merge, it becomes the tangent line.

  Q20. What does 'instantaneous rate of change' mean?
