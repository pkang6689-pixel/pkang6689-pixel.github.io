--- Lesson 7.1: Pythagorean Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Pythagorean Identities
Pythagorean Identities
The three Pythagorean identities are derived from sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.
The Three Forms  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1  • 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ (divide by cos²θ)  • 1 + cot²θ = csc²θ (divide by sin²θ)Using Identities  • Simplify expressions by substituting one identity for another.  • Verify identities by transforming one side to match the other.  • Rewrite in terms of sine and cosine as a common strategy.

--- Lesson 7.1: Pythagorean Identities (Quiz) ---

  Q1. sin²θ + cos²θ = ?
    [ ] 0
    [ ] sin θ
    [*] 1
    [ ] 2
    Explanation: Fundamental Pythagorean identity.

  Q2. 1 + tan²θ = ?
    [ ] csc²θ
    [*] sec²θ
    [ ] cos²θ
    [ ] sin²θ
    Explanation: Divide sin²+cos²=1 by cos²θ.

  Q3. 1 + cot²θ = ?
    [ ] sec²θ
    [*] csc²θ
    [ ] tan²θ
    [ ] sin²θ + cos²θ
    Explanation: Divide sin²+cos²=1 by sin²θ.

  Q4. If sin θ = 3/5 (Q I), find cos θ.
    [ ] 3/5
    [*] 4/5
    [ ] 5/3
    [ ] 4/3
    Explanation: cos²θ = 1 − 9/25 = 16/25; cos θ = 4/5.

  Q5. If cos θ = −5/13 (Q II), find sin θ.
    [ ] 5/13
    [*] 12/13
    [ ] −12/13
    [ ] −5/13
    Explanation: sin²θ = 1 − 25/169 = 144/169; sin θ = 12/13 (positive in Q II).

  Q6. Simplify: sec²θ − tan²θ.
    [ ] 0
    [ ] sin²θ
    [*] 1
    [ ] 2sec²θ
    Explanation: sec²θ − tan²θ = 1 (from 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ).

  Q7. Simplify: csc²θ − cot²θ.
    [ ] 0
    [*] 1
    [ ] csc²θ
    [ ] sin²θ
    Explanation: Same idea: 1 + cot²θ = csc²θ → csc² − cot² = 1.

  Q8. Simplify: sin²θ/(1 − cos²θ).
    [ ] cos²θ
    [ ] 0
    [*] 1
    [ ] sin θ
    Explanation: 1 − cos²θ = sin²θ. So sin²θ/sin²θ = 1.

  Q9. Express tan²θ in terms of sec θ.
    [ ] sec θ − 1
    [*] sec²θ − 1
    [ ] 1 − sec²θ
    [ ] sec²θ + 1
    Explanation: tan²θ = sec²θ − 1.

  Q10. Simplify: (1 − sin²θ)/cos θ.
    [ ] sin θ
    [ ] 1
    [*] cos θ
    [ ] tan θ
    Explanation: 1 − sin²θ = cos²θ. cos²θ/cos θ = cos θ.

  Q11. Verify: (sin θ)(csc θ) = 1.
    [ ] Not an identity
    [*] True: sin θ · (1/sin θ) = 1
    [ ] Only for θ = π/4
    [ ] Only for θ = 0
    Explanation: csc θ = 1/sin θ, so the product is 1.

  Q12. Simplify: tan θ · cos θ.
    [ ] cos²θ
    [ ] 1
    [*] sin θ
    [ ] sec θ
    Explanation: tan θ cos θ = (sin θ/cos θ)(cos θ) = sin θ.

  Q13. Simplify: (1 + sin θ)(1 − sin θ).
    [ ] 1 − sin θ
    [*] cos²θ
    [ ] sin²θ
    [ ] 1
    Explanation: Difference of squares: 1 − sin²θ = cos²θ.

  Q14. If tan θ = 2 (Q I), find sec θ.
    [ ] 2
    [ ] 3
    [*] √5
    [ ] √2
    Explanation: sec²θ = 1 + 4 = 5; sec θ = √5.

  Q15. Simplify: cos²θ · tan²θ + cos²θ.
    [ ] sin²θ
    [ ] 2cos²θ
    [ ] cos²θ + sin²θ
    [*] 1
    Explanation: cos²θ tan²θ = cos²θ · sin²θ/cos²θ = sin²θ. sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.

  Q16. Simplify: sec θ − cos θ.
    [ ] 1
    [*] sin θ tan θ
    [ ] tan θ
    [ ] sin²θ
    Explanation: 1/cos θ − cos θ = (1 − cos²θ)/cos θ = sin²θ/cos θ = sin θ · tan θ.

  Q17. Which is NOT a Pythagorean identity?
    [ ] sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
    [ ] 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
    [*] sin θ + cos θ = 1
    [ ] 1 + cot²θ = csc²θ
    Explanation: sin θ + cos θ ≠ 1 in general.

  Q18. Simplify: cot²θ · sin²θ + sin²θ.
    [ ] cos²θ + sin²θ
    [*] 1
    [ ] cot²θ
    [ ] 2sin²θ
    Explanation: cot²θ sin²θ = cos²θ. cos²θ + sin²θ = 1.

  Q19. Express csc²θ − 1 as:
    [ ] sec²θ
    [ ] sin²θ
    [*] cot²θ
    [ ] tan²θ
    Explanation: csc²θ − 1 = cot²θ.

  Q20. Verify: tan²θ − sin²θ = tan²θ · sin²θ.
    [*] True (LHS = sin²θ/cos²θ − sin²θ = sin²θ(1/cos²θ − 1) = sin²θ(sec²θ − 1) = sin²θ tan²θ)
    [ ] False
    [ ] Only sometimes true
    [ ] Only in Q I
    Explanation: Factor sin²θ: sin²θ(sec²θ − 1) = sin²θ tan²θ = tan²θ sin²θ.

--- Lesson 7.2: Sum & Difference Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Sum & Difference Formulas
Sum & Difference FormulasSine  • sin(A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B  • sin(A − B) = sin A cos B − cos A sin BCosine  • cos(A + B) = cos A cos B − sin A sin B  • cos(A − B) = cos A cos B + sin A sin BTangent  • tan(A + B) = (tan A + tan B)/(1 − tan A tan B)  • tan(A − B) = (tan A − tan B)/(1 + tan A tan B)

--- Lesson 7.3: Double-Angle & Half-Angle Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Double-Angle & Half-Angle Formulas
Double-Angle & Half-Angle FormulasDouble-Angle  • sin 2θ = 2 sin θ cos θ  • cos 2θ = cos²θ − sin²θ = 2cos²θ − 1 = 1 − 2sin²θ  • tan 2θ = 2 tan θ / (1 − tan²θ)Half-Angle  • sin(θ/2) = ±√((1 − cos θ)/2)  • cos(θ/2) = ±√((1 + cos θ)/2)  • tan(θ/2) = sin θ/(1 + cos θ) = (1 − cos θ)/sin θ  • Sign (±) depends on the quadrant of θ/2.

--- Lesson 7.4: Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product Identities
Product-to-Sum and Sum-to-Product IdentitiesProduct-to-Sum  • sin A cos B = (1/2)[sin(A+B) + sin(A−B)]  • cos A cos B = (1/2)[cos(A−B) + cos(A+B)]  • sin A sin B = (1/2)[cos(A−B) − cos(A+B)]Sum-to-Product  • sin C + sin D = 2 sin((C+D)/2) cos((C−D)/2)  • sin C − sin D = 2 cos((C+D)/2) sin((C−D)/2)  • cos C + cos D = 2 cos((C+D)/2) cos((C−D)/2)  • cos C − cos D = −2 sin((C+D)/2) sin((C−D)/2)

--- Lesson 7.5: Solving Trigonometric Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Trigonometric Equations
Solving Trigonometric EquationsStrategy  • Isolate the trig function (use identities if needed).  • Find reference angle solutions.  • Use the unit circle to find all solutions in [0, 2π).  • Add the period (2πn for sin/cos, πn for tan) for general solutions.Quadratic-Type Equations  • Substitute u = sin θ or cos θ, solve the quadratic, then back-substitute.

--- Lesson 7.6: Applications in Modeling Periodic Phenomena (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Modeling Periodic Phenomena
Applications in Modeling Periodic Phenomena
Trigonometric functions model any quantity that repeats at regular intervals.
Examples  • Temperature: T(t) = A sin(B(t − C)) + D models daily/yearly temperature cycles.  • Tides: Height varies sinusoidally with period ≈ 12.4 hours.  • Sound: Pressure waves modeled by sine functions; frequency = pitch.  • Daylight hours: Varies sinusoidally over a year.Fitting a Sinusoidal Model  • Amplitude = (max − min)/2  • Vertical shift D = (max + min)/2  • Period = distance between repeating patterns → B = 2π/period.  • Phase shift: adjust C to align with data.

--- Lesson 7.8: AP Calculus Prep (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: AP Calculus Prep
AP Calculus Prep: Trigonometric Skills
Calculus builds heavily on trig. These skills are essential before starting AP Calculus.
Must-Know for Calculus  • Unit circle values: Instant recall of sin, cos, tan at standard angles.  • Identities: Pythagorean, double-angle, and sum/difference formulas.  • Key limits: lim(x→0) sin x/x = 1 and lim(x→0) (1 − cos x)/x = 0.  • Derivatives preview: d/dx[sin x] = cos x, d/dx[cos x] = −sin x.  • Trig substitution: For integrals involving √(a² − x²), substitute x = a sin θ.
