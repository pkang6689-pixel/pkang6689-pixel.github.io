--- Lesson 1.1: Review of Algebra (factoring, radicals, rational expressions) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Review of Algebra (factoring, radicals, rational expressions)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 'factoring' in algebra?
Factoring is the process of rewriting an expression as a product of its factors, essentially the reverse of expanding or distributing.
What is the 'greatest common factor' (GCF) of a polynomial?
The GCF is the largest factor (including coefficients and variables) that divides into every term of the polynomial without a remainder.
What is a 'rational expression'?
A rational expression is a ratio (fraction) of two polynomials, such as (x² + 1)/(x − 3).
What is the 'least common denominator' (LCD) when adding rational expressions?
The LCD is the least common multiple of all denominators, providing the simplest shared denominator for combining fractions.
What is a 'perfect square trinomial'?
A perfect square trinomial has the form a² + 2ab + b² = (a + b)² or a² − 2ab + b² = (a − b)².
An engineer needs to simplify the expression (x² − 16)/(x + 4) to calculate a load factor. What is the simplified result?
Factor the numerator as a difference of squares: (x + 4)(x − 4)/(x + 4) = x − 4, for x ≠ −4.
A physics student must simplify √72 meters when reporting a measurement. What is the simplified form?
√72 = √(36 · 2) = √36 · √2 = 6√2 meters.
A square courtyard has an area of (x² − 10x + 25) square meters. What is the length of one side?
x² − 10x + 25 is a perfect square trinomial: (x − 5)². The side length is (x − 5) meters.
A scientist calculates a ratio of concentrations as 5/(3 + √2) mg/L. After rationalizing the denominator, what is the expression?
Multiply by the conjugate: 5(3 − √2)/[(3 + √2)(3 − √2)] = 5(3 − √2)/(9 − 2) = 5(3 − √2)/7.
Two pipes fill a tank. Pipe A fills 1/(x + 2) of the tank per hour and Pipe B fills 1/(x − 1) per hour. What is their combined rate using the LCD?
LCD = (x + 2)(x − 1). Rewrite: (x − 1)/((x + 2)(x − 1)) + (x + 2)/((x + 2)(x − 1)) = (x − 1 + x + 2)/((x + 2)(x − 1)) = (2x + 1)/((x + 2)(x − 1)).
A farmer's field has a total area of 6x² + 11x + 3 square yards. He wants to divide it into rectangular plots. If one dimension is (2x + 3), what is the other?
Factor 6x² + 11x + 3. Find factors of 18 (6 × 3) that add to 11: 9 and 2. Rewrite: 6x² + 9x + 2x + 3 = 3x(2x + 3) + 1(2x + 3) = (3x + 1)(2x + 3).

--- Lesson 1.2: Order of Operations & Exponents (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Order of Operations & Exponents
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'rational exponent'?
A rational exponent is a fraction m/n, where a^(m/n) means the nth root of a raised to the mth power: a^(m/n) = (ⁿ√a)^m.
What is the 'zero exponent rule'?
The zero exponent rule states that a⁰ = 1 for any a ≠ 0. This follows from aⁿ/aⁿ = a^(n−n) = a⁰ = 1.
What is the difference between (−2)⁴ and −2⁴?
In (−2)⁴, the entire base (−2) is raised to the 4th power, giving 16. In −2⁴, only 2 is raised to the 4th power, then negated: −(2⁴) = −16.
What is an 'indeterminate form'?
An indeterminate form, such as 0⁰ or 0/0, cannot have its value determined solely from its algebraic form and requires additional context or limits to evaluate.
A bacteria population doubles every hour. If the initial count is 500, the population after t hours is 500 · 2ᵗ. What is the population after 3 hours?
After 3 hours: 500 · 2³ = 500 · 8 = 4,000 bacteria.
A student evaluates the expression 12 ÷ 3 + 2 × 5 − 1. Following the correct order of operations, what is the result?
Division and multiplication first (left to right): 12 ÷ 3 = 4, 2 × 5 = 10. Then addition and subtraction: 4 + 10 − 1 = 13.
An investment of $1,000 grows by a factor of (1.05)ⁿ after n years. What is the growth factor after 0 years?
At n = 0 years: (1.05)⁰ = 1. Any nonzero number to the zero power is 1, meaning no growth has occurred yet.
A chemist measures the concentration of a solution as 27^(2/3) milligrams per liter. What is this value?
27^(2/3) = (∛27)² = 3² = 9 milligrams per liter.
A box has dimensions x², x³, and x⁴ centimeters. The volume is found by multiplying all three. What is the simplified expression for the volume?
Multiply by adding exponents: x² · x³ · x⁴ = x^(2+3+4) = x⁹ cm³.
A recipe calls for (3a²b)² grams of an ingredient. A cook needs to simplify this. What is the simplified form?
Apply the power of a product rule: (3a²b)² = 3² · (a²)² · b² = 9a⁴b².
A physics formula involves the term (x^(1/2))⁶ meters. What is the simplified expression?
Using the power of a power rule: (x^(1/2))⁶ = x^(1/2 · 6) = x^(6/2) = x³ meters.
An electronics engineer calculates a resistance ratio as (x/y)⁻². What is the simplified expression?
A negative exponent flips the fraction: (x/y)⁻² = (y/x)² = y²/x².
A spreadsheet formula computes 2³ + 2². A student claims this equals 2⁵ = 32. What is the correct value?
You cannot add exponents when adding terms. Evaluate each: 2³ = 8 and 2² = 4, so 8 + 4 = 12.
A biologist records a cell count of x⁻² · x⁵ cells per mL after simplification. What is the simplified expression?
Add exponents when multiplying like bases: x⁻² · x⁵ = x^(−2+5) = x³ cells per mL.
Real-World Applications
Example: A box has dimensions x², x³, and x⁴ centimeters. The volume is found by multiplying all three. What is the simplified expression for the volume?
Multiply by adding exponents: x² · x³ · x⁴ = x^(2+3+4) = x⁹ cm³.

--- Lesson 1.3: Relations vs. Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Relations vs. Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'relation' in mathematics?
A relation is any set of ordered pairs (x, y). It may or may not be a function.
What is the defining property of a 'function'?
A function is a relation in which each element of the domain (input) is paired with exactly one element of the range (output).
What is the 'domain' of a function?
The domain is the complete set of all possible input values (x-values) for which the function is defined.
What is the 'range' of a function?
The range is the set of all actual output values (y-values) that the function produces.
What is a 'mapping diagram'?
A mapping diagram displays domain elements on one side and range elements on the other, with arrows showing which inputs map to which outputs.
What is the difference between a 'function' and a 'one-to-one function'?
Every one-to-one function is a function, but a one-to-one function adds the requirement that no two different inputs produce the same output.
Real-World Applications
Example: A parking garage charges a flat rate based on the number of hours parked: $5 for 0-2 hours, $10 for 2-4 hours, $15 for 4-6 hours. Is the cost a function of hours parked?
Each input (hours parked) maps to exactly one cost, making it a function. The fact that multiple hour values share the same cost does not violate the function definition.

  Key Concepts: Domain & Range (interval notation)
Overview

Key Definitions
A drone can fly at altitudes between 0 and 400 feet inclusive. What is the range of the altitude function in interval notation?
Since the drone can fly at exactly 0 feet (ground) and exactly 400 feet, both endpoints are included, giving [0, 400].

Time starts at t = 0, and 500 - 10t >= 0 gives t <= 50. Both endpoints are achievable, so the domain is [0, 50].

For the square root to be defined, x − 100 ≥ 0, so x ≥ 100. Domain: [100, ∞).

An engineer models signal strength as S(d) = 100/d² watts, where d is distance in meters. If the receiver works between 1 and 50 meters, what is the domain?
The receiver operates from 1 to 50 meters inclusive, so the practical domain is [1, 50].
A ball is thrown upward with height h(t) = −16t² + 64t feet. What is the range of the height function?
The ball starts at h = 0, reaches max at t = 2: h(2) = −16(4) + 64(2) = 64 feet, then returns to 0. Range is [0, 64].

A factory's efficiency is E(w) = w/(w + 10) where w is number of workers. For w > 0, efficiency approaches but never reaches 1. What is the range?
For w > 0, E(w) is always positive (never 0) and always less than 1 (approaches 1 as w → ∞ but never reaches it). Range: (0, 1).

--- Lesson 1.8: Inverse Functions & Composition (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Inverse Functions & Composition
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
The Celsius-to-Fahrenheit formula is F(C) = (9/5)C + 32. What is the inverse that converts Fahrenheit to Celsius?
Solve F = (9/5)C + 32 for C: F − 32 = (9/5)C, so C = (5/9)(F − 32).

(P ∘ g)(3) = P(g(3)) = P(6) = 200 + 50(6) = 500.

An encryption function is f(x) = 2x + 7. What is the decryption (inverse) function?
y = 2x + 7 -> y − 7 = 2x -> x = (y − 7)/2. So f⁻¹(x) = (x − 7)/2.
