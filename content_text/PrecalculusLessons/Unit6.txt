--- Lesson 6.1: Angles in Degrees & Radians (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angles in Degrees & Radians
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a radian?
A radian is defined as the central angle for which the intercepted arc length equals the radius.
What is the 'terminal side' of an angle?
The terminal side is the ray that has been rotated from the initial side; its position determines the measure of the angle.
What is the conversion factor from degrees to radians?
To convert degrees to radians, multiply the degree measure by π/180.
What is a 'quadrantal angle'?
A quadrantal angle has its terminal side on the x-axis or y-axis (e.g., 0°, 90°, 180°, 270°).
What is meant by the 'degree' as a unit of angle measure?
One degree is defined as 1/360 of a full revolution.
A skateboard ramp rises at an angle of π/6 radians above the horizontal. What is this angle in degrees?
(π/6)(180/π) = 30°.
A surveyor measures a turn of −150° while mapping a trail. What is this angle in radians?
−150 × π/180 = −5π/6 radians. The negative sign indicates a clockwise rotation.
A compass app shows a hiker has turned 7π/4 radians clockwise from north. What is the equivalent positive angle in degrees, measured counter-clockwise from north?
7π/4 radians clockwise = 315° clockwise. The equivalent counter-clockwise angle is 360° − 315° = 45°.

--- Lesson 6.2: Arc Length & Sector Area (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arc Length & Sector Area
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A circle has radius 8. What is the arc length subtended by a central angle of π/4?
s = rθ = 8 × π/4 = 2π.
A wheel of radius 3 ft rotates through an angle of 4 radians. What is the distance traveled by a point on the rim?
s = rθ = 3 × 4 = 12 ft.
If an arc length is 15 cm on a circle of radius 5 cm, what is the central angle in radians?
θ = s/r = 15/5 = 3 radians.
What is the angular speed (in rad/s) of a wheel that completes one full revolution in 4 seconds?
ω = θ/t = 2π/4 = π/2 rad/s.
A circle has circumference 20π. What is its radius?
C = 2πr, so 20π = 2πr, giving r = 10.
A wheel with radius 2 m has a linear speed of 6 m/s at the rim. What is the angular speed?
ω = v/r = 6/2 = 3 rad/s.
What is the formula for arc length when the central angle is given in radians?
Arc length s = rθ, where r is the radius and θ is the central angle in radians.
What is 'angular speed'?
Angular speed ω = θ/t measures how fast the angle changes, typically in radians per second.
What is the formula for the area of a sector when the angle is in radians?
The area of a sector is A = (1/2)r²θ, where θ must be in radians.
What is a 'central angle'?
A central angle has its vertex at the center of the circle and its sides are radii.
What is the relationship between linear speed v, radius r, and angular speed ω?
Linear speed equals the product of radius and angular speed: v = rω.
What is a 'segment' of a circle (as distinct from a sector)?
A segment is the region between a chord and the arc it cuts off, whereas a sector is bounded by radii.
A bicycle tire has a radius of 0.35 m. If the wheel rotates at 4 rad/s, what is the linear speed of a point on the rim?
v = rω = 0.35 × 4 = 1.4 m/s.
A pendulum swings through an arc of 18 cm on a string 45 cm long. What is the angle of the swing in radians?
θ = s/r = 18/45 = 0.4 radians.
A merry-go-round with radius 5 m makes one full revolution every 10 seconds. What is the linear speed of a child on the edge?
ω = 2π/10 = π/5 rad/s. v = rω = 5 × π/5 = π m/s.
A drone's camera covers a circular sector with radius 100 m and area 5000 m². What is the central angle of the sector in radians?
A = (1/2)r²θ → 5000 = (1/2)(10000)θ → θ = 1 radian.
A car tire with radius 0.3 m spins at 25 rev/s. What is the car's speed in m/s?
ω = 25 × 2π = 50π rad/s. v = rω = 0.3 × 50π = 15π m/s.
A record turntable spins at 33⅓ RPM. What is its angular speed in radians per second?
33⅓ RPM = 100/3 rev/min = (100/3)/60 rev/s = 5/9 rev/s. ω = (5/9)(2π) = 10π/9 rad/s.
Real-World Applications
Example: A windshield wiper blade is 20 inches long and sweeps through an angle of 2π/3 radians. What length of arc does the tip trace?
s = rθ = 20 × 2π/3 = 40π/3 inches.

--- Lesson 6.3: Unit Circle & Special Angles (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Unit Circle & Special Angles
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is sin(π/6)?
On the unit circle, sin(30°) = sin(π/6) = 1/2.
What is cos(π/4)?
cos(45°) = cos(π/4) = √2/2.
What is tan(π/3)?
tan(60°) = sin(60°)/cos(60°) = (√3/2)/(1/2) = √3.
What is cos(π)?
The point on the unit circle at π (180°) is (−1, 0), so cos(π) = −1.
What is sin(3π/2)?
At 3π/2 (270°), the point is (0, −1), so sin(3π/2) = −1.
What is cos(2π/3)?
2π/3 is in Q II with reference angle π/3. cos is negative in Q II, so cos(2π/3) = −1/2.
What is sin(5π/4)?
5π/4 is in Q III with reference angle π/4. sin is negative in Q III, so sin(5π/4) = −√2/2.
What is tan(π/4)?
tan(π/4) = sin(π/4)/cos(π/4) = (√2/2)/(√2/2) = 1.
What is cos(7π/6)?
7π/6 is in Q III with reference angle π/6. cos is negative in Q III, so cos(7π/6) = −√3/2.
What is the 'unit circle'?
The unit circle has radius 1 and is centered at the origin; a point at angle θ has coordinates (cos θ, sin θ).
What is a 'reference angle'?
A reference angle is the acute angle formed between the terminal side of the angle and the nearest part of the x-axis.
What is the 'ASTC' sign pattern for cosine across the four quadrants?
Cosine (the x-coordinate) is positive in Q I and Q IV and negative in Q II and Q III.
What is the meaning of 'ordered pair' on the unit circle at angle θ?
On the unit circle, the x-coordinate is cos θ and the y-coordinate is sin θ.
A clock shows 2:00. If the hour hand's position corresponds to an angle on the unit circle measured from 12 o'clock (the positive y-axis), what is the equivalent standard-position angle and the cosine of that angle?
At 2:00 the hour hand is at 60° clockwise from 12, which is 90° − 60° = 30° from the positive x-axis, so cos 30° = √3/2.
An architect designs a ramp that rises at an angle whose sine equals 1/2. Using the unit circle, what is this angle?
sin θ = 1/2 at θ = 30° (π/6) from the unit circle special values.
A boat sails on a heading that makes an angle of 5π/3 with the positive x-axis. Using the unit circle, what is sin(5π/3)?
5π/3 is in Q IV with reference angle π/3. sin is negative in Q IV, so sin(5π/3) = −√3/2.
A physics student needs the exact value of cos(3π/4) for a force-component calculation. What is it?
3π/4 is in Q II with reference angle π/4. cos is negative in Q II, so cos(3π/4) = −√2/2.
A Ferris wheel seat starts at the 3 o'clock position (0 radians) and rotates to 2π/3. What is the y-coordinate (height component relative to center) at that point?
The y-coordinate is sin(2π/3). Reference angle is π/3, and sin is positive in Q II, so sin(2π/3) = √3/2.
A drone flies in a circular pattern. At an angle of 11π/6 from the starting direction, what is the cosine of that angle, representing the east-west component of its position?
11π/6 is in Q IV with reference angle π/6. cos is positive in Q IV, so cos(11π/6) = √3/2.
An engineer calculates the horizontal displacement using cos(4π/3). What is the exact value?

--- Lesson 6.4: Sine, Cosine, Tangent Definitions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Sine, Cosine, Tangent Definitions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In a right triangle with opposite = 3, hypotenuse = 5, what is cos θ?
Adjacent = √(25 − 9) = 4. cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse = 4/5.
What is csc θ if sin θ = 3/5?
csc θ = 1/sin θ = 1/(3/5) = 5/3.
If tan θ = 7/24, and the triangle is a right triangle, what is sin θ?
Hypotenuse = √(7² + 24²) = √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25. sin θ = 7/25.
What is sec θ if cos θ = 5/13?
sec θ = 1/cos θ = 1/(5/13) = 13/5.
If sin θ = 0.6 and cos θ = 0.8, what is tan θ?
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = 0.6/0.8 = 0.75.
What is cot θ if tan θ = 4/3?
cot θ = 1/tan θ = 3/4.
In a right triangle, if the adjacent side is 8 and the hypotenuse is 10, what is tan θ?
Opposite = √(100 − 64) = 6. tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 6/8 = 3/4.
What is sin(90°)?
sin(90°) = 1, as the point on the unit circle at 90° is (0, 1).
What is the 'Pythagorean identity' in trigonometry?
The fundamental Pythagorean identity states that sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 for all angles θ.
What is the 'cosecant' function?
Cosecant is the reciprocal of sine: csc θ = 1/sin θ = hypotenuse/opposite.
What is the 'opposite side' in a right triangle relative to an angle θ?
The opposite side is the side directly across from angle θ and does not form either ray of that angle.
What is the 'hypotenuse' of a right triangle?
The hypotenuse is the longest side, always opposite the 90° angle.
A 13-ft ladder leans against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 5 ft from the wall. What is sin θ, where θ is the angle between the ladder and the ground?
Height = √(13² − 5²) = 12. sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 12/13.
A ramp rises 6 ft over a horizontal distance of 8 ft. What is the tangent of the ramp's angle of incline?
tan θ = opposite/adjacent = rise/run = 6/8 = 3/4.
A security camera is mounted 12 ft high on a wall. The camera tilts down at angle θ to view a point on the floor 9 ft from the wall. What is sec θ?
Hypotenuse = √(12² + 9²) = √(225) = 15. cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse = 12/15 = 4/5. sec θ = 5/4.
An electrician needs to run cable from the top of a 15-ft pole to a junction box on the ground 20 ft from the base. What is csc θ, where θ is the angle of elevation from the junction box?
Hypotenuse = √(15² + 20²) = 25. sin θ = 15/25 = 3/5. csc θ = 5/3.
A ship sails 10 km due east, then turns and sails 10 km due north. What is the sine of the angle between the ship's final position (from the starting point) and the eastward direction?
The displacement makes a 45° angle with the east direction (since both legs are 10 km). sin 45° = √2/2.

--- Lesson 6.5: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (amplitude, period, phase shift) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (amplitude, period, phase shift)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the amplitude of y = 3 sin x?
Amplitude = |A| = |3| = 3.
What is the period of y = sin(2x)?
Period = 2π/|B| = 2π/2 = π.
What is the phase shift of y = sin(x − π/4)?
Phase shift = C/B = (π/4)/1 = π/4 to the right.
What is the vertical shift (midline) of y = cos x + 5?
The vertical shift D = 5, so the midline is y = 5.
What is the period of y = tan x?
The standard tangent function has period π.
What is the amplitude of y = −2 cos(3x)?
Amplitude = |A| = |−2| = 2. The negative sign reflects the graph but does not change amplitude.
What is the period of y = cos(x/2)?
Period = 2π/|B| = 2π/(1/2) = 4π.
What is the maximum value of y = 4 sin x − 1?
Max = A + D = 4 + (−1) = 3.
What is 'amplitude' of a sinusoidal function?
Amplitude is |A|, the vertical distance from the midline to a peak or trough.

--- Lesson 6.9: Case Studies in Navigation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Case Studies in Navigation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Using the Law of Sines, if a/sin A = 10, and sin B = 0.5, what is b?
b/sin B = 10, so b = 10 × sin B = 10 × 0.5 = 5.
By the Law of Cosines, c² = a² + b² − 2ab cos C. If a = 3, b = 4, C = 90°, what is c?
c² = 9 + 16 − 2(3)(4)cos 90° = 25 − 0 = 25, so c = 5 (Pythagorean triple).
A ship sails 10 km due north, then 10 km due east. What is the straight-line distance from the starting point?
d = √(10² + 10²) = √200 = 10√2 km.
What is the bearing S 25° W as a standard compass bearing (clockwise from north)?
S 25° W = 180° + 25° = 205° measured clockwise from north.
What is a 'bearing' in navigation?
A bearing is a direction expressed as an angle measured clockwise from north (000° to 360°).
What is the 'Law of Sines'?
The Law of Sines states that in any triangle, the ratio of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is constant.
What is the 'Law of Cosines'?
The Law of Cosines relates the sides and an angle of any triangle; it reduces to the Pythagorean theorem when C = 90°.
What is the 'angle of elevation'?
The angle of elevation is measured from horizontal upward to the line of sight to a higher object.
What is the 'angle of depression'?
The angle of depression is measured from horizontal downward to the line of sight to a lower object.
What is a 'heading' in aviation?
A heading is the compass direction (0°-360°) that the aircraft's nose points.
A surveyor stands at point C and measures angles to points A and B. Angle ACB = 40°, CA = 100 m, CB = 80 m. By the Law of Cosines, what is AB?
AB² = 100² + 80² − 2(100)(80)cos 40° = 10000 + 6400 − 16000(0.766) ≈ 16400 − 12256 = 4144. AB ≈ 64.3 m.
A plane flies 250 km on bearing 150° then turns and flies 300 km on bearing 060°. What is the included angle between the two flight legs?
At the turn point, the angle between continuing on 150° and turning to 060° = 180° − 150° + 060° = 90° (the external and internal bearing angles give 90°).
