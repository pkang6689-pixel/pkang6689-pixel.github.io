--- Lesson 6.1: Angles in Degrees & Radians (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angles in Degrees & Radians
Angles in Degrees & Radians
Angles can be measured in degrees or radians.
Conversions  • 360° = 2π radians; 180° = π radians.  • Degrees → radians: multiply by π/180.  • Radians → degrees: multiply by 180/π.Standard Position  • Vertex at origin, initial side along positive x-axis.  • Positive angles: counter-clockwise. Negative angles: clockwise.Quadrants  • Q I: 0°–90°; Q II: 90°–180°; Q III: 180°–270°; Q IV: 270°–360°.

  Key Concepts: Arc Length & Sector Area
Arc Length & Sector AreaArc Length  • s = rθ, where r = radius and θ is in radians.Sector Area  • A = (1/2)r²θ, where θ is in radians.Linear & Angular Speed  • Angular speed: ω = θ/t (rad/s).  • Linear speed: v = rω = s/t.

--- Lesson 6.3: Unit Circle & Special Angles (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Unit Circle & Special Angles
Unit Circle & Special Angles
The unit circle has radius 1 centered at the origin. A point on it at angle θ has coordinates (cos θ, sin θ).
Special Angles  • 0° (0): (1, 0)  • 30° (π/6): (√3/2, 1/2)  • 45° (π/4): (√2/2, √2/2)  • 60° (π/3): (1/2, √3/2)  • 90° (π/2): (0, 1)Symmetry  • Use reference angles and quadrant signs to extend to all angles.  • ASTC: All, Sine, Tangent, Cosine positive in Q I, II, III, IV.

--- Lesson 6.4: Sine, Cosine, Tangent Definitions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Sine, Cosine, Tangent Definitions
Sine, Cosine, Tangent DefinitionsRight Triangle Definitions  • sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse  • cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse  • tan θ = opposite / adjacent = sin θ / cos θReciprocal Functions  • csc θ = 1/sin θ = hyp/opp  • sec θ = 1/cos θ = hyp/adj  • cot θ = 1/tan θ = adj/opp = cos θ / sin θPythagorean Identity  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

--- Lesson 6.5: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (amplitude, period, phase shift) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (amplitude, period, phase shift)
Graphs of Trigonometric Functionsy = A sin(Bx − C) + D  • Amplitude = |A|: vertical stretch.  • Period = 2π/|B|: horizontal length of one cycle.  • Phase shift = C/B: horizontal shift.  • Vertical shift = D: midline moves to y = D.Key Graphs  • sin x: starts at 0, period 2π.  • cos x: starts at 1, period 2π.  • tan x: period π, vertical asymptotes at π/2 + nπ.

--- Lesson 6.6: Inverse Trigonometric Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Inverse Trigonometric Functions
Inverse Trigonometric Functions
Inverse trig functions find the angle given a ratio.
Definitions & Ranges  • sin⁻¹(x) or arcsin(x): domain [−1, 1], range [−π/2, π/2].  • cos⁻¹(x) or arccos(x): domain [−1, 1], range [0, π].  • tan⁻¹(x) or arctan(x): domain (−∞, ∞), range (−π/2, π/2).Key Relationships  • sin(sin⁻¹(x)) = x for x ∈ [−1, 1].  • sin⁻¹(sin θ) = θ only if θ ∈ [−π/2, π/2].

--- Lesson 6.7: Applications in Physics & Engineering (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Physics & Engineering
Applications in Physics & EngineeringSimple Harmonic Motion  • x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ) models oscillation (springs, pendulums).  • A = amplitude, ω = angular frequency, φ = phase, period T = 2π/ω.Waves  • y = A sin(kx − ωt): traveling wave. k = 2π/λ (wave number), λ = wavelength.AC Circuits  • V(t) = V₀ sin(2πft + φ). f = frequency (Hz), peak voltage V₀.Projectile Motion  • Horizontal: x = v₀ cos θ · t. Vertical: y = v₀ sin θ · t − (1/2)gt².

--- Lesson 6.8: Coterminal & Reference Angles (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Coterminal & Reference Angles
Coterminal & Reference AnglesCoterminal Angles  • Two angles are coterminal if they share the same terminal side.  • Add or subtract 360° (or 2π) to find coterminal angles.Reference Angles  • The reference angle is the positive acute angle between the terminal side and the x-axis.  • Q I: ref = θ; Q II: ref = 180° − θ; Q III: ref = θ − 180°; Q IV: ref = 360° − θ.
