--- Lesson 7.1: Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming
Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming
The natural greenhouse effect keeps Earth ~33°C warmer than it would be without an atmosphere (−18°C → +15°C). Greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, H₂O, CFCs) absorb and re-emit infrared radiation.
Enhanced Greenhouse Effect
Human activities (fossil fuels, deforestation, agriculture) have increased CO₂ from ~280 ppm (pre-industrial) to ~424 ppm (2024). Global temperature has risen ~1.1°C above pre-industrial levels.

--- Lesson 7.2: Evidence of Climate Change (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Evidence of Climate Change
Evidence of Climate ChangeMultiple Lines of Evidence  • Temperature records: Instrumental records since 1850; 10 warmest years all since 2010.  • Ice cores: Trapped air bubbles show CO₂/temperature correlation over 800,000 years.  • Sea level rise: ~20 cm since 1900; accelerating (~3.6 mm/year now).  • Arctic ice decline: Summer Arctic sea ice volume down ~75% since 1979.  • Ocean warming & acidification: Oceans absorbed ~90% of excess heat and ~30% of CO₂.

--- Lesson 7.3: Impacts on Ecosystems & Humans (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Impacts on Ecosystems & Humans
Climate Change ImpactsEcosystems  • Range shifts: species moving poleward and to higher elevations.  • Coral bleaching: 14% of coral lost 2009–2018.  • Phenology disruption: mismatches between species (pollinators arriving at wrong time).Humans  • Extreme weather: more intense heat waves, droughts, hurricanes, and floods.  • Food security: crop yield declines; fisheries shifting.  • Health: heat-related illness, vector-borne diseases expanding range, air quality worsening.  • Climate refugees: displacement from sea level rise, drought, and extreme weather.

--- Lesson 7.4: Mitigation & Adaptation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Mitigation & Adaptation
Mitigation & AdaptationMitigation (reducing causes)  • Transition to renewable energy.  • Energy efficiency improvements.  • Carbon pricing (tax or cap-and-trade).  • Reforestation and soil carbon sequestration.Adaptation (adjusting to impacts)  • Sea walls and flood barriers.  • Drought-resistant crops.  • Urban cooling (green infrastructure, reflective surfaces).  • Early warning systems for extreme weather.

--- Lesson 7.5: International Agreements (Kyoto, Paris) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: International Agreements (Kyoto, Paris)
International Climate AgreementsKyoto Protocol (1997)
First binding agreement to reduce GHG emissions. Required developed nations to reduce emissions ~5% below 1990 levels. US never ratified. Mixed results.
Paris Agreement (2015)
Nearly every nation (196 parties). Goal: limit warming to well below 2°C, preferably 1.5°C. Countries set own targets (NDCs — Nationally Determined Contributions). Not legally binding. Updated every 5 years.

--- Lesson 7.7: AP Prep: Climate Models (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: AP Prep: Climate Models
AP Prep: Climate ModelsKey Concepts  • Climate models (GCMs): Computer simulations of Earth's climate system using physics equations. Multiple models run for different emission scenarios.  • Emission scenarios (RCPs/SSPs): RCP 2.6 (aggressive mitigation) to RCP 8.5 (business as usual). SSPs update these.  • Model outputs: Temperature projections range from +1.5°C to +4.5°C+ by 2100 depending on emissions.  • Carbon budget: Total CO₂ that can still be emitted to stay below a temperature target (~400 Gt CO₂ remaining for 1.5°C).
