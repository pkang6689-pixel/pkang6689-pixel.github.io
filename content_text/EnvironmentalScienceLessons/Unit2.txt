--- Lesson 2.1: Energy Flow in Ecosystems (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Energy Flow in Ecosystems
Energy Flow in Ecosystems
Energy flows in one direction through ecosystems: Sun → producers → consumers → heat. It is not recycled.
Key Measures  • GPP (Gross Primary Productivity): Total energy fixed by photosynthesis.  • NPP (Net Primary Productivity): GPP minus plant respiration; energy available to consumers.  • 1st Law of Thermodynamics: Energy is conserved — transferred, not created or destroyed.  • 2nd Law: Energy conversions increase entropy; energy is lost as heat at each transfer.

--- Lesson 2.2: Food Chains & Food Webs (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Food Chains & Food Webs
Food Chains & Food Webs
Food chain: Linear sequence of who eats whom (grass → rabbit → fox → eagle). Food web: Complex, interconnected food chains showing multiple feeding relationships in an ecosystem.
Key Concepts  • Grazing food chain: Starts with producers (most terrestrial/aquatic systems).  • Detrital food chain: Starts with dead organic matter (decomposition-based).  • Keystone species: Removal causes disproportionate ecosystem change.  • Trophic cascades: Changes at one level ripple through the web.

--- Lesson 2.3: Population Dynamics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Population Dynamics
Population DynamicsGrowth Models  • Exponential growth: J-curve; unlimited resources; dN/dt = rN (r = birth rate − death rate).  • Logistic growth: S-curve; limited resources; dN/dt = rN(1 − N/K) where K = carrying capacity.Strategies  • r-selected: Many offspring, little parental care, rapid growth (bacteria, insects).  • K-selected: Few offspring, extensive parental care, slow growth (elephants, whales).Regulation
Density-dependent (competition, predation, disease) and density-independent (weather, fire, flood) factors.

  Q1. Exponential growth produces a:
    [ ] S-curve
    [*] J-curve (population increases without limit when resources are abundant)
    [ ] Flat line
    [ ] Declining curve

  Q2. The logistic growth equation is:
    [ ] dN/dt = rN

  Q3. Carrying capacity (K) is defined as:

--- Lesson 2.4: Community Interactions (Competition, Symbiosis) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Community Interactions (Competition, Symbiosis)
Community InteractionsTypes  • Competition: Interspecific (between species) and intraspecific (within species). Competitive exclusion principle — two species can't occupy the same niche indefinitely.  • Predation: One organism kills and eats another; drives coevolution (camouflage, warning coloration).  • Symbiosis: Mutualism (+/+), commensalism (+/0), parasitism (+/−).  • Niche partitioning: Species reduce competition by using different parts of the same resource.

--- Lesson 2.5: Succession (Primary & Secondary) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Succession (Primary & Secondary)
Ecological SuccessionPrimary Succession
Occurs on bare substrate with no soil (lava flows, retreating glaciers). Pioneer species (lichens, mosses) colonize first → soil develops → grasses → shrubs → trees → climax community. Takes centuries to millennia.
Secondary Succession
Occurs after disturbance that leaves soil intact (fire, logging, abandonment). Faster than primary — soil, seeds, and organisms remain. Grasses/weeds → shrubs → trees → climax community.

--- Lesson 2.7: Case Studies in Ecology (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Case Studies in Ecology
Case Studies in EcologyYellowstone Wolf Reintroduction (1995)
Wolves reintroduced → elk behavior changed → riparian vegetation recovered → streambank stability → beaver return → trophic cascade demonstrating top-down regulation.
Coral Reef Decline
Warming → coral bleaching (loss of zooxanthellae) → biodiversity collapse. 14% of world's coral lost 2009–2018.
Amazon Deforestation
~17% of Amazon cleared for cattle and soy; approaching tipping point where forest could convert to savanna, releasing massive CO₂.

--- Lesson 2.8: AP Prep: Population Models (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: AP Prep: Population Models
AP Prep: Population ModelsCalculations  • Growth rate (r): r = (births − deaths) / N or r = birth rate − death rate.  • Doubling time: t = 70/r (Rule of 70); r in percent.  • Exponential: dN/dt = rN.  • Logistic: dN/dt = rN(1 − N/K). Maximum growth at N = K/2.  • Population change: ΔN = (births + immigration) − (deaths + emigration).

--- Lesson 2.8: AP Prep: Population Models (Quiz) ---

  Q1. If a population has r = 2% per year, doubling time is:
    [ ] 35 years
    [*] 35 years (70/2 = 35)
    [ ] 70 years
    [ ] 100 years
    Explanation: Rule of 70.

  Q2. If births = 500, deaths = 300, immigration = 50, emigration = 50, the population change is:
    [ ] 100
    [*] 200 (500 + 50 − 300 − 50 = 200)
    [ ] 500
    [ ] 0
    Explanation: ΔN = (B+I) − (D+E).

  Q3. For a population of N = 1,000 with r = 0.03, exponential growth (dN/dt) is:
    [ ] 3
    [*] 30 (0.03 × 1000 = 30 individuals/time period)
    [ ] 1,000
    [ ] 0.03
    Explanation: dN/dt = rN.

  Q4. For logistic growth with r = 0.04, N = 400, K = 800, dN/dt is:
    [ ] 16
    [*] 8 (0.04 × 400 × (1-400/800) = 0.04 × 400 × 0.5 = 8)
    [ ] 32
    [ ] 0
    Explanation: dN/dt = rN(1-N/K).

  Q5. At what population size is logistic growth rate maximized?
    [ ] N = 0
    [ ] N = K
    [*] N = K/2
    [ ] Any N
    Explanation: Inflection point.

  Q6. If K = 1,000 and N = 1,000, the logistic growth rate is:
    [ ] Maximum
    [*] 0 (1 − 1000/1000 = 0; population is at carrying capacity)
    [ ] r
    [ ] K
    Explanation: At K, growth stops.

  Q7. The term (1 − N/K) in the logistic equation represents:
    [ ] Growth rate
    [*] The fraction of carrying capacity still available (environmental resistance)
    [ ] Population size
    [ ] Carrying capacity
    Explanation: Unused capacity.

  Q8. If a country has r = 3.5%, its doubling time is:
    [ ] 35 years
    [*] 20 years (70/3.5 = 20)
    [ ] 7 years
    [ ] 70 years
    Explanation: Rule of 70.

  Q9. Per capita growth rate (r) can be calculated as:

    [*] r = (births − deaths)/N or birth rate minus death rate

    [ ] dN/dt only
    Explanation: Rate per individual.

  Q10. A population in decline has r that is:
    [ ] Positive
    [ ] Zero
    [*] Negative (deaths exceed births)
    [ ] Undefined
    Explanation: Shrinking population.

  Q11. Crude birth rate is expressed as:
    [ ] Births per year
    [*] Births per 1,000 individuals per year
    [ ] Births per family
    [ ] Births total
    Explanation: Standard demographic measure.

  Q12. If crude birth rate = 25/1000 and crude death rate = 10/1000, r =:

    [ ] 10/1000

  Q13. Total fertility rate (TFR) of 2.1 is called:

  Q14. Age structure diagrams with wide bases indicate:

  Q15. In an AP free-response question about population growth, you should:

    [ ] 650

    [*] More (competition, disease, and stress intensify at high density)

    [ ] Not
