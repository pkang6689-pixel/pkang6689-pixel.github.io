  Q1. A triangle with no congruent sides is:
    [ ] Equilateral
    [ ] Isosceles
    [*] Scalene
    [ ] Right
    Explanation: Classifying by Sides Scalene Triangle : No sides are congruent. Isosceles Triangle — At least two sides are congruent. The other options — 'Isosceles', 'Equilateral', and 'Right' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q2. A triangle with all sides congruent is:
    [ ] Scalene
    [ ] Isosceles
    [ ] Obtuse
    [*] Equilateral
    Explanation: Equilateral Triangle — All three sides are congruent. Key Facts Every Equilateral Triangle is also equiangular (and vice versa). The other options — 'Scalene', 'Isosceles', and 'Obtuse' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q3. A triangle with one 90° angle is:
    [ ] Equiangular
    [ ] Obtuse
    [*] Right
    [ ] Acute
    Explanation: Right Triangle — One Angle is exactly 90°. Obtuse Triangle — One Angle is greater than 90°. The other options — 'Acute', 'Obtuse', and 'Equiangular' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q4. An equiangular triangle has angle measures of:
    [*] 60°, 60°, 60°
    [ ] 90°, 90°, 0°
    [ ] 120°, 30°, 30°
    [ ] 90°, 45°, 45°
    Explanation: Right Triangle — One Angle is exactly 90°. Obtuse Triangle — One Angle is greater than 90°. The other options — '90°, 45°, 45°', '120°, 30°, 30°', and '90°, 90°, 0°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q5. Every equilateral triangle is also:
    [ ] Scalene
    [ ] Right
    [ ] Obtuse
    [*] Equiangular
    Explanation: Key Facts Every Equilateral Triangle is also equiangular (and vice versa). Equiangular Triangle — All angles are equal (each is 60°). The other options — 'Right', 'Scalene', and 'Obtuse' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q6. An isosceles triangle has:
    [ ] One right angle always
    [ ] No congruent sides
    [ ] All sides different
    [*] At least two congruent sides
    Explanation: Isosceles Triangle — At least two sides are congruent. An Isosceles Triangle has a vertex Angle (between the congruent sides) and two base angles (which are congruent). The other options — 'No congruent sides', 'All sides different', and 'One right angle always' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q7. A triangle with one angle of 120° is:
    [ ] Acute
    [*] Obtuse
    [ ] Right
    [ ] Equiangular
    Explanation: Obtuse Triangle — One Angle is greater than 90°. Right Triangle — One Angle is exactly 90°. The other options — 'Acute', 'Right', and 'Equiangular' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

--- Lesson 4.2: Angles of Triangles (Quiz) ---

  Q1. The sum of interior angles of a triangle is:
    [ ] 270°
    [ ] 90°
    [ ] 360°
    [*] 180°
    Explanation: Sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon = (n-2) × 180°. Each interior angle of a regular polygon = (n-2) × 180° / n. The answer is 180°. The other options — '360°', '90°', and '270°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q2. An exterior angle of a triangle equals:
    [ ] The adjacent interior angle
    [ ] 180° minus all three angles
    [*] The sum of the two remote interior angles
    [ ] Half the largest interior angle
    Explanation: The sum of exterior angles of any convex polygon is always 360°. Each exterior angle of a regular n-gon = 360°/n. An exterior angle of a triangle = sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles. The answer is The sum of the two remote interior angles. The other options — 'The adjacent interior angle', '180° minus all three angles', and 'Half the largest interior angle' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q3. In a right triangle, the two acute angles sum to:
    [*] 90°
    [ ] 45°
    [ ] 180°
    [ ] 60°
    Explanation: Corollaries The acute angles of a right Triangle are complementary (sum to 90°). Triangle Angle Sum Theorem The sum of the measures of the interior angles of a Triangle is 180°. The other options — '180°', '45°', and '60°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q4. If two angles of a triangle are 50° and 60°, the third is:
    [*] 70°
    [ ] 90°
    [ ] 80°
    [ ] 50°
    Explanation: Triangle Angle Sum Theorem The sum of the measures of the interior angles of a Triangle is 180°. m∠A + m∠B + m∠C = 180° Exterior Angle Theorem The measure of an exterior Angle of a Triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles (remote interior angles).

  Q5. A triangle can have at most how many obtuse angles?
    [ ] Two
    [ ] Zero
    [*] One
    [ ] Three
    Explanation: A Triangle can have at most one right Angle or obtuse Angle. Triangle Angle Sum Theorem The sum of the measures of the interior angles of a Triangle is 180°. m∠A + m∠B + m∠C = 180° Exterior Angle Theorem The measure of an exterior Angle of a Triangle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles (remote interior angles).

  Q6. The exterior angle of a triangle is always:
    [ ] Less than any interior angle
    [ ] Equal to 90°
    [*] Greater than either remote interior angle
    [ ] Equal to the adjacent angle
    Explanation: The sum of exterior angles of any convex polygon is always 360°. Each exterior angle of a regular n-gon = 360°/n. An exterior angle of a triangle = sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles. The answer is Greater than either remote interior angle. The other options — 'Less than any interior angle', 'Equal to 90°', and 'Equal to the adjacent angle' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q7. If an exterior angle is 130°, the sum of the remote interior angles is:
    [ ] 180°
    [ ] 260°
    [*] 130°
    [ ] 50°
    Explanation: Sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon = (n-2) × 180°. Each interior angle of a regular polygon = (n-2) × 180° / n. The answer is 130°. The other options — '50°', '180°', and '260°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
