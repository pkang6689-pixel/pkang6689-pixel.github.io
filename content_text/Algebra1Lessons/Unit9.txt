--- Lesson 9.1: Simplifying Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Simplifying Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the excluded value for 5/(x − 7)?
Set the denominator equal to zero: x − 7 = 0 gives x = 7. A rational expression is undefined whenever its denominator equals zero.
What is the excluded value for 1/(x²)?
Set the denominator equal to zero: x² = 0 gives x = 0. This is the only value that makes the expression undefined.
What is a rational expression?
A rational expression is a ratio (fraction) of two polynomials, such as (x + 1)/(x − 2). The denominator cannot be zero.
What is an excluded value?
An excluded value is any value of the variable that makes the denominator equal to zero, rendering the expression undefined. These values must be stated as restrictions on the domain.
What is a common factor?
A common factor is an expression that divides evenly into two or more expressions. When simplifying rational expressions, you cancel common factors from the numerator and denominator.
What is the domain of a rational expression?
The domain of a rational expression is the set of all real numbers except the excluded values, which are the values that make the denominator equal to zero.
What is the difference of squares pattern?
The difference of squares is a factoring pattern where a² − b² = (a + b)(a − b). This pattern is essential for simplifying many rational expressions that contain squared terms.
What is the greatest common factor (GCF)?
The greatest common factor (GCF) is the largest expression that divides evenly into each of the given expressions. Finding the GCF is the first step when simplifying rational expressions.
What is a restriction on a variable?
A restriction on a variable states which values the variable cannot take, typically because those values would make a denominator zero. Restrictions must always be stated when working with rational expressions.
A car travels (x² − 16) miles in (x + 4) hours. What is the simplified expression for the car's speed in miles per hour?
Speed = distance/time = (x² − 16)/(x + 4). Factor the numerator: x² − 16 = (x + 4)(x − 4). Cancel (x + 4) to get x − 4 miles per hour.
A rectangular garden has an area of (x² + 7x + 12) square feet and a width of (x + 3) feet. What is the simplified expression for the length?
Length = area/width = (x² + 7x + 12)/(x + 3). Factor the numerator: (x + 3)(x + 4). Cancel (x + 3) to get x + 4 feet.
A water tank holds (4x³) gallons and drains at a rate of (8x) gallons per minute. What is the simplified expression for the number of minutes to drain the tank?
Time = volume/rate = (4x³)/(8x). Divide: 4/8 = 1/2 and x³/x = x². So the drain time is x²/2 minutes.
A runner covers a distance of (x² − x − 12) meters in (x − 4) seconds. What is her simplified speed in meters per second?
Speed = distance/time = (x² − x − 12)/(x − 4). Factor: (x − 4)(x + 3)/(x − 4). Cancel (x − 4) to get x + 3 m/s.
A rectangular pool has an area of (x² + 6x + 8) square meters and a length of (x + 4) meters. If x = 6, what is the actual width of the pool?
Width = area/length = (x² + 6x + 8)/(x + 4). Factor: (x + 2)(x + 4)/(x + 4) = x + 2. Substitute x = 6: width = 6 + 2 = 8 meters.
Real-World Applications
Example: A company's profit is modeled by (10x²)/(15x). A manager simplifies this. What should the simplified expression be?
Divide numerator and denominator by the GCF of 5x: 10x²/(15x) = 2x/3. Both the coefficient and the variable part must be simplified.

--- Lesson 9.2: Multiplying & Dividing Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Multiplying & Dividing Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the reciprocal of a fraction?
The reciprocal of a fraction a/b is b/a. When dividing by a fraction, you multiply by its reciprocal instead.
What is cross-cancellation?
Cross-cancellation is a shortcut where you cancel common factors between the numerator of one fraction and the denominator of another before multiplying. This simplifies the work and avoids large numbers.
What is a rational number?
A rational number is any number that can be expressed as a ratio p/q where p and q are integers and q is not zero. This includes fractions, integers, terminating decimals, and repeating decimals.
What is a product in mathematics?
A product is the result obtained by multiplying two or more numbers or expressions together. When multiplying rational expressions, the product is found by multiplying numerators together and denominators together.
What is a quotient?
A quotient is the result obtained by dividing one number or expression by another. When dividing rational expressions, the quotient is found by multiplying by the reciprocal of the divisor.
What is a common factor in the context of multiplying rational expressions?
When multiplying rational expressions, a common factor is a factor that appears in both a numerator and a denominator. These can be canceled before multiplying to simplify the computation.
What is the divisor in a division problem?
The divisor is the number or expression you are dividing by. In (a/b) ÷ (c/d), the divisor is c/d. When dividing rational expressions, you take the reciprocal of the divisor and multiply.
A painter can cover (x² − 9)/(2x) square feet per hour and works for (4x)/(x − 3) hours. What is the total area covered, simplified?
Area = rate × time = ((x²−9)/(2x)) × ((4x)/(x−3)). Factor: ((x+3)(x−3))/(2x) × (4x)/(x−3). Cancel (x−3) and x, simplify 4/2 = 2, giving 2(x+3).
An investor's return is modeled by ((x² + x − 6)/(x − 1)) ÷ ((x + 3)/(x − 1)). What is the simplified return?
Factor: x²+x−6 = (x+3)(x−2). Multiply by reciprocal: ((x+3)(x−2)/(x−1)) × ((x−1)/(x+3)). Cancel (x+3) and (x−1) to get x−2.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does it mean to 'multiply by the reciprocal'?
Multiplying by the reciprocal is the standard technique for dividing fractions: instead of dividing by a/b, you multiply by b/a. This converts a division problem into a multiplication problem.
Example: What is a common factor in the context of multiplying rational expressions?
When multiplying rational expressions, a common factor is a factor that appears in both a numerator and a denominator. These can be canceled before multiplying to simplify the computation.
Example: What does 'simplify the result' mean after multiplying rational expressions?
After multiplying rational expressions, simplifying the result means factoring the numerator and denominator and canceling any remaining common factors so the answer is in lowest terms.
Example: A printer produces (8x²/3) pages per cartridge and uses (2x/9) cartridges per job. How many pages per job does it print?
Pages per job = (8x²/3) × (2x/9) = (8x² · 2x)/(3 · 9) = 16x³/27. Multiply numerators and denominators separately.

    [ ] 3x
    [ ] x/x²
    [ ] 9x/3x²
    [*] 3/x
    Explanation: Multiply numerators and denominators: (x · 9)/(3 · x²) = 9x/(3x²). Simplify by dividing: 9/3 = 3 and x/x² = 1/x, giving 3/x.

Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the least common denominator (LCD)?
The least common denominator (LCD) is the smallest expression that every denominator in the problem divides into evenly. It is found by taking the highest power of each factor that appears in any denominator.
What is a common denominator?
A common denominator is any expression that each original denominator divides into evenly. The LCD is the smallest such expression, but any common multiple of the denominators will work.
What is a complex fraction?
A complex fraction is a fraction where the numerator, the denominator, or both contain fractions themselves. These can be simplified by multiplying by the LCD of the inner fractions.
What is a numerator?
The numerator is the top part of a fraction. When adding or subtracting rational expressions with the same denominator, you combine the numerators while keeping the denominator the same.
What is a denominator?
The denominator is the bottom expression in a fraction. When adding or subtracting rational expressions, the denominators must be the same before you can combine the numerators.

--- Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Rational Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is an extraneous solution?
An extraneous solution is a value that emerges from the algebraic solving process but does not satisfy the original equation. In rational equations, this typically happens when the value makes a denominator zero.
What is a rational equation?
A rational equation is an equation that contains at least one rational expression, meaning at least one fraction with a variable in the denominator. Solving these often requires multiplying by the LCD.
What is a proportion?
A proportion is an equation stating that two ratios (fractions) are equal, such as a/b = c/d. Proportions can be solved by cross multiplying or by multiplying both sides by the LCD.
What is a solution set?
The solution set is the collection of all values that satisfy the equation. For rational equations, values that make any denominator zero are excluded, even if they arise algebraically.
What is an inverse operation?
An inverse operation undoes another operation: addition and subtraction are inverses, as are multiplication and division. In solving rational equations, you use inverse operations to isolate the variable.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'cross multiply' mean?
Cross multiplication is a shortcut for solving proportions: when a/b = c/d, multiply the numerator of each side by the denominator of the other to get ad = bc.

    [ ] An equation with only whole numbers
    [ ] An equation that has exactly two solutions
    [*] An equation that contains at least one rational expression (fraction with a variable in the denominator)
    Explanation: A rational equation is an equation that contains at least one rational expression, meaning at least one fraction with a variable in the denominator. Solving these often requires multiplying by the LCD.

  Q13. What does LCD stand for in solving rational equations?
    [*] Least Common Denominator
    [ ] Lowest Common Difference
    [ ] Linear Common Divisor
    [ ] Largest Common Divisor
    Explanation: LCD stands for Least Common Denominator, which is the smallest expression that all denominators in an equation divide into evenly. Multiplying every term by the LCD eliminates all fractions.

  Q14. What does 'check your solution' mean in the context of rational equations?
    [ ] Multiply the solution by the LCD
    [ ] Graph the equation
    [*] Verify that the solution does not make any denominator equal to zero
    [ ] Round the answer to the nearest integer
    Explanation: Checking your solution means substituting it back into the original equation to verify it is valid. You must confirm that the solution does not make any denominator zero, which would make it extraneous.

  Q15. What does 'cross multiply' mean?
    [ ] Multiply the numerators of both fractions
    [ ] Add the cross products
    [*] When a/b = c/d, multiply a·d and b·c and set them equal: ad = bc
    [ ] Divide both fractions by each other
    Explanation: Cross multiplication is a shortcut for solving proportions: when a/b = c/d, multiply the numerator of each side by the denominator of the other to get ad = bc.

  Q16. What is a proportion?
    [ ] The reciprocal of a fraction
    [ ] An inequality involving fractions
    [ ] A fraction greater than 1
    [*] An equation stating that two ratios are equal
    Explanation: A proportion is an equation stating that two ratios (fractions) are equal, such as a/b = c/d. Proportions can be solved by cross multiplying or by multiplying both sides by the LCD.

  Q17. What does 'eliminate the denominator' mean?
    [ ] Move the denominator to the other side of the equation
    [*] Multiply both sides of the equation by a value that removes all fractions
    [ ] Delete the bottom part of each fraction
    [ ] Set the denominator equal to zero
    Explanation: Eliminating the denominator means multiplying every term on both sides of the equation by the LCD so that all fractions are cleared. This transforms the rational equation into a simpler polynomial equation.

  Q18. What is a solution set?
    [*] The collection of all values that make an equation true
    [ ] The list of all possible denominators
    [ ] The set of all real numbers
    [ ] The set of all excluded values
    Explanation: The solution set is the collection of all values that satisfy the equation. For rational equations, values that make any denominator zero are excluded, even if they arise algebraically.

  Q19. What does 'no solution' mean for a rational equation?
    [*] Every algebraic solution turns out to be extraneous, so no valid value satisfies the equation
    [ ] The solution is zero
    [ ] The equation has not been solved yet
    [ ] The equation has infinitely many solutions
    Explanation: A rational equation has no solution when every candidate value obtained through solving is extraneous. This means no real number satisfies the original equation, often written as the empty set.

  Q20. What is an inverse operation?
    [ ] An operation that makes the equation more complex
    [*] An operation that undoes another operation, such as multiplication undoing division
    [ ] An operation that only works with negative numbers
    [ ] An operation that is the same as the original
    Explanation: An inverse operation undoes another operation: addition and subtraction are inverses, as are multiplication and division. In solving rational equations, you use inverse operations to isolate the variable.

  Q21. A plumber can complete a job in x hours, and an apprentice can do it in (x + 3) hours. Working together, they finish in 2 hours. Which equation models this situation?
    [ ] x + (x+3) = 2
    [ ] 1/x − 1/(x+3) = 2
    [ ] 2/x + 2/(x+3) = 1/2
    [*] 1/x + 1/(x+3) = 1/2
    Explanation: Each worker's rate is 1/(time). Together: 1/x + 1/(x+3) = 1/2, because their combined rate equals 1/2 of the job per hour to finish in 2 hours.

  Q22. A kayaker paddles 12 miles upstream (against a current of 2 mph) in the same time it takes to paddle 18 miles downstream. If the kayaker's still-water speed is r mph, which equation models the situation?
    [ ] 12/r = 18/(r+2)
    [*] 12/(r−2) = 18/(r+2)
    [ ] 12/(r+2) = 18/(r−2)
    [ ] 12r = 18(r−2)
    Explanation: Upstream speed = r − 2 and downstream speed = r + 2. Since the times are equal, distance/speed gives 12/(r−2) = 18/(r+2).

  Q23. A recipe for 1 batch of cookies uses 2/3 cup of sugar. If you have 4 cups of sugar, solve 2/3 · x = 4 to find how many batches you can make.
    [ ] x = 8/3
    [ ] x = 4
    [*] x = 6
    [ ] x = 12
    Explanation: Solve (2/3)x = 4 by multiplying both sides by 3/2: x = 4 × 3/2 = 6. You can make 6 batches of cookies.

  Q24. Two friends plan to share the cost of a $60 gift equally. If x more friends join, the cost per person is 60/(x + 2). If the cost per person drops to $10, how many additional friends joined?
    [*] x = 4
    [ ] x = 8
    [ ] x = 6
    [ ] x = 3
    Explanation: Set up: 60/(x+2) = 10. Multiply both sides by (x+2): 60 = 10(x+2) = 10x + 20. So 40 = 10x and x = 4 additional friends.

  Q25. A car travels 180 miles at speed r and then 120 miles at speed (r − 10). The total trip takes 5 hours. Which equation models this?
    [*] 180/r + 120/(r−10) = 5
    [ ] 180r + 120(r−10) = 5
    [ ] (180 + 120)/(r + r−10) = 5
    [ ] 180/(r−10) + 120/r = 5
    Explanation: Time = distance/speed for each leg. The total time is 180/r + 120/(r−10) = 5 hours.

  Q26. A school collects $300 for a field trip. If each student pays 300/x dollars and 5 more students sign up, lowering the cost to 300/(x+5), which equation finds the original number of students if the cost drops by $2?
    [ ] 300/x + 300/(x+5) = 2
    [ ] 300x − 300(x+5) = 2
    [ ] 300/(x+5) − 300/x = 2
    [*] 300/x − 300/(x+5) = 2
    Explanation: The original cost per person minus the new cost per person equals $2: 300/x − 300/(x+5) = 2. The drop is positive because fewer dollars per person means the first fraction is larger.

  Q27. Pipe A fills a pool in 6 hours and Pipe B fills it in 4 hours. Solve 1/6 + 1/4 = 1/t to find how long both pipes take working together.
    [ ] t = 5 hours
    [*] t = 2.4 hours
    [ ] t = 3 hours
    [ ] t = 10 hours
    Explanation: Combined rate: 1/6 + 1/4 = 2/12 + 3/12 = 5/12. Since 5/12 = 1/t, then t = 12/5 = 2.4 hours working together.

  Q28. A club divides a $120 prize equally. If 5 more members join, each person gets $2 less. Solve 120/x − 120/(x+5) = 2 to find the original number of members x.
    [*] x = 15
    [ ] x = 25
    [ ] x = 20
    [ ] x = 10
    Explanation: Multiply by x(x+5): 120(x+5) − 120x = 2x(x+5). Simplify: 600 = 2x² + 10x, so x² + 5x − 300 = 0. Factor: (x+20)(x−15) = 0. Since x must be positive, x = 15.

  Q29. A boat travels 24 miles downstream and 16 miles upstream in the same total time of 4 hours. The current is 2 mph. If the boat's speed in still water is r, which equation applies?
    [*] 24/(r+2) + 16/(r−2) = 4
    [ ] (24 + 16)/r = 4
    [ ] 24/(r−2) + 16/(r+2) = 4
    [ ] 24r + 16r = 4
    Explanation: Downstream speed is r + 2 and upstream speed is r − 2. Time = distance/speed, so total time = 24/(r+2) + 16/(r−2) = 4.

  Q30. A student solves 3/(x−2) + 1 = 5/(x−2) and gets x = 2. Is this a valid solution?
    [ ] No, because the equation has no fractions
    [ ] Yes, because 3 + 1 = 4 is close to 5
    [*] No, because x = 2 makes the denominator (x−2) equal to zero, so it is extraneous
    [ ] Yes, because x = 2 satisfies the simplified equation
    Explanation: Substituting x = 2 makes the denominator (x−2) = 0, which is undefined. Therefore x = 2 is extraneous and must be rejected, leaving this equation with no valid solution.

--- Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: How do you solve a rational equation?
     A: Multiply every term by the LCD to clear all fractions

  2. Q: What is an extraneous solution?
     A: A result that makes a denominator zero — it must be rejected

  3. Q: Why must you check solutions?
     A: Squaring or multiplying by a variable can introduce extraneous solutions

  4. Q: Solve: x/2 + x/3 = 5
     A: LCD = 6: 3x + 2x = 30 → 5x = 30 → x = 6

  5. Q: Solve: 1/x = 1/4
     A: Cross multiply: 4 = x → x = 4

  6. Q: Solve: 2/(x−1) = 4
     A: 2 = 4(x−1) → 2 = 4x − 4 → 6 = 4x → x = 3/2

  7. Q: If a solution gives 0 in a denominator:
     A: It is extraneous and must be rejected

  8. Q: What is the first step?
     A: Find the LCD of all fractions in the equation

  9. Q: What is a cross-multiplication shortcut?
     A: For a/b = c/d, cross multiply: ad = bc

  10. Q: Solve: 3/(x + 1) + 1/(x + 1) = 2
     A: 4/(x + 1) = 2 → x + 1 = 2 → x = 1
