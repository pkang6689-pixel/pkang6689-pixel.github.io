--- Lesson 10.1: Simplifying Radicals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Simplifying Radicals
√(ab) = √a · √b. Factor out perfect squares from under the radical.

Example: √50 = √(25·2) = 5√2.

√(a/b) = √a / √b. Rationalize the denominator by multiplying by √b/√b.

Simplified form: no perfect square factors under the radical, no radicals in the denominator.

--- Lesson 10.2: Operations with Radicals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Operations with Radicals
Add/Subtract radicals: only combine like radicals (same radicand). Example: 3√5 + 2√5 = 5√5.

Multiply radicals: √a · √b = √(ab). Example: √3 · √12 = √36 = 6.

Divide radicals: √a / √b = √(a/b). Rationalize the denominator if needed.

To add unlike radicals, simplify first: √12 + √27 = 2√3 + 3√3 = 5√3.

--- Lesson 10.3: Rational Exponents (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Rational Exponents
A rational exponent a^(m/n) = ⁿ√(aᵐ) = (ⁿ√a)ᵐ.

a^(1/2) = √a, a^(1/3) = ∛a, a^(2/3) = (∛a)² = ∛(a²).

Laws of exponents apply to rational exponents: aᵐ · aⁿ = a^(m+n), (aᵐ)ⁿ = a^(mn), etc.

Rational exponents provide an alternative notation for radicals.

--- Lesson 10.4: Solving Radical Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Radical Equations
To solve a radical equation, isolate the radical, then raise both sides to the appropriate power.

For square roots: square both sides. For cube roots: cube both sides.

Always check for extraneous solutions — squaring can introduce false solutions.

Example: √(x + 3) = 5 → x + 3 = 25 → x = 22. Check: √25 = 5 ✓
