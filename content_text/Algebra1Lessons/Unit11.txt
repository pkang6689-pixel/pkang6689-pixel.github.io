--- Lesson 11.1: Arithmetic & Geometric Sequences Review (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arithmetic & Geometric Sequences Review
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common difference of 10, 7, 4, 1, ...?
The common difference d = 7 − 10 = −3. Each term decreases by 3, confirming this is an arithmetic sequence with d = −3.
What is the common ratio of 5, 15, 45, 135, ...?
The common ratio r = 15/5 = 3. Each term is multiplied by 3 to get the next term.
The first three terms of a geometric sequence are 4, 12, 36. What is a(4)?
The common ratio r = 12/4 = 3. So a(4) = 36 × 3 = 108.
What is an arithmetic sequence?
An arithmetic sequence is one in which the difference between consecutive terms is constant. This constant is called the common difference (d). The sequence can increase or decrease.
What is a geometric sequence?
A geometric sequence is one where each term is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a fixed constant called the common ratio (r). The ratio between consecutive terms is always the same.
What is the common difference?
The common difference (d) is the constant value added to each term to produce the next term in an arithmetic sequence. It is found by subtracting any term from the term that follows it.
What is the common ratio?
The common ratio (r) is the constant by which each term is multiplied to get the next term in a geometric sequence. It is found by dividing any term by the previous term.
What is the explicit formula for an arithmetic sequence?
The explicit formula a(n) = a(1) + (n−1)d lets you find any term directly. Here a(1) is the first term, n is the term number, and d is the common difference.
What is the explicit formula for a geometric sequence?
The explicit formula a(n) = a(1) · r^(n−1) lets you find any term of a geometric sequence. Here a(1) is the first term and r is the common ratio.
What is a term in a sequence?
A term is each individual number in a sequence. Terms are usually labeled by their position: a(1) is the first term, a(2) is the second term, and so on.
What is a sequence in mathematics?
A sequence is an ordered list of numbers following a specific pattern or rule. Each number in the list is called a term, and the order matters.
What is a finite sequence?
A finite sequence has a specific, countable number of terms and eventually ends. For example, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 is a finite sequence with 5 terms.
A car depreciates by losing $2,000 in value each year. If it starts at $30,000, what is its value after 8 years?
Arithmetic with a(1) = 30000 and d = −2000. a(9) = 30000 + (9−1)(−2000) = 30000 − 16000 = $14,000 after 8 years.

--- Lesson 11.2: Recursive & Explicit Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Recursive & Explicit Formulas
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a recursive formula?
A recursive formula defines each term based on the previous term(s) along with an initial condition (usually the first term). To find a specific term, you must compute all preceding terms first.
What is an explicit formula?
An explicit formula (also called a closed-form formula) calculates any term directly using its position number n. You do not need to know any previous terms to use it.
What is an initial condition in a recursive formula?
The initial condition specifies the first term (and sometimes additional early terms) of the sequence. Without it, the recursive rule alone cannot generate the sequence because there is no starting point.
What is the advantage of an explicit formula over a recursive formula?
The main advantage of an explicit formula is efficiency: you can compute any term directly without needing all the previous terms. This is especially useful for finding terms far along in the sequence.
What is the advantage of a recursive formula?
A recursive formula clearly shows the relationship between consecutive terms, making the pattern easy to understand. It directly states how each new term is built from the previous one.
What is the closed-form expression of a sequence?
A closed-form expression is another name for the explicit formula. It expresses a(n) purely in terms of n and constants, with no reference to other terms in the sequence.
What is a linear function's connection to arithmetic sequences?
The explicit formula a(n) = a(1) + (n−1)d simplifies to a linear function of the form a(n) = dn + c, where d is the slope. Arithmetic sequences are discrete linear functions.
What is an exponential function's connection to geometric sequences?
The explicit formula a(n) = a(1) · r^(n−1) is an exponential function where the variable n appears in the exponent. Geometric sequences are the discrete version of exponential growth or decay.
A concert hall adds chairs in rows: row 1 has 15 chairs, and each subsequent row has 2 more. What is the explicit formula and how many chairs are in row 20?
a(n) = 15 + (n−1)(2) = 15 + 2n − 2 = 2n + 13. For row 20: a(20) = 2(20) + 13 = 53 chairs.
A gym membership starts at $50/month. The cost decreases by $3 each month as a loyalty discount. What is the cost in month 10?
Arithmetic with a(1) = 50, d = −3. a(10) = 50 + (10−1)(−3) = 50 − 27 = $23.

--- Lesson 11.2: Recursive &amp; Explicit Formulas (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: What is a recursive formula?
     A: A formula that defines each term based on the previous term(s)

  2. Q: What is an explicit formula?
     A: A formula that calculates any term directly from its position number n

  3. Q: Recursive for arithmetic:
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) + d, with a(1) given

  4. Q: Recursive for geometric:
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) · r, with a(1) given

  5. Q: Advantage of explicit formulas:
     A: You can find any term without computing all previous terms

  6. Q: Write recursive formula for 2, 5, 8, 11, ...
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) + 3, a(1) = 2

  7. Q: Write recursive formula for 4, 12, 36, ...
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) · 3, a(1) = 4

  8. Q: Convert a(n) = a(n−1) + 5, a(1) = 3 to explicit:
     A: a(n) = 5n − 2

  9. Q: Advantage of recursive formulas:
     A: Easy to compute the next term from the previous one

  10. Q: Convert a(n) = a(n−1) · 2, a(1) = 5 to explicit:
     A: a(n) = 5 · 2^(n−1)

--- Lesson 11.3: Introduction to Series (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Introduction to Series
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a series?
A series is the sum of the terms of a sequence. While a sequence is a list, a series adds the terms together. For example, the sequence 1, 2, 3 becomes the series 1 + 2 + 3 = 6.
What is sigma notation (Σ)?
Sigma notation (Σ) is a compact way to write a series. It includes the expression being summed, the starting index below the Σ, and the ending index above it.
What is a partial sum?
A partial sum S(n) is the sum of the first n terms of a series. It represents the cumulative total up to the nth term.
What is an arithmetic series?
An arithmetic series is the sum of the terms of an arithmetic sequence. Its sum formula is S(n) = n/2 · (a₁ + aₙ) or equivalently S(n) = n/2 · (2a₁ + (n−1)d).
What is a geometric series?
A geometric series is the sum of the terms of a geometric sequence. Its sum formula is S(n) = a₁(1 − rⁿ)/(1 − r), where r ≠ 1.
What is the index of summation?
The index of summation is the variable that takes on successive integer values from the lower limit to the upper limit in sigma notation. It acts as a counter that generates each term of the series.
What is the difference between a sequence and a series?
A sequence is an ordered list of numbers (e.g., 1, 2, 3), while a series is the sum of those numbers (e.g., 1 + 2 + 3 = 6). The key difference is listing versus adding.
What is a finite series?
A finite series has a definite number of terms and produces a definite sum. For example, 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 is a finite series with 5 terms summing to 15.
What is an infinite series?
An infinite series continues indefinitely with no last term, indicated by '...' or an upper limit of infinity in sigma notation. Some infinite series converge to a finite sum while others diverge.
A company pays a signing bonus where the employee receives $100 the first week, $200 the second week, $300 the third week, and so on for 10 weeks. What is the total bonus?
Arithmetic series: a₁ = 100, d = 100, n = 10, a₁₀ = 1000. S(10) = 10/2 · (100 + 1000) = 5 · 1100 = $5,500.
A basketball player scores 2 points in the first quarter, 4 in the second, 8 in the third, and 16 in the fourth. What is the player's total score?
Geometric series: 2 + 4 + 8 + 16 = 30. Using formula: S(4) = 2(1−2⁴)/(1−2) = 2(−15)/(−1) = 30.
A staircase has 15 steps. Step 1 is 6 inches high, and each subsequent step is also 6 inches. What is the total height climbed?
Since each step is 6 inches with d = 0, S(15) = 15 × 6 = 90 inches. This is a constant arithmetic series.
A fundraiser collects donations over 8 days: $50 on day 1, $60 on day 2, $70 on day 3, and so on. What is the total collected?
Arithmetic: a₁ = 50, d = 10, n = 8, a₈ = 50 + 7(10) = 120. S(8) = 8/2 · (50 + 120) = 4 · 170 = $680.
Real-World Applications
Example: A basketball player scores 2 points in the first quarter, 4 in the second, 8 in the third, and 16 in the fourth. What is the player's total score?
Geometric series: 2 + 4 + 8 + 16 = 30. Using formula: S(4) = 2(1−2⁴)/(1−2) = 2(−15)/(−1) = 30.
