--- Lesson 2.1: One-Step &amp; Two-Step Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: One-Step &amp; Two-Step Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the first step in solving 5x - 2 = 13?
In a two-step equation, undo addition/subtraction first, then undo multiplication/division. Add 2 to both sides to get 5x = 15.
What is an equation?
An equation is a mathematical sentence stating that two expressions have the same value, joined by an equal sign (=).
What is an 'inverse operation'?
Inverse operations reverse each other: addition undoes subtraction, and multiplication undoes division. They are essential for isolating variables.
What is a 'one-step equation'?
A one-step equation can be solved with a single inverse operation, such as x + 5 = 12 (subtract 5) or 3x = 21 (divide by 3).
What is a 'two-step equation'?
A two-step equation like 2x + 3 = 11 requires two operations: first subtract 3 (undo addition), then divide by 2 (undo multiplication).
What is a 'coefficient'?
A coefficient is the numerical factor in front of a variable. In 5x, the coefficient is 5. In x (or 1x), the coefficient is 1.
What is a 'constant' in an equation?
A constant is a number without a variable attached. In 3x + 7 = 19, the constants are 7 and 19.
A thermometer reads a temperature in Celsius. To convert to Fahrenheit, use F = 1.8C + 32. If the temperature is 68 degrees F, what is C?
68 = 1.8C + 32. Subtract 32: 36 = 1.8C. Divide by 1.8: C = 20 degrees.
An architect designing a rectangular room knows the perimeter is 56 feet and the length is 18 feet. Using P = 2l + 2w, what is the width?
56 = 2(18) + 2w. Simplify: 56 = 36 + 2w. Subtract 36: 20 = 2w. Divide by 2: w = 10 feet.
Real-World Applications
Example: A phone plan charges $10 per month plus $0.05 per text message. This month's bill is $22.50. The equation is 0.05t + 10 = 22.50. How many texts were sent?
Subtract 10: 0.05t = 12.50. Divide by 0.05: t = 250 texts.

--- Lesson 2.2: Multi-Step Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Multi-Step Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the first step in solving 3(2x + 1) = 21?
The first step is to distribute: 3(2x + 1) = 6x + 3 = 21. Alternatively, you could divide both sides by 3 first, but distributing is the standard approach.
What is a 'multi-step equation'?
A multi-step equation requires several operations to solve, such as distributing, combining like terms, and using inverse operations.
What is a 'term' in an algebraic expression?
A term is a part of an expression separated by + or - signs. In 3x^2 + 5x - 7, there are three terms: 3x^2, 5x, and -7.
What is an 'expression' in algebra?
An expression is a combination of numbers, variables, and operations (like 3x + 5) without an equal sign. An equation has an equal sign; an expression does not.
A garden has a rectangular and a square section. The rectangular section is 3 times the width plus 2 feet long, and the square section has sides equal to the width. If the total perimeter of both is 54 feet, and the equation simplifies to 10w + 4 = 54, what is w?
Subtract 4: 10w = 50. Divide by 10: w = 5 feet.
An online store offers free shipping on orders over $50. A customer buys 3 identical shirts and a $14 belt, and the total before tax is $59. The equation is 3s + 14 = 59. What is the price of each shirt?
Subtract 14: 3s = 45. Divide by 3: s = $15 per shirt.
Real-World Applications
Example: A parking garage charges $5 for the first hour and $3 for each additional hour. If a driver paid $20, the equation is 3(h - 1) + 5 = 20. How many total hours did the driver park?
Distribute: 3h - 3 + 5 = 20. Combine: 3h + 2 = 20. Subtract 2: 3h = 18. Divide by 3: h = 6 hours.

--- Lesson 2.8: Systems of Equations — Substitution &amp; Elimination (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Systems of Equations — Substitution &amp; Elimination
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a system of equations?
A system of equations is a set of two or more equations involving the same variables. A solution must satisfy every equation in the system simultaneously.
What is the 'substitution method'?
Substitution involves isolating one variable in one equation and replacing it in the other equation, reducing the system to a single equation in one variable.
What is the 'elimination method'?
Elimination (also called the addition method) involves adding or subtracting the equations so that one variable cancels out, leaving a single-variable equation.
What is a 'solution of a system'?
A solution is the point (x, y) where both equations are true. Graphically, it is the intersection point of the two lines.
What is an 'ordered pair'?
An ordered pair (x, y) specifies a unique point where the first value is the x-coordinate and the second is the y-coordinate. Order matters.

--- Lesson 2.9: Systems of Inequalities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Systems of Inequalities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a system of inequalities?
A system of inequalities is a set of two or more inequalities in the same variables. The solution is the region of the coordinate plane where all inequalities are satisfied.
What is a 'half-plane'?
A linear inequality divides the coordinate plane into two half-planes. The solution set is one of these half-planes, possibly including the boundary line.
What is a 'boundary line'?
The boundary line is obtained by treating the inequality as an equation. It divides the plane into two regions; the inequality determines which side to shade.
What is a 'feasible region'?
The feasible region is the overlap of all solution regions. In linear programming, it represents the set of all possible solutions to a problem.
What is a 'test point'?
A test point (commonly (0,0) when it is not on the boundary) is substituted into the inequality to check which half-plane should be shaded.
What is 'shading' in the context of graphing inequalities?
Shading visually represents the solution set by filling in the half-plane that satisfies the inequality.
A farmer has 100 acres and $6000 for planting. Corn costs $40/acre and wheat costs $80/acre. The system is c + w <= 100 and 40c + 80w <= 6000. What is the maximum acres of wheat if all land is used?
If c + w = 100 and 40c + 80w = 6000: substitute c = 100 - w: 40(100 - w) + 80w = 6000, 4000 + 40w = 6000, 40w = 2000, w = 50 acres.
A company makes two products. Product X requires 3 hours of labor and Product Y requires 2 hours. There are 120 labor hours available, and they must make at least 10 of each product. The system includes 3x + 2y <= 120, x >= 10, y >= 10. What is the maximum total products?
To maximize total, minimize the higher-cost item. Set x = 10: 30 + 2y <= 120, 2y <= 90, y <= 45. Total = 10 + 45 = 55, using all 120 labor hours.
A student has at most 8 hours for homework and volunteering. Homework must be at least 3 hours and volunteering at least 1 hour. The system is h + v <= 8, h >= 3, v >= 1. What is the maximum volunteering time?
To maximize v, minimize h. Set h = 3 (minimum): 3 + v <= 8, v <= 5. Maximum volunteering is 5 hours.
A shipping company can send at most 40 packages per trip. Small packages weigh 5 lbs and large packages weigh 20 lbs. The truck holds at most 500 lbs. Using s + l <= 40 and 5s + 20l <= 500, what is the maximum number of large packages?
To maximize large packages, set s = 0: 0 + l <= 40 and 20l <= 500, l <= 25. So the maximum is 25 large packages (checking: 25 <= 40 and 500 <= 500).
