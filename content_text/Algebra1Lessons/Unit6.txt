--- Lesson 6.1: Key Features of Parabolas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Key Features of Parabolas
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the vertex of a parabola?
The vertex is the turning point of a parabola, representing either its maximum (if the parabola opens down) or its minimum (if it opens up).
What is the axis of symmetry of a parabola?
The axis of symmetry is the vertical line passing through the vertex, given by x = −b/(2a), which creates two symmetrical halves of the parabola.
What is the y-intercept of a quadratic function y = ax² + bx + c?
The y-intercept occurs when x = 0, so y = a(0)² + b(0) + c = c, making the y-intercept the point (0, c).
What is a 'vertical stretch' of a parabola?
A vertical stretch occurs when |a| > 1, making each y-value farther from the x-axis than in the parent function y = x², which produces a narrower parabola.
A park has an arched entrance shaped like a downward-opening parabola described by y = −0.5x² + 4x, where y is the height in feet and x is the horizontal distance in feet. What is the maximum height of the arch?
The maximum height occurs at the vertex: x = −4/(2·(−0.5)) = 4, and y = −0.5(16) + 4(4) = −8 + 16 = 8 feet.
A bridge has a parabolic support cable modeled by y = 0.01x² − 2x + 100, where y is the cable height in feet. What is the lowest point of the cable?
The vertex x-coordinate is x = −(−2)/(2·0.01) = 2/0.02 = 100, and y = 0.01(10000) − 2(100) + 100 = 100 − 200 + 100 = 0 feet.
A rocket's height is modeled by h(t) = −5t² + 40t + 10. What is the maximum height the rocket reaches?
The vertex occurs at t = −40/(2·(−5)) = 4, and h(4) = −5(16) + 40(4) + 10 = −80 + 160 + 10 = 90 meters.
An athlete throws a shot put, and its trajectory is modeled by y = −0.02x² + x + 6, where y is the height and x is the horizontal distance in feet. What is the y-intercept, and what does it represent?
The y-intercept occurs at x = 0, giving y = 6, which represents the height at which the athlete releases the shot put before it travels forward.
A gardener makes a rectangular flower bed using 24 meters of edging. The area as a function of width w is A = w(12 − w) = −w² + 12w. What is the maximum possible area?
The vertex is at w = −12/(2·(−1)) = 6, and A(6) = −36 + 72 = 36 square meters, which is the maximum area when the flower bed is a 6 × 6 square.

--- Lesson 6.2: Graphing in Standard Form (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphing in Standard Form
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If the axis of symmetry is x = 3 and one point on the parabola is (1, 5), what is its mirror point?
The point (1, 5) is 2 units left of x = 3, so its mirror point is 2 units right of x = 3 at (5, 5), keeping the same y-value due to symmetry.
What is 'standard form' of a quadratic equation?
Standard form is y = ax² + bx + c where a, b, and c are real numbers and a ≠ 0, making it easy to identify the y-intercept (c) and compute the axis of symmetry.
What is the 'table of values' method for graphing?
The table of values method involves selecting x-values (often centered around the axis of symmetry), computing each corresponding y-value, and plotting the ordered pairs to draw the curve.
What is the 'direction of opening' of a parabola?
The direction of opening refers to whether the parabola opens upward (when a > 0, creating a U-shape) or downward (when a < 0, creating an inverted U-shape).
What is the 'leading coefficient' of a quadratic?
The leading coefficient is the value of a in y = ax² + bx + c, which is the coefficient of the highest-degree term and determines both the direction and width of the parabola.
What is the first step when graphing a quadratic in standard form?
The first step is to find the axis of symmetry using x = −b/(2a), then substitute to find the vertex, then plot the y-intercept and its mirror point, and finally connect the points in a smooth curve.
A baseball is hit from a height of 3 feet and follows the path y = −0.01x² + 0.5x + 3, where x is horizontal distance and y is height in feet. What is the maximum height the ball reaches?
The vertex x-value is x = −0.5/(2·(−0.01)) = 25, and y = −0.01(625) + 0.5(25) + 3 = −6.25 + 12.5 + 3 = 9.25 feet.
A suspension bridge cable follows the equation y = 0.004x² − 1.6x + 200, where y is height in feet above the road. What is the minimum cable height?
The vertex is at x = −(−1.6)/(2·0.004) = 1.6/0.008 = 200, and y = 0.004(40000) − 1.6(200) + 200 = 160 − 320 + 200 = 40 feet.
A diver jumps off a 10-meter platform with height modeled by h(t) = −5t² + 3t + 10. What is the y-intercept and what does it represent?
The y-intercept occurs at t = 0, giving h(0) = 10, which represents the initial height of the diver standing on the 10-meter platform before jumping.
An engineer models the shape of an arch with y = −0.25x² + 3x, where y is the height in meters. What is the vertex, and what does it represent?
The vertex is at x = −3/(2·(−0.25)) = 6, and y = −0.25(36) + 3(6) = −9 + 18 = 9, so the highest point of the arch is 9 meters at a horizontal distance of 6 meters.
Real-World Applications
Example: A school fundraiser sells cupcakes. The profit function is P(x) = −x² + 50x − 400, where x is the number of cupcakes sold. What are the x-intercepts and what do they represent?
Setting P = 0: −x² + 50x − 400 = 0 → x² − 50x + 400 = 0 → (x − 10)(x − 40) = 0, so x = 10 and x = 40 are the break-even points where revenue equals costs.

--- Lesson 6.4: Transformations of Quadratic Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Transformations of Quadratic Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'transformation' in the context of quadratic functions?
A transformation is any operation applied to the parent function y = x² that changes its position, shape, or orientation on the coordinate plane.
What is the 'parent function' for quadratic transformations?
The parent function y = x² is the most basic quadratic function with vertex at the origin, and all other quadratic functions can be described as transformations of it.
What is a 'reflection' of a quadratic function?
A reflection flips the parabola over the x-axis by changing the sign of a (from positive to negative or vice versa), reversing the direction of opening.
What is a 'vertical stretch' of a parabola?
A vertical stretch occurs when |a| > 1, which multiplies every y-value by a factor greater than 1, pulling the parabola away from the x-axis and making it narrower.
What is a 'vertical compression' of a parabola?
A vertical compression occurs when 0 < |a| < 1, which reduces every y-value, pushing the parabola closer to the x-axis and making it wider.
A model train track has a dip modeled by y = 0.1(x − 10)² + 1. What is the lowest point of the dip, and how does its shape compare to y = x²?
The vertex is (10, 1), and since a = 0.1 (between 0 and 1), the parabola is wider than y = x² due to vertical compression.
A child throws a ball following the path y = −(x − 5)² + 25. If the child moves 3 feet to the right and throws identically, what is the new equation?
Moving 3 feet to the right adds 3 to the h-value: h changes from 5 to 8, giving y = −(x − 8)² + 25, a horizontal translation only.
A pool builder wants to make an existing pool deeper by modifying y = 0.25x² to have its lowest point 5 feet lower. What is the new equation?
To shift the vertex down 5 units, subtract 5 from the function: y = 0.25x² − 5, which moves the lowest point from (0, 0) to (0, −5).

  Key Concepts: Comparing Linear, Exponential, &amp; Quadratic Models
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

--- Lesson 6.6: Quadratic Regression (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Quadratic Regression
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 'quadratic regression'?
Quadratic regression is a statistical technique that determines the quadratic equation (parabola) that best fits a set of data points by minimizing the overall error.
What is R² (coefficient of determination)?
R² ranges from 0 to 1 and represents what fraction of the total variation in the dependent variable is explained by the regression model; values closer to 1 indicate a better fit.
What is a 'residual' in regression analysis?
A residual is the difference between the actual y-value and the predicted y-value from the regression equation (residual = observed − predicted), and ideally these should be small and random.
What is a 'residual plot'?
A residual plot displays residuals versus x-values; if the residuals show a random scatter with no pattern, the model is appropriate, but a pattern suggests a different model might be better.
What is a 'second-order polynomial'?
A second-order polynomial has the highest exponent of 2, making it a quadratic function y = ax² + bx + c, which produces a parabolic curve.
A physics student launches a projectile and records height (ft) at various times (s): (0, 3), (1, 35), (2, 51), (3, 51), (4, 35), (5, 3). Quadratic regression gives h = −8t² + 40t + 3. What is the predicted maximum height?
The vertex is at t = −40/(2·(−8)) = 2.5, and h(2.5) = −8(6.25) + 40(2.5) + 3 = −50 + 100 + 3 = 53 feet.
A company's monthly revenue data forms an inverted U. Quadratic regression gives R = −50x² + 800x + 1000, where x is months since launch. What is the predicted maximum revenue?
The vertex is at x = −800/(2·(−50)) = 8, and R(8) = −50(64) + 800(8) + 1000 = −3200 + 6400 + 1000 = $4,200.
A roller coaster designer uses quadratic regression on height data and gets h = 0.5d² − 10d + 80, where d is distance in meters. What is the lowest height of the track?
The vertex is at d = −(−10)/(2·0.5) = 10, and h(10) = 0.5(100) − 10(10) + 80 = 50 − 100 + 80 = 30 meters.
A farmer records crop yield versus rainfall. The regression model is Y = −5r² + 60r + 100, where r is rainfall in inches. If there are 8 inches of rain, what is the predicted yield?
Substituting r = 8: Y = −5(64) + 60(8) + 100 = −320 + 480 + 100 = 260 bushels.
Real-World Applications
Example: A baseball player's batting average over 10 seasons shows data that curves upward then downward. Using quadratic regression, the model is y = −0.003x² + 0.04x + 0.200. In which season does the player peak?
The vertex is at x = −0.04/(2·(−0.003)) = 0.04/0.006 ≈ 6.67, so the player peaks around season 7 (rounding to the nearest whole season).
Example: A sprinkler's water coverage area was measured at different pressure settings. Regression gives A = −0.1p² + 4p + 10, where p is pressure in psi and A is area in ft². If the sprinkler operates at 15 psi, what area is predicted?
