--- Lesson 8.1: Adding &amp; Subtracting Polynomials (Summary) ---

  Notes
Key Concepts: Adding & Subtracting Polynomials

A polynomial is a sum of terms with whole-number exponents. The degree is the highest exponent.

To add polynomials, combine like terms (same variable and exponent).

To subtract, distribute the negative sign to every term in the second polynomial, then combine like terms.

Arrange terms in descending order of degree (standard form).

--- Lesson 8.2: Multiplying Polynomials (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Multiplying Polynomials
Use the distributive property (FOIL for binomials) to multiply polynomials.

FOIL: First, Outer, Inner, Last — used for (a + b)(c + d).

For larger polynomials, distribute each term of the first polynomial to every term of the second.

Combine like terms after multiplying.

--- Lesson 8.3: Dividing Polynomials (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Dividing Polynomials
Long division of polynomials follows the same process as long division of numbers.

Steps: (1) Divide the leading terms. (2) Multiply and subtract. (3) Bring down the next term. (4) Repeat.

Synthetic division is a shortcut when dividing by (x âˆ’ c). Use only the coefficients.

The Remainder Theorem: when P(x) is divided by (x âˆ’ c), the remainder equals P(c).

--- Lesson 8.4: Polynomial Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Polynomial Identities
A polynomial identity is an equation that is true for all values of the variables.

Key identities: (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b², (a − b)² = a² − 2ab + b², (a + b)(a − b) = a² − b².

Binomial theorem (introduction): (a + b)ⁿ can be expanded using patterns from Pascal's Triangle.

Identities are useful for mental math, simplification, and proofs.
