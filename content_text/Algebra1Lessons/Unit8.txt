--- Lesson 8.1: Adding &amp; Subtracting Polynomials (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Adding &amp; Subtracting Polynomials
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a polynomial?
A polynomial is an algebraic expression made of terms with variables raised to non-negative integer exponents. Examples include 3x² + 2x − 5 and x⁴ − 7x + 1. Expressions with negative exponents or variables in denominators are not polynomials.
What is the degree of a polynomial?
The degree of a polynomial is determined by the term with the greatest exponent. For example, 4x³ − 2x + 7 has degree 3. A constant like 5 has degree 0.
What is a monomial?
A monomial is a polynomial consisting of a single term. It can be a constant (like 7), a variable (like x), or a product of constants and variables (like 5x³). The prefix 'mono' means one.
What is a binomial?
A binomial is a polynomial with exactly two terms separated by addition or subtraction, such as x + 5 or 3x² − 7x. The prefix 'bi' means two.
What is a trinomial?
A trinomial is a polynomial with exactly three terms, such as 2x² − 5x + 1. The prefix 'tri' means three. Note that a trinomial does not necessarily have degree three.
What is the leading coefficient?
The leading coefficient is the coefficient of the highest-degree term when the polynomial is written in standard form. In −3x⁴ + 5x² − 1, the leading coefficient is −3.
What is the constant term of a polynomial?
The constant term is the term in a polynomial that has no variable attached, meaning it has degree 0. It represents the y-intercept when the polynomial is graphed as y = f(x).
A student earns (3x² + 5x + 10) dollars in January and (2x² − x + 15) dollars in February. What is the total income for both months?
Add the polynomials: (3x² + 2x²) + (5x + (−x)) + (10 + 15) = 5x² + 4x + 25. The total income for both months is 5x² + 4x + 25 dollars.
A company's revenue is (7x² + 12x + 50) and its costs are (4x² + 8x + 30). What is the profit (revenue minus costs)?
Subtract: (7x² − 4x²) + (12x − 8x) + (50 − 30) = 3x² + 4x + 20. Remember to distribute the negative to all terms in the cost polynomial.
A rectangular garden has a perimeter expressed as two lengths of (3x + 4) meters and two widths of (2x − 1) meters. What is the total perimeter?
Perimeter = 2(3x + 4) + 2(2x − 1) = 6x + 8 + 4x − 2 = 10x + 6 meters.
A school's enrollment for three classes is (x² + 20), (2x² − 5), and (x² + 10). What is the total enrollment?
Add all three: (x² + 2x² + x²) + (20 + (−5) + 10) = 4x² + 25.
The temperature in a city over two days can be modeled as Day 1: (−x² + 8x + 60) and Day 2: (−2x² + 6x + 55). What is the difference in temperature (Day 1 minus Day 2)?
Subtract: (−x² − (−2x²)) + (8x − 6x) + (60 − 55) = (−x² + 2x²) + 2x + 5 = x² + 2x + 5.
A pool's volume at two different times is modeled by V₁ = 3x³ + 2x² + x and V₂ = x³ + 4x² − 3x. What is V₁ + V₂?
Add like terms: (3x³ + x³) + (2x² + 4x²) + (x + (−3x)) = 4x³ + 6x² − 2x.
An investor has two accounts with balances (5x² + 3x + 100) and (−2x² + 7x + 250). She transfers the second account into the first. What is the new balance?
Add: (5x² + (−2x²)) + (3x + 7x) + (100 + 250) = 3x² + 10x + 350.
A student's math scores on three tests are modeled by (x² + 5), (2x² − 3x + 8), and (−x² + 4x − 1). What is the sum of all three scores?
Add: (x² + 2x² + (−x²)) + (0x + (−3x) + 4x) + (5 + 8 + (−1)) = 2x² + x + 12.
Real-World Applications
Example: A student's math scores on three tests are modeled by (x² + 5), (2x² − 3x + 8), and (−x² + 4x − 1). What is the sum of all three scores?
Add: (x² + 2x² + (−x²)) + (0x + (−3x) + 4x) + (5 + 8 + (−1)) = 2x² + x + 12.

    Explanation: Combine like terms: 3x² + 5x² = 8x² and 2x + (−x) = x. The result is 8x² + x.

    Explanation: Distribute the negative sign: 4x³ − 2x + 1 − x³ − 3x + 5. Combine: (4x³ − x³) + (−2x − 3x) + (1 + 5) = 3x³ − 5x + 6.

  Q3. The degree of 7x⁴ − 3x² + 5 is:
    [ ] 7
    [ ] 2
    [*] 4
    [ ] 5
    Explanation: The degree of a polynomial is the highest exponent on any variable. In 7x⁴ − 3x² + 5, the highest power of x is 4.

  Q4. Like terms must have:
    [ ] The same coefficient
    [ ] Different exponents
    [*] The same variable and exponent
    [ ] The same sign
    Explanation: Like terms are terms that have the same variable(s) raised to the same power(s). Only their coefficients may differ. For example, 3x² and −5x² are like terms.

--- Lesson 8.2: Multiplying Polynomials (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Multiplying Polynomials
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the distributive property in the context of polynomial multiplication?
The distributive property states a(b + c) = ab + ac. When multiplying polynomials, each term in one polynomial must be multiplied by every term in the other polynomial, then like terms are combined.
What is a perfect square trinomial?
A perfect square trinomial results from squaring a binomial. The first and last terms are perfect squares, and the middle term is twice the product of the square roots of the first and last terms.
What is the difference of squares pattern?
When multiplying conjugate binomials (a + b)(a − b), the outer and inner products cancel (+ab and −ab), leaving only a² − b². This is the difference of squares pattern.
What is the degree of the product of two polynomials?
When multiplying polynomials, the degree of the product equals the sum of the degrees of the factors. For example, a degree 2 polynomial times a degree 3 polynomial gives a degree 5 product.
What is the 'area model' for polynomial multiplication?
The area model (or box method) arranges terms along the sides of a grid. Each cell contains the product of the corresponding row and column terms. All cells are then added to get the final product.
What is a conjugate pair of binomials?
Conjugate binomials differ only in the sign between their terms. Their product is always a difference of squares: (a + b)(a − b) = a² − b², because the cross terms cancel.
What is a partial product in polynomial multiplication?
A partial product is an individual multiplication result between one term from each polynomial. In FOIL, each of the four products (First, Outer, Inner, Last) is a partial product that is then combined with the others.
A rectangular room is (x + 4) feet long and (x + 3) feet wide. What is the area of the room?
Area = length × width = (x + 4)(x + 3). FOIL: x² + 3x + 4x + 12 = x² + 7x + 12 square feet.
A square garden has side length (2x + 5) meters. What is the area of the garden?
Area = (2x + 5)² = (2x)² + 2(2x)(5) + 5² = 4x² + 20x + 25 square meters. Remember the middle term: 2ab = 2(2x)(5) = 20x.
A box has dimensions x, (x + 2), and (x + 3). What is the volume? (Hint: multiply two factors first, then multiply by the third.)
First multiply (x + 2)(x + 3) = x² + 5x + 6. Then multiply by x: x(x² + 5x + 6) = x³ + 5x² + 6x.
A painter charges (3x + 10) dollars per hour and works (x + 2) hours. What is the total charge?
Total = (3x + 10)(x + 2). FOIL: 3x² + 6x + 10x + 20 = 3x² + 16x + 20 dollars.
Two adjacent square plots have side lengths (x + 1) and (x − 1). What is the difference in their areas?
Area₁ = (x + 1)² = x² + 2x + 1. Area₂ = (x − 1)² = x² − 2x + 1. Difference = (x² + 2x + 1) − (x² − 2x + 1) = 4x.
A company sells (x + 5) units at (2x − 3) dollars each. What is the revenue?
Revenue = (x + 5)(2x − 3). FOIL: 2x² − 3x + 10x − 15 = 2x² + 7x − 15 dollars.
A farmer has a field that is (x + 6) meters by (x − 6) meters. What is the area?
This is a conjugate pair: (x + 6)(x − 6) = x² − 36 (difference of squares). The cross terms cancel out.
A movie theater has (x + 10) rows and (x − 4) seats per row. If they add 50 extra premium seats, what is the total seating capacity?
Regular seats: (x + 10)(x − 4) = x² − 4x + 10x − 40 = x² + 6x − 40. Add 50 premium: x² + 6x − 40 + 50 = x² + 6x + 10.
Real-World Applications
Example: What is the distributive property in the context of polynomial multiplication?
The distributive property states a(b + c) = ab + ac. When multiplying polynomials, each term in one polynomial must be multiplied by every term in the other polynomial, then like terms are combined.
Example: What is the 'area model' for polynomial multiplication?
The area model (or box method) arranges terms along the sides of a grid. Each cell contains the product of the corresponding row and column terms. All cells are then added to get the final product.
Example: What is a partial product in polynomial multiplication?
A partial product is an individual multiplication result between one term from each polynomial. In FOIL, each of the four products (First, Outer, Inner, Last) is a partial product that is then combined with the others.
Example: A box has dimensions x, (x + 2), and (x + 3). What is the volume? (Hint: multiply two factors first, then multiply by the third.)
First multiply (x + 2)(x + 3) = x² + 5x + 6. Then multiply by x: x(x² + 5x + 6) = x³ + 5x² + 6x.

--- Lesson 8.2: Multiplying Polynomials (Quiz) ---

  Q1. (x + 3)(x + 5) = ?
    [*] x² + 8x + 15
    [ ] x² + 3x + 5
    [ ] x² + 15x + 8
    [ ] x² + 8x + 8
    Explanation: Using FOIL: First x·x = x², Outer x·5 = 5x, Inner 3·x = 3x, Last 3·5 = 15. Combine: x² + 8x + 15.

  Q2. (2x − 1)(x + 4) = ?
    [*] 2x² + 7x − 4
    [ ] 2x² + 3x − 4
    [ ] 2x² + 5x − 4
    [ ] 2x² − x − 4
    Explanation: FOIL: 2x·x = 2x², 2x·4 = 8x, (−1)·x = −x, (−1)·4 = −4. Combine: 2x² + 8x − x − 4 = 2x² + 7x − 4.

  Q3. FOIL stands for:
    [ ] First, Only, Inner, Least
    [*] First, Outer, Inner, Last
    [ ] Find, Organize, Identify, List
    [ ] Factor, Order, Inverse, Last
    Explanation: FOIL is a mnemonic for multiplying two binomials: multiply the First terms, then the Outer terms, then the Inner terms, then the Last terms, and combine like terms.

  Q4. (x − 3)(x + 3) = ?
    [ ] x² − 6x − 9
    [ ] x² + 9
    [*] x² − 9
    [ ] x² − 6x + 9
    Explanation: This is a conjugate pair (difference of squares pattern): (x − 3)(x + 3) = x² − 9. The middle terms (−3x + 3x) cancel out.

  Q5. (x + 2)² = ?
    [*] x² + 4x + 4
    [ ] 2x² + 4
    [ ] x² + 4
    [ ] x² + 2x + 4
    Explanation: Using the perfect square pattern: (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b². Here (x + 2)² = x² + 2(x)(2) + 4 = x² + 4x + 4.

  Q6. x(x² + 3x − 2) = ?
    [ ] x³ + 3x − 2
    [ ] 3x³ − 2x
    [ ] x² + 3x² − 2x
    [*] x³ + 3x² − 2x
    Explanation: Distribute x to each term: x·x² = x³, x·3x = 3x², x·(−2) = −2x. Result: x³ + 3x² − 2x.

  Q7. (3x)(4x²) = ?
    [*] 12x³
    [ ] 12x²
    [ ] 7x²
    [ ] 7x³
    Explanation: Multiply coefficients: 3 × 4 = 12. Add exponents of x: 1 + 2 = 3. Result: 12x³.

  Q8. (x + 2)(x + 5) = ?
    [ ] x² + 5x + 10
    [ ] x² + 10x + 7
    [ ] x² + 7x + 7
    [*] x² + 7x + 10
    Explanation: FOIL: x² + 5x + 2x + 10 = x² + 7x + 10. The middle term is the sum of 5 and 2, and the last term is their product.

  Q9. (2x + 1)(3x − 2) = ?
    [ ] 5x² − x − 2
    [ ] 6x² + x − 2
    [*] 6x² − x − 2
    [ ] 6x² − 5x − 2
    Explanation: FOIL: 6x² − 4x + 3x − 2 = 6x² − x − 2. Combine the middle terms: −4x + 3x = −x.

  Q10. 3x(2x² − 4x + 1) = ?
    [*] 6x³ − 12x² + 3x
    [ ] 3x² − 4x + 1
    [ ] 6x³ − 4x + 1
    [ ] 6x² − 12x + 3
    Explanation: Distribute 3x to each term: 3x(2x²) = 6x³, 3x(−4x) = −12x², 3x(1) = 3x. Result: 6x³ − 12x² + 3x.

  Q11. What does the FOIL method do?
    [*] A systematic method for multiplying two binomials by computing First, Outer, Inner, and Last products, then combining like terms
    [ ] A method for dividing polynomials, particularly regarding letter under equilibrium conditions

--- Lesson 8.3: Dividing Polynomials (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Dividing Polynomials
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the remainder when (x² + 3x + 5) is divided by (x + 1)?
By the Remainder Theorem, substitute c = −1 into P(x): P(−1) = 1 − 3 + 5 = 3, so the remainder is 3.
What is the first step in polynomial long division?
The first step is to divide the leading term of the dividend by the leading term of the divisor to get the first term of the quotient.
What is the Remainder Theorem?
The Remainder Theorem states that when a polynomial P(x) is divided by (x − c), the remainder is exactly P(c), found by substituting c into the polynomial.
What is synthetic division?
Synthetic division is a streamlined process that uses only the coefficients of the dividend and the value c from the divisor (x − c), making it faster than long division for linear divisors.
What is the Factor Theorem?
The Factor Theorem is a special case of the Remainder Theorem: if P(c) = 0, the remainder is 0, which means (x − c) divides P(x) evenly and is therefore a factor.
What is the dividend in polynomial division?
The dividend is the polynomial being divided, which appears inside the division symbol or in the numerator of the fraction.
What is a zero-coefficient placeholder in polynomial division?
When a polynomial is missing a term of a certain degree, such as the x-term in x³ + 5, you insert 0x (or 0x²) as a placeholder to keep columns aligned during division.
What is a root or zero of a polynomial P(x)?
A root or zero is a value c where the polynomial evaluates to zero, meaning P(c) = 0, and by the Factor Theorem, (x − c) is a factor of P(x).
What is the divisor in polynomial division?
The divisor is the polynomial you divide by, appearing outside the division symbol or in the denominator, such as (x − 3) in P(x) ÷ (x − 3).
What is the division algorithm for polynomials?
The division algorithm states that Dividend = (Divisor)(Quotient) + Remainder, which can be used to verify any polynomial division by checking that the equation holds.
A student uses the Remainder Theorem to evaluate P(x) = 2x³ − 3x² + x − 5 at x = 3. What is P(3)?
P(3) = 2(27) − 3(9) + 3 − 5 = 54 − 27 + 3 − 5 = 25. By the Remainder Theorem, this is also the remainder when P(x) is divided by (x − 3).
A landscape designer has a rectangular plot with area A(x) = 3x² + 14x − 5 square meters and one side measuring (3x − 1) meters. What is the length of the other side?
Dividing (3x² + 14x − 5) by (3x − 1): 3x²/3x = x, then x(3x − 1) = 3x² − x, subtract to get 15x − 5, then 15x/3x = 5, and 5(3x − 1) = 15x − 5 with remainder 0. The other side is x + 5.
A scientist models a chemical reaction as R(x) = 2x³ + 3x² − 8x + 3 and tests whether (x − 1) is a factor. What is the quotient when R(x) is divided by (x − 1)?
First verify: R(1) = 2 + 3 − 8 + 3 = 0, so (x − 1) is a factor. Synthetic division with c = 1 on 2, 3, −8, 3: bring down 2, then 2(1)+3 = 5, then 5(1)+(−8) = −3, then (−3)(1)+3 = 0. The quotient is 2x² + 5x − 3.
Real-World Applications
Example: A factory models production costs as C(x) = x³ + 2x² − 5x − 6. A manager wants to know if (x + 1) is a factor. What does the Factor Theorem tell us?
By the Factor Theorem, (x + 1) is a factor if C(−1) = 0. C(−1) = −1 + 2 + 5 − 6 = 0, so (x + 1) is indeed a factor of C(x).

     A: When P(x) is divided by (x − c), the remainder equals P(c)

  5. Q: (x² + 5x + 6) ÷ (x + 2) = ?
     A: x + 3

  6. Q: If P(x) ÷ (x − 2) has remainder 0:
     A: Then x = 2 is a root of P(x)

  7. Q: Synthetic division can only be used when dividing by:
     A: (x − c) — a linear binomial

  8. Q: (x³ − 8) ÷ (x − 2) = ?
     A: x² + 2x + 4

  9. Q: When using synthetic division, what values do you write?
     A: The coefficients of the polynomial (use 0 for missing degrees)

  10. Q: What is the Factor Theorem?
     A: If P(c) = 0, then (x − c) is a factor of P(x)

--- Lesson 8.4: Polynomial Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Polynomial Identities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a polynomial identity?
A polynomial identity is an equation that holds true regardless of what values are substituted for the variables, such as (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b².
What is the difference of squares identity?
The difference of squares identity states that a² − b² factors into the product of conjugates (a + b)(a − b), and this is one of the most frequently used identities in algebra.
What is a perfect square trinomial?
A perfect square trinomial takes the form a² + 2ab + b² or a² − 2ab + b², and it is the result of squaring the binomial (a + b) or (a − b) respectively.
What is Pascal's Triangle?
Pascal's Triangle is a triangular array of numbers where each entry equals the sum of the two entries directly above it, and the nth row provides the binomial coefficients for expanding (a + b)ⁿ.
What is the sum of cubes identity?
The sum of cubes identity factors a³ + b³ into (a + b)(a² − ab + b²), where the binomial matches the sum sign and the trinomial has an opposite middle term.
What is the difference of cubes identity?
The difference of cubes identity factors a³ − b³ into (a − b)(a² + ab + b²), where the binomial matches the subtraction sign and the trinomial has all positive terms.
What is the Binomial Theorem used for?
The Binomial Theorem provides a systematic formula for expanding (a + b)ⁿ, where the coefficients come from Pascal's Triangle or the combination formula C(n, k).
What is a trinomial square pattern?
A trinomial square pattern occurs when the first and last terms are perfect squares and the middle term equals twice the product of their square roots, allowing the trinomial to be written as a binomial squared.
A student wants to mentally compute 103² using the identity (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b². What is the result?
Write 103 = 100 + 3, so 103² = (100 + 3)² = 100² + 2(100)(3) + 3² = 10000 + 600 + 9 = 10609.
A rectangular window measures (x + 6) inches by (x − 6) inches. Using the difference of squares identity, what is the area of the window?
Using (a + b)(a − b) = a² − b² with a = x and b = 6: (x + 6)(x − 6) = x² − 36 square inches.
A shipping company packs items in a cube with side length (x + 2). Using the identity (a + b)³ = a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³, what is the volume?
Using (a + b)³ with a = x and b = 2: x³ + 3(x²)(2) + 3(x)(4) + 8 = x³ + 6x² + 12x + 8.
A chemistry teacher asks students to factor 27x³ + 8 using a polynomial identity. What is the factored form?
Recognize 27x³ + 8 = (3x)³ + 2³ as a sum of cubes. Using a³ + b³ = (a + b)(a² − ab + b²): (3x + 2)(9x² − 6x + 4).
A store owner wants to multiply 47 × 53 mentally using the difference of squares identity. What is the result?
Write 47 × 53 as (50 − 3)(50 + 3) = 50² − 3² = 2500 − 9 = 2491, using the identity (a − b)(a + b) = a² − b².
An architect designs two square rooms. One has side (2x + 3) meters and the other has side (2x − 3) meters. What is the difference in their areas?
The difference in areas is (2x + 3)² − (2x − 3)². Using the identity a² − b² = (a + b)(a − b) with a = (2x + 3) and b = (2x − 3): (4x)(6) = 24x square meters.
A science student needs to factor x³ − 125 for a lab calculation. Using the difference of cubes identity, what is the factored form?
Recognize x³ − 125 = x³ − 5³ as a difference of cubes. Using a³ − b³ = (a − b)(a² + ab + b²): (x − 5)(x² + 5x + 25).
A math team uses Pascal's Triangle to expand (a + b)⁴. What is the expanded form?
Row 4 of Pascal's Triangle gives coefficients 1, 4, 6, 4, 1. So (a + b)⁴ = a⁴ + 4a³b + 6a²b² + 4ab³ + b⁴.
Real-World Applications
Example: A store owner wants to multiply 47 × 53 mentally using the difference of squares identity. What is the result?
Write 47 × 53 as (50 − 3)(50 + 3) = 50² − 3² = 2500 − 9 = 2491, using the identity (a − b)(a + b) = a² − b².
