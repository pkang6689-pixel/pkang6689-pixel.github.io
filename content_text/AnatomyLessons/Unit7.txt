--- Lesson 7.1: Anatomy of the Respiratory System (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Anatomy of the Respiratory System
Anatomy of the Respiratory SystemUpper Respiratory Tract  • Nose/nasal cavity: Warms, moistens, filters air. Pharynx: Throat; shared with digestive tract.  • Larynx: Voice box; epiglottis prevents food from entering the trachea.Lower Respiratory Tract  • Trachea: Windpipe; C-shaped cartilage rings.  • Bronchi → bronchioles → terminal bronchioles → respiratory bronchioles → alveolar ducts → alveoli.  • Alveoli: ~300 million; thin-walled sacs where gas exchange occurs.  • Lungs: Right (3 lobes), left (2 lobes). Pleura: Serous membrane lining.

--- Lesson 7.2: Mechanics of Breathing (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Mechanics of Breathing
Mechanics of BreathingInspiration (Inhalation)
Diaphragm contracts (flattens) + external intercostals lift ribs → thoracic volume increases → intrapulmonary pressure drops below atmospheric → air flows in.
Expiration (Exhalation)
Passive at rest: diaphragm relaxes (domes up) + elastic recoil of lungs → thoracic volume decreases → pressure increases → air flows out. Forced expiration uses internal intercostals and abdominal muscles.
Lung Volumes
Tidal volume (~500 mL), vital capacity, residual volume, total lung capacity. Spirometry measures these.

--- Lesson 7.3: Gas Exchange in the Alveoli (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Gas Exchange in the Alveoli
Gas Exchange in the AlveoliPrinciples
Gas exchange occurs by simple diffusion across the respiratory membrane. O₂ diffuses from alveoli (high PO₂ ~104 mmHg) into pulmonary capillary blood (low PO₂ ~40 mmHg). CO₂ diffuses from blood (PCO₂ ~45 mmHg) into alveoli (PCO₂ ~40 mmHg).
Factors Affecting Exchange  • Surface area: ~70 m²; reduced in emphysema.  • Membrane thickness: Thickened in fibrosis → impaired diffusion.  • Ventilation-perfusion (V/Q) matching: Adequate airflow matched with blood flow for efficient exchange.

--- Lesson 7.4: Transport of O₂ & CO₂ (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Transport of O₂ & CO₂
Transport of O₂ & CO₂Oxygen Transport  • ~98.5% bound to hemoglobin (Hb) → oxyhemoglobin (HbO₂).  • ~1.5% dissolved in plasma.  • O₂-Hb dissociation curve: Sigmoidal; right shift (↑temp, ↓pH, ↑CO₂, ↑2,3-BPG) → easier O₂ release to tissues.CO₂ Transport  • ~70% as bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻) via carbonic anhydrase: CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃ → H⁺ + HCO₃⁻.  • ~23% bound to Hb (carbaminohemoglobin). ~7% dissolved.

--- Lesson 7.5: Regulation of Breathing (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Regulation of Breathing
Regulation of BreathingNeural Control  • Medullary respiratory center: Sets the basic rhythm — dorsal respiratory group (inspiration), ventral respiratory group (forced breathing).  • Pontine respiratory group: Smooths transitions between inspiration and expiration.Chemical Control  • Central chemoreceptors (medulla): Detect ↑CO₂ (via ↑H⁺ in CSF) — primary driver of ventilation.  • Peripheral chemoreceptors (carotid/aortic bodies): Detect ↓O₂, ↑CO₂, ↓pH.

--- Lesson 7.6: Disorders of the Respiratory System (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Disorders of the Respiratory System
Disorders of the Respiratory System  • Asthma: Chronic airway inflammation with reversible bronchospasm → wheezing, dyspnea. Treated with bronchodilators and inhaled corticosteroids.  • COPD: Chronic bronchitis (excess mucus) + emphysema (alveolar destruction). Mostly from smoking. Irreversible airflow limitation.  • Pneumonia: Infection (bacterial, viral, fungal) filling alveoli with fluid/pus → impaired gas exchange.  • Tuberculosis (TB): Mycobacterium tuberculosis; granuloma formation.  • Lung cancer: Leading cancer killer; strong association with smoking.

--- Lesson 7.7: Case Studies in Pulmonology (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Case Studies in Pulmonology
Case Studies in PulmonologyCase 1: Acute Asthma Exacerbation
A 22-year-old with wheezing, dyspnea, and prolonged expiratory phase after allergen exposure. Peak flow 50% of predicted. Treatment: nebulized albuterol + systemic corticosteroids. Improvement within hours.
Case 2: Community-Acquired Pneumonia
A 68-year-old with fever, productive cough (rusty sputum), dyspnea. CXR: right lower lobe consolidation. WBC elevated. Treatment: antibiotics (empiric then targeted).
Case 3: COPD Exacerbation
A 65-year-old smoker with worsening dyspnea, increased sputum, and hypoxemia. ABG: PaO₂ 55, PaCO₂ 60 (respiratory acidosis). Treatment: controlled O₂, bronchodilators, antibiotics, systemic steroids.

AP Prep: Respiratory PhysiologyKey Topics for Exam  • Breathing mechanics: Boyle's law, diaphragm, intrapulmonary vs. intrapleural pressure.  • Gas laws: Dalton's (partial pressures), Henry's (gas solubility).  • O₂ transport: Hb saturation curve, Bohr effect, factors shifting curve.  • CO₂ transport: bicarbonate, carbaminohemoglobin, dissolved.  • Regulation: chemoreceptors (central vs. peripheral), respiratory centers.  • Acid-base: respiratory acidosis/alkalosis, compensation.
