--- Lesson 1.1: Layers of the Earth (Crust, Mantle, Core) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Layers of the Earth (Crust, Mantle, Core)
Earth's Internal StructureLayers by Composition  • Crust: Thin outermost layer. Continental crust (25-70 km, granitic) and oceanic crust (5-10 km, basaltic).  • Mantle: Thick layer of silicate rock (2,900 km). Upper mantle contains the asthenosphere (partially molten).  • Core: Iron-nickel. Outer core is liquid (2,200 km), inner core is solid (1,220 km radius) due to immense pressure.Layers by Physical Properties  • Lithosphere: Rigid crust + uppermost mantle; broken into tectonic plates.  • Asthenosphere: Partially molten, flows slowly; plates glide over it.  • Mesosphere: Lower mantle, stronger but still capable of slow flow.

--- Lesson 1.2: Minerals (Classification, Identification Tests) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Minerals (Classification, Identification Tests)
MineralsWhat Defines a Mineral?  • Naturally occurring, inorganic, solid, definite chemical composition, and crystalline structure.Identification Tests  • Hardness: Mohs scale (1-talc to 10-diamond).  • Luster: Metallic vs. non-metallic (vitreous, pearly, earthy).  • Streak: Color of powdered mineral on unglazed porcelain.  • Cleavage/Fracture: How mineral breaks — along flat planes (cleavage) or irregular surfaces (fracture).  • Specific Gravity: Density relative to water.

--- Lesson 1.3: Rock Cycle (Igneous, Sedimentary, Metamorphic) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Rock Cycle (Igneous, Sedimentary, Metamorphic)
The Rock CycleThree Rock Types  • Igneous: From cooled magma/lava. Intrusive (slow cooling, large crystals) vs. extrusive (fast cooling, small/no crystals).  • Sedimentary: From compacted/cemented sediments. Clastic (broken pieces), chemical (precipitation), organic (biological remains).  • Metamorphic: From existing rock changed by heat and/or pressure. Foliated (banded) vs. non-foliated.Cycle Processes  • Weathering → Erosion → Deposition → Compaction/Cementation → Uplift → Metamorphism → Melting → Crystallization

--- Lesson 1.4: Plate Tectonics (Continental Drift, Seafloor Spreading) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Plate Tectonics (Continental Drift, Seafloor Spreading)
Plate TectonicsEvidence for Continental Drift  • Puzzle-fit of coastlines (Africa & South America).  • Matching fossils across oceans (Mesosaurus, Glossopteris).  • Glacial striations in tropical regions.Seafloor Spreading  • Mid-ocean ridges produce new oceanic crust.  • Magnetic striping: symmetric reversals on either side of ridge.  • Age of ocean floor increases with distance from ridge.Plate Boundaries  • Divergent: Plates move apart → new crust (mid-ocean ridges, rift valleys).  • Convergent: Plates collide → subduction, mountains, or volcanic arcs.  • Transform: Plates slide past each other → earthquakes (San Andreas Fault).

--- Lesson 1.5: Earthquakes (Seismic Waves, Measurement, Prediction) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Earthquakes (Seismic Waves, Measurement, Prediction)
EarthquakesCauses
Sudden release of energy from rock fracturing along faults. Most occur at plate boundaries.
Seismic Waves  • P-waves (Primary): Fastest; compressional; travel through solids, liquids, and gases.  • S-waves (Secondary): Slower; shear; travel only through solids.  • Surface waves: Slowest but most destructive; Rayleigh and Love waves.Measurement  • Moment Magnitude Scale: Measures total energy released. Each whole number = ~32× more energy.  • Seismograph: Instrument that records ground motion.

--- Lesson 1.6: Volcanoes (Types, Eruptions, Hazards) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Volcanoes (Types, Eruptions, Hazards)
VolcanoesTypes of Volcanoes  • Shield: Broad, gently sloping; low-viscosity basaltic lava (Mauna Loa, Hawaii).  • Cinder Cone: Smallest; steep sides; explosive fragments (Parícutin).  • Composite (Stratovolcano): Tall, steep; alternating lava/ash layers; explosive (Mt. --- Lesson 1.7: Mountain Building & Geological Structures (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Mountain Building & Geological Structures
Mountain Building (Orogeny)Types of Mountains  • Fold Mountains: Formed by compression at convergent boundaries (Himalayas, Appalachians).  • Fault-Block Mountains: Formed by tensional forces lifting blocks along normal faults (Sierra Nevada, Tetons).  • Volcanic Mountains: Built by eruptions (Mt. Rainier, Mt. Fuji).  • Dome Mountains: Uplifted by magma pushing up without erupting (Black Hills).Geological Structures  • Anticline: Upward fold (arch). Syncline: Downward fold (trough).  • Fault types: Normal (tension), reverse/thrust (compression), strike-slip (shear).

--- Lesson 1.8: Geological Resources (Ores, Fossil Fuels) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Geological Resources (Ores, Fossil Fuels)
Geological ResourcesMetallic Ores  • Formed by magmatic, hydrothermal, sedimentary, and weathering processes.  • Examples: iron (hematite), copper (chalcopyrite), gold (native), aluminum (bauxite).Fossil Fuels  • Coal: From ancient swamp vegetation; peat → lignite → bituminous → anthracite.  • Oil & Natural Gas: From marine organisms buried under sediment; require source rock, migration, and trap.Non-Metallic Resources  • Sand, gravel, limestone (cement), gypsum (drywall), clay, phosphates (fertilizer).
