--- Lesson 3.1: Hydrologic Cycle (Evaporation, Condensation, Precipitation) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Hydrologic Cycle (Evaporation, Condensation, Precipitation)
The Hydrologic (Water) CycleKey Processes  • Evaporation: Liquid water → water vapor (solar energy driven). Transpiration adds plant water.  • Condensation: Water vapor → liquid droplets (forms clouds when air cools to dew point).  • Precipitation: Water falls as rain, snow, sleet, or hail when cloud droplets grow large enough.  • Infiltration: Water soaks into soil and rock, recharging groundwater.  • Runoff: Water flows over the surface into streams, rivers, lakes, and oceans.Water Budget
Earth's water is constant — the cycle redistributes it. ~97.5% salt water, ~2.5% fresh (most locked in ice).

--- Lesson 3.2: Oceans (Currents, Salinity, Temperature Layers) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Oceans (Currents, Salinity, Temperature Layers)
Ocean PropertiesSalinity  • Average ocean salinity ~35 ppt (parts per thousand). Higher near evaporative regions, lower near river mouths and rain.Temperature Layers  • Surface (mixed) layer: Warm, well-mixed by waves and wind (0-200 m).  • Thermocline: Rapid temperature decrease with depth (200-1,000 m).  • Deep ocean: Cold and nearly uniform (~1-4°C) below 1,000 m.Currents  • Surface currents: Wind-driven; form gyres (clockwise NH, counterclockwise SH).  • Deep currents: Thermohaline circulation driven by density differences (temperature + salinity).

--- Lesson 3.3: Waves & Tides (Causes, Effects) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Waves & Tides (Causes, Effects)
Waves & TidesWaves  • Generated primarily by wind. Wave height depends on wind speed, duration, and fetch.  • Waves transport energy, not water — water moves in circular orbits.  • Waves break when depth becomes less than half the wavelength.Tides  • Caused by: Gravitational pull of the Moon (primary) and Sun.  • Spring tides: Sun and Moon aligned → highest highs, lowest lows.  • Neap tides: Sun and Moon at 90° → smaller tidal range.

--- Lesson 3.4: Freshwater Systems (Rivers, Lakes, Aquifers) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Freshwater Systems (Rivers, Lakes, Aquifers)
Freshwater SystemsRivers & Streams  • Drainage networks from headwaters to mouth. Shaped by gradient, discharge, and load.  • Meanders form on gently sloping terrain; V-shaped valleys in steep terrain.Lakes  • Formed by glaciers, tectonics, volcanic activity, or river/dam processes.  • Stratified in summer: warm epilimnion, thermocline, cold hypolimnion.Groundwater  • Stored in aquifers — porous rock below the water table. Ogallala Aquifer supplies ~30% of US irrigation.

--- Lesson 3.5: Glaciers & Ice Caps (Formation, Melting Impacts) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Glaciers & Ice Caps (Formation, Melting Impacts)
Glaciers & Ice CapsFormation & Types  • Glaciers form when snow accumulates faster than it melts, compressing into ice over years.  • Alpine (valley): Flow down mountain valleys.  • Continental (ice sheets): Cover vast areas (Greenland, Antarctica).Glacial Features  • Erosion: cirques, U-shaped valleys, horns, arêtes.  • Deposition: moraines, drumlins, erratics, outwash plains.Climate Implications  • If all ice melted, sea level would rise ~65-70 meters. Current rate: ~3.5 mm/year sea level rise.
