--- Lesson 2.1: Composition & Layers of the Atmosphere (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Composition & Layers of the Atmosphere
Atmospheric Composition & StructureComposition  • Nitrogen 78%, Oxygen 21%, Argon 0.93%, CO₂ 0.04%, trace gases and water vapor.Atmospheric Layers  • Troposphere (0-12 km): Weather occurs here; temperature decreases with altitude.  • Stratosphere (12-50 km): Contains ozone layer; temperature increases with altitude.  • Mesosphere (50-85 km): Coldest layer; meteors burn up here.  • Thermosphere (85-600 km): Very hot but thin; auroras occur here.  • Exosphere (600+ km): Transitions into space.

--- Lesson 2.2: Energy Transfer (Radiation, Conduction, Convection) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Energy Transfer (Radiation, Conduction, Convection)
Energy Transfer in Earth's AtmosphereThree Methods  • Radiation: Transfer by electromagnetic waves; how the Sun heats Earth (no medium required).  • Conduction: Direct contact transfer; Earth's surface heats air touching it.  • Convection: Transfer by fluid movement; warm air rises, cool air sinks → convection cells.Earth's Energy Budget  • ~30% of solar radiation reflected (albedo), ~70% absorbed by surface and atmosphere.  • Earth radiates infrared back; greenhouse gases absorb and re-emit some, warming the surface.

--- Lesson 2.3: Weather Systems (Fronts, Pressure Systems) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Weather Systems (Fronts, Pressure Systems)
Weather SystemsAir Masses & Fronts  • Air masses: Large bodies of air with uniform T and humidity (Continental/Maritime × Tropical/Polar/Arctic).  • Cold front: Cold air pushes under warm air → steep boundary, heavy rain, then clearing.  • Warm front: Warm air slides over cold air → gradual boundary, steady precipitation.  • Stationary front: Neither air mass advances; prolonged cloudiness/rain.  • Occluded front: Cold front catches warm front; complex weather.Pressure Systems  • Low pressure (cyclone): Rising air, convergence, clouds, precipitation.  • High pressure (anticyclone): Sinking air, divergence, clear skies.

--- Lesson 2.4: Clouds & Precipitation Types (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Clouds & Precipitation Types
Clouds & PrecipitationCloud Classification  • Cirrus: High, thin, wispy. Made of ice crystals.  • Stratus: Low, flat, layered. Steady drizzle or overcast.  • Cumulus: Puffy, vertical development. Fair weather or thunderstorms (cumulonimbus).  • Nimbus prefix/suffix: Rain-bearing (nimbostratus, cumulonimbus).Precipitation Formation  • Bergeron process: Ice crystals grow at the expense of supercooled water droplets in mixed clouds.  • Collision-coalescence: Droplets collide and merge (warm tropical clouds).

--- Lesson 2.5: Climate Zones & Global Circulation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Climate Zones & Global Circulation
Climate Zones & Global CirculationGlobal Wind Belts  • Trade Winds: 0-30° latitude; blow NE in NH, SE in SH.  • Westerlies: 30-60°; prevailing winds from west to east.  • Polar Easterlies: 60-90°; cold, dry winds from the poles.Climate Classification (Köppen)  • A (Tropical): Warm year-round, high precipitation.  • B (Dry/Arid): Evaporation exceeds precipitation.  • C (Temperate): Moderate; mild winters.  • D (Continental): Large temperature range; cold winters.  • E (Polar): Cold year-round.
