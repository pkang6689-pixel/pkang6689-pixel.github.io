--- Lesson 2.1: Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent
Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent
Using the unit circle, trig functions are defined for all real numbers, not just acute angles.
Unit-Circle Definitions  • sin θ = y-coordinate of the point on the unit circle.  • cos θ = x-coordinate of the point on the unit circle.  • tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = y/x (undefined when cos θ = 0).Domain & Range  • sin θ: domain = all reals, range = [−1, 1].  • cos θ: domain = all reals, range = [−1, 1].  • tan θ: domain = all reals except θ = π/2 + nπ, range = (−∞, ∞).
These definitions are consistent with SOH-CAH-TOA for acute angles but extend to all quadrants.

--- Lesson 2.2: Reciprocal Functions (csc, sec, cot) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Reciprocal Functions (csc, sec, cot)
Reciprocal Trigonometric Functions
Three additional trig functions are defined as reciprocals of the primary three:
  • csc θ = 1/sin θ (cosecant) — undefined when sin θ = 0.  • sec θ = 1/cos θ (secant) — undefined when cos θ = 0.  • cot θ = 1/tan θ = cos θ/sin θ (cotangent) — undefined when sin θ = 0.Domains  • csc θ: all reals except nπ.  • sec θ: all reals except π/2 + nπ.  • cot θ: all reals except nπ.Ranges  • csc θ and sec θ: (−∞, −1] ∪ [1, ∞).  • cot θ: (−∞, ∞).

--- Lesson 2.3: Graphs of Sine & Cosine (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphs of Sine & Cosine
Graphs of Sine & CosineThe Sine Graph: y = sin x  • Starts at (0, 0), rises to 1 at π/2, returns to 0 at π, drops to −1 at 3π/2, returns to 0 at 2π.  • Period: 2π (one full cycle). Amplitude: 1. Midline: y = 0.The Cosine Graph: y = cos x  • Starts at (0, 1), drops to 0 at π/2, reaches −1 at π, returns to 0 at 3π/2, back to 1 at 2π.  • Same period, amplitude, and midline as sine.
cos x is a phase-shifted sine: cos x = sin(x + π/2).

Both graphs are smooth, continuous waves called sinusoidal curves.

--- Lesson 2.5: Transformations of Trig Graphs (amplitude, period, phase shift) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Transformations of Trig Graphs (amplitude, period, phase shift)
Transformations of Trig Graphs
The general sinusoidal form is y = A sin(Bx − C) + D (or cosine).
Parameters  • A (amplitude): |A| = vertical stretch/compression. If A < 0, the graph is reflected over the x-axis.  • B (period change): Period = 2π/|B|. Larger |B| → shorter period.  • C/B (phase shift): Horizontal shift = C/B to the right.  • D (vertical shift): The midline moves to y = D.
Example: y = 3 sin(2x − π) + 1 has amplitude 3, period π, phase shift π/2 right, midline y = 1.

--- Lesson 2.6: Symmetry & Periodicity (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Symmetry & Periodicity
Symmetry & PeriodicityEven and Odd Functions  • Cosine is even: cos(−θ) = cos θ. Graph is symmetric about the y-axis.  • Sine is odd: sin(−θ) = −sin θ. Graph is symmetric about the origin.  • Tangent is odd: tan(−θ) = −tan θ.Periodicity  • sin and cos have period 2π: f(θ + 2π) = f(θ).  • tan and cot have period π: f(θ + π) = f(θ).  • Periodicity means you only need one cycle to know all values.
Symmetry and periodicity together reduce calculations: use reference angles and sign rules.

--- Lesson 2.7: Applications in Sound Waves (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Sound Waves
Applications in Sound Waves
Sound waves are modeled by sinusoidal functions because they involve periodic oscillations of air pressure.
Modeling Sound  • A basic sound wave: y = A sin(2πft) where A = amplitude (loudness), f = frequency (pitch), t = time.  • Frequency is measured in hertz (Hz): cycles per second. Higher frequency → higher pitch.  • Period = 1/f: time for one complete cycle.Musical Concepts  • Standard tuning: A4 = 440 Hz.  • An octave doubles the frequency: A5 = 880 Hz.  • Overlapping sound waves create beats — periodic fluctuations in loudness.
Understanding sinusoidal functions helps in acoustics, audio engineering, and music theory.
