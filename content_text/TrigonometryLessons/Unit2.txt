--- Lesson 2.1: Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Definition of Sine, Cosine, Tangent
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A Ferris wheel has radius 20 m. A rider is at an angle of 30° from the horizontal center. What is the rider's height above the center using sine?
Height above center = r sin θ = 20 sin 30° = 20(0.5) = 10 m.
A child on a swing reaches a maximum angle of 45° from vertical. If the swing chain is 3 m long, what is the horizontal displacement at the peak? (Use sin 45° = √2/2)
Horizontal displacement = chain × sin 45° = 3(√2/2) = 3√2/2 m.
A surveyor stands on flat ground and needs to find the height of a tower. She measures the angle of elevation as θ where sin θ = 3/5 and stands 40 m from the base. What is tan θ?
sin θ = 3/5 → cos θ = 4/5 (Pythagorean triple). tan θ = sin θ/cos θ = (3/5)/(4/5) = 3/4.
A drone flies at a constant altitude. Its camera is tilted at angle θ from vertical, where cos θ = 5/13. What is sin θ for this camera angle?
cos θ = 5/13 → sin θ = √(1 − 25/169) = √(144/169) = 12/13.
A cable car travels along a wire that makes angle θ with the horizontal. If sin θ = 0.6 and cos θ = 0.8, what is tan θ?
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = 0.6/0.8 = 0.75.
A roof has a pitch angle θ with sin θ = 5/13. The roof spans 24 m horizontally. What is the length of the roof surface (the hypotenuse)?
sin θ = 5/13 → cos θ = 12/13. Horizontal = hyp × cos θ → 24 = hyp × 12/13 → hyp = 26 m.

--- Lesson 2.2: Reciprocal Functions (csc, sec, cot) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Reciprocal Functions (csc, sec, cot)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A building's shadow indicates the sun's elevation angle θ, where sin θ = 3/5. A technician needs csc θ to complete a calculation. What is csc θ?
csc θ = 1/sin θ = 1/(3/5) = 5/3.
A security camera has a field of view described by angle θ where cos θ = 5/13. What is sec θ for this viewing angle?
sec θ = 1/cos θ = 1/(5/13) = 13/5.
An engineer measures that tan θ = 4/3 for a slope. What is cot θ for the same slope?
cot θ = 1/tan θ = 1/(4/3) = 3/4.
A physics student finds that for a projectile launched at angle θ, csc θ = 2. What is sin θ?
sin θ = 1/csc θ = 1/2, so θ = 30°.
A pilot needs sec θ for a navigation formula. If the aircraft climbs at angle θ where cos θ = √3/2, what is sec θ?
sec θ = 1/cos θ = 1/(√3/2) = 2/√3 = 2√3/3.
A satellite dish is tilted at angle θ. Measurements show sec θ = √2. What is the tilt angle in degrees?
sec θ = √2 → cos θ = 1/√2 = √2/2, so θ = 45°.
An architect finds that sec θ = 5/4 for a roof angle. What is tan θ for this roof?
sec θ = 5/4 → cos θ = 4/5 → sin θ = 3/5. tan θ = sin/cos = (3/5)/(4/5) = 3/4.

--- Lesson 2.3: Graphs of Sine & Cosine (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphs of Sine & Cosine
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A buoy bobs up and down following y = 3 sin(πt/6). What is the maximum height above equilibrium?
The amplitude is 3, so the maximum displacement from equilibrium is 3.
A Ferris wheel's height is modeled by h(t) = −20 cos(πt/15) + 22. What is the period of the ride in seconds?
Period = 2π/B = 2π/(π/15) = 30 seconds.
A spring oscillates according to y = 5 cos(4t). What is the amplitude and period?
Amplitude = 5. Period = 2π/4 = π/2.
A temperature over 24 hours is modeled by T(t) = 10 sin(πt/12 − π/2) + 65. What is the average temperature?
The average (midline) is the vertical shift D = 65°F.
Ocean tides are modeled by y = 4 cos(π/6 · t) + 6, where y is in feet and t in hours. What is the period of the tidal cycle?
Period = 2π/(π/6) = 12 hours.
A piston in an engine moves according to y = 6 sin(100πt + π/4). What is the phase shift?
Phase shift = −C/B = −(π/4)/(100π) = −1/400, meaning a shift of 1/400 to the left.
Daylight hours in a city are modeled by D(t) = 2.5 sin(2π/365 · (t − 80)) + 12. What is the maximum number of daylight hours?
Maximum = midline + amplitude = 12 + 2.5 = 14.5 hours.
A vibrating guitar string is modeled by y = 0.003 sin(880πt). What is the frequency of the vibration in Hz?
Period = 2π/(880π) = 1/440 s. Frequency = 1/period = 440 Hz.
Real-World Applications

The average (midline) is the vertical shift D = 65°F.

--- Lesson 2.6: Symmetry & Periodicity (Summary) ---

Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
