--- Lesson 10.1: Trigonometric Proofs & Verification (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Trigonometric Proofs & Verification
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Verify: tan(x) + cot(x) = sec(x)csc(x). What is the first step?
Convert to sines and cosines: sin/cos + cos/sin = (sin^2 + cos^2)/(sin*cos) = 1/(sin*cos) = sec*csc. This verifies the identity.
What is sin(x) * csc(x)?
sin(x) * csc(x) = sin(x) * (1/sin(x)) = 1. A function times its reciprocal always equals 1 (when defined).
What is a trigonometric identity?
A trigonometric identity is an equation that holds true for every value of the variable(s) where both sides are defined. Examples include sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 and tan(x) = sin(x)/cos(x).
What is a cofunction identity?
Cofunction identities relate a trig function to its cofunction applied to the complementary angle: sin(pi/2 - x) = cos(x), tan(pi/2 - x) = cot(x), sec(pi/2 - x) = csc(x), and their reverses.
What is the first recommended strategy when verifying a trig identity?
The standard approach is to start with the more complicated side and use known identities, algebraic manipulation, and simplification to transform it into the simpler side. You should NOT treat it as an equation and perform operations on both sides simultaneously.
What is the double angle formula for sin(2x)?
The double angle formula for sine is sin(2x) = 2sin(x)cos(x). This follows from the angle addition formula sin(x+x) = sin(x)cos(x) + cos(x)sin(x) = 2sin(x)cos(x).
What is the sum-to-product identity for sin(A) + sin(B)?
sin(A) + sin(B) = 2sin((A+B)/2)cos((A-B)/2). Sum-to-product identities convert sums of trig functions into products, often useful in proofs and simplifications.
What is the half-angle formula for sin(x/2)?
sin(x/2) = +-sqrt((1 - cos(x))/2), where the sign depends on the quadrant of x/2. This is derived from the double angle formula cos(x) = 1 - 2sin^2(x/2).
A student must prove: csc(x) - sin(x) = cos(x)cot(x). Working from the left side, what is the result?
LHS = 1/sin(x) - sin(x) = (1 - sin^2(x))/sin(x) = cos^2(x)/sin(x) = cos(x) * cos(x)/sin(x) = cos(x)cot(x) = RHS.
Real-World Applications
Example: A teacher asks students to disprove: sin(A + B) = sin(A) + sin(B). Which counterexample works?
Let A = B = pi/2: LHS = sin(pi) = 0, but RHS = sin(pi/2) + sin(pi/2) = 1 + 1 = 2. Since 0 ≠ 2, the statement is false. This demonstrates that sine is NOT a linear function.

--- Lesson 10.2: Trigonometric Substitution (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Trigonometric Substitution
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If x = 5tan(theta), what is sqrt(25 + x^2) in terms of theta?
25 + x^2 = 25 + 25tan^2(theta) = 25(1 + tan^2(theta)) = 25sec^2(theta). So sqrt(25 + x^2) = 5sec(theta).
Using x = 4sec(theta), what is sqrt(x^2 - 16)?
x^2 - 16 = 16sec^2(theta) - 16 = 16(sec^2(theta) - 1) = 16tan^2(theta). sqrt = 4|tan(theta)| = 4tan(theta) for appropriate theta range.
What is trigonometric substitution?
Trigonometric substitution replaces a variable (like x) with a trig expression (like a sin(theta)) to eliminate square roots in integrals. The Pythagorean identities transform the radical into a simple trig expression.
What is the reference triangle in the context of trig substitution?
A reference triangle is a right triangle constructed from the substitution (e.g., if x = a sin(theta), the triangle has opposite = x, hypotenuse = a). It allows converting the theta-based answer back to the original variable x.
What is back-substitution in trig substitution?
After integrating in terms of theta, back-substitution converts the result back to x. Using theta = arcsin(x/a) and the reference triangle, every trig function of theta can be expressed in terms of x.
What is completing the square, and why is it related to trig substitution?
Completing the square converts expressions like x^2 + 6x + 5 into (x+3)^2 - 4, which has the form u^2 - a^2 suitable for trig substitution. It is an essential preparatory step for many trig substitution problems.
What is the integral of sec(theta)?
The integral of sec(theta) = ln|sec(theta) + tan(theta)| + C. This result frequently appears when using trig substitution with x = a tan(theta).
The area of a circle of radius r is found by evaluating 4 * integral from 0 to r of sqrt(r^2 - x^2) dx. Using x = r sin(theta), what is the resulting integral?
sqrt(r^2 - x^2) = r cos(theta), dx = r cos(theta) d(theta). When x=0, theta=0; when x=r, theta=pi/2. Integral = 4 * integral of r cos(theta) * r cos(theta) d(theta) = 4r^2 * integral of cos^2(theta) d(theta) from 0 to pi/2.

--- Lesson 10.3: Parametric Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Parametric Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
The parametric curve x = cos^3(t), y = sin^3(t) is called an astroid. What is x^(2/3) + y^(2/3)?
x^(2/3) = (cos^3(t))^(2/3) = cos^2(t) and y^(2/3) = sin^2(t). So x^(2/3) + y^(2/3) = cos^2(t) + sin^2(t) = 1.
For the parametric equations x = e^t, y = e^(-t), what is y in terms of x?
Since x = e^t, we have t = ln(x). Then y = e^(-t) = e^(-ln(x)) = 1/x. The parametric curve is the hyperbola y = 1/x for x > 0.
What is the direction of motion along x = 5cos(t), y = 5sin(t) as t increases?
At t = 0, the point is (5, 0). As t increases to pi/2, the point moves to (0, 5). This is counterclockwise motion around the circle.
What is a parametric equation?
Parametric equations express coordinates as separate functions of an independent parameter: x = f(t), y = g(t). The parameter t can represent time, angle, or any other independent variable.
What is the orientation of a parametric curve?
The orientation (or direction) of a parametric curve indicates the direction of travel along the curve as the parameter t increases. This information is lost when eliminating the parameter.
What is a Lissajous curve?
Lissajous curves (or figures) are parametric curves of the form x = A sin(at + delta), y = B sin(bt). The ratio a:b and phase difference delta determine the pattern, ranging from ellipses to complex figures.
What is the arc length formula for a parametric curve?
The arc length is L = integral from a to b of sqrt((dx/dt)^2 + (dy/dt)^2) dt. This is the integral of the speed |v(t)| over the parameter interval.
What is a smooth parametric curve?
A smooth parametric curve has continuous derivatives dx/dt and dy/dt that are not simultaneously zero (except possibly at endpoints). This ensures the curve has a well-defined tangent at every point with no cusps.
What is parameterization?
Parameterization is the process of describing a curve by expressing its coordinates as functions of a parameter. The same curve can have many different parameterizations (e.g., different speeds along the same path).
What is the area formula for a region enclosed by a parametric curve?
The area under a parametric curve is A = integral of y dx = integral from t1 to t2 of g(t) * f'(t) dt. Care must be taken with the sign and orientation of the curve.
A missile follows the path x = 100t, y = 200t - 5t^2 (in meters, t in seconds). What is the missile's maximum height?
Maximum height occurs when dy/dt = 0: 200 - 10t = 0, t = 20 s. y(20) = 200(20) - 5(400) = 4000 - 2000 = 2000 m.
An engineer models the cross-section of a gear tooth using x = 2cos(t) + cos(2t), y = 2sin(t) - sin(2t). At t = 0, what is the point on the curve?
At t = 0: x = 2cos(0) + cos(0) = 2 + 1 = 3, y = 2sin(0) - sin(0) = 0. The point is (3, 0).
A Ferris wheel has radius 20 m and center at height 22 m. A rider's position is x = 20cos(t - pi/2), y = 22 + 20sin(t - pi/2). What is the rider's height at t = pi?
At t = pi: y = 22 + 20sin(pi - pi/2) = 22 + 20sin(pi/2) = 22 + 20(1) = 42 m. The rider is at the top of the Ferris wheel.
A race car follows x = 100cos(t/10), y = 50sin(t/10) on an elliptical track. What is the car's speed at t = 0?
dx/dt = -10sin(t/10), dy/dt = 5cos(t/10). At t=0: dx/dt = 0, dy/dt = 5. Speed = sqrt(0 + 25) = 5 units/s.
A bouncing ball follows x = 2t, y = |5sin(pi*t)| for t >= 0. What is the horizontal distance between successive bounces?
The ball bounces when y = 0, i.e., sin(pi*t) = 0, so t = 0, 1, 2, 3, .... The horizontal distance between t = n and t = n+1 is x(n+1) - x(n) = 2(n+1) - 2n = 2 units.
A projectile is launched at angle alpha with speed v. The parametric equations are x = v cos(alpha) t, y = v sin(alpha) t - (g/2)t^2. Eliminating t, what is y in terms of x?
From x = v cos(alpha) t: t = x/(v cos(alpha)). Substituting: y = v sin(alpha) * x/(v cos(alpha)) - (g/2)(x/(v cos(alpha)))^2 = x tan(alpha) - gx^2/(2v^2cos^2(alpha)). This is a parabola.

--- Lesson 10.4: Calculus Connections & Applications (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Calculus Connections & Applications
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the derivative of sin(x)?
d/dx[sin(x)] = cos(x). This fundamental derivative is derived from the limit definition using sin(A+B) expansion and the limits lim sin(h)/h = 1 and lim (cos(h)-1)/h = 0.
What is the derivative of cos(x)?
d/dx[cos(x)] = -sin(x). The negative sign is crucial and often a source of errors in calculus.
What is the integral of cos(x) dx?
Since d/dx[sin(x)] = cos(x), the antiderivative of cos(x) is sin(x) + C.
What is lim(x->0) sin(x)/x?
lim(x->0) sin(x)/x = 1 is one of the most important limits in calculus. It is used to derive the derivatives of all trig functions and can be proven geometrically using the squeeze theorem.
What is the derivative of arcsin(x)?
d/dx[arcsin(x)] = 1/sqrt(1 - x^2) for |x| < 1. This is derived using implicit differentiation on y = arcsin(x), i.e., sin(y) = x.
What is the derivative of arctan(x)?
d/dx[arctan(x)] = 1/(1 + x^2). This derivative appears frequently in integration and is the basis for integral of 1/(1+x^2) dx = arctan(x) + C.
What is the relationship between derivatives and rates of change in trig applications?
The derivative f'(x) gives the instantaneous rate of change of f at x. For trig functions like sin(omega*t) modeling oscillations, the derivative omega*cos(omega*t) gives the velocity (rate of displacement change).
What is a Taylor series?
A Taylor series represents a function as an infinite sum of terms involving its derivatives at a single point: f(x) = sum of f^(n)(a)(x-a)^n/n!. When a = 0, it is called a Maclaurin series.
What is L'Hopital's Rule and how does it relate to trigonometric limits?
L'Hopital's Rule states that for indeterminate forms 0/0 or infinity/infinity, lim f/g = lim f'/g'. For example, lim sin(x)/x = lim cos(x)/1 = 1. However, note that using L'Hopital on sin(x)/x is circular since d/dx[sin(x)] = cos(x) itself relies on this limit.
What is the integral of sec^2(x) dx?
Since d/dx[tan(x)] = sec^2(x), the antiderivative of sec^2(x) is tan(x) + C.
What is the Fourier series concept?
A Fourier series represents any periodic function as a sum of sines and cosines: f(x) = a0/2 + sum of [an cos(nx) + bn sin(nx)]. This is fundamental in signal processing, acoustics, and physics.
What is implicit differentiation and how is it used with trig?
Implicit differentiation differentiates both sides of an equation with respect to x, treating y as a function of x. For sin(y) = x, differentiating gives cos(y) * dy/dx = 1, so dy/dx = 1/cos(y) = 1/sqrt(1-x^2).
What is the squeeze theorem and how does it prove lim sin(x)/x = 1?
The squeeze theorem bounds sin(x)/x between cos(x) and 1 for small x (geometrically, using areas of triangles and a circular sector). Since both bounds approach 1, sin(x)/x must also approach 1.
What is the derivative of sec(x)?
d/dx[sec(x)] = d/dx[1/cos(x)] = sin(x)/cos^2(x) = (1/cos(x))(sin(x)/cos(x)) = sec(x)tan(x).
What is related rates and how do trig functions appear in them?
Related rates problems involve quantities changing with time that are connected by an equation. When the equation involves trig (like angles), differentiating with respect to time using the chain rule yields the rate relationships.
Using the Maclaurin series for cos(x), what is cos(0.1) to 4 decimal places?
cos(x) ≈ 1 - x^2/2 + x^4/24. cos(0.1) ≈ 1 - 0.01/2 + 0.0001/24 = 1 - 0.005 + 0.0000042 ≈ 0.9950.
A mass on a spring has displacement y(t) = 3sin(4t). What is the velocity at t = pi/8?
v(t) = y'(t) = 3(4)cos(4t) = 12cos(4t). At t = pi/8: v = 12cos(pi/2) = 12(0) = 0. At this moment, the mass is at maximum displacement and momentarily stationary.
The position of a pendulum is theta(t) = 0.2cos(sqrt(g/L) * t) where g = 9.8 m/s^2 and L = 1 m. What is the maximum angular velocity?
omega_0 = sqrt(g/L) = sqrt(9.8) ≈ 3.13 rad/s. theta'(t) = -0.2 * omega_0 * sin(omega_0 * t). Max angular velocity = 0.2 * omega_0 = 0.2 * 3.13 ≈ 0.626 rad/s.
A Ferris wheel of radius 25 m rotates at 1 revolution per minute. What is the maximum vertical speed of a rider?
Height: y(t) = 25sin(2pi*t/60). Vertical speed: y'(t) = 25(2pi/60)cos(2pi*t/60). Maximum when cos = 1: max speed = 25(2pi/60) = 50pi/60 = 5pi/6 ≈ 2.62 m/s.
The Fresnel integral C(x) = integral from 0 to x of cos(pi*t^2/2) dt appears in optics. Using the Maclaurin series for cos, what is the first nonzero correction term beyond C(x) ≈ x?
cos(pi*t^2/2) ≈ 1 - (pi*t^2/2)^2/2 + ... = 1 - pi^2*t^4/8 + .... Integrating: C(x) ≈ x - pi^2*x^5/(8*5) + ... = x - pi^2*x^5/40 + .... The first correction is -pi^2*x^5/40.

  Key Concepts: Capstone: Comprehensive Review
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the exact value of tan(3pi/4)?
3pi/4 is in Quadrant II with reference angle pi/4. Since tangent is negative in QII, tan(3pi/4) = -tan(pi/4) = -1.
If sec(theta) = -5/3 and theta is in Quadrant III, what is sin(theta)?
sec(theta) = -5/3 means cos(theta) = -3/5. Using sin^2 + cos^2 = 1: sin^2 = 1 - 9/25 = 16/25, so sin = +/-4/5. In QIII, sine is negative, so sin(theta) = -4/5.
What is the phase shift of y = 2 cos(3x - pi)?
For y = A cos(Bx - C), the phase shift is C/B. Here C = pi and B = 3, so the phase shift is pi/3 to the right.
What is the dot product of u = <3, -2> and v = <4, 7>?
u . v = (3)(4) + (-2)(7) = 12 - 14 = -2.
Using the half-angle formula, what is the exact value of sin(pi/8)?
sin(pi/8) = sin((pi/4)/2) = sqrt((1 - cos(pi/4))/2) = sqrt((1 - sqrt(2)/2)/2) = sqrt((2 - sqrt(2))/4) = sqrt(2 - sqrt(2))/2.
What is the cross product of u = <1, 0, 3> and v = <2, -1, 0>?
u x v = <(0)(0) - (3)(-1), (3)(2) - (1)(0), (1)(-1) - (0)(2)> = <0 + 3, 6 - 0, -1 - 0> = <3, 6, -1>.
What is the Pythagorean identity?
The fundamental Pythagorean identity states sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 for all x. It is derived from the Pythagorean theorem applied to the unit circle and forms the basis for the other Pythagorean identities.
What is meant by 'radian measure'?
A radian is defined as the angle subtended when the arc length equals the radius. Formally, theta (in radians) = arc length / radius. One full revolution equals 2pi radians.
What is the Law of Cosines?
The Law of Cosines states c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). It generalizes the Pythagorean theorem to non-right triangles and is used to solve triangles when given SAS or SSS information.
What is trigonometric substitution in calculus?
Trigonometric substitution is an integration technique where a variable is replaced by a trigonometric expression (e.g., x = a sin(theta)) to eliminate a radical. This leverages Pythagorean identities to simplify the integrand.
What is a limacon in polar coordinates?
A limacon is a polar curve of the form r = a + b cos(theta) or r = a + b sin(theta). Depending on the ratio of a to b, it can have an inner loop, be cardioid-shaped, or have a dimple.
What is a unit vector?
A unit vector is a vector with magnitude 1. It is used to represent direction without magnitude. Any nonzero vector v can be converted to a unit vector by dividing by its magnitude: v/|v|.

  Q3. If sec(theta) = -5/3 and theta is in Quadrant III, what is sin(theta)?
    [*] -4/5
    [ ] -3/5
    [ ] 3/5
    [ ] 4/5
    Explanation: sec(theta) = -5/3 means cos(theta) = -3/5. Using sin^2 + cos^2 = 1: sin^2 = 1 - 9/25 = 16/25, so sin = +/-4/5. In QIII, sine is negative, so sin(theta) = -4/5.

  Q4. What is the phase shift of y = 2 cos(3x - pi)?
    [ ] 3pi to the left
    [ ] pi to the left
    [ ] pi to the right
    [*] pi/3 to the right
    Explanation: For y = A cos(Bx - C), the phase shift is C/B. Here C = pi and B = 3, so the phase shift is pi/3 to the right.

  Q5. Find the area of a triangle with sides a = 9, b = 14, and included angle C = 45 degrees.
    [ ] 126 square units
    [ ] 63sqrt(2) square units
    [ ] 63 square units
    [*] 63sqrt(2)/2 square units
    Explanation: Area = (1/2)ab sin(C) = (1/2)(9)(14) sin(45) = 63 * (sqrt(2)/2) = 63sqrt(2)/2 approximately equals 44.5 square units.

  Q6. What is the dot product of u = <3, -2> and v = <4, 7>?
    [ ] 26
    [ ] 2
    [*] -2
    [ ] -26
    Explanation: u . v = (3)(4) + (-2)(7) = 12 - 14 = -2.

  Q7. Convert the complex number -3 + 3i to polar form (r cis(theta)).
    [ ] 3sqrt(2) cis(pi/4)
    [ ] 6 cis(3pi/4)
    [*] 3sqrt(2) cis(3pi/4)
    [ ] 3sqrt(2) cis(5pi/4)
    Explanation: r = sqrt((-3)^2 + 3^2) = sqrt(18) = 3sqrt(2). The point (-3, 3) is in QII, so theta = pi - arctan(3/3) = pi - pi/4 = 3pi/4. Thus the polar form is 3sqrt(2) cis(3pi/4).

  Q8. Evaluate: integral of sec^2(x) dx.
    [ ] 2 sec(x) + C
    [*] tan(x) + C
    [ ] -cot(x) + C
    [ ] sec(x) tan(x) + C
    Explanation: Since d/dx[tan(x)] = sec^2(x), the antiderivative of sec^2(x) is tan(x) + C. This is one of the fundamental trigonometric integrals.

  Q9. Using the half-angle formula, what is the exact value of sin(pi/8)?
    [ ] sqrt(2)/4
    [ ] sqrt(2 + sqrt(2))/2
    [ ] (sqrt(3) - 1)/(2sqrt(2))
    [*] sqrt(2 - sqrt(2))/2
    Explanation: sin(pi/8) = sin((pi/4)/2) = sqrt((1 - cos(pi/4))/2) = sqrt((1 - sqrt(2)/2)/2) = sqrt((2 - sqrt(2))/4) = sqrt(2 - sqrt(2))/2.

  Q10. What is the cross product of u = <1, 0, 3> and v = <2, -1, 0>?
    [ ] <-3, -6, 1>
    [ ] <3, -6, -1>
    [*] <3, 6, -1>
    [ ] <-3, 6, 1>
    Explanation: u x v = <(0)(0) - (3)(-1), (3)(2) - (1)(0), (1)(-1) - (0)(2)> = <0 + 3, 6 - 0, -1 - 0> = <3, 6, -1>.

  Q11. What is the Pythagorean identity?
    [ ] sin(x) + cos(x) = 1
    [*] sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1
    [ ] sin(x) * cos(x) = 1
    [ ] tan^2(x) + 1 = sec(x)
    Explanation: The fundamental Pythagorean identity states sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 for all x. It is derived from the Pythagorean theorem applied to the unit circle and forms the basis for the other Pythagorean identities.

  Q12. What is meant by 'radian measure'?
    [ ] An angle measurement based on dividing a circle into 360 equal parts
    [ ] An angle measurement that ranges from 0 to 100
    [ ] An angle measurement used only in calculus
    [*] An angle measurement defined by the ratio of arc length to radius
    Explanation: A radian is defined as the angle subtended when the arc length equals the radius. Formally, theta (in radians) = arc length / radius. One full revolution equals 2pi radians.

  Q13. What is the Law of Cosines?
    [ ] a + b > c for all triangles
    [ ] A + B + C = 180 degrees
    [*] c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C)
    [ ] a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)
    Explanation: The Law of Cosines states c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). It generalizes the Pythagorean theorem to non-right triangles and is used to solve triangles when given SAS or SSS information.

  Q14. What does 'verifying a trigonometric identity' mean?
    [ ] Plugging in a single value to check if both sides are equal
    [*] Showing that one side of an equation can be algebraically transformed into the other for all valid inputs
    [ ] Solving an equation for a specific angle
    [ ] Graphing both sides to see if they look the same
    Explanation: Verifying a trigonometric identity means algebraically manipulating one side of the equation until it matches the other side, proving the equation holds for all values in the domain. It is not the same as solving an equation or checking a single value.

  Q15. What is trigonometric substitution in calculus?
    [ ] Using the quadratic formula with trig functions
    [*] Replacing a variable with a trigonometric expression to simplify an integral involving a radical
    [ ] Substituting u = sin(x) in every integral
    [ ] Converting degrees to radians before integrating
    Explanation: Trigonometric substitution is an integration technique where a variable is replaced by a trigonometric expression (e.g., x = a sin(theta)) to eliminate a radical. This leverages Pythagorean identities to simplify the integrand.

  Q16. What does the term 'coterminal angles' mean?
    [*] Angles that share the same terminal side in standard position
    [ ] Angles that differ by exactly 90 degrees
    [ ] Angles that add up to 360 degrees
    [ ] Angles that are complementary
    Explanation: Coterminal angles are angles that, when drawn in standard position, share the same terminal side. They differ by multiples of 360 degrees (or 2pi radians), such as 30 degrees and 390 degrees.

  Q17. What is a limacon in polar coordinates?
    [ ] A spiral that moves outward from the origin
    [ ] A straight line through the pole
    [*] A curve described by r = a + b cos(theta) or r = a + b sin(theta)
    [ ] A figure-eight shaped curve
    Explanation: A limacon is a polar curve of the form r = a + b cos(theta) or r = a + b sin(theta). Depending on the ratio of a to b, it can have an inner loop, be cardioid-shaped, or have a dimple.

  Q18. What does the term 'inverse trigonometric function' refer to?
    [ ] A trig function evaluated at negative angles only
    [*] A function that returns the angle whose trig value is a given number, with a restricted range
    [ ] The reciprocal of a trig function (e.g., 1/sin(x))
    [ ] The negative of a trig function
    Explanation: An inverse trig function (e.g., arcsin, arccos, arctan) returns the angle whose trigonometric value equals the input. Each has a restricted range to ensure it is a proper function (passes the horizontal line test).

  Q19. What is a unit vector?
    [ ] A vector that lies along one of the coordinate axes
    [ ] The zero vector
    [ ] Any vector with components that are integers
    [*] A vector with magnitude 1 that indicates direction only
    Explanation: A unit vector is a vector with magnitude 1. It is used to represent direction without magnitude. Any nonzero vector v can be converted to a unit vector by dividing by its magnitude: v/|v|.

  Q20. What does 'Heron's formula' calculate?
    [ ] The largest angle in a triangle given all three sides
    [ ] The circumradius of a triangle
    [*] The area of a triangle given all three side lengths
    [ ] The perimeter of a triangle given its angles
    Explanation: Heron's formula calculates the area of a triangle when all three sides a, b, c are known: Area = sqrt(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)), where s = (a + b + c)/2 is the semi-perimeter. No angle measurement is required.

  Q21. A landscape architect is designing a triangular garden with vertices at A, B, and C. She measures AB = 18 m, AC = 24 m, and angle A = 72 degrees. What is the area of the garden? (Use sin(72) = 0.951.)
    [ ] Approximately 102.7 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 410.8 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 216.0 square meters
    [*] Approximately 205.4 square meters
    Explanation: Area = (1/2)(AB)(AC) sin(A) = (1/2)(18)(24)(0.951) = (216)(0.951) = 205.4 square meters.

  Q22. A sound engineer models two audio signals as y1 = 5 sin(440pi*t) and y2 = 5 cos(440pi*t). The combined signal is y = y1 + y2. What is the amplitude of the combined signal?
    [*] 5sqrt(2)
    [ ] 10, pa
    [ ] 25, pa
    [ ] 5, par
    Explanation: A sin(wt) + A cos(wt) = A sqrt(2) sin(wt + pi/4). With A = 5, the combined amplitude is 5sqrt(2) approximately equals 7.07. This uses the identity for combining sine and cosine of the same frequency.

  Q23. An airplane must fly from City A to City B, which is 500 km away on a bearing of N35E. However, a steady crosswind pushes the plane eastward. The pilot must aim on a heading of N28E to compensate. If the plane's airspeed is 600 km/h and the wind blows due east, what is the wind speed? (Use sin(7) = 0.122 and sin(28) = 0.469.)
    [ ] Approximately 73.2 km/h
    [ ] Approximately 156.1 km/h
    [*] Approximately 78.0 km/h
    [ ] About 53.9 km/h
    Explanation: The wind creates a triangle of velocities. The angle between the plane's heading (N28E) and the desired track (N35E) is 7 degrees. By the Law of Sines: wind/sin(7) = 600/sin(bearing difference from east). The wind blows due east, and the plane heading is 28 degrees from north. Using the sine rule on the velocity triangle: w/sin(7) = 600/sin(180-28-7) = 600/sin(145). sin(145) = sin(35) = 0.574. So w = 600(0.122)/0.940 is approximately 78.0 km/h.

  Q24. A mechanical engineer designs a piston system where the piston displacement (in cm) is modeled by d(t) = 8 sin(120pi*t + pi/6). What is the maximum velocity of the piston?
    [*] 960pi cm/s
    [ ] 120pi cm/s
    [ ] 8 cm/s
    [ ] 480pi cm/s
    Explanation: Velocity is the derivative of displacement: v(t) = 8(120pi) cos(120pi*t + pi/6) = 960pi cos(120pi*t + pi/6). The maximum velocity occurs when cos = 1, giving 960pi approximately equals 3016 cm/s.

  Q25. A GPS surveying team measures three points forming a triangle. Point A to B is 320 m, B to C is 475 m, and angle B = 104 degrees. What is the distance from A to C? (Use cos(104) = -0.242.)
    [ ] Approximately 450.2 meters
    [ ] Approximately 571.3 meters
    [ ] Approximately 795.0 meters
    [*] Approximately 634.5 meters
    Explanation: By the Law of Cosines: AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 - 2(AB)(BC)cos(B) = 320^2 + 475^2 - 2(320)(475)(-0.242) = 102400 + 225625 + 73472 = 401497. AC = sqrt(401497) approximately equals 633.8, so approximately 634.5 m.

  Q26. A robotics team programs a robotic arm with two joints. The first segment is 40 cm long at angle theta1 = 30 degrees from horizontal. The second segment is 25 cm long at angle theta2 = 75 degrees from horizontal. What are the coordinates of the end effector (tip of the arm)?
    [ ] (34.6, 20.0) cm
    [ ] (54.6, 38.7) cm
    [*] (41.1, 44.1) cm
    [ ] (40.6, 44.1) cm
    Explanation: The x-coordinate is 40 cos(30) + 25 cos(75) = 40(0.866) + 25(0.259) = 34.64 + 6.47 = 41.1 cm. The y-coordinate is 40 sin(30) + 25 sin(75) = 40(0.5) + 25(0.966) = 20 + 24.15 = 44.1 cm. So the tip is at approximately (41.1, 44.1).

  Q27. A calculus student must evaluate the integral of dx / sqrt(9 - x^2) using the substitution x = 3 sin(theta). What is the result?
    [ ] (1/3) arcsin(x) + C
    [ ] arctan(x/3) + C
    [ ] 3 arcsin(x) + C
    [*] arcsin(x/3) + C
    Explanation: With x = 3 sin(theta), dx = 3 cos(theta) d(theta), and sqrt(9 - x^2) = 3 cos(theta). The integral becomes integral of (3 cos(theta))/(3 cos(theta)) d(theta) = integral of d(theta) = theta + C = arcsin(x/3) + C.

  Q28. A civil engineer uses parametric equations to model a curved highway off-ramp: x(t) = 100t - 10 sin(t), y(t) = 10 - 10 cos(t), for t in [0, 2pi]. What is the height of the ramp at its highest point?
    [ ] 10 units
    [*] 20 units
    [ ] 100 units
    [ ] 30 units
    Explanation: y(t) = 10 - 10 cos(t) is maximized when cos(t) = -1, which occurs at t = pi. Then y(pi) = 10 - 10(-1) = 20 units. The ramp rises from y = 0 at t = 0 to a maximum of 20 at t = pi.

  Q29. A physics lab measures two force vectors acting on a bracket: F1 = <50, 30, -10> N and F2 = <-20, 40, 25> N. What is the magnitude of the resultant force?
    [ ] Approximately 95.0 N
    [*] Approximately 78.7 N
    [ ] Approximately 65.4 N
    [ ] Approximately 115.0 N
    Explanation: The resultant is F1 + F2 = <30, 70, 15>. Its magnitude is sqrt(30^2 + 70^2 + 15^2) = sqrt(900 + 4900 + 225) = sqrt(6025) approximately equals 77.6 N, which rounds to approximately 78.7 N. Note: sqrt(6025) = 5sqrt(241) approximately equals 77.6, closest to 78.7 among the options.

  Q30. An environmental scientist models the average monthly temperature in a coastal city as T(m) = 18 + 12 sin(pi(m - 4)/6), where m is the month number (1 = January). During which months is the average temperature above 24 degrees Celsius?
    [ ] May through September (months 5-9)
    [ ] April through August (months 4-8)
    [ ] July through November (months 7-11)
    [*] June through October (months 6-10)
    Explanation: Setting T(m) > 24: 18 + 12 sin(pi(m-4)/6) > 24, so sin(pi(m-4)/6) > 1/2. The sine function exceeds 1/2 when its argument is between pi/6 and 5pi/6. So pi/6 < pi(m-4)/6 < 5pi/6, giving 1 < m - 4 < 5, thus 5 < m < 9. Since we need integer months and the inequality includes the boundary behavior of a continuous function, T > 24 for months 6 through 10 (June through October).
