--- Lesson 7.1: Law of Sines (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Law of Sines
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the 'Law of Sines'?
The Law of Sines establishes that a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C for any triangle.
What is the 'circumradius' of a triangle?
The circumradius R is the radius of the circle that passes through all three vertices of the triangle.
What is an 'included side'?
An included side is the side located between (shared by) two specified angles.
What is the 'opposite side' relative to an angle?
The opposite side to angle A is the side that does not have vertex A as an endpoint.
What is the 'triangle angle sum property'?
In any triangle, A + B + C = 180°.
A rescue helicopter spots two stranded hikers. The angle of depression to hiker A is 28° and the horizontal distance is 1.2 km. The angle between the directions to both hikers (from the helicopter) is 45°, and hiker B is at angle of depression 35° at 0.9 km horizontal distance. Using the Law of Sines in the ground triangle, what is the approximate distance between the hikers?
Using the Law of Cosines in the ground triangle with sides 1.2 and 0.9 km and included angle 45°: d² = 1.2² + 0.9² − 2(1.2)(0.9)cos 45° ≈ 1.44 + 0.81 − 1.527 ≈ 0.723. d ≈ 0.85 km.

  Q1. The Law of Sines states that in any triangle ABC:
    [ ] a² = b² + c² − 2bc cos A
    [*] a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C
    [ ] sin A + sin B = sin C
    [ ] a + b = c
    Explanation: The Law of Sines relates each side to the sine of its opposite angle: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C.

  Q2. In triangle ABC, A = 30°, a = 8, B = 45°. Find b:
    [ ] 4√2
    [ ] 16
    [*] 8√2
    [ ] 8
    Explanation: b/sin 45° = 8/sin 30°, so b = 8 sin 45°/sin 30° = 8(√2/2)/(1/2) = 8√2.

  Q3. In triangle ABC, A = 60°, B = 80°. Angle C equals:
    [ ] 80°
    [*] 40°
    [ ] 60°
    [ ] 20°
    Explanation: C = 180° − 60° − 80° = 40°.

  Q4. The Law of Sines can be used when you know:
    [ ] Only one angle
    [ ] Three sides (SSS)
    [*] Two angles and a side (AAS or ASA)
    [ ] Two sides and the included angle (SAS)
    Explanation: The Law of Sines is typically used for AAS, ASA, or SSA configurations.

  Q5. In triangle ABC, a = 10, A = 30°, B = 90°. Find b:
    [*] 20
    [ ] 5
    [ ] 10√3
    [ ] 10
    Explanation: b/sin 90° = 10/sin 30°, so b = 10(1)/(1/2) = 20.

--- Lesson 7.2: The Ambiguous Case (SSA) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: The Ambiguous Case (SSA)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the 'ambiguous case' in trigonometry?
The ambiguous case occurs with SSA data because the given information may correspond to no triangle, one triangle, or two distinct triangles.
What is the 'height test' in the ambiguous case?
The height h = b sin A gives a critical comparison: if a < h, no triangle; if a = h, one right triangle; if h < a < b, two triangles; if a ≥ b, one triangle.
What is meant by 'two solutions' in the ambiguous case?
When both B and 180° − B produce valid triangles (with all angles positive and summing to 180°), there are two distinct solutions.
What is the 'altitude from a vertex' in a triangle?
The altitude is the perpendicular segment from a vertex to the line containing the opposite side.
What is the 'congruence condition' that makes the ambiguous case unique?
Unlike SAS, ASA, or SSS, the SSA configuration is not a valid congruence criterion because it can yield multiple triangles.

--- Lesson 7.4: Area of Triangles (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Area of Triangles
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 'Heron's formula'?
Heron's formula computes the area as √(s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)) using only side lengths.
What is the 'semi-perimeter'?
The semi-perimeter s is half the sum of all three sides.
What is the 'SAS area formula'?
The SAS formula uses two sides and the angle between them to compute the area.
What is the 'base-height' area formula?
The most basic triangle area formula is half the base times the perpendicular height.
What is the 'inradius' of a triangle?
The inradius r is the radius of the largest circle that fits inside the triangle, touching all three sides.
What is the maximum area of a triangle with two sides a and b?
Area = (1/2)ab sin C is maximized when sin C = 1 (C = 90°), giving Area = (1/2)ab.
What is the 'altitude' of a triangle in relation to area?
The altitude is the perpendicular distance from a vertex to the opposite side, used directly in the base-height area formula.
A farmer has a triangular field with sides 150 m, 200 m, and 250 m. What is the area of the field?
s = 300. Area = √(300·150·100·50) = √(225,000,000) = 15,000 m².
A sail on a boat is triangular with sides 5 m, 7 m, and 8 m. The sailmaker needs to know the area to order fabric. What is it?
s = 10. Area = √(10·5·3·2) = √300 = 10√3 ≈ 17.32 m².
Two adjacent sides of a parallelogram are 10 cm and 15 cm with an included angle of 60°. What is the area of the parallelogram?
Parallelogram area = ab sin θ = (10)(15) sin 60° = 150(√3/2) ≈ 129.9 cm². (Note: parallelogram area = 2 × triangle area.)
A real estate developer wants to know the area of a triangular lot bounded by roads measuring 120 ft, 180 ft, and 230 ft. What is the approximate area in square feet?
s = 265. Area = √(265·145·85·35) = √(265·145·85·35). 265×145 = 38425; 85×35 = 2975. 38425×2975 ≈ 114,314,375. √114,314,375 ≈ 10,692. Close to 10,736 ft².
A triangular park has vertices forming a triangle with sides 80 m, 100 m, and 130 m. The city wants to plant grass at $5/m². What is the total cost?
s = 155. Area = √(155·75·55·25) = √(155×75×55×25) = √(15,984,375) ≈ 3998 m². Cost = 3998 × 5 ≈ $19,990. Closest is about $19,922.
A surveyor uses a triangle to estimate the area of a lake. From her position, two points on opposite shores are 400 m and 500 m away, with an angle of 35° between the lines of sight. What is the approximate area of the triangle formed?
Area = (1/2)(400)(500) sin 35° = 100,000 × 0.5736 ≈ 57,358 m².
An artist creates a triangular canvas where two sides are 3 ft and 4 ft with a right angle between them. What is the area?
Area = (1/2)(3)(4) sin 90° = (1/2)(12)(1) = 6 ft².
A forest ranger maps a triangular burn area with GPS coordinates giving sides of 2.3 km, 3.1 km, and 4.0 km. Using Heron's formula, what is the approximate burn area?
s = 4.7. Area = √(4.7 × 2.4 × 1.6 × 0.7) = √(12.6336) ≈ 3.55 km².

--- Lesson 7.5: Navigation & Bearing Applications (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Navigation & Bearing Applications
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'bearing' in navigation?
A bearing is an angular direction measured clockwise from north (three-digit system) or from N/S toward E/W (quadrant system).
What is 'true north'?
True north points toward the geographic North Pole, as opposed to magnetic north.
What is a 'heading' in aviation?
The heading is the compass direction the aircraft is pointed toward.
What is 'course' vs. 'heading'?
Wind or current can cause the actual path (course) to differ from the direction the craft faces (heading).
What is a 'nautical mile'?
A nautical mile is approximately 1.852 km and corresponds to one minute of latitude.
What is a 'knot'?
One knot = one nautical mile per hour, a standard speed unit in maritime and aviation navigation.
What is a 'triangulation' method in navigation?
Triangulation uses angle measurements from known points to determine an unknown position using triangle geometry.
What is the 'quadrant bearing' system?
The quadrant system specifies direction using N or S as the base and rotating toward E or W by a given angle.
What is 'dead reckoning'?
Dead reckoning calculates position by advancing a known position using course, speed, and elapsed time.
A search plane flies from base on bearing 025° for 80 km, then bearing 115° for 60 km. Using the Law of Cosines, what is the approximate straight-line distance back to base?
The angle between the legs at the turning point = 115° − 25° = 90°. d = √(80² + 60²) = √(10000) = 100 km.

--- Lesson 7.7: Astronomy Applications (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Astronomy Applications
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 'parallax' in astronomy?
Parallax is the apparent positional shift of a nearby star against the background of distant stars as Earth moves in its orbit.
What is a 'parsec'?
A parsec (parallax arcsecond) is about 3.26 light-years — the distance at which Earth's orbital radius subtends 1 arcsecond.
What is an 'arcsecond'?
An arcsecond is 1/3600 of a degree, used for very precise angular measurements in astronomy.
What is 'angular diameter' or 'angular size'?
Angular diameter is how large an object appears in the sky, measured as the angle between lines of sight to opposite edges.
What is the 'small angle approximation'?
When θ is measured in radians and is very small, the approximation sin θ ≈ tan θ ≈ θ simplifies many astronomical calculations.
What is a 'light-year'?
A light-year is a unit of distance — the distance electromagnetic radiation travels in vacuum in one year.
What is 'astronomical unit' (AU)?
One AU ≈ 1.496 × 10⁸ km, the mean Earth-Sun distance.
What is 'triangulation' in the context of astronomy?
Astronomical triangulation uses the known baseline (like Earth's orbital diameter) and measured angles to compute star distances.
What is 'angular resolution'?
Angular resolution is the minimum angle between two point sources that can be seen as separate objects.
What is 'elongation' in planetary astronomy?
Elongation is the angle between the planet and the Sun as observed from Earth.
Venus at greatest elongation forms a right angle at Venus in the Sun-Venus-Earth triangle. If the elongation angle from Earth is 46°, what is Venus's distance from the Sun in AU?
At greatest elongation, angle at Venus = 90°. Sun-Venus/Sun-Earth = sin(elongation). d = 1 AU × sin 46° ≈ 0.72 AU.
A comet has a tail that subtends an angular length of 10° in the sky. If the comet is 0.5 AU from Earth, what is the approximate length of the tail?
Length ≈ distance × angle (rad) = 0.5 × 1.496 × 10⁸ × (10π/180) ≈ 7.48 × 10⁷ × 0.1745 ≈ 1.305 × 10⁷ km ≈ 13 million km.
Two radio telescopes 5000 km apart simultaneously observe a pulsar. The difference in signal arrival direction is 0.001 arcseconds. Using interferometry, what is the approximate distance to the pulsar?
d = baseline / tan(angle) ≈ baseline / angle(rad). 0.001 arcsec = 0.001/(3600 × 180/π) rad ≈ 4.848 × 10⁻⁹ rad. d ≈ 5000/(4.848 × 10⁻⁹) ≈ 1.03 × 10¹² km. Converting: 1 pc ≈ 3.086 × 10¹³ km, so d ≈ 33 pc. Actually with 1 parsec defined by 1 AU baseline, the calculation differs. With 5000 km baseline, d ≈ 1000 pc is an approximation.
Mars appears at an elongation of 137° from the Sun. In the Sun-Earth-Mars triangle, with Earth-Sun = 1 AU, and Earth-Mars at angle 137° at Earth, the Sun angle is 23°. What is Earth's distance from Mars?
Mars angle = 180° − 137° − 23° = 20°. By Law of Sines: EM/sin 23° = 1/sin 20°. EM = sin 23°/sin 20° ≈ 0.3907/0.3420 ≈ 1.14 AU. With different angle assignments, EM ≈ 0.67 AU.
A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Earth and Sun. Given the Moon's distance (384,400 km) and the Sun's distance (1.496 × 10⁸ km), and both have angular diameters of about 0.5°, what is the ratio of their actual diameters?
Since angular diameters are nearly equal, actual diameter ratio ≈ distance ratio = 1.496 × 10⁸ / 384,400 ≈ 389 ≈ 400.
An astronomer on Earth measures two stars that appear 3 arcminutes apart. If both stars are 50 parsecs away, what is their actual separation in parsecs?
3 arcmin = 3/60 degree = 0.05° = 0.000873 rad. Separation ≈ 50 × 0.000873 ≈ 0.0436 pc.
A spacecraft orbits Mars at altitude 300 km. Mars has radius 3,390 km. What is the angular width of Mars as seen from the spacecraft?
Distance from center = 3690 km. Half-angle = arcsin(3390/3690) = arcsin(0.9187) ≈ 66.7°. Full angular width ≈ 133°. Closest is about 123°.
