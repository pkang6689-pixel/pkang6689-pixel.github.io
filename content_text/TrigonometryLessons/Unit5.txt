--- Lesson 5.1: Sum & Difference Formulas for Sine & Cosine (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Sum & Difference Formulas for Sine & Cosine
Sum & Difference FormulasSine  • sin(A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B  • sin(A − B) = sin A cos B − cos A sin BCosine  • cos(A + B) = cos A cos B − sin A sin B  • cos(A − B) = cos A cos B + sin A sin B
These formulas let you compute exact values for non-standard angles: e.g., sin 75° = sin(45° + 30°).

--- Lesson 5.2: Double-Angle Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Double-Angle Formulas
Double-Angle Formulas
Setting B = A in the sum formulas:
  • sin 2A = 2 sin A cos A  • cos 2A = cos²A − sin²A = 2cos²A − 1 = 1 − 2sin²A  • tan 2A = 2 tan A / (1 − tan²A)
There are three equivalent forms for cos 2A, chosen based on what's most convenient.

--- Lesson 5.3: Half-Angle Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Half-Angle Formulas
Half-Angle Formulas
Derived from the double-angle cosine formulas by replacing A with A/2:
  • sin(A/2) = ±√((1 − cos A)/2)  • cos(A/2) = ±√((1 + cos A)/2)  • tan(A/2) = sin A/(1 + cos A) = (1 − cos A)/sin A
The ± sign depends on the quadrant of A/2.

--- Lesson 5.4: Product-to-Sum & Sum-to-Product Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Product-to-Sum & Sum-to-Product Formulas
Product-to-Sum & Sum-to-Product FormulasProduct-to-Sum  • sin A cos B = ½[sin(A+B) + sin(A−B)]  • cos A sin B = ½[sin(A+B) − sin(A−B)]  • cos A cos B = ½[cos(A−B) + cos(A+B)]  • sin A sin B = ½[cos(A−B) − cos(A+B)]Sum-to-Product  • sin A + sin B = 2 sin((A+B)/2) cos((A−B)/2)  • sin A − sin B = 2 cos((A+B)/2) sin((A−B)/2)  • cos A + cos B = 2 cos((A+B)/2) cos((A−B)/2)  • cos A − cos B = −2 sin((A+B)/2) sin((A−B)/2)

--- Lesson 5.5: Simplifying Expressions with These Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Simplifying Expressions with These Formulas
Simplifying Expressions with Sum, Double, & Half-Angle Formulas
Combine all the identities learned so far to simplify complex expressions.
Common Simplifications  • sin²x = (1 − cos 2x)/2 (power reduction).  • cos²x = (1 + cos 2x)/2.  • sin x cos x = sin(2x)/2.  • Products of different-frequency trig → sums via product-to-sum.

--- Lesson 5.6: Engineering Cases (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Engineering Cases
Engineering Applications of Sum/Double/Half-Angle FormulasElectrical Engineering  • AC power: P(t) = V₀I₀ sin(ωt) sin(ωt+φ). Use product-to-sum → P = (V₀I₀/2)[cos φ − cos(2ωt+φ)].  • Average power = V₀I₀ cos φ / 2 (the constant term; the oscillating term averages to 0).Mechanical Vibrations
Two vibrations at close frequencies create beats: sin ω₁t + sin ω₂t = 2 cos((ω₁−ω₂)t/2) sin((ω₁+ω₂)t/2).
Signal Processing
AM radio multiplies a carrier by a signal; frequency shifting uses product-to-sum conversions.

AP Calculus Connections

Integration  • ∫ sin²x dx uses power reduction: (1 − cos 2x)/2.  • ∫ sin mx cos nx dx uses product-to-sum.  • ∫ sec²x dx = tan x + C (from tan²x + 1 = sec²x).Limits & Derivatives  • lim(x→0) sin x / x = 1 (used with double-angle rearrangements).  • d/dx[sin 2x] = 2 cos 2x (chain rule + double angle).
