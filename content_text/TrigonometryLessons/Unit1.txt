--- Lesson 1.1: Angles in Degrees & Radians (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angles in Degrees & Radians
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the radian measure of a straight angle?
A straight angle is 180° = π radians.
A satellite dish must be rotated 150° to align with a new satellite. What is this angle in radians?
150° × (π/180) = 5π/6 radians.
A skateboard ramp is designed with an incline angle of π/9 radians. What is this angle in degrees?
π/9 × (180/π) = 20°.
An engineer programs a motor to turn 7π/3 radians. How many complete revolutions does the motor make, and what is the remaining angle in radians?
7π/3 = 2π + π/3, so 1 full revolution (2π) with π/3 radians remaining.

--- Lesson 1.2: Arc Length & Sector Area (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arc Length & Sector Area
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
For a circle with r = 10, what is the arc length for a full rotation?
Full rotation: θ = 2π, s = 10 · 2π = 20π.
If the arc length is 6 and the radius is 3, what is θ in radians?
θ = s/r = 6/3 = 2 radians.
A sector has area 12 and radius 4. What is θ?
A = ½r²θ → 12 = ½(16)θ → 12 = 8θ → θ = 3/2 radians.
A pizza has radius 12 inches. A single slice has a central angle of 45°. What is the area of one slice?
45° = π/4. A = ½(144)(π/4) = 18π in².
A circular garden has radius 5 m. A gardener fences off a sector with arc length 10 m. What is the area of the fenced sector?
θ = s/r = 10/5 = 2 rad. Area = ½r²θ = ½(25)(2) = 25 m².

--- Lesson 1.3: Right Triangle Ratios (SOH-CAH-TOA) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Right Triangle Ratios (SOH-CAH-TOA)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is tan θ if opposite = 3 and adjacent = 4?
tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 3/4.
If sin θ = 0.5 in a right triangle, what is θ?
sin 30° = 0.5.
If sin θ = 5/13, what is the opposite side when hypotenuse = 13?
sin θ = opp/hyp → opp = 13 · 5/13 = 5.
A ramp rises 3 feet over a horizontal distance of 4 feet. What is sin θ for the angle of incline?
The hypotenuse = √(3² + 4²) = 5. sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 3/5.
An airplane is flying at an altitude of 5000 feet. The pilot sees a runway at an angle of depression where cos θ = 12/13. What is the horizontal distance to the runway?
If cos θ = 12/13, and the triangle has sides 5-12-13 (scaled), the altitude 5000 maps to the side 5, so the scale factor is 1000. Horizontal distance = 12 × 1000 = 12,000 ft.
A ship sails 40 km due east, then 30 km due north. What is the tangent of the angle between the ship's final position and due east, measured from the starting point?
tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 30/40 = 3/4.
A firefighter's ladder is 20 m long and reaches 16 m up a building. What is cos θ, where θ is the angle between the ladder and the ground?
Adjacent = √(20² − 16²) = √(400 − 256) = √144 = 12. cos θ = 12/20 = 3/5.
A zip-line cable is 130 m long and drops 50 m in elevation from start to finish. What is sin θ for the angle the cable makes with the horizontal?
sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 50/130 = 5/13.

--- Lesson 1.5: Unit Circle Basics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Unit Circle Basics
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the radius of the unit circle?
The unit circle has radius 1.
What is cos(π)?
cos 180° = −1.
What is sin(3π/2)?
sin 270° = −1.
What is sin²θ + cos²θ for any point on the unit circle?
The Pythagorean identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.
What is cos(2π)?
cos 360° = cos 0° = 1 (full rotation back to start).
A robotic arm rotates to the angle 2π/3 on the unit circle. What is the x-coordinate of its tip?
At 2π/3 (120°), the reference angle is π/3 in Q II. cos(2π/3) = −1/2.
A Ferris wheel is modeled with the unit circle. A rider is at the 7π/6 position. What is the sine of this angle (the rider's vertical coordinate)?
7π/6 is in Q III with reference angle π/6. sin(7π/6) = −sin(π/6) = −1/2.
A radar system tracks an object on a circular path. At angle 11π/6, what is the cosine of the object's position?
11π/6 is in Q IV with reference angle π/6. cos(11π/6) = cos(π/6) = √3/2.
A physics student models a pendulum at angle 5π/3 on the unit circle. What is sin(5π/3)?
5π/3 is in Q IV with reference angle π/3. sin(5π/3) = −sin(π/3) = −√3/2.
Real-World Applications
Example: A robotic arm rotates to the angle 2π/3 on the unit circle. What is the x-coordinate of its tip?
At 2π/3 (120°), the reference angle is π/3 in Q II. cos(2π/3) = −1/2.

From 3 o'clock (0 rad) to 9 o'clock is π radians (180°). The point is (cos π, sin π) = (−1, 0).

13π/4 − 2π = 13π/4 − 8π/4 = 5π/4.

135° is in Q II. Reference angle = 180° − 135° = 45°.

--- Lesson 1.7: Applications in Geometry (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications in Geometry
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Two buildings are 50 m apart. From the top of the shorter building, the angle of elevation to the top of the taller building is 30°. If the shorter building is 20 m tall, what is the height of the taller building?
tan 30° = Δh/50 → Δh = 50 tan 30° = 50/√3. Total height = 20 + 50/√3 m.
A park has a triangular garden with two sides measuring 10 m and 14 m and an included angle of 90°. What is the area of the garden?
Area = ½ × base × height = ½ × 10 × 14 = 70 m².
A road rises 5 m for every 100 m of horizontal distance. Using trigonometry, what is the angle of incline (approximately)?
tan θ = 5/100 = 0.05 → θ = arctan(0.05) ≈ 2.86° ≈ 2.9°.
An airplane at altitude 1000 m spots a landmark on the ground at an angle of depression of 30°. What is the horizontal distance to the landmark?
tan 30° = 1000/d → d = 1000/tan 30° = 1000/(1/√3) = 1000√3 m.
A triangular plot of land has a base of 80 m and a height found by using trigonometry from an adjacent side of 50 m at a 30° angle. What is the height?
Height = 50 × sin 30° = 50 × 0.5 = 25 m.
