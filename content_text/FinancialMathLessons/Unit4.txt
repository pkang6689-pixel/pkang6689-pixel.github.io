--- Lesson 4.1: Stocks, Bonds, & Mutual Funds (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Stocks, Bonds, & Mutual Funds
Core Investment TypesStocks  • Shares of ownership in a company.  • Returns: dividends + capital gains (price increase).  • Higher risk, higher potential return.Bonds  • Loans to a government or corporation.  • Returns: regular interest (coupon) payments + face value at maturity.  • Lower risk, lower return.Mutual Funds & ETFs  • Pooled investments holding many stocks/bonds.  • Instant diversification; managed (mutual funds) or passive (index ETFs).

--- Lesson 4.2: Risk vs. Return (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Risk vs. Return
Risk vs. ReturnCore Principle
Higher potential returns come with higher risk. The key is finding the right balance for your situation.
Risk Spectrum  • Low risk: Savings accounts, CDs, T-bills (1–5% return).  • Medium risk: Bonds, balanced funds (4–8% return).  • High risk: Stocks, real estate, crypto (8–12%+ return, with volatility).Measuring Risk
Standard deviation measures how much returns vary from the average.

--- Lesson 4.3: Annuities & Pension Calculations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Annuities & Pension Calculations
AnnuitiesFuture Value of an Annuity
FV = PMT × [(1+r)^n − 1] / r

Calculates what regular payments grow to over time.
Present Value of an Annuity
PV = PMT × [1 − (1+r)^(−n)] / r

Calculates the lump sum value of a stream of future payments.
Example
$500/month at 7%/12 for 30 years: FV = 500 × [(1.00583)^360 − 1]/0.00583 ≈ $566,765

Retirement Account PlanningAccount Types  • 401(k)/403(b): Employer-sponsored; pre-tax contributions; employer match = free money.  • Traditional IRA: Tax-deductible contributions; taxed on withdrawal.  • Roth IRA: After-tax contributions; tax-FREE growth and withdrawals.Key Numbers (2024)  • 401(k) max: $23,000/year ($30,500 if 50+).  • IRA max: $7,000/year ($8,000 if 50+).  • Full Social Security age: 67 for those born after 1960.

--- Lesson 4.5: Long-Term Investment Strategies (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Long-Term Investment Strategies
Long-Term Investment StrategiesCore Strategies  • Buy and hold: Invest consistently; ignore short-term fluctuations.  • Dollar-cost averaging: Fixed amount invested regularly captures market dips.  • Rebalancing: Periodically adjust portfolio to maintain target allocation.  • Tax-loss harvesting: Sell losers to offset gains and reduce tax burden.
Key insight: Time in the market beats timing the market.

--- Lesson 4.6: Diversification & Portfolio Theory (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Diversification & Portfolio Theory
Diversification & Portfolio TheoryCore Concepts  • Diversification: Spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk without sacrificing return.  • Correlation: How assets move relative to each other. Low or negative correlation provides the best diversification.  • Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT): Optimal portfolios maximize return for a given level of risk.
