--- Lesson 7.1: Starting a Business (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Starting a Business
Starting a BusinessBusiness Formation Basics  • Sole Proprietorship: Simplest; owner = business. Unlimited personal liability.  • Partnership: Two+ owners share profits, losses, and liability.  • LLC: Limited liability protection; pass-through taxation. Most popular for small businesses.  • Corporation: Separate legal entity. C-Corp: double taxation. S-Corp: pass-through.Start-up Costs
One-time costs (equipment, licenses, legal fees) vs. recurring costs (rent, utilities, payroll).

--- Lesson 7.2: Profit & Loss Analysis (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Profit & Loss Analysis
Profit & Loss (Income Statement)Key Components  • Revenue: Total income from sales.  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): Direct costs of producing what was sold.  • Gross Profit: Revenue − COGS.  • Operating Expenses: Rent, utilities, marketing, salaries (not directly tied to production).  • Net Profit: Revenue − COGS − Operating Expenses − Taxes.

--- Lesson 7.3: Break-Even Analysis (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Break-Even Analysis
Break-Even AnalysisThe Break-Even Point
Break-even (units) = Fixed Costs ÷ (Price per Unit − Variable Cost per Unit)

Break-even (revenue) = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin Ratio
Graphical Representation
Total Revenue line crosses Total Cost line at the break-even point. Below = loss; above = profit.

--- Lesson 7.4: Small Business Financing (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Small Business Financing
Small Business FinancingFunding Sources  • Bootstrapping: Self-funding from savings, revenue reinvestment.  • Bank loans: Traditional debt with interest; requires collateral and good credit.  • SBA loans: Government-backed; easier qualification, favorable terms.  • Angel investors: Wealthy individuals investing for equity stake.  • Venture capital: Firms investing large sums for significant equity in high-growth startups.  • Crowdfunding: Small amounts from many people (Kickstarter, GoFundMe).

Entrepreneurship Case StudiesCase 1: Food Truck Startup
Start-up: $85,000 (truck $50K, equipment $20K, licenses/permits $5K, initial inventory $10K). Monthly fixed costs: $3,000 (parking fees, insurance, loan payment). Variable cost: $4/meal. Selling price: $12/meal. CM: $8/meal. BEP: 375 meals/month.

--- Lesson 7.6: Marketing & Finance (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Marketing & Finance
Marketing & FinanceMarketing Budget & ROI
Marketing budget is typically 5-10% of revenue for established businesses, 15-20% for startups.
Key Marketing Metrics  • CAC: Customer Acquisition Cost = Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers.  • LTV: Customer Lifetime Value = Avg Purchase × Frequency × Customer Lifespan.  • ROAS: Return on Ad Spend = Revenue from Ads ÷ Cost of Ads.  • Conversion Rate: Purchasers ÷ Visitors × 100.

--- Lesson 7.7: Business Risk Management (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Business Risk Management
Business Risk ManagementTypes of Business Risk  • Market risk: Demand changes, competition, economic downturns.  • Financial risk: Cash flow problems, excessive debt, interest rate changes.  • Operational risk: Supply chain disruptions, equipment failure, employee issues.  • Legal/Regulatory risk: Lawsuits, regulation changes, compliance failures.
