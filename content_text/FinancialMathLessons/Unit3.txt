--- Lesson 3.1: Loan Terminology & Concepts (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Loan Terminology & Concepts
Loan Fundamentals  • Principal: Amount borrowed.  • Interest rate: Cost of borrowing, expressed as APR (Annual Percentage Rate).  • Term: Loan duration (e.g., 30-year mortgage, 5-year auto loan).  • Collateral: Asset pledged to secure a loan (home for mortgage, car for auto).  • Secured vs. unsecured: Secured has collateral (lower rate); unsecured does not (higher rate).Key Concepts  • APR: Total annual cost of borrowing including fees.  • Down payment: Upfront portion paid to reduce principal borrowed.  • Amortization: Gradual repayment through scheduled payments.

--- Lesson 3.2: Amortization Schedules (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Amortization Schedules
Amortization Schedules
An amortization schedule shows the breakdown of each payment into principal and interest.
Monthly Payment Formula
PMT = P × r(1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n − 1]
  • P = loan principal  • r = monthly rate (annual rate ÷ 12)  • n = total number of monthly payments
Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal.

--- Lesson 3.3: Mortgage Calculations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Mortgage Calculations
Mortgage CalculationsKey Numbers  • Home price − down payment = loan amount  • Total monthly cost = PITI (Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance)  • Affordability rule: Housing should be ≤ 28% of gross monthly income.Example
Home: $350,000. Down: $70,000 (20%). Loan: $280,000 at 6.5% for 30 years.

Monthly P&I: ~$1,770. Taxes: ~$365. Insurance: ~$150. Total PITI: ~$2,285.

--- Lesson 3.4: Credit Card Mathematics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Credit Card Mathematics
Credit Card MathematicsKey Features  • APR: Typical credit card APR ranges from 15–25%.  • Daily rate: APR ÷ 365 (used for daily balance calculations).  • Minimum payment: Often 1–3% of balance or $25 (whichever is greater).  • Grace period: ~25 days to pay in full before interest accrues on purchases.
Example: $5,000 balance at 20% APR with minimum payments only → takes ~25 years and costs ~$8,400 in interest!

--- Lesson 3.5: Student Loan Analysis (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Student Loan Analysis
Student Loan AnalysisTypes  • Federal subsidized: Government pays interest while in school.  • Federal unsubsidized: Interest accrues while in school.  • Private loans: From banks/lenders; usually higher rates, fewer protections.Repayment Plans  • Standard (10-year): Fixed payments, least total interest.  • Income-driven: Payments based on income (IBR, PAYE, REPAYE).  • Extended (25-year): Lower payments but more total interest.

Case Studies in Debt ManagementCase 1: Debt Avalanche

--- Lesson 3.8: Loan Comparison & Decision-Making (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Loan Comparison & Decision-Making
Loan Comparison & Decision-MakingComparing Loan Offers  • Compare total cost: Not just monthly payment — calculate total payments over life of loan.  • APR vs. interest rate: APR includes fees; same interest rate can have different APRs.  • Opportunity cost: Could the money earn more invested than the interest saved?Decision Framework
1. Calculate total cost of each option. 2. Consider flexibility and risks. 3. Account for opportunity costs. 4. Choose based on complete analysis.
