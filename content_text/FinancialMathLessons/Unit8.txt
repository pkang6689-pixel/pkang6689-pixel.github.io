--- Lesson 8.1: Stock Market Basics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Stock Market Basics
Stock Market BasicsWhat Is a Stock?
A stock represents fractional ownership of a company. Shareholders earn returns through price appreciation and dividends.
Major Exchanges  • NYSE: New York Stock Exchange — largest by market capitalization.  • NASDAQ: Technology-heavy electronic exchange.  • Market capitalization = Share Price × Shares Outstanding.

--- Lesson 8.2: Reading Financial Tables (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Reading Financial Tables
Reading Financial Tables & StatementsStock Quote Components  • Ticker: Abbreviated symbol (AAPL, MSFT).  • Open/Close: Price at market open/close.  • High/Low: Highest/lowest price during the trading day.  • Volume: Number of shares traded.  • P/E Ratio: Price ÷ Earnings Per Share — valuation metric.

--- Lesson 8.3: Market Indices & Benchmarks (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Market Indices & Benchmarks
Market Indices & BenchmarksMajor US Indices  • S&P 500: 500 large US companies — the most-used US market benchmark.  • Dow Jones (DJIA): 30 large industrial companies — price-weighted.  • NASDAQ Composite: All NASDAQ-listed stocks — tech-heavy.  • Russell 2000: 2,000 small-cap companies.Index Weighting
Market-cap weighted (S&P 500): larger companies have more influence. Price-weighted (DJIA): higher-priced stocks have more influence.

The SPIVA scorecard shows ~90% of active funds underperform their benchmark over 20 years, making low-cost index funds the better choice for most investors.

--- Lesson 8.4: Trading Strategies (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Trading Strategies
Trading & Investment StrategiesApproaches to the Market  • Buy and Hold: Long-term ownership; ignoring short-term fluctuations.  • Value Investing: Buying undervalued stocks (low P/E, below book value).  • Growth Investing: Buying companies with high earnings growth potential.  • Index Investing: Passive; match the market through index funds.  • Dollar Cost Averaging: Fixed amount at regular intervals.

--- Lesson 8.5: Global Finance & Currency (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Global Finance & Currency
Global Finance & CurrencyForeign Exchange (Forex)
Currencies trade in pairs (USD/EUR). Exchange rates fluctuate based on supply/demand, interest rates, and economics.
Key Concepts  • Exchange rate: Price of one currency in terms of another.  • Appreciation/Depreciation: Currency gaining/losing value relative to another.  • Purchasing Power Parity: Theory that exchange rates should equalize prices across countries.

--- Lesson 8.8: Behavioral Finance (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Behavioral Finance
Behavioral FinanceCommon Cognitive Biases  • Loss aversion: Pain of loss > pleasure of equal gain (2:1 ratio).  • Confirmation bias: Seeking info that supports existing beliefs.  • Anchoring: Over-relying on first piece of information (purchase price).  • Overconfidence: Overestimating one's ability to pick stocks or time markets.  • Recency bias: Overweighting recent events in predictions.

--- Lesson 8.8: Behavioral Finance (Quiz) ---

  Q1. Loss aversion means the pain of losing $100 feels equivalent to the pleasure of gaining approximately:
    [ ] $100
    [ ] $150

    Explanation: 2:1 pain ratio.

  Q2. Loss aversion leads investors to:
    [ ] Sell winners too soon
