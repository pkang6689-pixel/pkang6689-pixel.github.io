--- Lesson 6.1: Understanding Income Tax (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Understanding Income Tax
Income Tax FundamentalsHow Income Tax Works  • Gross income: All taxable income (wages, interest, dividends, etc.).  • Adjusted Gross Income (AGI): Gross income minus above-the-line deductions.  • Taxable income: AGI minus standard/itemized deductions and exemptions.  • Tax owed: Calculated by applying tax brackets to taxable income.
The US uses a progressive tax system — higher income is taxed at higher rates.

    [ ] All expenses
    [ ] Gross income

    [ ] Your total tax liability from the previous year
    Explanation: Taxable income = AGI minus deductions. You choose the larger of the standard deduction or your itemized deductions (mortgage interest, state taxes, charitable giving). Most filers use the standard deduction because it exceeds their itemizable expenses.

  Q6. A person earning $50,000 with $14,600 standard deduction has taxable income of:
    [ ] $50,000
    [*] $35,400
    [ ] $14,600
    [ ] $64,600
    Explanation: $50,000 − $14,600 = $35,400. The standard deduction reduces your taxable income before tax rates are applied. Many people confuse gross income with taxable income — the deduction makes a significant difference in actual tax owed.

--- Lesson 6.3: Property, Sales, & Other Taxes (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Property, Sales, & Other Taxes
Property, Sales, & Other TaxesProperty Tax
Based on assessed value of property. Rate varies by location (0.3% to 2.5%+).
Sales Tax
Percentage added to purchases. Varies by state (0% to 7.25%+) plus local additions.
Other Taxes  • Estate/Inheritance tax: On transfers at death (federal exemption: $13.6M in 2024).  • Excise tax: On specific goods (gas, alcohol, tobacco).  • Payroll tax: FICA (Social Security + Medicare).

--- Lesson 6.4: Types of Insurance (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Types of Insurance
Types of InsuranceCore Insurance Types  • Health insurance: Covers medical expenses (deductible, copay, coinsurance, out-of-pocket max).  • Auto insurance: Liability, collision, comprehensive.  • Homeowner's/Renter's: Property damage, liability, personal belongings.  • Life insurance: Term (pure protection) vs. Whole (protection + cash value).  • Disability insurance: Replaces income if you can't work.

--- Lesson 6.5: Risk Management Basics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Risk Management Basics
Risk ManagementRisk Management Strategies  • Avoid: Don't do the risky activity (don't own a pool).  • Reduce: Take precautions (smoke detectors, safe driving).  • Transfer: Insurance shifts financial risk to the insurer.  • Retain: Accept the risk and self-insure (high deductible, emergency fund).Expected Value
E(X) = Probability × Impact. Used to evaluate whether insurance is worthwhile.
