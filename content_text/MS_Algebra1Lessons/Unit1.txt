--- Lesson 1.1: Measures of Center (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Measures of Center
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the mean of the data set {2, 4, 6, 8, 10}?
The mean is the sum divided by the count: (2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10) / 5 = 30 / 5 = 6.
What is the median of {3, 7, 1, 9, 5}?
First order the data: {1, 3, 5, 7, 9}. The middle value in a set of 5 numbers is the 3rd value, which is 5.
What is the mean of {10, 20, 30, 40}?
The mean is (10 + 20 + 30 + 40) / 4 = 100 / 4 = 25.
What is the median of {12, 15, 18, 22, 25, 30}?
With an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle numbers: (18 + 22) / 2 = 40 / 2 = 20.
A data set has a sum of 72 and contains 9 values. What is the mean?
Mean = sum / count = 72 / 9 = 8.
What is the mode of {5, 5, 3, 3, 7}?
Both 5 and 3 appear twice, which is more than any other value. When two values share the highest frequency, the data set is bimodal.
What is the median of {100, 200, 300}?
With 3 values already in order, the median is the middle (2nd) value, which is 200.
The mean of five numbers is 12. What is the sum of those five numbers?
Since mean = sum / count, then sum = mean x count = 12 x 5 = 60.
What is the mean of a data set?
The mean (or arithmetic average) is calculated by adding all the values together and dividing by the total number of values.
What is the mode of a data set?
The mode is the value that appears most frequently in a data set. A data set can have no mode, one mode, or multiple modes.
What is an 'outlier' in the context of measures of center?
An outlier is a data value that is significantly different from the other values in the data set, and it can strongly affect the mean.
What is the 'arithmetic average'?
The arithmetic average is simply another name for the mean, calculated by summing all values and dividing by the count.
A teacher recorded test scores for 7 students: 85, 90, 78, 92, 88, 76, 95. She wants to report the typical score to the principal. What is the mean score?
The sum is 85 + 90 + 78 + 92 + 88 + 76 + 95 = 604. The mean is 604 / 7 ≈ 86.29.
A basketball player scored the following points in 6 games: 14, 22, 18, 30, 22, 16. What is the mode of her scores?
The score 22 appears twice while all other scores appear only once, making 22 the mode.
A grocery store tracked the number of apples sold each day for a week: 45, 52, 38, 61, 55, 42, 50. What is the median number of apples sold?
Ordered: {38, 42, 45, 50, 52, 55, 61}. With 7 values, the median is the 4th value, which is 50.
A company pays its 5 employees the following annual salaries: $35,000, $40,000, $42,000, $38,000, and $200,000. Which measure of center best represents a typical salary, and what is its value?
The $200,000 salary is an outlier that pulls the mean up to $71,000. The median ($40,000) better represents a typical salary because it is resistant to outliers. Ordered: {35000, 38000, 40000, 42000, 200000}.
A farmer measures the weight (in kg) of 8 pumpkins: 4.5, 5.2, 6.1, 4.8, 5.5, 6.3, 5.0, 5.8. What is the median weight?
Ordered: {4.5, 4.8, 5.0, 5.2, 5.5, 5.8, 6.1, 6.3}. With 8 values, the median is the average of the 4th and 5th values: (5.2 + 5.5) / 2 = 10.7 / 2 = 5.35.
How to Calculate
Example: What is the mean of the data set {2, 4, 6, 8, 10}?
Solution: The mean is the sum divided by the count: (2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10) / 5 = 30 / 5 = 6.
Example: Find the mode of {4, 2, 4, 7, 4, 9, 2}.
Solution: The mode is the value that appears most often. The number 4 appears 3 times, more than any other value.
Example: What is the mean of {10, 20, 30, 40}?
Solution: The mean is (10 + 20 + 30 + 40) / 4 = 100 / 4 = 25.
Example: Find the mean of {0, 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30}.
Solution: The sum is 0 + 5 + 10 + 15 + 20 + 25 + 30 = 105, and 105 / 7 = 15.
Outliers and Resistant Measures
An outlier is a data value that is significantly different from the other values in the data set, and it can strongly affect the mean.
A measure that is resistant to outliers does not change significantly when an extreme value is added or removed. The median is resistant to outliers, while the mean is not.
Original mean: (25+30+28+35+27+31)/6 = 176/6 ≈ 29.33. New mean with 90 added: (176+90)/7 = 266/7 = 38. The outlier raised the mean from about 29.33 to 38.
Real-World Applications
Example: What is the 'arithmetic average'?
The arithmetic average is simply another name for the mean, calculated by summing all values and dividing by the count.
Example: A teacher recorded test scores for 7 students: 85, 90, 78, 92, 88, 76, 95. She wants to report the typical score to the principal. What is the mean score?
The sum is 85 + 90 + 78 + 92 + 88 + 76 + 95 = 604. The mean is 604 / 7 ≈ 86.29.
Example: A basketball player scored the following points in 6 games: 14, 22, 18, 30, 22, 16. What is the mode of her scores?
The score 22 appears twice while all other scores appear only once, making 22 the mode.
Example: A student's quiz scores are 88, 76, 92, 84, and one unknown score. If the student needs a mean of 85, what must the unknown score be?
For a mean of 85 with 5 scores, the sum must be 85 x 5 = 425. The known scores sum to 88 + 76 + 92 + 84 = 340. So the unknown score is 425 - 340 = 85.
Example: A shoe store tracks daily sales: Monday 12 pairs, Tuesday 15, Wednesday 12, Thursday 18, Friday 22, Saturday 30. The manager wants to use the measure of center that shows the most common daily sales figure. Which value should the manager report?
The mode is the most frequently occurring value. The value 12 appears twice (Monday and Wednesday), while all other values appear once, so the mode is 12.
Important Concepts

--- Lesson 1.2: Measures of Spread (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Measures of Spread
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the range of {2, 5, 8, 11, 20}?
The range is the difference between the maximum and minimum values: 20 - 2 = 18.
What is the interquartile range (IQR) of {1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13}?
Q1 is the median of {1, 3, 5} = 3 and Q3 is the median of {9, 11, 13} = 11. IQR = Q3 - Q1 = 11 - 3 = 8.
For the data set {4, 4, 4, 4, 4}, what is the range?
Since all values are the same, the range is 4 - 4 = 0, meaning there is no spread.
The five-number summary of a data set is: Min = 10, Q1 = 15, Median = 20, Q3 = 28, Max = 35. What is the IQR?
IQR = Q3 - Q1 = 28 - 15 = 13.
What is the range of a data set?
The range is the simplest measure of spread, calculated by subtracting the minimum value from the maximum value in the data set.
What is a 'quartile'?
Quartiles divide an ordered data set into four equal parts. Q1 is the 25th percentile, Q2 (the median) is the 50th, and Q3 is the 75th.
What is a 'five-number summary'?
The five-number summary consists of the minimum, first quartile (Q1), median (Q2), third quartile (Q3), and maximum, providing a concise overview of the data's distribution.
What is an 'absolute deviation'?
An absolute deviation is the absolute value of the difference between a data point and the mean, ensuring the distance is always positive.
A gym tracks the number of visitors each day for a week: 45, 52, 48, 60, 55, 43, 50. The manager needs to report the spread to the owner. What is the range of daily visitors?
Range = maximum - minimum = 60 - 43 = 17 visitors.
A teacher recorded quiz scores for 9 students: 65, 70, 72, 75, 78, 80, 85, 88, 95. She wants to describe the spread of the middle half of scores. What is the IQR?
Q1 is the median of {65, 70, 72, 75} = (70+72)/2 = 71. Q3 is the median of {80, 85, 88, 95} = (85+88)/2 = 86.5. IQR = 86.5 - 71 = 15.5, but using the standard method with the median included, Q1 = 71 and Q3 = 86.5 gives IQR = 15.5. Rounded: 15.
A student scored 70, 82, 78, 85, and 90 on five tests. Her teacher says the MAD for the class is 4.5 points. If the student's MAD is higher than the class, her scores are more spread out. What is the student's MAD?
Mean = (70+82+78+85+90)/5 = 405/5 = 81. Deviations: |70-81|=11, |82-81|=1, |78-81|=3, |85-81|=4, |90-81|=9. MAD = (11+1+3+4+9)/5 = 28/5 = 5.6, which is greater than 4.5.
A factory produces bolts that should be 10 cm long. A quality inspector measures 6 bolts: 9.8, 10.1, 10.0, 9.9, 10.2, 10.0. What is the range of the bolt lengths?
Range = maximum - minimum = 10.2 - 9.8 = 0.4 cm.
A school cafeteria tracked the number of lunches sold each day for two weeks (10 days): 120, 135, 128, 145, 130, 125, 140, 132, 138, 127. What is the IQR of lunches sold?
Ordered: {120, 125, 127, 128, 130, 132, 135, 138, 140, 145}. Q1 = median of {120,125,127,128,130} = 127. Q3 = median of {132,135,138,140,145} = 138. IQR = 138 - 127 = 11.
How to Calculate
Example: Find the range of {14, 22, 7, 35, 18}.
Solution: Range = maximum - minimum = 35 - 7 = 28.
Example: Find Q1 for the data set {2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16}.
Solution: The lower half is {2, 4, 6, 8}. Q1 is the median of the lower half: (4 + 6) / 2 = 5.
Example: Calculate the mean absolute deviation (MAD) of {2, 4, 6, 8, 10}.
Solution: Mean = 6. Absolute deviations: |2-6|=4, |4-6|=2, |6-6|=0, |8-6|=2, |10-6|=4. MAD = (4+2+0+2+4)/5 = 12/5 = 2.4.
Example: Find Q3 for the ordered data set {5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35}.
Solution: The upper half (values above the median of 20) is {25, 30, 35}. Q3 is the median of the upper half, which is 30.
Outliers and Resistant Measures
IQR = 26 - 12 = 14. Upper fence = Q3 + 1.5 x IQR = 26 + 1.5(14) = 26 + 21 = 47. Any value greater than 47 is an outlier.
Q1 = (7+8)/2 = 7.5, Q3 = (14+15)/2 = 14.5. IQR = 14.5 - 7.5 = 7. Upper fence = 14.5 + 1.5(7) = 14.5 + 10.5 = 25. Since 25 is equal to the fence, it is on the boundary; however, values at or above the fence are typically flagged as potential outliers.
Real-World Applications
Example: A gym tracks the number of visitors each day for a week: 45, 52, 48, 60, 55, 43, 50. The manager needs to report the spread to the owner. What is the range of daily visitors?
Range = maximum - minimum = 60 - 43 = 17 visitors.
Example: A teacher recorded quiz scores for 9 students: 65, 70, 72, 75, 78, 80, 85, 88, 95. She wants to describe the spread of the middle half of scores. What is the IQR?
Q1 is the median of {65, 70, 72, 75} = (70+72)/2 = 71. Q3 is the median of {80, 85, 88, 95} = (85+88)/2 = 86.5. IQR = 86.5 - 71 = 15.5, but using the standard method with the median included, Q1 = 71 and Q3 = 86.5 gives IQR = 15.5. Rounded: 15.
Example: A student scored 70, 82, 78, 85, and 90 on five tests. Her teacher says the MAD for the class is 4.5 points. If the student's MAD is higher than the class, her scores are more spread out. What is the student's MAD?
Mean = (70+82+78+85+90)/5 = 405/5 = 81. Deviations: |70-81|=11, |82-81|=1, |78-81|=3, |85-81|=4, |90-81|=9. MAD = (11+1+3+4+9)/5 = 28/5 = 5.6, which is greater than 4.5.

--- Lesson 1.5: Box Plots &amp; Dot Plots (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Box Plots &amp; Dot Plots
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A box plot has Q1 = 20, median = 30, and Q3 = 45. What is the IQR?
IQR = Q3 - Q1 = 45 - 20 = 25.
A box plot shows: Min = 5, Q1 = 12, Median = 18, Q3 = 24, Max = 32. What is the range?
Range = Max - Min = 32 - 5 = 27.
A box plot has Q1 = 40, Q3 = 60, and a point at 95 marked with a dot beyond the whisker. What is this point?
IQR = 60 - 40 = 20. Upper fence = 60 + 1.5(20) = 90. Since 95 > 90, it is plotted as an outlier beyond the whisker.
What is a 'box plot' (also called a box-and-whisker plot)?
A box plot (box-and-whisker plot) is a graphical display of the five-number summary: minimum, Q1, median, Q3, and maximum.
What is a 'dot plot'?
A dot plot displays each data value as a dot above a number line, with multiple dots stacked to show frequency.
A coach recorded the number of push-ups each player completed: 15, 18, 20, 22, 22, 25, 28, 30, 32, 35, 55. She creates a box plot. What is the five-number summary?
With 11 values ordered, the median (6th value) = 25. Q1 is the median of the lower 5 values {15,18,20,22,22} = 20. Q3 is the median of the upper 5 values {28,30,32,35,55} = 32.
A PE teacher tracks the number of laps students ran. The dot plot shows most students ran between 4 and 8 laps, with one student running 15 laps. She wants to report the median. If the ordered data is 4, 4, 5, 5, 6, 6, 7, 7, 8, 8, 15, what is the median?
With 11 values, the median is the 6th value. Counting from the ordered data: 4, 4, 5, 5, 6, 6, ... the 6th value is 6.
Outliers and Resistant Measures
On a box plot, outliers are shown as individual points beyond the whiskers, representing values that fall more than 1.5 times the IQR above Q3 or below Q1.
Q1 = 1, Q3 = 3, IQR = 2. Upper fence = 3 + 1.5(2) = 6. Since 8 > 6, the value 8 is an outlier.
Real-World Applications
Example: On a dot plot of test scores, there is a large cluster of dots between 80 and 90 and a single dot at 45. The dot at 45 is most likely a(n):
A score of 45 is far from the cluster of 80-90, making it an outlier—a data value significantly different from the rest.
Example: A city planner compares commute times (in minutes) for two bus routes using side-by-side box plots. Route A: Q1 = 15, Med = 20, Q3 = 30. Route B: Q1 = 18, Med = 22, Q3 = 25. Which route has more consistent travel times?
Route A IQR = 30 - 15 = 15 min. Route B IQR = 25 - 18 = 7 min. Route B's smaller IQR means more consistent travel times.
Example: A bookstore compares daily sales for fiction and non-fiction using side-by-side box plots. Fiction: Med = 25, IQR = 10. Non-fiction: Med = 18, IQR = 20. Which genre has higher and more consistent daily sales?
Fiction has a higher median (25 vs. 18) and a smaller IQR (10 vs. 20), meaning it has both higher typical sales and less variation.
Example: A teacher creates a dot plot for quiz scores: 5, 6, 6, 7, 7, 7, 8, 8, 8, 8, 9, 9, 10. She wants to describe the distribution shape based on the dot plot. What shape does this distribution have?
The dots build up symmetrically around 8 (the tallest stack with 4 dots), with frequencies decreasing on both sides, forming an approximately symmetric, unimodal shape.
Important Concepts
What does 'frequency' mean on a dot plot
On a dot plot, frequency is represented by the count of dots stacked above each value on the number line.
A teacher uses a dot plot to display students' shoe sizes: 6, 6, 7, 7, 7, 8, 8, 8, 8, 9, 9, 10. Which shoe size has the highest frequency
Size 8 appears 4 times (the most dots on the dot plot), making it the value with the highest frequency.

--- Lesson 1.6: Two-Way Tables (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Two-Way Tables
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In a two-way table: Boys who play soccer = 12, Boys who don't = 8, Girls who play soccer = 14, Girls who don't = 6. What is the row total for Girls?
Row total for Girls = girls who play soccer + girls who don't = 14 + 6 = 20.
Using the same table: Boys soccer = 12, Boys no soccer = 8, Girls soccer = 14, Girls no soccer = 6. What is the column total for 'Play Soccer'?
Column total for Play Soccer = boys who play + girls who play = 12 + 14 = 26.
In a two-way table with row totals of 40 and 60, what is the grand total?
The grand total is the sum of all row totals: 40 + 60 = 100.
What is a 'two-way table'?
A two-way table (also called a contingency table) organizes data based on two categorical variables, with one variable defining the rows and the other defining the columns.
Real-World Applications
Example: In a two-way table, 50 out of 200 surveyed people are left-handed men. What percentage of all people surveyed are left-handed men?
Percentage = (50/200) x 100 = 25%.
Example: A survey of 300 residents asked about their transportation and neighborhood. Downtown: Car = 40, Bus = 80, Bike = 30. Suburbs: Car = 100, Bus = 30, Bike = 20. What percentage of downtown residents use a car?
Downtown total = 40 + 80 + 30 = 150. Percentage of downtown using car = (40/150) x 100 ≈ 26.7%.
Example: A movie theater surveyed 400 customers on movie genre preference by age group. Teens: Action = 80, Comedy = 60. Adults: Action = 90, Comedy = 50. Seniors: Action = 30, Comedy = 90. Which age group has the highest proportion of comedy preference?
Teens comedy: 60/140 ≈ 42.9%. Adults comedy: 50/140 ≈ 35.7%. Seniors comedy: 90/120 = 75%. Seniors have the highest proportion.
Important Concepts
What does 'joint frequency' mean in a two-way table
A joint frequency is the count in the interior of the table where a specific row category meets a specific column category.
What does 'marginal frequency' mean in a two-way table
Marginal frequencies are the row totals and column totals found along the margins (edges) of the two-way table.

--- Lesson 2.1: One-Step &amp; Two-Step Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: One-Step &amp; Two-Step Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is an 'equation'?
An equation is a mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal, connected by an equals sign (=).
What is a 'one-step equation'?
A one-step equation requires only one operation (such as adding, subtracting, multiplying, or dividing) to isolate the variable.
What is a 'two-step equation'?
A two-step equation requires two inverse operations to solve, typically undoing addition/subtraction first, then undoing multiplication/division.
A student bought a book and paid $3 in tax, for a total of $15. She can represent this as x + 3 = 15, where x is the book price. What is the price of the book?
Solve x + 3 = 15 by subtracting 3: x = 15 - 3 = 12. The book costs $12.
A baker needs to divide dough equally into 6 pieces. Each piece should weigh 8 ounces. What equation represents the total dough needed, and what is the answer?
Total dough divided by 6 equals 8 ounces per piece: x/6 = 8. Multiply both sides by 6: x = 48 ounces.
A gym membership costs $25 per month plus a one-time registration fee. After 4 months, a member has paid $150 total. The equation is 25(4) + f = 150. What is the registration fee?
100 + f = 150. Subtract 100: f = 50. The registration fee is $50.
Real-World Applications
Example: A phone plan charges a $10 monthly fee plus $0.05 per text message. If the total bill is $17.50, the equation is 0.05x + 10 = 17.50. How many text messages were sent?
0.05x + 10 = 17.50. Subtract 10: 0.05x = 7.50. Divide by 0.05: x = 150 messages.
Example: A school fundraiser sells candy bars for $2 each. After paying a $30 booth rental, the profit equation is 2c - 30 = P. If the goal is $120 profit, how many candy bars must be sold?
2c - 30 = 120. Add 30: 2c = 150. Divide by 2: c = 75 candy bars.

Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.

What is the solution of x + 3 > 7 and x + 3 < 5?
First: x > 4. Second: x < 2. No number is both greater than 4 and less than 2, so there is no solution.
What is a 'compound inequality'?

Example: Two friends have a combined age of 40. One is 6 years older than the other. The system is a + b = 40 and a = b + 6. What are their ages?
Substitute a = b + 6: (b + 6) + b = 40, 2b + 6 = 40, 2b = 34, b = 17. a = 23.

--- Lesson 2.9: Systems of Inequalities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Systems of Inequalities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'system of inequalities'?
A system of inequalities consists of two or more inequalities. The solution is the set of all points that satisfy every inequality in the system.
A student must study at least 2 hours of math (m ≥ 2) and at least 1 hour of science (s ≥ 1), but no more than 6 hours total (m + s ≤ 6). If she studies 3 hours of math, what is the range of science hours she can study?
With m = 3: s ≥ 1 and 3 + s ≤ 6, so s ≤ 3. The range is 1 ≤ s ≤ 3.
A bakery makes cakes (c) and pies (p). Each cake requires 3 hours and each pie requires 2 hours. The baker has at most 24 hours available (3c + 2p ≤ 24) and must make at least 2 of each (c ≥ 2, p ≥ 2). What is the maximum number of cakes if exactly 2 pies are made?
With p = 2: 3c + 4 ≤ 24, 3c ≤ 20, c ≤ 6.67. Since c must be a whole number, c ≤ 6. Maximum is 6 cakes.
A concert venue has floor seats (f) and balcony seats (b). The venue holds at most 500 people (f + b ≤ 500). Floor seats cost $75 and balcony seats cost $50. The venue wants at least $30,000 in revenue (75f + 50b ≥ 30000). What is the minimum floor seats needed if all 500 seats are filled?
If f + b = 500, then b = 500 - f. Revenue: 75f + 50(500 - f) ≥ 30000, 75f + 25000 - 50f ≥ 30000, 25f ≥ 5000, f ≥ 200.
A student buys notebooks (n at $3 each) and pens (p at $1 each). She has at most $15 to spend (3n + p ≤ 15) and needs at least 2 notebooks (n ≥ 2) and at least 3 pens (p ≥ 3). If she buys 3 notebooks, what is the maximum number of pens?
3(3) + p ≤ 15, 9 + p ≤ 15, p ≤ 6. Maximum 6 pens (and p ≥ 3 is satisfied).
A gardener has space for at most 50 plants. She wants at least 15 roses (r ≥ 15) and at least 10 tulips (t ≥ 10), with r + t ≤ 50. She also wants at least twice as many roses as tulips (r ≥ 2t). If she plants exactly 10 tulips, what is the range of roses she can plant?
With t = 10: r ≥ 15 (constraint 1), r ≥ 2(10) = 20 (constraint 4), r + 10 ≤ 50 so r ≤ 40 (constraint 3). The binding lower bound is r ≥ 20, so 20 ≤ r ≤ 40.

--- Lesson 3.1: Scatter Plots &amp; Correlation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Scatter Plots &amp; Correlation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a scatter plot?
A scatter plot is a graph where paired data values are plotted as individual points on a coordinate plane to show the relationship between two variables.
What is the correlation coefficient (r)?
The correlation coefficient r is a value between −1 and 1 that quantifies both the strength and direction of the linear association between two variables.
What is the independent variable in a scatter plot?
The independent variable is the factor that is thought to influence the dependent variable. It is plotted on the x-axis of a scatter plot.
What is a dependent variable?
The dependent variable is the outcome being measured; it may change in response to the independent variable and is typically plotted on the y-axis.
A school counselor surveys 30 students and plots their weekly screen time (hours) against GPA. The correlation coefficient is r = -0.64. One student has 40 hours of screen time and a 3.9 GPA. What is this data point likely considered?
With r = −0.64, the general trend is that more screen time is associated with lower GPAs. A student with very high screen time and a high GPA goes against this pattern, making it an outlier.
A marketing team tracks advertising spending (thousands of dollars) and monthly revenue (thousands of dollars) for 12 months. They find r = 0.91. The team proposes doubling the advertising budget. What is the most statistically responsible conclusion?
A strong r value (0.91) suggests a strong association, but correlation does not prove causation or guarantee specific future outcomes. The conclusion should remain cautious.
A biologist measures the wing length (cm) and body mass (g) of 25 birds of the same species. The scatter plot shows a clear upward trend with r = 0.87. A new bird is found with a wing length of 14 cm and a body mass of only 10 g, far below the trend. What is the most likely effect if this bird is included in the data set?
Adding an outlier that falls far from the existing positive trend weakens the linear pattern, which decreases the r value. One outlier is unlikely to reverse the overall direction of correlation.
Outliers and Resistant Measures
An outlier in a scatter plot is a data point that lies far from the general trend of the other data points and can affect the correlation coefficient.
Real-World Applications
Example: A scatter plot of hours studied vs. test score shows a correlation coefficient of r = 0.85. What does this tell you?
An r value of 0.85 is close to 1 and positive, indicating a strong positive linear association between the two variables.
Example: As the temperature outside increases, ice cream sales also increase. This is an example of what type of correlation?
When both variables increase together, the relationship is a positive correlation. As temperature goes up, ice cream sales also go up.
Example: A teacher records the number of absences and final exam scores for 10 students. The scatter plot shows that as absences increase, exam scores decrease. The correlation coefficient is r = -0.81. What conclusion can be drawn?
An r of −0.81 is close to −1, indicating a strong negative linear association. Students with more absences tend to earn lower exam scores.
Example: A car dealership plots the age of used cars (x) against their selling price (y). The data shows a downward trend with r = -0.88. If a car is 2 years old and sells for $25,000, while a 10-year-old car sells for $8,000, which statement is most accurate?
The r value of −0.88 confirms a strong negative correlation. The data examples support this: a newer 2-year-old car sells for much more than a 10-year-old car.
Example: A researcher records the elevation (feet above sea level) and average temperature (degrees F) for 15 cities. The scatter plot shows a downward trend with r = -0.77. If a city at 500 feet has an average temperature of 72 degrees F, what average temperature might you expect for a city at 5,000 feet?
The negative correlation (r = −0.77) indicates that as elevation increases, temperature tends to decrease. A city at 5,000 feet would be expected to have a lower average temperature than one at 500 feet.
Example: A sports analyst plots the number of practice hours per week (x) against free-throw percentage (y) for 20 basketball players. The correlation is r = 0.78. A new player practices 15 hours per week. Based on the scatter plot trend, how should the analyst interpret this?
With r = 0.78, there is a strong positive association. Players who practice more tend to have higher free-throw percentages, so 15 hours of practice suggests a relatively high percentage, though it is not guaranteed.

--- Lesson 3.2: Line of Best Fit (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Line of Best Fit
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A line of best fit for a scatter plot has the equation y = 2x + 5. What is the predicted y-value when x = 4?
Substituting x = 4 into y = 2x + 5 gives y = 2(4) + 5 = 8 + 5 = 13.
The line of best fit for a data set is y = -3x + 20. What is the slope, and what does it mean?
In y = -3x + 20, the slope is -3. This means that for every 1-unit increase in x, the predicted y-value decreases by 3.
A line of best fit passes through the points (2, 10) and (6, 18). What is the slope of this line?
The slope is (18 - 10) / (6 - 2) = 8 / 4 = 2.
Using the line of best fit y = 0.5x + 3, what is the y-intercept and what does it represent?
In y = 0.5x + 3, the y-intercept is 3. It represents the predicted value of y when x equals 0.
A trend line has the equation y = 4x - 1. What is the predicted y-value when x = 10?
Substituting x = 10 into y = 4x - 1 gives y = 4(10) - 1 = 40 - 1 = 39.
A scatter plot has data points (1, 5), (2, 7), (3, 8), (4, 11), and (5, 13). A student draws a line of best fit through (1, 5) and (5, 13). What is the equation of this line?
Slope = (13 - 5) / (5 - 1) = 8 / 4 = 2. Using point (1, 5): 5 = 2(1) + b, so b = 3. The equation is y = 2x + 3.
The line of best fit for a data set is y = 1.2x + 8. What is the predicted difference in y between x = 5 and x = 10?
At x = 5: y = 1.2(5) + 8 = 14. At x = 10: y = 1.2(10) + 8 = 20. The difference is 20 - 14 = 6.
What is a line of best fit?
A line of best fit (or trend line) is a straight line that best represents the overall direction and pattern of data in a scatter plot, minimizing the distances between the line and the data points.
What is a 'prediction' in the context of a line of best fit?
A prediction is an estimated value of y found by plugging an x-value into the line of best fit equation. It is an approximation, not a guaranteed outcome.
A farmer records rainfall (inches) and crop yield (bushels per acre). The line of best fit is y = 8.5x + 20. In a year with 6 inches of rainfall, the actual yield was 75 bushels per acre. What is the residual?
Predicted y = 8.5(6) + 20 = 51 + 20 = 71. Residual = actual - predicted = 75 - 71 = 4. The actual yield was 4 bushels above the predicted value.
A delivery company finds that the line of best fit for distance traveled (miles, x) and delivery time (minutes, y) is y = 1.4x + 10. If a package must arrive within 80 minutes, what is the maximum distance the model predicts can be covered?
Setting 80 = 1.4x + 10, we get 1.4x = 70, so x = 50. The model predicts a maximum distance of 50 miles within 80 minutes.
A real estate analyst finds the line of best fit for house size (hundreds of sq ft, x) and price (thousands of dollars, y) is y = 15x + 50. A house that is 2,000 sq ft (x = 20) sells for $380,000. What is the residual?
Predicted price = 15(20) + 50 = 300 + 50 = 350 (thousands). Actual is $380,000 = 380 (thousands). Residual = 380 - 350 = 30. The house sold $30,000 above the predicted price.
A gym tracks months of membership (x) and average weekly visits (y). The line of best fit is y = 0.3x + 1.5. A member has been enrolled for 10 months. What is the predicted average weekly visits, and would predicting for x = 60 months be interpolation or extrapolation if the data only goes up to 24 months?
At x = 10: y = 0.3(10) + 1.5 = 3 + 1.5 = 4.5 visits. Since the data only goes up to 24 months, predicting at x = 60 is extrapolation (predicting beyond the data range).
A school district plots per-pupil spending (hundreds of dollars, x) against average test score (y). The line of best fit is y = 0.8x + 40. District A spends $6,000 per pupil (x = 60) and has an average score of 92. What is the residual?
Predicted score = 0.8(60) + 40 = 48 + 40 = 88. Residual = 92 - 88 = 4. District A scored 4 points above the predicted value.
A city planner collects data on the number of parks (x) and median home value (thousands of dollars, y) across 20 neighborhoods. The line of best fit is y = 18x + 150. If a new neighborhood is planned with 5 parks, what is the predicted median home value?
Substituting x = 5: y = 18(5) + 150 = 90 + 150 = 240 (thousands). The predicted median home value is $240,000.

--- Lesson 3.4: Correlation vs. Causation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Correlation vs. Causation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Ice cream sales and drowning incidents are positively correlated. What is the most likely explanation?
Warm weather is a confounding variable that increases both ice cream sales and swimming activity, which leads to more drowning incidents. The ice cream does not cause the drownings.
A newspaper headline states: 'Shoe size correlated with reading ability in children.' What is the most reasonable explanation?
Age is the confounding variable. As children grow older, both their shoe size and reading ability increase, creating a spurious correlation between the two.
What is a confounding variable?
A confounding variable is an unmeasured or uncontrolled factor that affects both variables being studied, potentially creating a false or misleading association between them.
What is a lurking variable?
A lurking variable is one that is not included in the analysis but may influence the relationship between the variables being studied, potentially leading to incorrect conclusions.
What is an observational study?
In an observational study, the researcher collects data by observing subjects without manipulating any variables. This type of study can show correlation but generally cannot prove causation.
What is a 'placebo' in an experimental study?
A placebo is an inactive treatment (such as a sugar pill) given to the control group to compare against the actual treatment, helping researchers isolate the true effect of the treatment.
A school district finds that students who participate in band also have higher math scores. The superintendent proposes requiring all students to join band. What is the flaw in this reasoning?
Students who choose band may already be motivated, disciplined, or academically strong. The correlation between band participation and math scores does not prove that band causes higher math performance.
A university finds that students who sit in the front row earn higher grades. A professor requires all students to sit in the front row next semester. What is wrong with this approach?
Students who voluntarily sit in the front may already be more engaged and motivated. The correlation between seating and grades may be driven by student motivation, not the seat location itself.
Real-World Applications
Example: Ice cream sales and drowning incidents are positively correlated. What is the most likely explanation?
Warm weather is a confounding variable that increases both ice cream sales and swimming activity, which leads to more drowning incidents. The ice cream does not cause the drownings.
Example: A study finds a strong positive correlation between the number of books in a home and children's test scores. A reporter writes: 'Buying more books boosts test scores.' What error is the reporter making?
The reporter is assuming causation from correlation. Other factors such as parental education or income level could be confounding variables that explain both.
Example: A school district finds that students who participate in band also have higher math scores. The superintendent proposes requiring all students to join band. What is the flaw in this reasoning?
Students who choose band may already be motivated, disciplined, or academically strong. The correlation between band participation and math scores does not prove that band causes higher math performance.
Example: A study shows a strong positive correlation between the number of hours spent on social media and levels of anxiety among teenagers. A parent group demands that social media be banned to reduce anxiety. What critical thinking principle should they consider?
The direction of causation is unclear: social media might contribute to anxiety, but anxious teens might also turn to social media. Without a controlled experiment, the causal direction cannot be determined.
Example: A tech company observes that employees who take more vacation days are more productive. Management considers mandating additional vacation. Before doing so, they should consider which of the following?
The direction of causation is ambiguous. Productive employees may feel more secure taking vacation, rather than vacation causing productivity. A controlled study would be needed to establish the causal direction.

--- Lesson 3.5: Two-Way Frequency Tables (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Two-Way Frequency Tables
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In a two-way table, the row totals are 45 and 55. What is the grand total?
The grand total is the sum of all row totals: 45 + 55 = 100.
A two-way table shows: Male & Cat = 12, Male & Dog = 18, Female & Cat = 15, Female & Dog = 25. What is the total number of dog owners?
Total dog owners = Male & Dog + Female & Dog = 18 + 25 = 43.
In a two-way table, the column totals are 60 and 90. What is the grand total?
The grand total is the sum of all column totals: 60 + 90 = 150.
What is a two-way frequency table?
A two-way frequency table organizes data for two categorical variables simultaneously, using rows for one variable and columns for the other, with counts in each cell.
What is a 'joint frequency' in a two-way table?
A joint frequency is the count in a single cell of the table, representing how many data points fall into the intersection of a specific row category and a specific column category.
What is a 'marginal frequency'?
A marginal frequency is the total of a row or column, found along the margins (edges) of the two-way table. It summarizes data for one category across all categories of the other variable.
What is the 'grand total' in a two-way frequency table?
The grand total is the total count of all observations in the table. It equals the sum of all row totals or equivalently the sum of all column totals.
A veterinary clinic records the type of pet (cat or dog) and whether the pet is vaccinated (yes or no). Results: Cat & Yes = 28, Cat & No = 12, Dog & Yes = 35, Dog & No = 25. If a pet is randomly selected, what is the probability it is a vaccinated dog?
Grand total = 28 + 12 + 35 + 25 = 100. Vaccinated dogs = 35. Probability = 35/100 = 0.35.
Real-World Applications
Example: A two-way table has cells A = 10, B = 20, C = 30, D = 40. What percentage of the total does cell C represent?
Grand total = 10 + 20 + 30 + 40 = 100. Cell C as a percentage = (30/100) x 100% = 30%.

Total pizza = 40 + 45 = 85. Percentage = (85/150) x 100% = 56.67%, which is approximately 56.7%.

Total Engineering = 60 + 60 = 120. Engineering using transit = 60. Fraction = 60/120 = 1/2.

Example: A restaurant surveys 250 customers about meal preference (chicken or fish) and dining time (lunch or dinner). Results: Lunch & Chicken = 60, Lunch & Fish = 40, Dinner & Chicken = 80, Dinner & Fish = 70. What percentage of lunch customers chose fish?
Total lunch = 60 + 40 = 100. Fish at lunch = 40. Percentage = (40/100) x 100% = 40%.
Example: A phone store records sales by phone type (Android or iPhone) and payment method (cash or financing). Results: Android & Cash = 20, Android & Financing = 40, iPhone & Cash = 15, iPhone & Financing = 25. What percentage of all phone sales used financing?
Total financing = 40 + 25 = 65. Grand total = 20 + 40 + 15 + 25 = 100. Percentage = (65/100) x 100% = 65%.
Example: A library surveys 300 patrons about reading preference (fiction or nonfiction) and age group (under 30 or 30 and over). Results: Under 30 & Fiction = 80, Under 30 & Nonfiction = 40, 30+ & Fiction = 90, 30+ & Nonfiction = 90. A librarian claims that younger patrons prefer fiction more strongly than older patrons. Do the data support this?
Under 30: 80/120 = 66.7% fiction. 30+: 90/180 = 50% fiction. The data support the claim: younger patrons prefer fiction at a notably higher rate.
Important Concepts
A two-way frequency table shows that 30 students play soccer, 20 play basketball, 10 play both, and 15 play neither. How many students were surveyed in total
Using the inclusion-exclusion principle: total = soccer + basketball - both + neither = 30 + 20 - 10 + 15 = 55 students.
What is a two-way frequency table
A two-way frequency table organizes data for two categorical variables simultaneously, using rows for one variable and columns for the other, with counts in each cell.
What is a 'joint frequency' in a two-way table
A joint frequency is the count in a single cell of the table, representing how many data points fall into the intersection of a specific row category and a specific column category.
What is a 'marginal frequency'
A marginal frequency is the total of a row or column, found along the margins (edges) of the two-way table. It summarizes data for one category across all categories of the other variable.
What does 'frequency' mean in a frequency table
Frequency is the count of how many times a specific value, response, or category appears in the data set.
What is the 'grand total' in a two-way frequency table
The grand total is the total count of all observations in the table. It equals the sum of all row totals or equivalently the sum of all column totals.
What does 'conditional frequency' mean
A conditional frequency is the frequency of one variable calculated within a specific category of another variable, such as the number of males who prefer cats out of all males.
What does 'cell' mean in the context of a two-way frequency table
A cell is the individual box located at the intersection of a specific row and column in the table, containing the count (frequency) of observations that belong to both categories.

--- Lesson 3.7: Association in Categorical Data (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Association in Categorical Data
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A researcher calculates the expected frequency for a cell in a two-way table using the formula: (row total x column total) / grand total. The row total is 60, the column total is 80, and the grand total is 200. What is the expected frequency?
Expected frequency = (60 x 80) / 200 = 4800 / 200 = 24.
Real-World Applications
Example: A school examines the relationship between grade level (9th or 12th) and whether students have a part-time job. The conditional frequency of having a job is 15% for 9th graders and 55% for 12th graders. What does this indicate?
The large difference (15% vs. 55%) between the conditional frequencies indicates a strong association between grade level and employment status.
Example: A marketing team surveys 400 customers about brand preference (Brand A or Brand B) and age group (18-30 or 31-50). Results: 18-30 & Brand A = 100, 18-30 & Brand B = 60, 31-50 & Brand A = 90, 31-50 & Brand B = 150. Is there an association between age group and brand preference?
18-30: 100/160 = 62.5% prefer Brand A. 31-50: 90/240 = 37.5% prefer Brand A. The substantial difference in conditional frequencies indicates an association.
Example: A school examines whether there is an association between gender (male or female) and preferred learning style (visual or auditory). Results: Male & Visual = 45, Male & Auditory = 55, Female & Visual = 48, Female & Auditory = 52. Is there an association?
Males: 45/100 = 45% visual. Females: 48/100 = 48% visual. The conditional distributions are nearly identical, suggesting no meaningful association.
Example: A college admissions study looks at acceptance rates by gender. Overall, males are accepted at 40% and females at 35%. But when examined by department, females have higher acceptance rates in every department. This is an example of:
This is Simpson's Paradox: the overall trend (males accepted at a higher rate) reverses in every subgroup (females accepted at a higher rate in each department). This happens because females apply more to competitive departments.
Example: A restaurant chain examines customer satisfaction (satisfied or not) at two locations. Location A: 150 satisfied, 50 not. Location B: 100 satisfied, 100 not. A manager creates a segmented bar graph. What would the graph reveal?
Location A: 150/200 = 75% satisfied. Location B: 100/200 = 50% satisfied. The segmented bar graph would clearly show the different proportions, indicating an association.
Example: A school district examines graduation rates by program (traditional or alternative) and socioeconomic status (low or high income). Overall, traditional programs have higher graduation rates. But within each income group, alternative programs have higher rates. What explains this reversal?
This is Simpson's Paradox. Alternative programs serve a disproportionately larger share of low-income students, who tend to have lower overall graduation rates, which lowers the aggregate rate despite higher within-group performance.
Example: A phone manufacturer surveys 500 users about phone preference (Model X or Model Y) and usage pattern (heavy or light). Results: Heavy & X = 130, Heavy & Y = 70, Light & X = 120, Light & Y = 180. Does usage pattern appear to be associated with phone preference?
Heavy users: 130/200 = 65% prefer X. Light users: 120/300 = 40% prefer X. The substantial difference in conditional distributions indicates an association.
Example: A researcher examines whether handedness (left or right) is associated with career field (STEM or non-STEM) among 600 professionals. Results: Left & STEM = 18, Left & Non-STEM = 42, Right & STEM = 162, Right & Non-STEM = 378. Is there an association?
Left-handed STEM rate: 18/60 = 30%. Right-handed STEM rate: 162/540 = 30%. The identical conditional distributions indicate no association between handedness and career field.
Important Concepts
If two categorical variables are independent, what should be true about their conditional relative frequency distributions
If two variables are independent, knowing the category of one variable does not change the distribution of the other. The conditional distributions will be approximately equal.
A school examines the relationship between grade level (9th or 12th) and whether students have a part-time job. The conditional frequency of having a job is 15% for 9th graders and 55% for 12th graders. What does this indicate
The large difference (15% vs. 55%) between the conditional frequencies indicates a strong association between grade level and employment status.
A researcher calculates the expected frequency for a cell in a two-way table using the formula: (row total x column total) / grand total. The row total is 60, the column total is 80, and the grand total is 200. What is the expected frequency
Expected frequency = (60 x 80) / 200 = 4800 / 200 = 24.
Two variables show similar conditional relative frequency distributions across all categories. This suggests the variables are most likely:
When conditional distributions are similar across all categories, the variables do not influence each other, suggesting independence.
A two-way table shows a large difference between the observed frequency and the expected frequency for one cell. What does this suggest
When observed frequencies differ substantially from expected frequencies (those computed assuming independence), it suggests the variables are associated.
What does 'expected frequency' mean
Expected frequency is the count a cell would have if the two variables were completely independent. It is calculated as (row total x column total) / grand total.
