--- Lesson 11.1: Arithmetic &amp; Geometric Sequences Review (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arithmetic &amp; Geometric Sequences Review
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common difference of 10, 7, 4, 1, −2?
7 − 10 = −3. The common difference is −3.
What is the common ratio of 4, 12, 36, 108?
12/4 = 3. Each term is multiplied by 3, so r = 3.
What is an 'arithmetic sequence'?
An arithmetic sequence has a constant difference between consecutive terms. Example: 2, 5, 8, 11 has d = 3.
What is a 'geometric sequence'?
A geometric sequence has a constant ratio between consecutive terms. Example: 3, 6, 12, 24 has r = 2.
What is the 'common difference' (d)?
The common difference d is found by subtracting any term from the next term: d = aₙ₊₁ − aₙ.
What is the 'common ratio' (r)?
The common ratio r is found by dividing any term by the previous term: r = aₙ₊₁/aₙ.
What is the 'nth term formula' for an arithmetic sequence?
The nth term of an arithmetic sequence is aₙ = a₁ + (n − 1)d, where a₁ is the first term and d is the common difference.
What is the 'nth term formula' for a geometric sequence?
The nth term of a geometric sequence is aₙ = a₁ · r^(n−1), where a₁ is the first term and r is the common ratio.
What is a 'term' in a sequence?
A term is a single value in a sequence. In the sequence 2, 5, 8, 11, the first term is 2, the second is 5, and so on.
A staircase has 12 steps. The first step is 8 inches high and each subsequent step is 8 inches higher than ground level (constant height). What is the total height?
Each step is 8 inches: 12 × 8 = 96 inches. This is an arithmetic sequence where every term is 8.
A car depreciates by $2,000 each year. If it starts at $30,000, what is its value after 7 years?
Arithmetic: a₁ = 30000, d = −2000. a₈ = 30000 + 7(−2000) = 30000 − 14000 = $16,000.

--- Lesson 11.2: Recursive &amp; Explicit Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Recursive &amp; Explicit Formulas
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'recursive formula'?
A recursive formula defines each term based on the preceding term(s). It requires knowing the starting value(s). Example: aₙ = aₙ₋₁ + 3, a₁ = 5.
What is an 'explicit formula'?
An explicit formula lets you find any term directly by plugging in n. Example: aₙ = 3n + 2 gives you a₁₀₀ = 302 without computing a₁ through a₉₉.
What is the advantage of an explicit formula over a recursive formula?
Explicit formulas allow direct computation. To find a₁₀₀, just plug in n = 100. Recursive formulas would require calculating all 99 preceding terms.
What is 'subscript notation' in sequences?
Subscript notation uses a small number (subscript) to identify the position of a term: a₁ is the first term, a₂ is the second, aₙ is the nth.
A gym membership costs $50 to join and $30 per month. Using aₙ = 50 + 30n, what is the total cost after 6 months?
a₆ = 50 + 30(6) = 50 + 180 = $230.
A radioactive substance has half its mass remaining each hour: mₙ = mₙ₋₁/2, m₁ = 400 g. What is the explicit formula, and how much remains after 5 hours?
Geometric with r = 1/2. m₅ = 400 · (1/2)⁴ = 400 · 1/16 = 25. Wait: m₅ = 400 · (1/2)^4 = 400/16 = 25. After 5 hours at m₆: 400 · (1/2)^5 = 12.5 g.
An investment doubles every 5 years. Starting at $1,000, the explicit formula is aₙ = 1000 · 2^(n−1). What is the value after the 4th doubling period?
a₄ = 1000 · 2³ = 1000 · 8 = $8,000.
Real-World Applications
Example: What is the advantage of an explicit formula over a recursive formula?
Explicit formulas allow direct computation. To find a₁₀₀, just plug in n = 100. Recursive formulas would require calculating all 99 preceding terms.

  4. Q: Recursive for geometric:
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) · r, with a(1) given

  5. Q: Advantage of explicit formulas:
     A: You can find any term without computing all previous terms

  6. Q: Write recursive formula for 2, 5, 8, 11, ...
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) + 3, a(1) = 2

  7. Q: Write recursive formula for 4, 12, 36, ...
     A: a(n) = a(n−1) · 3, a(1) = 4

  8. Q: Convert a(n) = a(n−1) + 5, a(1) = 3 to explicit:
     A: a(n) = 5n − 2

  9. Q: Advantage of recursive formulas:
     A: Easy to compute the next term from the previous one

  10. Q: Convert a(n) = a(n−1) · 2, a(1) = 5 to explicit:
     A: a(n) = 5 · 2^(n−1)

--- Lesson 11.3: Introduction to Series (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Introduction to Series
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is ∑ₖ₌₁⁴ (2k)?
∑(2k) for k = 1 to 4: 2 + 4 + 6 + 8 = 20.
What is the sum of the first 4 terms of a geometric series with a₁ = 2, r = 3?
2 + 6 + 18 + 54 = 80. Or S₄ = 2(1 − 81)/(1 − 3) = 2(−80)/(−2) = 80.
What is a 'series'?
A series is the sum of the terms of a sequence. A sequence lists terms; a series adds them up.
What is an 'arithmetic series'?
An arithmetic series is the sum of an arithmetic sequence. Its formula is Sₙ = n/2(a₁ + aₙ).
What is a 'geometric series'?
A geometric series is the sum of a geometric sequence. Its formula is Sₙ = a₁(1 − rⁿ)/(1 − r) when r ≠ 1.
What is the 'partial sum' Sₙ?
A partial sum Sₙ is the sum of the first n terms. It's called 'partial' because the sequence may continue beyond n terms.
What is the formula for the sum of a finite arithmetic series?
The arithmetic series sum formula is Sₙ = n/2(a₁ + aₙ), which averages the first and last terms then multiplies by the number of terms.
What is the formula for the sum of a finite geometric series?
The geometric series sum formula is Sₙ = a₁(1 − rⁿ)/(1 − r). This only works when r ≠ 1.
What is a 'finite series'?
A finite series has a definite number of terms and therefore a definite sum. It contrasts with an infinite series that continues without end.
What is an 'infinite series'?
An infinite series has infinitely many terms. Some infinite geometric series converge (have a finite sum) when |r| < 1.
A bouncing ball travels 10 + 8 + 6.4 + ... feet. If it bounces 4 times total, what is the total distance?
Geometric: a₁ = 10, r = 0.8, n = 4. S₄ = 10(1 − 0.8⁴)/(1 − 0.8) = 10(1 − 0.4096)/0.2 = 10(0.5904)/0.2 = 29.52 feet.
A company pays a new employee $200 on day 1, $205 on day 2, $210 on day 3. What is the total pay for 30 days?
Arithmetic: a₁ = 200, d = 5, n = 30. a₃₀ = 200 + 29(5) = 345. S₃₀ = 30/2(200 + 345) = 15(545) = $8,175.
An athlete runs 1 mile on day 1, 1.5 miles on day 2, 2 miles on day 3 (adding 0.5 each day). What is the total distance after 14 days?
Arithmetic: a₁ = 1, d = 0.5, n = 14. a₁₄ = 1 + 13(0.5) = 7.5. S₁₄ = 14/2(1 + 7.5) = 7(8.5) = 59.5 miles.
A pension plan pays $500 in month 1, and each month the payment increases by $50. What is the total paid over 12 months?
