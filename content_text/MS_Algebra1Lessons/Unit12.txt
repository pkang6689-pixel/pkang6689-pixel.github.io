--- Lesson 12.2: Independent &amp; Dependent Events (Summary) ---

  Notes
Key Concepts: Independent & Dependent Events

Events are independent if the outcome of one does NOT affect the other. P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B).

Events are dependent if the outcome of one affects the other. P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A).

Drawing with replacement → independent. Drawing without replacement → dependent.

P(B|A) is the probability of B given that A has already occurred.

--- Lesson 12.3: Conditional Probability (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conditional Probability
Conditional probability: P(B|A) = P(A and B) / P(A).

P(B|A) is the probability of B occurring given that A has already occurred.

Use two-way tables to calculate conditional probabilities by restricting to a row or column.

If P(B|A) = P(B), then A and B are independent.

--- Lesson 12.4: Probability with Combinatorics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Probability with Combinatorics
Counting Principle: if event A has m outcomes and event B has n outcomes, there are m × n total outcomes.

Permutations: order matters. P(n, r) = n! / (n−r)!

Combinations: order does NOT matter. C(n, r) = n! / (r!(n−r)!).

Use combinatorics to count outcomes, then calculate probability as favorable/total.
