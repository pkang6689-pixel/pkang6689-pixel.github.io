--- Lesson 9.1: Simplifying Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Simplifying Rational Expressions
A rational expression is a fraction where the numerator and/or denominator are polynomials.

To simplify, factor both the numerator and denominator, then cancel common factors.

The expression is undefined where the denominator equals zero. State excluded values.

Example: (x² − 4)/(x + 2) = (x+2)(x−2)/(x+2) = x − 2, where x ≠ −2.

     A: A fraction where the numerator and/or denominator are polynomials

     A: When the denominator equals zero

  4. Q: What are excluded values?

     A: (x+3)(x−3)/(x+3) = x − 3, where x ≠ −3

  10. Q: Find excluded values: (x + 1)/(x² − 4)
     A: x ≠ 2 and x ≠ −2 (where x² − 4 = 0)

--- Lesson 9.2: Multiplying &amp; Dividing Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Notes
Key Concepts: Multiplying & Dividing Rational Expressions

To multiply: factor everything, cancel common factors, then multiply remaining numerators and denominators.

To divide: multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction, then simplify.

Always factor first — cancellation only works with factors, not terms.

State excluded values from all original denominators.

--- Lesson 9.3: Adding &amp; Subtracting Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Notes
Key Concepts: Adding & Subtracting Rational Expressions

With the same denominator: add/subtract the numerators and keep the denominator.

With different denominators: find the LCD (Least Common Denominator), rewrite each fraction, then add/subtract.

The LCD is the LCM of the denominators.

Always simplify the final result and state excluded values.

--- Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Rational Equations
A rational equation has at least one rational expression. Solve by multiplying every term by the LCD to clear fractions.

After clearing, solve the resulting polynomial equation.

Always check for extraneous solutions — solutions that make a denominator zero are not valid.

Example: 1/x + 1/2 = 1 → LCD = 2x → 2 + x = 2x → x = 2.

--- Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: How do you solve a rational equation?
     A: Multiply every term by the LCD to clear all fractions

  2. Q: What is an extraneous solution?
     A: A result that makes a denominator zero — it must be rejected

  3. Q: Why must you check solutions?
     A: Squaring or multiplying by a variable can introduce extraneous solutions

  4. Q: Solve: x/2 + x/3 = 5
     A: LCD = 6: 3x + 2x = 30 → 5x = 30 → x = 6

  5. Q: Solve: 1/x = 1/4
     A: Cross multiply: 4 = x → x = 4

  6. Q: Solve: 2/(x−1) = 4
     A: 2 = 4(x−1) → 2 = 4x − 4 → 6 = 4x → x = 3/2

  7. Q: If a solution gives 0 in a denominator:
     A: It is extraneous and must be rejected

  8. Q: What is the first step?
     A: Find the LCD of all fractions in the equation

  9. Q: What is a cross-multiplication shortcut?
     A: For a/b = c/d, cross multiply: ad = bc

  10. Q: Solve: 3/(x + 1) + 1/(x + 1) = 2
     A: 4/(x + 1) = 2 → x + 1 = 2 → x = 1
