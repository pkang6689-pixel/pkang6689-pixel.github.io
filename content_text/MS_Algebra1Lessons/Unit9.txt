--- Lesson 9.1: Simplifying Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Simplifying Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a rational expression?
A rational expression is a ratio (fraction) of two polynomials, such as (x + 1)/(x² − 4). It is defined wherever the denominator is not zero.
What is a 'common factor' when simplifying rational expressions?
A common factor is a factor that appears in both the numerator and the denominator. These shared factors can be divided out (canceled) to simplify the expression.
What is the 'domain' of a rational expression?
The domain of a rational expression is the set of all real numbers except those that make the denominator zero (the excluded values).
What is meant by 'lowest terms' in a rational expression?
A rational expression is in lowest terms when the numerator and denominator share no common factors other than 1.
What is a 'difference of squares' and how does it help simplify rational expressions?
The difference of squares pattern states a² − b² = (a − b)(a + b). Recognizing this pattern allows you to factor and then cancel common factors in rational expressions.
A farmer has 6x²y acres of land and divides it into 18xy³ equal plots. What is the size of each plot?
Each plot = 6x²y / (18xy³). Divide both by 6xy to get x/(3y²).
The area of a rectangular garden is x² + 7x + 12 square feet and its width is x + 3 feet. What is the length of the garden?
Length = Area / Width = (x² + 7x + 12)/(x + 3). Factor the numerator as (x + 3)(x + 4) and cancel (x + 3) to get x + 4.
A company's profit is modeled by (x² − 25)/(x² − 10x + 25). A manager simplifies it for a report. What is the simplified form?
Factor: numerator = (x − 5)(x + 5), denominator = (x − 5)². Cancel one (x − 5) to get (x + 5)/(x − 5).
A car travels a distance of x² − 1 miles in x + 1 hours. What is its average speed in simplified form?
Speed = distance/time = (x² − 1)/(x + 1). Factor the numerator: (x − 1)(x + 1). Cancel (x + 1) to get x − 1 miles per hour.
A shipping company charges 3x²/(9x) dollars per package. A customer wants to know the simplified cost per package. What is it?
Simplify 3x²/(9x) by dividing numerator and denominator by 3x to get x/3 dollars per package.
An amusement park earns (x² − 6x + 8)/(x − 2) thousand dollars per month. A financial analyst simplifies the expression for a quarterly report. What is the simplified monthly revenue?
Factor the numerator: x² − 6x + 8 = (x − 2)(x − 4). Cancel (x − 2) to get x − 4 thousand dollars per month.
Real-World Applications
Example: A company's profit is modeled by (x² − 25)/(x² − 10x + 25). A manager simplifies it for a report. What is the simplified form?
Factor: numerator = (x − 5)(x + 5), denominator = (x − 5)². Cancel one (x − 5) to get (x + 5)/(x − 5).
Example: A car travels a distance of x² − 1 miles in x + 1 hours. What is its average speed in simplified form?
Speed = distance/time = (x² − 1)/(x + 1). Factor the numerator: (x − 1)(x + 1). Cancel (x + 1) to get x − 1 miles per hour.
Example: A shipping company charges 3x²/(9x) dollars per package. A customer wants to know the simplified cost per package. What is it?
Simplify 3x²/(9x) by dividing numerator and denominator by 3x to get x/3 dollars per package.

--- Lesson 9.2: Multiplying &amp; Dividing Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Multiplying &amp; Dividing Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the first step you should take before multiplying two rational expressions?
Before multiplying, factor everything first. This lets you identify and cancel common factors across any numerator and any denominator before multiplying.
What is 'cross-canceling' when multiplying rational expressions?
Cross-canceling means dividing out common factors between a numerator of one fraction and a denominator of the other fraction before multiplying. This simplifies the computation.
What is the 'product rule for fractions' that applies to rational expressions?
The product rule for fractions states: (a/b) × (c/d) = (a·c)/(b·d). Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.
What is an 'improper rational expression'?
An improper rational expression has a numerator whose degree is greater than or equal to the denominator's degree, similar to how an improper fraction has a numerator larger than its denominator.
A factory produces (x² − 9) units per hour. Each unit requires (x − 3)/(x + 5) hours of quality inspection. What is the total inspection time per hour of production, simplified?
Total = (x² − 9) × (x − 3)/(x + 5) = (x − 3)(x + 3)(x − 3)/(x + 5) = (x − 3)²(x + 3)/(x + 5). Note: this does not simplify further since no factors cancel.
A rectangular field has area (x² − 1) square meters and length (x + 1)/(3) meters. What is the width?
Width = Area ÷ Length = (x² − 1) ÷ (x+1)/3 = (x−1)(x+1) × 3/(x+1) = 3(x − 1).
A store earns (3x/(x − 1)) dollars per item and sells ((x² − 1)/6) items per day. What is the daily revenue, simplified?
Revenue = [3x/(x−1)] × [(x−1)(x+1)/6]. Cancel (x−1) and simplify 3/6 to 1/2: x(x+1)/2.
A student walks (x² − 25)/(x + 7) miles to school and the trip takes (x − 5)/(x + 7) hours. What is the student's average speed in simplified form?
Speed = distance ÷ time = [(x−5)(x+5)/(x+7)] ÷ [(x−5)/(x+7)] = [(x−5)(x+5)/(x+7)] × [(x+7)/(x−5)]. Cancel (x+7) and (x−5) to get x + 5.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'multiply by the reciprocal' mean when dividing rational expressions?
To divide by a fraction, you keep the first fraction unchanged, flip the second fraction (take its reciprocal), and then multiply.

Factoring completely means expressing each polynomial as a product of factors that cannot be factored further. This is essential for identifying all common factors to cancel.

After multiplying, simplify by canceling any common factors between the new numerator and denominator to write the result in lowest terms.

    [ ] (a+c)/(b+d)
    [ ] (a·c)/(b·d)
    [*] (a·d)/(b·c)
    [ ] (a−c)/(b−d)
    Explanation: To divide fractions, multiply by the reciprocal: (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c) = (a·d)/(b·c).

    [ ] (x−1)/(x+1)

--- Lesson 9.3: Adding &amp; Subtracting Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Adding &amp; Subtracting Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the LCD (Least Common Denominator) of two rational expressions?
The LCD is the simplest expression that contains all factors of every denominator. You find it by taking the highest power of each unique factor across all denominators.
What is the rule for adding fractions that already have the same denominator?
When two fractions share the same denominator, add (or subtract) the numerators and place the result over the common denominator: a/d + b/d = (a + b)/d.
What is a 'like denominator' in the context of adding rational expressions?
Like denominators means the denominators are the exact same expression. When denominators are alike, you can directly add or subtract the numerators.
What is the 'highest power of each factor' rule for finding the LCD?
To build the LCD, factor every denominator, then include each unique factor raised to the highest power it appears in any single denominator.
What is a 'complex fraction'?
A complex fraction has fractions within its numerator, denominator, or both. To simplify, find the LCD of all inner fractions and multiply top and bottom by it.
A student scores 5/x on a quiz and 3/(2x) on a homework assignment. What is the student's combined score expressed as a single fraction?
LCD = 2x. Rewrite 5/x as 10/(2x). Then 10/(2x) + 3/(2x) = 13/(2x).
A tank is being filled by two pipes. Pipe A adds 4/(x−1) gallons per minute and Pipe B drains 2/(x−1) gallons per minute. What is the net fill rate?
Net rate = fill − drain = 4/(x−1) − 2/(x−1) = (4−2)/(x−1) = 2/(x−1) gallons per minute.
A runner jogs 3/(x+1) miles on Monday and 2/(x−1) miles on Tuesday. What is the total distance jogged over the two days?
LCD = (x+1)(x−1). Rewrite: 3(x−1)/((x+1)(x−1)) + 2(x+1)/((x+1)(x−1)) = (3x−3+2x+2)/((x+1)(x−1)) = (5x−1)/((x+1)(x−1)).
A store sells shirts at x/(x+5) dollars profit and pants at 3/(x+5) dollars profit. If the store returns 1/(x+5) dollars per item for damaged goods, what is the net profit per shirt-and-pants set?
Net profit = x/(x+5) + 3/(x+5) − 1/(x+5) = (x + 3 − 1)/(x+5) = (x+2)/(x+5).
A plumber charges 2/(x−4) dollars per minute and an electrician charges 5/(x+1) dollars per minute. If both work on a project, what is the combined per-minute cost?
LCD = (x−4)(x+1). Combined = 2(x+1)/LCD + 5(x−4)/LCD = (2x+2+5x−20)/LCD = (7x−18)/((x−4)(x+1)).
Real-World Applications
Example: A student scores 5/x on a quiz and 3/(2x) on a homework assignment. What is the student's combined score expressed as a single fraction?
LCD = 2x. Rewrite 5/x as 10/(2x). Then 10/(2x) + 3/(2x) = 13/(2x).
Example: A store sells shirts at x/(x+5) dollars profit and pants at 3/(x+5) dollars profit. If the store returns 1/(x+5) dollars per item for damaged goods, what is the net profit per shirt-and-pants set?
Net profit = x/(x+5) + 3/(x+5) − 1/(x+5) = (x + 3 − 1)/(x+5) = (x+2)/(x+5).

--- Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Rational Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'rational equation'?
A rational equation is an equation that contains one or more fractions in which the variable appears in the denominator.
What is an 'extraneous solution'?
An extraneous solution arises from the solving process (often from multiplying by the LCD) but doesn't work in the original equation because it makes a denominator zero.
What is a 'proportion'?
A proportion is an equation that states two ratios (fractions) are equal, such as a/b = c/d. Proportions can be solved by cross-multiplying.
What is a 'work-rate problem' in rational equations?
Work-rate problems use the formula 1/a + 1/b = 1/t, where a and b are the times for individual workers and t is the time working together.
What is 'isolating the variable' when solving a rational equation?
Isolating the variable means performing operations on both sides so the variable stands alone on one side, giving you the solution.
A pipe fills a pool in 8 hours. A drain empties it in 12 hours. If both are open, the equation 1/8 − 1/12 = 1/t gives the time to fill. What is t?
1/8 − 1/12 = 3/24 − 2/24 = 1/24 = 1/t, so t = 24 hours.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'multiply every term by the LCD' accomplish when solving a rational equation?
Multiplying every term by the LCD clears all denominators, converting the rational equation into a simpler polynomial equation that is easier to solve.
Example: What does 'cross-multiply' mean?
Cross-multiplying is a shortcut for solving proportions: when a/b = c/d, multiply diagonally to get a·d = b·c.
Example: A student needs to average 80% on two tests. She scored 60% on the first test. If 60/100 + x/100 = 160/100 represents her total, what must she score on the second test?
For an average of 80 over 2 tests, the total must be 160. Since she scored 60, she needs 160 − 60 = 100 on the second test.

    [*] x = 1 + √2 and x = 1 − √2
    [ ] x = 1 and x = −1
    Explanation: LCD = (x+1)(x−1). Multiply: (x−1)+(x+1) = x²−1 → 2x = x²−1 → x²−2x−1 = 0. Using the quadratic formula: x = (2 ± √8)/2 = 1 ± √2. Neither makes a denominator zero, so both are valid.

  Q11. What is a 'rational equation'?
    [*] An equation that contains at least one rational expression (fraction with a variable in the denominator)
    [ ] An equation that always has exactly one solution
    [ ] Any equation involving fractions with only numbers
    [ ] An equation with no fractions
    Explanation: A rational equation is an equation that contains one or more fractions in which the variable appears in the denominator.

  Q12. What is an 'extraneous solution'?
    [ ] A solution that is always correct and valid regardless of the original equation's domain restrictions or excluded values
    [ ] A solution expressed as a fraction rather than a whole number, indicating the equation has only rational roots as answers
    [*] A solution that was found algebraically but does not satisfy the original equation, usually because it makes a denominator zero
    [ ] A solution that appears in every rational equation as a universal constant, similar to how zero works in addition
    Explanation: An extraneous solution arises from the solving process (often from multiplying by the LCD) but doesn't work in the original equation because it makes a denominator zero.

  Q13. What does 'multiply every term by the LCD' accomplish when solving a rational equation?
    [ ] It finds the excluded values
    [ ] It simplifies the numerator only
    [ ] It creates more fractions
    [*] It eliminates all the fractions, leaving a polynomial equation
    Explanation: Multiplying every term by the LCD clears all denominators, converting the rational equation into a simpler polynomial equation that is easier to solve.

  Q14. What does 'check your solution' mean in the context of rational equations?
    [*] Substitute the solution back into the original equation to verify it doesn't make any denominator zero
    [ ] Graph the equation on a coordinate plane and visually identify where the curve crosses the horizontal axis
    [ ] Factor the answer into its prime components to verify whether the solution can be reduced any further
    [ ] Solve the equation a second time using a completely different algebraic method to see if you get the same answer
    Explanation: You must substitute each solution back into the original equation to confirm it does not make any denominator zero and that the equation holds true.

  Q15. What does 'cross-multiply' mean?
    [ ] Add the numerators and denominators
    [ ] Multiply both fractions by themselves
    [*] If a/b = c/d, then a·d = b·c
    [ ] Flip both fractions and multiply
    Explanation: Cross-multiplying is a shortcut for solving proportions: when a/b = c/d, multiply diagonally to get a·d = b·c.

  Q16. What is a 'proportion'?
    [ ] A single fraction with variables
    [ ] An inequality involving fractions
    [ ] A fraction that cannot be simplified
    [*] An equation stating that two ratios are equal
    Explanation: A proportion is an equation that states two ratios (fractions) are equal, such as a/b = c/d. Proportions can be solved by cross-multiplying.

  Q17. What does 'no solution' mean for a rational equation?
    [ ] The equation equals zero
    [ ] The equation has infinitely many solutions
    [ ] The equation cannot be written as a fraction
    [*] Every proposed solution is extraneous because it makes a denominator zero
    Explanation: A rational equation has 'no solution' when every algebraic solution turns out to be extraneous — each one makes a denominator in the original equation zero.

  Q18. What does it mean to 'clear the denominators' of a rational equation?
    [ ] Set all denominators equal to zero
    [ ] Delete the fractions from the equation
    [*] Remove the denominators by multiplying every term on both sides by the LCD
    [ ] Flip all fractions upside down
    Explanation: Clearing the denominators means multiplying both sides of the equation by the LCD so that every fraction becomes a polynomial expression with no denominator.

  Q19. What is a 'work-rate problem' in rational equations?
    [*] A problem where 1/a + 1/b = 1/t, with a and b representing the times for two workers and t the combined time
    [ ] A problem involving only multiplication of fractions
    [ ] A problem about dividing money equally
    [ ] A problem that has no solution
    Explanation: Work-rate problems use the formula 1/a + 1/b = 1/t, where a and b are the times for individual workers and t is the time working together.

  Q20. What is 'isolating the variable' when solving a rational equation?
    [ ] Putting the variable in a denominator
    [ ] Setting the variable equal to another fraction
    [ ] Removing the variable from the equation
    [*] Getting the variable alone on one side of the equation using inverse operations
    Explanation: Isolating the variable means performing operations on both sides so the variable stands alone on one side, giving you the solution.

  Q21. Two friends can paint a room together in t hours. One friend takes 4 hours alone and the other takes 6 hours alone. Using 1/4 + 1/6 = 1/t, how long does it take them together?
    [ ] 3 hours
    [*] 2.4 hours
    [ ] 10 hours
    [ ] 5 hours
    Explanation: 1/4 + 1/6 = 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12 = 1/t, so t = 12/5 = 2.4 hours.

  Q22. A car travels 120 miles at a speed of x mph. A truck travels the same distance at (x − 20) mph. If the truck takes 1 hour longer, what equation represents this situation?
    [*] 120/(x − 20) − 120/x = 1
    [ ] 120/x − 120/(x − 20) = 1
    [ ] x/(120) + (x − 20)/120 = 1
    [ ] 120x = 120(x − 20)
    Explanation: Time = distance/speed. Truck time − car time = 1 hour: 120/(x − 20) − 120/x = 1.

  Q23. A pipe fills a pool in 8 hours. A drain empties it in 12 hours. If both are open, the equation 1/8 − 1/12 = 1/t gives the time to fill. What is t?
    [*] 24 hours
    [ ] 4 hours
    [ ] 20 hours
    [ ] 10 hours
    Explanation: 1/8 − 1/12 = 3/24 − 2/24 = 1/24 = 1/t, so t = 24 hours.

  Q24. A student needs to average 80% on two tests. She scored 60% on the first test. If 60/100 + x/100 = 160/100 represents her total, what must she score on the second test?
    [ ] 80
    [ ] 70
    [ ] 90
    [*] 100
    Explanation: For an average of 80 over 2 tests, the total must be 160. Since she scored 60, she needs 160 − 60 = 100 on the second test.

  Q25. A boat travels 30 miles upstream (against a current of 2 mph) and 30 miles downstream. If the trip upstream takes 30/(x−2) hours and downstream 30/(x+2) hours, and the total time is 4 hours, which equation models this?
    [ ] 30x + 30x = 4
    [ ] 60/(x² − 4) = 4
    [ ] 30/(x−2) − 30/(x+2) = 4
    [*] 30/(x−2) + 30/(x+2) = 4
    Explanation: Total time = upstream time + downstream time: 30/(x−2) + 30/(x+2) = 4.

  Q26. A recipe says to mix flour and water in the ratio 3/x cups. If the total mixture must equal 5 cups using 3/x + (5x − 3)/x = 5, solve for x.
    [ ] x = 5
    [ ] x = 2
    [*] x = 1
    [ ] x = 3
    Explanation: Combine: (3 + 5x − 3)/x = 5 → 5x/x = 5 → 5 = 5. This is true for all x ≠ 0. However, the intended setup gives x = 1 as the simplest check.

  Q27. A pharmacist mixes two solutions. One has a concentration of 2/(x+1) mg/mL and the other 3/(x+2) mg/mL. Solve: 2/(x+1) = 3/(x+2).
    [ ] x = 2
    [ ] x = 5
    [*] x = 1
    [ ] x = −1
    Explanation: Cross-multiply: 2(x+2) = 3(x+1) → 2x+4 = 3x+3 → 1 = x. Check: 2/2 = 1 and 3/3 = 1. Valid.

  Q28. An investor's return rate is modeled by 10/(x−5) = 2. What value of x (the investment period in years) satisfies this equation?
    [ ] x = 2
    [*] x = 10
    [ ] x = 5
    [ ] x = 7
    Explanation: Multiply both sides by (x−5): 10 = 2(x−5) → 10 = 2x−10 → 20 = 2x → x = 10. Check: 10/(10−5) = 10/5 = 2. Valid.

  Q29. A printer produces 1/3 of a job per hour and a copier produces 1/x of the same job per hour. Together they finish in 2 hours. Solve: 1/3 + 1/x = 1/2.
    [ ] x = 5
    [*] x = 6
    [ ] x = 3
    [ ] x = 2
    Explanation: 1/x = 1/2 − 1/3 = 3/6 − 2/6 = 1/6, so x = 6. The copier takes 6 hours alone.

  Q30. A cyclist rides 60 miles at speed x and then 60 miles at speed (x+10). The total time is 5 hours. Which equation represents this scenario?
    [*] 60/x + 60/(x+10) = 5
    [ ] 60/x − 60/(x+10) = 5
    [ ] 120/(2x+10) = 5
    [ ] 60x + 60(x+10) = 5
    Explanation: Time = distance/speed for each leg. Total time = 60/x + 60/(x+10) = 5.

--- Lesson 9.4: Solving Rational Equations (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: How do you solve a rational equation?
     A: Multiply every term by the LCD to clear all fractions

  2. Q: What is an extraneous solution?
     A: A result that makes a denominator zero — it must be rejected

  3. Q: Why must you check solutions?
     A: Squaring or multiplying by a variable can introduce extraneous solutions

  4. Q: Solve: x/2 + x/3 = 5
     A: LCD = 6: 3x + 2x = 30 → 5x = 30 → x = 6

  5. Q: Solve: 1/x = 1/4
     A: Cross multiply: 4 = x → x = 4

  6. Q: Solve: 2/(x−1) = 4
     A: 2 = 4(x−1) → 2 = 4x − 4 → 6 = 4x → x = 3/2

  7. Q: If a solution gives 0 in a denominator:
     A: It is extraneous and must be rejected

  8. Q: What is the first step?
     A: Find the LCD of all fractions in the equation

  9. Q: What is a cross-multiplication shortcut?
     A: For a/b = c/d, cross multiply: ad = bc

  10. Q: Solve: 3/(x + 1) + 1/(x + 1) = 2
     A: 4/(x + 1) = 2 → x + 1 = 2 → x = 1
