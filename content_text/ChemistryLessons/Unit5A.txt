--- Lesson 5A.1: Introduction to Nomenclature (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Introduction to Nomenclature
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is chemical nomenclature?
Chemical nomenclature is the systematic method of naming chemical compounds according to IUPAC rules.
What is the correct name for NaCl?
NaCl is sodium chloride: the cation (Na⁺) is named first, then the anion (Cl⁻) with the -ide suffix.
What is the correct formula for calcium oxide?
Calcium (Ca²⁺) and oxide (O²⁻) combine in a 1:1 ratio since their charges balance, giving CaO.
What is the stock system in nomenclature used for?
The stock system uses Roman numerals in parentheses after the metal name to indicate its charge, e.g., iron(III) chloride for FeCl₃.
What is the formula for aluminum oxide?
Aluminum is Al³⁺ and oxide is O²⁻. Cross-multiplying the charges gives Al₂O₃ (2 aluminum ions and 3 oxide ions).
What is the correct name for MgBr₂?
MgBr₂ is magnesium bromide. Since magnesium only forms Mg²⁺, no Roman numeral is needed. Greek prefixes are not used for ionic compounds.
What is the name of the compound with formula MgO?
MgO: Mg²⁺ (magnesium, main-group metal, fixed +2) + O²⁻ (oxide). Name: magnesium oxide. No Roman numerals needed; no prefixes for ionic compounds.
What is the formula for calcium fluoride?
Ca²⁺ and F⁻: charges must balance. 2 F⁻ needed to balance Ca²⁺. Formula: CaF₂.

--- Lesson 5A.2: Net Charge (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Net Charge
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the charge of a sodium ion?
Sodium (Na) is in Group 1 and loses one electron to form Na⁺ with a +1 charge.
What is the charge on a calcium ion?
Calcium is in Group 2 and loses two electrons to form Ca²⁺.
What is the charge on an oxide ion?
Oxygen is in Group 16 and gains two electrons to form O²⁻ with a -2 charge.
What is the net charge calculation for Al₂O₃?
In Al₂O₃: 2 aluminum ions (Al³⁺) give +6 total, and 3 oxide ions (O²⁻) give -6 total. Net charge = +6 + (-6) = 0.
What is the formula for a compound of potassium and sulfur?
Potassium forms K⁺ and sulfur forms S²⁻. Two K⁺ ions are needed to balance one S²⁻: 2(+1) + (-2) = 0, giving K₂S.
What is the charge on a nitride ion (N³⁻)?
Nitrogen is in Group 15 and gains 3 electrons to form N³⁻ with a -3 charge.
In the formula Fe₂O₃, what is the charge of each iron ion?
In Fe₂O₃, total negative charge is 3×(-2)=-6. For the 2 iron ions to balance: 2x=-(-6), so x=+3. Each iron ion is Fe³⁺.
What is the formula for lithium fluoride?
Li⁺ and F⁻ have equal and opposite charges, so they combine 1:1 to give LiF.
What is the formula for barium chloride?
Barium forms Ba²⁺ and chlorine forms Cl⁻. Two Cl⁻ ions balance one Ba²⁺: +2 + 2(-1) = 0, giving BaCl₂.
What is the charge of a phosphide ion?
Phosphorus is in Group 15 and gains 3 electrons to form P³⁻ (phosphide), achieving an argon electron configuration.
What is the charge on the sulfate ion SO₄²⁻?
Sulfate ion SO₄²⁻ carries a 2− charge. This is a polyatomic anion (multiple atoms with overall negative charge).
In the compound Al₂O₃, what is the charge on each aluminum ion?
Al₂O₃: 3 oxide ions each at 2− = 6− total. 2 Al ions must provide 6+ total → each Al = 3+. Al is always Al³⁺ in compounds.
What is the charge balance in CaCl₂?
CaCl₂: Ca²⁺ (+2) + 2×Cl⁻ (2×−1=−2) = 0. Charge is balanced. Formula: CaCl₂.
In the formula Na₂SO₄, what is the charge on the sulfate group?
Na₂SO₄: 2 Na⁺ gives +2. To balance, SO₄ must be 2−. Sulfate is always SO₄²⁻.

--- Lesson 5A.3: Bond Formation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Bond Formation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a crystal lattice?
A repeating 3D arrangement of ions in an ionic compound. This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
What is the electron configuration of Na⁺?
Same as neon (1s²2s²2p⁶). This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
What is a formula unit?
The simplest ratio of ions in an ionic compound. This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
What is the octet rule?
Atoms tend to gain, lose, or share electrons to have 8 valence electrons. This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
What is the lattice energy?
The energy released when gaseous ions form a solid ionic compound. This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
What is an electrolyte?
A substance that conducts electricity when dissolved in water. This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
What is the coordination number in a crystal lattice?
The number of nearest neighbor ions surrounding an ion. This is an important concept in Bond Formation.
