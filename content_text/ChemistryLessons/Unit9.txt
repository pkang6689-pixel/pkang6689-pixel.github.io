--- Lesson 9.1:  (Summary) ---

  Notes
Key Concepts: Solution Nomenclature

Core Definitions

  • Solution: A homogeneous mixture of two or more substances evenly distributed at the molecular level.
  • Solute: The substance that is dissolved in the solution (usually present in smaller amount).
  • Solvent: The substance that does the dissolving (usually present in larger amount).
  • Aqueous Solution: A solution where water is the solvent, denoted by (aq) in chemical equations.

--- Lesson 9.2:  (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Concentration
Core Definitions

  • Concentration: A measure of how much solute is dissolved in a given amount of solution.
  • Concentrated Solution: Contains a large amount of solute relative to solvent.
  • Dilute Solution: Contains a small amount of solute relative to solvent.
  • Percent Concentration: (mass of solute / mass of solution) × 100%.

--- Lesson 9.3:  (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Dilution
Core Definitions

  • Dilution: The process of reducing the concentration of a solution by adding more solvent.
  • Dilution Equation: M₁V₁ = M₂V₂, where M is molarity and V is volume.
  • Stock Solution: A concentrated solution that is diluted to make a less concentrated solution.
  • Key Principle: The amount of solute stays the same during dilution — only solvent is added.

--- Lesson 9.4:  (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Molarity
Core Definitions

  • Molarity (M): The number of moles of solute per liter of solution. M = mol / L.

--- Lesson 9.5:  (Summary) ---

  Notes
Key Concepts: Solution Types

Core Definitions

  • Saturated Solution: Contains the maximum amount of dissolved solute at a given temperature.
  • Unsaturated Solution: Contains less than the maximum amount of solute — more can dissolve.
  • Supersaturated Solution: Contains more dissolved solute than a saturated solution; unstable.
  • Solubility: The maximum amount of solute that dissolves in a given amount of solvent at a specific temperature.

--- Lesson 9.6:  (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Factors Affecting Solubility
Core Definitions

  • Like Dissolves Like: Polar solvents dissolve polar/ionic solutes; nonpolar solvents dissolve nonpolar solutes.
  • Temperature Effect: Increasing temperature usually increases solubility of solids but decreases solubility of gases.
  • Pressure Effect: Increasing pressure increases solubility of gases (Henry's Law) but has little effect on solids/liquids.
  • Surface Area & Agitation: Crushing solute and stirring increases the rate of dissolving (not solubility itself).

--- Lesson 9.7:  (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Colligative Properties
Core Definitions

  • Colligative Properties: Properties that depend on the number of solute particles, not their identity.
  • Boiling Point Elevation: Adding solute raises the boiling point of a solution above that of the pure solvent.
  • Freezing Point Depression: Adding solute lowers the freezing point of a solution below that of the pure solvent.
  • Osmotic Pressure: Pressure required to prevent osmosis across a semipermeable membrane.

--- Lesson 9.8:  (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solubility Curves
Core Definitions

  • Solubility Curve: A graph showing the relationship between solubility of a substance and temperature.
  • Reading the Curve: Points on the line = saturated; below = unsaturated; above = supersaturated.
  • Endothermic Dissolving: Most solid solutes show increasing solubility with temperature (curve slopes up).
  • Gas Solubility: Gas curves slope downward — gases become less soluble as temperature increases.
