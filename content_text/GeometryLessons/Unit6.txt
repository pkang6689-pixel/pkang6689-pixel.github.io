    [ ] (n + 2) × 180°
    [*] (n − 2) × 180°
    [ ] n × 180°
    [ ] 360° × n
    Explanation: Sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon = (n-2) × 180°. Each interior angle of a regular polygon = (n-2) × 180° / n. The answer is (n − 2) × 180°. --- Lesson 6.2: Parallelograms (Quiz) ---

  Q1. In a parallelogram, opposite sides are:
    [ ] Parallel but not congruent
    [ ] Congruent but not parallel
    [ ] Perpendicular
    [*] Parallel and congruent
    Explanation: A parallelogram is a Quadrilateral with both pairs of opposite sides parallel. Properties Opposite sides are congruent. The other options — 'Perpendicular', 'Parallel but not congruent', and 'Congruent but not parallel' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q2. Consecutive angles in a parallelogram are:
    [ ] Complementary
    [ ] Congruent
    [ ] Right angles
    [*] Supplementary
    Explanation: Consecutive angles are supplementary (sum to 180°). AB = CD and AD = BC (opposite sides congruent). ∠A = ∠C and ∠B = ∠D (opposite angles congruent). ∠A + ∠B = 180° (consecutive angles supplementary). The other options — 'Congruent', 'Complementary', and 'Right angles' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q3. The diagonals of a parallelogram:
    [ ] Are congruent
    [ ] Are perpendicular
    [ ] Bisect the angles
    [*] Bisect each other
    Explanation: Diagonals bisect each other. Diagonals AC and BD bisect each other at Point M (AM = MC, BM = MD). The other options — 'Are congruent', 'Are perpendicular', and 'Bisect the angles' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q4. If ∠A = 70° in parallelogram ABCD, what is ∠B?
    [ ] 140°
    [ ] 90°
    [ ] 70°
    [*] 110°
    Explanation: Using Properties If ABCD is a parallelogram: AB ∥ CD and AD ∥ BC (definition). The other options — '70°', '90°', and '140°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q5. If ∠A = 70° in parallelogram ABCD, what is ∠C?
    [*] 70°
    [ ] 140°
    [ ] 110°
    [ ] 90°
    Explanation: Using Properties If ABCD is a parallelogram: AB ∥ CD and AD ∥ BC (definition). The other options — '110°', '90°', and '140°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q6. The diagonals of a parallelogram divide it into:
    [ ] Four right triangles
    [ ] Two congruent triangles
    [*] Two pairs of congruent triangles
    [ ] Four congruent triangles
    Explanation: A parallelogram is a Quadrilateral with both pairs of opposite sides parallel. The other options — 'Four congruent triangles', 'Two congruent triangles', and 'Four right triangles' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q7. If one diagonal of a parallelogram is 16 cm, each half (at the intersection) is:
    [ ] 16 cm
    [*] 8 cm
    [ ] 4 cm
    [ ] 32 cm
    Explanation: The correct answer is '8 cm'. The other options — '16 cm', '4 cm', and '32 cm' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

    [ ] One

    [ ] Three

    Explanation: Metric prefixes: kilo = 10³, centi = 10⁻², milli = 10⁻³, micro = 10⁻⁶, nano = 10⁻⁹. To convert, multiply/divide by the appropriate power of 10. The answer is Five. The other options — 'Three', 'One', and 'Seven' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q2. If both pairs of opposite sides of a quadrilateral are congruent, it is a:
    [*] Parallelogram

The other options — 'Rectangle only', 'Rhombus only', and 'Trapezoid' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q3. If one pair of opposite sides is parallel and congruent, the shape is a:
    [ ] Trapezoid

    Explanation: To prove a Quadrilateral is a parallelogram, you can use any one of the following tests: Both pairs of opposite sides are parallel (definition). Having congruent diagonals alone does NOT prove a parallelogram (it could be an isosceles trapezoid). The other options — 'Trapezoid', 'Kite', and 'Rectangle only' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q4. If only one pair of sides is parallel, the shape is a:

Having congruent diagonals alone does NOT prove a parallelogram (it could be an isosceles trapezoid). The other options — 'Rectangle', 'Square', and 'Kite' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q1. A rhombus is a parallelogram with:
    [*] Four congruent sides
    [ ] Congruent diagonals
    [ ] Four right angles
    [ ] No parallel sides
    Explanation: Rhombus A rhombus is a parallelogram with four congruent sides. It has all the properties of both: Four congruent sides (rhombus). The other options — 'Four right angles', 'Congruent diagonals', and 'No parallel sides' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q2. The diagonals of a rhombus are:
    [*] Perpendicular
    [ ] Congruent
    [ ] Parallel
    [ ] Neither perpendicular nor congruent
    Explanation: Diagonals are congruent (rectangle), perpendicular (rhombus), and bisect each other and the angles. The other options — 'Congruent', 'Parallel', and 'Neither perpendicular nor congruent' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q3. Each diagonal of a rhombus:
    [ ] Is congruent to a side
    [ ] Passes through all four vertices
    [ ] Is parallel to a side
    [*] Bisects a pair of opposite angles
    Explanation: Each diagonal bisects a pair of opposite angles. Diagonals are congruent (rectangle), perpendicular (rhombus), and bisect each other and the angles. The other options — 'Is congruent to a side', 'Is parallel to a side', and 'Passes through all four vertices' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q4. A square is both:
    [ ] A rhombus and a trapezoid
    [*] A rectangle and a rhombus
    [ ] A rectangle and a kite
    [ ] A trapezoid and a kite
    Explanation: Square A square is a parallelogram that is both a rectangle and a rhombus. Quadrilateral Hierarchy Parallelogram → Rectangle (right angles), Rhombus (equal sides) → Square (both). The other options — 'A trapezoid and a kite', 'A rectangle and a kite', and 'A rhombus and a trapezoid' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q5. Which property do squares have that rhombi do not necessarily have?
    [*] Congruent diagonals
    [ ] Diagonals that bisect each other
    [ ] Perpendicular diagonals
    [ ] Four congruent sides
    Explanation: Diagonals are congruent (rectangle), perpendicular (rhombus), and bisect each other and the angles. Diagonals bisect each other (parallelogram property). The other options — 'Perpendicular diagonals', 'Four congruent sides', and 'Diagonals that bisect each other' — are all valid examples, making 'Congruent diagonals' the correct exception.

  Q6. If a rhombus has diagonals of 6 and 8, each side measures:
    [ ] 7 (found by averaging the two full diagonal lengths 6 and 8)
    [*] 5 (using half-diagonals 3 and 4 with Pythagorean theorem)
    [ ] 14 (found by summing the two full diagonal lengths 6 and 8)
    [ ] 10 (found by adding each full diagonal and dividing by the number of sides)
    Explanation: Diagonals are congruent (rectangle), perpendicular (rhombus), and bisect each other and the angles. The other options — '7', '10', and '14' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q7. Is every square a rhombus?
    [ ] Only if it has right angles
    [*] Yes
    [ ] No
    [ ] Only if the diagonals are perpendicular
    Explanation: Square A square is a parallelogram that is both a rectangle and a rhombus. Quadrilateral Hierarchy Parallelogram → Rectangle (right angles), Rhombus (equal sides) → Square (both). The other options — 'No', 'Only if it has right angles', and 'Only if the diagonals are perpendicular' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

--- Lesson 6.7: Regular Polygons and Symmetry (Quiz) ---

  Q1. A regular polygon is:
    [*] Both equilateral and equiangular
    [ ] Only equiangular
    [ ] Only equilateral
    [ ] Any polygon with more than 4 sides
    Explanation: Regular Polygon A regular Polygon is both equilateral (all sides congruent) and equiangular (all angles congruent). Center and Apothem The center of a regular Polygon is equidistant from all vertices (circumradius) and from all sides (apothem). The other options — 'Only equilateral', 'Only equiangular', and 'Any polygon with more than 4 sides' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q2. A regular hexagon has how many lines of symmetry?
    [ ] 3
    [ ] 2
    [*] 6
    [ ] 12
    Explanation: Regular hexagon — 6 lines of symmetry. The smallest rotation Angle that maps it onto itself is 360°/n. The other options — '3', '12', and '2' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q3. The smallest rotation that maps a regular pentagon onto itself is:
    [ ] 60°
    [ ] 90°
    [ ] 45°
    [*] 72°
    Explanation: A rotation turns a figure around a fixed point by a given angle. 90° rotations: (x,y) → (-y,x). 180°: (x,y) → (-x,-y). The answer is 72°. The other options — '60°', '45°', and '90°' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q4. The apothem of a regular polygon is:
    [ ] The perimeter divided by n
    [ ] Half the diagonal
    [ ] The distance from center to a vertex
    [*] The perpendicular distance from the center to a side
    Explanation: The apothem is the perpendicular distance from the center to a side. Center and Apothem The center of a regular Polygon is equidistant from all vertices (circumradius) and from all sides (apothem). The other options — 'The distance from center to a vertex', 'Half the diagonal', and 'The perimeter divided by n' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q5. Area of a regular polygon formula:
    [ ] A = base × height
    [ ] A = side²
    [*] A = (1/2) × apothem × perimeter
    [ ] A = π × r²
    Explanation: Area = (1/2) × apothem × perimeter. Center and Apothem The center of a regular Polygon is equidistant from all vertices (circumradius) and from all sides (apothem). The other options — 'A = side²', 'A = π × r²', and 'A = base × height' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q6. How many lines of symmetry does an equilateral triangle have?
    [ ] 0
    [*] 3
    [ ] 1
    [ ] 6
    Explanation: Equilateral Triangle — 3 lines of symmetry. The smallest rotation Angle that maps it onto itself is 360°/n. The other options — '1', '6', and '0' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.

  Q7. Rotational symmetry order of a square:
    [ ] 8
    [*] 4
    [ ] 2
    [ ] 1
    Explanation: A rotation turns a figure around a fixed point by a given angle. 90° rotations: (x,y) → (-y,x). 180°: (x,y) → (-x,-y). The answer is 4. The other options — '2', '8', and '1' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
