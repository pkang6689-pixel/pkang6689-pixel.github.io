  Key Concepts: Sampling Distribution of Sample Means
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Sampling distribution'?
"Sampling distribution" means: The distribution of all possible values of a statistic computed from every possible sample of the same size from a population. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Standard error of x̄'?
"Standard error of x̄" means: σ/√n; the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample mean; decreases as sample size increases. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Unbiased estimator'?

What is the definition of 'Variability of a statistic'?

What is the definition of 'Sampling variability'?
"Sampling variability" means: The natural variation of a statistic from sample to sample; different random samples yield different values of x̄. The other options describe different concepts.

  Key Concepts: Central Limit Theorem
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Central Limit Theorem (CLT)'?
"Central Limit Theorem (CLT)" means: For large samples, the sampling distribution of x̄ is approximately N(μ, σ/√n) regardless of the population distribution shape. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Normal approximation'?
"Normal approximation" means: Using a normal distribution to approximate the sampling distribution of a statistic when the CLT conditions are satisfied. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Sample size condition'?
"Sample size condition" means: General guideline: n ≥ 30 for the CLT to produce a good normal approximation; smaller n may suffice for populations already near normal. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Shape of sampling distribution'?
"Shape of sampling distribution" means: Approaches normality as n increases; the more skewed or irregular the population, the larger n must be for good approximation. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'CLT for proportions'?
"CLT for proportions" means: The sampling distribution of p̂ is approximately N(p, √(p(1-p)/n)) when np ≥ 10 and n(1-p) ≥ 10. The other options describe different concepts.

Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Addition rule for means'?
"Addition rule for means" means: E(X + Y) = E(X) + E(Y) and E(X - Y) = E(X) - E(Y); applies regardless of independence. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Addition rule for variances'?
"Addition rule for variances" means: Var(X + Y) = Var(X) + Var(Y) and Var(X - Y) = Var(X) + Var(Y); ONLY valid when X and Y are independent. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Standard deviation of a sum/difference'?
"Standard deviation of a sum/difference" means: SD(X ± Y) = √(Var(X) + Var(Y)) for independent random variables; note that SDs do NOT simply add. The other options describe different concepts.
