  Key Concepts: Data Collection and Types of Variables
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Categorical variable'?
"Categorical variable" means: A variable that places individuals into groups or categories (e.g., gender, political party, blood type) and is summarized using counts or proportions. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Quantitative variable'?
"Quantitative variable" means: A variable that takes numerical values for which arithmetic operations (mean, standard deviation) are meaningful; can be discrete (counts) or continuous (measurements). The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Observational unit'?
"Observational unit" means: The individual person, animal, object, or entity on which data are collected in a study. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Explanatory variable'?
"Explanatory variable" means: The variable suspected of influencing or predicting the response variable in a study. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Response variable'?
"Response variable" means: The outcome variable being measured or observed; the variable we want to explain or predict. The other options describe different concepts.

--- Lesson 1.1: Data Collection and Types of Variables (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: Define: Population
     A: The entire group or set of items or individuals that are the subject of study

  2. Q: Define: Sample
     A: A subset of the population selected for study to make inferences about the entire population

  3. Q: Define: Random Sampling
     A: A method of selecting samples where every member of the population has an equal chance of being selected

  4. Q: Define: Standard Deviation
     A: A measure of the spread or dispersion of data values from the mean

  5. Q: Define: Probability Distribution
     A: A mathematical function that gives the probabilities of all possible outcomes of a random experiment

  6. Q: Define: Hypothesis Test
     A: A statistical procedure for making decisions about population parameters based on sample data

  7. Q: Define: Correlation
     A: A measure of the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables

  8. Q: Define: Regression Analysis
     A: A statistical method for modeling the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables

  9. Q: Which of the following best describes a population in statistical terms?
     A: The entire group of individuals or objects about which information is desired

  10. Q: Which sampling method involves selecting every kth item from a population list?
     A: Systematic sampling
