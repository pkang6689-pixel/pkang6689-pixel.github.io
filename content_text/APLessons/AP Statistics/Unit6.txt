--- Lesson 6.1: Confidence Intervals for Proportions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Confidence Intervals for Proportions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Confidence interval for p'?
"Confidence interval for p" means: p̂ ± z*·√(p̂(1-p̂)/n); a range of plausible values for the population proportion based on sample data. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Confidence level'?
"Confidence level" means: The long-run proportion of intervals that capture the true parameter; e.g., 95% means 95 out of 100 intervals would contain p. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Margin of error'?
"Margin of error" means: z*·√(p̂(1-p̂)/n); the maximum likely difference between p̂ and the true p at the given confidence level. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Critical value (z*)'?
"Critical value (z*)" means: The z-score corresponding to the desired confidence level: z* = 1.645 for 90%, 1.96 for 95%, 2.576 for 99%. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Sample size determination'?
"Sample size determination" means: n = (z*/ME)²·p̂(1-p̂); formula for choosing n to achieve a desired margin of error; use p̂ = 0.5 for conservative estimate. The other options describe different concepts.

"Null hypothesis (H₀)" means: The claim of no effect, no difference, or no change; the default assumption we test against using sample data. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Alternative hypothesis (Hₐ)'?
