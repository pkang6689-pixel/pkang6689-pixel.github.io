--- Lesson 4.1: Basic Probability Rules (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Basic Probability Rules
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Addition rule (general)'?
"Addition rule (general)" means: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B); applies to any two events, subtracting the overlap to avoid double-counting. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Mutually exclusive events'?
"Mutually exclusive events" means: Events that cannot occur simultaneously; P(A and B) = 0, so P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B). The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Multiplication rule (general)'?
"Multiplication rule (general)" means: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A); the probability of both events equals the probability of the first times the conditional probability of the second. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Complement'?
"Complement" means: The complement of event A (Aᶜ) consists of all outcomes not in A; P(Aᶜ) = 1 - P(A). The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Union and intersection'?
"Union and intersection" means: A ∪ B ("A or B") includes outcomes in either event; A ∩ B ("A and B") includes only outcomes in both events. The other options describe different concepts.

--- Lesson 4.2: Conditional Probability (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conditional Probability
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Conditional probability'?
"Conditional probability" means: P(A|B) = P(A and B)/P(B); the probability of A occurring given that B has already occurred or is known to be true. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Bayes' Theorem'?
"Bayes' Theorem" means: P(A|B) = P(B|A)·P(A)/P(B); reverses conditional probability to update the probability of A after observing evidence B. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Tree diagram'?
"Tree diagram" means: A branching diagram showing sequential probabilities along paths; multiplying along a path gives joint probability, adding across paths gives marginal probability. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Prior probability'?
"Prior probability" means: The initial probability of an event before new evidence is considered; the starting point in Bayesian reasoning. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'False positive/negative'?
"False positive/negative" means: A false positive incorrectly identifies a condition as present; a false negative incorrectly identifies it as absent. The other options describe different concepts.

  Key Concepts: Binomial and Geometric Distributions
Overview

Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Binomial distribution'?
"Binomial distribution" means: Models the count of successes in n independent trials, each with success probability p; requires BINS: Binary outcomes, Independent trials, fixed Number, Same probability. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Binomial probability formula'?
"Binomial probability formula" means: P(X = k) = C(n,k)·p^k·(1-p)^(n-k), where C(n,k) = n!/(k!(n-k)!) counts the number of ways to choose k successes. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Geometric distribution'?
"Geometric distribution" means: Models the number of trials until the first success in independent trials with constant probability p; P(X = k) = (1-p)^(k-1)·p. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Mean of binomial'?
"Mean of binomial" means: μ = np; the expected number of successes in n trials. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Mean of geometric'?
"Mean of geometric" means: μ = 1/p; the expected number of trials needed to achieve the first success. The other options describe different concepts.
