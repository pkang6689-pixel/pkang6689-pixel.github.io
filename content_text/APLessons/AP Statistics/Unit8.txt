--- Lesson 8.1: Chi-Square Test for Independence (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Chi-Square Test for Independence
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Chi-square test for independence'?
"Chi-square test for independence" means: Tests H₀: two categorical variables are independent (not associated) in a single population. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Expected count formula'?
"Expected count formula" means: E = (row total × column total) / grand total; the count expected in each cell if the variables were independent. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Interpreting results'?
"Interpreting results" means: A significant result means the variables are associated; examine which cells contribute most to χ² to understand the nature of the association. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Degrees of freedom'?
In Chi-Square Test for Independence, "Degrees of freedom" refers to: df = (r - 1)(c - 1), where r = number of rows and c = number of columns in the two-way table.
What is the definition of 'Conditions'?
In Chi-Square Test for Independence, "Conditions" refers to: Random sample, all expected counts ≥ 5 (or no more than 20% below 5), and independent observations.
