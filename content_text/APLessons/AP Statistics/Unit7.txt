--- Lesson 7.1: t-Distribution and t-Procedures (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: t-Distribution and t-Procedures
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'One-sample t-test'?
"One-sample t-test" means: Tests H₀: μ = μ₀ using t = (x̄ - μ₀)/(s/√n) with df = n - 1; used when σ is unknown. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'One-sample t-interval'?
"One-sample t-interval" means: x̄ ± t*·(s/√n); a confidence interval for the population mean using the t-distribution critical value. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Conditions for t-procedures'?
"Conditions for t-procedures" means: Random sample, 10% condition (n < 10% of population), and Normal/Large Sample (population approximately normal or n ≥ 30). The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 't* critical value'?
"t* critical value" means: The t-value with area C in the middle of the t-distribution with df = n-1; found from t-table or technology for the desired confidence level. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Standard error of x̄'?
In t-Distribution and t-Procedures, "Standard error of x̄" refers to: SE = s/√n; estimates the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of x̄ using the sample standard deviation s.

--- Lesson 7.2: Confidence Intervals for Means (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Confidence Intervals for Means
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Confidence interval for μ'?
"Confidence interval for μ" means: x̄ ± t*·(s/√n); gives a range of plausible values for the population mean based on sample data. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Margin of error (for means)'?
"Margin of error (for means)" means: t*·(s/√n); the maximum likely difference between x̄ and μ at the stated confidence level. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Interpretation of confidence level'?
"Interpretation of confidence level" means: If we repeated the sampling process many times, C% of the resulting intervals would capture the true population mean. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Effect of sample size'?
"Effect of sample size" means: Increasing n decreases the margin of error proportionally to 1/√n, producing a narrower (more precise) interval. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Required sample size'?
"Required sample size" means: n = (t*·s/ME)²; the sample size needed to achieve a desired margin of error, often requiring an initial pilot study to estimate s. The other options describe different concepts.
