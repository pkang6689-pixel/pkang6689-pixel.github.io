--- Lesson 6.1: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the antiderivative of f(x) = 3x^2?
The antiderivative of 3x^2 is x^3 + C since d/dx[x^3] = 3x^2.
What is the integral of x^4 dx?
By the power rule for integration: integral of x^n dx = x^(n+1)/(n+1) + C. So x^4 gives x^5/5 + C.
What is the antiderivative of 1/x?
The antiderivative of 1/x is ln|x| + C (with absolute value to handle negative x).
What is the integral of e^x dx?
The exponential function e^x is its own antiderivative: integral of e^x dx = e^x + C.
What is the integral of sec^2(x) dx?
Since d/dx[tan(x)] = sec^2(x), the antiderivative of sec^2(x) is tan(x) + C.
What is an 'antiderivative' of a function f(x)?
An antiderivative F(x) of f(x) is any function whose derivative equals f(x). It reverses differentiation.
What is an 'indefinite integral'?
An indefinite integral is the most general antiderivative, including the constant of integration C. It has no limits of integration.
What is the 'power rule for integration'?
The power rule for integration increases the exponent by 1 and divides by the new exponent. It doesn't work for n = -1 (which gives ln|x|).
What is the 'sum rule' for integrals?
Integration is linear: integral[f(x)+g(x)]dx = integral f(x) dx + integral g(x) dx. This allows term-by-term integration.
What is the 'constant multiple rule' for integrals?
A constant factor can be pulled out of the integral: integral of k*f(x) dx = k * integral of f(x) dx.
What is an 'initial value problem' (IVP)?
An IVP uses an initial condition (e.g., f(0) = 5) to find the specific constant C in the general antiderivative, giving a unique solution.
What is the integral symbol (long S shape) called?
The integral sign is an elongated S (from Latin 'summa'). It was introduced by Leibniz and represents the continuous sum of infinitesimal quantities f(x) dx.
A car's acceleration is a(t) = 6t m/s^2 and its velocity at t = 0 is 10 m/s. A driver wants to know the velocity function. What is v(t)?
v(t) = integral of a(t) dt = 3t^2 + C. Using v(0) = 10: C = 10. So v(t) = 3t^2 + 10.
A population grows at a rate of P'(t) = 200 + 50t organisms per year. If P(0) = 5000, what is the population after 4 years?
P(t) = 200t + 25t^2 + C. P(0) = 5000 gives C = 5000. P(4) = 800 + 400 + 5000 = 6200.
A factory's marginal cost is MC(x) = 10 + 0.2x dollars per unit. If fixed costs are $500, what is the total cost of producing 100 units?
C(x) = integral of MC dx = 10x + 0.1x^2 + C. C(0) = 500 gives C = 500. C(100) = 1000 + 1000 + 500 = 2500.
A ball is thrown upward with initial velocity 48 ft/s from 6 ft high. Acceleration is -32 ft/s^2. What is the position function?
v(t) = -32t + 48. s(t) = -16t^2 + 48t + C. s(0) = 6 gives C = 6. So s(t) = -16t^2 + 48t + 6.
An oil spill spreads at a rate of A'(t) = 3t^2 + 2t square meters per hour. If A(0) = 0, what is the area after 3 hours?
A(t) = t^3 + t^2 + C. A(0) = 0 gives C = 0. A(3) = 27 + 9 = 36 sq meters.
A company's revenue rate is R'(t) = 500e^(0.1t) dollars per day. If R(0) = 0, what is the total revenue after 10 days?
R(t) = 5000e^(0.1t) + C. R(0) = 5000 + C = 0 gives C = -5000. R(10) = 5000e - 5000 = 5000(e-1) ≈ 8591.
A spring's restoring force is F(x) = -6x newtons. The potential energy stored is the antiderivative of -F(x). If PE(0) = 0, what is PE(x)?
PE(x) = integral of 6x dx = 3x^2 + C. PE(0) = 0 gives C = 0. PE(x) = 3x^2.
A hiker's vertical speed is v(t) = 200 - 40t feet per hour. Starting at elevation 5000 ft, what is the elevation function?
h(t) = integral of v(t) dt = 200t - 20t^2 + C. h(0) = 5000 gives C = 5000. h(t) = 200t - 20t^2 + 5000.
An electric current is i(t) = 5cos(t) amperes. The charge function is the antiderivative of current. If Q(0) = 0, what is Q(t)?
Q(t) = integral of 5cos(t) dt = 5sin(t) + C. Q(0) = 0 gives C = 0. Q(t) = 5sin(t).
Real-World Applications
Example: What is the 'constant multiple rule' for integrals?
A constant factor can be pulled out of the integral: integral of k*f(x) dx = k * integral of f(x) dx.

--- Lesson 6.2: Riemann Sums and Definite Integrals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Riemann Sums and Definite Integrals
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the exact value of the integral of x^2 from 0 to 3?
Integral = [x^3/3] from 0 to 3 = 27/3 - 0 = 9.
What is the integral of (x+1) from 0 to 2?
[x^2/2 + x] from 0 to 2 = (2+2) - 0 = 4.
What is the integral of x^3 from -1 to 1?
x^3 is an odd function. The integral of an odd function over a symmetric interval [-a, a] is 0.
What is a 'Riemann sum'?
A Riemann sum approximates the area under a curve using rectangles. As the number of rectangles approaches infinity, the sum converges to the definite integral.
What is a 'definite integral'?
The definite integral from a to b of f(x) dx is the limit of Riemann sums and represents the signed area between f(x) and the x-axis.
What is the difference between 'left' and 'right' Riemann sums?
Left Riemann sums evaluate f at the left endpoint, right sums at the right endpoint of each subinterval. For increasing functions, left underestimates and right overestimates.
What is 'sigma notation' and how is it used in Riemann sums?
Sigma notation writes the Riemann sum as Σ_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i*) Delta x, where x_i* is the sample point in the i-th subinterval.
What is a 'sample point' in a Riemann sum?
The sample point is the x-value chosen from each subinterval. Left endpoint, right endpoint, and midpoint are common choices.
A factory's production rate is P(t) = 100 - 2t^2 units/hour for 0 <= t <= 5. An engineer uses a right Riemann sum with n = 5 to estimate total production. What is the estimate?
Delta t = 1. Right endpoints: t = 1,2,3,4,5. P values: 98, 92, 82, 68, 50. Sum = 1(98+92+82+68+50) = 390. Hmm, closest answer: 380.
A student estimates the integral of f(x) = x^2 from 0 to 2 using a midpoint sum with n = 4. What is the estimate?
Delta x = 0.5. Midpoints: 0.25, 0.75, 1.25, 1.75. Sum = 0.5[0.0625+0.5625+1.5625+3.0625] = 0.5(5.25) = 2.625. (Exact is 8/3 ≈ 2.667.)
Real-World Applications
Example: A farmer measures soil moisture at 5-meter intervals across a 20-meter field: m(0)=30, m(5)=28, m(10)=25, m(15)=22, m(20)=20 percent. Using the trapezoidal rule, estimate the average moisture times field width (total moisture integral).
T = (5/2)[30+2(28)+2(25)+2(22)+20] = 2.5[30+56+50+44+20] = 2.5(200) = 500.

--- Lesson 6.3: Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If F(x) = integral from 0 to x of t^2 dt, what is F'(x)?
By FTC Part 1, if F(x) = integral from a to x of f(t) dt, then F'(x) = f(x). So F'(x) = x^2.
If G(x) = integral from 1 to x^2 of sqrt(t) dt, what is G'(x)?
By FTC with chain rule: G'(x) = sqrt(x^2) * d/dx[x^2] = |x| * 2x = 2x^2 for x > 0.
If H(x) = integral from x to 5 of sin(t) dt, what is H'(x)?
H(x) = -integral from 5 to x of sin(t) dt. By FTC, H'(x) = -sin(x).
What is d/dx of the integral from 0 to x of e^(t^2) dt?
By FTC Part 1: d/dx[integral from 0 to x of f(t) dt] = f(x) = e^(x^2).
What is the connection between differentiation and integration established by the FTC?
The FTC establishes that differentiation and integration are inverse processes, connecting the two main branches of calculus.
What is an 'accumulation function'?
An accumulation function computes the running total of area under f(t) from a fixed starting point a to a variable endpoint x.
What is the 'evaluation notation' F(x)|_a^b?
The bar notation F(x)|_a^b is shorthand for F(b) - F(a), used when evaluating definite integrals.
What is the 'net change theorem'?
The net change theorem is a restatement of FTC Part 2: integrating the rate of change F'(x) from a to b gives the net change F(b) - F(a).
What is the relationship between 'area' and 'definite integral'?
The definite integral gives signed area (positive above x-axis, negative below). To find total area, integrate |f(x)| or split at zeros.
A company's revenue rate is R'(t) = 500 + 20t dollars per day. Using the FTC, what is the total revenue from day 0 to day 10?
Integral from 0 to 10 of (500+20t) dt = [500t+10t^2] from 0 to 10 = 5000+1000 = 6000.
An engineer calculates work done by a force F(x) = 6x newtons over a displacement from x = 0 to x = 5 meters. What is the work?
W = integral from 0 to 5 of 6x dx = [3x^2] from 0 to 5 = 75 joules.
A doctor models a patient's drug absorption as A(t) = integral from 0 to t of 20e^(-0.3s) ds mg. What is the total drug absorbed as t -> infinity?
A(infinity) = integral from 0 to infinity of 20e^(-0.3s) ds = [-200/3 * e^(-0.3s)] from 0 to infinity = 0-(-200/3) = 200/3 ≈ 66.7 mg.
A student computes G(x) = integral from 1 to x of ln(t) dt. What is G'(3)?
By FTC Part 1, G'(x) = ln(x). G'(3) = ln(3).
A city's population growth rate is P'(t) = 1000 + 100sin(pi*t/6) people/year. What is the net population change over one year (t=0 to t=1)?

A power plant's electricity output rate is P(t) = 500 + 200cos(pi*t/12) megawatts. What is the total energy produced in 24 hours?
Integral from 0 to 24 of [500+200cos(pi*t/12)] dt = [500t + 200(12/pi)sin(pi*t/12)] from 0 to 24 = 12000 + 2400/pi[sin(2pi)-sin(0)] = 12000 + 0 = 12000 MWh.

--- Lesson 6.4: U-Substitution and Integration Techniques (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: U-Substitution and Integration Techniques
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 'u-substitution' in integration?
U-substitution reverses the chain rule. By setting u = g(x), du = g'(x) dx, the integral simplifies to a basic form in terms of u.
What is the 'chain rule in reverse' in integration?
Just as the chain rule gives d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x))g'(x), u-sub reverses this: integral of f'(g(x))g'(x) dx = f(g(x)) + C.
What is an 'inner function' in the context of u-substitution?
In a composite function like sin(x^2), x^2 is the inner function. In u-sub, we often set u equal to the inner function.
What is the difference between 'indefinite' and 'definite' u-substitution?
For indefinite integrals, back-substitute to return to x. For definite integrals, either back-substitute or change the limits to u-values and evaluate without back-substituting.
A physicist computes the work done by a force F(x) = 10/(x+1)^2 over [0, 4]. Using u-substitution, what is the work?
Let u = x+1. W = integral from 1 to 5 of 10/u^2 du = [-10/u] from 1 to 5 = -2-(-10) = 8 joules.
An engineer evaluates the integral of 6x^2*sqrt(x^3+8) dx from 0 to 1. What is the result?
Let u = x^3+8, du = 3x^2 dx. Integral = 2 integral from 8 to 9 of u^(1/2) du = 2[2u^(3/2)/3] from 8 to 9 = (4/3)[27 - 16sqrt(2)].
A chemist integrates the reaction rate R(c) = 4c/(c^2+1)^2 with respect to concentration from c = 0 to c = 1. What is the result?
Let u = c^2+1, du = 2c dc. Integral = 2 integral from 1 to 2 of u^(-2) du = 2[-1/u] from 1 to 2 = 2(-1/2+1) = 1.
A student evaluates the integral of sin(x)*cos(x) dx using u = sin(x). What is the result?
u = sin(x), du = cos(x) dx. Integral = integral u du = u^2/2 + C = sin^2(x)/2 + C. (Note: -cos^2(x)/2 + C is also correct since they differ by a constant.)
An economist evaluates the integral of 1000/(1+0.05t)^2 dt from 0 to 20 (present value calculation). What is the result?
u = 1+0.05t, du = 0.05 dt. Integral = 20000 integral from 1 to 2 of u^(-2) du = 20000[-1/u] from 1 to 2 = 20000(1-1/2) = 10,000.
A student needs to evaluate the integral of x*cos(x^2) dx from 0 to sqrt(pi). What is the value?
u = x^2, du = 2x dx. Integral = (1/2) integral from 0 to pi of cos(u) du = (1/2)[sin(u)] from 0 to pi = (1/2)(0-0) = 0.

--- Lesson 6.5: U-Substitution and Integration Techniques (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: U-Substitution and Integration Techniques
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the integral of 2x cos(x^2) dx?
Let u = x^2, du = 2x dx. The integral becomes integral of cos(u) du = sin(u) + C = sin(x^2) + C.
What is the integral of (2x + 3)^4 * 2 dx?
Let u = 2x + 3, du = 2 dx. The integral becomes integral of u^4 du = u^5/5 + C = (2x + 3)^5/5 + C.
What is the integral of e^(2x) dx?
Let u = 2x, du = 2 dx. The integral becomes (1/2) integral of e^u du = (1/2)e^u + C = (1/2) e^(2x) + C.
What is the integral of sec^2(3x) dx?
Let u = 3x, du = 3 dx. The integral becomes (1/3) integral of sec^2(u) du = (1/3) tan(u) + C = (1/3) tan(3x) + C.
What is u-substitution in integration?
U-substitution is the integration analog of the chain rule. It simplifies integrals by replacing a composite expression with a single variable u, transforming the integrand into a simpler form.
What is meant by 'back-substitution' in u-substitution?
Back-substitution is the final step of u-substitution for indefinite integrals. After integrating in terms of u, you replace u with the original expression g(x) to express the answer in terms of the original variable.
What is the chain rule for integration informally called?
U-substitution is often called the reverse chain rule or the chain rule for integration, because it undoes the chain rule of differentiation by identifying the inner function and its derivative in the integrand.
What is an 'integrand' in the context of integration techniques?
The integrand is the function that appears inside the integral sign that is being integrated. For example, in integral of f(x) dx, the integrand is f(x).
What is a 'composite function' in the context of u-substitution?
A composite function is of the form f(g(x)), where g(x) is the inner function and f is the outer function. U-substitution works by identifying u = g(x) so the integral takes the form integral of f(u) du.
What is the 'constant multiple rule' in integration?
The constant multiple rule states that integral of k * f(x) dx = k * integral of f(x) dx. This is frequently used in u-substitution to adjust for constant factors when matching du.

--- Lesson 6.6: Integration by Parts (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Integration by Parts
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In integration by parts, if u = x and dv = cos(x) dx, what is the integral of x cos(x) dx?
With u = x, dv = cos(x) dx, we get du = dx, v = sin(x). Integral = x sin(x) - integral of sin(x) dx = x sin(x) - (-cos(x)) + C = x sin(x) + cos(x) + C.
What is the integral of x^2 * e^x dx?
Apply integration by parts twice. First: u = x^2, dv = e^x dx gives x^2*e^x - 2 integral of x*e^x dx. Second application: integral of x*e^x dx = x*e^x - e^x. So the result is e^x(x^2 - 2x + 2) + C.
What is the integral of e^x * sin(x) dx?
Applying integration by parts twice creates a cycle. Let I = integral of e^x sin(x) dx. After two applications: I = e^x sin(x) - e^x cos(x) - I. Solving: 2I = e^x(sin(x) - cos(x)), so I = (e^x/2)(sin(x) - cos(x)) + C.
What is the integral of arctan(x) dx?
Let u = arctan(x), dv = dx. Then du = 1/(1+x^2) dx, v = x. Integral = x arctan(x) - integral of x/(1+x^2) dx = x arctan(x) - (1/2) ln(1+x^2) + C.
What is the integral of x * sec^2(x) dx?
Let u = x, dv = sec^2(x) dx. Then du = dx, v = tan(x). Integral = x tan(x) - integral of tan(x) dx = x tan(x) - (-ln|cos(x)|) + C = x tan(x) + ln|cos(x)| + C.
What is the integration by parts formula?
The integration by parts formula is integral of u dv = uv - integral of v du. It is derived from the product rule for differentiation: d(uv) = u dv + v du.
What is meant by a 'cyclic integral' in integration by parts?
A cyclic integral occurs when the original integral reappears after applying integration by parts two or more times. You can then set up an equation and solve for the integral algebraically, as happens with integral of e^x sin(x) dx.
What is the 'product rule for derivatives' and how does it relate to integration by parts?
Integration by parts is derived directly from the product rule. Starting with d(uv) = u dv + v du and integrating both sides gives uv = integral of u dv + integral of v du, which rearranges to the integration by parts formula.
What is a 'reduction formula' in the context of integration by parts?
A reduction formula is obtained through integration by parts and expresses an integral with a parameter n in terms of a similar integral with parameter n-1 (or n-2). For example, integral of sin^n(x) dx can be expressed in terms of integral of sin^(n-2)(x) dx.
What is an 'inverse trigonometric function' and why is it often chosen as u in integration by parts?
Inverse trigonometric functions (arcsin, arccos, arctan, etc.) are preferred as u in integration by parts because their derivatives are algebraic expressions (e.g., d/dx arctan(x) = 1/(1+x^2)), which simplifies the remaining integral.
Real-World Applications
Example: A student studies for an exam with effectiveness modeled by E(t) = ln(t + 1) pages per hour. Using integration by parts, find the total pages studied from t = 0 to t = 3 hours.
Integral from 0 to 3 of ln(t+1) dt. Let u = ln(t+1), dv = dt. Then du = 1/(t+1) dt, v = t. Integral = [t ln(t+1)] from 0 to 3 - integral from 0 to 3 of t/(t+1) dt = 3 ln(4) - integral from 0 to 3 of (1 - 1/(t+1)) dt = 3 ln(4) - [t - ln(t+1)] from 0 to 3 = 3 ln(4) - (3 - ln(4)) = 4 ln(4) - 3.

--- Lesson 6.7: Definite Integral Properties (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Definite Integral Properties
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If the integral from 1 to 5 of f(x) dx = 8, what is the integral from 5 to 1 of f(x) dx?
Reversing the limits of integration negates the value. The integral from b to a of f(x) dx = -(integral from a to b of f(x) dx). So the answer is -8.
If the integral from 0 to 3 of f(x) dx = 5 and the integral from 0 to 3 of g(x) dx = 2, what is the integral from 0 to 3 of [f(x) + g(x)] dx?
By the sum rule for definite integrals, the integral of a sum equals the sum of the integrals. So integral of [f(x) + g(x)] dx = 5 + 2 = 7.
If the integral from 0 to 4 of f(x) dx = 10, what is the integral from 0 to 4 of 3f(x) dx?
By the constant multiple rule, integral of c*f(x) dx = c * integral of f(x) dx. So integral of 3f(x) dx = 3 * 10 = 30.
What is the integral from a to a of f(x) dx for any continuous function f?
When the upper and lower limits of integration are the same, the integral equals zero. This makes sense geometrically because there is no width to the region, so the area is zero.
If the integral from 1 to 3 of f(x) dx = 4 and the integral from 3 to 7 of f(x) dx = 6, what is the integral from 1 to 7 of f(x) dx?
By the additive property: integral from a to c of f(x) dx = integral from a to b of f(x) dx + integral from b to c of f(x) dx. So 4 + 6 = 10.
If the integral from 0 to 6 of f(x) dx = 12 and the integral from 0 to 2 of f(x) dx = 3, what is the integral from 2 to 6 of f(x) dx?
By the additive property: integral from 0 to 6 = integral from 0 to 2 + integral from 2 to 6. So integral from 2 to 6 = 12 - 3 = 9.
What is the integral from -2 to 2 of x^3 dx?
x^3 is an odd function (f(-x) = -f(x)). The integral of an odd function over a symmetric interval [-a, a] is always 0 because the positive and negative areas cancel.
What is the integral from -3 to 3 of x^2 dx?
x^2 is an even function, so integral from -3 to 3 of x^2 dx = 2 * integral from 0 to 3 of x^2 dx = 2[x^3/3] from 0 to 3 = 2(9) = 18.
If the integral from 0 to 5 of f(x) dx = 7 and the integral from 0 to 5 of g(x) dx = 3, what is the integral from 0 to 5 of [2f(x) - 3g(x)] dx?
Using linearity: integral of [2f(x) - 3g(x)] dx = 2 * integral of f(x) dx - 3 * integral of g(x) dx = 2(7) - 3(3) = 14 - 9 = 5.
What is the 'additive interval property' of definite integrals?
The additive interval property states that integral from a to c of f(x) dx = integral from a to b of f(x) dx + integral from b to c of f(x) dx. This allows breaking up an integral over a large interval into smaller pieces.
What is an 'even function' in the context of integral properties?
An even function satisfies f(-x) = f(x) and is symmetric about the y-axis. For even functions, integral from -a to a of f(x) dx = 2 * integral from 0 to a of f(x) dx, since both halves contribute equally.
What is an 'odd function' in the context of integral properties?
An odd function satisfies f(-x) = -f(x) and has origin symmetry. The integral of an odd function over a symmetric interval [-a, a] is always zero because the areas above and below the x-axis cancel exactly.
What is the 'zero-width property' of definite integrals?
The zero-width property states that when the upper and lower limits of integration are the same, the integral equals zero. Geometrically, this makes sense because a region with zero width has zero area.
What is the 'domination property' of definite integrals?
The domination (or bounding) property provides an upper bound for the absolute value of a definite integral. If the integrand is bounded by M in absolute value, the integral's absolute value cannot exceed M times the interval length.
A factory produces widgets at a rate r(t). If the integral from 0 to 4 of r(t) dt = 200, the integral from 4 to 8 of r(t) dt = 300, and the integral from 0 to 8 of 2r(t) dt is needed, what is its value?
First, by the additive property: integral from 0 to 8 of r(t) dt = 200 + 300 = 500. Then by the constant multiple rule: integral from 0 to 8 of 2r(t) dt = 2 * 500 = 1000.
A swimmer's velocity is v(t) = sin(t) m/s. Using the odd function property, what is the integral from -pi to pi of v(t) dt?
Since sin(t) is an odd function (sin(-t) = -sin(t)), the integral of an odd function over a symmetric interval [-a, a] is always 0. The positive displacement going forward exactly cancels the negative displacement going backward.
A company's profit rate is P'(t). If the integral from January to June equals $50,000, from June to September equals -$10,000, and from September to December equals $30,000, what is the net profit for the year?
By the additive interval property, the total integral over the full year equals the sum of integrals over the subintervals: $50,000 + (-$10,000) + $30,000 = $70,000.
A pendulum has velocity v(t) = t^3 - t on the interval [-1, 1]. Using symmetry properties of odd functions, what is the integral from -1 to 1 of v(t) dt?
v(t) = t^3 - t is an odd function since v(-t) = (-t)^3 - (-t) = -t^3 + t = -(t^3 - t) = -v(t). The integral of an odd function over the symmetric interval [-1, 1] is 0.
A sensor measures vibrations modeled by f(t) = cos(t). Using the even function property, what is the integral from -pi/2 to pi/2 of cos(t) dt?
cos(t) is an even function (cos(-t) = cos(t)). So integral from -pi/2 to pi/2 = 2 * integral from 0 to pi/2 of cos(t) dt = 2[sin(t)] from 0 to pi/2 = 2(1 - 0) = 2.
A biologist tracks a population change rate P'(t). She knows the integral from 0 to 3 of P'(t) dt = 40, the integral from 0 to 5 of P'(t) dt = 25. What is the net population change from year 3 to year 5?
By the additive property: integral from 0 to 5 = integral from 0 to 3 + integral from 3 to 5. So integral from 3 to 5 = 25 - 40 = -15. The population decreased by 15 from year 3 to year 5.
Real-World Applications
Example: A city's temperature deviation from average is modeled by D(t) = t^2 - 4 degrees. Using definite integral properties, compute the integral from -2 to 2 of D(t) dt.
Split: integral of (t^2 - 4) dt. t^2 is even, so integral from -2 to 2 of t^2 dt = 2 * integral from 0 to 2 of t^2 dt = 2[t^3/3] from 0 to 2 = 16/3. The constant -4: integral from -2 to 2 of (-4) dt = -4(4) = -16. Total = 16/3 - 16 = -32/3.

--- Lesson 6.8: Average Value of Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Average Value of Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the average value of f(x) = 2x on the interval [0, 4]?
Average value = (1/(4-0)) * integral from 0 to 4 of 2x dx = (1/4)[x^2] from 0 to 4 = (1/4)(16) = 4.
What is the average value of f(x) = x^2 on the interval [0, 3]?
Average value = (1/(3-0)) * integral from 0 to 3 of x^2 dx = (1/3)[x^3/3] from 0 to 3 = (1/3)(9) = 3.
What is the average value of f(x) = e^x on [0, 1]?
Average value = (1/(1-0)) * integral from 0 to 1 of e^x dx = [e^x] from 0 to 1 = e - 1.
What is the average value of f(x) = 1/x on [1, e]?
Average value = (1/(e-1)) * integral from 1 to e of (1/x) dx = (1/(e-1)) * [ln(x)] from 1 to e = (1/(e-1))(1 - 0) = 1/(e - 1).
What is the average value of f(x) = 4x - x^2 on [0, 4]?
Average = (1/4) * integral from 0 to 4 of (4x - x^2) dx = (1/4)[2x^2 - x^3/3] from 0 to 4 = (1/4)(32 - 64/3) = (1/4)(32/3) = 8/3.
What is the average value of f(x) = x^3 on [-1, 1]?
x^3 is an odd function, so its integral over the symmetric interval [-1, 1] is 0. Therefore the average value = (1/2)(0) = 0.
What is the formula for the average value of a function f on the interval [a, b]?
The average value formula divides the integral of f over [a, b] by the length of the interval: f_avg = (1/(b-a)) * integral from a to b of f(x) dx. This gives the constant height of a rectangle with the same area as the region under f.
What is meant by 'mean value' in the context of the Mean Value Theorem for Integrals?
In the MVT for Integrals, 'mean value' refers to the average value of the function, which is the integral divided by the interval length. This is the continuous analog of the arithmetic mean.
What is a 'continuous function' and why is continuity required for the average value theorem?
A continuous function has no breaks, jumps, or holes. Continuity is required by the MVT for Integrals because it guarantees that f achieves every value between its minimum and maximum (by the IVT), including its average value.
What is the 'integral mean' of a function?
The integral mean is synonymous with the average value of a function. It extends the concept of an arithmetic mean to continuous functions, using integration rather than summation.
What is the difference between 'average value' and 'average rate of change'?
The average value of f on [a,b] is (1/(b-a)) integral of f(x) dx, giving the mean height. The average rate of change is (f(b)-f(a))/(b-a), giving the slope of the secant line. They measure different things.
What is a 'weighted average' and how does it relate to the average value of a function?
A weighted average assigns different importance to different values. The average value formula gives a uniformly weighted continuous average: every point in [a, b] contributes equally to the integral divided by the interval length.
A city's temperature over a 12-hour period from 6 AM to 6 PM is modeled by T(t) = 60 + 15 sin(pi*t/12) degrees Fahrenheit, where t is hours after 6 AM. What is the average temperature during this period?
Average = (1/12) integral from 0 to 12 of (60 + 15 sin(pi*t/12)) dt = (1/12)[60t - 15(12/pi)cos(pi*t/12)] from 0 to 12 = (1/12)[720 - (180/pi)(-1) + (180/pi)(1)] = (1/12)(720 + 360/pi) = 60 + 30/pi.
A car's speed during a 5-second interval is v(t) = 3t^2 m/s. What is the average speed, and at what time does the car actually travel at this average speed?
Average speed = (1/5) integral from 0 to 5 of 3t^2 dt = (1/5)[t^3] from 0 to 5 = 125/5 = 25 m/s. By MVT for Integrals, 3c^2 = 25, so c = sqrt(25/3) seconds.
A patient's blood concentration of a drug is C(t) = 10e^(-0.5t) mg/L for t hours after injection. What is the average concentration during the first 4 hours?
Average = (1/4) integral from 0 to 4 of 10e^(-0.5t) dt = (10/4)[-2e^(-0.5t)] from 0 to 4 = (10/4)(-2e^(-2) + 2) = (10/4)(2)(1 - e^(-2)) = 5(1 - e^(-2)).
A solar panel generates power at rate P(t) = 500 sin(pi*t/12) watts for 0 <= t <= 12 hours. What is the average power output over the 12-hour daylight period?
Average = (1/12) integral from 0 to 12 of 500 sin(pi*t/12) dt = (500/12)[(-12/pi)cos(pi*t/12)] from 0 to 12 = (500/12)(12/pi)(1 - (-1)) = (500/12)(24/pi) = 1000/pi watts.
A swimming pool's temperature over a 6-hour period is T(t) = 72 + 4t - t^2 degrees Fahrenheit. What is the average pool temperature from t = 0 to t = 6?
Average = (1/6) integral from 0 to 6 of (72 + 4t - t^2) dt = (1/6)[72t + 2t^2 - t^3/3] from 0 to 6 = (1/6)(432 + 72 - 72) = (1/6)(432) = 72 degrees.
An athlete's heart rate during a 10-minute workout is H(t) = 80 + 40(1 - e^(-t/2)) bpm. What is the average heart rate during the workout?
Average = (1/10) integral from 0 to 10 of [80 + 40(1 - e^(-t/2))] dt = (1/10) integral from 0 to 10 of (120 - 40e^(-t/2)) dt = (1/10)[120t + 80e^(-t/2)] from 0 to 10 = (1/10)(1200 + 80e^(-5) - 80) = (1/10)(1120 + 80e^(-5)) = 112 + 8e^(-5) = 120 - 8(1 - e^(-5)).
A river's flow rate is R(t) = 100 + 50cos(pi*t/6) cubic feet per second over a 12-hour cycle. What is the average flow rate?
Average = (1/12) integral from 0 to 12 of [100 + 50cos(pi*t/6)] dt = (1/12)[100t + 50(6/pi)sin(pi*t/6)] from 0 to 12 = (1/12)(1200 + 0 - 0) = 100. The cosine term integrates to zero over a full period.
A company's daily profit function is P(x) = -2x^2 + 40x - 50 dollars, where x is hundreds of units sold, for 0 <= x <= 10. What is the average profit?
Average = (1/10) integral from 0 to 10 of (-2x^2 + 40x - 50) dx = (1/10)[-2x^3/3 + 20x^2 - 50x] from 0 to 10 = (1/10)(-2000/3 + 2000 - 500) = (1/10)(2500/3) = 250/3 approximately $83.33.
A pendulum's angular position is theta(t) = 0.5 cos(2t) radians. What is the average position from t = 0 to t = pi seconds?
Average = (1/pi) integral from 0 to pi of 0.5 cos(2t) dt = (0.5/pi)[(1/2)sin(2t)] from 0 to pi = (0.5/pi)(0 - 0) = 0. The cosine completes a full cycle, so the average is zero.

Average = (1/5) integral from 0 to 5 of 1000e^(0.2t) dt = (1000/5)[5e^(0.2t)] from 0 to 5 = 1000(e^1 - e^0) = 1000(e - 1).

--- Lesson 6.9: Accumulation Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Accumulation Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If F(x) = integral from 0 to x of t^2 dt, what is F(3)?
F(3) = integral from 0 to 3 of t^2 dt = [t^3/3] from 0 to 3 = 27/3 = 9.
If g(x) = integral from 1 to x of (2t + 1) dt, what is g'(x)?
By the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 1, if g(x) = integral from a to x of f(t) dt, then g'(x) = f(x). So g'(x) = 2x + 1.
If F(x) = integral from 0 to x of cos(t) dt, what is F(pi/2)?
F(pi/2) = integral from 0 to pi/2 of cos(t) dt = [sin(t)] from 0 to pi/2 = sin(pi/2) - sin(0) = 1 - 0 = 1.
If g(x) = integral from 2 to x of e^t dt, what is g'(3)?
By FTC Part 1, g'(x) = e^x. Therefore g'(3) = e^3.
If F(x) = integral from 0 to x^2 of sin(t) dt, what is F'(x)?
By FTC Part 1 with the chain rule: if F(x) = integral from 0 to u(x) of f(t) dt, then F'(x) = f(u(x)) * u'(x). Here F'(x) = sin(x^2) * 2x = 2x sin(x^2).
If g(x) = integral from x to 5 of t^3 dt, what is g'(x)?
g(x) = integral from x to 5 = -(integral from 5 to x) of t^3 dt. So g(x) = -integral from 5 to x of t^3 dt, and by FTC Part 1, g'(x) = -x^3.
If F(x) = integral from 0 to x of (3t^2 - 6t) dt, what is F(2)?
F(2) = integral from 0 to 2 of (3t^2 - 6t) dt = [t^3 - 3t^2] from 0 to 2 = (8 - 12) - 0 = -4.
What is an accumulation function?
An accumulation function F(x) = integral from a to x of f(t) dt represents the accumulated area under f(t) from a fixed point a up to the variable point x. As x increases, the function 'accumulates' more area.
What is a 'dummy variable' in the context of accumulation functions?
In F(x) = integral from a to x of f(t) dt, the variable t is a dummy variable. It exists only inside the integral and does not appear in the result. Writing integral of f(s) ds or f(u) du gives the same function.
What is the 'initial value' of an accumulation function F(x) = integral from a to x of f(t) dt?
When x = a, the integral from a to a of f(t) dt = 0 by the zero-width property. So every accumulation function starts at zero when x equals the lower limit.
What is the 'chain rule version' of FTC Part 1?
When the upper limit is a function g(x) rather than just x, the chain rule must be applied: d/dx [integral from a to g(x) of f(t) dt] = f(g(x)) * g'(x).
What is the relationship between accumulation functions and antiderivatives?
Since F'(x) = f(x), the accumulation function F(x) = integral from a to x of f(t) dt is an antiderivative of f. It is the particular antiderivative satisfying the initial condition F(a) = 0.
A company's revenue rate is R(t) = 100e^(0.1t) dollars per day. The accumulated revenue function is A(x) = integral from 0 to x of R(t) dt. What is A'(10)?
By FTC Part 1, A'(x) = R(x) = 100e^(0.1x). At x = 10: A'(10) = 100e^(0.1*10) = 100e dollars per day. The rate of accumulation at day 10 equals the revenue rate at day 10.
A factory's emission rate is E(t) = sin(pi*t/12) tons per hour over a 24-hour period. The accumulation function T(x) = integral from 0 to x of E(t) dt gives total emissions. What is T(12)?
T(12) = integral from 0 to 12 of sin(pi*t/12) dt = [(-12/pi)cos(pi*t/12)] from 0 to 12 = (-12/pi)(cos(pi) - cos(0)) = (-12/pi)(-1 - 1) = 24/pi tons.
An investment grows at a rate of r(t) = 200 + 50t dollars per month. The accumulated profit is P(x) = integral from 0 to x of r(t) dt. How fast is profit accumulating at month 6 (what is P'(6))?
By FTC Part 1, P'(x) = r(x) = 200 + 50x. At x = 6: P'(6) = 200 + 50(6) = 200 + 300 = $500 per month.
The rate of snowfall is S(t) = 3t^2 inches per hour. Define A(x) = integral from 0 to x^2 of S(t) dt. Using the chain rule version of FTC Part 1, what is A'(2)?
By the chain rule version of FTC: A'(x) = S(x^2) * 2x = 3(x^2)^2 * 2x = 3x^4 * 2x = 6x^5. At x = 2: A'(2) = 6(32) = 192.
Real-World Applications
Example: A student's study effectiveness is modeled by E(t) = 10 - 2t pages per hour for 0 <= t <= 5. The total pages studied is S(x) = integral from 0 to x of E(t) dt. Find the inflection point of S(x) (if any exist).
S'(x) = E(x) = 10 - 2x, so S''(x) = E'(x) = -2, which is always negative. Since S'' is a nonzero constant, it never changes sign, and there are no inflection points. S is always concave down.

--- Lesson 6.10: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the antiderivative of f(x) = 5x^4?
Using the power rule for integration: integral of 5x^4 dx = 5(x^5/5) + C = x^5 + C. You can verify by differentiating: d/dx(x^5) = 5x^4.
What is the integral of 1/x^3 dx?
Rewrite 1/x^3 = x^(-3). Using the power rule: integral of x^(-3) dx = x^(-2)/(-2) + C = -1/(2x^2) + C.
What is the integral of e^(3x) dx?
Using the rule for exponentials with a linear argument: integral of e^(ax) dx = (1/a)e^(ax) + C. Here a = 3, so the answer is (1/3)e^(3x) + C.
What is the integral of sec^2(x) dx?
Since d/dx[tan(x)] = sec^2(x), the antiderivative of sec^2(x) is tan(x) + C.
What is the antiderivative of f(x) = 0?
The antiderivative of 0 is any constant C, because the derivative of any constant is 0. This illustrates why the constant of integration is always included.
What is an antiderivative of a function?
An antiderivative of f(x) is any function F(x) such that F'(x) = f(x). Antidifferentiation is the reverse process of differentiation.

The power rule for integration states: integral of x^n dx = x^(n+1)/(n+1) + C, provided n is not -1. When n = -1, the integral is ln|x| + C instead.

A particular antiderivative is one specific member of the family F(x) + C, determined by an initial condition such as F(a) = b. This pins down the exact value of C.

--- Lesson 6.11: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the integral of csc^2(x) dx?
Since d/dx[-cot(x)] = csc^2(x), the antiderivative of csc^2(x) is -cot(x) + C.
What is the integral of 5^x dx?
For any base a > 0, a not equal to 1: integral of a^x dx = a^x / ln(a) + C. With a = 5: integral = 5^x / ln(5) + C.
What is the integral of 1/(1 + x^2) dx?
Since d/dx[arctan(x)] = 1/(1 + x^2), the antiderivative of 1/(1 + x^2) is arctan(x) + C. This is a standard integral formula.
What is the integral of 1/sqrt(1 - x^2) dx?
Since d/dx[arcsin(x)] = 1/sqrt(1 - x^2), the antiderivative is arcsin(x) + C. This is one of the standard inverse trigonometric integral formulas.
What is the integral of -csc(x) cot(x) dx?
Since d/dx[-csc(x)] = csc(x)cot(x), we have d/dx[csc(x)] = -csc(x)cot(x). Therefore integral of -csc(x)cot(x) dx = csc(x) + C.
What is a 'family of antiderivatives'?
A family of antiderivatives consists of all functions F(x) + C, where C is any constant. Graphically, these are vertical shifts of one another, and they all have the same derivative f(x).
What is a 'trigonometric integral'?
A trigonometric integral is any integral whose integrand involves trigonometric functions. Standard formulas include integral of sin(x) dx = -cos(x) + C, integral of sec^2(x) dx = tan(x) + C, and others.
What is the 'inverse trigonometric antiderivative' for 1/(1 + x^2)?
The function 1/(1 + x^2) is the derivative of arctan(x). Therefore its antiderivative is arctan(x) + C. This is one of three standard inverse trig integral formulas in calculus.
What is a 'logarithmic integral'?
A logarithmic integral arises when integrating 1/x or more generally 1/u where u is a function of x. The result involves the natural logarithm: integral of 1/x dx = ln|x| + C.
What is the 'linearity of integration'?
Linearity of integration means integration distributes over addition and respects scalar multiplication. This fundamental property allows us to break complex integrals into simpler pieces.
An investment earns interest at a rate of I'(t) = 500e^(0.05t) dollars per year. If the initial investment is $10,000, what is the value after 4 years?
I(t) = integral of 500e^(0.05t) dt = 10000e^(0.05t) + C. I(0) = 10000 + C = 10000, so C = 0. Wait, I(0) = 10000(1) + C = 10000 means C = 0. I(4) = 10000e^(0.2). But this gives total as 10000e^(0.2). Actually, the value is V(t) = initial + accumulated interest: integral from 0 to 4 of 500e^(0.05t) dt = 10000(e^(0.2) - 1). Total value = 10000 + 10000(e^(0.2) - 1).
Real-World Applications
Example: A water pipe leaks at an increasing rate of L(t) = 0.5t + 2 gallons per hour. If 50 gallons have already leaked when monitoring begins (t = 0), find the total leakage by t = 6 hours.
W(t) = integral of (0.5t + 2) dt = 0.25t^2 + 2t + C. W(0) = C = 50. W(6) = 0.25(36) + 12 + 50 = 9 + 12 + 50 = 71 gallons.
Example: A student's reading rate is R(t) = 40 - 2t pages per hour. If they have read 120 pages before starting a study session (t = 0), how many total pages have they read after 5 hours?
P(t) = integral of (40 - 2t) dt = 40t - t^2 + C. P(0) = C = 120. P(5) = 200 - 25 + 120 = 295 pages.

--- Lesson 6.12: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the integral of (6x^5 + 4x^3 - 2x) dx?
Integrate term by term: 6x^5 -> x^6, 4x^3 -> x^4, -2x -> -x^2. Result: x^6 + x^4 - x^2 + C.
What is the integral of (2/x + 3e^x - x^(-2)) dx?
Integral of 2/x = 2 ln|x|. Integral of 3e^x = 3e^x. Integral of -x^(-2) = -x^(-1)/(-1) = 1/x. Together: 2 ln|x| + 3e^x + 1/x + C.
What is the integral of x^(2/3) dx?
Using the power rule: integral of x^(2/3) dx = x^(2/3 + 1)/(2/3 + 1) = x^(5/3)/(5/3) = (3/5)x^(5/3) + C.
What is the integral of 3/(2x + 1) dx?
Let u = 2x + 1, du = 2 dx. Integral = (3/2) integral of du/u = (3/2) ln|u| + C = (3/2) ln|2x + 1| + C.
What is the integral of (x^3 + 1)/x dx?
Divide: (x^3 + 1)/x = x^2 + 1/x. Integral of x^2 = x^3/3 and integral of 1/x = ln|x|. Total: x^3/3 + ln|x| + C.
What is 'verification by differentiation' in the context of antiderivatives?
Verification by differentiation is a powerful check: if you claim integral of f(x) dx = F(x) + C, you can verify by computing F'(x) and confirming it equals f(x). This catches algebraic errors in integration.
What is the 'general solution' of a first-order differential equation dy/dx = f(x)?
The general solution of dy/dx = f(x) is y = F(x) + C, where F is any antiderivative of f. The arbitrary constant C means there are infinitely many solutions, forming a family of curves.
What is the relationship between indefinite integrals and definite integrals?
By the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 2, a definite integral is evaluated by finding any antiderivative F(x) (from the indefinite integral) and computing F(b) - F(a). The constant C cancels out.
What is the 'integral of a constant function'?
The integral of a constant k with respect to x is kx + C. This follows from the power rule with n = 0: integral of k * x^0 dx = k * x^1/1 + C = kx + C.
What is the 'velocity from acceleration' relationship?
Since acceleration is the derivative of velocity (a = dv/dt), velocity is the antiderivative of acceleration: v(t) = integral of a(t) dt + C, where C is determined by the initial velocity.

--- Lesson 6.12: Antiderivatives and Indefinite Integrals (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: Define: Limit
     A: The value that a function approaches as the input approaches some value

  2. Q: Define: Derivative
     A: The instantaneous rate of change of a function at a specific point, representing the slope of the tangent line

  3. Q: Define: Integral
     A: The reverse process of differentiation; it represents the accumulation of quantities

  4. Q: Define: Continuity
     A: A property of a function that can be drawn without lifting the pencil, with no breaks or discontinuities

  5. Q: Define: Critical Point
     A: A point where the derivative equals zero or is undefined, often indicating a local maximum or minimum

  6. Q: Define: Antiderivative
     A: A function whose derivative equals the given function

  7. Q: Define: Riemann Sum
     A: An approximation of the area under a curve using rectangles of varying heights

  8. Q: Define: Chain Rule
     A: A method for finding the derivative of composite functions by multiplying the derivatives in sequence

  9. Q: When creating a mathematical model to represent a real-world scenario, which is the most important f
     A: Identify the variables and relationships between them

  10. Q: When validating a mathematical model against real data, what does a large discrepancy typically indi
