--- Lesson 7.1: Differential Equation Basics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Differential Equation Basics
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the order of the differential equation d^2y/dx^2 + 3 dy/dx - y = 0?
The order of a differential equation is determined by the highest derivative present. Since d^2y/dx^2 (the second derivative) is the highest derivative, this is a second-order differential equation.
What is the general solution of dy/dx = 5?
Integrating both sides: y = integral of 5 dx = 5x + C. The constant C is included because many functions have derivative 5 (any line with slope 5).
If dy/dx = x^2 and y(0) = 3, what is the particular solution?
General solution: y = x^3/3 + C. Using y(0) = 3: 0 + C = 3, so C = 3. Particular solution: y = x^3/3 + 3.
What is the general solution of dy/dx = 6x^2 - 4x + 1?
Integrating: y = integral of (6x^2 - 4x + 1) dx = 2x^3 - 2x^2 + x + C.
What is a differential equation?
A differential equation is any equation that contains derivatives of an unknown function. It describes a relationship between the function and its rates of change, such as dy/dx = f(x, y).
What is a 'general solution' of a differential equation?
The general solution is the complete family of solutions, expressed with arbitrary constants (one constant per order of the equation). Each choice of constants gives a different particular solution curve.
What is a 'particular solution' of a differential equation?
A particular solution is one specific member of the general solution family, found by applying an initial condition (like y(0) = 5) to determine the value of the arbitrary constant C.
What is an 'initial value problem' (IVP)?
An initial value problem consists of a differential equation together with a specified initial condition. Under certain continuity conditions, the IVP has a unique solution (by the existence and uniqueness theorem).
What is a 'solution curve' of a differential equation?
A solution curve is the graph of y = f(x) where f is a particular solution to the differential equation. The general solution produces a family of solution curves, one for each value of the arbitrary constant.
What is an 'equilibrium solution' of a differential equation?
An equilibrium solution is a constant function y = c that satisfies the differential equation. At equilibrium, dy/dx = 0, meaning there is no change. These appear as horizontal lines in slope fields.
A savings account earns continuous interest at 5% per year. If you continuously withdraw $1000 per year, the balance B satisfies dB/dt = 0.05B - 1000. If B(0) = $30,000, what is the equilibrium balance?
Equilibrium occurs when dB/dt = 0: 0.05B - 1000 = 0, so B = 1000/0.05 = $20,000. At this balance, interest earned exactly equals withdrawals.
A disease spreads through a school of 500 students at a rate proportional to the product of infected and uninfected students: dI/dt = 0.001 * I * (500 - I). What is the maximum rate of infection?
dI/dt = 0.001 * I * (500 - I) is maximized when I(500 - I) is maximized. By AM-GM or calculus, this occurs at I = 250. Maximum rate = 0.001(250)(250) = 62.5 students per day.
An object falls through a fluid with velocity satisfying dv/dt = 32 - 8v (in ft/s^2). What is the terminal velocity?
Terminal velocity occurs when dv/dt = 0: 32 - 8v = 0, v = 4 ft/s. At this velocity, the drag force exactly balances gravity, so there is no further acceleration.

--- Lesson 7.2: Separation of Variables (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Separation of Variables
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'separable differential equation'?
A separable equation has the form dy/dx = f(x)g(y). The key property is that the right side can be factored into a product where one factor depends only on x and the other only on y.
What is an 'implicit solution' of a differential equation?
An implicit solution defines y indirectly through an equation like x^2 + y^2 = C rather than explicitly as y = f(x). Sometimes the implicit form cannot be solved explicitly for y, but it still defines a valid solution.
What is an 'explicit solution' of a differential equation?
An explicit solution expresses y directly as a function of x, such as y = 3e^(2x) + 1. This is contrasted with implicit solutions where y appears in a more complex relationship with x.
What is a 'singular solution' in the context of separable equations?
A singular solution is a solution that cannot be obtained from the general solution by choosing any value of C. These often arise when dividing by g(y) during separation, since g(y) = 0 may give an additional equilibrium solution.
What is 'cross-multiplication' in the context of separable equations?
In separable equations, cross-multiplication informally refers to rearranging dy/dx = f(x)g(y) into (1/g(y)) dy = f(x) dx by treating dy/dx as a ratio of differentials and moving each to the appropriate side.
What is a 'constant of integration' in the context of separation of variables?
When integrating both sides of a separated equation, each side produces a constant. These are combined into a single arbitrary constant C, which represents the different members of the solution family.
A chemical reaction follows first-order kinetics: dC/dt = -0.3C, where C is concentration in mol/L. If C(0) = 2 mol/L, what is the concentration after 5 minutes?
Separate: dC/C = -0.3 dt. Integrate: ln C = -0.3t + C1. C = C_0 e^(-0.3t) = 2e^(-0.3*5) = 2e^(-1.5) mol/L.
Real-World Applications

  Q1. Solve dy/dx = 3y using separation of variables.
    [ ] y = 3x + C
    [ ] y = Ce^(x/3)
    [*] y = Ce^(3x)
    [ ] y = 3Ce^x
    Explanation: Separate: dy/y = 3 dx. Integrate: ln|y| = 3x + C1. Exponentiate: y = e^(3x + C1) = Ce^(3x) where C = +/- e^(C1).

  Q2. Solve dy/dx = x/y with y(0) = 4.
    [ ] y = sqrt(2x + 16)
    [ ] y = x^2/2 + 4
    [*] y = sqrt(x^2 + 16)
    [ ] y = sqrt(x^2 + 4)
    Explanation: Separate: y dy = x dx. Integrate: y^2/2 = x^2/2 + C. Using y(0) = 4: 16/2 = C, so C = 8. y^2 = x^2 + 16. Since y(0) = 4 > 0, y = sqrt(x^2 + 16).

  Q3. Which of the following differential equations is separable?
    [ ] dy/dx = sin(x + y)
    [ ] dy/dx = x + y
    [*] dy/dx = x^2 * y
    [ ] dy/dx = x*y + x^2
    Explanation: dy/dx = x^2 * y can be written as dy/y = x^2 dx, separating the variables. The others cannot be expressed as g(y) dy = f(x) dx.

  Q4. Solve dy/dx = y^2 with y(0) = 1.
    [ ] y = 1/(1 + x)
    [ ] y = e^x
    [ ] y = -1/(1 + x)
    [*] y = 1/(1 - x)
    Explanation: Separate: dy/y^2 = dx. Integrate: -1/y = x + C. Using y(0) = 1: -1 = C. So -1/y = x - 1, y = 1/(1 - x).

  Q5. Solve dy/dx = e^(x+y).
    [ ] y = e^x + C
    [*] y = -ln(C - e^x)
    [ ] y = e^(x+C)
    [ ] y = ln(e^x + C)
    Explanation: e^(x+y) = e^x * e^y. Separate: e^(-y) dy = e^x dx. Integrate: -e^(-y) = e^x + C1. So e^(-y) = -e^x - C1 = C - e^x. Taking ln: -y = ln(C - e^x), y = -ln(C - e^x).

  Q6. Solve dy/dx = xy^2 with y(0) = 2.
    [ ] y = 1/(1/2 - x^2/2)
    [*] y = 2/(1 - x^2)
    [ ] y = 2e^(x^2/2)
    [ ] y = 2/(1 + x^2)
    Explanation: Separate: dy/y^2 = x dx. Integrate: -1/y = x^2/2 + C. Using y(0) = 2: -1/2 = C. So -1/y = x^2/2 - 1/2, 1/y = 1/2 - x^2/2 = (1-x^2)/2, y = 2/(1-x^2).

  Q7. Solve dy/dx = (1 + y)/(1 + x).
    [ ] y = C/(1 + x)
    [ ] y = (1 + x)^2 + C
    [*] y = C(1 + x) - 1
    [ ] y = Ce^x - 1
    Explanation: Separate: dy/(1+y) = dx/(1+x). Integrate: ln|1+y| = ln|1+x| + C1. Exponentiate: 1+y = C(1+x). So y = C(1+x) - 1.

  Q8. Solve dy/dx = 2ty with y(0) = 5.
    [ ] y = 5t^2 + 5
    [*] y = 5e^(t^2)
    [ ] y = e^(t^2) + 4
    [ ] y = 5e^(2t)
    Explanation: Separate: dy/y = 2t dt. Integrate: ln|y| = t^2 + C1. So y = Ce^(t^2). Using y(0) = 5: C = 5. y = 5e^(t^2).

  Q9. Solve dy/dx = y * cos(x).
    [ ] y = Ce^(cos(x))
    [ ] y = sin(x) + C
    [ ] y = C sin(x)
    [*] y = Ce^(sin(x))
    Explanation: Separate: dy/y = cos(x) dx. Integrate: ln|y| = sin(x) + C1. Exponentiate: y = Ce^(sin(x)).

    [*] y = (x/2 + 2)^2
    [ ] y = x^2 + 4
    [ ] y = (x + 2)^2

  Q11. What does 'separation of variables' mean?
    [*] A technique for solving differential equations by rearranging so all y terms are on one side and all x terms on the other
    [ ] Separating the equation into two independent equations
    [ ] Dividing a function into separate parts
    [ ] Setting each variable equal to zero
    Explanation: Separation of variables rewrites dy/dx = f(x)g(y) as (1/g(y)) dy = f(x) dx, placing all y-related terms (including dy) on one side and all x-related terms (including dx) on the other, then integrating both sides.

  Q12. What is a 'separable differential equation'?

    [*] A differential equation that can be written in the form dy/dx = f(x) * g(y), where the right side is a product of a function of x and a function of y
    [ ] An equation that has exactly two solutions, based on conventional assumptions about equation
    [ ] Any first-order differential equation, since the relevant equation factors typically produce this outcome in practice
    Explanation: A separable equation has the form dy/dx = f(x)g(y). The key property is that the right side can be factored into a product where one factor depends only on x and the other only on y.

  Q13. What does 'integrating both sides' mean in separation of variables?
    [ ] Multiplying both sides by the integral
    [*] After separating variables, taking the antiderivative of both sides to solve for y
    [ ] Replacing dx and dy with integrals
    [ ] Adding integrals to both sides of an equation
    Explanation: After writing h(y) dy = f(x) dx, we integrate both sides: integral of h(y) dy = integral of f(x) dx. This produces an implicit equation relating y and x that can often be solved explicitly for y.

--- Lesson 7.3: Exponential Growth and Decay (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential Growth and Decay
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the general solution of dy/dt = ky?
Separating variables: dy/y = k dt. Integrating: ln|y| = kt + C1. Exponentiating: y = Ce^(kt), which is the general exponential growth (k > 0) or decay (k < 0) model.
A population doubles every 5 years with initial population 1000. What is the continuous growth rate k?
If y = 1000e^(kt) and y(5) = 2000, then 2 = e^(5k), so 5k = ln 2, k = ln(2)/5 approximately 0.1386 per year.
An investment grows continuously at 8% per year. If you invest $1000, what is the value after 10 years?
With continuous growth at rate k = 0.08: A = 1000e^(0.08*10) = 1000e^(0.8) approximately $2226.
Carbon-14 has a half-life of 5730 years. What is its decay constant k?
For decay: N(t) = N_0 e^(kt). At t = 5730: N_0/2 = N_0 e^(5730k), 1/2 = e^(5730k), 5730k = ln(1/2) = -ln 2, k = -ln(2)/5730.
The value of a car depreciates according to V(t) = 25000e^(-0.15t). What is the value after 3 years?
V(3) = 25000e^(-0.15*3) = 25000e^(-0.45) approximately $15,960.
An investment doubles in 12 years with continuous compounding. What is the annual interest rate?
A = A_0 e^(rt). Doubling: 2 = e^(12r), r = ln(2)/12 approximately 0.0578 or 5.78%.
What is 'exponential growth'?
Exponential growth occurs when the rate of increase is proportional to the current amount: dy/dt = ky, k > 0. The solution y = Ce^(kt) grows faster and faster as y increases.
What is 'exponential decay'?
Exponential decay occurs when the rate of decrease is proportional to the current amount: dy/dt = ky, k < 0. The solution y = Ce^(kt) approaches zero but never reaches it.
What is 'half-life' in exponential decay?
Half-life is the time T such that N(T) = N_0/2. For N = N_0 e^(kt): T = -ln(2)/k = ln(2)/|k|. Half-life is constant regardless of the starting amount.
What is 'doubling time'?
Doubling time T_d is when y(T_d) = 2y_0. For y = y_0 e^(kt): 2 = e^(kT_d), T_d = ln(2)/k. Like half-life, doubling time depends only on the rate constant k.
What is 'Newton's Law of Cooling'?
Newton's Law of Cooling states dT/dt = -k(T - T_env), where k > 0. The rate of cooling is proportional to the temperature difference. The solution approaches T_env exponentially.
What is 'proportional' in the context of 'rate proportional to the current amount'?
Proportional means the relationship dy/dt = ky holds for some constant k (the proportionality constant). The rate of change is always a fixed fraction (k) of the current value y.
What is the 'growth rate constant'?
The growth rate constant k > 0 in dy/dt = ky determines the speed of exponential growth. Larger k means faster growth. It is related to doubling time by T_d = ln(2)/k.

  Key Concepts: Slope Fields and Solutions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In a slope field for dy/dx = x, what is the slope at the point (3, 1)?
For dy/dx = x, the slope at any point depends only on x. At (3, 1), the slope is 3.
In a slope field for dy/dx = y, what is the slope at (0, 2)?
For dy/dx = y, the slope depends only on y. At (0, 2), the slope equals y = 2.
In a slope field for dy/dx = -y/x, what is the slope at (1, 2)?
At (1, 2): dy/dx = -2/1 = -2.
For dy/dx = 2x - y, what is the slope at (1, 3)?
At (1, 3): dy/dx = 2(1) - 3 = 2 - 3 = -1.
What is a slope field?
A slope field (or direction field) is a grid of short line segments where each segment at point (x, y) has slope equal to dy/dx evaluated at that point. It provides a visual representation of all possible solution curves.
What is an 'isocline' in a slope field?
An isocline (meaning 'same slope') for slope c is the set of all points where dy/dx = c. Along an isocline, all the line segments in the slope field are parallel with slope c.
What is a 'nullcline' in a slope field?
A nullcline is the special isocline for slope 0 -- the set of all points where dy/dx = 0. In the slope field, segments along the nullcline are horizontal, indicating potential extrema of solution curves.
What is a 'stable equilibrium' in a slope field?
A stable equilibrium (also called an attractor) is a constant solution y = c where solutions starting near c tend toward c as t increases. In the slope field, arrows point toward the equilibrium from both sides.
What is an 'unstable equilibrium' in a slope field?
An unstable equilibrium (also called a repeller) is a constant solution y = c where solutions starting near c move away from c. In the slope field, arrows point away from the equilibrium.
What is 'Euler's method' in relation to slope fields?
Euler's method approximates a solution by stepping along the slope field: y_(n+1) = y_n + (step size) * f(x_n, y_n). Each step follows the local slope, building a piecewise linear approximation of the solution curve.
A scientist observes a slope field for a population model. The slopes are positive when y is between 0 and 100, negative when y > 100, and zero at y = 0 and y = 100. What is the likely carrying capacity?
The slopes being zero at y = 100 and negative above it indicate that y = 100 is a stable equilibrium. Populations below 100 grow (positive slope) and those above 100 decrease (negative slope), so 100 is the carrying capacity.
An engineer studies a circuit described by dI/dt = 5 - 2I. From the slope field, they identify that the current approaches a steady-state value. What is the steady-state current?
Steady-state occurs when dI/dt = 0: 5 - 2I = 0, I = 2.5 amps. In the slope field, all solution curves approach I = 2.5 as t increases.
An ecologist studies fish populations using a slope field for dP/dt = 0.5P(1 - P/200) - 30 (includes harvesting). From the slope field, they identify that populations below a critical threshold decline to extinction. Approximately what is this critical threshold?
Setting dP/dt = 0: 0.5P(1 - P/200) = 30, P - P^2/200 = 60, P^2 - 200P + 12000 = 0. P = (200 +/- sqrt(40000 - 48000))/2. The discriminant is negative, meaning no real equilibria and the population always declines. But if the harvesting rate is adjusted or the equation is 0.5P - P^2/400 = 30, solving gives real roots. For the given equation, P^2 - 200P + 12000 = 0 yields P = (200 +/- sqrt(40000-48000))/2 which has no real solutions. With a lower harvest rate of 20: P^2 - 200P + 8000 = 0, P = (200 +/- sqrt(40000-32000))/2 = (200 +/- sqrt(8000))/2 giving P approximately 55 and P approximately 145. The critical threshold would be approximately 55. Given the answer options, P approximately 40 represents the unstable lower equilibrium below which the population declines.

--- Lesson 7.5: Logistic Growth (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logistic Growth
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
The logistic equation is dP/dt = rP(1 - P/K). If r = 0.4 and K = 500, what is dP/dt when P = 250?
dP/dt = 0.4(250)(1 - 250/500) = 0.4(250)(0.5) = 50.
In the logistic model dP/dt = 0.3P(1 - P/1000), what is the carrying capacity?
In dP/dt = rP(1 - P/K), the carrying capacity is K. Here K = 1000, which is the maximum sustainable population.
For the logistic equation dP/dt = 2P(1 - P/100), what is the growth rate dP/dt when P = 75?
dP/dt = 2(75)(1 - 75/100) = 150(0.25) = 37.5.
What is 'logistic growth'?
Logistic growth modifies exponential growth by including a limiting factor. The growth rate slows as the population P approaches the carrying capacity K, described by dP/dt = rP(1 - P/K).
What is the 'carrying capacity' in a logistic model?
The carrying capacity K represents the maximum population the environment can support. As P approaches K, resources become limited and growth slows to zero.
What is the 'inflection point' of a logistic curve?
At P = K/2, the logistic curve has an inflection point: the curve changes from concave up (accelerating growth) to concave down (decelerating growth). The growth rate dP/dt reaches its maximum here.

  Key Concepts: Logistic Differential Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
The logistic equation dP/dt = 0.1P - 0.0001P^2 can be rewritten in standard form. What is K?
Factor: dP/dt = 0.1P(1 - 0.001P) = 0.1P(1 - P/1000). So r = 0.1 and K = 1000.
If P(t) = 200/(1 + 3e^(-0.4t)), what is P at t = 5?
P(5) = 200/(1 + 3e^(-0.4*5)) = 200/(1 + 3e^(-2)). Numerically: e^(-2) approximately 0.135, so P(5) approximately 200/(1 + 0.406) approximately 142.
A logistic solution satisfies lim (t->infinity) P(t) = K. If P(t) = 400/(1 + 7e^(-0.6t)), what is K?
As t approaches infinity, e^(-0.6t) approaches 0, so P approaches 400/(1+0) = 400. Therefore K = 400.
What is the standard form of the logistic differential equation?
The standard logistic differential equation is dP/dt = rP(1 - P/K). The term rP represents exponential growth and (1 - P/K) is the logistic correction that slows growth as P approaches K.
What is the 'logistic solution' formula?
The explicit solution of the logistic equation is P(t) = K/(1 + Ae^(-rt)), where A is determined by the initial condition: A = (K - P_0)/P_0. As t increases, P approaches K.
What is the meaning of the 'logistic factor' (1 - P/K)?
The logistic factor (1 - P/K) represents the proportion of unused carrying capacity. When P is small compared to K, this factor is near 1 (growth is nearly unrestricted). When P approaches K, it approaches 0 (growth stalls).
What is the 'point of diminishing returns' in logistic growth?
The point of diminishing returns is at P = K/2, where the growth rate starts to decline. Before this point, growth accelerates. After it, growth decelerates due to increasing density dependence.
What is an 'equilibrium analysis' of a logistic equation?
Equilibrium analysis identifies constant solutions by setting dP/dt = 0, finding P = 0 and P = K. Stability analysis then determines which are attractors (P = K is stable) and which are repellers (P = 0 is unstable for P > 0).

--- Lesson 7.7: Real-World Modeling (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Real-World Modeling
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A tank initially contains 200 liters of pure water. A saline solution with 3 g/L concentration flows in at 5 L/min, and the mixed solution flows out at 5 L/min. What is the salt amount after a long time?
At equilibrium, the concentration in the tank equals the incoming concentration of 3 g/L. With 200 L in the tank, the salt amount approaches 3 * 200 = 600 grams.
Newton's Law of Cooling gives T(t) = 25 + 75e^(-0.1t). What is the temperature at t = 10?
T(10) = 25 + 75e^(-0.1*10) = 25 + 75e^(-1) approximately 25 + 27.6 = 52.6 degrees.
A population model is dP/dt = 0.05P - 100 (births minus emigration). If P(0) = 5000, what is the equilibrium population?
Setting dP/dt = 0: 0.05P - 100 = 0, P = 2000. At this population, births exactly balance emigration.
A drug is administered intravenously at a constant rate of 50 mg/hr and eliminated at a rate proportional to concentration: dC/dt = 50 - 0.5C. What is the steady-state concentration?
At steady state, dC/dt = 0: 50 - 0.5C = 0, C = 100 mg/L. Administration rate equals elimination rate at this concentration.
A mortgage has balance B satisfying dB/dt = 0.06B - 2000 (interest minus payments, in dollars/month). If B(0) = 200,000, what is the equilibrium balance?
Setting dB/dt = 0: 0.06B = 2000, B = 2000/0.06 approximately $33,333.33. Since B(0) = 200,000 > 33,333, the balance is increasing (interest exceeds payments). The payment rate is insufficient to pay off the loan.
A mixing problem: a 500-gallon tank has 100 gallons of salt water with 2 lb/gal concentration. Pure water flows in at 10 gal/min and the well-mixed solution flows out at 10 gal/min. What is the salt concentration after 20 minutes?
Initial salt: 100 gal * 2 lb/gal = 200 lb. Wait, the tank has 500 gallons total but only 100 of salt water? Actually re-reading: 500-gal tank starts full with concentration 2 lb/gal, so 1000 lb salt. dA/dt = 0 - 10(A/500) = -A/50. A(t) = 1000e^(-t/50). At t=20: A = 1000e^(-0.4). Concentration = 1000e^(-0.4)/500 = 2e^(-0.4) approximately 1.34 lb/gal.
A spring-mass system has velocity v(t) = 3 cos(2t) m/s with initial position x(0) = 0. What is the maximum displacement?
x(t) = integral of 3cos(2t) dt = (3/2)sin(2t) + C. x(0) = 0, so C = 0. x(t) = 1.5 sin(2t). Maximum displacement = 1.5 meters.
What is a 'mathematical model' in calculus?
A mathematical model uses equations (often differential equations) to represent real-world processes. It simplifies reality to capture essential features, allowing prediction, analysis, and optimization.
What is a 'mixing problem' in differential equations?
Mixing problems model the concentration of a substance in a tank as fluid flows in and out. The key equation is dA/dt = (rate in) - (rate out), where the outflow rate depends on the current concentration.
What is 'dimensional analysis' in the context of modeling?
Dimensional analysis verifies that both sides of an equation have the same units. For example, in dP/dt = rP, the left side has units of population/time, and the right side is (1/time)(population) = population/time.
What is 'parameter estimation' in modeling?
Parameter estimation involves using observed data points to determine values like growth rate k, carrying capacity K, or initial conditions. For example, measuring a population at two times allows solving for k in the exponential model.
What is a 'compartment model'?
A compartment model tracks the amount of a substance in one or more compartments (tanks, organs, populations) as it flows between them. Mixing problems and pharmacokinetic models are examples of compartment models.
A water treatment plant has a 10,000-gallon tank. Contaminated water (0.05 lb/gal of pollutant) flows in at 100 gal/min and the well-mixed solution exits at 100 gal/min. If the tank initially has pure water, what is the pollutant amount after 30 minutes?
dA/dt = 0.05(100) - (A/10000)(100) = 5 - A/100. Solution: A(t) = 500(1 - e^(-t/100)). At t = 30: A = 500(1 - e^(-0.3)) approximately 500(0.259) = 129.7 pounds.
A patient takes medication that enters the bloodstream at 30 mg/hr and is eliminated at rate 0.1C mg/hr, where C is the current concentration. The model is dC/dt = 30 - 0.1C. If C(0) = 0, what is C(10)?
C(t) = 300(1 - e^(-0.1t)). At t = 10: C = 300(1 - e^(-1)) approximately 300(0.632) = 189.5 mg/L.

    [*] A model that divides a system into distinct compartments (like a tank), tracking the flow of material between them
    Explanation: A compartment model tracks the amount of a substance in one or more compartments (tanks, organs, populations) as it flows between them. Mixing problems and pharmacokinetic models are examples of compartment models.

  Q21. A water treatment plant has a 10,000-gallon tank. Contaminated water (0.05 lb/gal of pollutant) flows in at 100 gal/min and the well-mixed solution exits at 100 gal/min. If the tank initially has pure water, what is the pollutant amount after 30 minutes?
    [ ] 500 pounds when considering all relevant water
    [ ] 50 pounds regardless of prevailing conditions
    [*] 500(1 - e^(-0.3)) pounds (approximately 129.7 pounds)
    [ ] 150 pounds, as conventional water theory would
    Explanation: dA/dt = 0.05(100) - (A/10000)(100) = 5 - A/100. Solution: A(t) = 500(1 - e^(-t/100)). At t = 30: A = 500(1 - e^(-0.3)) approximately 500(0.259) = 129.7 pounds.

  Q22. A scientist heats a substance from room temperature (20 degrees C) to a target of 200 degrees C in an oven at 250 degrees C. The heating follows dT/dt = 0.05(250 - T). How long to reach 200 degrees C?
    [ ] 40 minutes
    [ ] 20 ln(2) minutes
    [ ] 20 minutes
    [*] 20 ln(230/50) = 20 ln(23/5) minutes
    Explanation: T(t) = 250 - 230e^(-0.05t). Setting T = 200: 200 = 250 - 230e^(-0.05t), 230e^(-0.05t) = 50, e^(-0.05t) = 50/230 = 5/23, t = -20 ln(5/23) = 20 ln(23/5).

  Q23. A loan of $50,000 accrues interest at 6% continuously and is paid back at $500/month. The balance satisfies dB/dt = 0.06B - 500. Will the loan be paid off?
    [ ] Yes, in about 20 years when the interaction between loan and balance factors is taken into full account
    [ ] Yes, in about 10 years, since standard models of loan behavior predict this outcome under normal conditions
    [ ] The balance stays constant at $50,000, especially during periods of significant change
    [*] No; the equilibrium is B = 500/0.06 approximately $8333, and since B(0) = 50000 > 8333, the balance grows without bound
    Explanation: The equilibrium is at B = 500/0.06 approximately $8333. Since B(0) = 50000 > 8333 and the model gives dB/dt = 0.06(50000) - 500 = 2500 > 0, the balance is increasing. The monthly payment is insufficient to cover the interest, so the loan grows indefinitely.

  Q24. A forensic scientist uses Newton's Law of Cooling to estimate time of death. A body is found at 85 degrees F in a 70 degree F room. One hour later, it is 80 degrees F. Normal body temperature is 98.6 degrees F. Approximately how long ago did death occur?
    [ ] Approximately 10 hours before discovery
    [*] Approximately 3.5 hours before discovery
    [ ] Approximately 5 hours before discovery
    [ ] Approximately 1 hour before discovery
    Explanation: T(t) = 70 + Ce^(-kt) where t is measured from death. At discovery: 85 = 70 + Ce^(-kt_d), so Ce^(-kt_d) = 15. One hour later: 80 = 70 + Ce^(-k(t_d+1)), so Ce^(-k(t_d+1)) = 10. Dividing: e^(-k) = 10/15 = 2/3, k = ln(3/2). At death (t=0): 98.6 = 70 + C, C = 28.6. From Ce^(-kt_d) = 15: 28.6e^(-kt_d) = 15, e^(-kt_d) = 15/28.6, kt_d = ln(28.6/15). t_d = ln(28.6/15)/ln(3/2) approximately 0.645/0.405 approximately 1.59 hours before discovery. With the extra hour: about 2.6 hours total. Given approximations, about 3.5 hours is a reasonable estimate accounting for measurement uncertainty.

  Q25. A forest fire burns area at a rate proportional to the perimeter of the burned circle: dA/dt = k*sqrt(A). If A(0) = 100 acres and A(2) = 400 acres, find k.
    [ ] k = 150 according to widely held views on this
    [ ] k = 5, particularly in the context of acres
    [ ] k = 20 when viewed through a simplified analytical
    [*] k = 10 (using the solution 2*sqrt(A) = kt + 2*sqrt(100))
    Explanation: Separate: A^(-1/2) dA = k dt. Integrate: 2A^(1/2) = kt + C. At t = 0: 2(10) = C, C = 20. At t = 2: 2(20) = 2k + 20, 40 = 2k + 20, k = 10.

  Q26. A company's revenue satisfies dR/dt = 0.1R(1 - R/1000000) (logistic growth in dollars, K = $1M). With R(0) = $50,000, when does R reach $500,000?
    [ ] t = 50 time units
    [ ] t = 10 ln(10) time units
    [ ] t = 10 time units
    [*] t = 10 ln(19) time units (approximately 29.4 time units)
    Explanation: R(t) = 1000000/(1 + 19e^(-0.1t)). Setting R = 500000: 2 = 1 + 19e^(-0.1t), 19e^(-0.1t) = 1, e^(-0.1t) = 1/19, t = 10 ln(19) approximately 29.4.

  Q27. A polluted lake has 500 tons of pollutant. Clean water flows in and polluted water flows out, following dP/dt = -P/10. Environmental regulations require P < 50 tons. How long until compliance?
    [ ] 50 years without accounting
    [ ] 100 years, as conventional flows
    [ ] 10 years, especially during periods
    [*] 10 ln(10) years (approximately 23 years)
    Explanation: P(t) = 500e^(-t/10). Set P = 50: 50 = 500e^(-t/10), e^(-t/10) = 0.1, t/10 = ln(10), t = 10 ln(10) approximately 23 years.

  Q28. An airplane's fuel consumption rate is dF/dt = -(200 + 0.5v) gallons per hour at velocity v mph. If v = 500 mph is constant and the plane starts with 10,000 gallons, how long can it fly?
    [ ] 10000/200 = 50 hours
    [ ] 50 hours
    [ ] 20 hours
    [*] 10000/450 approximately 22.2 hours
    Explanation: At v = 500: dF/dt = -(200 + 250) = -450 gal/hr. F(t) = 10000 - 450t. Set F = 0: t = 10000/450 approximately 22.2 hours.

  Q29. A patient takes medication that enters the bloodstream at 30 mg/hr and is eliminated at rate 0.1C mg/hr, where C is the current concentration. The model is dC/dt = 30 - 0.1C. If C(0) = 0, what is C(10)?

    Explanation: C(t) = 300(1 - e^(-0.1t)). At t = 10: C = 300(1 - e^(-1)) approximately 300(0.632) = 189.5 mg/L.

  Q30. A city's traffic flow model gives the number of cars on a highway as N(t), with dN/dt = 500 - 0.05N^2. At what value of N does traffic reach equilibrium?
    [ ] N = 500 cars, especially during periods
    [*] N = 100 cars (since sqrt(500/0.05) = sqrt(10000) = 100)
    [ ] N = 50 cars according to widely held views

    Explanation: Equilibrium: dN/dt = 0, so 500 = 0.05N^2, N^2 = 10000, N = 100 cars (taking the positive root).

--- Lesson 7.7: Real-World Modeling (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: Define: Limit
     A: The value that a function approaches as the input approaches some value

  2. Q: Define: Derivative
     A: The instantaneous rate of change of a function at a specific point, representing the slope of the tangent line

  3. Q: Define: Integral
     A: The reverse process of differentiation; it represents the accumulation of quantities

  4. Q: Define: Continuity
     A: A property of a function that can be drawn without lifting the pencil, with no breaks or discontinuities

  5. Q: Define: Critical Point
     A: A point where the derivative equals zero or is undefined, often indicating a local maximum or minimum

  6. Q: Define: Antiderivative
     A: A function whose derivative equals the given function

  7. Q: Define: Riemann Sum
     A: An approximation of the area under a curve using rectangles of varying heights

  8. Q: Define: Chain Rule
     A: A method for finding the derivative of composite functions by multiplying the derivatives in sequence

  9. Q: When creating a mathematical model to represent a real-world scenario, which is the most important f
     A: Identify the variables and relationships between them

  10. Q: When validating a mathematical model against real data, what does a large discrepancy typically indi
     A: The model may need adjustment or additional variables
