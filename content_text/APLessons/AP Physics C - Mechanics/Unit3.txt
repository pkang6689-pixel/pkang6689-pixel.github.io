--- Lesson 3.1: Circular Motion (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Circular Motion
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A car travels around a circular track of radius 50 m at 20 m/s. What is its centripetal acceleration?
a_c = v²/r = (20)²/50 = 400/50 = 8 m/s².
An object moves in a circle of radius 2 m with a period of 4 seconds. What is its speed?
v = 2πr/T = 2π(2)/4 = π m/s ≈ 3.14 m/s.
A ball on a 0.5 m string swings in a horizontal circle at 3 m/s. What is the centripetal acceleration?
a_c = v²/r = 9/0.5 = 18 m/s².
What is the frequency of an object that completes 120 revolutions per minute?
f = 120 rev/min ÷ 60 s/min = 2 rev/s = 2 Hz.
A particle moves in uniform circular motion. Its speed is 5 m/s and its centripetal acceleration is 10 m/s². What is the radius of the circle?
r = v²/a_c = 25/10 = 2.5 m.
A point on a turntable 0.15 m from the center moves at 0.3 m/s. What is the angular velocity?
ω = v/r = 0.3/0.15 = 2 rad/s.
A Ferris wheel has a radius of 15 m and rotates once every 30 seconds. What is the speed of a rider on the rim?
v = 2πr/T = 2π(15)/30 = π m/s ≈ 3.14 m/s.
A marble rolls inside a bowl following a circular path of radius 0.3 m at 2 rad/s. What is the centripetal acceleration?
a_c = rω² = 0.3(4) = 1.2 m/s².
What is uniform circular motion?
In uniform circular motion, the speed is constant but the direction changes continuously, meaning there is always centripetal acceleration directed toward the center.
What is centripetal acceleration?
Centripetal acceleration is directed radially inward toward the center of the circular path, changing the velocity's direction without changing its magnitude.
What is the definition of 'period' in circular motion?
The period T is the time to complete one full revolution. It relates to angular velocity by T = 2π/ω and to speed by T = 2πr/v.
What is the meaning of 'frequency' in circular motion?
Frequency f = 1/T = ω/(2π) is the number of complete cycles per second, measured in hertz (Hz) where 1 Hz = 1 revolution per second.
What is the relationship between period and angular velocity?
Since one revolution equals 2π radians and takes time T, the angular velocity ω = 2π/T, so T = 2π/ω.
What is the direction of velocity in circular motion?
The velocity vector in circular motion is always tangent to the circle, perpendicular to the radius at each point.
What is meant by 'centrifugal force'?
Centrifugal force is a pseudo-force that appears only in rotating reference frames. In an inertial frame, there is only the inward centripetal force; there is no real outward force.
A roller coaster car (600 kg) enters a vertical loop of radius 10 m at the bottom at 20 m/s. What is the normal force on the car at the bottom of the loop? (g = 10 m/s²)
At the bottom: N - mg = mv²/r. N = m(g + v²/r) = 600(10 + 400/10) = 600(10 + 40) = 600(50) = 30,000 N.
A conical pendulum has a 0.2 kg bob on a 1.5 m string making a 25° angle with the vertical. What is the speed of the bob?
r = L sinθ = 1.5 sin25° = 0.634 m. tan θ = v²/(rg), so v² = rg tanθ = 0.634(10)(tan25°) = 0.634(10)(0.466) = 2.96. v ≈ 1.72 m/s.
A race car on a banked circular track (radius 200 m, bank angle 30°) with μ_s = 0.5 between tires and track. What is the maximum speed the car can maintain? (g = 10 m/s²)
v_max = sqrt(rg(tanθ + μ_s)/(1 - μ_s tanθ)) = sqrt(200(10)(0.577 + 0.5)/(1 - 0.289)) = sqrt(2000(1.077)/0.711) = sqrt(3030) ≈ 55 m/s.
A child on a swing reaches the highest point of the arc where the swing makes 60° with the vertical. The swing's length is 3 m. What is the child's speed at the lowest point? (g = 10 m/s²)
Height drop: h = L(1-cosθ) = 3(1-cos60°) = 3(0.5) = 1.5 m. By energy conservation: v = sqrt(2gh) = sqrt(2(10)(1.5)) = sqrt(30) ≈ 5.48 m/s.
A satellite in low Earth orbit experiences an acceleration of approximately 9.2 m/s² (slightly less than g at the surface). If the orbit is at altitude 300 km above Earth (R_Earth = 6371 km), what is the orbital speed?
r = 6671 km = 6,671,000 m. v = sqrt(a_c × r) = sqrt(9.2 × 6,671,000) = sqrt(61,373,200) ≈ 7,834 m/s ≈ 7.73 km/s.
Important Concepts
What is the frequency of an object that completes 120 revolutions per minute
f = 120 rev/min ÷ 60 s/min = 2 rev/s = 2 Hz.
What is the meaning of 'frequency' in circular motion
Frequency f = 1/T = ω/(2π) is the number of complete cycles per second, measured in hertz (Hz) where 1 Hz = 1 revolution per second.

  1. Q: Define: Kinematics
     A: The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

  2. Q: Define: Force
     A: A push or pull that can change an object's velocity, measured in newtons

  3. Q: Define: Acceleration
     A: The rate of change of velocity with respect to time

  4. Q: Define: Momentum
     A: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its resistance to motion changes

  5. Q: Define: Work
     A: The transfer of energy that occurs when a force acts on an object over a distance

  6. Q: Define: Power
     A: The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts

  7. Q: Define: Torque
     A: The rotational equivalent of force; a measure of how much a force causes an object to rotate about an axis

  8. Q: Define: Angular Momentum
     A: The product of an object's moment of inertia and its angular velocity

  9. Q: Which of the following is the primary way water returns to the atmosphere?
     A: Evaporation from oceans and transpiration from plants

  10. Q: Which human activity most directly affects the water cycle?
     A: Urbanization reduces infiltration and increases runoff

--- Lesson 3.2: Centripetal Force (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Centripetal Force
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A 2 kg ball moves in a circle of radius 1.5 m at 6 m/s. What is the centripetal force?
F_c = mv²/r = 2(36)/1.5 = 72/1.5 = 48 N.
A 0.5 kg ball on a string moves in a horizontal circle of radius 0.8 m with a period of 0.5 s. What is the tension in the string?
ω = 2π/T = 2π/0.5 = 4π rad/s. F = mω²r = 0.5(16π²)(0.8) = 0.5(157.9)(0.8) ≈ 63.2 N.
A 70 kg person on a Ferris wheel of radius 12 m feels a normal force of 500 N at the top. What is the Ferris wheel's speed? (g = 10 m/s²)
At the top: mg - N = mv²/r. 700 - 500 = 70v²/12. 200 = 70v²/12. v² = 2400/70 = 34.3. v ≈ 5.9 m/s.
A 3 kg object moves in a vertical circle of radius 2 m. At the top, the string tension is 10 N. What is the speed at the top? (g = 10 m/s²)
At top: T + mg = mv²/r. 10 + 30 = 3v²/2. 40 = 1.5v². v² = 26.67. v ≈ 5.16 m/s.
The Moon orbits Earth at a distance of 3.84 × 10⁸ m with a period of 27.3 days. What is the centripetal acceleration?
T = 27.3 × 86400 = 2,358,720 s. ω = 2π/T ≈ 2.66 × 10⁻⁶ rad/s. a_c = rω² = 3.84×10⁸ × (2.66×10⁻⁶)² ≈ 0.0027 m/s².
What is centripetal force?
Centripetal force is not a new type of force; it is the net inward force that maintains circular motion. It can be provided by gravity, tension, friction, normal force, or any combination.
What is the formula for centripetal force?
Centripetal force F_c = mv²/r = mω²r, always directed radially inward toward the center of the circular path.
What is the purpose of banking a curve?
Banking tilts the normal force inward, so its horizontal component provides (or assists with) the centripetal force. At the design speed, no friction is needed.
What is the 'critical speed' at the top of a vertical loop?
At critical speed, gravity alone provides the centripetal force (N = 0): mg = mv²/r, giving v_c = sqrt(rg). Below this speed, the object loses contact.
What is a conical pendulum?
A conical pendulum has a mass on a string that traces a horizontal circle, with the string tracing the surface of a cone. The horizontal component of tension provides centripetal force.
What is the role of the normal force in circular motion on a vertical loop?
In a vertical loop, the normal force is always perpendicular to the surface (toward center) and varies: largest at the bottom (N = mg + mv²/r) and smallest at the top (N = mv²/r - mg).
A 1200 kg car enters a dip in the road with radius of curvature 30 m at 15 m/s. What is the normal force on the car at the bottom of the dip? (g = 10 m/s²)
At the bottom: N - mg = mv²/r. N = m(g + v²/r) = 1200(10 + 225/30) = 1200(10 + 7.5) = 1200(17.5) = 21,000 N.
A 0.4 kg ball attached to a 1.2 m string is swung in a vertical circle. At the bottom of the arc, the speed is 8 m/s. What is the tension in the string? (g = 10 m/s²)
At bottom: T - mg = mv²/r. T = mg + mv²/r = 0.4(10) + 0.4(64)/1.2 = 4 + 21.3 = 25.3 N.
A roller coaster car (500 kg) enters a vertical loop of radius 8 m. At the top of the loop, passengers feel 'weightless.' What is the car's speed at the top? (g = 10 m/s²)
'Weightless' means N = 0, so gravity provides all centripetal force: mg = mv²/r, v = sqrt(rg) = sqrt(80) ≈ 8.94 m/s.

--- Lesson 3.3: Universal Gravitation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Universal Gravitation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Two 10 kg masses are 0.5 m apart. What is the gravitational force between them? (G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²)
F = Gm₁m₂/r² = (6.67×10⁻¹¹)(10)(10)/(0.25) = 6.67×10⁻⁹/0.25 = 2.67×10⁻⁸ N.
What is the acceleration due to gravity at a height equal to Earth's radius above the surface? (g_surface = 10 m/s²)
At height h = R, distance from center is 2R. g = GM/(2R)² = GM/(4R²) = g_surface/4 = 10/4 = 2.5 m/s².
What is the escape velocity from Earth's surface? (R = 6.37 × 10⁶ m, g = 9.8 m/s²)
v_esc = sqrt(2gR) = sqrt(2 × 9.8 × 6.37×10⁶) = sqrt(1.25×10⁸) ≈ 11,180 m/s ≈ 11.2 km/s.
A planet has twice Earth's mass and twice Earth's radius. What is the surface gravity compared to Earth's?
g = GM/R². With M → 2M and R → 2R: g' = G(2M)/(2R)² = 2GM/4R² = g/2.
What is the gravitational potential energy of a 100 kg satellite at a distance of 2R from Earth's center? (g = 10 m/s², R = 6.37 × 10⁶ m)
U = -GMm/r = -gR²m/r = -(10)(6.37×10⁶)²(100)/(2×6.37×10⁶) = -(10)(6.37×10⁶)(100)/2 = -3.19×10⁹ J.
What is the gravitational force between Earth (6 × 10²⁴ kg) and the Moon (7.35 × 10²² kg) at a distance of 3.84 × 10⁸ m?
F = Gm₁m₂/r² = (6.67×10⁻¹¹)(6×10²⁴)(7.35×10²²)/(3.84×10⁸)² ≈ 1.98 × 10²⁰ N.
What is Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation?
Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation states that every particle in the universe attracts every other particle with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.
What is the gravitational constant G?
G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² is a fundamental constant of nature. It is extremely small, which is why gravity is the weakest of the four fundamental forces.
What is the gravitational field?
The gravitational field g = F/m = GM/r² represents the gravitational force per unit mass at any point in space, measured in N/kg (equivalent to m/s²).
What is gravitational potential energy in the universal form?
The universal gravitational PE is U = -GMm/r, where the negative sign indicates a bound system. U → 0 as r → ∞, and U becomes more negative as objects get closer.
What is escape velocity?
Escape velocity is the minimum launch speed to reach infinite distance with zero remaining kinetic energy: v_esc = sqrt(2GM/R) = sqrt(2gR).
What is the shell theorem?
Newton's shell theorem has two parts: (1) outside a uniform sphere, it acts as a point mass at the center, and (2) inside a uniform shell, the gravitational force is zero.
What is the Cavendish experiment?
Henry Cavendish (1798) used a torsion balance to measure the tiny gravitational attraction between lead spheres, determining G and effectively 'weighing the Earth.'
What is the relationship between g and G?
The local gravitational acceleration g at a planet's surface equals GM/R². While g varies by location, G is a universal constant.
NASA plans to send a probe to Jupiter (mass 1.9 × 10²⁷ kg, radius 7.15 × 10⁷ m). What is the surface gravity and escape velocity of Jupiter? (G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹)
g = GM/R² = (6.67×10⁻¹¹)(1.9×10²⁷)/(7.15×10⁷)² ≈ 24.8 m/s². v_esc = sqrt(2gR) = sqrt(2(24.8)(7.15×10⁷)) ≈ 59,500 m/s ≈ 59.5 km/s.
A binary star system has two stars of masses M and 2M orbiting their common center of mass at distance d apart. What is the orbital period?
For a binary system, Kepler's third law uses total mass: T² = 4π²d³/(G(M+2M)) = 4π²d³/(3GM). So T = 2π√(d³/(3GM)).
GPS satellites orbit at 20,200 km altitude. What is the gravitational acceleration at that altitude? (g₀ = 9.8 m/s², R_E = 6371 km)
r = 6371 + 20200 = 26571 km. g = g₀(R_E/r)² = 9.8(6371/26571)² = 9.8(0.2398)² = 9.8(0.0575) ≈ 0.56 m/s².

--- Lesson 3.4: Orbital Motion (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Orbital Motion
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the orbital velocity of a satellite in a circular orbit of radius r around Earth? (Use M_E for Earth's mass)
Setting gravitational force equal to centripetal force: GMm/r² = mv²/r gives v = sqrt(GM/r).
A satellite orbits Earth at radius 2R (R = Earth's radius). If g at the surface is 10 m/s², what is its orbital speed?
v = sqrt(GM/r) = sqrt(gR²/(2R)) = sqrt(gR/2). With g = 10: v = sqrt(10R/2) = sqrt(5R) m/s.
What is the total mechanical energy of a satellite in a circular orbit of radius r?
E = KE + PE = ½mv² + (-GMm/r) = GMm/(2r) - GMm/r = -GMm/(2r). Negative energy means a bound orbit.
A geostationary orbit has a period of 24 hours. If the orbital radius is approximately 42,164 km, what is the orbital speed?
v = 2πr/T = 2π(42,164,000)/(86,400) ≈ 264,924,000/86,400 ≈ 3066 m/s ≈ 3.07 km/s.
A satellite's orbit has a period of 90 minutes. What is the altitude above Earth's surface? (R_E = 6371 km, g = 9.8 m/s²)
T = 2π sqrt(r³/(gR²)). r³ = gR²T²/(4π²) = 9.8(6.371×10⁶)²(5400)²/(4π²). Solving gives r ≈ 6,651 km, altitude ≈ 280 km.
What is the ratio of escape velocity to circular orbital velocity at the same radius?
v_orb = sqrt(GM/r) and v_esc = sqrt(2GM/r). Ratio = v_esc/v_orb = sqrt(2) ≈ 1.414.
Planet A has 4 times the orbital radius of Planet B around the same star. What is the ratio T_A/T_B?
T² ∝ r³. (T_A/T_B)² = (4)³ = 64. T_A/T_B = 8.
What is Kepler's first law?
Kepler's first law (Law of Ellipses) states that planetary orbits are ellipses with the central body at one focus.
What is Kepler's second law?
Kepler's second law (Law of Equal Areas) means planets move faster when closer to the Sun (perihelion) and slower when farther (aphelion). This reflects conservation of angular momentum.
What is Kepler's third law?
Kepler's third law relates period and orbital size: T² = (4π²/GM)a³, where a is the semi-major axis.
What is a geostationary orbit?
A geostationary orbit is a circular equatorial orbit at approximately 35,786 km altitude where the orbital period matches Earth's rotation, keeping the satellite above the same point.
What is perihelion?
Perihelion is the closest approach point to the Sun in a solar orbit. The analogous term for Earth orbits is perigee.
What is the semi-major axis of an elliptical orbit?
The semi-major axis a is half the longest dimension of the ellipse. It determines the orbital energy (E = -GMm/(2a)) and period via Kepler's third law.
What is the relationship between orbital energy and semi-major axis?
The total orbital energy depends only on the semi-major axis: E = -GMm/(2a). This holds for both circular and elliptical orbits.
What is a Hohmann transfer orbit?
A Hohmann transfer is the most fuel-efficient two-impulse maneuver to move between coplanar circular orbits, using an elliptical transfer orbit tangent to both.
A spacecraft must transfer from a circular orbit at r₁ = 7000 km to r₂ = 14000 km using a Hohmann transfer. What is the semi-major axis of the transfer orbit?
The Hohmann transfer ellipse has periapsis at r₁ and apoapsis at r₂, so a = (r₁ + r₂)/2 = (7000 + 14000)/2 = 10,500 km.
Jupiter's moon Io orbits at 4.22 × 10⁸ m with period 1.77 days, and Europa orbits at 6.71 × 10⁸ m. Using Kepler's third law, what is Europa's orbital period?
T_E/T_I = (r_E/r_I)^(3/2) = (6.71/4.22)^(3/2) = (1.59)^(3/2) = 2.006. T_E = 1.77 × 2.006 = 3.55 days.
An elliptical orbit has perigee altitude 200 km and apogee altitude 600 km above Earth. What is the orbital energy per unit mass? (R_E = 6371 km, g = 9.8 m/s²)
r_p = 6571 km, r_a = 6971 km. a = (6571+6971)/2 = 6771 km. E/m = -GM/(2a) = -gR²/(2a) = -9.8(6.371×10⁶)²/(2×6.771×10⁶) ≈ -29.3 MJ/kg.
A spy satellite needs to photograph a location from the lowest possible altitude. If atmospheric drag becomes significant below 150 km, what is the maximum orbital speed? (R_E = 6371 km, g = 9.8 m/s²)
r = 6521 km. v = sqrt(gR²/r) = sqrt(9.8(6.371×10⁶)²/6.521×10⁶) = sqrt(6.1×10⁷) ≈ 7810 m/s ≈ 7.81 km/s.
A comet approaches the Sun with speed 20 km/s at 5 AU from the Sun. What is its speed at perihelion (0.5 AU)? Assume the comet's orbit is governed only by the Sun.
Using energy conservation: ½v₁² - GM/r₁ = ½v₂² - GM/r₂. With GM/1AU = v_E² = (30 km/s)²: ½(400) - 900/5 = ½v₂² - 900/0.5. 200 - 180 = ½v₂² - 1800. ½v₂² = 1820. v₂ ≈ 60 km/s. Approximately 55 km/s.
