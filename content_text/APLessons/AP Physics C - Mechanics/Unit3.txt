--- Lesson 3.1: Circular Motion (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: Define: Kinematics
     A: The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

  2. Q: Define: Force
     A: A push or pull that can change an object's velocity, measured in newtons

  3. Q: Define: Acceleration
     A: The rate of change of velocity with respect to time

  4. Q: Define: Momentum
     A: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its resistance to motion changes

  5. Q: Define: Work
     A: The transfer of energy that occurs when a force acts on an object over a distance

  6. Q: Define: Power
     A: The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts

  7. Q: Define: Torque
     A: The rotational equivalent of force; a measure of how much a force causes an object to rotate about an axis

  8. Q: Define: Angular Momentum
     A: The product of an object's moment of inertia and its angular velocity

  9. Q: Which of the following is the primary way water returns to the atmosphere?
     A: Evaporation from oceans and transpiration from plants

  10. Q: Which human activity most directly affects the water cycle?
     A: Urbanization reduces infiltration and increases runoff
