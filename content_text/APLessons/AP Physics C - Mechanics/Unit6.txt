--- Lesson 6.1: Angular Kinematics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angular Kinematics
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A wheel rotates at 600 revolutions per minute (RPM). What is its angular velocity in rad/s?
ω = 600 rev/min × 2π rad/rev × 1 min/60 s = 600(2π)/60 = 20π rad/s.
A disk starts from rest and accelerates uniformly at 4 rad/s² for 5 seconds. What is its angular velocity?
ω = ω₀ + αt = 0 + 4(5) = 20 rad/s.
A wheel decelerates from 30 rad/s to rest in 6 seconds. What is the angular acceleration?
α = (ω - ω₀)/t = (0 - 30)/6 = -5 rad/s².
The angular position of a disk is θ(t) = 3t² + 2t (radians). What is ω at t = 3 s?
ω = dθ/dt = 6t + 2. At t = 3: ω = 18 + 2 = 20 rad/s.
A centrifuge spins at 40 rad/s and has radius 0.2 m. What is the centripetal acceleration at the rim?
a_c = ω²r = (40)²(0.2) = 1600(0.2) = 320 m/s².
A wheel makes 10 complete revolutions while decelerating from 20 rad/s to 10 rad/s. What is α?
θ = 10(2π) = 20π rad. Using ω² = ω₀² + 2αθ: 100 = 400 + 2α(20π). α = -300/(40π) ≈ -2.39 rad/s².
A gear with radius 0.1 m turns at 50 rad/s and drives a second gear of radius 0.25 m. What is the angular velocity of the second gear?
Tangential speeds must match: r₁ω₁ = r₂ω₂. ω₂ = (0.1/0.25)(50) = 20 rad/s.
If ω = 6t² rad/s, what is the angular acceleration at t = 2 s?
α = dω/dt = 12t. At t = 2: α = 24 rad/s².
What is angular displacement?
Angular displacement (θ) is the angle through which an object rotates, measured in radians.
What is angular velocity?
Angular velocity ω is the rate of change of angular position: ω = dθ/dt, measured in rad/s.
What is angular acceleration?
Angular acceleration α = dω/dt = d²θ/dt², measured in rad/s².
What is the relationship between tangential velocity and angular velocity?
Tangential velocity v = rω. Points farther from the axis (larger r) have greater tangential speed.
What is a radian?
A radian is the angle at which the arc length s equals the radius r. One full revolution = 2π radians.
What is tangential acceleration?
Tangential acceleration a_t = rα is the component along the direction of motion, responsible for changing speed.
What is centripetal acceleration in terms of angular velocity?
Centripetal acceleration a_c = ω²r = v²/r, always directed radially inward toward the axis of rotation.
What is the period of rotation?
The period T is the time for one full revolution: T = 2π/ω. Its reciprocal is frequency: f = 1/T.
A car rounds a curve of radius 50 m at 20 m/s. Simultaneously, the driver accelerates at 3 m/s². What is the magnitude of the car's total acceleration?
a_c = v²/r = 400/50 = 8 m/s². a_t = 3 m/s². Total a = √(a_c² + a_t²) = √(64 + 9) = √73 ≈ 8.54 m/s².
A washing machine drum (radius 0.25 m) spins at 1200 RPM during the spin cycle. What is the centripetal acceleration at the drum wall in terms of g? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
ω = 1200(2π)/60 = 40π rad/s. a_c = ω²r = (40π)²(0.25) = 1600π²(0.25) = 400π² ≈ 3948 m/s². In g: 3948/10 ≈ 395g ≈ 400g.
A hard drive disk spins at 7200 RPM. A read/write head is positioned 3 cm from the center. What is the linear speed of the disk surface under the head?
ω = 7200(2π)/60 = 240π rad/s. v = rω = 0.03(240π) = 7.2π ≈ 22.6 m/s.
A Ferris wheel of radius 15 m completes one revolution every 30 seconds. A bolt falls from the top of the wheel. What is the bolt's horizontal velocity at the moment it falls?
ω = 2π/T = 2π/30 = π/15 rad/s. v = rω = 15(π/15) = π ≈ 3.14 m/s.
A turbine blade accelerates from 100 rad/s to 400 rad/s while making 500 revolutions. What is the angular acceleration?
θ = 500(2π) = 1000π rad. ω² = ω₀² + 2αθ. 400² - 100² = 2α(1000π). 150000 = 2000πα. α = 75/π ≈ 23.9 rad/s².
A satellite orbits Earth at a constant angular velocity, completing one orbit in 90 minutes. The orbital radius is 6,700 km. What is the satellite's speed?
ω = 2π/T = 2π/(90×60) = 2π/5400 rad/s. v = rω = 6,700,000 × 2π/5400 ≈ 6,700,000 × 0.001164 ≈ 7,798 m/s ≈ 7,800 m/s.

--- Lesson 6.1: Angular Kinematics (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: Define: Kinematics
     A: The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

  2. Q: Define: Force
     A: A push or pull that can change an object's velocity, measured in newtons

  3. Q: Define: Acceleration
     A: The rate of change of velocity with respect to time

  4. Q: Define: Momentum
     A: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its resistance to motion changes

  5. Q: Define: Work
     A: The transfer of energy that occurs when a force acts on an object over a distance

  6. Q: Define: Power
     A: The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts

  7. Q: Define: Torque
     A: The rotational equivalent of force; a measure of how much a force causes an object to rotate about an axis

  8. Q: Define: Angular Momentum
     A: The product of an object's moment of inertia and its angular velocity

  9. Q: Which of the following is the primary way water returns to the atmosphere?
     A: Evaporation from oceans and transpiration from plants

  10. Q: Which human activity most directly affects the water cycle?
     A: Urbanization reduces infiltration and increases runoff

--- Lesson 6.2: Torque and Rotational Dynamics (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Torque and Rotational Dynamics
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A 20 N force is applied perpendicular to a wrench 0.3 m from the bolt. What is the torque?
τ = rF sinθ = 0.3 × 20 × sin 90° = 6 N·m.
A force of 50 N is applied at 30° to a 0.4 m lever arm. What is the torque?
τ = rF sinθ = 0.4 × 50 × sin 30° = 0.4 × 50 × 0.5 = 10 N·m.
A disk with moment of inertia 2 kg·m² has a net torque of 10 N·m applied. What is its angular acceleration?
α = τ/I = 10/2 = 5 rad/s².
Two forces create torques of +8 N·m and −3 N·m on a pivot. What is the net torque?
τ_net = 8 + (−3) = 5 N·m.
A torque of 12 N·m acts on a body with I = 4 kg·m² starting from rest. What is ω after 3 s?
α = τ/I = 12/4 = 3 rad/s². ω = αt = 3 × 3 = 9 rad/s.
What is the SI unit of torque?
Torque is measured in newton-meters (N·m). Although dimensionally equivalent to joules, the unit N·m is used for torque.
A disk with I = 0.1 kg·m² experiences angular acceleration of 40 rad/s². What is the net torque?
τ = Iα = 0.1 × 40 = 4 N·m.
What is torque?
Torque is the rotational analog of force. It measures how effectively a force causes rotation about an axis, calculated as τ = r × F.
What is Newton's Second Law for rotation?
Newton's Second Law for rotation states τ_net = Iα. It is the rotational analog of F_net = ma.
What is static rotational equilibrium?
Static rotational equilibrium means the net torque about any axis is zero (Στ = 0) and the object has no angular acceleration.
What is a couple in mechanics?
A couple consists of two equal, opposite, parallel forces that produce a net torque but zero net force. The torque of a couple is independent of the pivot point.
What is the cross product in the context of torque?
The cross product τ = r × F is a vector operation. Its magnitude is rF sinθ and its direction is perpendicular to both r and F, determined by the right-hand rule.
What is the standard sign convention for torque?
By standard convention, counterclockwise torques are positive and clockwise torques are negative, consistent with the right-hand rule.
What is the condition for translational equilibrium in a torque problem?
Translational equilibrium requires ΣF = 0. In combined equilibrium problems, both ΣF = 0 (translational) and Στ = 0 (rotational) must hold.
A uniform beam of mass 20 kg and length 4 m rests on two supports at each end. A 60 kg person stands 1 m from the left end. What is the normal force at the left support? (g = 10 m/s²)
Take torques about the right support. N_L × 4 = 20(10)(2) + 60(10)(3) = 400 + 1800 = 2200. N_L = 2200/4 = 550 N.
A solid disk (I = ½MR²) of mass 4 kg and radius 0.2 m has a string wrapped around its rim. A constant 5 N tension is applied tangentially. What is the angular acceleration?
I = ½(4)(0.2²) = ½(4)(0.04) = 0.08 kg·m². τ = FR = 5 × 0.2 = 1 N·m. α = τ/I = 1/0.08 = 12.5 rad/s².
A 3 m uniform plank (10 kg) extends 1 m over a table edge. What is the maximum mass that can be placed at the overhanging end without tipping? (g = 10 m/s²)
Pivot at table edge. The plank's center of mass is 1.5 m from the left end, which is 0.5 m from the table edge on the supported side. Stabilizing torque: 10(10)(0.5) = 50 N·m. Tipping torque: m(10)(1) = 10m. Setting equal: 10m = 50, so m = 5 kg.
A pulley of radius 0.1 m and I = 0.02 kg·m² has a rope with tension 30 N on one side and 20 N on the other. What is its angular acceleration?
τ_net = (30 − 20) × 0.1 = 1 N·m. α = τ/I = 1/0.02 = 50 rad/s².
A sign of mass 8 kg hangs from the end of a uniform horizontal beam (mass 4 kg, length 2 m) attached to a wall. A cable at 30° to the beam supports the far end. What is the cable tension? (g = 10 m/s²)
Torques about the wall: T sin 30° × 2 = 4(10)(1) + 8(10)(2) = 40 + 160 = 200. T × 1 = 200. T = 200 N.
A merry-go-round (I = 500 kg·m²) is pushed with 200 N tangential force at its 2 m radius rim. Starting from rest, what is its angular speed after 10 s?
τ = FR = 200 × 2 = 400 N·m. α = τ/I = 400/500 = 0.8 rad/s². ω = αt = 0.8 × 10 = 8 rad/s.
An Atwood machine has a solid disk pulley (mass 4 kg, radius 0.1 m) with masses 6 kg and 2 kg. What is the system's acceleration? (g = 10 m/s²)
I = ½(4)(0.01) = 0.02 kg·m². I/R² = 0.02/0.01 = 2 kg. a = (m₁−m₂)g/(m₁+m₂+I/R²) = (6−2)(10)/(6+2+2) = 40/10 = 4 m/s².
A 5 m ladder (15 kg) leans against a frictionless wall at 60° to the ground. A 75 kg person stands at the ladder's midpoint. What is the friction force at the ground? (g = 10 m/s²)
Torques about the base: F_wall × 5 sin 60° = 15(10)(2.5 cos 60°) + 75(10)(2.5 cos 60°). F_wall × (5√3/2) = 187.5 + 937.5 = 1125. F_wall = 1125/(2.5√3) = 450/√3 = 150√3 ≈ 260 N. Friction at the ground equals F_wall = 150√3 ≈ 260 N.
Real-World Applications
Example: A 3 m uniform plank (10 kg) extends 1 m over a table edge. What is the maximum mass that can be placed at the overhanging end without tipping? (g = 10 m/s²)
Pivot at table edge. The plank's center of mass is 1.5 m from the left end, which is 0.5 m from the table edge on the supported side. Stabilizing torque: 10(10)(0.5) = 50 N·m. Tipping torque: m(10)(1) = 10m. Setting equal: 10m = 50, so m = 5 kg.

--- Lesson 6.3: Rotational Inertia (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Rotational Inertia
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the moment of inertia of a point mass of 3 kg located 2 m from the axis of rotation?
I = mr² = 3 × 2² = 3 × 4 = 12 kg·m².
What is the moment of inertia of a solid disk of mass 4 kg and radius 0.5 m about its central axis?
I = ½MR² = ½(4)(0.25) = 0.5 kg·m².
A thin hoop of mass 2 kg and radius 0.3 m rotates about its central axis. What is its moment of inertia?
For a hoop: I = MR² = 2 × (0.3)² = 2 × 0.09 = 0.18 kg·m².
Using the parallel axis theorem, if a disk has I_cm = 0.5 kg·m², mass 4 kg, and is rotated about an axis 1 m from its center, what is I?
Parallel axis theorem: I = I_cm + Md² = 0.5 + 4(1²) = 0.5 + 4 = 4.5 kg·m².
A solid sphere has mass 5 kg and radius 0.2 m. What is its moment of inertia about a diameter?
I = (2/5)MR² = (2/5)(5)(0.04) = 0.08 kg·m².
A uniform thin rod of mass 3 kg and length 2 m is rotated about its center. What is its moment of inertia?
I = (1/12)ML² = (1/12)(3)(4) = 1 kg·m².
The same 3 kg, 2 m rod is rotated about one end. What is its moment of inertia?
I = (1/3)ML² = (1/3)(3)(4) = 4 kg·m².
Two point masses, 2 kg each, are connected by a massless rod 1 m long and rotate about the center. What is the total I?
Each mass is 0.5 m from the center. I_total = m₁r₁² + m₂r₂² = 2(0.5)² + 2(0.5)² = 0.5 + 0.5 = 1.0 kg·m².
What is moment of inertia?
Moment of inertia (I) is the rotational analog of mass. It quantifies how difficult it is to change an object's rotational motion, depending on mass distribution relative to the axis.
What is the parallel axis theorem?
The parallel axis theorem states I = I_cm + Md², where d is the distance between the center-of-mass axis and the new parallel axis. It always increases I relative to the center of mass.
What is the perpendicular axis theorem?
The perpendicular axis theorem applies to planar (flat) objects: I_z = I_x + I_y, where z is perpendicular to the plane and x, y are in the plane.
What is a 'principal axis' of rotation?
A principal axis is one about which the angular momentum and angular velocity are parallel. Products of inertia are zero, and the object rotates without wobbling.
What is the formula for the moment of inertia of a continuous body?
For a continuous mass distribution, I = ∫ r² dm, where r is the perpendicular distance from each mass element dm to the axis of rotation.
What is the difference between a solid cylinder and a hollow cylinder of the same mass and radius in terms of I?
Solid cylinder: I = ½MR². Hollow (thin-walled) cylinder: I = MR². The hollow cylinder has twice the I because all mass is at maximum distance from the axis.
A 2 kg uniform disk (R = 0.15 m) is mounted on an axle 0.25 m from its center using the parallel axis theorem. What is I about the axle?
I_cm = ½MR² = ½(2)(0.0225) = 0.0225 kg·m². I = I_cm + Md² = 0.0225 + 2(0.0625) = 0.0225 + 0.125 = 0.1475 kg·m².
Two identical solid spheres (each 1 kg, radius 0.05 m) are connected by a 0.4 m massless rod and rotated about the center of the rod. What is I_total?
Each sphere: I_cm = (2/5)(1)(0.0025) = 0.001 kg·m². Distance from center of rod to sphere center: d = 0.2 m. By parallel axis theorem: I_each = 0.001 + 1(0.04) = 0.041. I_total = 2 × 0.041 = 0.082 kg·m².
A flywheel has I = 8 kg·m² and rotates at 300 RPM. What is its rotational kinetic energy?
ω = 300 × 2π/60 = 10π rad/s. KE = ½Iω² = ½(8)(100π²) = 400π² ≈ 3,950 J.
A composite object consists of a solid disk (mass 3 kg, radius 0.2 m) with a point mass of 1 kg attached at the rim. What is the total I about the disk's center?
I_disk = ½(3)(0.04) = 0.06 kg·m². I_point = 1 × (0.2)² = 0.04 kg·m². I_total = 0.06 + 0.04 = 0.10 kg·m².
A uniform door (mass 12 kg, width 0.9 m) is modeled as a thin rectangular slab rotating about its hinges. What is its moment of inertia? (I = ⅓ML² for a rod about one end)
Treating the door as a slab rotating about one edge: I = (1/3)Mw² = (1/3)(12)(0.81) = (1/3)(9.72) = 3.24 kg·m².

--- Lesson 6.4: Angular Momentum and Conservation (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angular Momentum and Conservation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A disk with I = 2 kg·m² rotates at 5 rad/s. What is its angular momentum?
L = Iω = 2 × 5 = 10 kg·m²/s.
A 0.3 kg ball moves at 4 m/s in a circle of radius 0.5 m. What is its angular momentum about the center?
L = mvr = 0.3 × 4 × 0.5 = 0.6 kg·m²/s.
If no external torque acts on a system, what is conserved?
When the net external torque on a system is zero, angular momentum is conserved: L_initial = L_final.
A spinning figure skater pulls her arms in, reducing her moment of inertia from 4 to 2 kg·m². If she was spinning at 3 rad/s, what is her new angular velocity?
L is conserved: I₁ω₁ = I₂ω₂. ω₂ = (4 × 3)/2 = 6 rad/s.
What is the SI unit of angular momentum?
Angular momentum L = Iω has units of kg·m²/s (equivalent to N·m·s or J·s).
A point particle of mass 2 kg moves at 3 m/s along a straight line that passes 4 m from a fixed point. What is its angular momentum about that point?
L = mvr⊥ = 2 × 3 × 4 = 24 kg·m²/s. Here r⊥ is the perpendicular distance from the point to the line of motion.
A torque of 6 N·m is applied to a wheel for 3 s. What is the change in angular momentum?
ΔL = τ × Δt = 6 × 3 = 18 kg·m²/s. This is the rotational analog of impulse.
What is angular momentum?
Angular momentum (L) is the rotational analog of linear momentum. For a rigid body, L = Iω; for a particle, L = r × p.
What is the law of conservation of angular momentum?
Conservation of angular momentum: when the net external torque on a system is zero, the total angular momentum is constant (L_i = L_f).
What is angular impulse?
Angular impulse is τ_net × Δt. By the angular impulse–momentum theorem, it equals the change in angular momentum: ΔL = τ_net Δt.
What is 'precession' in the context of angular momentum?
Precession is the gradual rotation of the spin axis of a rotating body when an external torque is applied perpendicular to the angular momentum. The rate is Ω = τ/L.
What is the angular momentum of a particle?
For a particle, L = r × p = r × (mv). Its magnitude is L = mvr sinθ, and its direction is perpendicular to both r and v.
What is a 'gyroscope'?
A gyroscope is a rapidly spinning object that resists changes to its orientation due to conservation of angular momentum. It is used in navigation and stabilization.
What is the relationship between angular momentum and rotational kinetic energy?
KE = ½Iω² = ½(Iω)ω = ½Lω. Also KE = L²/(2I). Both expressions are valid.
A 50 kg child runs at 4 m/s tangent to the rim of a stationary merry-go-round (I = 600 kg·m², radius 2 m) and jumps on. What is the final angular velocity?
L_child = mvr = 50 × 4 × 2 = 400 kg·m²/s. I_child on rim = mr² = 50(4) = 200 kg·m². I_total = 600 + 200 = 800 kg·m². ω_f = L/I_total = 400/800 = 0.5 rad/s.
Two identical disks (each I = 3 kg·m²) spin about the same vertical axis. Disk 1 spins at 10 rad/s and Disk 2 at 4 rad/s in the same direction. They are pressed together until they reach a common angular velocity. What is it?
L_total = Iω₁ + Iω₂ = 3(10) + 3(4) = 42 kg·m²/s. I_total = 3 + 3 = 6 kg·m². ω_f = 42/6 = 7 rad/s.
A neutron star collapses and its radius shrinks by a factor of 1000. If it originally rotated once every 10 days, what is the new rotation period?
For a uniform sphere: I ∝ R². Conserving angular momentum: I₁ω₁ = I₂ω₂. Since ω = 2π/T: R₁²/T₁ = R₂²/T₂. T₂ = T₁ × (R₂/R₁)² = 10 days × (1/1000)² = 10 × 86400 s × 10⁻⁶ = 864000 × 10⁻⁶ = 0.864 s.
A turntable (I = 0.1 kg·m²) rotates freely at 10 rad/s. A 0.05 kg piece of clay is dropped vertically onto it at r = 0.2 m from the center. What is the new angular velocity?
I_clay = mr² = 0.05(0.04) = 0.002 kg·m². I_total = 0.1 + 0.002 = 0.102 kg·m². ω_f = I_iω_i/I_f = 0.1(10)/0.102 = 1/0.102 = 9.80 rad/s.
A playground roundabout (I = 200 kg·m²) spins at 2 rad/s. A 40 kg child at the rim (r = 1.5 m) moves to r = 0.5 m. What is the new angular velocity?
I_child_initial = 40(1.5)² = 90 kg·m². I_total_i = 200 + 90 = 290 kg·m². I_child_final = 40(0.25) = 10 kg·m². I_total_f = 200 + 10 = 210 kg·m². ω_f = 290(2)/210 = 580/210 = 2.76 rad/s ≈ 2.70 rad/s.
A bullet (0.01 kg, 400 m/s) strikes and embeds in a thin rod (2 kg, 1 m long) at its end. The rod is pivoted at the other end. What is the angular velocity just after impact?
L_bullet = mvr = 0.01 × 400 × 1 = 4 kg·m²/s. After impact: I_rod = (1/3)(2)(1) = 0.667 kg·m². I_bullet = 0.01(1)² = 0.01 kg·m². I_total = 0.677 kg·m². ω = L/I = 4/0.677 = 5.91 ≈ 6 rad/s.
Two skaters (each 60 kg) hold hands and spin around their midpoint at 3 rad/s, 0.8 m apart. They pull together to 0.4 m apart. What is the new angular velocity?
Initially each is 0.4 m from center. I_i = 2 × 60 × (0.4)² = 2(60)(0.16) = 19.2 kg·m². After: each is 0.2 m from center. I_f = 2 × 60 × (0.2)² = 2(60)(0.04) = 4.8 kg·m². ω_f = I_iω_i/I_f = 19.2(3)/4.8 = 57.6/4.8 = 12 rad/s.
A disk (I = 0.5 kg·m²) spins at 12 rad/s clockwise. A second disk (I = 0.3 kg·m²) spins at 8 rad/s counterclockwise. They are coupled together. What is the final angular velocity and direction?
Taking clockwise as positive: L = 0.5(12) + 0.3(−8) = 6 − 2.4 = 3.6 kg·m²/s. I_total = 0.8 kg·m². ω_f = 3.6/0.8 = 4.5 rad/s clockwise.
