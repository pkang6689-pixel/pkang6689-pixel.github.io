--- Lesson 4.1: Work and Kinetic Energy (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Work and Kinetic Energy
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A 3 kg object moves at 4 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?
KE = ½mv² = ½(3)(4²) = ½(3)(16) = 24 J.
A force of 20 N is applied at 60° to the displacement over a distance of 4 m. What is the work done?
W = Fd cos θ = 20(4)cos 60° = 80(0.5) = 40 J.
A spring with k = 200 N/m is compressed 0.3 m from equilibrium. What is the work done by the spring force when it returns to equilibrium?
The work done by a spring returning to equilibrium is W = ½kx² = ½(200)(0.3²) = ½(200)(0.09) = 9 J.
A 2 kg object initially at rest has 50 J of net work done on it. What is its final speed?
By the Work-Energy Theorem, W_net = ΔKE = ½mv². So 50 = ½(2)v², giving v² = 50, and v ≈ 7.07 m/s.
A force F(x) = 6x N acts on a particle from x = 0 to x = 3 m. What is the total work done?
W = ∫₀³ 6x dx = 6(x²/2)|₀³ = 3(9) = 27 J.
The net work done on a 5 kg object changes its speed from 2 m/s to 6 m/s. What is the net work?
W_net = ½mv_f² - ½mv_i² = ½(5)(36) - ½(5)(4) = 90 - 10 = 80 J.
What is the definition of work in physics?
Work is defined as the integral of force dot displacement: W = ∫F·dr. For a constant force, this simplifies to W = Fd cos θ.
What is kinetic energy?
Kinetic energy is the energy of motion, given by KE = ½mv², where m is mass and v is speed.
What is meant by a 'variable force' in the context of work calculations?
A variable force is one whose magnitude or direction changes with position. Work by such a force must be computed using integration: W = ∫F·dr.
What is the joule (J) as a unit of work or energy?
One joule is defined as the work done when a force of 1 newton displaces an object by 1 meter in the direction of the force: 1 J = 1 N·m.
What is the definition of the spring constant k?
The spring constant k measures stiffness. It equals the force needed per unit displacement (k = F/x), with units of N/m.
What is a line integral in the context of work?
A line integral computes the work done by a force along a specified path, accounting for variations in force magnitude and direction: W = ∫F·dr.
A warehouse worker pushes a 40 kg crate across a rough floor with a constant horizontal force of 120 N over 8 m. The coefficient of kinetic friction between the crate and the floor is 0.25. Using g = 10 m/s² and assuming the crate starts from rest, what is the speed of the crate after 8 m?
Friction force = μmg = 0.25(40)(10) = 100 N. Net work = (120 - 100)(8) = 160 J. By the Work-Energy Theorem, 160 = ½(40)v², so v² = 8, and v = 2√5 ≈ 4.47 m/s.
A roller coaster car (mass 500 kg) is at the top of a 20 m hill moving at 5 m/s. Friction does -34,000 J of work as the car descends to the bottom. What is the car's speed at the bottom? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Using the Work-Energy Theorem: ½mv_f² = ½mv_i² + mgh + W_friction. ½(500)v_f² = ½(500)(25) + 500(10)(20) + (-34000) = 6250 + 100000 - 34000 = 72250. So v_f² = 72250/250 = 289, and v_f = 17 m/s.
A physics student attaches a force sensor to a cart on a track. The sensor records F(x) = 12 - 2x (in newtons) as the cart moves from x = 0 to x = 6 m. What is the total work done by this force?
W = ∫₀⁶ (12 - 2x) dx = [12x - x²]₀⁶ = (72 - 36) - 0 = 36 J.
A crane lifts a 200 kg steel beam at constant velocity to a height of 15 m while wind exerts a constant horizontal force of 300 N. The beam also moves 4 m horizontally during the lift. Using g = 10 m/s², what is the total work done by the crane's cable tension on the beam?
At constant velocity, the net work is zero. The cable must do work against gravity (mgh = 200(10)(15) = 30,000 J) and against wind (300 × 4 = 1200 J). Total work by cable = 30,000 + 1,200 = 31,200 J.
A delivery truck (mass 2000 kg) traveling at 20 m/s enters a section of road with a rough patch 25 m long (μ_k = 0.4) followed by a smooth section. After passing the rough patch, what is the truck's speed? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Friction force = μmg = 0.4(2000)(10) = 8000 N. Work by friction = -8000(25) = -200,000 J. ΔKE = -200,000. ½(2000)v_f² - ½(2000)(400) = -200,000. 1000v_f² = 400,000 - 200,000 = 200,000. v_f² = 200, v_f = √200 ≈ 14.1 m/s.
An archer pulls back a bow that behaves like a spring with k = 500 N/m. She draws the string 0.6 m. The 0.05 kg arrow is launched horizontally from a height of 1.8 m above the ground. What is the arrow's speed just as it leaves the bow, and approximately how far downrange does it land? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Spring PE = ½kx² = ½(500)(0.36) = 90 J. Setting equal to KE: 90 = ½(0.05)v², v² = 3600, v = 60 m/s. Fall time: 1.8 = ½(10)t², t = 0.6 s. Range = 60 × 0.6 = 36 m.
Real-World Applications
Example: A factory conveyor belt lifts 50 kg packages at a constant speed through a height of 3 m while also moving them 4 m horizontally. The belt exerts a constant 500 N force at an angle of 37° above horizontal. How much work does the belt do on each package? (cos 37° = 0.8, sin 37° = 0.6)
The total displacement along the belt = √(3² + 4²) = 5 m. Work = F × d × cos(0°) since the force is along the belt = 500 × 5 = 2500 J.

  1. Q: Define: Kinematics
     A: The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

  2. Q: Define: Force
     A: A push or pull that can change an object's velocity, measured in newtons

  3. Q: Define: Acceleration
     A: The rate of change of velocity with respect to time

  4. Q: Define: Momentum
     A: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its resistance to motion changes

  5. Q: Define: Work
     A: The transfer of energy that occurs when a force acts on an object over a distance

  6. Q: Define: Power
     A: The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts

  7. Q: Define: Torque
     A: The rotational equivalent of force; a measure of how much a force causes an object to rotate about an axis

  8. Q: Define: Angular Momentum
     A: The product of an object's moment of inertia and its angular velocity

  9. Q: Which of the following is the primary way water returns to the atmosphere?
     A: Evaporation from oceans and transpiration from plants

  10. Q: Which human activity most directly affects the water cycle?
     A: Urbanization reduces infiltration and increases runoff

--- Lesson 4.2: Conservative Forces and Potential Energy (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conservative Forces and Potential Energy
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A 2 kg object is lifted 5 m above the ground. What is its gravitational potential energy relative to the ground? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Gravitational PE = mgh = 2(10)(5) = 100 J.
A spring with k = 300 N/m is compressed 0.2 m. What is the elastic potential energy stored in the spring?
Elastic PE = ½kx² = ½(300)(0.04) = 6 J.
An object moves from point A to point B along two different paths under a conservative force. The work done along path 1 is 40 J. What is the work done along path 2?
For a conservative force, the work done depends only on the initial and final positions, not the path. Both paths give 40 J.
The potential energy function is U(x) = 4x² - 8x (joules, x in meters). What is the force at x = 3 m?
F = -dU/dx = -(8x - 8) = -8x + 8. At x = 3: F = -8(3) + 8 = -24 + 8 = -16 N.
A 0.5 kg ball is dropped from 10 m. What is its speed just before hitting the ground, ignoring air resistance? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Using energy conservation: mgh = ½mv², so v = √(2gh) = √(2(10)(10)) = √200 ≈ 14.1 m/s.
If U(x) = 5x³ - 2x (joules), what is the x-component of force at x = 1 m?
F_x = -dU/dx = -(15x² - 2). At x = 1: F_x = -(15 - 2) = -13 N.
A conservative force does +25 J of work on an object moving from A to B. What is the change in potential energy?
For a conservative force, W = -ΔU. So ΔU = -W = -25 J. The potential energy decreases by 25 J.
A particle moves in a complete closed loop. What is the net work done by gravity over the entire loop?
Gravity is a conservative force. The work done over any closed path is zero.
What is a conservative force?
A conservative force is one whose work depends only on the starting and ending positions, not the path. Equivalently, the work done around any closed loop is zero.
What is potential energy?
Potential energy is energy stored due to position or configuration within a system acted on by conservative forces, such as gravity or springs.
What is the relationship between force and potential energy in one dimension?
In one dimension, the force is the negative gradient of PE: F = -dU/dx. The force points in the direction of decreasing potential energy.
What is gravitational potential energy near Earth's surface?
Near Earth's surface, gravitational PE = mgh, the energy stored in the object-Earth system due to height h above the chosen reference level.
What is elastic potential energy?
Elastic PE is the energy stored in a deformed elastic object. For an ideal spring, U = ½kx².
What is a stable equilibrium point on a potential energy curve?
Stable equilibrium occurs at a local minimum of U(x). Any small displacement produces a restoring force (F = -dU/dx) directed back toward the equilibrium.
What is an unstable equilibrium?
Unstable equilibrium occurs at a local maximum of U(x). Though the force is zero, any small displacement creates a force pushing the object further from equilibrium.
What is a turning point on a potential energy diagram?
A turning point occurs where E = U(x), meaning KE = 0. The particle momentarily stops and reverses direction.
A skier (mass 70 kg) starts from rest at the top of a frictionless slope 25 m above the base. At the bottom, the slope curves upward to a second hill 15 m high. What is the skier's speed at the top of the second hill? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Conservation of energy: mgh₁ = ½mv² + mgh₂. v = √(2g(h₁ - h₂)) = √(2(10)(10)) = √200 ≈ 14.1 m/s.
A laboratory cart (2 kg) on a frictionless track is attached to a spring (k = 250 N/m). The cart is pulled 0.4 m from equilibrium and released. What is its maximum speed as it passes through equilibrium?
At max displacement, all energy is spring PE: ½kx² = ½(250)(0.16) = 20 J. At equilibrium, all energy is KE: 20 = ½(2)v², v² = 20, v = 2√5 ≈ 4.47 m/s.
A child swings on a 3 m rope starting from 60° from vertical. What is the child's speed at the lowest point? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Height above lowest point: h = L - Lcos60° = 3 - 1.5 = 1.5 m. Energy conservation: v = √(2gh) = √(2(10)(1.5)) = √30 ≈ 5.48 m/s.
A hydroelectric dam releases water from 80 m above the turbines. If 5000 kg of water flows per second and the system is 60% efficient, what is the electrical power output? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Gravitational power = mgh/t = 5000(10)(80) = 4,000,000 W. At 60% efficiency: P = 0.6 × 4,000,000 = 2,400,000 W = 2.4 MW.

--- Lesson 4.3: Conservation of Energy (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conservation of Energy
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A 4 kg ball is thrown upward at 10 m/s. What is its maximum height above the release point? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
At max height, KE = 0. ½mv² = mgh → h = v²/(2g) = 100/20 = 5 m.
A 0.5 kg block slides from rest down a frictionless ramp from a height of 8 m. What is its speed at the bottom? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
mgh = ½mv². v = √(2gh) = √(2(10)(8)) = √160 ≈ 12.6 m/s.
A system has initial KE = 20 J and PE = 50 J. If only conservative forces act, what is the total mechanical energy at any later time?
Total mechanical energy E = KE + PE = 20 + 50 = 70 J. With only conservative forces, E is conserved.
A pendulum swings from a height of 0.5 m above its lowest point. What is its speed at the lowest point? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
mgh = ½mv². v = √(2gh) = √(2(10)(0.5)) = √10 ≈ 3.16 m/s.
A spring-mass system oscillates with total energy 12 J. At a point where PE = 8 J, what is the KE?
E = KE + PE. KE = E - PE = 12 - 8 = 4 J.
A 3 kg mass on a spring (k = 1200 N/m) is at maximum compression of 0.1 m. What is the maximum speed of the mass?
½kx² = ½mv². v = x√(k/m) = 0.1√(1200/3) = 0.1(20) = 2 m/s.
Friction does -20 J of work on a system with initial mechanical energy of 80 J. What is the final mechanical energy?
With non-conservative forces: E_f = E_i + W_nc = 80 + (-20) = 60 J.
A 2 kg object is at height 10 m with speed 5 m/s. What is its total mechanical energy? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
E = KE + PE = ½mv² + mgh = ½(2)(25) + 2(10)(10) = 25 + 200 = 225 J.
What is the law of conservation of energy?
The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted between forms. The total energy of an isolated system remains constant.
What is mechanical energy?
Mechanical energy is the sum of kinetic energy and potential energy: E = KE + PE.
What is thermal energy in the context of energy conservation?
Thermal energy (or internal energy) is the energy of random molecular motion. Friction converts mechanical energy into thermal energy.
What is the meaning of W_nc = ΔE in energy conservation?
W_nc = ΔE means the work done by non-conservative forces (like friction) equals the change in the system's total mechanical energy.
What is an isolated system in energy terms?
An isolated system has no energy exchange with its surroundings. Its total energy (all forms combined) remains constant.
What is energy dissipation?
Energy dissipation is the irreversible conversion of mechanical energy into thermal energy or other non-recoverable forms, typically due to friction or drag.
What is the definition of an energy bar chart?
An energy bar chart visually represents how energy is distributed among kinetic, potential, thermal, and other forms at key instants in a process.
What is the general energy conservation equation with non-conservative forces?
The generalized energy equation is KE_i + PE_i + W_nc = KE_f + PE_f. When W_nc = 0 (no friction), this reduces to the simple conservation of mechanical energy.
A roller coaster car (mass 600 kg) starts from rest at the top of a 40 m hill and descends to a valley before climbing a second 25 m hill. If friction does -18,000 J of work total, what is the car's speed at the top of the second hill? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
E_i + W_nc = E_f. mgh₁ + W_nc = ½mv² + mgh₂. 600(10)(40) - 18000 = 300v² + 600(10)(25). 240000 - 18000 = 300v² + 150000. 72000 = 300v². v² = 240. v = √240 ≈ 15.5 m/s.
A package (5 kg) slides from rest down a frictionless ramp of height 4 m, then across a 10 m rough horizontal surface (μ_k = 0.1). What is its speed at the end of the rough surface? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
½mv² = mgh - μmgd. Cancel m: ½v² = g(h - μd) = 10(4 - 0.1×10) = 10(3) = 30. v² = 60. v = √60 ≈ 7.75 m/s.
A 50 kg gymnast drops from a 3 m high bar onto a mat that compresses 0.2 m while stopping the gymnast. What is the average force exerted by the mat on the gymnast? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
The gymnast falls (h + d) = 3.2 m with gravity doing work, stopped over d = 0.2 m by the mat. Energy: mg(h + d) = F_mat × d. F_mat = mg(h+d)/d = 50(10)(3.2)/0.2 = 1600/0.2 = 8,000 N.
A wind turbine blade is hit by air at 12 m/s. If 200 kg of air passes per second and the turbine extracts 40% of the air's kinetic energy, what is the power output?
KE per second = ½(200)(144) = 14,400 W. At 40% efficiency: P = 0.4(14,400) = 5,760 W.
A 2 kg block is pushed along a rough surface (μ_k = 0.4) by a horizontal force of 20 N for 3 m, starting from rest. What is the block's final speed? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
W_applied = 20(3) = 60 J. W_friction = -μmg(d) = -0.4(2)(10)(3) = -24 J. W_net = 60 - 24 = 36 J = ½mv². v² = 36/1 = 36. v = 6 m/s.
A bungee jumper (mass 60 kg) leaps from a bridge. The bungee cord has a natural length of 20 m and spring constant k = 120 N/m. What is the maximum distance the jumper falls below the bridge? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Let d = total drop. Cord stretches (d - 20) m. Energy conservation: mgd = ½k(d - 20)². 60(10)d = ½(120)(d-20)². 600d = 60(d-20)². 10d = (d-20)². d² - 40d + 400 = 10d. d² - 50d + 400 = 0. d = (50 ± √(2500-1600))/2 = (50 ± 30)/2. d = 40 or 10. Since d must exceed 20 m (natural length), d = 40 m.

--- Lesson 4.4: Power and Efficiency (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Power and Efficiency
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
A motor does 600 J of work in 3 seconds. What is the average power output?
P = W/t = 600/3 = 200 W.
A force of 50 N moves an object at a constant velocity of 4 m/s in the direction of the force. What is the instantaneous power?
P = F · v = 50 × 4 = 200 W.
A machine takes in 1000 J of energy and produces 600 J of useful work. What is its efficiency?
Efficiency = useful work output / total energy input = 600/1000 = 0.60 = 60%.
A pump lifts 20 kg of water to a height of 5 m in 4 seconds. What is its power output? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Work = mgh = 20(10)(5) = 1000 J. P = W/t = 1000/4 = 250 W.
A car engine produces 150 kW while the car travels at 30 m/s. What is the total driving force?
P = Fv, so F = P/v = 150,000/30 = 5000 N.
A 60 kg person climbs 5 m of stairs in 10 s. What is their average power output? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
P = mgh/t = 60(10)(5)/10 = 3000/10 = 300 W.
A 2 kW motor is 80% efficient. What is its useful power output?
Useful output = efficiency × input = 0.80 × 2 = 1.6 kW.
A force F = 10t N (where t is in seconds) acts on an object moving at constant velocity 3 m/s. What is the instantaneous power at t = 4 s?
At t = 4 s, F = 10(4) = 40 N. P = Fv = 40 × 3 = 120 W.
What is power in physics?
Power is the rate of doing work or transferring energy. P = dW/dt. The SI unit is the watt (W).
What is a watt (W)?
The watt is the SI unit of power. 1 W = 1 J/s.
What is average power?
Average power is the total work done divided by the total time taken: P_avg = W/Δt.
What is instantaneous power?
Instantaneous power is the rate of doing work at a specific moment: P = dW/dt = F · v.
What is efficiency?
Efficiency = useful energy (or power) output / total energy (or power) input. It is always between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%).
What is a horsepower (hp)?
One horsepower (hp) equals 746 watts. It is a traditional unit of power used especially for engines and motors.
What is the kilowatt-hour (kWh)?
A kilowatt-hour is a unit of energy: 1 kWh = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3.6 × 10⁶ J. It measures total energy, not power.
What is energy loss in a system?
Energy loss is the energy input minus useful output. This energy is not destroyed but converted to less useful forms like heat and sound.
A crane lifts a 500 kg steel beam at 2 m/s to the top of a building 30 m high. The motor is 75% efficient. What is the electrical power input required? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Useful power = Fv = mg × v = 500(10)(2) = 10,000 W. Input power = useful/efficiency = 10,000/0.75 = 13,333 W.
An escalator carries 30 passengers (average mass 70 kg each) through a vertical rise of 6 m in 40 seconds. What is the minimum power required? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Total mass = 30 × 70 = 2100 kg. Work = mgh = 2100(10)(6) = 126,000 J. P = W/t = 126,000/40 = 3,150 W.
An electric car's motor produces 80 kW. The car travels at 40 m/s on level ground against a drag force of 1500 N and rolling friction of 500 N. What is the net power available for acceleration?
Power used against drag and friction = (1500 + 500) × 40 = 2000 × 40 = 80,000 W = 80 kW. This equals the motor power, so no power remains for acceleration.
A hydroelectric plant converts the PE of water falling 50 m. If 2000 kg of water flows per second and the turbine is 85% efficient, what is the electrical power output? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
Gravitational power = mgh/t = 2000(10)(50) = 1,000,000 W. At 85% efficiency: 0.85 × 1,000,000 = 850,000 W = 850 kW.
A wind turbine has blades sweeping an area of 100 m². Wind speed is 10 m/s and air density is 1.2 kg/m³. If the turbine captures 30% of the wind's kinetic energy, what is the power output?
Mass flow rate = ρAv = 1.2(100)(10) = 1200 kg/s. Wind power = ½(dm/dt)v² = ½(1200)(100) = 60,000 W. At 30% capture: P = 0.3 × 60,000 = 18,000 W.
Real-World Applications
Example: A motor does 600 J of work in 3 seconds. What is the average power output?
P = W/t = 600/3 = 200 W.
Example: A 60 kg person climbs 5 m of stairs in 10 s. What is their average power output? (Use g = 10 m/s²)
P = mgh/t = 60(10)(5)/10 = 3000/10 = 300 W.

Useful power = 0.90 × 5000 = 4500 W. F = P/v = 4500/2 = 2250 N.
