--- Lesson 7.1: Simple Harmonic Motion (Flashcards) ---

  1. Q: Define: Kinematics
     A: The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

  2. Q: Define: Force
     A: A push or pull that can change an object's velocity, measured in newtons

  3. Q: Define: Acceleration
     A: The rate of change of velocity with respect to time

  4. Q: Define: Momentum
     A: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its resistance to motion changes

  5. Q: Define: Work
     A: The transfer of energy that occurs when a force acts on an object over a distance

  6. Q: Define: Power
     A: The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts

  7. Q: Define: Torque
     A: The rotational equivalent of force; a measure of how much a force causes an object to rotate about an axis

  8. Q: Define: Angular Momentum
     A: The product of an object's moment of inertia and its angular velocity

  9. Q: Which of the following is the primary way water returns to the atmosphere?
     A: Evaporation from oceans and transpiration from plants

  10. Q: Which human activity most directly affects the water cycle?
     A: Urbanization reduces infiltration and increases runoff

  Key Concepts: Pendulums
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the definition of 'Simple pendulum period'?
"Simple pendulum period" means: T = 2π√(L/g); depends only on length and gravitational acceleration for small-angle oscillations (θ < ~15°). The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Small-angle approximation'?
"Small-angle approximation" means: sinθ ≈ θ (in radians) for small angles; this linearizes the restoring torque and produces SHM. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Physical pendulum'?
"Physical pendulum" means: T = 2π√(I/(mgd)); a real extended body oscillating about a pivot, where I is the moment of inertia about the pivot. The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Restoring torque'?
"Restoring torque" means: τ = -mgd sinθ for a pendulum; for small θ, τ ≈ -mgdθ, giving angular SHM with ω = √(mgd/I). The other options describe different concepts.
What is the definition of 'Energy in pendulum'?
"Energy in pendulum" means: E = KE + PE = ½mv² + mgh; maximum KE at bottom, maximum PE at maximum angle. The other options describe different concepts.

  1. Q: Define: Kinematics
     A: The branch of mechanics that describes the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

  2. Q: Define: Force
     A: A push or pull that can change an object's velocity, measured in newtons

  3. Q: Define: Acceleration
     A: The rate of change of velocity with respect to time

  4. Q: Define: Momentum
     A: The product of an object's mass and velocity, representing its resistance to motion changes

  5. Q: Define: Work
     A: The transfer of energy that occurs when a force acts on an object over a distance

  6. Q: Define: Power
     A: The rate at which work is done or energy is transferred, measured in watts

  7. Q: Define: Torque
     A: The rotational equivalent of force; a measure of how much a force causes an object to rotate about an axis

  8. Q: Define: Angular Momentum
     A: The product of an object's moment of inertia and its angular velocity

  9. Q: Which of the following is the primary way water returns to the atmosphere?
     A: Evaporation from oceans and transpiration from plants

  10. Q: Which human activity most directly affects the water cycle?
     A: Urbanization reduces infiltration and increases runoff
