  1. Q: Define: Geographic Scale
     A: The level of organizational complexity at which geographic phenomena are examined, from local to global

  2. Q: Define: Absolute Location
     A: The exact position of a place on Earth using coordinates such as latitude and longitude

  3. Q: Define: Relative Location
     A: The position of a place in relation to other places and the accessibility between them

  4. Q: Define: Place
     A: A location imbued with cultural, social, historical, and personal significance and meaning

  5. Q: Define: Spatial Perspective
     A: An approach in geography emphasizing how places connect through patterns of interaction and flows

  6. Q: Define: Cultural Diffusion
     A: The process by which cultural traits, ideas, practices, or innovations spread from one place to another

  7. Q: Define: Globalization
     A: The process by which the world becomes increasingly interconnected through technology, trade, and communication

  8. Q: Define: Cartography
     A: The art and science of creating maps and representing geographic information on two-dimensional surfaces

  9. Q: Which factor would most directly cause a population to increase?
     A: High birth rate and low death rate

  10. Q: Carrying capacity refers to:
     A: The maximum population size an environment can sustain indefinitely

  1. Q: Define: Geographic Scale
     A: The level of organizational complexity at which geographic phenomena are examined, from local to global

  2. Q: Define: Absolute Location
     A: The exact position of a place on Earth using coordinates such as latitude and longitude

  3. Q: Define: Relative Location
     A: The position of a place in relation to other places and the accessibility between them

  4. Q: Define: Place
     A: A location imbued with cultural, social, historical, and personal significance and meaning

  5. Q: Define: Spatial Perspective
     A: An approach in geography emphasizing how places connect through patterns of interaction and flows

  6. Q: Define: Cultural Diffusion
     A: The process by which cultural traits, ideas, practices, or innovations spread from one place to another

  7. Q: Define: Globalization
     A: The process by which the world becomes increasingly interconnected through technology, trade, and communication

  8. Q: Define: Cartography
     A: The art and science of creating maps and representing geographic information on two-dimensional surfaces

  9. Q: Which factor would most directly cause a population to increase?
     A: High birth rate and low death rate

  10. Q: Carrying capacity refers to:
     A: The maximum population size an environment can sustain indefinitely
