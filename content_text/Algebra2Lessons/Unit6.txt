--- Lesson 6.1: Arithmetic Sequences (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arithmetic Sequences
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common difference of the sequence −3, −1, 1, 3, ...?
Subtract consecutive terms: −1 − (−3) = 2. The common difference is d = 2.
What is the 20th term of the sequence 3, 8, 13, 18, ...?
d = 5, a₂₀ = 3 + 19(5) = 3 + 95 = 98.
What is an arithmetic sequence?
An arithmetic sequence is defined by having a constant difference d between consecutive terms, so each term equals the previous term plus d.
What is the nth-term formula for an arithmetic sequence?
The explicit formula for the nth term of an arithmetic sequence is aₙ = a₁ + (n−1)d, which is linear in n.
What is the formula for the sum of the first n terms of an arithmetic series?
The sum of an arithmetic series is Sₙ = n(a₁ + aₙ)/2, which is the number of terms times the average of the first and last terms.
What is a 'finite arithmetic series'?
A finite arithmetic series is the sum of a specific (finite) number of terms from an arithmetic sequence, computed using Sₙ = n(a₁ + aₙ)/2.
What is the meaning of 'partial sum' in the context of arithmetic series?
A partial sum Sₙ is the sum of a finite number (n) of terms from the beginning of a series, rather than the sum of all infinitely many terms.
An auditorium has 30 rows. The first row has 12 seats and each row adds 3 more seats. What is the total seating capacity?
a₁ = 12, d = 3, n = 30. a₃₀ = 12 + 29(3) = 99. S₃₀ = 30(12 + 99)/2 = 30(111)/2 = 1,665.
A staircase has steps where each step is 7 inches tall. If the first step starts at 7 inches and the staircase has 15 steps, what is the height of the top of the 15th step from the ground?
Each step adds 7 inches (d = 0, constant height per step). The height after 15 steps is simply 15 × 7 = 105 inches. This is an arithmetic sequence with a₁ = 7 and d = 0 for the step heights, so the cumulative height is 15(7) = 105.
A taxi charges a flat fee of $3 plus $2 for each mile. If a passenger travels 1 mile the cost is $5, 2 miles costs $7, and so on. What is the cost for a 12-mile trip?
The cost forms an arithmetic sequence with a₁ = 5 (1 mile) and d = 2. For 12 miles: a₁₂ = 5 + 11(2) = 5 + 22 = 27. Alternatively, cost = 3 + 2(12) = $27.

--- Lesson 6.2: Geometric Sequences (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Geometric Sequences
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common ratio of the sequence 4, 12, 36, 108, ...?
The common ratio r = 12/4 = 3. Each term is multiplied by 3 to get the next.
What is the geometric mean of 4 and 16?
The geometric mean = √(4 · 16) = √64 = 8.
In the sequence 100, 50, 25, 12.5, ..., what is r?
r = 50/100 = 1/2. Each term is half the previous term.
What is a geometric sequence?
A geometric sequence has a constant ratio r between consecutive terms, so aₙ₊₁ = r · aₙ.
What is the nth-term formula for a geometric sequence?
The explicit formula for the nth term of a geometric sequence is aₙ = a₁ · rⁿ⁻¹, which grows exponentially.
What is the formula for the sum of the first n terms of a geometric series when r ≠ 1?
The finite geometric series formula is Sₙ = a₁(1 − rⁿ)/(1 − r), valid when r ≠ 1.
What is the meaning of 'alternating sequence' in geometric sequences?
When the common ratio r is negative, multiplying by r flips the sign each time, causing the terms to alternate between positive and negative.
A car depreciates to 80% of its value each year. If the car is worth $25,000 new, what is it worth after 3 years?
a₁ = 25,000 and r = 0.80. After 3 years: 25,000 · (0.80)³ = 25,000 · 0.512 = $12,800.
An investment of $1,000 grows at 10% per year (compounding annually). What is the value after 4 years?
This is geometric with r = 1.10. Value = 1,000 · (1.10)⁴ = 1,000 · 1.4641 = $1,464.10.
A medication's concentration in the bloodstream halves every 4 hours. If a dose produces a concentration of 200 mg/L, what is the concentration after 12 hours?
In 12 hours there are 12/4 = 3 half-lives. Concentration = 200 · (1/2)³ = 200/8 = 25 mg/L.
Real-World Applications
Example: A pond's algae coverage doubles every week. If it covers 2 square meters initially, how many square meters does it cover after 8 weeks?
Coverage = 2 · 2⁸ = 2 · 256 = 512 square meters.

--- Lesson 6.4: Infinite Geometric Series (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Infinite Geometric Series
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 0.999... as a fraction?
0.999... = 9/10 + 9/100 + ... = (9/10)/(1 − 1/10) = (9/10)/(9/10) = 1 exactly.
What is the condition for convergence of an infinite geometric series?
An infinite geometric series converges if and only if the absolute value of the common ratio is less than 1: |r| < 1.
What is the formula for the sum of an infinite geometric series when |r| < 1?
When |r| < 1, the infinite geometric series sums to S = a₁/(1 − r). The finite formula Sₙ = a₁(1 − rⁿ)/(1 − r) approaches this as n → ∞ since rⁿ → 0.
What is the meaning of 'partial sum' in the context of an infinite series?
A partial sum Sₙ is the sum of only the first n terms. As n increases, partial sums approximate the infinite sum more closely.
What is the meaning of 'infinite series'?
An infinite series has infinitely many terms. Its value is defined as the limit of its partial sums, if that limit exists.
A bouncing ball is dropped from 10 meters and reaches 60% of its previous height on each bounce. What is the total distance the ball travels (up and down combined)?
Down = 10, Up+Down bounces = 2 · Σ (10 · 0.6ⁿ) for n=1 to ∞ = 2 · 10(0.6)/(1−0.6) = 2 · 15 = 30. Total = 10 + 30 = 40 m.
A pendulum swings 20 cm on its first swing and each subsequent swing is 90% of the previous one. What is the total distance the pendulum swings before stopping?
S = a₁/(1 − r) = 20/(1 − 0.9) = 20/0.1 = 200 cm.
A government stimulus gives $1,000 to each citizen. Each person spends 80% of what they receive, and those recipients also spend 80%, and so on. What is the total economic impact (spending) of the stimulus?
First spending = $800, then $640, $512, ... This is a geometric series with a₁ = 800, r = 0.8. Total spending = 800/(1 − 0.8) = 800/0.2 = $4,000.
A drug has a half-life such that 50% remains after each dose interval. If 100 mg is taken repeatedly at each interval, what is the long-run steady-state amount in the body?
At steady state, the amount right after a dose is S = 100/(1 − 0.5) = 100/0.5 = 200 mg.
A fractal pattern starts with a triangle of area 1. At each step, the total shaded area is multiplied by 3/4. What is the total shaded area after infinitely many steps?
S = 1/(1 − 3/4) = 1/(1/4) = 4.
A forest loses 10% of its deer population each year due to migration. If 200 new deer are born each year, what is the long-run stable population?
At equilibrium, births = losses: 200 = 0.10 · P → P = 2,000. Equivalently, the sum 200 + 200(0.9) + 200(0.9)² + ... = 200/(1 − 0.9) = 2,000.
An oil spill spreads in concentric rings. The first ring has area 100 m² and each subsequent ring has area equal to 1/2 the previous ring. What is the total area the spill eventually covers?
S = 100/(1 − 1/2) = 100/0.5 = 200 m².
A machine operates at full power, then at 70% power, then at 49% power, and so on. If full power produces 500 units, what is the total output over its entire lifetime?
r = 0.7. S = 500/(1 − 0.7) = 500/0.3 ≈ 1,666.67 ≈ 1,667 units.
A company donates $5,000. Each recipient organization passes 40% of what they receive to another organization. What is the total amount all organizations receive combined?
Total = 5000 + 5000(0.4) + 5000(0.4)² + ... = 5000/(1 − 0.4) = 5000/0.6 ≈ $8,333.
Real-World Applications
Example: A fractal pattern starts with a triangle of area 1. At each step, the total shaded area is multiplied by 3/4. What is the total shaded area after infinitely many steps?
S = 1/(1 − 3/4) = 1/(1/4) = 4.

--- Lesson 6.5: Applications & Mathematical Induction (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications & Mathematical Induction
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In mathematical induction, what is the base case for proving 1 + 2 + ... + n = n(n+1)/2 when n = 1?
For n = 1: LHS = 1, RHS = 1(2)/2 = 1. Since LHS = RHS, the base case holds.
$1,000 is invested at 5% annual compound interest. What is the balance after 3 years?
A = 1000(1.05)³ = 1000 · 1.157625 = $1,157.63 (rounded).
In a proof by induction that 2ⁿ > n for all n ≥ 1, what is the base case?
Since we are proving for all n ≥ 1, the base case is n = 1: 2¹ = 2 > 1, which is true.
What is the 3rd term in the expansion of (x + 2)⁴?
The 3rd term (k=2): C(4,2) · x² · 2² = 6 · x² · 4 = 24x².
What is mathematical induction?
Mathematical induction proves a statement P(n) holds for all positive integers by (1) verifying P(1) and (2) showing P(k) → P(k+1).
What is the 'inductive hypothesis'?
The inductive hypothesis is the assumption that the statement holds for some arbitrary positive integer k, which is then used to prove it for k + 1.
What is the 'base case' in mathematical induction?
The base case checks that the proposition is true at the starting point (usually n = 1), which anchors the chain of implications.
What is a 'recursive formula'?
A recursive formula defines aₙ in terms of previous terms (like aₙ₋₁ or aₙ₋₂), requiring initial conditions to compute specific terms.
What is an 'explicit formula'?
An explicit formula lets you compute any term directly from its position n, without needing prior terms. Example: aₙ = 2n + 1.
What is the Binomial Theorem?
The Binomial Theorem expands (a + b)ⁿ into a sum of n+1 terms using binomial coefficients C(n, k).
What is an 'annuity' in financial mathematics?
An annuity is a series of equal payments at regular time intervals, such as monthly mortgage payments or retirement fund contributions.
What is Pascal's Triangle?
Pascal's Triangle is a triangular array of numbers where each entry equals the sum of the two entries directly above it. The entries are binomial coefficients C(n, k).
