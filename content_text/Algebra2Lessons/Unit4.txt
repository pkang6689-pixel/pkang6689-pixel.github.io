--- Lesson 4.1: Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the domain of 1/(x^2 - 1)?
x^2 - 1 = (x - 1)(x + 1) = 0 when x = 1 or x = -1. These must be excluded from the domain.
What is a 'rational expression'?
A rational expression is a fraction where both the numerator and denominator are polynomials, such as (x + 1)/(x^2 - 4).
What is the 'domain' of a rational expression?
The domain includes every real number except the excluded values where the denominator equals zero.
What is a 'common factor' in the context of rational expressions?
A common factor is a polynomial factor shared by both the numerator and denominator that can be canceled during simplification.
What is the 'numerator' of a rational expression?
The numerator is the top part of the fraction. In (x + 1)/(x - 2), the numerator is x + 1.
What is the 'denominator' of a rational expression?
The denominator is the bottom part of the fraction. It determines the excluded values and must never equal zero.
A car travels (x^2 - 9) miles in (x - 3) hours. What is the simplified speed?
Speed = distance/time = (x^2 - 9)/(x - 3) = (x-3)(x+3)/(x-3) = x + 3 mph, for x != 3.
Real-World Applications
Example: A printer can print x^2 pages and uses (x^2 - 4)/(x - 2) ink cartridges. Simplify the cartridge expression.
Factor: (x - 2)(x + 2)/(x - 2) = x + 2, valid for x != 2.

  1. Q: Simplify: (x² - 4)/(x + 2)
     A: (x - 2). Factor numerator: (x-2)(x+2). Cancel (x+2).

  2. Q: Restriction on (x² + 1)/(x² - 9)?
     A: x ≠ ±3. Denominator cannot equal zero.

  3. Q: Simplify: (2x² + 6x)/(4x)
     A: (x + 3)/2. Factor: 2x(x+3)/(4x) = (x+3)/2.

  4. Q: Equivalent to (x-1)/(x+2)?
     A: Yes: (2(x-1))/(2(x+2)) = (2x-2)/(2x+4). Multiply numerator and denominator by same.

  5. Q: Domain of (x+5)/(x(x-3))?
     A: All x except 0 and 3. Set each factor in denominator ≠ 0.

  6. Q: Reduce (2x²-8)/(x²-4)
     A: 2. 2(x²-4)/(x²-4) = 2 (for x ≠ ±2).

  7. Q: Factor 3x² - 12 in numerator for (3x²-12)/(x-2)
     A: 3(x-2)(x+2)/(x-2) = 3(x+2), x ≠ 2.

  8. Q: Simplify (x³+8)/(x+2)
     A: x² - 2x + 4. Factor sum of cubes: (x+2)(x²-2x+4)/(x+2).

  9. Q: Restrictions on ((x+1)(x-3))/((x+1)(x+5))?
     A: x ≠ -1, -5. Both values make denominator zero.

  10. Q: Simplify (4x⁴)/(8x²)
     A: x²/2. Divide: 4/8 = 1/2, x⁴/x² = x².

--- Lesson 4.2: Operations on Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Operations on Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the LCD of 1/(x-1) and 1/(x+1)?
The LCD is the product of the distinct linear factors: (x - 1)(x + 1).
What is a 'complex fraction'?
A complex fraction (or compound fraction) contains one or more fractions within the numerator or denominator, such as (1/x)/(1/y).
What is the 'product rule' for rational expressions?
To multiply rational expressions, multiply the numerators to get the new numerator and the denominators to get the new denominator, then simplify.
What is the 'quotient rule' for rational expressions?
To divide rational expressions, multiply the first expression by the reciprocal of the second.
What is an 'equivalent fraction'?
Equivalent fractions represent the same value. Multiplying or dividing both the numerator and denominator by the same nonzero quantity produces an equivalent fraction.
A recipe requires mixing (3/x) liters of solution A with (2/x) liters of solution B. What is the total volume?
Add: 3/x + 2/x = 5/x liters.
A contractor's cost per unit is (x^2-4)/(x+2) dollars and he builds (x+2) units. What is the total cost?
Total = ((x^2-4)/(x+2)) * (x+2) = x^2 - 4 = (x-2)(x+2) dollars. Both A and B are correct, but x^2 - 4 is the simplified product.
A tank fills at (4/x) gallons per minute and drains at (1/x) gallons per minute. What is the net fill rate?
Net rate = 4/x - 1/x = 3/x gallons per minute.
An investor earns 1000/x dollars per share from x shares, and later buys x more shares earning 1000/(2x) per share. What is the total per-share if combined?
Total earnings = 1000/x * x + 1000/(2x) * x = 1000 + 500 = 1500. With 2x shares, per-share earnings require more context, but total = 1500/x for original x shares' rate.
A runner's pace is (x+1)/x minutes per lap. If she runs x laps, what is her total time?
Total time = ((x+1)/x) * x = x + 1 minutes.
A pipe fills a pool at rate 1/(x-1) pools per hour and a second pipe at 1/(x+1) pools per hour. What is their combined rate?
LCD = (x-1)(x+1): (x+1+x-1)/((x-1)(x+1)) = 2x/(x^2-1).
A student scores x/(x+5) on test 1 and (x+5)/(x) on test 2. What is the product of these two scores?
Product = (x/(x+5)) * ((x+5)/x) = 1, for x != 0 and x != -5.
A chemist mixes (6/(x+3)) mL of acid with (4/(x+3)) mL of base. What is the total volume?
Same denominator: (6 + 4)/(x + 3) = 10/(x + 3) mL.
A shipping company's cost is (x^2 - 1)/(x + 1) dollars per package, and it ships (x + 1) packages. What is the total shipping cost?
Total = ((x^2-1)/(x+1)) * (x+1) = x^2 - 1 dollars.
Real-World Applications
Example: Multiply: (x/3) * (9/x^2).
Multiply numerators and denominators: 9x/(3x^2) = 3/x, for x != 0.
Example: Multiply: ((x+2)/5) * (10/(x+2)).
Cancel (x+2): 10/5 = 2, for x != -2.
Example: Multiply: ((x-3)/(x+1)) * ((x+1)/(x-3)).
The factors cancel: (x-3)(x+1)/((x+1)(x-3)) = 1, for x != 3 and x != -1.
Example: What does 'cross-multiply' mean?
Cross-multiplication is a technique used when setting two fractions equal: if a/b = c/d, then ad = bc.
Example: A student scores x/(x+5) on test 1 and (x+5)/(x) on test 2. What is the product of these two scores?
Product = (x/(x+5)) * ((x+5)/x) = 1, for x != 0 and x != -5.
Example: A shipping company's cost is (x^2 - 1)/(x + 1) dollars per package, and it ships (x + 1) packages. What is the total shipping cost?
Total = ((x^2-1)/(x+1)) * (x+1) = x^2 - 1 dollars.

What is the slant asymptote of f(x) = (x^2 + 2x + 1)/(x + 3)?

An asymptote is a line that the graph of a function gets closer and closer to as the input or output increases without bound.
What is a 'discontinuity' in a function?
A discontinuity is any point in the domain where the function is not continuous, meaning the graph has a gap, hole, or asymptotic behavior.

A removable discontinuity occurs where a common factor cancels. The limit exists, but the function is undefined at that point.

What is an 'essential discontinuity'?
An essential discontinuity (or oscillating discontinuity) occurs when the function does not approach any finite or infinite limit, though this is rare in rational functions.
What is a 'jump discontinuity'?
A jump discontinuity occurs when a function jumps from one value to another. While rare in pure rational functions, it appears in piecewise-defined functions.

--- Lesson 4.6: Applications of Rational Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications of Rational Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If a car travels 200 miles in t hours, what is its average speed?
Average speed = distance/time = 200/t miles per hour.
Machine A produces 100 widgets in x hours. What is its rate?
Rate = work/time = 100/x widgets per hour.
If Worker A's rate is 1/4 job/hour and Worker B's is 1/6 job/hour, what is their combined rate?
Combined rate = 1/4 + 1/6 = 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12 job/hour.
The average cost of producing x items is C(x) = (500 + 2x)/x. What is C(100)?
C(100) = (500 + 200)/100 = 700/100 = 7 dollars.
A round trip of 100 miles each way: going at 50 mph, returning at 25 mph. What is the average speed?
Total distance = 200 miles. Total time = 100/50 + 100/25 = 2 + 4 = 6 hours. Average speed = 200/6 = 33.33 mph.
If total cost is C(x) = 1000 + 5x and average cost is C(x)/x, what is the average cost for 200 items?
Average cost = (1000 + 5(200))/200 = (1000 + 1000)/200 = 2000/200 = $10.
What is a 'work-rate problem'?
Work-rate problems use the formula rate times time = work to find how fast agents complete tasks individually or together.
What is 'average speed'?
Average speed is the total distance traveled divided by the total time taken, which is different from the arithmetic mean of speeds.
What is a 'mixture problem'?
Mixture problems involve combining quantities with different concentrations, prices, or qualities and finding the resulting mixture's properties.
What is an 'inverse variation' or 'inverse proportion'?
In inverse variation, y = k/x for some constant k. As x increases, y decreases proportionally.
What is a 'distance-rate-time problem'?
These problems apply d = rt to find unknown distances, speeds, or travel times, often involving rational equations.
What is 'concentration' in mixture problems?
Concentration measures how much of a particular substance is present relative to the total mixture, usually expressed as a percentage or fraction.
What is the 'harmonic mean' and when is it used?
The harmonic mean is 2ab/(a+b) for two values. It gives the correct average speed when equal distances are traveled at different speeds.
A delivery truck travels 150 miles to a warehouse at 50 mph and returns at 30 mph. What is the average speed for the round trip?
Total distance = 300. Total time = 150/50 + 150/30 = 3 + 5 = 8 hours. Average speed = 300/8 = 37.5 mph.
A kayaker paddles 12 miles upstream in a river with 2 mph current, then 12 miles back downstream. Her still-water speed is 6 mph. What is the total time?
Upstream speed: 6 - 2 = 4 mph, time = 12/4 = 3 hours. Downstream speed: 6 + 2 = 8 mph, time = 12/8 = 1.5 hours. Total = 3 + 1.5 = 4.5 hours.
A plane flies 600 miles with a 50 mph tailwind in the same time it takes to fly 400 miles against the wind. What is the plane's speed in still air?
Let s = still air speed. 600/(s+50) = 400/(s-50). Cross-multiply: 600(s-50) = 400(s+50). 600s - 30000 = 400s + 20000. 200s = 50000. s = 250 mph.
Real-World Applications
Example: If a car travels 200 miles in t hours, what is its average speed?
Average speed = distance/time = 200/t miles per hour.

C(100) = (500 + 200)/100 = 700/100 = 7 dollars.
