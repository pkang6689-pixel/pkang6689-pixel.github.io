--- Lesson 5.1: Exponential Functions & Growth (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential Functions & Growth
0, b ≠ 1. Base b determines growth (b > 1) or decay (0 Exponential Functions: Form f(x) = ab^x where a ≠ 0, b > 0, b ≠ 1. Base b determines growth (b > 1) or decay (0 Exponential Growth: When b > 1, f(x) = ab^x grows exponentially. The larger b, the faster the growth. Doubling time or tripling time describes how long for the function to double or triple in value.

Properties of Exponents: b^{x+y} = b^x · b^y, b^{xy} = (b^x)^y, b^{-x} = 1/b^x. These properties allow solving exponential equations algebraically when bases are equal.

Real-World Models: Continuous growth: A(t) = Pe^{rt}. Compound interest: A(t) = P(1 + r/n)^{nt}. Both are exponential models with slightly different forms.
