--- Lesson 5.1: Exponential Functions & Growth (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential Functions & Growth
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the y-intercept of f(x) = 5(2)^x?
The y-intercept is found by setting x = 0: f(0) = 5(2)^0 = 5(1) = 5. Any nonzero number raised to the zero power equals 1.
What is the growth factor in the model A = 500(1.08)^t?
In A = P(b)^t, the base b = 1.08 is the growth factor. It represents a quantity multiplied by 1.08 each time period, corresponding to an 8% increase.
What is an exponential function?
An exponential function has the form f(x) = ab^x where the variable is in the exponent, a is the initial value, and b is the base (positive and not equal to 1).
What is a growth factor?
The growth factor is the base b = 1 + r in an exponential model. For a 5% growth rate, the growth factor is 1.05, meaning the quantity is multiplied by 1.05 each period.
What is the growth rate in an exponential function?
The growth rate r is the decimal portion above 1 in the growth factor. If b = 1.05, then r = 0.05, meaning a 5% growth rate per period.
What is compound interest?
Compound interest is calculated on both the original principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods, causing the amount to grow exponentially rather than linearly.
What is Euler's number e?
Euler's number e is approximately 2.71828 and is the base of the natural logarithm. It arises naturally as the limit of (1 + 1/n)^n as n approaches infinity and is fundamental to continuous growth models.
What is a horizontal asymptote?
A horizontal asymptote is a horizontal line that the graph gets closer and closer to but never touches. For basic exponential functions f(x) = ab^x, the asymptote is y = 0.
What is continuous compounding?
Continuous compounding is the theoretical limit of compounding as the number of periods approaches infinity. It uses the formula A = Pe^(rt), where e is Euler's number.
An investor deposits $5,000 at 4% interest compounded quarterly. What is the balance after 2 years?
A = 5000(1 + 0.04/4)^(4*2) = 5000(1.01)^8 = 5000(1.08243) = $5,412.16. Quarterly compounding means n = 4, so the rate per period is 0.04/4 = 0.01.
A population of 100 organisms triples every 5 years. What is the population after 15 years?
In 15 years there are 15/5 = 3 tripling periods. Population = 100 * 3^3 = 100 * 27 = 2,700.
A house purchased for $250,000 appreciates at 5% per year. What is its value after 10 years?
V = 250000(1.05)^10 = 250000(1.62889) = $407,224 (rounded). The 5% annual appreciation compounds, causing the value to grow faster than a simple $12,500 per year.

--- Lesson 5.2: Exponential Decay & Applications (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential Decay & Applications
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the decay factor if a quantity decreases by 15% each year?
The decay factor is 1 - r = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85. Each year, 85% of the previous amount remains.
What is exponential decay?
Exponential decay occurs when a quantity decreases by a fixed percentage (not a fixed amount) each time period. The decay factor is between 0 and 1, and the quantity approaches but never reaches zero.
What is a half-life?
A half-life is the fixed time interval during which a decaying quantity is reduced to 50% of its current amount. It is constant regardless of how much substance remains.
What is a decay factor?
The decay factor b = 1 - r is the number between 0 and 1 that the quantity is multiplied by each period. For a 20% decay rate, the decay factor is 1 - 0.20 = 0.80.
What is the decay rate?
The decay rate r is the fraction by which a quantity decreases each period. If the decay factor is 0.85, the decay rate is 1 - 0.85 = 0.15, meaning 15% is lost per period.
What is carbon-14 dating?
Carbon-14 dating uses the known half-life of C-14 (about 5,730 years) and measures the remaining C-14 in once-living material to estimate how long ago the organism died.
What is the difference between exponential decay and linear decrease?
In exponential decay, a fixed percentage is lost each period (the amount lost gets smaller over time). In linear decrease, a fixed amount is subtracted each period. Exponential decay never reaches zero; linear decrease eventually does.
A car purchased for $25,000 depreciates at 20% per year. What is the car's value after 3 years?
V = 25000(0.80)^3 = 25000(0.512) = $12,800. Each year the car retains 80% of its previous value, so after 3 years: 25000 * 0.8 * 0.8 * 0.8 = 12,800.
A laptop worth $1,200 loses 25% of its value each year. What is it worth after 2 years?
V = 1200(0.75)^2 = 1200(0.5625) = $675. After year 1 it is worth $900, and after year 2 it is 75% of $900 = $675.
A city's air pollution index is 200 but improves by 6% per year due to new regulations. What is the pollution index after 10 years?
P = 200(0.94)^10 = 200(0.5386) = 108 (rounded). A 6% annual improvement means the decay factor is 0.94, and after 10 years the index is about 54% of the original.

--- Lesson 5.3: Logarithms & Properties (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logarithms & Properties
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a logarithm?
A logarithm is the inverse of exponentiation. The expression log_b(x) = y means 'b raised to the power y equals x.' Logarithms undo exponential functions.
What is the Product Rule of logarithms?
The Product Rule states that the logarithm of a product equals the sum of the logarithms. This property converts multiplication inside a logarithm into addition outside.
What is the Quotient Rule of logarithms?
The Quotient Rule states that the logarithm of a quotient equals the difference of the logarithms. Division inside becomes subtraction outside.
What is the Power Rule of logarithms?
The Power Rule states that the exponent inside a logarithm can be brought out as a multiplier. This converts exponentiation inside the log into multiplication outside.
What is the Change of Base Formula?
The Change of Base Formula allows you to evaluate a logarithm in any base by converting it to a ratio of logs in a more convenient base (like 10 or e): log_b(x) = log(x)/log(b).
What is a common logarithm?
A common logarithm has base 10 and is written simply as log(x) without a subscript. It is widely used in science (pH, decibels, Richter scale) and on most calculators.
What is a natural logarithm?
The natural logarithm uses Euler's number e as its base. Written as ln(x), it is the inverse of e^x and is fundamental in calculus, physics, and continuous growth/decay models.
What is the inverse relationship between logarithms and exponentials?
Logarithms and exponentials are inverse operations. Applying one after the other returns the original value: log_b(b^x) = x and b^(log_b(x)) = x, just as addition and subtraction undo each other.
A sound engineer measures a sound at 10^5 times the reference intensity I_0. Using dB = 10 log(I/I_0), what is the decibel level?
dB = 10 log(10^5/1) = 10 * log(10^5) = 10 * 5 = 50 dB. The decibel formula uses the common logarithm to compress large intensity ratios into manageable numbers.
A scientist uses the formula M = (2/3) log(E/E_0) to relate earthquake energy E to magnitude M. If an earthquake releases 10^12 joules and E_0 = 10^6 joules, what is the magnitude?
M = (2/3) log(10^12 / 10^6) = (2/3) log(10^6) = (2/3)(6) = 4. The logarithm simplifies the enormous energy ratio into a manageable magnitude number.

--- Lesson 5.4: Logarithmic Functions & Graphs (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logarithmic Functions & Graphs
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the x-intercept of y = log_10(x)?
The x-intercept is where y = 0. Setting log_10(x) = 0 gives x = 10^0 = 1. The x-intercept is at (1, 0).
What is the vertical asymptote of a logarithmic function?
For y = log_b(x - h), the vertical asymptote is x = h, the value that makes the argument zero. The graph gets infinitely close to this line but never reaches or crosses it.
What is the relationship between the graphs of y = log_b(x) and y = b^x?
Since logarithmic and exponential functions are inverses, their graphs are mirror images across the line y = x. Every point (a, b) on one graph corresponds to (b, a) on the other.
What is the x-intercept of a basic logarithmic function y = log_b(x)?
Setting y = 0 gives log_b(x) = 0, which means x = b^0 = 1. So the x-intercept is always at (1, 0) for the parent function y = log_b(x).
What is a reflection of a logarithmic graph over the x-axis?
Reflecting over the x-axis multiplies the entire function by -1, flipping each point's y-coordinate. The x-intercept at (1, 0) stays the same, but the graph curves downward instead of upward.
What is a logarithmic scale?
A logarithmic scale uses powers of a base (usually 10) as intervals, so each step represents multiplication rather than addition. It is useful for displaying data spanning many orders of magnitude, like the Richter scale or decibels.
A water treatment plant monitors water quality with pH = -log[H+]. A sample has [H+] = 10^(-6). What is the pH, and is the water acidic, neutral, or basic?
pH = -log(10^(-6)) = -(-6) = 6. Since pH < 7, the water is slightly acidic. Neutral water has pH = 7, and basic solutions have pH > 7.
A graphing calculator shows that log_2(x) = 3.5. A student needs to find the exact value of x. What is x?
If log_2(x) = 3.5, then x = 2^3.5 = 2^(7/2) = sqrt(2^7) = sqrt(128) = 8*sqrt(2), which is approximately 11.31.
An environmental scientist models noise levels. If a lawn mower has intensity I = 10^(-3) W/m^2 and I_0 = 10^(-12) W/m^2, what is its decibel level using dB = 10 log(I/I_0)?
dB = 10 log(10^(-3) / 10^(-12)) = 10 log(10^9) = 10 * 9 = 90 dB. The logarithmic decibel scale compresses the enormous intensity ratio of 1 billion into a manageable number.
A geologist finds that log(100x) can be rewritten as log(x) + k. What is k?
log(100x) = log(100) + log(x) = 2 + log(x), so k = 2. Multiplying the argument by 100 is equivalent to shifting the graph up by 2 units.
A real estate analyst graphs home values using y = log(x - 50000) to compress the wide range of prices. What is the domain of this function?
The argument (x - 50000) must be positive, so x - 50000 > 0, giving x > 50,000. The vertical asymptote is at x = 50,000.
A music producer knows that doubling the sound intensity increases the decibel level by approximately 3 dB. If a speaker plays at 70 dB, what is the level when two identical speakers play together?
Two identical speakers double the intensity. Since dB increase = 10 log(2) = 3.01 dB, the combined level is approximately 70 + 3 = 73 dB. The logarithmic scale means doubling intensity adds only about 3 dB.
A data analyst plots revenue data on a semi-log graph. The data points form a straight line with slope 0.3 and y-intercept 2. What is the exponential model for revenue R as a function of time t?
On a semi-log graph, log(R) = 0.3t + 2. Converting: R = 10^(0.3t + 2) = 10^2 * 10^(0.3t) = 100 * 10^(0.3t). The y-intercept of 2 on the log scale means the initial value is 10^2 = 100.

--- Lesson 5.5: Exponential & Logarithmic Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Exponential & Logarithmic Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is an extraneous solution?
An extraneous solution is a value obtained during algebraic manipulation that does not satisfy the original equation. This commonly occurs in logarithmic equations because the domain requires positive arguments.
What is the one-to-one property of exponential functions?
The one-to-one property states that if equal bases are raised to powers that give equal results, the exponents must be equal. This allows us to solve equations like 2^(x+1) = 2^5 by setting x + 1 = 5.
What is the one-to-one property of logarithmic functions?
If two logarithms with the same base are equal, their arguments must be equal. This property is used to solve equations like log(2x) = log(10) by setting 2x = 10.
What is a substitution technique for solving exponential equations like e^(2x) - 5e^x + 6 = 0?
Letting u = e^x converts the equation to a quadratic in u. Solving u^2 - 5u + 6 = 0 gives u = 2 or u = 3. Then e^x = 2 gives x = ln(2) and e^x = 3 gives x = ln(3).
What is the general strategy for solving a logarithmic equation with a single logarithm?
For an equation like log_b(expression) = c, convert to exponential form: expression = b^c. Then solve algebraically and check that the solution makes all logarithmic arguments positive.

--- Lesson 5.6: Applications of Exponentials & Logs (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications of Exponentials & Logs
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the Richter scale?
The Richter scale is logarithmic: each whole number increase represents 10 times more ground motion amplitude and approximately 31.6 times more energy released.
What is the doubling time?
Doubling time is a fixed interval during which an exponentially growing quantity doubles. For continuous growth at rate r, doubling time = ln(2)/r.
What is the Rule of 72?
The Rule of 72 is a mental math shortcut. For example, at 8% interest: 72/8 = 9 years to double. It works well for rates between 2% and 15%.
What is a logistic growth model?
Logistic growth produces an S-shaped curve. Growth is initially exponential, slows as resources become limited, and approaches a maximum (carrying capacity L). It models populations with limited resources.
What is the carrying capacity in a logistic model?
The carrying capacity L is the upper limit of population size that the environment can support indefinitely. In the logistic model, the population asymptotically approaches L.
What is exponential regression?
Exponential regression finds the best-fit exponential function for a data set. It is used when data shows constant percentage (multiplicative) change rather than constant additive change.
What is the effective annual rate (EAR)?
EAR = (1 + r/n)^n - 1, where r is the nominal rate and n is the number of compounding periods per year. It reflects the true annual return including compound interest effects.
What is the decibel (dB) scale?
The decibel scale uses a logarithmic formula to compare sound intensity I to a reference intensity I_0 = 10^(-12) W/m^2. Every 10 dB increase represents a tenfold increase in intensity.
What is the difference between nominal and effective interest rates?
The nominal rate is the stated annual percentage. The effective rate reflects actual earnings after compounding. For example, 12% nominal compounded monthly yields an effective rate of about 12.68%.
$5,000 is invested at 4% compounded continuously. Using A = Pe^(rt), what is the balance after 10 years?
A = 5000 * e^(0.04 * 10) = 5000 * e^0.4 = 5000 * 1.49182 = $7,459.12. Continuous compounding at 4% for 10 years multiplies the principal by e^0.4.

--- Lesson 5.7: Natural Logarithms & e (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Natural Logarithms & e
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the natural logarithm?
The natural logarithm ln(x) uses Euler's number e as its base. It is the inverse of the natural exponential function e^x and is fundamental in calculus, science, and continuous growth models.
What is the mathematical definition of e?
Euler's number e is defined as the limit of (1 + 1/n)^n as n goes to infinity. This limit naturally arises from the concept of continuous compounding and equals approximately 2.71828.
What is the inverse property of ln and e?
The natural logarithm and the natural exponential are inverse functions. Composing them in either order returns the original input: ln(e^x) = x and e^(ln x) = x (with x > 0 for the second).
What is the relationship between log_10 and ln?
By the change of base formula, log_10(x) = ln(x)/ln(10). Since ln(10) is approximately 2.303, the common log is always the natural log divided by about 2.303.
What is the decay constant k in the model N = N_0 * e^(-kt)?
The decay constant k is a positive number in the exponent (with a negative sign). A larger k means faster decay. It relates to half-life by t_(1/2) = ln(2)/k.
What is the half-life formula in terms of ln?
Starting from N_0/2 = N_0 * e^(-kt), divide: 1/2 = e^(-kt). Take ln: -ln(2) = -kt, so t = ln(2)/k. This connects the half-life to the continuous decay constant.
What is the domain and range of ln(x)?
The natural logarithm is only defined for positive inputs (x > 0), but it can produce any real number as output. For very small positive x, ln(x) is very negative; for large x, ln(x) is large and positive.
A radioactive isotope has a decay constant of k = 0.035 per year. What is its half-life?
t_(1/2) = ln(2)/k = 0.6931/0.035 = 19.8 years. The half-life is inversely proportional to the decay constant.
A cup of boiling water (100 degrees C) is placed in a room at 20 degrees C. Using Newton's Law of Cooling T(t) = 20 + 80e^(-0.1t), what is the temperature after 10 minutes?
T(10) = 20 + 80e^(-0.1*10) = 20 + 80e^(-1) = 20 + 80(0.3679) = 20 + 29.43 = 49.4 degrees C. The temperature exponentially approaches the room temperature of 20 degrees C.
A population of 500 organisms grows continuously. After 4 hours, the population is 1,200. What is the continuous growth rate r?
1200 = 500e^(4r). Divide: 2.4 = e^(4r). Take ln: ln(2.4) = 4r. So r = ln(2.4)/4 = 0.8755/4 = 0.2189, or about 21.9% per hour.
A savings account earns interest continuously at rate r. If $3,000 grows to $5,000 in 8 years, what is the interest rate?
5000 = 3000e^(8r). Divide: 5/3 = e^(8r). Take ln: ln(5/3) = 8r. So r = ln(5/3)/8 = 0.5108/8 = 0.0639, or approximately 6.4%.
