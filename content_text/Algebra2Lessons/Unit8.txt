--- Lesson 8.1: Angles & Angle Measures (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angles & Angle Measures
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the reference angle for 210°?
210° is in Quadrant III. Reference angle = 210° − 180° = 30°.
What is the reference angle for 5π/4?
5π/4 is in Quadrant III. Reference angle = 5π/4 − π = π/4.
What is a 'radian'?
One radian is the angle at the center of a circle that intercepts an arc whose length equals the radius. A full circle is 2π radians.
What is a 'reference angle'?
The reference angle is always positive and between 0° and 90° (or 0 and π/2). It helps evaluate trig functions for angles in any quadrant.
What is the conversion factor from degrees to radians?
To convert degrees to radians, multiply by π/180. To go the other way, multiply by 180/π.
What is 'arc length'?
Arc length s = rθ gives the length of the curved path along a circle of radius r subtended by angle θ (in radians).
What is a 'sector' of a circle?
A sector is the 'pie slice' shaped region enclosed by two radii and the arc between them. Its area is A = (1/2)r²θ.
A pizza has a 14-inch diameter. A slice has a central angle of 45°. What is the area of the slice?
r = 7 in, θ = 45° = π/4. Area = (1/2)r²θ = (1/2)(49)(π/4) = 49π/8 ≈ 19.2 sq in.
An analog clock reads 4:00. What is the angle (in degrees) between the hour and minute hands?
Each hour mark is 360°/12 = 30°. At 4:00, the hands are 4 × 30° = 120° apart.
Real-World Applications
Example: A clock's minute hand is 12 cm long. How far does the tip travel in 20 minutes?
20 minutes = 20/60 of a full rotation = 1/3 of 2π = 2π/3 radians. s = 12(2π/3) = 8π ≈ 25.1 cm.

--- Lesson 8.2: Unit Circle & Trigonometric Ratios (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Unit Circle & Trigonometric Ratios
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the 'unit circle'?
The unit circle has center (0,0) and radius 1. For angle θ, the point on the circle is (cos θ, sin θ).
What is the mnemonic 'SOH-CAH-TOA'?
SOH-CAH-TOA helps remember the right triangle trig ratios: Sine = Opp/Hyp, Cosine = Adj/Hyp, Tangent = Opp/Adj.
What is a 'special angle' in trigonometry?
Special angles have exact trig values involving simple fractions and square roots (e.g., sin 30° = 1/2, cos 45° = √2/2).
What is the Pythagorean identity?
The fundamental Pythagorean identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 comes from the equation of the unit circle x² + y² = 1.
A wheelchair ramp must rise 2 feet. Building codes require the angle with the ground to be no more than 4.8°. What is the minimum ramp length?
sin(4.8°) = 2/L → L = 2/sin(4.8°) ≈ 2/0.0837 ≈ 23.9 ft.

--- Lesson 8.3: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the period of y = sin x?
The basic sine function y = sin x completes one full cycle every 2π units.
What is the amplitude of y = 3sin x?
The amplitude is the absolute value of the coefficient: |3| = 3.
What is the period of y = cos(2x)?
Period = 2π/|b| = 2π/2 = π.
What is the phase shift of y = sin(x − π/3)?
y = sin(x − c) shifts right by c. Here c = π/3, so shift is π/3 to the right.
What is the vertical shift of y = cos x + 4?
Adding 4 outside the function shifts the entire graph up by 4 units.
What is the range of y = 2sin x?
Amplitude = 2, so the function oscillates between −2 and 2.
What is the period of y = tan x?
The tangent function repeats every π units, unlike sine and cosine which repeat every 2π.
What is the 'amplitude' of a trigonometric function?
Amplitude = (max − min)/2. For y = A sin(Bx), the amplitude is |A|.
What is the 'period' of a trigonometric function?
The period is the smallest positive value T such that f(x + T) = f(x). For y = sin(Bx), period = 2π/|B|.
What is 'phase shift'?
Phase shift is the horizontal displacement of a trig graph. In y = sin(Bx − C), the phase shift is C/B.
What is the 'midline' of a trigonometric function?
The midline is y = D, the average of the max and min. The graph oscillates above and below this line.
What is 'frequency' in trigonometry?
Frequency = 1/period = |B|/(2π). It tells how many cycles the function completes per unit interval.
What is a 'vertical asymptote' in the context of y = tan x?
tan x has vertical asymptotes at x = π/2 + nπ where cos x = 0 and tan x is undefined.
What is the domain of y = sin x?
Sine is defined for all real numbers. Its domain is (−∞, ∞).
What is an 'asymptote'?
An asymptote is a line that the graph gets arbitrarily close to but typically does not cross. Tangent has vertical asymptotes.

--- Lesson 8.4: Trigonometric Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Trigonometric Identities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the double-angle formula for sin 2x?
The double-angle formula for sine is sin 2x = 2 sin x cos x. This can be derived from the angle addition formula sin(a + b) with a = b = x.
If sin x = 3/5 and x is in Quadrant I, what is cos x?
Using sin²x + cos²x = 1: (3/5)² + cos²x = 1, so cos²x = 1 - 9/25 = 16/25, and cos x = 4/5 (positive in Quadrant I).
What is cos(A + B) equal to?
The cosine addition formula states cos(A + B) = cos A cos B - sin A sin B. Note the subtraction sign, which distinguishes it from cos(A - B).
What is sin(π/2 - x) equal to?
By the cofunction identity, sin(π/2 - x) = cos x. Cofunctions of complementary angles are equal.
What is a trigonometric identity?
A trigonometric identity is an equation involving trigonometric functions that is true for all values of the variable in its domain. Unlike equations, identities hold universally.
What is the definition of a reciprocal identity in trigonometry?
A reciprocal identity expresses one trig function as 1 divided by another: csc x = 1/sin x, sec x = 1/cos x, and cot x = 1/tan x.
What is the 'double-angle formula' in trigonometry?
A double-angle formula expresses a trig function of twice an angle (2x) in terms of trig functions of x. For example, sin 2x = 2 sin x cos x.
What is a 'half-angle formula' used for?
A half-angle formula lets you find sin(x/2), cos(x/2), or tan(x/2) using known values of sin x and cos x. For example, sin(x/2) = ±√((1 - cos x)/2).
What is meant by an 'even function' in the context of trigonometric identities?
An even function satisfies f(-x) = f(x) and is symmetric about the y-axis. Cosine is an even function because cos(-x) = cos x.
What is a 'power-reducing formula' in trigonometry?
Power-reducing formulas express squared (or higher power) trig functions in terms of first-power trig functions of a multiple angle. For example, sin²x = (1 - cos 2x)/2.
A sound engineer models a wave as y = sin²(x) + cos²(x). After applying the Pythagorean identity, what is the simplified output of the wave?
By the Pythagorean identity, sin²x + cos²x = 1 for all x. The simplified output is the constant y = 1.
A physics student needs to find the exact value of sin 75° using the sum formula sin(45° + 30°). What is the result?
sin 75° = sin(45° + 30°) = sin 45° cos 30° + cos 45° sin 30° = (√2/2)(√3/2) + (√2/2)(1/2) = (√6 + √2)/4.
A data analyst needs cos(θ/2) for a rotation transform, given that cos θ = 7/25 and θ is in Quadrant I. Using the half-angle formula, what is cos(θ/2)?
cos(θ/2) = √((1 + cos θ)/2) = √((1 + 7/25)/2) = √((32/25)/2) = √(32/50) = √(16/25) = 4/5. Positive because θ/2 is in Quadrant I.
Real-World Applications
Example: An electrical engineer is analyzing a circuit where the voltage is modeled by V = 2 sin x cos x. Using the double-angle formula, which simplified expression should the engineer use?
The double-angle identity states sin 2x = 2 sin x cos x. Therefore V = 2 sin x cos x simplifies directly to V = sin 2x.

--- Lesson 8.5: Solving Trigonometric Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Solving Trigonometric Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the general solution for sin x = 0?
sin x = 0 at every integer multiple of π: x = 0, ±π, ±2π, ... The general solution is x = nπ for any integer n.
What is a 'trigonometric equation'?
A trigonometric equation contains trig functions and is satisfied only by specific values of the variable, unlike identities which are true for all values in the domain.
What is a 'reference angle'?
A reference angle is the positive acute angle between the terminal side of an angle and the nearest part of the x-axis. It is always between 0° and 90°.
What is meant by 'principal solution' of a trigonometric equation?
Principal solutions are the solutions within one complete cycle of the trig function, usually [0, 2π). All other solutions are found by adding period multiples.

--- Lesson 8.6: Applications of Trigonometry (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications of Trigonometry
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In a triangle, A = 30°, a = 8, and b = 10. Using the Law of Sines, what is sin B?
By the Law of Sines: sin B/b = sin A/a, so sin B = b sin A/a = 10 sin 30°/8 = 10(1/2)/8 = 5/8.
What is the area of a triangle with sides a = 6, b = 10, and included angle C = 30°?
Area = (1/2)ab sin C = (1/2)(6)(10) sin 30° = (1/2)(60)(1/2) = 15 square units.
Using Heron's formula, what is the area of a triangle with sides 3, 4, and 5?
s = (3 + 4 + 5)/2 = 6. Area = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)) = √(6 · 3 · 2 · 1) = √36 = 6.
Find angle A using the Law of Cosines if a = 7, b = 5, c = 8. What is cos A?
cos A = (b² + c² - a²)/(2bc) = (25 + 64 - 49)/(2 · 5 · 8) = 40/80 = 1/2. So A = 60°.
What is the area of a triangle with vertices at angles A = 40°, B = 60°, C = 80° and side a = 10 (opposite angle A)?
When given AAS/ASA information, area = (a² sin B sin C)/(2 sin A). This formula derives from combining the Law of Sines with the standard area formula.
A triangle has sides a = 13, b = 14, c = 15. What is the semi-perimeter s?
The semi-perimeter is s = (a + b + c)/2 = (13 + 14 + 15)/2 = 42/2 = 21.
What is the Law of Sines?
The Law of Sines states a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C, meaning each side divided by the sine of its opposite angle gives the same value for all three sides.
What is the Law of Cosines?
The Law of Cosines (c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C) generalizes the Pythagorean theorem. When C = 90°, cos 90° = 0 and it reduces to c² = a² + b².
What is the 'ambiguous case' in the Law of Sines?
The ambiguous case occurs with SSA (side-side-angle) information. Depending on the values, there could be 0, 1, or 2 possible triangles, making the solution ambiguous.
What is a 'semi-perimeter' of a triangle?
The semi-perimeter is half the triangle's perimeter. It equals s = (a + b + c)/2 and is used in Heron's formula to calculate area.
What is 'bearing' in navigation?
In navigation, a bearing is a direction expressed as an angle measured clockwise from due north. For example, due east is a bearing of 090°.
A triangular plot of land has sides measuring 150 ft, 200 ft, and 250 ft. A developer needs to calculate the area for a building permit. Using Heron's formula, what is the area?
s = (150 + 200 + 250)/2 = 300. Area = √(300 · 150 · 100 · 50) = √(225,000,000) = 15,000 sq ft.
An architect designs a triangular garden with two sides of 12 m and 18 m and an included angle of 45°. What is the area of the garden?
Area = (1/2)(12)(18) sin 45° = (1/2)(216)(√2/2) = 108(√2/2) = 54√2 square meters.
Two sides of a parallelogram are 7 cm and 8 cm, and one diagonal is 13 cm. Using the Law of Cosines, what is the angle between the two sides adjacent to that diagonal?
Using the triangle formed by sides 7, 8 and diagonal 13: cos θ = (7² + 8² - 13²)/(2 · 7 · 8) = (49 + 64 - 169)/112 = -56/112 = -1/2. So θ = 120°.

  Q12. What is the Law of Cosines?
    [ ] A formula that only works for obtuse triangles
    [ ] A formula for finding the perimeter of a triangle
    [ ] The ratio of cosines of all three angles
    [*] A generalization of the Pythagorean theorem that relates the sides and one angle of any triangle: c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C
    Explanation: The Law of Cosines (c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C) generalizes the Pythagorean theorem. When C = 90°, cos 90° = 0 and it reduces to c² = a² + b².

  Q13. What is the 'ambiguous case' in the Law of Sines?
    [ ] The case where all three sides are given
    [*] The SSA case (two sides and an angle not between them) where zero, one, or two triangles may exist
    [ ] The case where the triangle is equilateral
    [ ] The case where two angles and a side are given
    Explanation: The ambiguous case occurs with SSA (side-side-angle) information. Depending on the values, there could be 0, 1, or 2 possible triangles, making the solution ambiguous.

  Q14. What does 'Heron's formula' calculate?
    [ ] The perimeter of a triangle
    [*] The area of a triangle when all three side lengths are known
    [ ] The altitude of a triangle
    [ ] The angles of a triangle
    Explanation: Heron's formula calculates the area of a triangle using only its three side lengths: Area = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)), where s is the semi-perimeter.

  Q15. What is a 'semi-perimeter' of a triangle?
    [ ] The median of the triangle
    [ ] The average of two sides
    [ ] Half of the longest side
    [*] Half of the perimeter: s = (a + b + c)/2
    Explanation: The semi-perimeter is half the triangle's perimeter. It equals s = (a + b + c)/2 and is used in Heron's formula to calculate area.

  Q16. What does 'angle of elevation' mean?
    [*] The angle between the ground and a line of sight looking upward from the observer
    [ ] The angle at the top of a triangle
    [ ] The height of an object above the ground
    [ ] The steepness of a hill measured in degrees
    Explanation: The angle of elevation is formed between the horizontal and an upward line of sight from the observer to an object above. It is always measured from the horizontal.

  Q17. What does 'angle of depression' mean?
    [ ] The smallest angle in a triangle
    [ ] A negative angle measurement
    [ ] The angle between two sides of a triangle
    [*] The angle between the horizontal and a line of sight looking downward from the observer
    Explanation: The angle of depression is formed between the horizontal line from the observer's eye and the downward line of sight to an object below. It equals the angle of elevation from the object.

  Q18. What does 'oblique triangle' mean?
    [ ] A triangle with all acute angles
    [ ] A triangle with two equal sides
    [*] A triangle that does not contain a right angle
    [ ] A triangle with one right angle
    Explanation: An oblique triangle is any triangle that does not have a 90° angle. It can be either acute (all angles < 90°) or obtuse (one angle > 90°). The Law of Sines and Cosines are used to solve oblique triangles.

  Q19. What is 'bearing' in navigation?
    [ ] The altitude of a location
    [ ] The speed at which you travel
    [ ] The distance between two points
    [*] A direction measured as an angle clockwise from north
    Explanation: In navigation, a bearing is a direction expressed as an angle measured clockwise from due north. For example, due east is a bearing of 090°.

  Q20. What does 'SAS' stand for in triangle-solving contexts?
    [ ] Side-Area-Side: two sides and the area are known
    [ ] Sum-of-All-Sides: the perimeter is known
    [ ] Sine-Angle-Sine: two sine values and an angle are known
    [*] Side-Angle-Side: two sides and the included angle between them are known
    Explanation: SAS stands for Side-Angle-Side, meaning two sides and the angle between them (the included angle) are known. This case is solved using the Law of Cosines.

  Q21. A surveyor stands 100 meters from the base of a tower and measures the angle of elevation to the top as 60°. How tall is the tower?
    [ ] 50√3 meters
    [*] 100√3 meters
    [ ] 200 meters
    [ ] 100 meters
    Explanation: tan 60° = height/100, so height = 100 tan 60° = 100√3 meters (approximately 173.2 meters).

  Q22. Two fire stations are 20 miles apart. Station A spots a fire at a bearing of N 40° E, and Station B spots the same fire at N 30° W. In the triangle formed, the angle at Station A is 50° and the angle at Station B is 60°. Using the Law of Sines, approximately how far is the fire from Station A?
    [ ] 20 sin 50° / sin 70°
    [*] 20 sin 60° / sin 70°
    [ ] 20 sin 40° / sin 30°
    [ ] 20 sin 70° / sin 60°
    Explanation: The angle at the fire is 180° - 50° - 60° = 70°. By Law of Sines: d/sin 60° = 20/sin 70°, so d = 20 sin 60°/sin 70° (the side opposite 60° is the distance from A to the fire).

  Q23. A triangular plot of land has sides measuring 150 ft, 200 ft, and 250 ft. A developer needs to calculate the area for a building permit. Using Heron's formula, what is the area?
    [ ] 10,000 sq ft
    [ ] 12,500 sq ft
    [*] 15,000 sq ft
    [ ] 18,750 sq ft
    Explanation: s = (150 + 200 + 250)/2 = 300. Area = √(300 · 150 · 100 · 50) = √(225,000,000) = 15,000 sq ft.

  Q24. A pilot flies 300 km on a bearing of 060°, then turns and flies 400 km on a bearing of 150°. The angle between the two legs is 90°. How far is the pilot from the starting point?
    [ ] 600 km
    [ ] 700 km
    [ ] 350 km
    [*] 500 km
    Explanation: With a 90° angle between the legs, use the Pythagorean theorem (special case of Law of Cosines): d = √(300² + 400²) = √(90,000 + 160,000) = √250,000 = 500 km.

  Q25. A ship sails from port A to port B (a distance of 120 nautical miles) at a bearing of 070°. It then sails from B to port C (80 nautical miles) at a bearing of 160°. If the angle ABC = 90°, what expression gives the distance from A to C?
    [ ] √(120² - 80²)
    [ ] 120 sin 90° / sin 70°
    [ ] 120 + 80
    [*] √(120² + 80² - 2(120)(80)cos 90°)
    Explanation: By the Law of Cosines: AC² = 120² + 80² - 2(120)(80)cos 90°. Since cos 90° = 0, this simplifies to AC = √(120² + 80²) = √20,800.

  Q26. An architect designs a triangular garden with two sides of 12 m and 18 m and an included angle of 45°. What is the area of the garden?
    [ ] 108√2 square meters
    [ ] 54 square meters
    [ ] 108 square meters
    [*] 54√2 square meters
    Explanation: Area = (1/2)(12)(18) sin 45° = (1/2)(216)(√2/2) = 108(√2/2) = 54√2 square meters.

  Q27. From the top of a 200-foot cliff, a ranger spots a boat at an angle of depression of 30°. How far is the boat from the base of the cliff?
    [*] 200√3 feet
    [ ] 200 feet
    [ ] 100√3 feet
    [ ] 400 feet
    Explanation: The angle of depression equals the angle of elevation from the boat. tan 30° = 200/d, so d = 200/tan 30° = 200/(1/√3) = 200√3 feet.

  Q28. Two sides of a parallelogram are 7 cm and 8 cm, and one diagonal is 13 cm. Using the Law of Cosines, what is the angle between the two sides adjacent to that diagonal?
    [*] 120°
    [ ] 60°
    [ ] 150°
    [ ] 90°
    Explanation: Using the triangle formed by sides 7, 8 and diagonal 13: cos θ = (7² + 8² - 13²)/(2 · 7 · 8) = (49 + 64 - 169)/112 = -56/112 = -1/2. So θ = 120°.

  Q29. A cell tower casts a shadow 50 meters long when the angle of elevation of the sun is 53°. Approximately how tall is the tower? (Use tan 53° ≈ 1.33)
    [ ] 75 meters
    [*] 66.5 meters
    [ ] 37.6 meters
    [ ] 50 meters
    Explanation: tan 53° = height/50, so height = 50 · tan 53° ≈ 50 · 1.33 = 66.5 meters.

  Q30. A rescue helicopter is at point H, 5 km from victim A and 8 km from victim B. The angle AHB is 60°. Using the Law of Cosines, how far apart are the two victims?
    [*] √49 = 7 km
    [ ] 13 km
    [ ] √89 km
    [ ] √129 km
    Explanation: AB² = 5² + 8² - 2(5)(8)cos 60° = 25 + 64 - 80(1/2) = 89 - 40 = 49. So AB = √49 = 7 km.

  5. Q: SAS case use?
     A: Law of Cosines.

  6. Q: ASA case use?
     A: Law of Sines (find third angle first).

  7. Q: Ambiguous case SSA?
     A: 0, 1, or 2 triangles possible.

  8. Q: Area formula: K = ?
     A: ½ab sin(C).

     A: K = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)) where s = (a+b+c)/2.
