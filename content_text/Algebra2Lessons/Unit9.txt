--- Lesson 9.1: Conic Sections (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conic Sections
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the standard form equation of a circle with center (2, 3) and radius 5?
The standard form of a circle is (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r². With center (2, 3) and radius 5: (x - 2)² + (y - 3)² = 25.
What is the equation of a parabola with vertex at the origin and focus at (0, 3)?
For a vertical parabola with vertex at origin: x² = 4py. Since the focus is at (0, 3), p = 3, so x² = 4(3)y = 12y.
What is the center of the ellipse (x - 1)²/4 + (y + 2)²/9 = 1?
The center of (x - h)²/a² + (y - k)²/b² = 1 is (h, k). Here h = 1 and k = -2, so the center is (1, -2).
What is the directrix of the parabola x² = 8y?
x² = 4py gives 4p = 8, so p = 2. The parabola opens upward, so the directrix is y = -p = -2.
What is the eccentricity of the ellipse x²/25 + y²/16 = 1?
c² = a² - b² = 25 - 16 = 9, so c = 3. Eccentricity e = c/a = 3/5. For an ellipse, 0 < e < 1.
What is a 'conic section'?
A conic section is the curve produced when a plane intersects a double-napped cone at various angles, producing circles, ellipses, parabolas, or hyperbolas.
What is the definition of an 'ellipse'?
An ellipse is defined as the set of all points where the sum of the distances to two fixed points (foci) is a constant. This constant equals 2a, the length of the major axis.
What is a 'focus' (plural: foci) of a conic section?
A focus is a special fixed point that, together with other geometric properties (like a directrix or another focus), defines the shape of the conic section.
What is the definition of a 'hyperbola'?
A hyperbola is defined as the set of all points where the absolute value of the difference of the distances to two foci is a constant equal to 2a.
What is a 'directrix' of a parabola?
The directrix is a fixed line used in the definition of a parabola. Each point on the parabola is equidistant from the focus and the directrix.
What is the 'major axis' of an ellipse?
The major axis is the longest diameter of the ellipse, passing through both foci and the center. Its length is 2a, where a is the semi-major axis.
What is an 'asymptote' of a hyperbola?
Asymptotes are straight lines that the two branches of a hyperbola approach as x and y grow large. For x²/a² - y²/b² = 1, they are y = ±(b/a)x.
What is the 'latus rectum' of a parabola?
The latus rectum is the chord through the focus perpendicular to the axis of symmetry. Its length is |4p|, where p is the distance from the vertex to the focus.
A satellite orbits Earth in an elliptical path described by x²/49 + y²/25 = 1 (in thousands of km). What is the distance between the two foci of the orbit?
c² = a² - b² = 49 - 25 = 24, so c = √24. The distance between foci is 2c = 2√24 thousand km.
A circular fountain has the equation x² + y² - 6x + 4y - 12 = 0. An engineer needs to determine the radius to order materials. What is the radius?
Complete the square: (x² - 6x + 9) + (y² + 4y + 4) = 12 + 9 + 4, giving (x - 3)² + (y + 2)² = 25. The radius is √25 = 5.
A tunnel has a semi-elliptical cross-section that is 30 feet wide and 10 feet tall at the center. A truck that is 8 feet tall needs to pass through. What is the maximum width of the truck that can fit?
The ellipse equation is x²/225 + y²/100 = 1 (a = 15, b = 10). At y = 8: x²/225 + 64/100 = 1, x²/225 = 0.36, x² = 81, x = 9. The full width is 2(9) = 18 feet.

--- Lesson 9.2: Parametric Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Parametric Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Given parametric equations x = 3t and y = t², what is the rectangular equation after eliminating the parameter?
From x = 3t, solve for t: t = x/3. Substitute into y = t²: y = (x/3)² = x²/9.
Given x = t + 1 and y = 2t - 3, what is y in terms of x?
From x = t + 1: t = x - 1. Substitute into y = 2t - 3: y = 2(x - 1) - 3 = 2x - 2 - 3 = 2x - 5.
For the parametric equations x = t², y = t³, what is dy/dx?
dy/dx = (dy/dt)/(dx/dt) = 3t²/(2t) = 3t/2 (for t ≠ 0).
What is the direction of motion for the parametric curve x = 2 - t, y = t² as t increases?
As t increases, x = 2 - t decreases (moves left) and y = t² increases (moves upward for t > 0). So the curve moves left and upward.
Given x = 5 sec t, y = 3 tan t, what is the rectangular equation?
x/5 = sec t and y/3 = tan t. Using sec²t - tan²t = 1: x²/25 - y²/9 = 1. This is a hyperbola.
What is a 'parametric equation'?
Parametric equations express coordinates x and y as separate functions of an independent variable (parameter), usually denoted t. For example, x = f(t) and y = g(t).
What is a 'parameter' in the context of parametric equations?
The parameter (typically t) is an independent variable that determines the x and y values simultaneously. It often represents time, giving a sense of motion along the curve.
What is a 'rectangular equation' (also called Cartesian equation)?
A rectangular (Cartesian) equation relates x and y directly, such as y = x² + 3 or x² + y² = 1, without using a third variable (parameter).
What is a 'plane curve' in the context of parametric equations?
A plane curve is a curve that lies in a single flat (2D) plane. Parametric equations x = f(t) and y = g(t) trace out a plane curve in the xy-plane.
An airplane is tracked by radar with parametric equations x = 300t and y = 200t + 5000, where x is horizontal distance and y is altitude (in meters, t in minutes). After 10 minutes, what is the plane's position?
At t = 10: x = 300(10) = 3000 meters, y = 200(10) + 5000 = 7000 meters. The position is (3000, 7000).
A Ferris wheel has parametric equations x = 20 cos(t) and y = 20 sin(t) + 25, where distances are in feet. What is the lowest point a rider reaches?
The minimum of y = 20 sin(t) + 25 occurs when sin(t) = -1: y = 20(-1) + 25 = 5 feet. The rider's lowest point is 5 feet above the ground.
An engineer models the path of a drone as x = 3t² and y = 2t³ for t ≥ 0. What is the slope of the path when t = 1?
dy/dx = (dy/dt)/(dx/dt) = 6t²/(6t) = t. At t = 1: dy/dx = 1.
A car drives along a path given by x = t² - 4 and y = t + 2 (distances in km, t in hours). What is the rectangular equation of the path?
From y = t + 2: t = y - 2. Substitute into x = t² - 4: x = (y - 2)² - 4. This is a horizontal parabola.
A roller coaster track is modeled by x = 10t, y = 5 sin(t) + 20 for 0 ≤ t ≤ 4π. A safety engineer needs the horizontal distance covered in one complete cycle. What is it?
One complete cycle of sin(t) has period 2π. During that time, x changes by 10(2π) - 10(0) = 20π meters.

--- Lesson 9.3: Vectors & Vector Operations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Vectors & Vector Operations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the component form of a vector with initial point (1, 3) and terminal point (4, 7)?
The component form is found by subtracting initial from terminal: ⟨4 - 1, 7 - 3⟩ = ⟨3, 4⟩.
What is the magnitude of the vector ⟨3, 4⟩?
The magnitude is |⟨3, 4⟩| = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5.
If u = ⟨2, 5⟩ and v = ⟨-1, 3⟩, what is u + v?
Vector addition is component-wise: u + v = ⟨2 + (-1), 5 + 3⟩ = ⟨1, 8⟩.
What is 3⟨2, -1⟩?
Scalar multiplication: 3⟨2, -1⟩ = ⟨3(2), 3(-1)⟩ = ⟨6, -3⟩.
What is the dot product of u = ⟨3, 4⟩ and v = ⟨-2, 5⟩?
u · v = (3)(-2) + (4)(5) = -6 + 20 = 14.
Two vectors are orthogonal (perpendicular). What is their dot product?
If two vectors are orthogonal (perpendicular), their dot product is zero: u · v = |u||v| cos 90° = 0.
What is the unit vector in the direction of ⟨6, 8⟩?
|⟨6, 8⟩| = √(36 + 64) = 10. The unit vector is ⟨6/10, 8/10⟩ = ⟨3/5, 4/5⟩.
If u = ⟨4, 3⟩ and v = ⟨1, -2⟩, what is u - v?
u - v = ⟨4 - 1, 3 - (-2)⟩ = ⟨3, 5⟩.
A vector has magnitude 10 and direction angle 60°. What is its component form?
v = ⟨|v| cos θ, |v| sin θ⟩ = ⟨10 cos 60°, 10 sin 60°⟩ = ⟨10(1/2), 10(√3/2)⟩ = ⟨5, 5√3⟩.
What is a 'vector'?
A vector is a mathematical quantity that has both magnitude (size/length) and direction. Examples include velocity, force, and displacement.
What is a 'scalar' in the context of vectors?
A scalar is a single real number with magnitude only (no direction). When a scalar multiplies a vector, it changes the vector's magnitude (and possibly reverses its direction).
What is a 'unit vector'?
A unit vector has a magnitude of 1. It indicates direction only. Any nonzero vector v can be made into a unit vector by dividing by its magnitude: v/|v|.
What is a 'resultant vector'?
The resultant vector is obtained by adding two or more vectors. It represents the combined effect, such as the net force when multiple forces act on an object.
What is the 'direction angle' of a vector?
The direction angle θ is the angle measured counterclockwise from the positive x-axis to the vector. It is found using tan θ = b/a for vector ⟨a, b⟩.
A boat heads due east at 12 mph while a river current flows due south at 5 mph. What is the boat's actual speed (magnitude of the resultant velocity)?
The velocity vectors are ⟨12, 0⟩ (east) and ⟨0, -5⟩ (south). Resultant = ⟨12, -5⟩. Magnitude = √(144 + 25) = √169 = 13 mph.
An airplane flies with a velocity vector of ⟨200, 0⟩ mph (due east) while wind blows at ⟨-30, 40⟩ mph. What is the resultant ground speed?
Resultant = ⟨200 - 30, 0 + 40⟩ = ⟨170, 40⟩. Speed = √(170² + 40²) = √(28900 + 1600) = √30500 ≈ 174.6 mph.
A worker pushes a box with a force of 50 N at an angle of 30° above the horizontal. What is the horizontal component of the force?
Horizontal component = 50 cos 30° = 50(√3/2) = 25√3 N (≈ 43.3 N).
Two forces act on an object: F₁ = ⟨8, 6⟩ N and F₂ = ⟨-3, 4⟩ N. A physics student needs the magnitude of the resultant force. What is it?
Resultant = ⟨8 + (-3), 6 + 4⟩ = ⟨5, 10⟩. Magnitude = √(25 + 100) = √125 = 5√5 N.
A soccer player kicks a ball with velocity ⟨10, 15⟩ m/s. The wind adds a velocity of ⟨2, -3⟩ m/s. What is the ball's actual direction angle (to the nearest degree)?
Resultant = ⟨10 + 2, 15 + (-3)⟩ = ⟨12, 12⟩. Direction angle = tan⁻¹(12/12) = tan⁻¹(1) = 45°.
A physics experiment measures two forces: F₁ = ⟨4, 3⟩ and F₂ = ⟨-4, 3⟩. A student needs to determine if the forces are perpendicular. What is their dot product?
F₁ · F₂ = (4)(-4) + (3)(3) = -16 + 9 = -7. Since the dot product is not zero, the vectors are not perpendicular.
A delivery drone must travel from point A at (0, 0) to point B at (6, 8) in a coordinate grid measured in km. What direction angle should it fly, and what is the distance?
Displacement vector = ⟨6, 8⟩. Distance = √(36 + 64) = 10 km. Direction angle = tan⁻¹(8/6) = tan⁻¹(4/3) ≈ 53.1°.
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'scalar multiplication' do to a vector?
Multiplying a vector by a positive scalar changes its magnitude but keeps its direction. A negative scalar reverses the direction. The result is k⟨a, b⟩ = ⟨ka, kb⟩.

--- Lesson 9.4: Complex Numbers in Polar Form (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Complex Numbers in Polar Form
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Convert the complex number 3 + 3i to polar form. What is the modulus r?
r = |3 + 3i| = √(3² + 3²) = √(9 + 9) = √18 = 3√2.
What is the argument (angle θ) of the complex number 1 + √3 i?
tan θ = √3/1 = √3. Since both parts are positive (Quadrant I), θ = 60°.
What is the modulus of -4 + 3i?
|-4 + 3i| = √((-4)² + 3²) = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5.
What is the polar form of -2i?
-2i has modulus 2. It lies on the negative imaginary axis, so θ = 270°. Polar form: 2(cos 270° + i sin 270°).
What is the 'polar form' of a complex number?
The polar form r(cos θ + i sin θ) expresses a complex number using its distance from the origin (modulus r) and the angle it makes with the positive real axis (argument θ).
What is the 'modulus' of a complex number?
The modulus (or absolute value) of a + bi is |a + bi| = √(a² + b²). It represents the distance from the origin to the point (a, b) in the complex plane.
What is the 'argument' of a complex number?
The argument (arg) is the angle θ from the positive real axis to the line connecting the origin and the complex number. It is found using θ = tan⁻¹(b/a), adjusted for quadrant.
What is 'De Moivre's Theorem'?
De Moivre's Theorem states that raising a complex number in polar form to the nth power gives r^n(cos nθ + i sin nθ). It greatly simplifies computing powers and roots.
A signal processing engineer multiplies two impedances: Z₁ = 3(cos 60° + i sin 60°) and Z₂ = 4(cos 30° + i sin 30°). What is the resulting impedance in polar form?
Multiply moduli: 3 · 4 = 12. Add arguments: 60° + 30° = 90°. Result: 12(cos 90° + i sin 90°) = 12i.
A physicist uses De Moivre's Theorem to compute (1 + i)⁶. First, 1 + i in polar form is √2 cis 45°. What is the result?
(√2)⁶ cis(6 · 45°) = 8 cis 270° = 8(cos 270° + i sin 270°) = 8(0 + i(-1)) = -8i.
A robotics engineer needs the three cube roots of 8(cos 90° + i sin 90°). What is the modulus of each cube root?
The modulus of each cube root is 8^(1/3) = 2. All three roots lie on a circle of radius 2, separated by 120°.
An electrical circuit has impedance Z = -3 + 4i ohms. An engineer needs the impedance in polar form for analysis. What is the modulus?
|Z| = √((-3)² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5 ohms.
A quantum computing student needs to find (cos 72° + i sin 72°)⁵. What is the result?
By De Moivre's Theorem: 1⁵(cos 5 · 72° + i sin 5 · 72°) = cos 360° + i sin 360° = 1 + 0i = 1. This is a 5th root of unity.
A control systems engineer divides Z₁ = 10 cis 150° by Z₂ = 2 cis 60°. What is the quotient?
Divide moduli: 10/2 = 5. Subtract arguments: 150° - 60° = 90°. Quotient: 5 cis 90° = 5i.
A telecommunications engineer represents a signal as z = 6 cis 240°. She needs to express z² in rectangular form for further processing. What is z²?
z² = 36 cis 480° = 36 cis 120° = 36(cos 120° + i sin 120°) = 36(-1/2 + i√3/2) = -18 + 18√3 i.
Real-World Applications
Example: Multiply: 2(cos 40° + i sin 40°) · 3(cos 50° + i sin 50°).
Multiply moduli: 2 · 3 = 6. Add angles: 40° + 50° = 90°. Result: 6(cos 90° + i sin 90°) = 6i.
Example: An electrical engineer models AC voltage as V = 10(cos 45° + i sin 45°). What are the real and imaginary components of the voltage?
Real = 10 cos 45° = 10(√2/2) = 5√2. Imaginary = 10 sin 45° = 10(√2/2) = 5√2.
