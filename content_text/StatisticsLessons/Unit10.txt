--- Lesson 10.1: Review of Key Formulas (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Review of Key Formulas
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is Bessel's correction?
Bessel's correction divides by n − 1 rather than n to give an unbiased estimator of the population variance.
What is conditional probability?
Conditional probability P(A|B) = P(A and B)/P(B) gives the probability of A given that B has occurred.
What is the 1.5 × IQR rule used for?
Any value falling more than 1.5 × IQR below Q₁ or above Q₃ is flagged as a suspected outlier.
What is the z-statistic for testing a single proportion?
The z-test for a proportion uses p₀ in the standard error because we assume H₀ is true when computing the test statistic.
What is the standard deviation of a binomial distribution?
The standard deviation σ = √(np(1−p)) measures the typical spread of the number of successes around the mean np.
What is the margin of error?
Margin of error = (critical value) × SE, and it determines how far the confidence interval extends above and below the point estimate.
A student scores 72 on a biology exam where the class mean is 64 and the standard deviation is 8. What is the student's z-score?
z = (72 − 64)/8 = 8/8 = 1.00. The student scored exactly one standard deviation above the mean.
A researcher surveys 49 patients and finds a sample mean recovery time of 12 days with a standard deviation of 3.5 days. What is the standard error of the sample mean?
SE = s/√n = 3.5/√49 = 3.5/7 = 0.50 days. This tells us how much the sample mean is expected to vary.
A poll of 400 voters finds that 55% support a new policy. What is the approximate 95% confidence interval for the true proportion?
SE = √(0.55 × 0.45/400) = √(0.2475/400) = √0.00061875 ≈ 0.0249. The 95% CI is 0.55 ± 1.96(0.0249) = 0.55 ± 0.049, giving approximately (0.501, 0.599).
A dataset has Q₁ = 20, median = 28, and Q₃ = 36. Using the 1.5 × IQR rule, what is the upper fence for identifying outliers?
IQR = Q₃ − Q₁ = 36 − 20 = 16. Upper fence = Q₃ + 1.5 × IQR = 36 + 1.5(16) = 36 + 24 = 60. Any value above 60 is a suspected outlier.
A hospital administrator wants to test whether the average ER wait time has decreased from the historical mean of 45 minutes. A sample of 36 patients has a mean wait of 40 minutes with s = 12. What is the t-statistic?
t = (x-bar − μ₀)/(s/√n) = (40 − 45)/(12/√36) = −5/(12/6) = −5/2 = −2.50. The negative value indicates the sample mean is below the hypothesized mean.
P(A) = 0.4, P(B) = 0.3, and P(A and B) = 0.12. A student uses the addition rule to find P(A or B). What is the result?
P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B) = 0.4 + 0.3 − 0.12 = 0.58. Subtracting the overlap prevents double-counting.
A quality inspector tests a random sample of 200 light bulbs and finds that 18 are defective. She computes a 95% CI for the defect rate. What is the approximate margin of error?
p-hat = 18/200 = 0.09. SE = √(0.09 × 0.91/200) = √(0.0819/200) = √0.0004095 ≈ 0.0202. Margin of error = 1.96 × 0.0202 ≈ 0.040. The closest answer is 0.038, reflecting minor rounding.
Outliers and Resistant Measures

Real-World Applications
Example: The formula for a population z-score is:
The z-score standardizes a value by subtracting the mean and dividing by the standard deviation.
Example: A student scores 72 on a biology exam where the class mean is 64 and the standard deviation is 8. What is the student's z-score?
z = (72 − 64)/8 = 8/8 = 1.00. The student scored exactly one standard deviation above the mean.
Example: A hospital administrator wants to test whether the average ER wait time has decreased from the historical mean of 45 minutes. A sample of 36 patients has a mean wait of 40 minutes with s = 12. What is the t-statistic?
t = (x-bar − μ₀)/(s/√n) = (40 − 45)/(12/√36) = −5/(12/6) = −5/2 = −2.50. The negative value indicates the sample mean is below the hypothesized mean.
Example: A study finds the regression equation ŷ = 2.4 + 0.8x, where x is hours of study and y is exam score. Given r = 0.75, sᵧ = 12, and sₓ = 10, verify the slope using the formula b = r(sᵧ/sₓ). Which statement is correct?
b = r(sᵧ/sₓ) = 0.75(12/10) = 0.75 × 1.2 = 0.90. Since 0.90 does not equal 0.80, the slope and the given statistics are inconsistent.

--- Lesson 10.4: Real-World Applications (medicine, economics, sports) (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Real-World Applications (medicine, economics, sports)
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is relative risk in epidemiology?
Relative risk (RR) = P(disease|exposed)/P(disease|unexposed). An RR of 2 means the exposed group is twice as likely to develop the disease.
What is expected goals (xG) in soccer analytics?
xG uses logistic regression on historical shot data to assign each shot a probability of resulting in a goal.
What is econometrics?
Econometrics bridges statistics and economics, using data to estimate models, test theories, and make predictions.
What is survival analysis?
Survival analysis models the time until a specific event occurs and identifies factors that affect the timing of that event.
What is process capability analysis in manufacturing?
Process capability compares the natural variation of a process to the specification limits to assess whether the process can consistently meet requirements.
What is statistical surveillance in public health?
Statistical surveillance methods analyze patterns in disease data to detect anomalies that may signal an emerging outbreak.
What is the most important skill in AP Statistics?
Conceptual understanding and contextual interpretation are valued most highly on the AP exam and in real-world applications.
A pharmaceutical company tests a new blood pressure medication. The treatment group (n = 150) has a mean reduction of 12 mmHg with s = 8, while the placebo group (n = 150) has a mean reduction of 4 mmHg with s = 7. What is the estimated difference in mean reduction and which procedure should be used?
Difference = 12 − 4 = 8 mmHg. Two independent groups with quantitative outcomes require a two-sample t-test. A paired test would only apply if the same subjects were in both groups.
An economist models housing prices using multiple regression: Price = 50,000 + 120(sqft) + 15,000(bedrooms) − 2,000(age). A house has 1,500 sqft, 3 bedrooms, and is 20 years old. What is the predicted price?
Price = 50,000 + 120(1,500) + 15,000(3) − 2,000(20) = 50,000 + 180,000 + 45,000 − 40,000 = $235,000.
Real-World Applications
Example: What is expected goals (xG) in soccer analytics?

  Key Concepts: Capstone Project: Statistical Analysis of a Dataset
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a confounding variable?
Confounders create spurious associations or mask true effects because they are related to both the explanatory and response variables.
What is the purpose of proper sampling in a capstone project?
Representative sampling is the foundation of valid statistical inference, allowing conclusions to extend beyond the sample.
What is the role of a confidence interval in a capstone project?
CIs quantify the precision of the estimate, showing a plausible range for the true parameter value.
What is the purpose of reflection at the end of a capstone project?
Reflection deepens learning by encouraging you to consider what worked, what was challenging, and how you would improve.

Two categorical variables in a two-way table call for a chi-square test of independence. The key condition is that all expected cell counts are at least 5.
A student collects data on sleep hours and GPA for 80 students. After finding a significant positive correlation (r = 0.45, p = 0.002), she writes: 'Getting more sleep causes higher GPAs.' What is wrong with this conclusion?
Observational studies cannot establish causation because confounding variables may be responsible for the observed association.
Outliers and Resistant Measures
Transparent handling of outliers includes investigating the cause, reporting results with and without the value, and discussing the impact in the limitations section.
