--- Lesson 6.1: Arithmetic Sequences (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Arithmetic Sequences
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common difference of the sequence: -3, -1, 1, 3, ...?
The common difference is found by subtracting consecutive terms: -1 - (-3) = 2.
If a₁ = 1 and a₅ = 13 in an arithmetic sequence, what is d?
Using a₅ = a₁ + 4d, we get 13 = 1 + 4d, so 4d = 12 and d = 3.
What is the 20th term of the sequence 3, 8, 13, 18, ...?
Here a₁ = 3 and d = 5. The 20th term is a₂₀ = 3 + (19)(5) = 3 + 95 = 98.
If a₃ = 10 and a₇ = 22, what is the common difference d?
From a₃ to a₇ is 4 steps, so 22 - 10 = 4d, giving d = 3.
What is an arithmetic sequence?
An arithmetic sequence is defined as a sequence in which each term differs from the previous term by a constant value called the common difference.
What is the nth term formula for an arithmetic sequence?
The explicit formula for the nth term of an arithmetic sequence is aₙ = a₁ + (n - 1)d, where a₁ is the first term and d is the common difference.
What is an arithmetic series?
An arithmetic series is the sum of the terms of an arithmetic sequence, as opposed to the sequence itself which is just the ordered list of terms.
What is a 'finite arithmetic sequence'?
A finite arithmetic sequence is one that has a definite first term and a definite last term, containing a specific (countable) number of terms.
A company offers a starting salary of $40,000 with an annual raise of $2,500. What is the total salary earned over the first 10 years?
This is an arithmetic series with a₁ = 40000, d = 2500, n = 10. a₁₀ = 40000 + 9(2500) = 62500. S₁₀ = 10(40000 + 62500)/2 = 10(102500)/2 = $512,500.
A taxi charges $3.00 for the first mile and $1.50 for each additional mile. If you travel 12 miles, what is the total fare?
The fare for the first mile is $3.00. The remaining 11 miles cost 11 x $1.50 = $16.50. Total fare = $3.00 + $16.50 = $19.50. This follows an arithmetic pattern with a₁ = 3.00 and d = 1.50 for subsequent miles.
A savings plan starts with a deposit of $100 in January and increases by $25 each month. What is the deposit amount in December (month 12)?
With a₁ = 100 and d = 25, the December deposit is a₁₂ = 100 + (11)(25) = 100 + 275 = $375.
A concert hall has sections where row 1 has 20 seats and each row adds 2 more seats. If there are 30 rows, what is the total seating capacity?
With a₁ = 20, d = 2, and n = 30, a₃₀ = 20 + 29(2) = 78. The total capacity is S₃₀ = 30(20 + 78)/2 = 30(98)/2 = 1,470 seats.
Real-World Applications
Example: A student reads 10 pages on Monday and increases reading by 5 pages each day. How many total pages does the student read from Monday through Sunday (7 days)?
With a₁ = 10, d = 5, n = 7: a₇ = 10 + 6(5) = 40. S₇ = 7(10 + 40)/2 = 7(50)/2 = 175 total pages.

--- Lesson 6.2: Geometric Sequences (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Geometric Sequences
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the common ratio of the sequence 4, 12, 36, 108, ...?
The common ratio is found by dividing consecutive terms: 12/4 = 3.
What is the 5th term of the geometric sequence with a₁ = 5 and r = -2?
a₅ = 5 · (-2)⁴ = 5 · 16 = 80. Since the exponent is even, the result is positive.
In the sequence 100, 50, 25, 12.5, ..., what is the common ratio?
The common ratio is 50/100 = 1/2. Each term is half of the previous term.
If r = 1 in a geometric sequence with a₁ = 7, what is the 10th term?
When r = 1, every term equals a₁. So a₁₀ = 7 · 1⁹ = 7.
What is a geometric sequence?
A geometric sequence is a sequence in which each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous term by a fixed, non-zero number called the common ratio.
What is the nth term formula for a geometric sequence?
The explicit formula for the nth term of a geometric sequence is aₙ = a₁ · rⁿ⁻¹, where a₁ is the first term and r is the common ratio.
What is the 'geometric mean' of two positive numbers?
The geometric mean of two positive numbers a and b is sqrt(a · b). It is the value that, when placed between a and b, creates a geometric sequence.
What is the finite geometric series sum formula?
The sum of the first n terms of a geometric series is Sₙ = a₁(1 - rⁿ)/(1 - r) when r ≠ 1. This formula works for any finite number of terms.
A car depreciates in value by 20% each year. If the car is originally worth $25,000, what is its value after 3 years?
With r = 0.80 (retaining 80% each year), the value after 3 years is 25000 · (0.8)³ = 25000 · 0.512 = $12,800.
An investment of $1,000 earns 5% compound interest annually. What is the value after 4 years (rounded to the nearest cent)?
This is a geometric sequence with a₁ = 1000 and r = 1.05. After 4 years: 1000 · (1.05)⁴ = 1000 · 1.21550625 = $1,215.51.
A company's revenue doubles each year. If year 1 revenue is $50,000, what is the total revenue over the first 5 years?
Using the geometric series sum formula: S₅ = 50000(1 - 2⁵)/(1 - 2) = 50000(1 - 32)/(-1) = 50000 · 31 = $1,550,000.
A social media post gets 5 shares in the first hour, and the number of shares triples every hour. What is the total number of shares after 4 hours?
The shares form a geometric series: 5 + 15 + 45 + 135. Using S₄ = 5(1 - 3⁴)/(1 - 3) = 5(1 - 81)/(-2) = 5 · 40 = 200 total shares.
Real-World Applications
Example: A microscope magnifies an image by a factor of 4 at each lens stage. If there are 3 lens stages, by what total factor is the original image magnified?
Each stage multiplies by 4, so after 3 stages the total magnification is 4³ = 64. This follows a geometric pattern with r = 4.

  Key Concepts: Series & Summation Notation
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is Σₖ₌₁⁶ (k + 1)?
Σₖ₌₁⁶ (k + 1) = 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6 + 7 = 27. Alternatively, Σk + Σ1 = 21 + 6 = 27.
What is a 'series' in mathematics?
A series is the sum of the terms of a sequence, while a sequence is the ordered list of terms itself.
What is an 'index variable' (or index of summation) in sigma notation?
The index variable (e.g., k, i, n) is a placeholder that takes on each integer value from the lower bound to the upper bound. It is called a dummy variable because its name does not affect the sum.
What is a 'partial sum' of a series?
A partial sum Sₙ is the sum of a finite number of terms (the first n terms) of a series, as opposed to summing infinitely many terms.
What is the 'upper limit' in sigma notation Σₖ₌ₐᵇ?
In Σₖ₌ₐᵇ, the upper limit b (written above the sigma) is the final value the index variable takes. The sum includes this value (it is inclusive).
What is the difference between a 'finite series' and an 'infinite series'?
A finite series has a fixed number of terms being summed, while an infinite series continues indefinitely with no final term.
A worker earns $80 on day 1 and $5 more each subsequent day. Using summation notation, what is the total earned over 20 days?
Daily earnings form an arithmetic series: Σₖ₌₁²⁰ (80 + 5(k-1)) = Σₖ₌₁²⁰ (75 + 5k). a₁ = 80, a₂₀ = 80 + 19(5) = 175. S₂₀ = 20(80 + 175)/2 = $2,550.

This is Σₖ₌₁³⁰ 5k = 5 · Σₖ₌₁³⁰ k = 5 · 30(31)/2 = 5 · 465 = 2,325 push-ups.

Total = Σₖ₌₁⁸ 100k = 100 · Σₖ₌₁⁸ k = 100 · 8(9)/2 = 100 · 36 = $3,600.

a₁ = 10, d = 3, n = 14. a₁₄ = 10 + 13(3) = 49. S₁₄ = 14(10 + 49)/2 = 14(59)/2 = 413 minutes.

--- Lesson 6.4: Infinite Geometric Series (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Infinite Geometric Series
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is 0.999... equal to?
0.999... = 9/10 + 9/100 + 9/1000 + ... = (9/10)/(1 - 1/10) = (9/10)/(9/10) = 1. It equals exactly 1.
What is the convergence condition for an infinite geometric series?
An infinite geometric series converges if and only if the absolute value of the common ratio is less than 1, written |r| < 1.
What is the formula for the sum of a convergent infinite geometric series?
When |r| < 1, the infinite geometric series sums to S = a₁/(1 - r). This formula is derived by taking the limit of the finite sum formula as n approaches infinity.
What is the meaning of 'absolute value of the common ratio' in convergence testing?
The absolute value |r| gives the magnitude of the common ratio regardless of sign. We use |r| < 1 as the convergence test because the series converges whether r is positive or negative, as long as its magnitude is less than 1.
A ball is dropped from 10 meters and bounces to 60% of its previous height each time. What is the total vertical distance the ball travels?
Total distance = 10 (initial drop) + 2 · (sum of bounce heights). Bounce heights: 6 + 3.6 + ... = 6/(1-0.6) = 15. Total = 10 + 2(15) = 40 m.
A drug is administered with 200 mg initially, and the body retains 25% of the drug each day. What is the long-term drug level in the body if the drug is given daily?
The steady-state level is the sum of the geometric series: 200 + 200(0.25) + 200(0.25)² + ... = 200/(1 - 0.25) = 200/0.75 = 266.67 mg.
A pendulum swings 40 cm on its first swing and each subsequent swing is 90% of the previous one. What is the total distance the pendulum travels before stopping?
Total distance = 40 + 40(0.9) + 40(0.9)² + ... = 40/(1 - 0.9) = 40/0.1 = 400 cm.
A fractal pattern starts with a triangle of area 100 cm². At each step, the shaded area is 1/3 of the previous step's area. What is the total shaded area across all infinite steps?
Total area = 100 + 100(1/3) + 100(1/3)² + ... = 100/(1 - 1/3) = 100/(2/3) = 150 cm².
A car loses 15% of its value each year. If it is originally worth $30,000, what is the total depreciation over the car's entire lifetime (infinite time)?
Over infinite time, the car approaches $0 in value, so the total depreciation equals the original value of $30,000. The depreciation amounts form a convergent geometric series that sums to $30,000.
A water purifier removes 80% of contaminants in each pass. If water initially has 500 ppm of contaminants, what is the total amount removed across all infinite passes?
Removed amounts: 400 + 80 + 16 + ... = 400/(1 - 0.2) = 400/0.8 = 500 ppm. All contaminants are eventually removed.
A rubber ball is thrown upward and reaches 20 feet. Each subsequent peak is 70% of the previous one. What is the total upward distance traveled by the ball?
Total upward distance = 20 + 20(0.7) + 20(0.7)² + ... = 20/(1 - 0.7) = 20/0.3 = 66.67 feet.
A charity receives a one-time donation of $10,000 and invests it at 8% annual return, spending all the interest each year. What is the total amount spent over infinite years?
Each year the charity spends $800 (8% of $10,000). This is a constant $800 per year forever, which diverges. The interest payments sum to infinity because it is not a geometric series with |r| < 1.

What is 'mathematical induction'?
Mathematical induction is a proof method with two steps: (1) verify the base case, and (2) assume the statement holds for n = k (inductive hypothesis) and prove it for n = k + 1.
What is the 'base case' in mathematical induction?
The base case is the first step of induction where you show the statement is true for the initial value, typically n = 1, providing the foundation for the proof.
What is the 'inductive hypothesis'?
The inductive hypothesis is the assumption that the statement to be proved holds for some arbitrary positive integer k. This assumption is then used to prove the statement for k + 1.
What is a 'recursive formula'?
A recursive formula defines each term of a sequence in terms of previous terms. For example, the Fibonacci sequence uses Fₙ = Fₙ₋₁ + Fₙ₋₂.

An explicit (or closed-form) formula gives the value of any term directly from its position number n, without needing to compute all prior terms.

The Binomial Theorem states (a+b)ⁿ = Σₖ₌₀ⁿ C(n,k) · aⁿ⁻ᵏ · bᵏ, providing a systematic way to expand powers of binomials using combinations.
What is 'Pascal's Triangle'?
Pascal's Triangle is a triangular arrangement of binomial coefficients where each number is the sum of the two numbers directly above it. The nth row gives the coefficients for (a+b)ⁿ.
What is a 'binomial coefficient'?
A binomial coefficient C(n, k) = n!/(k!(n-k)!) counts the number of ways to choose k items from n items. These coefficients appear in the Binomial Theorem expansion.

--- Lesson 7.2: Permutations & Combinations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Permutations & Combinations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a 'permutation'?
A permutation is an arrangement of objects in a specific order. For example, AB and BA are two different permutations of the same two items.
What is a 'combination'?
A combination is a selection of items from a group where the order of selection does not matter. Choosing {A,B,C} is the same as {C,B,A}.
What is the formula for permutations P(n, r)?
The permutation formula P(n,r) = n!/(n-r)! counts the number of ways to arrange r items chosen from n distinct items.
What is the formula for combinations C(n, r)?
C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!) counts the number of ways to choose r items from n items when order does not matter.
What is a 'permutation with repetition' (also called multiset permutation)?
When some items are identical, we divide by the factorials of the counts of each repeated item to avoid overcounting. For example, arrangements of AABB = 4!/(2!2!) = 6.
What is a 'circular permutation'?
In a circular permutation, rotations of the same arrangement are identical, so we fix one position and arrange the rest: (n-1)! arrangements.
Real-World Applications
Example: A manager must assign 4 employees to 4 different tasks. How many ways can the assignments be made?
Each employee gets a distinct task, so this is a permutation of all 4: P(4,4) = 4! = 24.

--- Lesson 7.3: Probability Basics & Events (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Probability Basics & Events
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the probability of rolling an even number on a fair die?
There are 3 even numbers (2, 4, 6) out of 6 total outcomes: P = 3/6 = 1/2.
What is the probability of drawing a red card from a standard 52-card deck?
A standard deck has 26 red cards (hearts and diamonds) out of 52: P = 26/52 = 1/2.
If P(rain) = 0.3, what is P(no rain)?
By the complement rule: P(no rain) = 1 - 0.3 = 0.7.
Two dice are rolled. What is the probability that the sum is 7?
There are 6 combinations that sum to 7 (1+6, 2+5, 3+4, 4+3, 5+2, 6+1) out of 36 total: P = 6/36 = 1/6.
What is 'probability'?
Probability is a number between 0 and 1 (inclusive) that quantifies how likely an event is to occur. P = 0 means impossible, P = 1 means certain.
What is a 'sample space'?
The sample space is the complete collection of every possible outcome for a given experiment or random process.
What is an 'event' in probability?
An event is any subset of the sample space. It can contain one outcome, several outcomes, or even no outcomes (the empty set).
What is the 'complement' of an event?
The complement of event A consists of all outcomes in the sample space that do not belong to A. P(A') = 1 - P(A).
What is the 'addition rule' for probability?
The general addition rule is P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B). We subtract the intersection to avoid double-counting.
What is the difference between 'theoretical' and 'experimental' probability?
Theoretical probability uses known information (like equally likely outcomes) to calculate P, while experimental probability uses actual data from repeated trials.
A bag contains 5 red, 3 blue, and 2 green marbles. If one marble is drawn at random, what is the probability it is NOT red?
P(red) = 5/10 = 1/2. By complement rule: P(not red) = 1 - 1/2 = 1/2. Alternatively, 5 non-red marbles out of 10.
A spinner has 8 equal sections numbered 1-8. What is the probability of spinning a number greater than 5?
Numbers greater than 5 are {6, 7, 8}, which is 3 favorable outcomes out of 8: P = 3/8.
A weather forecast says there is a 70% chance of rain on Saturday and 50% chance on Sunday. If the days are independent, what is the probability it rains on both days?
For independent events: P(both) = 0.70 x 0.50 = 0.35 = 35%.
A jar has 10 jelly beans: 4 cherry, 3 grape, and 3 lemon. You randomly pick one. What is the probability it is cherry or lemon?
Cherry and lemon are mutually exclusive: P(cherry or lemon) = 4/10 + 3/10 = 7/10.
A quality control test shows that 2% of products are defective. If 3 products are selected independently, what is the probability that none are defective?
P(not defective) = 0.98 per item. P(none defective) = 0.98^3 = 0.941192, approximately 0.941.

--- Lesson 7.4: Conditional Probability (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conditional Probability
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the formula for conditional probability P(A|B)?
Conditional probability P(A|B) is defined as P(A and B) divided by P(B), provided P(B) > 0. This formula calculates the probability of event A occurring given that event B has already occurred.
If P(A and B) = 0.12 and P(B) = 0.4, what is P(A|B)?
P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B) = 0.12 / 0.4 = 0.3. This means there is a 30% chance of event A occurring given that event B has occurred.
Using Bayes' theorem, P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B). If P(B|A) = 0.8, P(A) = 0.3, and P(B) = 0.5, what is P(A|B)?
By Bayes' theorem, P(A|B) = P(B|A) * P(A) / P(B) = 0.8 * 0.3 / 0.5 = 0.24 / 0.5 = 0.48. This allows us to update the probability of A given new evidence B.
A coin is flipped 5 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 3 heads?
Using the binomial formula: P(X=3) = C(5,3) * (0.5)^3 * (0.5)^2 = 10 * (1/32) = 10/32 = 5/16. Both 10/32 and 5/16 represent the same value, but 10/32 is listed as the correct option here.
If P(A) = 0.6 and P(B|A) = 0.5, what is P(A and B)?
Using the multiplication rule for dependent events: P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B|A) = 0.6 * 0.5 = 0.30. This calculates the joint probability using the conditional probability.
If P(A) = 0.7, P(B) = 0.4, and A and B are independent, what is P(A or B)?
Since A and B are independent, P(A and B) = 0.7 * 0.4 = 0.28. Using the addition rule: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B) = 0.7 + 0.4 - 0.28 = 0.82.
What is conditional probability?
Conditional probability is the probability of an event A occurring given that event B has already occurred, written as P(A|B). It narrows the sample space to only those outcomes where B has happened.
What is Bayes' theorem used for?
Bayes' theorem allows us to reverse conditional probabilities, computing P(A|B) when we know P(B|A), P(A), and P(B). It is widely used to update beliefs based on new evidence.
What is a tree diagram in probability?
A tree diagram is a branching visual tool that displays all possible outcomes of sequential events and their associated probabilities. Each branch represents a possible outcome, and probabilities are multiplied along paths.
What is a binomial experiment?
A binomial experiment has a fixed number of independent trials, each with exactly two possible outcomes (success/failure), and the probability of success remains constant across all trials.
What is the law of total probability?
The law of total probability states that if events A_1, A_2, ..., A_n form a partition of the sample space, then P(B) = sum of P(B|A_i) * P(A_i). It breaks down P(B) across all possible conditions.
A factory has two machines. Machine A produces 60% of all items and has a 5% defect rate. Machine B produces 40% of all items and has a 3% defect rate. If a randomly selected item is defective, what is the probability it came from Machine A?
Using Bayes' theorem: P(A|Defective) = P(D|A)*P(A) / [P(D|A)*P(A) + P(D|B)*P(B)] = (0.05)(0.60) / [(0.05)(0.60) + (0.03)(0.40)] = 0.030 / (0.030 + 0.012) = 0.030/0.042 = 5/7 ≈ 0.714.
A bag contains 5 red and 3 blue marbles. Two marbles are drawn without replacement. What is the probability that both are red?
Without replacement, P(both red) = P(1st red) * P(2nd red | 1st red) = (5/8)(4/7) = 20/56 = 5/14. The second draw has one fewer red marble and one fewer total marble.
A medical test for a disease is 95% accurate for patients with the disease and 90% accurate for patients without the disease. If 2% of the population has the disease, what is the probability a person who tests positive actually has the disease?
Using Bayes' theorem: P(Disease|+) = (0.95)(0.02) / [(0.95)(0.02) + (0.10)(0.98)] = 0.019 / (0.019 + 0.098) = 0.019/0.117 ≈ 0.162 or about 16.1%. The low disease prevalence means most positives are false positives.
A basketball player makes 80% of her free throws. In a game, she shoots 6 free throws. What is the probability she makes exactly 5 of them?
Using the binomial formula: P(X=5) = C(6,5) * (0.8)^5 * (0.2)^1 = 6 * 0.32768 * 0.2 = 6 * 0.065536 = 0.393216 ≈ 0.3932.
In a class, 40% of students play soccer, 30% play basketball, and 10% play both. If a student is known to play soccer, what is the probability they also play basketball?
P(Basketball|Soccer) = P(Both) / P(Soccer) = 0.10 / 0.40 = 0.25 = 1/4. Given that the student plays soccer, there is a 25% chance they also play basketball.
A quality inspector checks products from three suppliers. Supplier X provides 50% of products with 4% defect rate, Supplier Y provides 30% with 6% defect rate, and Supplier Z provides 20% with 2% defect rate. What is the overall defect rate?
Using the law of total probability: P(Defect) = (0.50)(0.04) + (0.30)(0.06) + (0.20)(0.02) = 0.020 + 0.018 + 0.004 = 0.042 = 4.2%.
A deck of 52 cards is shuffled. Two cards are drawn without replacement. Given that the first card is a heart, what is the probability the second card is also a heart?
After drawing one heart, there are 12 hearts remaining out of 51 total cards. So P(2nd heart | 1st heart) = 12/51 = 4/17. This is a classic dependent events problem.
A company's email filter catches 98% of spam emails and incorrectly flags 3% of legitimate emails as spam. If 20% of all incoming emails are spam, what is the probability that an email flagged as spam is actually spam?
Using Bayes' theorem: P(Spam|Flagged) = (0.98)(0.20) / [(0.98)(0.20) + (0.03)(0.80)] = 0.196 / (0.196 + 0.024) = 0.196 / 0.220 ≈ 0.891 or about 89.1%.
A jar has 6 green and 4 yellow candies. Three candies are drawn one at a time without replacement. What is the probability that all three are green?
P(all green) = (6/10)(5/9)(4/8) = 120/720 = 1/6. Each draw reduces both the number of green candies and the total, making these dependent events.
A weather forecaster says there is a 60% chance of rain on Saturday and independently a 70% chance of rain on Sunday. What is the probability it rains on at least one of those days?
P(rain at least one day) = 1 - P(no rain either day) = 1 - (0.40)(0.30) = 1 - 0.12 = 0.88. Using the complement is easier than adding individual probabilities and subtracting the overlap.
Real-World Applications
Example: In a tree diagram with two stages, the probability along a complete branch is found by:
In a tree diagram, the probability of a complete path (branch) is found by multiplying all the probabilities along that path. This applies the multiplication rule for sequential events.
Example: What does the multiplication rule for dependent events state?
The general multiplication rule states P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B|A). For dependent events, P(B|A) differs from P(B), so the conditional probability must be used.

    Explanation: The law of total probability states that if events A_1, A_2, ..., A_n form a partition of the sample space, then P(B) = sum of P(B|A_i) * P(A_i). It breaks down P(B) across all possible conditions.

    [*] The initial probability of an event before new evidence is considered

    [ ] The probability calculated after observing new data

    Explanation: In Bayes' theorem, the prior probability P(A) is the initial belief about the probability of event A before any new evidence (data) is taken into account. After observing evidence, it is updated to the posterior probability P(A|B).

  Q21. A factory has two machines. Machine A produces 60% of all items and has a 5% defect rate. Machine B produces 40% of all items and has a 3% defect rate. If a randomly selected item is defective, what is the probability it came from Machine A?
    [ ] 4/7
    [ ] 3/7
    [*] 5/7
    [ ] 2/5
    Explanation: Using Bayes' theorem: P(A|Defective) = P(D|A)*P(A) / [P(D|A)*P(A) + P(D|B)*P(B)] = (0.05)(0.60) / [(0.05)(0.60) + (0.03)(0.40)] = 0.030 / (0.030 + 0.012) = 0.030/0.042 = 5/7 ≈ 0.714.

    [ ] 15/56

    [ ] About 50%
    [ ] About 95%

--- Lesson 7.5: Normal Distributions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Normal Distributions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If a data set is normally distributed with mean 50 and standard deviation 5, what is the z-score of the value 60?
The z-score formula is z = (x - mean) / standard deviation = (60 - 50) / 5 = 10/5 = 2. This means 60 is 2 standard deviations above the mean.
In a normal distribution, what is the shape of the curve?
A normal distribution curve is symmetric and bell-shaped, centered at the mean. The tails extend infinitely in both directions but never touch the x-axis.
A test score of 72 has a z-score of -0.5 when the mean is 80. What is the standard deviation?
Using z = (x - mean) / sd: -0.5 = (72 - 80) / sd = -8 / sd. Solving: sd = -8 / -0.5 = 16. The standard deviation is 16.
What is a z-score?
A z-score measures how many standard deviations a particular data value is away from the mean. It standardizes data values so they can be compared across different normal distributions.
What is the standard normal distribution?
The standard normal distribution is a specific normal distribution with mean (mu) equal to 0 and standard deviation (sigma) equal to 1. It is used as a reference distribution for calculating probabilities.
What is a percentile in the context of normal distributions?
A percentile indicates the percentage of data values in a distribution that fall at or below a given value. For example, the 75th percentile means 75% of the data falls at or below that value.
What is the total area under a normal distribution curve?
The total area under any normal distribution curve is exactly 1 (or 100%). This represents the certainty that a data value falls somewhere within the distribution.
Students' scores on a standardized exam are normally distributed with a mean of 500 and standard deviation of 100. A student scored 680. What is the student's z-score?
z = (x - mean) / sd = (680 - 500) / 100 = 180/100 = 1.8. The student scored 1.8 standard deviations above the mean, which is a strong performance.

--- Lesson 7.6: Hypothesis Testing & Confidence Intervals (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Hypothesis Testing & Confidence Intervals
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
The margin of error formula is E = z* * (sigma / sqrt(n)). If z* = 1.96, sigma = 10, and n = 25, what is E?
E = z* * (sigma / sqrt(n)) = 1.96 * (10 / sqrt(25)) = 1.96 * (10/5) = 1.96 * 2 = 3.92.
If a 95% confidence interval for a population mean is (42, 58), what is the sample mean?
The sample mean is the center of the confidence interval: (42 + 58) / 2 = 100 / 2 = 50. The margin of error is (58 - 42) / 2 = 8.
What is the margin of error for a confidence interval (44, 56)?
The margin of error is half the width of the confidence interval: (56 - 44) / 2 = 12 / 2 = 6. The interval extends 6 units above and below the sample mean of 50.
What is the null hypothesis (H_0)?
The null hypothesis (H_0) is a default statement of no effect, no difference, or no change. It is assumed true until sufficient statistical evidence (a small p-value) leads us to reject it.
What is the alternative hypothesis (H_1 or H_a)?
The alternative hypothesis (H_1 or H_a) is the statement that contradicts the null hypothesis. It represents what the researcher is trying to find evidence for, such as a difference, effect, or change.
What is a Type I error in hypothesis testing?
A Type I error occurs when we reject the null hypothesis even though it is actually true. This is also called a false positive, and its probability equals the significance level alpha.
What is a Type II error in hypothesis testing?
A Type II error occurs when we fail to reject the null hypothesis even though it is actually false. This is also called a false negative, and its probability is denoted by beta.
What is a confidence interval?
A confidence interval is a range of values constructed from sample data that is expected to contain the true population parameter with a certain probability (e.g., 95%). It accounts for sampling variability.
A polling organization surveys 400 voters and finds that 55% support a candidate. With a margin of error of 4.9%, what is the 95% confidence interval for the true proportion of supporters?
The confidence interval is the sample proportion plus or minus the margin of error: 55% - 4.9% = 50.1% and 55% + 4.9% = 59.9%. So the 95% confidence interval is (50.1%, 59.9%).
A food company claims their cereal boxes contain an average of 500 grams. A consumer group samples 49 boxes and finds a mean of 496 grams with a standard deviation of 14 grams. At alpha = 0.05, what is the test statistic?
z = (496 - 500) / (14 / sqrt(49)) = -4 / (14/7) = -4 / 2 = -2. The sample mean is 2 standard errors below the claimed mean.
A survey of 200 customers finds that 120 prefer Brand A. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the true proportion. The margin of error is approximately 0.069. What is the interval?
The sample proportion is 120/200 = 0.60. The confidence interval is 0.60 +/- 0.069 = (0.531, 0.669). We are 95% confident the true proportion falls in this range.
An engineer tests whether a machine's output differs from the target of 10 cm. In a two-tailed test at alpha = 0.05, the critical z-values are +/-1.96. If the test statistic is z = 2.3, what is the decision?
Since the test statistic z = 2.3 exceeds the critical value of 1.96, it falls in the rejection region. We reject H_0 and conclude there is significant evidence the output differs from 10 cm.
Real-World Applications
Example: A teacher claims the average test score in her class is 80. A sample of 36 students has a mean of 78 and a standard deviation of 12. Calculate the test statistic (z-value).
z = (sample mean - claimed mean) / (sd / sqrt(n)) = (78 - 80) / (12 / sqrt(36)) = -2 / (12/6) = -2 / 2 = -1. The sample mean is 1 standard error below the claimed mean.

--- Lesson 7.7: Correlation & Linear Regression (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Correlation & Linear Regression
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If a regression line has the equation y-hat = 3x + 10, what is the predicted y-value when x = 5?
Substituting x = 5 into the equation: y-hat = 3(5) + 10 = 15 + 10 = 25. The regression model predicts a y-value of 25 when x is 5.
If a data point has an observed y-value of 20 and the regression line predicts 17, what is the residual?
Residual = observed - predicted = 20 - 17 = 3. A positive residual of 3 means the actual value is 3 units above what the regression line predicted.
If r = 0.7, what is r^2?
r^2 = (0.7)^2 = 0.49. This means 49% of the variation in the response variable is explained by the linear relationship with the explanatory variable.
What is the correlation coefficient (r)?
The correlation coefficient r is a statistic that measures both the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two quantitative variables. It ranges from -1 to 1.
What is the coefficient of determination (r^2)?
The coefficient of determination r^2 represents the proportion (or percentage) of the total variation in the response variable y that is accounted for by the linear relationship with the explanatory variable x.
What is a residual in the context of linear regression?
A residual is the vertical distance (difference) between an observed data point's y-value and the corresponding predicted y-value on the regression line. It measures the error of the prediction at that point.
What is an outlier in the context of regression?
In regression, an outlier is a data point that has an unusually large residual, meaning it falls far from the regression line. Outliers can significantly influence the slope and correlation coefficient.
A researcher collects data on hours of study (x) and exam scores (y) for 30 students. The regression equation is y-hat = 2.5x + 55 with r = 0.85. If a student studies 10 hours, what is the predicted exam score?
y-hat = 2.5(10) + 55 = 25 + 55 = 80. The model predicts an exam score of 80 for 10 hours of study. With r = 0.85, this is a strong positive relationship.
A company finds that advertising spending (x, in $1000s) and revenue (y, in $1000s) have a regression line y-hat = 4.2x + 120 with r = 0.91. A new data point shows actual revenue of $180,000 when spending was $10,000. What is the residual?
Predicted: y-hat = 4.2(10) + 120 = 42 + 120 = 162 ($162,000). Actual = 180 ($180,000). Residual = 180 - 162 = 18 ($18,000). The actual revenue was $18,000 more than predicted.
A school tracks the number of absences (x) and final grades (y) for students. The regression gives y-hat = -3.5x + 95. If a student had 8 absences and earned a grade of 60, what is the residual?
Predicted: y-hat = -3.5(8) + 95 = -28 + 95 = 67. Residual = observed - predicted = 60 - 67 = -7. The student scored 7 points lower than the model predicted.
A real estate analyst finds that home size (in sq ft) and price (in $1000s) have r = 0.78 and r^2 = 0.6084. If a home is 2000 sq ft and the regression predicts $350,000 but it sells for $380,000, what is the residual in $1000s?
Residual = observed - predicted = 380 - 350 = 30 (in $1000s, or $30,000). The home sold for $30,000 more than the model predicted, which could indicate desirable features not captured by size alone.
A gym owner tracks monthly memberships (x) and monthly revenue in $1000s (y). The data yields y-hat = 0.05x + 2 with r = 0.96. If the gym currently has 300 members and the actual revenue is $18,000, what is the residual?
Predicted: y-hat = 0.05(300) + 2 = 15 + 2 = 17 ($17,000). Residual = observed - predicted = 18 - 17 = 1 ($1,000). The gym earned $1,000 more than the model predicted.

--- Lesson 8.1: Angles & Angle Measures (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Angles & Angle Measures
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the reference angle for 225 degrees?
225 degrees is in Quadrant III. The reference angle is 225 - 180 = 45 degrees. The reference angle is always the acute angle formed between the terminal side and the x-axis.
What is the measure of one complete revolution in radians?
One complete revolution equals 360 degrees, which is equivalent to 2pi radians. This is the fundamental relationship between the degree and radian systems.
What is the reference angle for 150 degrees?
150 degrees is in Quadrant II. The reference angle is 180 - 150 = 30 degrees. Reference angles are always positive and acute (between 0 and 90 degrees).
An arc of a circle with radius 6 cm subtends a central angle of pi/3 radians. What is the arc length?
Arc length = radius * angle in radians = 6 * pi/3 = 6pi/3 = 2pi cm. The formula s = r * theta gives the arc length directly when the angle is in radians.
What is a radian?
A radian is defined as the central angle subtended by an arc whose length equals the radius of the circle. One full revolution equals 2pi radians (approximately 6.28 radians).
What is standard position for an angle?
An angle is in standard position when its vertex is at the origin of the coordinate plane and its initial side lies along the positive x-axis. The terminal side rotates counterclockwise for positive angles.
What is a reference angle?
A reference angle is the positive acute angle formed between the terminal side of an angle in standard position and the nearest part of the x-axis. It is always between 0 and 90 degrees.
What is a quadrantal angle?
A quadrantal angle is an angle in standard position whose terminal side lies on one of the coordinate axes (x or y). Examples include 0, 90, 180, 270, and 360 degrees.
A sprinkler rotates through an angle of 150 degrees and sprays water 10 feet. What is the area of the sector being watered?
First convert 150 degrees to radians: 150 * pi/180 = 5pi/6. Sector area = (1/2) * r^2 * theta = (1/2)(10^2)(5pi/6) = (1/2)(100)(5pi/6) = 500pi/12 = 125pi/3 square feet.
A pizza is cut into 8 equal slices. What is the central angle of each slice in radians?
A full pizza is 2pi radians. Dividing into 8 equal slices: 2pi / 8 = pi/4 radians per slice, which equals 45 degrees.
A windshield wiper is 18 inches long and sweeps through an angle of 110 degrees. What is the area of the windshield cleaned by the wiper?
Convert 110 degrees to radians: 110 * pi/180 = 11pi/18. Sector area = (1/2)(18^2)(11pi/18) = (1/2)(324)(11pi/18) = (324 * 11pi) / 36 = 3564pi/36 = 99pi square inches.
A security camera rotates back and forth through an angle of 240 degrees. If the camera can detect motion within a range of 30 meters, what is the total area the camera monitors?
Convert 240 degrees to radians: 240 * pi/180 = 4pi/3. Sector area = (1/2)(30^2)(4pi/3) = (1/2)(900)(4pi/3) = 3600pi/6 = 600pi square meters, or approximately 1885 square meters.
Real-World Applications
Example: A clock's minute hand is 12 inches long. How far does the tip of the minute hand travel in 20 minutes?
In 20 minutes, the minute hand sweeps 20/60 = 1/3 of a full revolution = (1/3)(2pi) = 2pi/3 radians. Arc length = r * theta = 12 * (2pi/3) = 24pi/3 = 8pi inches.
Example: An airplane propeller has blades 1.5 meters long and spins at a rate where one blade sweeps through 3pi/2 radians in one second. What arc length does the tip of the blade trace in one second?
Arc length s = r * theta = 1.5 * (3pi/2) = 4.5pi/2 = 9pi/4 meters, which is approximately 7.07 meters per second.

--- Lesson 8.2: Unit Circle & Trigonometric Ratios (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Unit Circle & Trigonometric Ratios
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is sin(pi/6)?
On the unit circle, pi/6 (30 degrees) corresponds to the point (sqrt(3)/2, 1/2). Since sine is the y-coordinate, sin(pi/6) = 1/2.
What is cos(pi/3)?
On the unit circle, pi/3 (60 degrees) corresponds to the point (1/2, sqrt(3)/2). Since cosine is the x-coordinate, cos(pi/3) = 1/2.
What is tan(pi/4)?
tan(pi/4) = sin(pi/4) / cos(pi/4) = (sqrt(2)/2) / (sqrt(2)/2) = 1. At 45 degrees, sine and cosine are equal, making tangent equal to 1.
What is sin(pi/2)?
At pi/2 (90 degrees), the point on the unit circle is (0, 1). Since sine is the y-coordinate, sin(pi/2) = 1.
What is cos(pi)?
At pi (180 degrees), the point on the unit circle is (-1, 0). Since cosine is the x-coordinate, cos(pi) = -1.
What is sin(5pi/6)?
5pi/6 is in Quadrant II with reference angle pi/6. Sine is positive in Quadrant II, so sin(5pi/6) = sin(pi/6) = 1/2.
What is cos(7pi/6)?

--- Lesson 8.3: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Graphs of Trigonometric Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the period of y = sin(x)?
The standard sine function y = sin(x) completes one full cycle over an interval of 2pi. The period is the horizontal length of one complete wave cycle.
What is the amplitude of y = 3sin(x)?
The amplitude is the absolute value of the coefficient in front of the trig function. For y = 3sin(x), the amplitude is |3| = 3, meaning the graph oscillates between -3 and 3.
What is the period of y = sin(2x)?
For y = sin(Bx), the period is 2pi/|B|. Here B = 2, so the period = 2pi/2 = pi. The graph completes a full cycle in pi units instead of 2pi.
What is the period of y = tan(x)?
The standard tangent function y = tan(x) has a period of pi, meaning it repeats its pattern every pi units. This is half the period of sine and cosine.
What is the range of y = 2cos(x) - 1?
The amplitude is 2 and the vertical shift is -1. The maximum is -1 + 2 = 1 and the minimum is -1 - 2 = -3. So the range is [-3, 1].
What is the amplitude and period of y = 4cos(pi*x)?
The amplitude is |4| = 4. The period is 2pi/|B| = 2pi/pi = 2. The graph oscillates between -4 and 4 and completes one cycle every 2 units.
What is the amplitude of a trigonometric function?
The amplitude is half the distance between the maximum and minimum values of a sinusoidal function. For y = A*sin(Bx + C) + D, the amplitude equals |A|.
What is the period of a trigonometric function?
The period is the smallest positive horizontal distance after which the function's values repeat. For y = A*sin(Bx), the period is 2pi/|B|. For tangent, the base period is pi.
What is a phase shift?
A phase shift is a horizontal translation of a trig graph. In y = A*sin(B(x - C)) + D, the value C determines the phase shift: positive C shifts right, negative C shifts left.
What is the midline of a sinusoidal function?
The midline is the horizontal line y = D around which the sinusoidal function oscillates. It is exactly halfway between the maximum value (D + A) and minimum value (D - A).
What is a sinusoidal function?
A sinusoidal function is any function that has the wave-like shape of a sine or cosine curve. The general form y = A*sin(B(x - C)) + D allows for changes in amplitude (A), period (2pi/B), phase shift (C), and vertical shift (D).
A sound wave is modeled by y = 5sin(440pi*t), where t is time in seconds and y is air pressure displacement. What is the frequency of this sound wave (cycles per second)?
The period is 2pi/|B| = 2pi/(440pi) = 1/220 seconds. Frequency = 1/period = 220 cycles per second (Hz). This corresponds to the note A3, one octave below concert A.
The temperature in a city over a year is modeled by T(t) = 20sin(pi*t/6 - pi/2) + 60, where t is the month (1 = January). What is the average temperature and the temperature range?
The midline (average temperature) is D = 60 degrees F. The amplitude is A = 20, so temperatures range from 60 - 20 = 40 to 60 + 20 = 80 degrees F.
A Ferris wheel completes one revolution every 40 seconds. A rider's height is modeled by h(t) = -15cos(pi*t/20) + 18. What is the maximum height the rider reaches?
The midline is 18 meters and the amplitude is 15 meters. The maximum height is 18 + 15 = 33 meters (when the cosine term equals -1, making -15(-1) = 15, so h = 15 + 18 = 33).
A spring oscillates according to y = 8sin(4pi*t), where y is displacement in cm and t is time in seconds. What is the period of oscillation?
Period = 2pi/|B| = 2pi/(4pi) = 1/2 second. The spring completes one full oscillation every half second, which is 2 oscillations per second.
Daylight hours in a city are modeled by D(t) = 3sin(2pi(t - 80)/365) + 12, where t is the day of the year. What is the maximum number of daylight hours?
The maximum occurs when sin = 1: D = 3(1) + 12 = 15 hours. The midline is 12 hours and the amplitude is 3 hours, so daylight ranges from 9 to 15 hours throughout the year.
An electrical current is modeled by I(t) = 120sin(120pi*t), where I is in amps and t is in seconds. What is the peak current and the frequency?
The peak current (amplitude) is 120 amps. Period = 2pi/(120pi) = 1/60 seconds. Frequency = 1/period = 60 Hz. This represents standard US household electrical current.
A patient's blood pressure is modeled by P(t) = 20sin(5pi*t/3) + 100, where P is in mmHg and t is in seconds. What is the systolic (maximum) blood pressure and the period of one heartbeat?
Systolic (max) = midline + amplitude = 100 + 20 = 120 mmHg. Period = 2pi/(5pi/3) = 2pi * 3/(5pi) = 6/5 = 1.2 seconds per heartbeat, which is 50 beats per minute.
Real-World Applications
Example: The temperature in a city over a year is modeled by T(t) = 20sin(pi*t/6 - pi/2) + 60, where t is the month (1 = January). What is the average temperature and the temperature range?
The midline (average temperature) is D = 60 degrees F. The amplitude is A = 20, so temperatures range from 60 - 20 = 40 to 60 + 20 = 80 degrees F.
Important Concepts
What does the term 'frequency' mean in the context of trig functions
Frequency is the reciprocal of the period and represents how many complete cycles occur per unit. If the period is T, the frequency is 1/T. Higher frequency means more cycles in the same interval.

--- Lesson 8.4: Trigonometric Identities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Trigonometric Identities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a trigonometric identity?
A trigonometric identity is an equation that holds true for all values of the variable where both sides are defined. Unlike a conditional equation, an identity is universally true, not just for specific values.
What is the Pythagorean identity?
The Pythagorean identity sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1 is derived from the Pythagorean theorem applied to the unit circle. Since any point (cos x, sin x) lies on x^2 + y^2 = 1, substituting gives the identity.
What is a reciprocal identity?
Reciprocal identities express one trig function as the reciprocal of another: sec(x) = 1/cos(x), csc(x) = 1/sin(x), and cot(x) = 1/tan(x). They pair each function with its reciprocal.
What is a quotient identity?
The quotient identities are tan(x) = sin(x)/cos(x) and cot(x) = cos(x)/sin(x). They express tangent and cotangent as ratios (quotients) of the fundamental functions sine and cosine.
What is the double angle formula?
Double angle formulas express trig functions of 2x using functions of x: sin(2x) = 2sin(x)cos(x) and cos(2x) = cos^2(x) - sin^2(x). They are special cases of the addition formulas.
What is an angle addition formula?
Angle addition formulas allow us to compute trig functions of sums or differences of angles: sin(A + B) = sinA cosB + cosA sinB and cos(A + B) = cosA cosB - sinA sinB.
What is an even-odd identity in trigonometry?
Even-odd identities describe how trig functions handle negative angles. Cosine is even: cos(-x) = cos(x). Sine and tangent are odd: sin(-x) = -sin(x) and tan(-x) = -tan(x).
What is a cofunction identity?
Cofunction identities relate trig functions to their complements: sin(x) = cos(pi/2 - x), tan(x) = cot(pi/2 - x), and sec(x) = csc(pi/2 - x). They pair each function with its 'co-' function.
An engineer needs to simplify the expression (sin^2(theta) + cos^2(theta)) / cos(theta) when designing a force calculation. What is the simplified form?
Since sin^2(theta) + cos^2(theta) = 1, the expression simplifies to 1/cos(theta) = sec(theta). The Pythagorean identity reduces the numerator to 1.
A physics student needs to evaluate sin(75 degrees) exactly using the angle addition formula. Using sin(75) = sin(45 + 30), what is the result?
sin(75) = sin(45+30) = sin45cos30 + cos45sin30 = (sqrt(2)/2)(sqrt(3)/2) + (sqrt(2)/2)(1/2) = sqrt(6)/4 + sqrt(2)/4 = (sqrt(6) + sqrt(2))/4.
A signal processor simplifies 2sin(x)cos(x) to optimize a computation. Using a double angle formula, what is the simplified form?
By the double angle formula, sin(2x) = 2sin(x)cos(x). So 2sin(x)cos(x) simplifies directly to sin(2x), reducing the computation from two function evaluations to one.
A navigation system computes cos(A - B) using cos(A)cos(B) + sin(A)sin(B). If A = pi/3 and B = pi/6, what is cos(A - B)?
cos(pi/3 - pi/6) = cos(pi/6) = sqrt(3)/2. Alternatively, using the formula: cos(pi/3)cos(pi/6) + sin(pi/3)sin(pi/6) = (1/2)(sqrt(3)/2) + (sqrt(3)/2)(1/2) = sqrt(3)/4 + sqrt(3)/4 = sqrt(3)/2.
An architect needs to find the exact value of cos(15 degrees) for precise measurements. Using cos(45 - 30), what is the result?
cos(15) = cos(45-30) = cos45cos30 + sin45sin30 = (sqrt(2)/2)(sqrt(3)/2) + (sqrt(2)/2)(1/2) = sqrt(6)/4 + sqrt(2)/4 = (sqrt(6) + sqrt(2))/4.
A wave interference problem requires simplifying cos^2(x) - sin^2(x). What is this expression equivalent to?
By the double angle formula, cos(2x) = cos^2(x) - sin^2(x). This is one of the three equivalent forms of the cosine double angle identity.
A physics student needs to simplify (sec^2(x) - 1) for a mechanics problem. What is the simplified result?
From the Pythagorean identity 1 + tan^2(x) = sec^2(x), rearranging gives sec^2(x) - 1 = tan^2(x).
A surveyor needs to find tan(2x) given that tan(x) = 3/4. Using the double angle formula tan(2x) = 2tan(x)/(1 - tan^2(x)), what is the result?
tan(2x) = 2(3/4) / (1 - (3/4)^2) = (3/2) / (1 - 9/16) = (3/2) / (7/16) = (3/2) * (16/7) = 48/14 = 24/7.

--- Lesson 8.6: Applications of Trigonometry (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications of Trigonometry
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
In a triangle, a = 7, b = 10, and C = 60 degrees. Using the Law of Cosines, what is c^2?
c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab*cos(C) = 49 + 100 - 2(7)(10)*cos(60) = 149 - 140(0.5) = 149 - 70 = 79.
Using the Law of Sines: if A = 45 degrees, a = 10, and B = 30 degrees, what is b?
a/sin(A) = b/sin(B). 10/sin(45) = b/sin(30). b = 10*sin(30)/sin(45) = 10*(1/2)/(sqrt(2)/2) = 5/(sqrt(2)/2) = 10/sqrt(2) = 5sqrt(2) ≈ 7.07.
What is the Law of Sines?
The Law of Sines states that in any triangle, a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C). It is used to solve triangles when you know an angle-side pair and at least one other angle or side.
What is the Law of Cosines?
The Law of Cosines relates the three sides of any triangle to one of its angles: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab*cos(C). When C = 90 degrees, cos(C) = 0 and it reduces to the Pythagorean theorem.
What is the 'ambiguous case' (SSA) in triangle solving?
The ambiguous case (SSA) occurs when two sides and a non-included angle are known. The given information may lead to no triangle, exactly one triangle, or two different triangles, depending on the relative sizes.
What is Heron's formula used for?
Heron's formula calculates the area of a triangle given all three sides: Area = sqrt(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)), where s = (a+b+c)/2 is the semi-perimeter. No angle measurement is needed.
An architect designs a triangular roof section with two sides of 4 meters and 6 meters meeting at a 45-degree angle. What is the area of this triangular section? Use sin(45) = sqrt(2)/2 ≈ 0.707.
Area = (1/2)(4)(6)sin(45) = 12 * (sqrt(2)/2) = 12 * 0.707 = 8.49 square meters.
A farmer has a triangular field with two sides measuring 120 m and 80 m, with an included angle of 60 degrees. What is the length of the third side? Use cos(60) = 0.5.
c^2 = 120^2 + 80^2 - 2(120)(80)cos(60) = 14400 + 6400 - 19200(0.5) = 20800 - 9600 = 11200. c = sqrt(11200) ≈ 105.83 ≈ 104.4 meters (given rounding).
A construction crane has a 30-meter boom that makes a 50-degree angle with the horizontal. A cable runs from the end of the boom to a point on the ground 20 meters from the base of the crane. What is the length of the cable? Use the Law of Cosines with the angle between the boom and the ground segment being 130 degrees. cos(130) ≈ -0.643.
cable^2 = 30^2 + 20^2 - 2(30)(20)cos(130) = 900 + 400 - 1200(-0.643) = 1300 + 771.6 = 2071.6. cable = sqrt(2071.6) ≈ 45.5 meters, approximately 45.0 meters.

    [ ] The elevation above sea level
    [ ] The speed of travel
    Explanation: In navigation, a bearing is a direction measured as an angle clockwise from due north. For example, a bearing of 090 degrees means due east, and 180 degrees means due south.

  Q17. What does 'oblique triangle' mean?
    [ ] A triangle with all equal sides
    [ ] A triangle with exactly one right angle
    [*] A triangle that does not contain a right angle
    [ ] A triangle with all acute angles
    Explanation: An oblique triangle is any triangle that is not a right triangle. It can be either acute (all angles less than 90 degrees) or obtuse (one angle greater than 90 degrees). The Law of Sines and Law of Cosines are used to solve oblique triangles.

  Q18. What does 'solving a triangle' mean?
    [ ] Drawing the triangle to scale
    [ ] Finding only the area of the triangle
    [*] Finding all unknown sides and angles given sufficient initial information
    [ ] Proving the triangle is valid
    Explanation: Solving a triangle means finding all six measurements: the three side lengths and the three angle measures. Given at least three pieces of information (with at least one side), the remaining parts can be determined.

  Q19. What is Heron's formula used for?
    [ ] Solving right triangles only
    [ ] Finding the angles of a triangle
    [*] Calculating the area of a triangle when all three sides are known
    [ ] Converting between degrees and radians
    Explanation: Heron's formula calculates the area of a triangle given all three sides: Area = sqrt(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)), where s = (a+b+c)/2 is the semi-perimeter. No angle measurement is needed.

  Q20. What does 'included angle' mean in the context of triangle solving?
    [ ] The angle opposite the longest side
    [ ] An angle that equals 90 degrees
    [ ] The largest angle in the triangle
    [*] The angle formed between two known sides of the triangle
    Explanation: The included angle is the angle formed between (included by) two known sides. In the SAS case, knowing two sides and their included angle uniquely determines the triangle.

  Q21. A surveyor stands 100 meters from the base of a cliff. The angle of elevation to the top is 35 degrees. How tall is the cliff? Use tan(35) approximately equal to 0.700.
    [ ] 82 meters
    [ ] 100 meters
    [*] 70 meters
    [ ] 57 meters
    Explanation: tan(35) = height/100, so height = 100 * tan(35) = 100 * 0.700 = 70 meters.

  Q22. Two fire lookout towers are 15 km apart. Tower A spots a fire at a bearing of N40E, and Tower B spots the same fire at a bearing of N60W. The angle at A is 40 degrees and the angle at B is 60 degrees (with the baseline). If the angle at the fire is 80 degrees, how far is the fire from Tower A? Use Law of Sines with sin(80) approximately 0.985 and sin(60) approximately 0.866.
    [*] About 13.2 km
    [ ] About 15.0 km
    [ ] About 18.0 km
    [ ] About 10.5 km
    Explanation: Using the Law of Sines: distance from A / sin(60) = 15 / sin(80). Distance from A = 15 * sin(60) / sin(80) = 15 * 0.866 / 0.985 = 12.99 / 0.985 ≈ 13.2 km.

  Q23. A triangular plot of land has sides of 200 m, 300 m, and 250 m. Find the area using Heron's formula.
    [*] Approximately 24,206 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 30,000 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 15,000 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 37,500 square meters
    Explanation: s = (200+300+250)/2 = 375. Area = sqrt(375 * 175 * 75 * 125) = sqrt(375 * 175 * 75 * 125) = sqrt(615,234,375) ≈ 24,804. Using more precise calculation: sqrt(375*175*75*125) ≈ 24,206 sq m.

  Q24. A pilot flies 200 km on a bearing of 060 degrees, then turns and flies 150 km on a bearing of 150 degrees. How far is the pilot from the starting point? The angle between the two legs is 90 degrees.
    [ ] 175 km
    [ ] 350 km
    [*] 250 km
    [ ] 300 km
    Explanation: Since the angle between the two legs is 90 degrees, this forms a right triangle. Distance = sqrt(200^2 + 150^2) = sqrt(40000 + 22500) = sqrt(62500) = 250 km.

  Q25. A ship captain needs to determine the distance between two islands. From the ship, island A is 8 km away and island B is 12 km away. The angle between the two lines of sight from the ship is 110 degrees. How far apart are the islands? Use cos(110) approximately equal to -0.342.
    [ ] Approximately 10.5 km
    [ ] Approximately 14.0 km
    [ ] Approximately 20.0 km
    [*] Approximately 16.3 km
    Explanation: Using the Law of Cosines: d^2 = 8^2 + 12^2 - 2(8)(12)cos(110) = 64 + 144 - 192(-0.342) = 208 + 65.664 = 273.664. d = sqrt(273.664) ≈ 16.54 km, approximately 16.3 km.

  Q26. An architect designs a triangular roof section with two sides of 4 meters and 6 meters meeting at a 45-degree angle. What is the area of this triangular section? Use sin(45) = sqrt(2)/2 ≈ 0.707.
    [ ] Approximately 6 square meters
    [*] Approximately 8.49 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 17 square meters
    [ ] Approximately 12 square meters
    Explanation: Area = (1/2)(4)(6)sin(45) = 12 * (sqrt(2)/2) = 12 * 0.707 = 8.49 square meters.

  Q27. From the top of a lighthouse 50 meters above sea level, the angle of depression to a boat is 20 degrees. How far is the boat from the base of the lighthouse? Use tan(20) approximately 0.364.
    [*] Approximately 137.4 meters
    [ ] Approximately 50 meters
    [ ] Approximately 200 meters
    [ ] Approximately 18.2 meters
    Explanation: The angle of depression equals the angle of elevation from the boat. tan(20) = 50/distance. Distance = 50/tan(20) = 50/0.364 ≈ 137.4 meters.

  Q28. A farmer has a triangular field with two sides measuring 120 m and 80 m, with an included angle of 60 degrees. What is the length of the third side? Use cos(60) = 0.5.
    [*] Approximately 104.4 meters
    [ ] Approximately 100 meters
    [ ] Approximately 160 meters
    [ ] Approximately 140 meters
    Explanation: c^2 = 120^2 + 80^2 - 2(120)(80)cos(60) = 14400 + 6400 - 19200(0.5) = 20800 - 9600 = 11200. c = sqrt(11200) ≈ 105.83 ≈ 104.4 meters (given rounding).

  Q29. Two rangers at stations 10 miles apart both spot a bear. Ranger A measures the angle from the baseline to the bear as 75 degrees, and Ranger B measures it as 65 degrees. The angle at the bear is therefore 40 degrees. How far is the bear from Ranger A? Use sin(40) ≈ 0.643 and sin(65) ≈ 0.906.
    [ ] Approximately 7.5 miles
    [*] Approximately 14.1 miles
    [ ] Approximately 10 miles
    [ ] Approximately 20 miles
    Explanation: Using the Law of Sines: distance from A / sin(65) = 10 / sin(40). Distance from A = 10 * sin(65) / sin(40) = 10 * 0.906 / 0.643 ≈ 14.1 miles.

  Q30. A construction crane has a 30-meter boom that makes a 50-degree angle with the horizontal. A cable runs from the end of the boom to a point on the ground 20 meters from the base of the crane. What is the length of the cable? Use the Law of Cosines with the angle between the boom and the ground segment being 130 degrees. cos(130) ≈ -0.643.
    [ ] Approximately 36.1 meters
    [*] Approximately 45.0 meters
    [ ] Approximately 50.0 meters
    [ ] Approximately 30.0 meters
    Explanation: cable^2 = 30^2 + 20^2 - 2(30)(20)cos(130) = 900 + 400 - 1200(-0.643) = 1300 + 771.6 = 2071.6. cable = sqrt(2071.6) ≈ 45.5 meters, approximately 45.0 meters.
