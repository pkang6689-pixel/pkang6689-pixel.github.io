--- Lesson 9.1: Conic Sections (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Conic Sections
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the standard form equation of a circle with center (2, 3) and radius 5?
The standard form of a circle is (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2, where (h, k) is the center and r is the radius. With center (2, 3) and r = 5: (x - 2)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 25.
What is the vertex of the parabola y = (x - 1)^2 + 4?
In vertex form y = a(x - h)^2 + k, the vertex is (h, k). Here h = 1 and k = 4, so the vertex is (1, 4).
For the ellipse x^2/25 + y^2/9 = 1, what is the length of the major axis?
The larger denominator is 25, so a^2 = 25 and a = 5. The major axis length is 2a = 10. The major axis runs along the x-axis since a^2 is under x^2.
What is the focus of the parabola y = x^2/(4p) when p = 2 and the vertex is at the origin?
For a vertical parabola x^2 = 4py with vertex at the origin, the focus is at (0, p). With p = 2, the focus is at (0, 2), which is 2 units above the vertex.
For the ellipse x^2/25 + y^2/16 = 1, what is the distance from the center to each focus?
For an ellipse, c^2 = a^2 - b^2 = 25 - 16 = 9, so c = 3. The foci are at (+/-3, 0), each 3 units from the center.
What is a conic section?
Conic sections are curves formed by the intersection of a plane with a double-napped cone. Depending on the angle of intersection, the result is a circle, ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola.
What is an ellipse?
An ellipse is defined as the set of all points in a plane such that the sum of the distances from any point on the ellipse to two fixed points (foci) is a constant equal to 2a.
What is a hyperbola?
A hyperbola is the set of all points where the absolute difference of distances from any point to two fixed foci equals a constant (2a). Unlike an ellipse, a hyperbola has two separate branches.
What is the focus of a parabola?
The focus is a fixed point such that any point on the parabola is equidistant from the focus and the directrix (a fixed line). The focus lies on the axis of symmetry, p units from the vertex.
What is the directrix of a parabola?
The directrix is a fixed line perpendicular to the axis of symmetry, located p units from the vertex on the side opposite the focus. Together with the focus, it defines the parabola.
A satellite orbits Earth in an elliptical path. The closest distance to Earth's center is 7,000 km and the farthest is 13,000 km. What is the semi-major axis of the orbit?
The semi-major axis a = (closest + farthest) / 2 = (7000 + 13000) / 2 = 20000 / 2 = 10,000 km. This is the average of the minimum and maximum distances.
A spotlight shines against a flat wall, creating a circular patch of light. If the equation of the circle is (x - 5)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 16, what is the diameter of the lit area?
The radius r = sqrt(16) = 4 units. The diameter is 2r = 8 units. The center of the lit patch is at (5, 3) on the wall.
A bridge has a parabolic arch that spans 40 meters and reaches a maximum height of 10 meters. If the vertex is at the top, write the equation of the arch with the vertex at the origin. What is the value of p?
With vertex at origin opening downward: x^2 = 4py. The arch passes through (20, -10) since half-span is 20 m and it drops 10 m. So 400 = 4p(-10) = -40p, giving p = -10.
A whispering gallery is in the shape of an ellipse where the foci are 30 feet apart and the major axis is 50 feet long. What is the length of the minor axis?
a = 25 (half of 50), c = 15 (half of 30). b^2 = a^2 - c^2 = 625 - 225 = 400, so b = 20. The minor axis length is 2b = 40 feet.
A cooling tower at a power plant has a hyperbolic cross-section given by x^2/400 - y^2/900 = 1 (in meters). What is the minimum width of the tower?
The minimum width occurs at the vertices where y = 0: x^2/400 = 1, so x = +/-20. The minimum width is the distance between vertices = 2(20) = 40 meters.
An elliptical running track has a semi-major axis of 100 meters and a semi-minor axis of 60 meters. What is the approximate perimeter of the track? Use the approximation P ≈ pi * [3(a+b) - sqrt((3a+b)(a+3b))].
Using the Ramanujan approximation: P ≈ pi[3(100+60) - sqrt((300+60)(100+180))] = pi[480 - sqrt(360*280)] = pi[480 - sqrt(100800)] = pi[480 - 317.5] ≈ pi * 162.5 ≈ 510.5, approximately 509 meters.
Two radio stations are 200 miles apart. A ship determines it is 40 miles closer to station A than to station B. The ship lies on a hyperbola with the stations as foci. What is the value of a for this hyperbola?
For a hyperbola, the difference of distances to the foci equals 2a. Since the ship is 40 miles closer to A: |d_B - d_A| = 40 = 2a, so a = 20.
A comet travels in a parabolic path around the sun. At its closest point (vertex), it is 50 million km from the sun (focus). What is the equation of the comet's path if the vertex is at the origin and the sun is on the positive x-axis?
For a parabola opening rightward with vertex at origin: y^2 = 4px, where p = 50 (distance from vertex to focus). y^2 = 4(50)x = 200x (in millions of km).

--- Lesson 9.2: Parametric Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Parametric Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Given x = t^2 and y = t^3, what is y when x = 4?
When x = 4: t^2 = 4, so t = +/-2. When t = 2: y = 8. When t = -2: y = -8. So y = +/-8.
For projectile motion, x = v_0*cos(theta)*t and y = v_0*sin(theta)*t - (1/2)gt^2. If v_0 = 20 m/s, theta = 30 degrees, and g = 10 m/s^2, what is y when t = 1?
y = 20*sin(30)*1 - (1/2)(10)(1)^2 = 20(0.5)(1) - 5 = 10 - 5 = 5 meters.
If x = 4sin(t) and y = 4cos(t), what is the radius of the circle traced?
x^2 + y^2 = 16sin^2(t) + 16cos^2(t) = 16(sin^2(t) + cos^2(t)) = 16. So x^2 + y^2 = 16, which is a circle of radius 4.
What is a 'parameter' in parametric equations?
The parameter is a third variable (usually t, representing time or angle) upon which both x and y depend. As the parameter varies, it traces out a curve in the xy-plane.
What is a 'rectangular equation' compared to a parametric equation?
A rectangular (Cartesian) equation relates x and y directly, such as y = x^2 + 1 or x^2 + y^2 = 25, without using an intermediate parameter t.
What is a 'cycloid' in parametric equations?
A cycloid is the curve traced by a point on the circumference of a circle as it rolls along a straight line. Its parametric equations are x = r(t - sin(t)) and y = r(1 - cos(t)).
A roller coaster track follows x = 10t and y = 5sin(pi*t/2) for 0 <= t <= 4. What is the maximum height of the track?
The maximum of y = 5sin(pi*t/2) occurs when sin(pi*t/2) = 1, giving y_max = 5 units. This happens when pi*t/2 = pi/2, or t = 1.
A water fountain shoots water with velocity 15 m/s at 60 degrees from horizontal. Using x = 15cos(60)t and y = 15sin(60)t - 5t^2, what is the maximum height of the water?
Max height when vertical velocity = 0: 15sin(60) - 10t = 0, so t = 15(sqrt(3)/2)/10 = 15sqrt(3)/20. y_max = 15sin(60)(15sqrt(3)/20) - 5(15sqrt(3)/20)^2 = (225*3/4)/20 - 5(675/400) = 675/40 - 675/80 = 675/80 ≈ 8.44 m.
A ski jumper launches from a ramp and follows a path given by x = 25t and y = 15t - 5t^2 (meters, seconds). What is the horizontal distance traveled before landing?
Landing when y = 0: 15t - 5t^2 = 0, so 5t(3 - t) = 0, giving t = 3 seconds. Horizontal distance: x = 25(3) = 75 meters.

  Q1. Given x = 3t and y = t^2, eliminate the parameter t to find y in terms of x.
    [ ] y = 3x^2
    [ ] y = x/3
    [ ] y = 9x^2
    [*] y = x^2/9
    Explanation: From x = 3t, solve for t: t = x/3. Substitute into y = t^2: y = (x/3)^2 = x^2/9. The parametric curve is a parabola.

  Q2. For the parametric equations x = cos(t) and y = sin(t), the resulting curve is a:
    [ ] Ellipse with a ≠ b
    [ ] Line
    [*] Circle of radius 1
    [ ] Parabola
    Explanation: Since x^2 + y^2 = cos^2(t) + sin^2(t) = 1, the parametric equations trace out a circle of radius 1 centered at the origin.

  Q3. Given x = 2t + 1 and y = 4t - 3, eliminate t. What type of curve is this?
    [*] A line: y = 2x - 5
    [ ] A line: y = 4x - 3
    [ ] A circle
    [ ] A parabola: y = x^2 - 5
    Explanation: From x = 2t + 1: t = (x-1)/2. Substitute: y = 4((x-1)/2) - 3 = 2(x-1) - 3 = 2x - 2 - 3 = 2x - 5. This is a line with slope 2.

    [ ] Hyperbola

  Key Concepts: Vectors & Vector Operations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
If u = <3, 4> and v = <1, -2>, what is u + v?
Vector addition is component-wise: u + v = <3+1, 4+(-2)> = <4, 2>.
What is the magnitude of the vector <3, 4>?
The magnitude is sqrt(3^2 + 4^2) = sqrt(9 + 16) = sqrt(25) = 5. This is the length of the vector.
If v = <6, -8>, what is 2v?
Scalar multiplication multiplies each component: 2v = 2<6, -8> = <12, -16>. The vector's direction stays the same but its magnitude doubles.
What is the dot product of u = <2, 5> and v = <3, -1>?
The dot product is u . v = (2)(3) + (5)(-1) = 6 + (-5) = 1.
What is u - v if u = <7, 2> and v = <3, 5>?
Vector subtraction is component-wise: u - v = <7-3, 2-5> = <4, -3>.
A vector has initial point (1, 3) and terminal point (5, 7). What is the component form?
The component form is found by subtracting initial from terminal: <5-1, 7-3> = <4, 4>.
What is the direction angle of the vector <1, sqrt(3)>?
The direction angle theta = arctan(y/x) = arctan(sqrt(3)/1) = arctan(sqrt(3)) = 60 degrees. The vector points at 60 degrees from the positive x-axis.
What is a vector?
A vector is a mathematical object that has both magnitude (length) and direction. Unlike a scalar (which has only magnitude), a vector tells you both how much and in which direction.
What is the magnitude of a vector?
The magnitude (or norm) of a vector  is its length: ||v|| = sqrt(x^2 + y^2). It is always a non-negative scalar value.
What is a unit vector?
A unit vector has a magnitude of exactly 1. To find the unit vector in the direction of any vector v, divide v by its magnitude: u = v / ||v||.
What is the dot product of two vectors?
The dot product (scalar product) of u =  and v =  is u . v = u1*v1 + u2*v2. The result is a scalar, not a vector.
What is 'scalar multiplication' of a vector?
Scalar multiplication multiplies each component by the scalar: k = . If k > 0, direction is preserved; if k < 0, direction is reversed. The magnitude is scaled by |k|.
What is the 'direction angle' of a vector?
The direction angle theta is the angle measured counterclockwise from the positive x-axis to the vector. For v = , theta = arctan(b/a) (adjusted for the correct quadrant).
What is the 'resultant vector'?
The resultant vector is the vector sum of two or more vectors. It represents the net effect of all the individual vectors combined, found by adding corresponding components.
What is the 'zero vector'?
The zero vector <0, 0> has magnitude 0 and no defined direction. It is the additive identity for vectors, meaning v + 0 = v for any vector v.
A plane flies at 400 km/h heading due north, but a wind blows from the west at 100 km/h (pushing the plane east). What is the magnitude of the plane's resultant velocity?
Plane velocity: <0, 400>. Wind velocity: <100, 0>. Resultant: <100, 400>. Magnitude = sqrt(100^2 + 400^2) = sqrt(10000 + 160000) = sqrt(170000) ≈ 412.3 km/h.
Two tugboats pull a ship. Boat A exerts a force of <3000, 4000> N and Boat B exerts a force of <5000, -2000> N. What is the resultant force on the ship?
Resultant = <3000+5000, 4000+(-2000)> = <8000, 2000> N. The magnitude is sqrt(8000^2 + 2000^2) = sqrt(68,000,000) ≈ 8246 N.
A hiker walks 5 km east and then 12 km north. What is the straight-line distance from the starting point to the ending point?
The displacement vector is <5, 12>. Distance = sqrt(5^2 + 12^2) = sqrt(25 + 144) = sqrt(169) = 13 km.
A 50 N force acts at 60 degrees above the horizontal to push a box. What is the horizontal component of the force? Use cos(60) = 0.5.
Horizontal component = magnitude * cos(angle) = 50 * cos(60) = 50 * 0.5 = 25 N.
A swimmer swims at 3 m/s perpendicular to a river that flows at 4 m/s. What is the actual speed of the swimmer relative to the ground?
The velocity vectors are perpendicular: <4, 0> for the river and <0, 3> for the swimmer. Resultant speed = sqrt(4^2 + 3^2) = sqrt(16 + 9) = sqrt(25) = 5 m/s.

Resultant velocity = <15+(-3), 20+2> = <12, 22>. Speed = sqrt(12^2 + 22^2) = sqrt(144 + 484) = sqrt(628) ≈ 25.1 m/s.

Total force = <0+(-2000), 5000+3000> = <-2000, 8000>. Magnitude = sqrt((-2000)^2 + 8000^2) = sqrt(4,000,000 + 64,000,000) = sqrt(68,000,000) ≈ 8246 N.
A GPS device shows a hiker walked displacement vectors <2, 3>, <-1, 4>, and <5, -2> (in km) on three trail segments. What is the total straight-line displacement from the starting point?
Total displacement = <2+(-1)+5, 3+4+(-2)> = <6, 5>. Magnitude = sqrt(6^2 + 5^2) = sqrt(36 + 25) = sqrt(61) ≈ 7.81 km.

--- Lesson 9.4: Complex Numbers in Polar Form (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Complex Numbers in Polar Form
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Convert the complex number 3 + 4i to polar form. What is the modulus (r)?
The modulus r = sqrt(a^2 + b^2) = sqrt(9 + 16) = sqrt(25) = 5.
Using De Moivre's theorem, what is [2(cos30 + i sin30)]^3?
By De Moivre's theorem: [r(cos theta + i sin theta)]^n = r^n(cos(n*theta) + i sin(n*theta)). So 2^3(cos(3*30) + i sin(3*30)) = 8(cos90 + i sin90) = 8(0 + i) = 8i.
What is the argument (angle) of the complex number -1 + i?
The reference angle is arctan(1/1) = 45 degrees. Since -1 + i is in Quadrant II (negative real, positive imaginary), the argument is 180 - 45 = 135 degrees.
What is the modulus of 5 - 12i?
Modulus = sqrt(5^2 + (-12)^2) = sqrt(25 + 144) = sqrt(169) = 13.
What is the polar form of the complex number -3?
-3 lies on the negative real axis. Modulus = 3, argument = 180 degrees. So -3 = 3(cos180 + i sin180).
What is the polar form of a complex number?
The polar form expresses a complex number using its modulus r (distance from origin) and argument theta (angle from positive real axis): z = r(cos theta + i sin theta), also written as r*cis(theta).
What is the modulus (or absolute value) of a complex number?
The modulus |z| of z = a + bi is the distance from z to the origin: |z| = sqrt(a^2 + b^2). It is always a non-negative real number and represents the 'size' of the complex number.
What is the argument of a complex number?
The argument (arg) is the angle theta between the positive real axis and the line from the origin to the point representing the complex number. It is found using theta = arctan(b/a), adjusted for the quadrant.
What is De Moivre's theorem?
De Moivre's theorem states that raising a complex number in polar form to a power n multiplies the argument by n and raises the modulus to the nth power. It simplifies computing powers and roots of complex numbers.
What is the complex plane?
The complex plane (or Argand plane) maps complex numbers to a 2D plane where the x-axis is the real axis and the y-axis is the imaginary axis. The complex number a + bi is plotted at the point (a, b).
What is the 'rectangular form' of a complex number?
The rectangular (or Cartesian) form of a complex number is a + bi, where a is the real part and b is the imaginary part. It corresponds to the point (a, b) in the complex plane.
Two electrical signals combine as complex numbers: z1 = 5(cos30 + i sin30) and z2 = 3(cos60 + i sin60). What is the modulus and argument of their product?
When multiplying in polar form: modulus = 5 * 3 = 15, argument = 30 + 60 = 90 degrees. So z1*z2 = 15(cos90 + i sin90) = 15i.
A quantum physics calculation requires computing (1 + i)^6. Using polar form and De Moivre's theorem, what is the result?
1 + i = sqrt(2)*cis(45). By De Moivre's: (sqrt(2))^6 * cis(6*45) = 8*cis(270) = 8(cos270 + i sin270) = 8(0 + i(-1)) = -8i.
An engineer models impedance as Z = 8 - 6i ohms. What is the impedance magnitude?
|Z| = sqrt(8^2 + (-6)^2) = sqrt(64 + 36) = sqrt(100) = 10 ohms.
A control system uses complex transfer functions. If H1 = 2cis(45) and H2 = 3cis(30), what is H1/H2?
Division in polar form: divide moduli and subtract arguments. H1/H2 = (2/3)*cis(45-30) = (2/3)*cis(15).
A wave analysis problem requires computing (sqrt(3) + i)^4. First convert to polar form, then apply De Moivre's theorem. What is the result?
sqrt(3) + i has modulus sqrt(3+1) = 2 and argument arctan(1/sqrt(3)) = 30 degrees. So z = 2cis(30). z^4 = 2^4 * cis(120) = 16(cos120 + i sin120) = 16(-1/2 + i*sqrt(3)/2) = -8 + 8i*sqrt(3).
Real-World Applications
Example: Multiply z1 = 3(cos45 + i sin45) and z2 = 2(cos30 + i sin30).
To multiply complex numbers in polar form: multiply the moduli and add the angles. z1*z2 = (3*2)(cos(45+30) + i sin(45+30)) = 6(cos75 + i sin75).
Example: An electrical engineer represents AC voltage as a complex number V = 120(cos(60) + i sin(60)). What are the real and imaginary components of this voltage?
V = 120(cos60 + i sin60) = 120(1/2 + i*sqrt(3)/2) = 60 + 60sqrt(3)*i. The real component is 60 V and the imaginary component is 60sqrt(3) ≈ 103.9 V.
Example: A radar system rotates a signal by multiplying it by cis(45) each time step. After 8 time steps, what total rotation has occurred?
Each multiplication by cis(45) rotates the signal by 45 degrees. After 8 steps: 8 * 45 = 360 degrees, which is one complete rotation back to the starting position.
