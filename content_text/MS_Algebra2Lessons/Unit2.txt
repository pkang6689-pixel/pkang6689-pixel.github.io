--- Lesson 4.1: Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the domain of (x − 3)/(x² − 4)?
The denominator x² − 4 = (x − 2)(x + 2) equals zero when x = 2 or x = −2. These values must be excluded from the domain.
What is the domain of 1/(x − 3)?
The denominator x − 3 = 0 when x = 3. Division by zero is undefined, so x = 3 must be excluded from the domain.
What is a 'rational expression'?
A rational expression is a fraction where both the numerator and the denominator are polynomials. Examples include x/(x + 1) and (x² − 4)/(x − 2).
What is the 'least common denominator' (LCD) of two rational expressions?
The LCD is found by factoring each denominator and taking each factor to its highest power. It is the smallest expression that both denominators divide into, used for adding and subtracting rational expressions.
What is a 'complex fraction'?
A complex fraction (or compound fraction) contains fractions within its numerator and/or denominator. It can be simplified by multiplying the numerator and denominator by the LCD of all inner fractions.
A company's production cost is modeled by C(x) = (x² + 5x + 6)/(x + 3) dollars per unit, where x is a scaling factor. What is the simplified cost expression?
Factor the numerator: x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3). Cancel (x + 3): C(x) = x + 2 for x ≠ −3.
A water tank has two inlets. Inlet A fills at a rate of 1/(x + 1) tanks per hour and Inlet B fills at 1/(x − 1) tanks per hour. What is their combined fill rate?
Add the rates: 1/(x + 1) + 1/(x − 1). LCD is (x + 1)(x − 1) = x² − 1. Compute: (x − 1 + x + 1)/(x² − 1) = 2x/(x² − 1).
An electrician calculates the total resistance of two resistors in parallel using 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂. If R₁ = x ohms and R₂ = (x + 4) ohms, what is R_total as a single rational expression?
1/R_total = 1/x + 1/(x + 4) = (x + 4 + x)/[x(x + 4)] = (2x + 4)/[x(x + 4)]. Taking the reciprocal: R_total = x(x + 4)/(2x + 4).
A delivery truck travels 120 miles at an average speed of r mph. The time for the trip is modeled by T(r) = 120/r. If the speed increases by 10 mph on the return, the return time is 120/(r + 10). What is the total round-trip time as a single fraction?
Total time = 120/r + 120/(r + 10). LCD is r(r + 10): [120(r + 10) + 120r]/[r(r + 10)] = (120r + 1200 + 120r)/[r(r + 10)] = (240r + 1200)/[r(r + 10)].
A landscaper divides a garden into equal plots. The area of each plot is modeled by A(x) = (x² − 9)/(x − 3) square feet, where x > 3. What does this simplify to, and what is the area when x = 7?
Factor: (x² − 9)/(x − 3) = (x − 3)(x + 3)/(x − 3) = x + 3 for x ≠ 3. When x = 7: A(7) = 7 + 3 = 10 square feet.
A chemistry student combines two solutions. The first has concentration (x + 1)/(x² − 1) and the second has concentration 2/(x + 1). She simplifies the first expression before adding. What is the sum of the two concentrations?
First expression: (x + 1)/(x² − 1) = 1/(x − 1) for x ≠ −1. Add: 1/(x − 1) + 2/(x + 1). LCD is (x − 1)(x + 1): [(x + 1) + 2(x − 1)]/[(x − 1)(x + 1)] = (3x − 1)/[(x − 1)(x + 1)].
Real-World Applications
Example: Multiply: (x + 2)/(x − 1) · (x − 1)/(x + 3)
Multiply numerators and denominators, then cancel common factor (x − 1): [(x + 2)(x − 1)]/[(x − 1)(x + 3)] = (x + 2)/(x + 3).
Example: A delivery truck travels 120 miles at an average speed of r mph. The time for the trip is modeled by T(r) = 120/r. If the speed increases by 10 mph on the return, the return time is 120/(r + 10). What is the total round-trip time as a single fraction?
Total time = 120/r + 120/(r + 10). LCD is r(r + 10): [120(r + 10) + 120r]/[r(r + 10)] = (120r + 1200 + 120r)/[r(r + 10)] = (240r + 1200)/[r(r + 10)].
Example: A manufacturing plant's average cost per widget is C(n) = (2000 + 8n)/n dollars, where n is the number of widgets produced. A manager wants to know how many widgets must be produced for the average cost to drop below $10.
Set (2000 + 8n)/n < 10. Simplify: 2000/n + 8 < 10, so 2000/n < 2, giving n > 1000. More than 1000 widgets must be produced.

    Explanation: Factor x² − 1 = (x − 1)(x + 1). Then x²/[(x − 1)(x + 1)] · (x − 1)/x. Cancel (x − 1) and one x to get x/(x + 1).

    [ ] (x + 1)/(x² + x)
    [ ] 2/(2x + 1)
    [ ] 2/(x² + x)
    Explanation: LCD is x(x + 1). Rewrite: (x + 1)/[x(x + 1)] + x/[x(x + 1)] = (2x + 1)/(x² + x).

  Q4. Simplify: (x² − 4)/(x − 2)
    [ ] x + 4

    Explanation: To simplify a rational expression, factor both the numerator and denominator completely, then cancel any common factors that appear in both. Domain restrictions from the original expression still apply.

    [ ] The greatest common factor of the denominators

    [ ] The sum of the two denominators

    Explanation: The LCD is found by factoring each denominator and taking each factor to its highest power. It is the smallest expression that both denominators divide into, used for adding and subtracting rational expressions.

    Explanation: Restrictions are values that make the denominator zero. Even after simplifying a rational expression, restrictions from the original expression must still be stated because the original is undefined at those values.

  Q17. When does a rational expression equal zero?
    [ ] When the denominator equals zero
    [ ] A rational expression can never equal zero
    [ ] When both numerator and denominator equal zero
    [*] When the numerator equals zero and the denominator does not equal zero
    Explanation: A fraction equals zero when its numerator is zero and its denominator is nonzero. If the denominator is also zero at that point, the expression is undefined, not zero.

  Q18. Why must restrictions come from the original expression, not the simplified form?
    [ ] The simplified form has no restrictions
    [ ] The simplified form always has more restrictions
    [*] Simplification may cancel factors that created restrictions; the original is still undefined there
    [ ] Restrictions change when you simplify
    Explanation: When common factors are cancelled, the simplified expression may appear to be defined at values that make the original denominator zero. However, the original expression was undefined there, so those restrictions must be kept.

    [ ] Divide the numerators and divide the denominators separately
    [*] Multiply by the reciprocal of the divisor (invert and multiply)
    [ ] Cross-multiply the two expressions
    Explanation: To divide rational expressions, multiply the first expression by the reciprocal (flipped version) of the second. Then factor and cancel common factors as usual.

--- Lesson 4.2: Operations on Rational Expressions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Operations on Rational Expressions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the vertical asymptote of f(x) = (2x + 1)/(x − 3)?
Set the denominator equal to zero: x − 3 = 0, so x = 3 is the vertical asymptote.
What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = (3x² − 1)/(2x² + 5)?
When the degrees of numerator and denominator are equal, the horizontal asymptote is the ratio of leading coefficients: y = 3/2.
What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = (x − 2)/(x³ + 1)?
When the degree of the numerator (1) is less than the degree of the denominator (3), the horizontal asymptote is y = 0.
What is the LCD of 1/x² and 1/(3x)?
The LCD uses the highest power of each factor: 3 appears once, x appears to the second power. LCD = 3x².
What is a 'vertical asymptote' of a rational function?
A vertical asymptote is a vertical line x = a where the denominator of a rational function equals zero but the numerator does not. The function values approach positive or negative infinity near this line.
What is a 'horizontal asymptote'?
A horizontal asymptote describes the end behavior of a rational function. It is found by comparing the degrees of the numerator and denominator.
What is a 'removable discontinuity' (hole) in a rational function?
A removable discontinuity occurs when both numerator and denominator equal zero at the same x-value. The common factor cancels, leaving a hole in the graph at that point.
What is the rule for finding a horizontal asymptote when the degrees of numerator and denominator are equal?
When deg(numerator) = deg(denominator), the horizontal asymptote is y = (leading coefficient of numerator)/(leading coefficient of denominator).
What is a 'slant asymptote' (or 'oblique asymptote')?
A slant asymptote is a linear (but non-horizontal) line that the graph approaches as x goes to infinity or negative infinity. It exists when the degree of the numerator is exactly one more than the denominator.
What is the LCD of 1/(x − 1) and 1/(x + 1)?
The LCD is the product of the distinct linear factors: (x − 1)(x + 1). Note that (x − 1)(x + 1) = x² − 1, but the factored form is preferred for clarity.
A chemist mixes two solutions with concentrations 1/(x + 2) and 3/(x − 2) grams per liter. What is the total concentration when the solutions are combined?
LCD = (x + 2)(x − 2). Add: [(x − 2) + 3(x + 2)]/[(x + 2)(x − 2)] = (x − 2 + 3x + 6)/[(x + 2)(x − 2)] = (4x + 4)/((x + 2)(x − 2)).
An engineer models the efficiency of a motor as E(x) = (x² − 4)/(x + 2), where x > 0. What is the simplified efficiency formula?
Factor: (x − 2)(x + 2)/(x + 2) = x − 2 for x ≠ −2. Since x > 0, the restriction x ≠ −2 is automatically satisfied.
A business analyst finds that profit is P(x) = (2x²)/(x + 3) · (x + 3)/(4x) million dollars, where x is production volume. What is the simplified profit?
Multiply: 2x²(x + 3)/[(x + 3)(4x)]. Cancel (x + 3) and simplify: 2x²/(4x) = 2x/4 = x/2.
A student divides (x² − 9)/(x + 4) by (x − 3)/(x + 4). She inverts the second fraction and multiplies. What is the result?
Invert and multiply: [(x − 3)(x + 3)/(x + 4)] · [(x + 4)/(x − 3)]. Cancel (x + 4) and (x − 3) to get x + 3.
A pharmacist combines drug rates of 2/(x + 1) and 5/(x + 1) milligrams per hour from two IVs. What is the total drug rate?
Same denominator, so add numerators: (2 + 5)/(x + 1) = 7/(x + 1).
A factory's output per hour is modeled by (x² − 1)/(x) · x/(x + 1) units, where x is the number of machines. What is the simplified output?
Factor x² − 1 = (x − 1)(x + 1). Multiply: [(x − 1)(x + 1)/x] · [x/(x + 1)]. Cancel x and (x + 1) to get x − 1.
A data analyst subtracts transfer rates: 5/(x + 2) − 3/(x + 2) megabits per second. What is the net rate?
Same denominator: (5 − 3)/(x + 2) = 2/(x + 2).
A nutritionist calculates that two food supplements provide protein at rates of 1/(x − 1) and x/(x + 1) grams per serving. What is the difference in protein rates (first minus second)?
LCD = (x − 1)(x + 1). Compute: [(x + 1) − x(x − 1)]/[(x − 1)(x + 1)] = (x + 1 − x² + x)/[(x − 1)(x + 1)] = (−x² + 2x + 1)/((x − 1)(x + 1)).
A plumber needs to find the LCD of pipe flow rates modeled by 1/(x + 1) and 1/(x² − 1). What is the LCD?
Factor: x² − 1 = (x − 1)(x + 1). The LCD must include each factor at its highest power: (x − 1)(x + 1). Since (x + 1) is already a factor of x² − 1, no additional factors are needed.
A financial analyst models two investment returns as (2x)/(x + 3) and (4x²)/(x + 3) dollars. She divides the first by the second: [(2x)/(x + 3)] ÷ [(4x²)/(x + 3)]. What is the result?
Invert and multiply: [2x/(x + 3)] · [(x + 3)/(4x²)]. Cancel (x + 3): 2x/(4x²) = 2/(4x) = 1/(2x).
Real-World Applications
Example: What does 'multiply numerators together, multiply denominators together' refer to?
When multiplying rational expressions, multiply the numerators together and the denominators together: (a/b) · (c/d) = ac/bd. Then simplify by canceling common factors.
Example: What does 'invert and multiply' mean when dividing rational expressions?
To divide by a rational expression, multiply by its reciprocal: (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) · (d/c) = ad/(bc). This is the 'invert and multiply' rule.
Example: What step should you do before multiplying rational expressions to make the work simpler?
Before multiplying, factor all numerators and denominators completely. Then cancel common factors that appear in both a numerator and a denominator, which simplifies the multiplication.
Example: A student divides (x² − 9)/(x + 4) by (x − 3)/(x + 4). She inverts the second fraction and multiplies. What is the result?
Invert and multiply: [(x − 3)(x + 3)/(x + 4)] · [(x + 4)/(x − 3)]. Cancel (x + 4) and (x − 3) to get x + 3.

--- Lesson 4.2: Operations on Rational Expressions (Quiz) ---

  Q1. What is the vertical asymptote of f(x) = (2x + 1)/(x − 3)?
    [*] x = 3
    [ ] x = −3
    [ ] y = 0
    [ ] y = 2
    Explanation: Set the denominator equal to zero: x − 3 = 0, so x = 3 is the vertical asymptote.

  Q2. What are the vertical asymptotes of f(x) = (x + 1)/(x² − 9)?
    [ ] No vertical asymptotes
    [ ] x = −1
    [ ] x = 9
    [*] x = 3 and x = −3
    Explanation: x² − 9 = (x − 3)(x + 3) = 0 at x = 3 and x = −3. Since the numerator is nonzero at these values, both are vertical asymptotes.

  Q3. What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = (3x² − 1)/(2x² + 5)?
    [*] y = 3/2
    [ ] y = 0
    [ ] y = 1

--- Lesson 4.3: Rational Equations (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Rational Equations
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is an extraneous solution in the context of rational equations?
Extraneous solutions appear after algebraic manipulation (like clearing denominators) but fail when substituted back because they create division by zero in the original equation.
What is a rational equation?
A rational equation is specifically an equation containing at least one rational expression where a variable appears in the denominator of a fraction.
What is cross-multiplication?
Cross-multiplication is a method for solving proportions: given a/b = c/d, you multiply diagonally to get ad = bc, eliminating both denominators at once.
What is a domain restriction in a rational equation?
Domain restrictions are values excluded from the solution set because they make a denominator equal to zero, which is undefined.
What is a proportion?
A proportion is an equation declaring that two ratios (fractions) are equal. Proportions are commonly solved by cross-multiplication.
What is the difference between a rational expression and a rational equation?
A rational expression is simply a ratio of polynomials (like (x+1)/(x−2)), whereas a rational equation contains an equals sign and is solved for the variable.
What is a work-rate equation?
A work-rate equation models shared tasks: if one person finishes in 'a' hours and another in 'b' hours, their combined rate is 1/a + 1/b, and the time together satisfies 1/a + 1/b = 1/t.
A boat travels 24 miles upstream in the same time it travels 36 miles downstream. If the river current is 3 mph, what is the boat's speed in still water?
Set up 24/(b − 3) = 36/(b + 3). Cross-multiply: 24(b + 3) = 36(b − 3), giving 24b + 72 = 36b − 108, so 180 = 12b and b = 15 mph.
Two trucks start 330 miles apart and drive toward each other. Truck A goes 10 mph faster than Truck B. They meet after 3 hours. What is Truck B's speed?
In 3 hours: 3r + 3(r + 10) = 330, so 6r + 30 = 330, giving 6r = 300 and r = 50 mph for Truck B.

Key Definitions
What is the vertical asymptote of f(x) = 1/(x − 3)?
Set the denominator equal to zero: x − 3 = 0, so x = 3 is the vertical asymptote.
What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = 1/x?
As x approaches infinity, 1/x approaches 0, so the horizontal asymptote is y = 0.
What is the horizontal asymptote of f(x) = (2x + 1)/(x − 1)?
When the degrees of numerator and denominator are equal, the horizontal asymptote is the ratio of leading coefficients: 2/1 = 2.
What is the y-intercept of f(x) = (x + 3)/(x − 1)?
Evaluate f(0) = (0 + 3)/(0 − 1) = 3/(−1) = −3, so the y-intercept is (0, −3).

What is a vertical asymptote?
A vertical asymptote is a vertical line x = a where the function is undefined and the output values grow without bound (approach positive or negative infinity) as x gets close to a.
What is a horizontal asymptote?
A horizontal asymptote is a horizontal line y = L that describes the long-run behavior of a function as x grows very large in the positive or negative direction.
What is a removable discontinuity (hole) in a rational function?
A removable discontinuity occurs when a factor cancels from both numerator and denominator. The graph has a 'hole' at that point because the function is undefined there, even though the limit exists.
What is a non-removable discontinuity?
A non-removable discontinuity, such as a vertical asymptote, is a break in the graph that cannot be fixed by simply assigning a function value at that point because the function grows without bound.
What is a slant (oblique) asymptote?
A slant asymptote is a diagonal line y = mx + b that the graph approaches as x goes to infinity or negative infinity. It occurs when the numerator's degree is exactly one more than the denominator's.
What is sign analysis for a rational expression?
Sign analysis involves finding where the expression equals zero or is undefined, then testing sample points in each resulting interval to determine if the expression is positive or negative there.
What is the domain of a rational function?
The domain of a rational function includes all real numbers except those x-values that make the denominator equal to zero, since division by zero is undefined.
An engineer models a measurement as d(x) = 10x/(x² + 4). What is the horizontal asymptote?
The degree of the numerator (1) is less than the degree of the denominator (2), so the horizontal asymptote is y = 0.

The degrees are equal (both degree 2), so the horizontal asymptote is the ratio of leading coefficients: 3/1 = 3. The carrying capacity is 3 thousand.

--- Lesson 4.5: Asymptotes & Discontinuities (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Asymptotes & Discontinuities
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is the LCD of (x + 1) and (x² − 1)?
Factor x² − 1 = (x − 1)(x + 1). Since (x + 1) is already a factor, the LCD is (x − 1)(x + 1).
What is a removable discontinuity?
A removable discontinuity (hole) occurs when a factor cancels from both numerator and denominator. The function is undefined at that point, but the limit exists and is finite.
What is a non-removable discontinuity?
A non-removable discontinuity, such as a vertical asymptote, cannot be fixed by assigning a value at that point because the function approaches infinity near that x-value.
What is a complex fraction?
A complex fraction is a fraction that has fractions within its numerator, denominator, or both. They are simplified by multiplying by the inner LCD or by the invert-and-multiply method.
What is the behavior of a function near a vertical asymptote?
Near a vertical asymptote, the denominator approaches zero while the numerator does not, causing the function values to grow without bound toward positive or negative infinity.
What is meant by 'end behavior' of a rational function?
End behavior describes how the function's output values behave as x grows extremely large or extremely small. For rational functions, this is captured by the horizontal or slant asymptote.
What is a one-sided limit?
A one-sided limit examines the function's behavior as x approaches a specific value from just one side. Near vertical asymptotes, the left and right one-sided limits often differ in sign.
What is the difference between a hole and a vertical asymptote?
A hole results from a common factor canceling, making the function undefined at a point where the limit still exists. A vertical asymptote occurs where the denominator is zero but the numerator is not, causing the function to blow up.
A student simplifies (1/x − 1/y) / (1/x + 1/y). What is the result?
Numerator: 1/x − 1/y = (y − x)/(xy). Denominator: 1/x + 1/y = (y + x)/(xy). Dividing: [(y − x)/(xy)] / [(y + x)/(xy)] = (y − x)/(y + x).
Subtract: 1/(x − 1) − x/(x + 1). What is the result?
LCD = (x − 1)(x + 1). Rewrite: [(x + 1) − x(x − 1)] / [(x − 1)(x + 1)] = [x + 1 − x² + x] / [(x − 1)(x + 1)] = (−x² + 2x + 1)/((x − 1)(x + 1)).
Real-World Applications
Example: Multiply: [(x² − 1)/x] times [x/(x + 1)].
Factor: (x² − 1) = (x − 1)(x + 1). The x's cancel and the (x + 1)'s cancel, leaving x − 1.
Example: A town's water treatment plant has efficiency E(x) = (100x)/(x + 5) percent, where x is the number of treatment stages. What efficiency does the plant approach with many stages?
As x approaches infinity, the leading terms dominate: 100x/x = 100. The horizontal asymptote is y = 100, so the efficiency approaches 100%.

--- Lesson 4.6: Applications of Rational Functions (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Applications of Rational Functions
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Worker A completes a job in 5 days and Worker B completes it in 4 days. What is their combined work rate per day?
Combined rate: 1/5 + 1/4 = 4/20 + 5/20 = 9/20 of the job per day.
Two cars travel 120 miles. One goes 60 mph and the other goes 40 mph. What is the difference in their travel times?
Time at 40 mph: 120/40 = 3 hours. Time at 60 mph: 120/60 = 2 hours. Difference: 3 − 2 = 1 hour.
What is a work-rate problem?
Work-rate problems involve combining individual rates (reciprocals of individual completion times) to find how long a shared task takes when entities work together.
What is a mixture problem?
Mixture problems involve combining two or more solutions with known concentrations to create a final mixture with a desired concentration, using the equation: amount1 + amount2 = total amount.
What is average cost?
Average cost is the total cost of production divided by the number of units produced. It is modeled as a rational function when there are fixed costs.
What is a rate in the context of word problems?
A rate is a ratio that compares a quantity to a unit of time or another measure, such as miles per hour (speed) or jobs per hour (work rate).
What is a combined rate?
When two or more entities work together, their individual rates add up. If A works at rate 1/a and B at rate 1/b, their combined rate is 1/a + 1/b.
In the context of mixture problems, what is 'concentration'?
Concentration is the proportion of a particular substance within a mixture, expressed as a fraction, decimal, or percentage of the total volume.
What is a 'fixed cost' in a business context?
Fixed costs do not change with production volume. Examples include rent, insurance, and equipment purchases. In the average cost formula C(x) = (F + vx)/x, F represents the fixed cost.
What is a 'variable cost'?
Variable costs increase proportionally with the number of units produced. Examples include raw materials, packaging, and direct labor per item.
A plane flies 600 miles with a 50 mph tailwind in the same time it would fly 400 miles against the wind. What is the plane's speed in still air?
Set up 600/(p + 50) = 400/(p − 50). Cross-multiply: 600(p − 50) = 400(p + 50), giving 600p − 30000 = 400p + 20000, so 200p = 50000 and p = 250 mph.
A cyclist rides 60 miles into a headwind and 60 miles back with a tailwind. The wind speed is 10 mph and the total round trip takes 8 hours. What is the cyclist's speed in still air?
Set up 60/(c − 10) + 60/(c + 10) = 8. Multiply by (c − 10)(c + 10): 60(c + 10) + 60(c − 10) = 8(c² − 100), giving 120c = 8c² − 800. Rearranging: c² − 15c − 100 = 0, so (c − 20)(c + 5) = 0 and c = 20 mph.

--- Lesson 5.3: Logarithms & Properties (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logarithms & Properties
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a logarithm?
A logarithm answers the question 'What exponent do I need?' — log_b(x) = y means b^y = x.
What is the Product Rule for logarithms?
The Product Rule states that the logarithm of a product equals the sum of the logarithms of the factors.
What is the Quotient Rule for logarithms?
The Quotient Rule states that the logarithm of a quotient equals the difference of the logarithms, in the same order as the numerator minus the denominator.
What is the Power Rule for logarithms?
The Power Rule states that the logarithm of a power equals the exponent times the logarithm of the base expression.
What is the Change of Base Formula for log_b(x)?
The Change of Base Formula converts any logarithm to a ratio of logs in any other base: log_b(x) = log(x)/log(b) = ln(x)/ln(b).
What is the value of log_b(b) for any valid base b?
Since b¹ = b, the exponent needed is 1, so log_b(b) = 1.
A scientist measures two sound intensities: I₁ = 10 and I₂ = 1,000. The difference in decibel levels is proportional to log(I₂/I₁). What is log(1000/10)?
log(1000/10) = log(100) = 2, since 10² = 100.
A chemist needs to expand log₂(4x³) using logarithm properties. What is the result?
log₂(4x³) = log₂(4) + log₂(x³) = 2 + 3 log₂(x), using the Product Rule and then the Power Rule.
A student needs to condense: log(x) + 2 log(y) − log(z). What is the single logarithmic expression?
First apply the Power Rule: 2 log(y) = log(y²). Then use Product and Quotient Rules: log(x) + log(y²) − log(z) = log(xy²/z).
An engineer uses the formula dB = 10 · log(P₂/P₁). If P₂/P₁ = 1,000, what is the decibel level?
dB = 10 · log(1000) = 10 × 3 = 30 dB, since log(1000) = 3.
A biologist models a population so that log₃(N) = 2 + log₃(5). What is N?
Rewrite 2 as log₃(9), so log₃(N) = log₃(9) + log₃(5) = log₃(45) by the Product Rule. Therefore N = 45.
A researcher needs to evaluate log₇(49) + log₇(1/7). What is the result?
log₇(49) = 2 (since 7² = 49) and log₇(1/7) = −1 (since 7⁻¹ = 1/7). The sum is 2 + (−1) = 1.
A student needs to rewrite log₆(x) using natural logarithms. What is the equivalent expression?
By the Change of Base Formula, log₆(x) = ln(x) / ln(6).
A lab report requires simplifying 2 ln(3) + ln(4) into a single natural logarithm. What is the result?
First apply the Power Rule: 2 ln(3) = ln(3²) = ln(9). Then use the Product Rule: ln(9) + ln(4) = ln(9 × 4) = ln(36).
An acoustics engineer knows that for two machines, log(I₁) = 3 and log(I₂) = 4. Using the Product Rule, what is log(I₁ · I₂)?
By the Product Rule, log(I₁ · I₂) = log(I₁) + log(I₂) = 3 + 4 = 7.
A chemistry student must solve: log(x) + log(x + 3) = log(10). What is x?
Condense the left side: log(x(x + 3)) = log(10), so x² + 3x = 10. This gives x² + 3x − 10 = 0, which factors as (x + 5)(x − 2) = 0. Since x must be positive for the logarithm, x = 2.

--- Lesson 5.4: Logarithmic Functions & Graphs (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Logarithmic Functions & Graphs
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
What is a logarithmic function?
A logarithmic function y = log_b(x) is the inverse of the exponential function y = bˣ, meaning it answers 'What exponent of b gives x?'
What is the vertical asymptote of a logarithmic graph?
The vertical asymptote marks the boundary of the domain; the graph gets infinitely close to this line but never reaches it.
What is a horizontal shift of a logarithmic function?
In y = log_b(x − h), the graph shifts right by h units (or left if h is negative), and the vertical asymptote moves to x = h.
What is a vertical shift of a logarithmic function?
In y = log_b(x) + k, every point on the graph moves up by k units (or down if k is negative), but the vertical asymptote does not change.
What is a logarithmic scale?
Logarithmic scales (like pH, Richter, and decibels) represent data spanning many orders of magnitude in a compact, readable form.
What is the x-intercept of y = log_b(x)?
Setting y = 0 gives log_b(x) = 0, so x = b⁰ = 1. The x-intercept is always (1, 0).
The pH of a solution is defined as pH = −log[H⁺]. If the hydrogen-ion concentration is [H⁺] = 0.001, what is the pH?
pH = −log(0.001) = −log(10⁻³) = −(−3) = 3.

The argument (x − 2) must be greater than 0, so x > 2. The vertical asymptote is at x = 2; the vertical shift of +1 does not affect it.

y = 2 log(1000) = 2 × 3 = 6, since log(1000) = 3.

The original value at x = 100 is log(100) = 2. Shifting up 3 gives 2 + 3 = 5.

5 = −log[H⁺] means log[H⁺] = −5, so [H⁺] = 10⁻⁵ = 0.00001.

L is the carrying capacity, the upper limit that the population approaches as t increases. The denominator ensures P(t) never exceeds L.

Exponential regression finds the best-fit exponential curve y = abˣ for data where each successive ratio of y-values is approximately constant.

--- Lesson 5.7: Natural Logarithms & e (Summary) ---

  Key Concepts: Natural Logarithms & e
Overview
This lesson covers the fundamental measures of center used to analyze and summarize data sets. You'll learn how to calculate and apply the mean, median, and mode, and understand when each is most appropriate.
Key Definitions
Solve: eˣ = 1. What is x?
Since e⁰ = 1, taking ln of both sides gives x = ln(1) = 0.
What is Euler's number e?
Euler's number e ≈ 2.71828 is an irrational number that arises naturally in continuous growth and compounding. It serves as the base for the natural logarithm.
What is the natural logarithm?
The natural logarithm uses e as its base and is written as ln(x) = logₑ(x). It is the inverse of the function eˣ.
What is the inverse property of e and ln?
The exponential function eˣ and the natural logarithm ln(x) are inverses: composing one with the other returns the original input.
What is the continuous compounding formula?
Continuous compounding uses A = Pe^(rt), the limit of A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) as n approaches infinity.
In the decay model N = N₀e^(−kt), what is k?
The decay constant k is a positive number; a larger k means the substance decays faster. The negative sign in the exponent ensures the quantity decreases.
What is the half-life formula expressed using natural logarithm?
Starting from N₀/2 = N₀e^(−kt), dividing gives 1/2 = e^(−kt), so kt = ln(2) and t₁/₂ = ln(2)/k.
What is the Power Rule for natural logarithms?
The Power Rule brings the exponent in front as a multiplier: ln(xⁿ) = n · ln(x).
A bank offers 5% interest compounded continuously. What is $1,000 worth after 4 years? (Use e^(0.2) ≈ 1.2214.)
A = 1,000e^(0.05 × 4) = 1,000e^(0.2) ≈ 1,000 × 1.2214 = $1,221.40.
A substance has a decay constant k = 0.1 per year. What is its half-life?
t₁/₂ = ln(2)/k = 0.6931/0.1 ≈ 6.93 years.
A physicist needs to solve e^(2x) = 25. What is x?
Take ln of both sides: 2x = ln(25), so x = ln(25)/2 ≈ 3.219/2 ≈ 1.61.
An investment doubles with continuous compounding in T = 10 years. What is the annual growth rate r?
From 2P = Pe^(rT), we get 2 = e^(10r), so r = ln(2)/10 ≈ 0.0693 = 6.93%.
A student needs to condense 3 ln(2) + ln(5) into a single natural logarithm. What is the result?
Apply the Power Rule: 3 ln(2) = ln(2³) = ln(8). Then the Product Rule: ln(8) + ln(5) = ln(8 × 5) = ln(40).
A student needs to rewrite log₅(x) using natural logarithms. What is the equivalent expression?
By the Change of Base Formula, log₅(x) = ln(x)/ln(5).
