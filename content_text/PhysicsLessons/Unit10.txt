Overview

Key Definitions
What is an electric field?
An electric field E exerts force on charges: F = qE. The field points away from positive charges and toward negative charges. Field strength: E = kQ/r² (point charge). The answer is 'A region where a charged particle experiences a force'. The other options — 'A region with no charges', 'A magnetic field around a wire', and 'The space inside a capacitor only' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
What is the formula for electric field?
An electric field E exerts force on charges: F = qE. The field points away from positive charges and toward negative charges. Field strength: E = kQ/r² (point charge). The answer is 'E = F/q = kQ/r²'. The other options — 'E = Fq', 'E = kQ/r', and 'E = FR²/q' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
What is electric potential?
Electric Potential (Voltage) Electric potential is the energy per unit charge : V = U/q = kQ/r. Potential difference (voltage): ΔV = W/q — work done per unit charge moving between two points. The other options — 'Force per unit charge', 'Charge per unit energy', and 'Current per unit time' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
What is the unit of electric potential?
Unit — Volt (V). 1 V = 1 J/C. Electric Potential (Voltage) Electric potential is the energy per unit charge : V = U/q = kQ/r. The other options — 'Ampere (A)', 'Ohm (Ω)', and 'Farad (F)' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
