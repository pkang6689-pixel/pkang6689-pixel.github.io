Key Definitions
What is simple harmonic motion (SHM)?
Displacement is the straight-line distance from start to finish with direction (vector). It differs from distance, which is the total path length (scalar). What is the period of a mass-spring system?
Hooke's Law: F = -kx, where k is the spring constant and x is displacement from equilibrium. Elastic PE = ½kx². The answer is 'T = 2π√(m/k)'. The other options — 'T = 2π√(k/m)', 'T = mk', and 'T = 2πmk' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
What is the period of a simple pendulum?
The period of a simple pendulum T = 2π√(L/g) depends only on length (L) and gravitational acceleration (g). It does NOT depend on mass or amplitude (for small angles). The answer is 'T = 2π√(L/g)'. The other options — 'T = 2π√(g/L)', 'T = 2πLg', and 'T = L/g' — are related concepts but do not correctly answer this specific question.
